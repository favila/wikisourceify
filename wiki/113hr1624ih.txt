{{Center|A BILL}}

 To safeguard the Crime Victims Fund.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Crime Victims Fund Preservation Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Exclusion of crime victims fund from all budgets=

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the receipts and disbursements of the Crime Victims Fund (established under  section 1402 of the Victims of Crime Act of 1984 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/10601 42 U.S.C. 10601])) shall not be counted as new budget authority, outlays, receipts, or deficit or surplus for purposes of—
:(1) the budget of the United States Government, as submitted by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]; or
:(2) the congressional budget. 

=Sec. 3. Lock-box protection=



==(a) In general– ==

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, it shall not be in order in the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]or the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]to consider any measure that would authorize the use of, or appropriate, amounts in the Crime Victims Fund, established under  section 1402 of the Victims of Crime Act of 1984 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/10601 42 U.S.C. 10601]), including amounts in such Fund that are designated to remain in the Fund for obligation in future fiscal years, for any purpose other than a purpose authorized under such Act.

==(b) 60-Vote waiver required in the Senate– ==



===(1) In general– ===

  Subsection (a) may be waived or suspended in the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]only by the affirmative vote of three-fifths of the Members, duly chosen and sworn.

===(2) Appeals– ===



====(A) Procedure– ====

Appeals in the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]from the decisions of the Chair relating to paragraph (1) shall be limited to 1 hour, to be equally divided between, and controlled by, the mover and the manager of the measure that would authorize the payment or use of amounts in the Crime Victims Fund for a purpose other than a purpose authorized under the  Victims of Crime Act of 1984 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/10601 42 U.S.C. 10601]).

====(B) 60 votes required– ====

An affirmative vote of three-fifths of the Members, duly chosen and sworn, shall be required in the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]to sustain an appeal of the ruling of the Chair on a point of order raised in relation to paragraph (1) .

==(c) Exercise of rulemaking powers– ==

This section is enacted by [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]—
:(1) as an exercise of the rulemaking power of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]], respectively, and as such they shall be considered as part of the rules of each House, respectively, or of that House to which they specifically apply, and such rules shall supersede other rules only to the extent that they are inconsistent therewith; and
:(2) with full recognition of the constitutional right of either House to change the rules (so far as relating to such House) at any time, in the same manner, and to the same extent as in the case of any other rule of that House.
