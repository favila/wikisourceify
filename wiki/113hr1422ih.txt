{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Environmental Research, Development, and Demonstration Authorization Act of 1978 to provide for Scientific Advisory Board member qualifications, public participation, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“EPA Science Advisory Board Reform Act of 2013”.

=Sec. 2. Science Advisory Board=



==(a) Membership– ==

 Section 8(b) of the Environmental Research, Development, and Demonstration Authorization Act of 1978( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4365 42 U.S.C. 4365(b)] ] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>
:(b) 
::(1) The [[w:|Board]] shall be composed of at least nine members, one of whom shall be designated Chairman, and shall meet at such times and places as may be designated by the Chairman in consultation with the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] .
::(2) Each member of the [[w:|Board]] shall be qualified by education, training, and experience to evaluate scientific and technical information on matters referred to the [[w:|Board]] under this section. The [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall select [[w:|Board]] members from nominations received as described in paragraph (3)and shall ensure that—
:::(A) the scientific and technical points of view represented on and the functions to be performed by the [[w:|Board]] are fairly balanced among the members of the [[w:|Board]] ;
:::(B) at least ten percent of the membership of the [[w:|Board]] are from State, local, or tribal governments;
:::(C) persons with substantial and relevant expertise are not excluded from the [[w:|Board]] due to affiliation with or representation of entities that may have a potential interest in the [[w:|Board’s]] advisory activities, so long as that interest is fully disclosed to the Administrator and the public and appointment to the [[w:|Board]] complies with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/208 section 208 of title 18, United States Code] ;
:::(D) in the case of a [[w:|Board]] advisory activity on a particular matter involving a specific party, no [[w:|Board]] member having an interest in the specific party shall participate in that activity;
:::(E)  [[w:|Board]] members may not participate in advisory activities that directly or indirectly involve review and evaluation of their own work; and
:::(F)  [[w:|Board]] members shall be designated as special Government employees.
::(3) The [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall—
:::(A) solicit public nominations for the [[w:|Board]] by publishing a notification in the Federal Register;
:::(B) solicit nominations from relevant Federal agencies, including the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Departments of Agriculture]] , [[w:United States Department of Defense|Defense]] , [[w:United States Department of Energy|Energy]] , and [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Health and Human Services]] ;
:::(C) make public the list of nominees, including the identity of the entities that nominated them, and shall accept public comment on the nominees;
:::(D) require that, upon their provisional nomination, nominees shall file a written report disclosing financial relationships and interests, including [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] grants, contracts, cooperative agreements, or other financial assistance, that are relevant to the [[w:|Board’s]] advisory activities for the three-year period prior to the date of their nomination, and relevant professional activities and public statements for the five-year period prior to the date of their nomination; and
:::(E) make such reports public, with the exception of specific dollar amounts, for each member of the [[w:|Board]] upon such member’s selection.
::(4) The terms of the members of the [[w:|Board]] shall be three years and shall be staggered so that the terms of no more than one-third of the total membership of the [[w:|Board]] shall expire within a single fiscal year. No member shall serve more than two terms over a ten-year period..
</blockquote>


==(b) Record– ==

 Section 8(c) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4365 42 U.S.C. 4365(c)] ] )is amended—
:(1) in paragraph (1)—
::(A) by inserting“risk or hazard assessment,”after“at the time any proposed”; and
::(B) by inserting“risk or hazard assessment,”after“to the [[w:|Board]] such proposed”; and
:(2) in paragraph (2)—
::(A) by inserting“risk or hazard assessment,”after“the scientific and technical basis of the proposed”; and
::(B) by adding at the end the following:“The [[w:|Board’s]] advice and comments, including dissenting views of [[w:|Board]] members, and the response of the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall be included in the record with respect to any proposed risk or hazard assessment, criteria document, standard, limitation, or regulation and published in the Federal Register.”.

==(c) Member committees and investigative panels– ==

 Section 8(e) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4365 42 U.S.C. 4365(e)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>These member committees and investigative panels—
:(1) shall be constituted and operate in accordance with the provisions set forth in paragraphs (2)and (3) of subsection (b), in subsection (h), and in subsection (i);
:(2) do not have authority to make decisions on behalf of the [[w:|Board]] ; and
:(3) may not report directly to the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] ..
</blockquote>


==(d) Public participation– ==

 Section 8 of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 42 U.S.C. 4365] )is amended by adding after subsection (g)the following:

<blockquote>
:(h) 
::(1) To facilitate public participation in the advisory activities of the Board, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] and the [[w:|Board]] shall make public all reports and relevant scientific information and shall provide materials to the public at the same time as received by members of the [[w:|Board]] .
::(2) Prior to conducting major advisory activities, the [[w:|Board]] shall hold a public information-gathering session to discuss the state of the science related to the advisory activity.
::(3) Prior to convening a member committee or investigative panel under subsection (e)or requesting scientific advice from the [[w:|Board]] , the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall accept, consider, and address public comments on questions to be asked of the [[w:|Board]] . The [[w:|Board]] , member committees, and investigative panels shall accept, consider, and address public comments on such questions and shall not accept a question that unduly narrows the scope of an advisory activity.
::(4) The [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] and the [[w:|Board]] shall encourage public comments, including oral comments and discussion during the proceedings, that shall not be limited by an insufficient or arbitrary time restriction. Public comments shall be provided to the [[w:|Board]] when received. The [[w:|Board’s]] reports shall include written responses to significant comments offered by members of the public to the [[w:|Board]] .
::(5) Following [[w:|Board]] meetings, the public shall be given 15 calendar days to provide additional comments for consideration by the [[w:|Board]] ..
</blockquote>


==(e) Operations– ==

 Section 8 of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 42 U.S.C. 4365] )is further amended by adding after subsection (h), as added by subsection (d)of this section, the following:

<blockquote>
:(i) 
::(1) In carrying out its advisory activities, the [[w:|Board]] shall strive to avoid making policy determinations or recommendations, and, in the event the [[w:|Board]] feels compelled to offer policy advice, shall explicitly distinguish between scientific determinations and policy advice.
::(2) The [[w:|Board]] shall clearly communicate uncertainties associated with the scientific advice provided to the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] .
::(3) The [[w:|Board]] shall ensure that advice and comments reflect the views of the members and shall encourage dissenting members to make their views known to the public and the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] .
::(4) The [[w:|Board]] shall conduct periodic reviews to ensure that its advisory activities are addressing the most important scientific issues affecting the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] ..
</blockquote>
