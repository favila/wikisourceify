{{section|H5EC2A0D76A9C4953B543D1FB1BDC4DE2|1021. Establishment}}
There is established for the judicial branch
				of the Government the [[Federal government of the United States|Office of Inspector General for the Judicial Branch]]
				(hereinafter in this chapter referred to as the Office
).

{{section|H3E0A48DB585845ABACB1A7339E6FFC06|1022. Appointment, term, and removal of Inspector General}}
(a)
Appointment
The
				head of the [[Federal government of the United States|Office]] shall be the [[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]], who shall be appointed by
				the [[|Chief Justice of the United States]] after consultation with the majority and
				minority leaders of the Senate and the [[United States House of Representatives|Speaker]] and [[United States House of Representatives|minority leader of the House
				of Representatives]].

(b)
Term
The
				[[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall serve for a term of four years and may be reappointed
				by the [[|Chief Justice of the United States]] for any number of additional
				terms.

(c)
Removal
The [[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] may be removed from
				office by the [[|Chief Justice of the United States]]. The [[|Chief Justice]] shall
				communicate the reasons for any such removal to both Houses of Congress.


{{section|H8B6E4F3BD9DF43899ED114455F539716|1023. Duties}}
With respect to the [[Federal government of the United States|Judicial Branch]], other
				than the [[Supreme Court of the United States|United States Supreme Court]], the [[Federal government of the United States|Office]] shall—
(1)
conduct
				investigations of alleged misconduct in the [[Federal government of the United States|Judicial Branch]] under chapter 16
,
				that may require oversight or other action within the [[Federal government of the United States|Judicial Branch]] or by
				[[United States Congress|Congress]];

(2)
conduct and
				supervise audits and investigations;

(3)
prevent and detect
				waste, fraud, and abuse; and

(4)
recommend changes
				in laws or regulations governing the Judicial Branch.


{{section|H307756A11F67415EA1CF9300B6949001|1024. Powers}}
(a)
Powers
In
				carrying out the duties of the [[Federal government of the United States|Office]], the [[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall have the
				power—
(1)
to make
				investigations and reports;

(2)
to obtain
				information or assistance from any Federal, State, or local governmental
				agency, or other entity, or unit thereof, including all information kept in the
				course of business by the [[|Judicial Conference of the United States]], the
				judicial councils of circuits, the [[Administrative Office of the United States Courts|Administrative Office of the United States
				Courts]], and the [[United States Sentencing Commission|United States Sentencing Commission]];

(3)
to require, by
				subpoena or otherwise, the attendance and testimony of such witnesses, and the
				production of such books, records, correspondence memoranda, papers, and
				documents, which subpoena, in the case of contumacy or refusal to obey, shall
				be enforceable by civil action;

(4)
to administer to
				or take from any person an oath, affirmation, or affidavit;

(5)
to employ such
				officers and employees, subject to the provisions of title 5
, governing
				appointments in the competitive service, and the provisions of chapter 51
 and
				subchapter III of chapter 53 of such title
 relating to classification and
				General Schedule pay rates;

(6)
to obtain services
				as authorized by section 3109
 of title 5 at daily rates not to exceed the
				equivalent rate prescribed for grade GS–18 of the General Schedule by section
				5332 of title 5, United States Code
; and

(7)
to the extent and
				in such amounts as may be provided in advance by appropriations Acts, to enter
				into contracts and other arrangements for audits, studies, analyses, and other
				services with public agencies and with private persons, and to make such
				payments as may be necessary to carry out the duties of the [[Federal government of the United States|Office]].


(b)
Chapter 16
				matters
The [[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]]
				shall not commence an investigation under section 1023(1)
 until the denial of a
				petition for review by the judicial council of the circuit under section 352(c)
				of this title
 or upon referral or certification to the [[|Judicial Conference of
				the United States]] of any matter under section 354(b) of this title
.

(c)
Limitation
The [[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall not have the
				authority—
(1)
to investigate or
				review any matter that is directly related to the merits of a decision or
				procedural ruling by any judge or court; or

(2)
to punish or
				discipline any judge or court.



{{section|HF8067A22A1DE49D1B8F3C7BF2120B683|1025. Reports}}
(a)
When To Be
				Made
The [[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall—
(1)
make an annual
				report to the [[|Chief Justice]] and to [[United States Congress|Congress]] relating to the activities of the
				[[Federal government of the United States|Office]]; and

(2)
make prompt
				reports to the [[|Chief Justice]] and to [[United States Congress|Congress]] on matters that may require action
				by them.


(b)
Sensitive
				Matter
If a report contains sensitive matter, the [[Federal government of the United States|Inspector
				General]] may so indicate and [[United States Congress|Congress]] may receive that report in closed
				session.

(c)
Duty To Inform
				Attorney General
In carrying out the duties of the [[Federal government of the United States|Office]], the
				[[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall report expeditiously to the [[United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] whenever
				the [[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] has reasonable grounds to believe there has been a
				violation of Federal criminal law.


{{section|H86D2216A7D22409683E85104D53D536C|1026. Whistleblower protection}}
(a)
In
				General
No officer, employee, agent, contractor or subcontractor
				in the [[Federal government of the United States|Judicial Branch]] may discharge, demote, threaten, suspend, harass or in
				any other manner discriminate against an employee in the terms and conditions
				of employment because of any lawful act done by the employee to provide
				information, cause information to be provided, or otherwise assist in an
				investigation regarding any possible violation of Federal law or regulation, or
				misconduct, by a judge or any other employee in the [[Federal government of the United States|Judicial Branch]], which may
				assist the [[Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] in the performance of duties under this
				chapter.

(b)
Civil
				Action
An employee injured by a violation of subsection (a)
 may,
				in a civil action, obtain appropriate
				relief.


.
