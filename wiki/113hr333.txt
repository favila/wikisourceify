{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 10, United States Code, to permit retired members of the Armed Forces who have a service-connected disability rated less than 50 percent to receive concurrent payment of both retired pay and veterans’ disability compensation, to eliminate the phase-in period for concurrent receipt, to extend eligibility for concurrent receipt to chapter 61 disability retirees with less than 20 years of service, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HA0FB54CC2BB1494C9E46E02106C756AD}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Disabled Veterans Tax Termination Act”.

={{anchor|H7CC51E6B20124A98A5DE7B34861D4BFF}}Sec. 2. Concurrent receipt of both retired pay and veterans’ disability compensation for military retirees with compensable service-connected disabilities=



=={{anchor|HCC64F0C3C6784F0088243E48C8FF380F}}(a) Inclusion of Retirees With Service-Connected Disabilities Rated Less Than 50 Percent– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 Subsection (a) of section 1414 of title 10, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by striking“Compensation”in the subsection heading and all that follows through“Subject”and inserting“Compensation.—Subject”;
:(2) by striking“qualifying service-connected disability”and inserting“service-connected disability”; and
:(3) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 paragraph (2)] .

=={{anchor|H5D34189FF7DC4D67BF6364451258B05B}}(b) Repeal of Phase-In of Concurrent Receipt of Retired Pay and Veterans’ Disability Compensation– ==

Such section is further amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 subsection (a)] , as amended by subsection (a) of this section, by striking the final sentence;
:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 subsection (c)] and redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 subsections (d)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 (e)] as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 subsections (c)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 (d)] , respectively; and
:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 subsection (d)] , as so redesignated, by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 paragraphs (3)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 (4)] .

=={{anchor|H01E33095B784485587CA904772653291}}(c) Inclusion of disability retirees with less than 20 years of service– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 Subsection (b)] of such section is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 paragraph (1)] , by striking“member retired”and inserting“qualified retiree who is retired”; and
:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1414 paragraph (2)] and inserting the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H2C40E83144804065942CD2A512C6EF8E}}(2) Disability retirees with less than 20 years of service– ====

The retired pay of a qualified retiree who is retired under chapter 61 of this title with fewer than 20 years of creditable service is subject to reduction under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5304 sections 5304] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5305 5305 of title 38] , but only by the amount (if any) by which the amount of the member’s retired pay under such chapter exceeds the amount equal to 2½ percent of the member’s years of creditable service multiplied by the member’s retired pay base under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1406 section 1406(b)(1)] or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1407 1407 of this title] , whichever is applicable to the member..
</blockquote>
