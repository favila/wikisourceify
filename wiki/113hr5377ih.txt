{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide for certain safeguards with respect to the sale of historic postal facilities, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Postal Facilities Preservation and Sales Reform Act   ”.

::::(B) provide adequate notice of its intention to close or consolidate such facility at least 180 days prior to the proposed date of such closing or consolidation to persons served by such facility to ensure that such persons will have an opportunity to present their views.

=Sec. 3. Amendments to title 39, United States Code, with respect to historic postal facilities=



==(a) Sale of postal facilities– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404  Section 404(d)of title 39, United States Code], is further amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
:(10) With respect to any historic postal facility (as that term is defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/417 section 417]) offered for sale or proposed for relocation or cessation of services by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]under this section, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]may not enter into any contract or agreement to sell the facility or relocate or cease services at such facility until the proposed decision to sell or relocate such facility is reviewed pursuant to  section 106 of the National Historic Preservation Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/470f 16 U.S.C. 470f] ). . 
</blockquote>


==(b) General historic postal facility requirements– ==

  Chapter 4of title 39, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 417. Historic postal facilities==



===(a) Historic Surplus Property Program– ===

If the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]makes a determination to sell a historic postal facility under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 section 404], and the preservation of such facility has been determined by the State Historic Preservation Officer or the Keeper of the National Register of Historic Places to be of national significance, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]shall use the Historic Surplus Property Program (administered by the [[w:National Park Service|National Park Service]]and the [[w:General Services Administration|General Services Administration]]) to dispose of such facility.

===(b) Federal preservation officer duties– ===

The Federal preservation officer at the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]shall be consulted in any decision with respect to the sale or lease of any historic postal facility or any historic art at such facility.

===(c) List of historic postal facilities; budget– ===

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]]shall maintain—
:(1) a list of historic postal facilities and publish such list on the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]Web site; and
:(2) a separate preservation budget to track obligations and expenditures by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]with respect to historic preservation activities.

===(d) New Deal art– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The Federal preservation officer at the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]shall promptly notify the National Museum of American Art in any instance where New Deal art owned by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]is loaned or relocated.

====(2) Availability of policies– ====

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]]shall make all [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]policies relating to historic preservation and New Deal art available to the public.

===(e) Definitions– ===

In this section—
:(1) the term“historic postal facility”means any postal facility listed, or eligible to be listed, in the National Register of Historic Places; and
:(2) the term“New Deal art”means any work of art located at a postal facility that—
::(A) was commissioned by the Federal Government between 1933 and 1936; and
::(B) is owned by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections for[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/ title 39, United States Code], is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/416 section 416]the following new item:

<blockquote>  417. Historic postal facilities.  . 
</blockquote>
 

=Section 4. Additional requirements with respect to historic postal facilities=



==(a) Moratorium on sale of historic postal facilities– ==

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General of the United States Postal Service]]may not enter into any agreement to sell a postal facility that is listed, or eligible to be listed, in the National Register of Historic Places unless the sale is conducted pursuant to the procedures established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404  section 404(d)of title 39, United States Code], as amended by sections 2 and 3 .

==(b) Application of National Historic Preservation Act– ==

  [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]reaffirms that sections 106 , 110 , and 111 of the National Historic Preservation Act apply to the [[w:United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]].

==(c) Development of Postal Service policies and procedures with respect to historic postal facilities– ==



===(1) Model Historic property covenant– ===



====(A) In general– ====

Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]]shall, in consultation with the [[w:Advisory Council on Historic Preservation|Advisory Council on Historic Preservation]], the National Conference of State Historic Preservation Officers, and the National Trust for Historic Preservation, develop a model covenant, to be used and applied with respect to any historic postal facility offered for sale by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]].

====(B) Covenant requirements– ====

Such covenant shall provide that—
:(i) the purchaser of such a facility shall allow public access to any historic artwork located within such facility;
:(ii) the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]shall contribute sufficient funds to the maintenance of any such artwork; and
:(iii) the purchaser of such a facility shall not demolish the historic postal facility or alter it in any way that is incompatible with the historic character of such facility.

====(C) Covenant guidance– ====

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]]shall, in consultation with the [[w:Advisory Council on Historic Preservation|Advisory Council on Historic Preservation]], the National Conference of State Historic Preservation Officers, and the National Trust for Historic Preservation, develop and implement guidelines with respect to notifying any private entity of the responsibilities of such entity under the covenant developed under subsection (a), including stewardship requirements and any pertinent information on Federal or State tax incentive programs.

===(2) Training– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]]shall, in consultation with the [[w:Advisory Council on Historic Preservation|Advisory Council on Historic Preservation]], develop guidance and training procedures for officers and employees of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]on the processes, requirements, and special considerations with respect to the sale of any historic postal facility, including—
:(A) the application of  section 106 of the National Historic Preservation Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/470f 16 U.S.C. 470f] )to such sale; and
:(B) consultation with stakeholders with respect to such sale.

===(3) Leasing– ===

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]]shall develop and implement a policy with respect to, as an alternative to selling any historical postal facility, leasing such facility, as permitted under  section 111 of the National Historic Preservation Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/470h–3 16 U.S.C. 470h–3] ).

===(4) Report on use of GSA for sale of property– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]]shall publish a report on whether the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]should use the [[w:General Services Administration|Office of Real Property Disposal of the General Services Administration]]with respect to selling any historic postal facility. 

=Sec. 5. Proposed Postal Service rule under the National Environmental Policy Act=

The proposed rule published by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]in the Federal Register on January 13, 2014 (79 Fed. Reg. 2102 et seq.), and any subsequent rule that is substantially the same shall have no force or effect.
