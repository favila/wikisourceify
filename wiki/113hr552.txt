{{Center|A BILL}}

To require the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to ensure
		  that the South Texas Veterans Affairs Health Care Center in Harlingen, Texas,
		  includes a full-service Department of Veterans Affairs inpatient health care
		  facility.

={{anchor|HAD7AE7448BFE451A83F7D28DAD00BD45}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Meeting the Inpatient Health Care
			 Needs of Far South Texas Veterans Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H5AD2054FF72E47C5A3BE366F28FD938D}}Sec. 2. Findings=

[[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress]] makes the following
			 findings:
:(1) The current and
			 future health care needs of veterans residing in the Far South Texas area are
			 not being fully met by the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].
:(2) The [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of
			 Veterans Affairs]] estimates that more than 117,000 veterans reside in Far South
			 Texas.
:(3) In its Capital
			 Asset Realignment for Enhanced Services study, the [[Wikipedia:|Department of Veterans
			 Affairs]] found that fewer than 3 percent of its enrollees in the Valley-Coastal
			 Bend Market of Veterans Integrated Service Network 17 reside within its acute
			 hospital access standards.
:(4) Travel times for
			 veterans from the market referred to in paragraph (3) can exceed six hours from
			 their residences to the nearest [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]] hospital for
			 acute inpatient health care.
:(5) Even with the
			 significant travel times, veterans from Far South Texas demonstrate a high
			 demand for health care services from the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].
:(6) Current
			 deployments involving members of the Texas National Guard and other members of
			 the reserve components of the Armed Forces who reside in Texas will continue to
			 increase demand for medical services provided by the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans
			 Affairs]].

={{anchor|HFD090136C41441AC8DD2051F7DFC0D1C}}Sec. 3. Inpatient health
			 care at the South Texas Veterans Affairs Health Care Center=



=={{anchor|HEF1A11070447403DA19B1C167755F643}}(a) In
			 general– ==

The [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall ensure that the
			 South Texas Veterans Affairs Health Care Center in Harlingen, Texas, includes a
			 full-service [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]] inpatient health care facility and,
			 if necessary, shall modify the existing facility to meet this
			 requirement.

=={{anchor|HEB0B6D9E7B92402BA01C0BD718B0539F}}(b) Report– ==

Not
			 later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Education|Secretary]]
			 shall submit to the Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the Senate and the
			 Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the House of Representatives a report
			 outlining the specific actions the Secretary plans to take to satisfy the
			 requirements in subsection (a), including a detailed estimate of the cost of
			 such actions, if any, and the time necessary for completion of any modification
			 required by such subsection.
