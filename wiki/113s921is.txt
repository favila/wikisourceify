{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amendchapter 301of title 49, United States Code, to prohibit the rental of motor vehicles that contain a defect related to motor vehicle safety, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Raechel and Jacqueline Houck Safe Rental Car Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Definitions=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102  Section 30102(a)of title 49, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraphs (10)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 (11)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraphs (12)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 (13)], respectively;
:(2) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraphs (1) through (9)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraphs (2) through (10)], respectively;
:(3) by inserting before[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraph (2)], as redesignated, the following:

<blockquote> 
::(1)   covered rental vehicle means a motor vehicle that—
:::(A) has a gross vehicle weight rating of 10,000 pounds or less;
:::(B) is rented without a driver for an initial term of less than 4 months; and
:::(C) is part of a motor vehicle fleet of 5 or more motor vehicles that are used for rental purposes by a rental company. ; and 
</blockquote>

:(4) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraph (10)], as redesignated, the following:

<blockquote> 
::(11)   rental company means a person who—
:::(A) is engaged in the business of renting covered rental vehicles; and
:::(B) uses for rental purposes a motor vehicle fleet of 5 or more covered rental vehicles. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Remedies for defects and noncompliance=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30120  Section 30120(i)of title 49, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in the subsection heading, by adding“ , or rental ”at the end;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraph (1)]—
::(A) by striking“(1) If notification”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

====(1) In general– ====

If notification ; 
</blockquote>

::(B) by indenting[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 subparagraphs (A)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 (B)]four ems from the left margin;
::(C) by inserting“or the manufacturer has provided to a rental company notification about a covered rental vehicle in the company’s possession at the time of notification”after“time of notification”;
::(D) by striking“the dealer may sell or lease,”and inserting“the dealer or rental company may sell, lease, or rent”; and
::(E) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 subparagraph (A)], by striking“sale or lease”and inserting“sale, lease, or rental agreement”;
:(3) by amending[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraph (2)]to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

===(2) Rule of construction– ===

Nothing in this subsection may be construed to prohibit a dealer or rental company from offering the vehicle or equipment for sale, lease, or rent. ; and 
</blockquote>

:(4) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

===(3) Specific rules for rental companies– ===



====(A) In general– ====

Except as otherwise provided under this paragraph, a rental company shall comply with the limitations on sale, lease, or rental set forth in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 subparagraph (C)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraph (1)]as soon as practicable, but not later than 24 hours after the earliest receipt of the notice to owner under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30118 subsection (b)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30118 (c) of section 30118](including the vehicle identification number for the covered vehicle) by the rental company, whether by electronic means or first class mail.

====(B) Special rule for large vehicle fleets– ====

Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 subparagraph (A)], if a rental company receives a notice to owner covering more than 5,000 motor vehicles in its fleet, the rental company shall comply with the limitations on sale, lease, or rental set forth in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 subparagraph (C)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraph (1)]as soon as practicable, but not later than 48 hours after the earliest receipt of the notice to owner under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30118 subsection (b)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30118 (c) of section 30018](including the vehicle identification number for the covered vehicle) by the rental company, whether by electronic means or first class mail.

====(C) Special rule for when remedies not immediately available– ====

If a notification required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30118 subsection (b)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30118 (c) of section 30118]indicates that the remedy for the defect or noncompliance is not immediately available and specifies actions to temporarily alter the vehicle that eliminate the safety risk posed by the defect or noncompliance, the rental company, after causing the specified actions to be performed, may rent (but may not sell or lease) the motor vehicle. Once the remedy for the rental vehicle becomes available to the rental company, the rental company may not rent the vehicle until the vehicle has been remedied, as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 subsection (a)].

====(D) Inapplicability to junk automobiles– ====

Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102 paragraph (1)], this subsection does not prohibit a rental company from selling a covered rental vehicle if such vehicle—
:(i) meets the definition of a junk automobile under  section 201 of the Anti-Car Theft Act of 1992 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30501 49 U.S.C. 30501] );
:(ii) is retitled as a junk automobile pursuant to applicable State law; and
:(iii) is reported to the National Motor Vehicle Information System, if required under  section 204 of such Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30504 49 U.S.C. 30504]). . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 4. Making safety devices and elements inoperative=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30122  Section 30122(b)of title 49, United States Code], is amended by inserting“rental company,”after“dealer,”each place such term appears. 

=Sec. 5. Inspections, investigations, and records=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30166  Section 30166of title 49, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30166 subsection (c)(2)], by striking“or dealer”each place such term appears and inserting“dealer, or rental company”;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30166 subsection (e)], by striking“or dealer”each place such term appears and inserting“dealer, or rental company”; and
:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30166 subsection (f)], by striking“or to owners”and inserting“, rental companies, or other owners”. 

=Sec. 6. Research authority=

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] may conduct a study of—
:(1) the effectiveness of the amendments made by this Act; and
:(2) other activities of rental companies (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30102  section 30102(a)(11)of title 49, United States Code]) related to their use and disposition of motor vehicles that are the subject of a notification required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/30118  section 30118of title 49, United States Code].

<blockquote>

Not later ;

Not later than 1 year after the date of the enactment of the“   Raechel and Jacqueline Houck Safe Rental Car Act of 2013   ”, the Secretary shall submit a report to the congressional committees set forth in paragraph (1) that contains—
:(A) the findings of the study conducted pursuant to subsection (b)(2)(F) ; and

=Sec. 8. Public comments=

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] shall solicit comments regarding the implementation of this Act from members of the public, including rental companies, consumer organizations, automobile manufacturers, and automobile dealers. 

=Sec. 9. Rulemaking=

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] may promulgate rules, as appropriate, to implement this Act and the amendments made by this Act. 

=Sec. 10. Effective date=

The amendments made by this Act shall take effect on the date that is 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act.
