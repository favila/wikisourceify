{{Center|A BILL}}

To direct the Election Assistance Commission to carry out
		  a pilot program under which the Commission shall provide funds to local
		  educational agencies for initiatives to provide voter registration information
		  to secondary school students in the 12th grade.

={{anchor|H4BF77F33B45C49CEB432EDFEEA7D36C7}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Students Voicing Opinions in Today’s
			 Elections (VOTE) Act”.

={{anchor|HB40549AB6978413D96F0DA18435177E8}}Sec. 2. Pilot program for
			 providing voter registration information to secondary school students prior to
			 graduation=



=={{anchor|HAEA189ACDB634CB2B37359DA459C1D8F}}(a) Pilot
			 program– ==

The [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Election
			 Assistance Commission]] (hereafter in this Act referred to as the
			 Commission) shall carry out a pilot program under which the
			 [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] shall provide funds during fiscal year 2013 to eligible local
			 educational agencies for initiatives to provide information on registering to
			 vote in elections for public office to secondary school students in the 12th
			 grade.

=={{anchor|H7B38F8E39EB94A0D8A0D17FEDC4749B8}}(b) Eligibility– ==

A
			 local educational agency is eligible to receive funds under the pilot program
			 under this Act if the agency submits to the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]], at such time and in
			 such form as the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] may require, an application containing—
:(1) a
			 description of the initiatives the agency intends to carry out with the
			 funds;
:(2) an estimate of the
			 costs associated with such initiatives; and
:(3) such other
			 information and assurances as the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] may require.

=={{anchor|H9F8961832CDE47B3AC8912EC45C938D6}}(c) Consultation
			 with election officials– ==

A local educational agency receiving
			 funds under the pilot program shall consult with the State and local election
			 officials who are responsible for administering elections for public office in
			 the area served by the agency in developing the initiatives the agency will
			 carry out with the funds.

=={{anchor|H25B55D663240463DBFAF5709B0A41CD3}}(d) Definitions– ==

In
			 this Act, the terms local educational agency and secondary
			 school have the meanings given such terms in section 9101 of the
			 Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/7801 20 U.S.C. 7801]).

={{anchor|HB01FF3CD17294329B7A71E428FE783F9}}Sec. 3. Reports=



=={{anchor|H1D65D3897ABF45708806F737F0CAC176}}(a) Reports by
			 recipients of funds– ==

Not later than the expiration of the 90-day
			 period which begins on the date of the receipt of the funds, each local
			 educational agency receiving funds under the pilot program under this Act shall
			 submit a report to the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] describing the initiatives carried out with
			 the funds and analyzing their effectiveness.

=={{anchor|HD7216580424149D28676C10024184103}}(b) Report by
			 Commission– ==

Not later than the expiration of the 60-day period
			 which begins on the date the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] receives the final report submitted by
			 a local educational agency under subsection (a), the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] shall submit a
			 report to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] on the pilot program under this Act.

={{anchor|HB571D90047E942B6BFEC896E93E6F470}}Sec. 4. Authorization of
			 appropriations=

There are
			 authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be necessaryto carry out this
			 Act.
