{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

Designating Diane K. Skvarla as Curator Emeritus of the United States Senate.Whereas Diane K. Skvarla will retire from the Senate after 18 years as Senate Curator, and more than 30 years of Senate service;Whereas she has diligently cared for and greatly enhanced the material history and historic spaces of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] as a legacy for future generations;Whereas she has educated and inspired the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] community, visitors to the Capitol, and the people of the United States with numerous exhibits, publications, and educational programs;Whereas her vision and leadership resulted in significant improvements to the restoration and historic interpretation of the Old Senate Chamber and other historic rooms of the Capitol;Whereas she has caused to be published significant catalogues of the fine and graphic art collections of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] for the benefit of the people of the United States;Whereas she has upheld the highest standards and traditions of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] with unwavering dedication; andWhereas she has earned the respect, affection, and esteem of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] : Now, therefore, be it

= =

That, effective January 27, 2014, as a token of the appreciation of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] for her long and faithful service, Diane K. Skvarla is hereby designated as Curator Emeritus of the United States Senate.
