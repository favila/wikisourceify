=={{anchor|HB9135A155B38442FB96EEA0F9A95101E}}Title II— Termination of
			 Election Assistance Commission==



=={{anchor|H1ECA5BB3C63D4620A4396E31981A89F5}}Sec. 201. Termination of
			 Election Assistance Commission==



==={{anchor|H5F6BEFF0BDB6443E98F5A2B2F5874C9D}}(a) Termination– ===


            The Help America Vote Act of 2002 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/15301 42
			 U.S.C. 15301 et seq.]) is amended by adding at the end the following new
			 title:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H4D3785CA12DC4902B73CEDDA7F49D6E1}}Title X— Termination of
				Commission====



====={{anchor|H1C9F47C93DD04DA79612CB39F37B6788}}A Termination– =====



====={{anchor|H9DD6D2295B4941DF898F74FCB67AFD61}}Sec. 1001. Termination=====

Effective on the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] termination
				date, the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] (including the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Election Assistance Commission Standards
				Board]] and the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Election Assistance Commission Board of Advisors]] under part 2 of
				subtitle A of title II) is terminated and may not carry out any programs or
				activities.

====={{anchor|H63DB29C6720D4ECDA586CDCC33AC6097}}Sec. 1002. Transfer of
				operations to office of management and budget during transition=====



======{{anchor|HC587C97A79EE4B3D8BEF2D3C23941EA9}}(a) In
				General– ======

The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the
				Office of Management and Budget]] shall, effective upon the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]]
				termination date—
:(1) perform the
				functions of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] with respect to contracts and agreements described
				in section 1003(a) until the expiration of such contracts and agreements, but
				shall not renew any such contract or agreement; and
:(2) shall take the
				necessary steps to wind up the affairs of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]].

======{{anchor|HBD4B7219F3F2431EAF4E0D60A9C4BE57}}(b) Exception for
				Functions Transferred to Other Agencies– ======

Subsection (a) does not
				apply with respect to any functions of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] that are transferred
				under subtitle B.

====={{anchor|H0E7B891E4DF04624BC066C0DC804CBCD}}Sec. 1003. Savings
				provisions=====



======{{anchor|H1D259FC93CB64B069986E7CB93092F47}}(a) Prior
				contracts– ======

The termination of
				the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] under this subtitle shall not affect any contract that has been
				entered into by the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] before the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] termination date. All such
				contracts shall continue in effect until modified, superseded, terminated, set
				aside, or revoked in accordance with law by an authorized Federal official, a
				court of competent jurisdiction, or operation of law.

======{{anchor|H260118CB2B6A46788326F2202831D31A}}(b) Obligations of
				recipients of payments– ======



======={{anchor|H4D4A7637BE7743F88A2553D7770CA0A1}}(1) In
				general– =======

The termination of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] under this subtitle
				shall not affect the authority of any recipient of a payment made by the
				[[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] under this Act prior to the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] termination date to use any
				portion of the payment that remains unobligated as of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]]
				termination date, and the terms and conditions that applied to the use of the
				payment at the time the payment was made shall continue to apply.

======={{anchor|HAAAED4341CF24A86ABD5E61C16AB4F28}}(2) Special rule for
				States receiving requirements payments– =======

In the case of a requirements payment made
				to a State under part 1 of subtitle D of title II, the terms and conditions
				applicable to the use of the payment for purposes of the State’s obligations
				under this subsection (as well as any obligations in effect prior to the
				termination of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] under this subtitle), and for purposes of any
				applicable requirements imposed by regulations promulgated by the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of
				the Office of Management and Budget]], shall be the general terms and conditions
				applicable under Federal law, rules, and regulations to payments made by the
				Federal Government to a State, except that to the extent that such general
				terms and conditions are inconsistent with the terms and conditions that are
				specified under part 1 of subtitle D of title II or section 902, the terms and
				conditions specified under such part and such section shall apply.

======{{anchor|HBAADFAD5CB8E4E32B352901CF2BDD4F3}}(c) Pending
				proceedings– ======



======={{anchor|H317ED3AE58DC4053914745FB4C8CEA9F}}(1) No effect on
				pending proceedings– =======

The
				termination of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] under this subtitle shall not affect any
				proceeding to which the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] is a party that is pending on such date,
				including any suit to which the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] is a party that is commenced prior
				to such date, and the applicable official shall be substituted or added as a
				party to the proceeding.

======={{anchor|HEE0E4B98E9DC4170B12F2F6B0BD3F42F}}(2) Treatment of
				orders– =======

In the case of a
				proceeding described in paragraph (1), an order may be issued, an appeal may be
				taken, judgments may be rendered, and payments may be made as if the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]]
				had not been terminated. Any such order shall continue in effect until
				modified, terminated, superseded, or revoked by an authorized Federal official,
				a court of competent jurisdiction, or operation of law.

======={{anchor|H74E9431124064A48B33ED5C1BD0628BC}}(3) Construction
				relating to discontinuance or modification– =======

Nothing in this
				subsection shall be deemed to prohibit the discontinuance or modification of
				any proceeding described in paragraph (1) under the same terms and conditions
				and to the same extent that such proceeding could have been discontinued or
				modified if the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] had not been terminated.

======={{anchor|H2A8FF248433A4807A7AD3F3E067D63AA}}(4) Regulations for
				transfer of proceedings– =======

The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management
				and Budget]] may issue regulations providing for the orderly transfer of
				proceedings described in paragraph (1).

======{{anchor|H66E10D79BBED44D1ACD765ADACB950A5}}(d) Judicial
				review– ======

Orders and actions of the applicable official in the
				exercise of functions of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] shall be subject to judicial review to
				the same extent and in the same manner as if such orders and actions had been
				issued or taken by the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]]. Any requirements relating to notice,
				hearings, action upon the record, or administrative review that apply to any
				function of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] shall apply to the exercise of such function by the
				applicable official.

======{{anchor|H126FB2E7F2F2481CB7EFEEF5DDB9B52F}}(e) Applicable
				official defined– ======

In this section, the “applicable
				official” means, with respect to any proceeding, order, or
				action—
:(1) the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management
				and Budget]], to the extent that the proceeding, order, or action relates to
				functions performed by the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]]
				under section 1002; or
:(2) the [[w:Federal Election Commission|Federal
				Election Commission]], to the extent that the proceeding, order, or action
				relates to a function transferred under subtitle B.

====={{anchor|HB0C77D1D33734E23A5272CCA34269F0E}}Sec. 1004. Commission
				termination date=====

The
				“[[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] termination date” is the first date following the
				expiration of the 60-day period that begins on the date of the enactment of
				this subtitle.

====={{anchor|HB16F0CBC99D54A20A08AB58CB9FDFD74}}B Transfer of
				Certain Authorities– =====



====={{anchor|H2A373620E784402BA41D313E893749C4}}Sec. 1011. Transfer of
				election administration functions to federal election commission=====

There are transferred to the [[w:Federal Election Commission|Federal
				Election Commission]] (hereafter in this section referred to as the
				“FEC”) the following functions of the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]]:
:(1) The adoption of
				voluntary voting system guidelines, in accordance with part 3 of subtitle A of
				title II.
:(2) The testing,
				certification, decertification, and recertification of voting system hardware
				and software by accredited laboratories, in accordance with subtitle B of title
				II.
:(3) The maintenance of
				a clearinghouse of information on the experiences of State and local
				governments in implementing voluntary voting system guidelines and in operating
				voting systems in general.
:(4) The development of
				a standardized format for reports submitted by States under section 102(c) of
				the Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee Voting Act, and the making of such
				format available to States and units of local government submitting such
				reports, in accordance with section 703(b).
:(5) Any functions
				transferred to the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] under section 801 (relating to functions of the
				former [[w:Federal Election Commission|Office of Election Administration of the FEC]]).
:(6) Any functions
				transferred to the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] under section 802 (relating to functions
				described in section 9(a) of the National Voter Registration Act of
				1993).
:(7) Any functions of
				the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] under section 1604(a) of the National Defense Authorization Act
				for Fiscal Year 2002 ([[Public Law 107-107|Public Law 107–107]]; 115 Stat. 1277; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1977ff 42 U.S.C. 1977ff
				note]) (relating to establishing guidelines and providing technical assistance
				with respect to electronic voting demonstration projects of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of
				Defense]]).
:(8) Any functions of
				the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] under section 589(e)(1) of the Military and Overseas Voter
				Empowerment Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1973ff–7 42 U.S.C. 1973ff–7(e)(1)]) (relating to providing technical
				assistance with respect to technology pilot programs for the benefit of absent
				uniformed services voters and overseas voters).

====={{anchor|H2897867B708C4339ABC875F3DB08AE05}}Sec. 1012. Effective
				date=====

The transfers under this
				subtitle shall take effect on the [[w:Election Assistance Commission|Commission]] termination date described in
				section
				1004..
</blockquote>

=={{anchor|HA235F332AF92450CAD164DE291832EE1}}Sec. 202. Replacement of
			 standards board and board of advisors with guidelines review board==



==={{anchor|HF0EB819A353C4AEF90AC2D449281ACCC}}(a) Replacement– ===

Part 2 of subtitle A of title II of the
			 Help America Vote Act of 2002 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/15341 42 U.S.C. 15341 et seq.]) is amended to read as
			 follows:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H6B4F2FCACD8F4AF5961282CC51EDE541}}2 Guidelines Review
				Board– ====



===={{anchor|HE4F43FCA975A4C6A92F9B2E6E7688877}}Sec. 211. Establishment====

There is established the Guidelines Review
				Board (hereafter in this part referred to as the “Board”).

===={{anchor|HB015BCC71C77448ABD719277D309229E}}Sec. 212. Duties====

The Board shall, in accordance with the
				procedures described in part 3, review the voluntary voting system guidelines
				under such part.

===={{anchor|H4BB69B50EF2449BE8D4079FAF7293A9E}}Sec. 213. Membership====



====={{anchor|HDE6BCD08A92644D38EF4D30144920043}}(a) In
				general– =====

The Board shall be
				composed of 82 members appointed as follows:
:(1) One State or local
				election official from each State, to be selected by the chief State election
				official of the State, who shall take into account the needs of both State and
				local election officials in making the selection.
:(2) 2 members
				appointed by the National Conference of State Legislatures.
:(3) 2 members
				appointed by the National Association of Secretaries of State.
:(4) 2 members
				appointed by the National Association of State Election Directors.
:(5) 2 members
				appointed by the National Association of County Recorders, Election
				Administrators, and Clerks.
:(6) 2 members
				appointed by the Election Center.
:(7) 2 members
				appointed by the International Association of County Recorders, Election
				Officials, and Treasurers.
:(8) 2 members
				appointed by the [[w:United States Commission on Civil Rights|United States Commission on Civil Rights]].
:(9) 2 members
				appointed by the [[w:United States Access Board|Architectural and Transportation Barrier Compliance Board]]
				under section 502 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/792 29 U.S.C. 792]).
:(10) The chief of the
				[[w:United States Department of Justice|Voting Section of the Civil Rights Division of the Department of Justice]] or the
				chief's designee.
:(11) The director of
				the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Federal Voting Assistance Program of the Department of Defense]].
:(12) The [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Director of
				the National Institute of Standards and Technology]] or the Director’s
				designee.
:(13) 4 members
				representing professionals in the field of science and technology, of
				whom—
::(A) one each shall be
				appointed by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker]] and the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Minority Leader of the House of
				Representatives]]; and
::(B) one each shall be
				appointed by the [[w:United States Senate|Majority Leader]] and the [[w:United States Senate|Minority Leader of the Senate]].
:(14) 4 members
				representing voter interests, of whom—
::(A) one each shall be
				appointed by the chair and ranking minority member of the Committee on House
				Administration of the House of Representatives; and
::(B) one each shall be
				appointed by the chair and ranking minority member of the Committee on Rules
				and Administration of the Senate.

====={{anchor|HCAD9247E317F46CFBE0F1582FD82EA1B}}(b) Manner of
				appointments– =====



======{{anchor|H134B179DFC484A6CA3187CE63D14E464}}(1) In
				general– ======

Appointments shall be made to the Board under subsection
				(a) in a manner which ensures that the Board will be bipartisan in nature and
				will reflect the various geographic regions of the United States.

======{{anchor|HE9C91BA8A32D4DD093D637DB64A46E7E}}(2) Special rule for
				certain appointments– ======

The 2 individuals who are appointed as
				members of the Board under each of the paragraphs (2) through (9) of subsection
				(a) may not be members of the same political party.

====={{anchor|H3CB78EFD9D634D3FB9EE70C36BE5DB32}}(c) Term of service;
				vacancy– =====

Members of the Board
				shall serve for a term of 2 years, and may be reappointed. Any vacancy in the
				Board shall be filled in the manner in which the original appointment was
				made.

====={{anchor|H0EDD12600F784578A7F9520723A6FE07}}(d) Executive
				board– =====



======{{anchor|H634430A0CBA143568B5DA29E0EB9FB8D}}(1) In
				general– ======

Not later than 60
				days after the day on which the appointment of its members is completed, the
				Board shall select 9 of its members to serve as the Executive Board of the
				Guidelines Review Board, of whom—
:(A) not more than 5
				may be State election officials;
:(B) not more than 5
				may be local election officials; and
:(C) not more than 5
				may be members of the same political party.

======{{anchor|H45858C0FB3F146E585FB86076EE8856C}}(2) Terms– ======

Except as provided in paragraph (3),
				members of the Executive Board of the Board shall serve for a term of 2 years
				and may not serve for more than 3 consecutive terms.

======{{anchor|H9F5C5A33C80748CCAB1A76BA5812026D}}(3) Staggering of
				initial terms– ======

Of the members
				first selected to serve on the Executive Board of the Board—
:(A) 3 shall serve for
				1 term;
:(B) 3 shall serve for
				2 consecutive terms; and
:(C) 3 shall serve for
				3 consecutive terms,as
				determined by lot at the time the members are first appointed.

======{{anchor|HD04C19CA8AC44F44895778C02FF43C4B}}(4) Duties– ======

The
				Executive Board of the Board shall carry out such duties of the Board as the
				Board may delegate.

====={{anchor|H096F62479FBD4521AD042D3F93EF3A49}}(e) Bylaws;
				delegation of authority– =====

The Board may promulgate such bylaws as
				it considers appropriate to provide for the operation of the Board, including
				bylaws that permit the Executive Board to grant to any of its members the
				authority to act on behalf of the Executive Board.

===={{anchor|H73A3C6BD04B9498885E12106F7F1F688}}Sec. 214. Powers; no
				compensation for service====



====={{anchor|H0CE1DE1BB0684E028010B149A86AAF70}}(a) Hearings and
				sessions– =====



======{{anchor|H8483015B83CC4854A287343AD7321E92}}(1) In
				general– ======

To the extent that
				funds are made available by the [[w:Federal Election Commission|Federal Election Commission]], the Board may hold
				such hearings for the purpose of carrying out this Act, sit and act at such
				times and places, take such testimony, and receive such evidence as the Board
				considers advisable to carry out this title, except that the Board may not
				issue subpoenas requiring the attendance and testimony of witnesses or the
				production of any evidence.

======{{anchor|H2C8B316D70284328ADAF069A0896731C}}(2) Meetings– ======

The
				Board shall hold a meeting of its members—
:(A) not less frequently than once every 2 years
				for purposes of selecting the Executive Board and voting on the voluntary
				voting system guidelines referred to it under section 222; and
:(B) at such other times as it considers
				appropriate for purposes of conducting such other business as it considers
				appropriate consistent with this title.

====={{anchor|HFCD1254A50964631917BAD6223BF64F6}}(b) Information from
				Federal agencies– =====

The Board
				may secure directly from any Federal department or agency such information as
				the Board considers necessary to carry out this Act. Upon request of the
				Executive Board, the head of such department or agency shall furnish such
				information to the Board.

====={{anchor|H8B839A1C68FD4F08A8512CF7669E34A7}}(c) Postal
				services– =====

The Board may use
				the United States mails in the same manner and under the same conditions as a
				department or agency of the Federal Government.

====={{anchor|HBE9C5EF3F44F4038985921D977681DD6}}(d) Administrative
				support services– =====

Upon the
				request of the Executive Board, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of the General Services
				Administration]] shall provide to the Board, on a reimbursable basis, the
				administrative support services that are necessary to enable the Board to carry
				out its duties under this title.

====={{anchor|H2EEC9CAC2008477FA9D5545E4E3463E9}}(e) No compensation
				for service– =====

Members of the
				Board shall not receive any compensation for their service, but shall be paid
				travel expenses, including per diem in lieu of subsistence, at rates authorized
				for employees of agencies under subchapter I of chapter 57 of title 5, United
				States Code, while away from their homes or regular places of business in the
				performance of services for the Board.

===={{anchor|H2F1823690705446AAA0534B0D4C46787}}Sec. 215. Status of board
				and members for purposes of claims against board====



====={{anchor|H132920D0202D40069A6C0E0810A4A660}}(a) In
				general– =====

The provisions of
				[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/161 chapters 161] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/171 171 of title 28, United States Code], shall apply with respect
				to the liability of the Board and its members for acts or omissions performed
				pursuant to and in the course of the duties and responsibilities of the
				Board.

====={{anchor|H806441A6866E4C46BDE10EDCBE7BE6B7}}(b) Exception for
				Criminal Acts and Other Willful Conduct– =====

Subsection (a) may not be construed to
				limit personal liability for criminal acts or omissions, willful or malicious
				misconduct, acts or omissions for private gain, or any other act or omission
				outside the scope of the service of a member of the
				Board..
</blockquote>

==={{anchor|HB7BB02FD52E74E44BB404113535A73C7}}(a) Procedures for

===={{anchor|HEC68761A4F9B451A9CC4021F157C31E5}}(1) Study– ====

			 paragraph (1), and shall include in the report the recommendations developed
			 under such paragraph.
