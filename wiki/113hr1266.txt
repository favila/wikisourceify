{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 40, United States Code, concerning the
		  calculation of transactions for the lease of land ports of entry and
		  international bridges, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HEBC0CCBA97B04648B10AB78F8C0EEDC3}}Section 1. Land ports of entry and
			 international bridges=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 Section
			 3307 of title 40, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the
			 following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HF8836837B9174C5A8C2E71073905C097}}(i) Land ports of
				entry and international bridges– ==



==={{anchor|H0D04E113AA814405919D0C31FA666AD8}}(1) Leases– ===

The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] is authorized to enter
				into contracts for the lease of a covered facility and its site for periods of
				not to exceed 30 years.

==={{anchor|H20AB9216BDB549828729767BB990B232}}(2) Calculation of
				transactions– ===

For purposes of
				the Balanced Budget and Emergency Deficit Control Act of 1985, the
				Congressional Budget Act of 1974, the Budget Enforcement Act of 1990, and
				scorekeeping guidelines, the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] and the
				[[w:Congressional Budget Office|Congressional Budget Office]] shall score any contract entered into by the
				[[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] under this chapter for the lease of a covered facility and its
				site in the same manner as if the contract was entered into on September 30,
				1990.

==={{anchor|HFA6A099D6A154D2C8F36AD634CB1CC5B}}(3) Analysis of
				transactions– ===



===={{anchor|HC4D320F8E5DF42B5933821C1A8CCFB64}}(A) Analysis
				required before leases or direct purchases or construction– ====

Before
				entering into a contract for the lease of a covered facility or directly
				purchasing or constructing a covered facility, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall transmit
				to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report containing an analysis of the cost effectiveness of
				leasing the covered facility as compared to directly purchasing or constructing
				the covered facility.

===={{anchor|H877EE31620584719BAECE66EBE505FD0}}(B) Limitation on
				direct purchases and construction– ====

The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] may not
				directly purchase or construct a covered facility if the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]]
				determines under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 subparagraph (A)] that leasing the covered facility is more
				cost effective.

==={{anchor|HDE5848E199AF4E2AB049766E9712765C}}(4) Definitions– ===

In this subsection, the following
				definitions apply:

===={{anchor|H7450D8BA228A4A66BB83F7428F09030D}}(A) Covered
				facility– ====

The term covered facility means a land port
				of entry or international bridge.

===={{anchor|HA6A68F1ED041408EB1B75A09B2BB11BA}}(B) Lease– ====

The
				term lease includes a lease purchase..
</blockquote>
