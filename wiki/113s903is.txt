{{Center|A BILL}}

 To clarify State of residence requirements for aliens and nonimmigrant requirements for purposes ofchapter 44of title 18, United States Code.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Alien Gun Violence Prevention Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. State of residence clarification=

 Chapter 44 of title 18, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 section 921], by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
::(c) For purposes of this chapter, an alien, as defined in  section 101(a)(3) of the Immigration and Nationality Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1101 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(3)] ), who is legally in the United States shall be considered to be a resident of a State only if the alien—
:::(1) is residing in the State; and
:::(2) has resided in the State for not less than 90 days before the date of sale or delivery of a firearm. ; and 
</blockquote>

:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922]—
::(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subsection (d)(5)(B)], by striking“(y)(2)”and all that follows and inserting“(y), is in the United States not as an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence;”;
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subsection (g)(5)(B)], by striking“(y)(2)”and all that follows and inserting“(y), is in the United States not as an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence;”; and
::(C) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subsection (y)]—
:::(i) in the heading by striking“ admitted under nonimmigrant visas ”and inserting“ not lawfully admitted for permanent residence ”;
:::(ii) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (1)], by amending[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subparagraph (B)]to read as follows:

<blockquote> 
::::(B) the term lawfully admitted for permanent residence has the same meaning as in  section 101(a)(20) of the Immigration and Nationality Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1101 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(20)]). ; 
</blockquote>

:::(iii) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (2)], by striking“under a nonimmigrant visa”and inserting“but not lawfully admitted for permanent residence”; and
:::(iv) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (3)(A)], by striking“admitted to the United States under a nonimmigrant visa”and inserting“lawfully admitted to the United States but not as an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence”.
