{{Center|A BILL}}

To repeal certain appropriations riders that limit the ability of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives to administer the Federal firearms laws.

={{anchor|H883F534C79794681AF7E05C1828A7B96}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Enforce Existing Gun Laws Act”.

={{anchor|H529DAA28A7DE4168A3CC76CD462DBDB0}}Sec. 2. Repeal of certain appropriations riders that limit the ability of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives to administer the Federal firearms laws=



=={{anchor|HC1F802A8BFD846FF82FCEB449BCD7764}}(a) Prohibition on consolidation or centralization in the Department of Justice of firearms acquisition and disposition records maintained by Federal firearms licensees– ==

The matter under the heading“Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives—Salaries and Expenses”in title I of division B of the Consolidated and Further Continuing Appropriations Act, 2012([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/923 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 18 U.S.C. 923] note] ;[[Public Law -|Public Law 112–55]] ;125 Stat. 609–610)is amended by striking the 1st proviso.

=={{anchor|H14675F6D469D4824BF0436919C82B394}}(b) Prohibition on imposition of requirement that firearms dealers conduct physical check of firearms inventory– ==

The matter under the heading“Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives—Salaries and Expenses”in title II of division B of the Consolidated and Further Continuing Appropriations Act, 2013([[Public Law -|Public Law 113–6]] )is amended by striking the 5th proviso.

=={{anchor|H5E873C64EF304DB68E9FF433EB11DB0C}}(c) Requirement that instant check records be destroyed within 24 hours– ==

 Section 511 of the Consolidated and Further Continuing Appropriations Act, 2012([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 18 U.S.C. 922 note] ;[[Public Law -|Public Law 112–55]] ;125 Stat. 632)is amended—
:(1) by striking“—”and all that follows through“(1)”; and
:(2) by striking the semicolon and all that follows and inserting a period.
