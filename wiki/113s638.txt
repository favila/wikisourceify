{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Federal antitrust laws to provide expanded coverage and to eliminate exemptions from such laws that are contrary to the public interest with respect to railroads.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Railroad Antitrust Enforcement Act of 2013”.

=Sec. 2. Injunctions against railroad common carriers=

The proviso in section 16 of the Clayton Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 26] )ending with“Code.”is amended to read as follows:“Provided,That nothing herein contained shall be construed to entitle any person, firm, corporation, or association, except the United States, to bring suit for injunctive relief against any common carrier that is not a railroad subject to the jurisdiction of the [[w:|Surface Transportation Board]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/ subtitle IV of title 49, United States Code] .”.

=Sec. 3. Mergers and acquisitions of railroads=

The sixth undesignated paragraph of section 7 of the Clayton Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 18] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>
: Nothing contained in this section shall apply to transactions duly consummated pursuant to authority given by the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] , [[w:|Federal Power Commission]] , [[w:|Surface Transportation Board]] (except for transactions described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/11321 section 11321 of that title] ), the [[w:U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|Securities and Exchange Commission]] in the exercise of its jurisdiction under section 10 (of the Public Utility Holding Company Act of 1935), the [[w:|United States Maritime Commission]] , or the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] under any statutory provision vesting such power in the Commission, Board, or Secretary..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 4. Limitation of primary jurisdiction=

The Clayton Actis amended by adding at the end thereof the following:

<blockquote>

=29. =

In any civil action against a common carrier railroad under section 4, 4C, 15, or 16of this Act, the district court shall not be required to defer to the primary jurisdiction of the [[w:|Surface Transportation Board]] ..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 5. Federal trade commission enforcement=



==(a) Clayton Act– ==

 Section 11(a) of the Clayton Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/21 15 U.S.C. 21(a)] ] )is amended by striking“subject to jurisdiction”and all that follows through the first semicolon and inserting“subject to jurisdiction under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/ subtitle IV of title 49, United States Code] (except for agreements described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 section 10706 of that title] and transactions described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/11321 section 11321 of that title] );”.

==(b) FTC Act– ==

 Section 5(a)(2) of the Federal Trade Commission Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/45 15 U.S.C. 45(a)(2)] )is amended by striking“common carriers subject”and inserting“common carriers, except for railroads, subject”.

=Sec. 6. Expansion of treble damages to rail common carriers=

 Section 4 of the Clayton Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 15] )is amended by—
:(1) redesignating subsections (b) and (c)as subsections (c) and (d), respectively; and
:(2) inserting after subsection (a)the following:

<blockquote>
::(b)  Subsection (a)shall apply to a common carrier by railroad subject to the jurisdiction of the [[w:|Surface Transportation Board]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/ subtitle IV of title 49, United States Code] , without regard to whether such railroads have filed rates or whether a complaint challenging a rate has been filed..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 7. Termination of exemptions in title49=



==(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 10706] of title 49, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 subsection (a)] —
::(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 paragraph (2)(A)] , by striking“, and the Sherman Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1 15 U.S.C. 1 et seq.] ] ),”and all that follows through“or carrying out the agreement”in the third sentence;
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 paragraph (4)] —
:::(i) by striking the second sentence; and
:::(ii) by striking“However, the”in the third sentence and inserting“The”; and
::(C) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 paragraph (5)(A)] , by striking“, and the antitrust laws set forth in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 paragraph (2)] of this subsection do not apply to parties and other persons with respect to making or carrying out the agreement”; and
:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 subsection (e)] and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

====(e) Application of antitrust laws– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

Nothing in this section exempts a proposed agreement described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 subsection (a)] from the application of the Sherman Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1 15 U.S.C. 1 et seq.] ), the Clayton Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 12] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/14 14 et seq.] ), the Federal Trade Commission Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/41 15 U.S.C. 41 et seq.] ] ), section 73or 74 of the Wilson Tariff Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/8 15 U.S.C. 8] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/9 9] ), or the Act of June 19, 1936([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 13] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/13a 13a] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/13b 13b] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/21a 21a] ).

=====(2) Antitrust analysis to consider impact– =====

In reviewing any such proposed agreement for the purpose of any provision of law described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10706 paragraph (1)] , the [[w:|Board]] shall take into account, among any other considerations, the impact of the proposed agreement on shippers, on consumers, and on affected communities..
</blockquote>


==(b) Combinations– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/11321 Section 11321 of title 49, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/11321 subsection (a)] —
::(A) by striking“The authority”in the first sentence and inserting“Except as provided in sections 4([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 15] ), 4C([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 15c] ), section 15([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/25 15 U.S.C. 25] ), and section 16([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 26] )of the Clayton Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/21 15 U.S.C. 21(a)] ] ), the authority”; and
::(B) by striking“is exempt from the antitrust laws and from all other law,”in the third sentence and inserting“is exempt from all other law (except the antitrust laws referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/11321 subsection (c)] ),”; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

====(c) Application of antitrust laws– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

Nothing in this section exempts a transaction described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/11321 subsection (a)] from the application of the Sherman Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1 15 U.S.C. 1 et seq.] ), the Clayton Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 12] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/14 14 et seq.] ), the Federal Trade Commission Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/41 15 U.S.C. 41 et seq.] ] ), section 73or 74 of the Wilson Tariff Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/8–9 15 U.S.C. 8–9] ), or the Act of June 19, 1936([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 13] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/13a 13a] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/13b 13b] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/21a 21a] ). The preceding sentence shall not apply to any transaction relating to the pooling of railroad cars approved by the [[w:|Surface Transportation Board]] or its predecessor agency pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/11322 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 11322] of title 49, United States Code] .

=====(2) Antitrust analysis to consider impact– =====

In reviewing any such transaction for the purpose of any provision of law described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/11321 paragraph (1)] , the [[w:|Board]] shall take into account, among any other considerations, the impact of the transaction on shippers and on affected communities..
</blockquote>

=Sec. 8. Effective date=



==(a) In general– ==

Subject to the provisions of subsection (b), this Act shall take effect on the date of enactment of this Act.

==(b) Conditions– ==



===(1) Previous conduct– ===

A civil action under section 4, 15, or 16 of the Clayton Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 15] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/25 25] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/26 26] ) or complaint under section 5 of the Federal Trade Commission Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 45] )may not be filed with respect to any conduct or activity that occurred prior to the date of enactment of this Act that was previously exempted from the antitrust laws as defined in section 1 of the Clayton Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 12] )by orders of the [[w:|Interstate Commerce Commission]] or the [[w:|Surface Transportation Board]] issued pursuant to law.

===(2) Grace period– ===

A civil action or complaint described in paragraph (1)may not be filed earlier than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act with respect to any previously exempted conduct or activity or previously exempted agreement that is continued subsequent to the date of enactment of this Act.
