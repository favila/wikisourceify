{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Public Health Service Act to authorize
		  appointment of Doctors of Chiropractic to regular and reserve corps of the
		  Public Health Service Commissioned Corps, and for other
		  purposes.

={{anchor|HC94EF8A4AA564CD18B3367E7DF1212EC}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Chiropractic Membership in the Public
			 Health Service Commissioned Corps Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H7A2BC1D8AA364658B54699D54447AB20}}Sec. 2. Doctors of
			 Chiropractic in the Public Health Service Commissioned Corps=



=={{anchor|H43014D3D51A14A4D8AD0C08D78B6A708}}(a) Appointment of
			 personnel– ==

Section 207(a)(1) of the Public Health Service Act (42
			 U.S.C. 209(a)(1)) is amended by inserting “chiropractic,” after
			 “nursing,”.

=={{anchor|HD446DE6451EC407680CF835EE777AEC8}}(b) Authorization of
			 appointment of Doctors of Chiropractic– ==

Section 207 of the Public
			 Health Service Act (42 U.S.C. 209) is amended by adding at the end the
			 following new subsection:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H01AF3DC167D843B6A98EB228DBC5C3B1}}(j) Appointment of
				Doctors of Chiropractic– ===

Doctors of Chiropractic who are graduates
				of colleges of chiropractic whose graduates are eligible for licensure to
				practice chiropractic in a majority of the States of the United States, or
				approved by a body or bodies acceptable to the [[Wikipedia:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]], shall be eligible for
				appointment as officers in the commissioned [[Wikipedia:United States Public Health Service|Regular Corps]] and the [[Wikipedia:United States Public Health Service|Ready Reserve
				Corps of the Public Health Service]]. The [[Wikipedia:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] and the [[Wikipedia:|Surgeon General]] shall
				ensure that such Doctors of Chiropractic are trained, equipped, and otherwise
				prepared to fulfill applicable public health and emergency response service
				responsibilities in the Commissioned
				Corps..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HCE224F23DD7C4EB4B9F51089B5D7AA52}}(c) Required
			 placement of Doctors of Chiropractic into Public Health Service Commissioned
			 Corps– ==



==={{anchor|HCD74A0E1C2274EF398CF5F55B1ACFD7E}}(1) In
			 general– ===

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of
			 this Act, the [[Wikipedia:Executive Office of the President|President]], in consultation with the [[Wikipedia:|Surgeon General]] and the
			 [[Wikipedia:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]], shall commence the appointment of no
			 fewer than 6 Doctors of Chiropractic into the commissioned [[Wikipedia:United States Public Health Service|Regular Corps]] and
			 the [[Wikipedia:United States Public Health Service|Ready Reserve Corps of the Public Health Service]].

==={{anchor|H6B28D074574A4EA3A67A5FB359A4385E}}(2) Reports– ===

The
			 [[Wikipedia:|Surgeon General]] shall submit to the Committee on Energy and Commerce of the
			 House of Representatives and the Committee on Health Education Labor and
			 Pensions of the Senate quarterly reports on all measures taken by the
			 President, the [[Wikipedia:|Surgeon General]], and the [[Wikipedia:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]]
			 to carry out the provisions of this Act, including the amendments made by this
			 Act. The first such report shall be submitted not later than 120 days after the
			 date of the enactment of this Act.

=={{anchor|H3CCC16AE1BB34BBB8E8701A331E16BDB}}(d) Authorization of
			 appropriations– ==

Out of the
			 total aggregate appropriations made available to the [[Wikipedia:Department of Health and Human Services|Department of Health and
			 Human Services]] through annual appropriations Acts for fiscal years 2014, 2015,
			 2016, and 2017, $1,500,000 is authorized and shall be made available in each of
			 such fiscal years only for the salaries and expenses necessary to carry out
			 this Act.
