=Sec. 2. Definitions=

In this Act:

==(a) Commission– ==

The term Commission means the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Nuclear Regulatory Commission]] .

==(b) Chairman– ==

The term Chairman means the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman of the Commission]] .

==Title I— Replacement of Reorganization Plan==



==Sec. 101. General functions==



===(a) Functions vested in the Commission– ===



====(1) In general– ====

There shall remain vested in the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] the functions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] relating to—
:(A) policy formulation;
:(B) rulemaking, as described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553 section 553of title 5, United States Code] , except that the matters described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553 subsections (a)(2)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553 (b)] of that section that do not pertain to policy formulation orders or adjudications shall be reserved to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] ;
:(C) orders and adjudications, as those terms are defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 paragraphs (6)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 (7) of section 551 of title 5, United States Code] , respectively; and
:(D) approving the distribution of appropriated funds according to programs and purposes proposed by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] .

====(2) Voting; policy proposals– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

If there is a doubt as to whether a matter, action, question, or area of inquiry pertains to one of the functions described in paragraph (1), the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may make a determination with respect to the pertinence, by majority vote.

=====(B) Request– =====

Any member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may—
:(i) request a vote under subparagraph (A); and
:(ii) propose a policy matter for consideration by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

====(3) Access to information– ====

All members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall have full, unfettered, timely, and equal access to information of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

====(4) Delegation of functions– ====

The performance of any portion of the functions described in paragraph (1)may be delegated by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] to—
:(A) a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] (including the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] ); and
:(B) the staff of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

===(b) Officers and employees– ===



====(1) Appointment and removal of certain officers– ====



=====(A) Appointment– =====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall initiate the appointment, subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , of the officers or successor officers established by law or by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] described in subparagraph (C).

=====(B) Removal– =====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] or a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may initiate an action for removal, subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , by majority vote, of the officers or successor officers established by law or by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] described in subparagraph (C).

=====(C) Description of officers– =====

The officers referred to in subparagraphs (A)and (B)consist of the following:
:(i) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] .
:(ii) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chief]] and [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Deputy Chief Financial Officer]] .
:(iii) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|General Counsel]] .
:(iv) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Commission Appellate Adjudication]] .
:(v) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Secretary of the Commission]] .
:(vi) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Public Affairs]] .
:(vii) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Congressional Affairs]] .
:(viii) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of International Programs]] .
:(ix) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chief Administrative Judge]] and members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Atomic Safety and Licensing Board Panel]] .

=====(D) Evaluations– =====

Any performance evaluation or rating of the officers described in subparagraph (C)shall be determined by a majority vote of the members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

=====(E) Replacement of officers– =====



======(i) In general– ======

If there is a vacancy in a position described in subparagraph (C), the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may designate an acting officer for a period of 60 days.

======(ii) Approval of extension required– ======

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may only extend the initial 60-day period under clause (i)with the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

======(iii) Failure to approve– ======

If, at the end of the 60-day period under clause (i), the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] has not proposed a replacement or the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] has not approved the appointment of an officer proposed by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , any member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may initiate the appointment, subject to approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

====(2) Appointment and removal of other officers– ====



=====(A) Appointment– =====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , after consultation with the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] , shall initiate the appointment, subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , of the officers or successor officers established by law or by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] described in subparagraph (C).

=====(B) Removal– =====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] or a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may initiate an action for removal, subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , by majority vote, of the officers or successor officers established by law or by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] described in subparagraph (C).

=====(C) Description of officers– =====

The officers referred to in subparagraphs (A)and (B)consist of the following:
:(i) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Nuclear Reactor Regulation]] .
:(ii) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Nuclear Material Safety and Safeguards]] .
:(iii) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Nuclear Regulatory Research]] .
:(iv) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Nuclear Security and Incident Response]] .
:(v) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of New Reactors]] .
:(vi) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Federal and State Materials and Environmental Management Programs]] .
:(vii) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Investigations]] .
:(viii) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Enforcement]] .

====(3) Appointment of Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] or a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall initiate the appointment of a member of the [[w:|Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards]] , subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

=====(B) Limitation– =====

Nothing in this Act affects—
:(i) the appointment of the [[w:|Chairman of the Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards]] ; or
:(ii) the term of the members of [[w:|Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards]] .

====(4) Delegation of staff supervision functions– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall delegate the function of appointing, removing, and supervising the staff of the following offices or successor offices to the respective heads of the offices:
:(A)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] .
:(B)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|General Counsel]] .
:(C)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Secretary of the Commission]] .
:(D)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chief Financial Officer]] .
:(E) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of Commission Appellate Adjudication]] .
:(F) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of Congressional Affairs]] .
:(G) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of Public Affairs]] .
:(H) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of International Programs]] .

====(5) Delegation of staff of panels and committees– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall delegate the functions of appointing, removing, and supervising the staff of the following panels and committee to the respective Chair of the panel or committee:
:(A) The [[w:|Atomic Safety and Licensing Board Panel]] .
:(B) The [[w:|Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards]] .

===(c) Commission member offices– ===

Each member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall continue to appoint, remove, and supervise the personnel employed in the immediate office of the member.

===(d) Performance of functions– ===

 Section 201(a)(1) of the Energy Reorganization Act of 1974( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/5841 42 U.S.C. 5841(a)(1)] )shall apply to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] in the performance of the functions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] as described in subsections (a)and (b).

==Sec. 102. Chairman==



===(a) Transfer of functions– ===

Any function of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] not described in section 101(a)(1)is transferred to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] .

===(b) Duties– ===

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall—
:(1) be the official spokesman for the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , which includes representing the policies established by a majority of the members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
:(2) be the principal executive officer of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
:(3) be responsible to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] for ensuring that the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] and the staff of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] (other than the officers and staff referred to in subsections (b)(4)and (c) of section 101) are responsive to the requirements of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] in the performance of the functions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
:(4) determine the use and expenditure of funds of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , in accordance with the distribution of appropriated funds according to programs and purposes approved by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
:(5) present to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , for consideration by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , the proposals and estimates prepared under paragraph (6)(C); and
:(6) be responsible for (which the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall delegate, subject to direction and supervision by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] , unless otherwise provided by this Act)—
::(A) administrative functions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
::(B) distribution of business among personnel, administrative units, and offices of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
::(C) preparation of proposals for the reorganization of the major offices of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ; and
::(D) appointing and removing, without any further action by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , all officers and employees under the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] other than the offices and employees, the appointment and removal of which are specifically provided for by subsections (b)(4)and (c) of section 101.

===(c) Governing principles– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] (as principal executive officer) and the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] , shall be governed by—
:(A) the general policies of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ; and
:(B) any regulatory decisions, findings, and determinations (including decisions, findings, and determinations for reorganization proposals, budget revisions, and the distribution of appropriated funds) as the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may by law be authorized to make.

====(2) Full and current information– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] and the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] shall be jointly responsible for ensuring that the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] is fully and currently informed about matters within the functions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

====(3) Failure to act in accordance– ====

If a majority of the members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] determine that the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] has not acted in accordance with paragraph (1)or (2), the members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall—
:(A) submit to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] written notice of the determination; and
:(B) transmit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] copies of the notice submitted under subparagraph (A).

==Sec. 103. Emergency authority==



===(a) In general– ===

Notwithstanding sections 101and 102and subject to subsection (b), the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] is authorized to exercise emergency authority of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] in responding to, issuing orders respecting, advising United States civil authorities and the United States public about, and directing and coordinating actions relating to an emergency incident.

===(b) Limitations– ===



====(1) Emergency declaration required– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may not exercise emergency authority under subsection (a)until—
:(i) the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] issues a declaration that a specific emergency exists; and
:(ii) not later than 24 hours after the issuance of a declaration under subparagraph (A), the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] provides notice of the declaration—
::(I) in writing to—
:::(aa) the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
:::(bb) the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] ; and
:::(cc) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] ; and
::(II) except as provided in subparagraph (B), to the public.

=====(B) Public notification– =====

Notwithstanding subclause (II) of subparagraph (A)(ii), public notification of a declaration under that subclause may be delayed beyond the 24-hour period specified in subparagraph (A)(ii)if the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] —
:(i) determines that disclosing the declaration to the public at that time would constitute a risk to public health or safety; and
:(ii) submits notice of the determination under clause (i)to—
::(I) the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
::(II) the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] ; and
::(III) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] .

====(2) Authorized emergencies– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may only exercise emergency authority under subsection (a)in response to—
:(A) an imminent safety threat pertaining to a facility or materials licensed or regulated by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ; or
:(B) a determination of an imminent security threat to a facility or materials licensed or regulated by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] is made by—
::(i) the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] ;
::(ii) the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]] ;
::(iii) the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] ;
::(iv) the [[w:Federal Bureau of Investigation|Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation]] ;
::(v) the [[w:Central Intelligence Agency|Director of the Central Intelligence Agency]] ; or
::(vi) the [[w:|Director of National Intelligence]] .

====(3) Duration– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may only exercise emergency authority under subsection (a)for the fewer of—
:(i) the duration of the emergency; or
:(ii) 30 days.

=====(B) Extension– =====

The initial period established under subparagraph (A)may be extended by 30 days if the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] —
:(i) approves the extension; and
:(ii) submits notice of the extension to—
::(I) the public;
::(II) the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] ; and
::(III) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] .

===(c) Delegation– ===



====(1) To members– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may delegate the authority to perform the emergency functions described in subsection (a), in whole or in part, to any of the other members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

====(2) To staff– ====

The authority to perform the emergency functions described in subsection (a)may be delegated or redelegated, in whole or in part, to the staff of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

===(d) Consultation– ===



====(1) In general– ====

To the maximum extent practicable, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall consult with the full Commission on any regulatory or policy actions taken under the emergency authority provided under this section.

====(2) Exemption– ====

A consultation under paragraph (1)shall be exempt from the requirements of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552b section 552bof title 5, United States Code] .

===(e) Guidelines and notice– ===

In acting under this section, the actions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , or other member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] delegated authority under subsection (c), shall conform to the policy guidelines of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

===(f) Termination of emergency– ===

On termination of the emergency, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall immediately submit notice of the termination to—
:(1) the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
:(2) the public;
:(3) the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] ; and
:(4) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] .

===(g) Report– ===

Not later than 30 days after the date on which the emergency is terminated under subsection (f), the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , or the member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] or staff member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] delegated the emergency functions under subsection (c), shall submit a report that describes all actions taken during the emergency, including a description of any actions taken using the authority provided by this section, to—
:(1) the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ;
:(2) the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] ; and
:(3) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] .

===(h) Commission procedures– ===



====(1) In general– ====

Not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall revise the procedures of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] to comply with the requirements of this section.

====(2) Requirements– ====

The revision under paragraph (1)shall define the roles of the members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] during an emergency, including providing for—
:(A) complete access to—
::(i) records and information relating to actions taken during the emergency;
::(ii)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] staff involved in the management of the emergency; and
::(iii) one or more locations at which decisions are made during the emergency; and
:(B) to the extent practicable, participation in decisions that may affect [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] actions and policies beyond the response to a particular emergency.

==Sec. 104. Reporting==



===(a) Delegation; reporting– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may make any delegations and provide for any reporting that the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] determines to be necessary, subject to applicable provisions of law.

====(2) Direct communication– ====

Any officer or employee under the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may communicate directly to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , or to any member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , if the officer or employee determines that a critical problem or matter of public health, public safety, or common defense and security is not being properly addressed.

===(b) Executive Director for Operations– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] shall report all matters to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] .

====(2) Submission of reports to executive director for operations– ====

The [[w:|Directors of Nuclear Reactor Regulations]] , [[w:|Nuclear Material Safety and Safeguards]] , and [[w:|Nuclear Regulatory Research]] shall report to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] .

===(c) Direct reporting– ===



====(1) Submission of reports– ====

The heads of the Commission-level offices or successor offices of the following offices shall report directly to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] :
:(A) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|General Counsel]] .
:(B) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Secretary of the Commission]] .
:(C) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of Commission Appellate Adjudication]] .
:(D) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of Congressional Affairs]] .
:(E) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of Public Affairs]] .
:(F) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of International Programs]] .
:(G) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Atomic Safety and Licensing Board Panel]] .
:(H) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards]] .

====(2) Receipt of reports– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall receive the reports submitted under paragraph (1).

==Sec. 105. Rescission of Reorganization Plan approval==

The approval of Reorganization Plan No. 1 of 1980 (45 Fed. Reg. 40561) (adopted pursuant to the Reorganization Act Amendments of 1984( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/901 5 U.S.C. 901 et seq.] )) is rescinded.

==Title II— Miscellaneous==



==Sec. 201. Certification of documents transmitted to Congress==

A letter or other document transmitted by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , on behalf of the full [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , to a member of Congress in the capacity of the member as Chairman or Ranking Minority Member of a Committee of Congress, shall include a certification that the letter or document is being sent to the Chairman and Ranking Minority Member of that Committee, in accordance with established [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] procedures.

==Sec. 202. Time limits for Commission review of Atomic Safety and Licensing Board decisions==



===(a) In general– ===

In reviewing the decisions and actions of the [[w:|Atomic Safety and Licensing Board]] , not later than 90 days after the date on which the Commission receives final briefs relating to the decision or action, each member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall vote on the matter under review.

===(b) Notification of nonvoters– ===

Once a majority position of the members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] has been established by members voting under subsection (a), the Secretary shall notify in writing any member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] that has not voted in accordance with that subsection that a majority position has been established with respect to the matter under review.

===(c) Deadline for voting– ===

A member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] that receives notice under subsection (b)—
:(1) shall have within 3 days of the date of the notice to vote on the matter under review; and
:(2) shall be considered by the Secretary as not participating in the vote if the member does not vote by the deadline specified in paragraph (1).

===(d) Publication– ===

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall publish any resulting decision of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] under this section, including adjudicatory orders and direction to agency staff—
:(1) if a majority position is established with respect to the matter under review under this section, not later than 30 days after the date on which the majority position is established; or
:(2) if a majority position is not established because of a tie vote of the participating members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , not later than 30 days after the date on which the voting is completed in accordance with this section.

==Sec. 203. Allegations of wrongdoing==



===(a) Referral to inspector general– ===

Not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall revise the procedures of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] to ensure that any allegation of wrongdoing on the part of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] is immediately referred to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Inspector General of the Commission]] .

===(b) Supervision of inspector general– ===

During any period in which an investigation by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Inspector General]] of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] is pending with respect to an allegation described in subsection (a), the Chairman shall delegate responsibility for supervising the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Inspector General]] to a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] other than the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , consistent with the Inspector General Act of 1978(5 U.S.C. App.).

==Sec. 204. Approval of travel==



===(a) Authorization by Chairman– ===

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall authorize all international travel requested by other members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] for official business unless the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] submits a notice of disapproval to the full [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] specifying the basis for the disapproval by that is 5 days after the date on which the request is submitted to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] .

===(b) Request considered To be approved– ===

If the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] fails to submit to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] the notice of disapproval by the deadline described in subsection (a), the travel shall be considered to be approved.

==Sec. 205. Implementation==

Except as otherwise specified in this Act, not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall revise the procedures of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] to conform the procedures with this Act.
