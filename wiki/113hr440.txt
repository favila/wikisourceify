{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 18, United States Code, to extend the post-employment restrictions on lobbying by Members of Congress and officers and employees of the legislative branch.

={{anchor|H18F72A79FD624EFABCBA974790B1B33C}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Stop the Revolving Door in Washington Act”.

={{anchor|H8A707F51D1824A16A834FC0276B923A7}}Sec. 2. Extension of post-employment restrictions=



=={{anchor|H4953E78B8C7845D980315635A78C5EDF}}(a) Extension of restrictions– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 Section 207(e) of title 18, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by amending[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraph (1)] to read as follows:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|HC5457799284A47989079CEB656B639BC}}(1) Members of Congress– ====

Any person who is a Senator or a Member of the House of Representatives and who, within 5 years after that person leaves office, knowingly makes, with the intent to influence, any communication to or appearance before any Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress or any employee of any other legislative office of the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] , on behalf of any other person (except the United States) in connection with any matter on which such former Senator or Member seeks action by a Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress, in his or her official capacity, shall be punished as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/216 section 216 of this title] .;
</blockquote>

:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraphs (2), (3), (4), (5), and (6)] and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H98D8A6DCF66C4D70AA5D1D4742B6DC01}}(2) Officers of the Congress– ====

Any person who is an elected officer of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] or of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] and who, within 2 years after that person leaves office, knowingly makes, with the intent to influence, any communication to or appearance before any Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress or any employee of any other legislative office of the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] , on behalf of any other person (except the United States) in connection with any matter on which such former elected officer seeks action by a Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress, in his or her official capacity, shall be punished as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/216 section 216 of this title] .

===={{anchor|H1013B1434F104FCA8770A5BD3450336E}}(3) Employees of the Congress– ====

Any person who is an employee of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] , or an employee of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] , to whom[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraph (5)(A)] applies and who, within 2 years after the termination of that employment, knowingly makes, with the intent to influence, any communication to or appearance before any Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress or any employee of any other legislative office of the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] , on behalf of any other person (except the United States) in connection with any matter on which such former employee seeks action by a Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress, in his or her official capacity, shall be punished as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/216 section 216 of this title] .

===={{anchor|H3FBB4090D7C24DD59495A077F187EB17}}(4) Employees of other legislative offices– ====

Any person who is an employee of any other legislative office of the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] to whom[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraph (5)(B)] applies and who, within 2 years after the termination of that employment, knowingly makes, with the intent to influence, any communication to or appearance before any Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress or any employee of any other legislative office of the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] , on behalf of any other person (except the United States) in connection with any matter on which such former employee seeks action by a Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress, in his or her official capacity, shall be punished as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/216 section 216 of this title] .;
</blockquote>

:(3) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraphs (7), (8), and (9)] , as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraphs (5), (6), and (7)] , respectively;
:(4) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraph (5)] , as redesignated—
::(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 subparagraph (A)] , by striking“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraphs (2), (3), (4), and (5)] ”and inserting“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraph (3)] ”; and
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 subparagraph (B)] , by striking“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraph (6)] ”and inserting“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraph (4)] ”; and
:(5) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 paragraph (7)(G)] , as redesignated, by striking“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 (3)] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 (4)] , or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 (5)] ”and inserting“or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/207 (3)] ”.

=={{anchor|HCC5405712B1D472A881CF65CAE821BA9}}(b) Conforming amendment– ==

 Section 103(a) of the Honest Leadership and Open Government Act of 2007([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/104d 2 U.S.C. 104d(a)] )is amended by striking“ paragraph (2), (3), (4), or (5)”and inserting“ paragraph (3)”.

={{anchor|H3D76265B238B4E00A299F6788DA1D4EA}}Sec. 3. Effective date=

The amendments made by section 2shall apply to individuals who leave office or employment to which such amendments apply on or after the date of the enactment of this Act.
