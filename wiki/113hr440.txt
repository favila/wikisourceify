{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 18, United States Code, to extend the
		  post-employment restrictions on lobbying by Members of Congress and officers
		  and employees of the legislative branch.


{{section|H18F72A79FD624EFABCBA974790B1B33C|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Stop the Revolving Door in Washington
			 Act

        
.

{{section|H8A707F51D1824A16A834FC0276B923A7|2. Extension of post-employment restrictions}}
(a)
Extension of
			 restrictions

          Section 207(e)
			 of title 18, United States Code
, is amended—
(1)
by amending paragraph (1)
 to read as
			 follows:
(1)
Members of
				Congress
Any person who is a
				Senator or a Member of the House of Representatives and who, within 5 years
				after that person leaves office, knowingly makes, with the intent to influence,
				any communication to or appearance before any Member, officer, or employee of
				either House of Congress or any employee of any other legislative office of the
				[[United States Congress|Congress]], on behalf of any other person (except the United States) in
				connection with any matter on which such former Senator or Member seeks action
				by a Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress, in his or her
				official capacity, shall be punished as provided in section 216 of this
				title
.

;


(2)
by striking
			 paragraphs (2), (3), (4), (5), and (6)
 and inserting the following:
(2)
Officers of the
				Congress
Any person who is an
				elected officer of the [[United States Senate|Senate]] or of the [[United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] and who,
				within 2 years after that person leaves office, knowingly makes, with the
				intent to influence, any communication to or appearance before any Member,
				officer, or employee of either House of Congress or any employee of any other
				legislative office of the [[United States Congress|Congress]], on behalf of any other person (except the
				United States) in connection with any matter on which such former elected
				officer seeks action by a Member, officer, or employee of either House of
				Congress, in his or her official capacity, shall be punished as provided in
				section 216 of this title
.

(3)
Employees of the
				Congress
Any person who is an
				employee of the [[United States Senate|Senate]], or an employee of the [[United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]], to whom
				paragraph (5)(A)
 applies and who, within 2 years after the termination of that
				employment, knowingly makes, with the intent to influence, any communication to
				or appearance before any Member, officer, or employee of either House of
				Congress or any employee of any other legislative office of the [[United States Congress|Congress]], on
				behalf of any other person (except the United States) in connection with any
				matter on which such former employee seeks action by a Member, officer, or
				employee of either House of Congress, in his or her official capacity, shall be
				punished as provided in section 216 of this title
.

(4)
Employees of
				other legislative offices
Any
				person who is an employee of any other legislative office of the [[United States Congress|Congress]] to
				whom paragraph (5)(B)
 applies and who, within 2 years after the termination of
				that employment, knowingly makes, with the intent to influence, any
				communication to or appearance before any Member, officer, or employee of
				either House of Congress or any employee of any other legislative office of the
				[[United States Congress|Congress]], on behalf of any other person (except the United States) in
				connection with any matter on which such former employee seeks action by a
				Member, officer, or employee of either House of Congress, in his or her
				official capacity, shall be punished as provided in section 216 of this
				title
.

;


(3)
by redesignating
			 paragraphs (7), (8), and (9)

,
			 respectively;
