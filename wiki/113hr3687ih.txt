{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the National Historic Preservation Act to provide that if the head of the agency managing Federal property objects to the inclusion of certain property on the National Register or its designation as a National Historic Landmark for reasons of national security, the Federal property shall be neither included nor designated until the objection is withdrawn, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Military Land and National Defense Act” or the “Military LAND Act” .

=Sec. 2. Amendments to the National Historic Preservation Act=

 Section 101(a) of the National Historic Preservation Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/470a 16 U.S.C. 470a(a)] )is amended as follows:
:(1) In paragraph (2)—
::(A) in subparagraph (E), by striking “; and” and inserting a semicolon;
::(B) in subparagraph (F), by striking the period and inserting “; and” ; and
::(C) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:::(G) notifying the [[w:United States House Committee on Natural Resources|Committee on Natural Resources of the United States House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources|Committee on Energy and Natural Resources of the Senate]] if the property is owned by the Federal Government when the property is being considered for inclusion on the National Register, for designation as a National Historic Landmark, or for nomination to the World Heritage List..
</blockquote>

:(2) By redesignating paragraphs (7)and (8)as paragraphs (8)and (9), respectively.
:(3) By inserting after paragraph (6)the following:

<blockquote>
::(7) If the head of the agency managing any Federal property objects to such inclusion or designation for reasons of national security, such as any impact the inclusion or designation would have on use of the property for military training or readiness purposes, that Federal property shall be neither included on the National Register nor designated as a National Historic Landmark until the objection is withdrawn..
</blockquote>

:(4) By adding after paragraph (9)(as so redesignated by paragraph (2) of this section) the following:

<blockquote>
::(10) The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall promulgate regulations to allow for expedited removal of Federal property listed on the National Register of Historic Places if the managing agency of that Federal property submits to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] a written request to remove the Federal property from the National Register of Historic Places for reasons of national security, such as any impact the inclusion or designation would have on use of the property for military training or readiness purposes..
</blockquote>
