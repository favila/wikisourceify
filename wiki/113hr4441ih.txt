{{Center|A BILL}}

 To require pipelines regulated by the Secretary of Transportation to be made of steel that is produced in the United States and originates from iron ore and taconite mined and processed in the United States, for safety, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   American Pipeline Jobs and Safety Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. American steel=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102  Section 60102of title 49, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 

==(q) American steel– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this subsection, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]shall prescribe minimum safety standards requiring that any pipeline subject to this chapter that is constructed or replaced after such date of enactment be made of steel that—
:(A) is produced in the United States; and
:(B) originates from iron ore and taconite mined and processed in the United States.

===(2) Use of recycled materials– ===

For purposes of this subsection, steel that is recycled in the United States shall be considered to be steel described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 subparagraphs (A)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 (B) of paragraph (1)]if the recycled materials are combined with iron ore and taconite mined and processed in the United States.

===(3) Waivers– ===



====(A) In general– ====

In prescribing safety standards under this subsection, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]shall permit a waiver of the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 paragraph (1)]to the extent that the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]determines such a waiver is necessary based on the estimate prepared under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 subparagraph (B)].

====(B) Annual demand and supply estimate– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]shall prepare annually an estimate of—
:(i) the amount of steel likely to be needed to be produced to meet the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 paragraph (1)]; and
:(ii) the production capacity of United States producers of steel described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 subparagraphs (A)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 (B) of such paragraph].

====(C) Approval of sources– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]shall ensure that steel permitted to be used in pipelines pursuant to a waiver under this paragraph meets the same standards as steel described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 subparagraphs (A)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 (B) of paragraph (1)], and shall publish a list of approved sources of steel so permitted. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Inspection and testing=



==(a) Testing– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60108  Section 60108(b)of title 49, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
:(4) Testing under this subsection shall include direct, pre-installation testing by the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]of pipelines for quality verification. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Authorization of appropriations– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60125  Section 60125of title 49, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 

===(d) Inspection and testing– ===

 There is authorized to be appropriated $10,000,000 for each fiscal year to carry out direct, pre-installation inspection and testing of pipelines under this chapter, including testing of pipelines under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60108 section 60108(b)(4)]and testing and inspection of steel pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/60102 section 60102(q)] .  . 
</blockquote>
