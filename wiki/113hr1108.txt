{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for alternative financing arrangements for the
		  provision of certain services and the construction and maintenance of
		  infrastructure at land border ports of entry, and for other
		  purposes.

={{anchor|HFD0C0268919F4CF5889BA5A8432CB829}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Cross-Border Trade Enhancement Act of
			 2013”.

={{anchor|H9FFF995ACBDB4247BA77016BE2CA843B}}Sec. 2. Definitions=

In this Act:

=={{anchor|H9B547E266E854B41A16DCEB6C67177A5}}(1) Administrator;
			 Administration– ==

The terms Administrator and
			 Administration mean the [[Wikipedia:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] and the
			 [[Wikipedia:General Services Administration|General Services Administration]], respectively.

=={{anchor|H86927C242AF149C8948BF3824BA921D3}}(2) Person– ==

The
			 term person means—
:(A) an individual;
			 or
:(B) a corporation,
			 partnership, trust, association, or any other public or private entity,
			 including a State or local government.

=={{anchor|H583A8A29AAF24B61BE602B909CBF47BE}}(3) Secretary– ==

The
			 term Secretary means the [[Wikipedia:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]].

={{anchor|H97B607C13A5A449388E78905C023D96D}}Sec. 3. Authority to
			 enter into agreements for the provision of certain services at land border
			 ports of entry=



=={{anchor|H0E06C69B44C54AEBA5470030625D16ED}}(a) Authority To
			 enter into agreements– ==



==={{anchor|H310EAC6E74BB447288F01AD8BDB53B50}}(1) In
			 general– ===

Notwithstanding section 451 of the Tariff Act of 1930 (19
			 U.S.C. 1451), the [[Wikipedia:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] may, during the 10-year period beginning on the
			 date of the enactment of this Act and upon the request of any person, enter
			 into an agreement with that person under which—
:(A) [[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and
			 Border Protection]] will provide services described in paragraph (2) at a land
			 border port of entry; and
:(B) that person will
			 pay a fee imposed under subsection (b) to reimburse [[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border
			 Protection]] for the costs incurred in providing such services.

==={{anchor|HE4E3793C883A4D8C9E5A24FA21D555D8}}(2) Services
			 described– ===

Services described in this paragraph are any services
			 related to customs and immigration matters provided by an employee or
			 contractor of [[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] at land border ports of
			 entry.

==={{anchor|H1350D0B92508452CBBF523B298D51A90}}(3) Limitation– ===

Nothing
			 in this paragraph may be construed to reduce the responsibilities or duties of
			 [[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] to provide services at land border ports of
			 entry that have been authorized or mandated by law and are funded in any
			 appropriation Act or from any accounts in the Treasury of the United States
			 derived by the collection of fees.

=={{anchor|H924A3DC0FE7D404CA81A154FCBCC1B2D}}(b) Fee– ==



==={{anchor|H22C5C5C4BAF14938866617BBB2B57186}}(1) In
			 general– ===

The [[Wikipedia:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall impose a fee on a person requesting
			 the provision of services by [[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] pursuant to an
			 agreement entered into under subsection (a) to reimburse [[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and
			 Border Protection]] for the costs of providing such services, including—
:(A) the salaries and
			 expenses of the employees or contractors of [[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]]
			 that provide such services and temporary placement or relocation costs for
			 those employees or contractors; and
:(B) any other costs
			 incurred by [[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] in providing services pursuant
			 to agreements entered into under subsection (a).

==={{anchor|HD5EB7F25AABA4E03A33C78DB59637736}}(2) Failure to pay
			 fee– ===

[[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] shall terminate the
			 provision of services pursuant to an agreement entered into under subsection
			 (a) with a person that, after receiving notice from the [[Wikipedia:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] that a fee
			 imposed under paragraph (1) is due, fails to pay the fee in a timely
			 manner.

==={{anchor|H7CEED91DE1A64879819921003BBF007E}}(3) Receipts
			 credited as offsetting collections– ===

Notwithstanding section 3302
			 of title 31, United States Code, a fee collected under paragraph (1) pursuant
			 to an agreement entered into under subsection (a) shall—
:(A) be credited as an
			 offsetting collection to the account that finances the salaries and expenses of
			 [[Wikipedia:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]];
:(B) be available for
			 expenditure only to pay the costs of providing services pursuant to that
			 agreement; and
:(C) remain available
			 until expended without fiscal year limitation.

={{anchor|HFD6B5BC8B836470BA3ED8EF4A0025453}}Sec. 4. Evaluation of
			 alternative financing arrangements for construction and maintenance of
			 infrastructure at land border ports of entry=



=={{anchor|HF05A505B46854E4EB281C41F94BB896E}}(a) In
			 general– ==

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment
			 of this Act, the [[Wikipedia:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall establish procedures for evaluating a
			 proposal submitted by any person to—
:(1) enter into a
			 cost-sharing or reimbursement agreement with the [[Wikipedia:General Services Administration|Administration]] to facilitate
			 the construction or maintenance of a facility or other infrastructure at a land
			 border port of entry; or
:(2) provide to the
			 [[Wikipedia:General Services Administration|Administration]] an unconditional gift of property pursuant to section 3175 of
			 title 40, United States Code, to be used in the construction or maintenance of
			 a facility or other infrastructure at a land border port of entry.

=={{anchor|H4DA0330ACFF34B37A69C6856F1984168}}(b) Requirements– ==

The
			 procedures established under subsection (a) shall provide, at a minimum, for
			 the following:
:(1) Not later than 90
			 days after receiving a proposal pursuant to subsection (a) with respect to the
			 construction or maintenance of a facility or other infrastructure at a land
			 border port of entry, the [[Wikipedia:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall—
::(A) make a
			 determination with respect to whether or not to approve the proposal;
			 and
::(B) notify the person
			 that submitted the proposal of—
:::(i) the
			 determination; and
:::(ii) if
			 the [[Wikipedia:General Services Administration|Administrator]] did not approve the proposal, the reasons for the
			 determination.
:(2) In determining
			 whether or not to approve such a proposal, the [[Wikipedia:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall
			 consider—
::(A) the impact of the
			 proposal on reducing wait times at that port of entry and other ports of entry
			 on the same border;
::(B) the potential of
			 the proposal to increase trade and travel efficiency through added capacity;
			 and
::(C) the potential of
			 the proposal to enhance the security of the port of entry.
