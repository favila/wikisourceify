{{Center|A BILL}}

To require the Secretary of Defense to develop and implement a plan to provide chiropractic health care services and benefits for certain new beneficiaries as part of the TRICARE program.

={{anchor|H9C7666A5CDEC476C8BE63930A145D780}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Chiropractic Health Parity for Military Beneficiaries Act”.

={{anchor|H567C9D9B17924F7396C59C59BFC0BD3B}}Sec. 2. Chiropractic health care for retirees, dependents, and survivors=



=={{anchor|H939A47EB29DB42E0ACF47CEC3B78D643}}(a) Plan required– ==

Not later than August 31, 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] shall complete development of a plan to provide chiropractic health care services and benefits, as a permanent part of the TRICARE program, for covered beneficiaries.

=={{anchor|H4113336B2512455FB70172CAE0BBCED3}}(b) Contents of plan– ==

The plan shall require that a contract entered into under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1097 section 1097 of title 10, United States Code] , for the delivery of health care services shall—
:(1) include the delivery of chiropractic services;
:(2) require that chiropractic services may be provided only by a doctor of chiropractic; and
:(3) provide that a covered beneficiary may select and have direct access to a doctor of chiropractic without referral by another health practitioner.

=={{anchor|H14FBC5AF16EC4D1E8ADD8C0B04C768CD}}(c) Implementation of plan– ==

The plan developed under subsection (a)shall provide for implementation of the plan to begin no later than January 31, 2014.

={{anchor|HCBEBC03B9AA64837AFC16173C09B72FC}}Sec. 3. Definitions=

In this Act:
:(1) The term chiropractic services —
::(A) includes diagnosis (including by diagnostic x-ray tests), evaluation and management, and therapeutic services for the treatment of a patient's health condition, including neuromusculoskeletal conditions and the subluxation complex, and such other services determined appropriate by the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] and as authorized under State law; and
::(B) does not include the use of drugs or surgery.
:(2) The term covered beneficiary has the meaning provided by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 section 1072(5) of title 10, United States Code] .
:(3) The term doctor of chiropractic means only a doctor of chiropractic who is licensed as a doctor of chiropractic, chiropractic physician, or chiropractor by a State, the District of Columbia, or a territory or possession of the United States.
:(4) The term TRICARE program has the meaning provided by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 section 1072(7) of title 10, United States Code] .
