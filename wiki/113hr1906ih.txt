{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend titles 23 and 49, United States Code, to modify provisions relating to the length and weight limitations for vehicles operating on Federal-aid highways, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HC9339893B7BF4ACF840D491975FC86B6}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H53C92A9461294D77B0939245838A25B5}}Sec. 2. Operation of restricted property-carrying units on National Highway System=



=={{anchor|HE144CE3FCC9A457280621E8010F823BA}}(a) Definition of restricted property-Carrying unit– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 31111(a)] of title 49, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 paragraph (3)] as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 paragraph (5)] ;
:(2) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 paragraph (2)] as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 paragraph (3)] ;
:(3) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 paragraph (4)] as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 paragraph (2)] ; and
:(4) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 paragraph (3)] the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H51CA9D95FBC6429AAA7BCEDAD5974A7A}}(4) Restricted property-carrying unit– ====

The term restricted property-carrying unit means any trailer, semitrailer, container, or other property-carrying unit that is longer than 53 feet..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HF98BD95FE32E4D5FA4D824630BDE456A}}(b) Prohibition on operation of restricted property-Carrying units– ==



==={{anchor|HD91ED0085FA142DC8DFD04F0CE5517E5}}(1) In general– ===

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 31111(b)(1)(C)] of title 49, United States Code] , is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>
:(C) allows operation on any segment of the National Highway System, including the Interstate System, of a restricted property-carrying unit unless the operation is specified on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 subsection (h)(2)] ;.
</blockquote>


==={{anchor|H6D743F7F1DF34C46B7BC9F98A4D1A1EC}}(2) Effective date– ===

The amendment made by paragraph (1)shall take effect on the date that is 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act.

=={{anchor|H2AA459768EA34C799DBFD793BC6DFE93}}(c) Limitations– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 Section 31111 of title 49, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|HA6AE253854AE4A21B7269594338F669A}}(h) Restricted property-Carrying units– ===



===={{anchor|H4BE89494EFC044218377E70C5975D9D8}}(1) Applicability of prohibition– ====



====={{anchor|H15C7FB7F215C42F4B7B77A766E0B870F}}(A) In general– =====

Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 subsection (b)(1)(C)] , a restricted property-carrying unit may continue to operate on a segment of the National Highway System if the operation of the unit is specified on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 paragraph (2)] .

====={{anchor|H5DF5F67C075E47918461E6849C93C42E}}(B) Applicability of State laws and regulations– =====

All operations specified on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 paragraph (2)] shall continue to be subject to all State laws, limitations, and conditions, including routing-specific, commodity-specific, and configuration-specific designations and all other restrictions, in effect as of June 1, 2008.

====={{anchor|H33AA37041C6E449F8CED0B06A5969083}}(C) Firefighting units– =====

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 Subsection (b)(1)(C)] shall not apply to the operation of a restricted property-carrying unit that is used exclusively for firefighting.

===={{anchor|H5FC9291BBE4842C3A20AE2FAF98A457C}}(2) Listing of restricted property-carrying units– ====



====={{anchor|H10DCA7D05568484588A348DDA6066507}}(A) In general– =====

Not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall initiate a proceeding to determine and publish a list of restricted property-carrying units that were—
:(i) authorized by State officials pursuant to State law as of June 1, 2008; and
:(ii) in actual and lawful operation on a regular or periodic basis (including seasonal operations) on or before June 1, 2008.

====={{anchor|H6DD6284E68DC40738873E9B387E9D79A}}(B) Limitation– =====

A restricted property-carrying unit may not be included on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 subparagraph (A)] on the basis that a State law could have authorized the operation of the unit as of a prior date, by permit or otherwise.

====={{anchor|HD46A6C1551AC4A64B8B443BA48774370}}(C) Publication of final list– =====

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall publish a final list of restricted property-carrying units described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 subparagraph (A)] .

====={{anchor|HEC2063250F8E4991B5ABC3B41C28C4CC}}(D) Updates– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall update the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 subparagraph (C)] as necessary to reflect new designations made to the National Highway System.

===={{anchor|H17ED62E198EC45B9A8C6E9DA7B4DB548}}(3) Applicability of prohibition– ====

The prohibition established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 subsection (b)(1)(C)] shall—
:(A) apply to any new designation made to the National Highway System; and
:(B) remain in effect on those portions of the National Highway System that cease to be designated as part of the National Highway System.

===={{anchor|HE1FDC5B69D97409B827DF2A186ECD706}}(4) Limitation on statutory construction– ====

Nothing in this subsection may be construed to prevent a State from further restricting in any manner or prohibiting the operation of a restricted property-carrying unit, except that any such restriction or prohibition shall be consistent with the provisions of this subchapter..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H35DF4D7F62E74D83BF34A84D3BC6DE1E}}(d) Enforcement– ==

The second sentence of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/141 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 141(a)] of title 23, United States Code] , is amended by striking“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 section 31112] ”and inserting“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31111 sections 31111] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 31112] ”.

={{anchor|H0AA1406E881D44C7AD2034BC82DF2050}}Sec. 3. Operation of longer combination vehicles on National Highway System=



=={{anchor|H650DFC65B0B14299AE00DEB3AB2764CF}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 31112] of title 49, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subsections (f) and (g)] as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subsections (g) and (h)] , respectively; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subsection (e)] the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H5CDD9D43BBEF4C618C19A96C36725D19}}(f) National Highway System– ====



====={{anchor|H1A8ED44EBA7A46CCBA7B95BD659D11E6}}(1) In general– =====

A State may not allow, on a segment of the National Highway System that is not covered under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subsection (b)] or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 (c)] , the operation of a commercial motor vehicle combination (except a vehicle or load that cannot be dismantled easily or divided easily and that has been issued a special permit under applicable State law) with more than 1 property-carrying unit (excluding the truck tractor) if the property-carrying units are more than—
:(A) the maximum combination trailer, semitrailer, or other type of length limitation allowed by applicable State law as of June 1, 2008; or
:(B) the length of the property-carrying units of those commercial motor vehicle combinations, by specific configuration, in actual and lawful operation on a regular or periodic basis (including continuing seasonal operation) in that State on or before June 1, 2008.

====={{anchor|H92141AF54AEE4A70B8B59CCFBF29FF85}}(2) Additional limitations– =====



======{{anchor|H1C7C30F51AE748499719371FF8BD2B54}}(A) Applicability of State restrictions– ======



======={{anchor|H1694498DDB1440EB95D1EDB7C602A479}}(i) In general– =======

Subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 clause (ii)] , a commercial motor vehicle combination that is not prohibited from operating in a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 paragraph (1)] may continue to operate in that State on highways described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 paragraph (1)] if such operation complies with all applicable State laws, limitations, and conditions, including routing-specific and configuration-specific designations, and all other restrictions in effect in that State as of June 1, 2008.

======={{anchor|H5973876A92464AA682BB43CD52CEECB2}}(ii) Minor adjustments– =======

Subject to regulations promulgated by the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subsection (h)] , a State may make minor temporary and emergency adjustments to route designations and vehicle operating restrictions in effect as of June 1, 2008, for specific safety purposes and road construction.

======{{anchor|H805FD6E09EAB44989DA9BFD923CA801D}}(B) Additional state restrictions– ======

Nothing in this subsection may be construed to prevent a State from further restricting or prohibiting the operation of a commercial motor vehicle combination subject to this section, except that such a restriction or prohibition shall be consistent with this section and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31113 sections 31113(a)] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31113 31113(b)] , and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31114 31114] .

======{{anchor|HC2CA5223FD69453EB95506714D69FDD3}}(C) Minor adjustments– ======



======={{anchor|HFD7834D15B29469EA1B1D7C535F7F221}}(i) In general– =======

Not later than 30 days after the date on which a State makes a minor adjustment of a temporary and emergency nature in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subparagraph (A)(ii)] or further restricting or prohibiting the operation of a commercial motor vehicle combination in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subparagraph (B)] , the State shall advise the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] of the action.

======={{anchor|H853FAFF0013847238B7F1AA309CC4740}}(ii) Publication– =======

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall publish a notice of an action taken by a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 clause (i)] in the Federal Register.

====={{anchor|H02863BC7BDCA4D92A9BC0A559954013C}}(3) List of state length limitations– =====



======{{anchor|H76CABCF31F1A4567BCDA51E8C6312CE6}}(A) State submissions– ======



======={{anchor|H7185715F5D2B4E5CBE607CC814D3DD00}}(i) In general– =======

Not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, each State shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] a complete list of State length limitations applicable to commercial motor vehicle combinations operating in the State on the highways described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 paragraph (1)] , including the applicable State laws associated with the length limitations.

======={{anchor|H415BE7C65AA94D89B40657C3CCFF8D37}}(ii) Failure to submit– =======

If a State fails to submit the information required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 clause (i)] , the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall compile and file the information on behalf of the State.

======{{anchor|H7A9DA1522676440F871E1766B3326935}}(B) Publication of interim list– ======

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall—
:(i) publish an interim list in the Federal Register consisting of all information submitted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subparagraph (A)] not later than 60 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013;
:(ii) review for accuracy all information submitted by a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subparagraph (A)] ; and
:(iii) solicit and consider public comment on the accuracy of the information.

======{{anchor|HDB579EFBFE0D4ECA93BAE3B74334D0B5}}(C) Limitation– ======

A law may not be included on the list submitted by a State or published by the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] merely because the law authorized, or could have authorized, by permit or otherwise, the operation of commercial motor vehicle combinations not in actual operation on a regular or periodic basis on or before June 1, 2008.

======{{anchor|HFCA5F81A4F894611AF41619699F6B18B}}(D) Publication of final list– ======



======={{anchor|H1140786513564D3EA4DFFF20F999B105}}(i) In general– =======

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall publish a final version of the list described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subparagraph (B)] in the Federal Register, as revised under this subparagraph or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subparagraph (E)] .

======={{anchor|HD43080D60A11479D8DBA644FB2537A0B}}(ii) Revisions– =======

In publishing the final list, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall make any revisions necessary to correct inaccuracies identified under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subparagraph (B)] .

======={{anchor|HF6AA852529BA449C96A0DFBB02F54ED6}}(iii) Prohibition on operation– =======

After the final list is published under this subparagraph, commercial motor vehicle combinations prohibited under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 paragraph (1)] may not operate on a highway described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 paragraph (1)] unless included on the list.

======{{anchor|H5F6B8B0F89704A61B0ACC48E1EB79F1C}}(E) Inaccuracies– ======



======={{anchor|H3BB1C1A927ED48ECAFCF79E61B84B93C}}(i) In general– =======

On the motion of the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] or on request by any person (including a State), the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall review the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subparagraph (D)] .

======={{anchor|H9D5DF221A05B47F3B618526FEF78185E}}(ii) Determination– =======

If the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] determines that there is reason to believe a mistake was made in the accuracy of the list—
:(I) the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall begin a proceeding to determine whether a mistake was made; and
:(II) if the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] makes an affirmative determination under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112 subclause (I)] , the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall publish the appropriate correction..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|H55C408E78779445CB56552B9EE61E218}}Sec. 4. Termination of determinations of grandfathered rights=



=={{anchor|H4B7A3733C1634768A58BA3D0E07AD8FE}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 127] of title 23, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|HF5DDBF2B917D41D0AB4283F4D7347755}}(i) Grandfathered rights– ===


:(1) Definition of Interstate weight limit– In this subsection and in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A section 127A] , the term Interstate weight limit means the 80,000-pound gross vehicle weight limitation, the 20,000-pound single axle weight limitation (including enforcement tolerances), the 34,000-pound tandem axle weight limitation (including enforcement tolerances), and the overall maximum gross weight (including enforcement tolerances) limitation on a group of 2 or more consecutive axles produced by application of the formula under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 subsection (a)(2)] .

===={{anchor|HDD6196353DA740BD816AECDFAF24168D}}(2) Limitation– ====

Beginning 90 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, a State may not allow, on a segment of the Interstate System, the operation of a vehicle or combination (other than a longer combination vehicle) exceeding an Interstate weight limit unless the operation is specified on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 paragraph (3)] .

===={{anchor|HFF7732C316C144C4B9EAF869556CA335}}(3) List of vehicles and combinations– ====



====={{anchor|HADC00D83074A47E7AD9217E10A1EEFCE}}(A) Proceeding– =====

Not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of this subsection, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall initiate a proceeding to determine and publish a list of vehicles and combinations (other than longer combination vehicles) otherwise exceeding an Interstate weight limit that the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Department of Transportation]] , any other Federal agency, or a State has determined, on or before June 1, 2008, could have been or could be lawfully operated within the State—
:(i) on July 1, 1956;
:(ii) in the case of the overall gross weight of any group of 2 or more consecutive axles, on January 4, 1975; or
:(iii) under a special rule applicable to a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 subsection (a)] .

====={{anchor|HE71F405D138F4D0586BF321F5956AABF}}(B) Limitations– =====



======{{anchor|H94FEB52E8EE34EC1B61056E11AA6557A}}(i) Actual and lawful operations required– ======

An operation of a vehicle or combination may be included on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 subparagraph (A)] only if the vehicle or combination was in actual and lawful operation in the State on a regular or periodic basis on or before June 1, 2008.

======{{anchor|H9237A5854DA0476CB5777DD3FC67DE6E}}(ii) State authority not sufficient– ======

An operation of a vehicle or combination may not be included on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 subparagraph (A)] on the basis that a State law could have authorized the operation of the vehicle or combination as of a prior date by permit or otherwise.

====={{anchor|H5879F2F7F5384401A92519A9B33BFD50}}(C) Publication of final list– =====

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of this subsection, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall publish a final list of vehicles and combinations described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 subparagraph (A)] .

===={{anchor|H039EAC93D5BC4277B0A6AE9B7E60ED0B}}(4) Limitation on effect of subsection– ====



====={{anchor|H233CEB671728474A87E4EA18ED957EE0}}(A) In general– =====

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 subparagraph (B)] , nothing in this subsection may be construed to prevent a State from reducing the gross vehicle weight limitation, the single and tandem axle weight limitations, or the overall maximum gross weight on a group of 2 or more consecutive axles applicable to portions of the Interstate System in the State for operations on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 paragraph (3)(C)] .

====={{anchor|H7F5B4988FE06409FB160E93521CFDF5C}}(B) Limitation– =====

A reduction described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 subparagraph (A)] may not result in a limitation that is less than an Interstate weight limit.

===={{anchor|HE59CAA5AF6F549A7ADF76F90D2D33887}}(5) Applicability of existing requirements– ====

All vehicles and combinations included on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 paragraph (3)] shall be subject to all routing-specific, commodity-specific, and weight-specific designations in effect in a State as of June 1, 2008..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|HCE56BAA796D340EE8A64B6391DD1118E}}Sec. 5. Nondivisible load proceeding=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 Section 127 of title 23, United States Code] , as amended by section 4, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H3146C90E850A4C6C9840B58E08E11E4D}}(j) Nondivisible loads– ==


:(1) Statement of policy– The purpose of this subsection is to promote conformity with Interstate weight limits to preserve publicly funded infrastructure and protect motorists by limiting maximum vehicle weight on key portions of the Federal-aid highway system.

==={{anchor|H8DCA4AC85B9C41DCB64C626662CF5C42}}(2) Proceeding– ===

Not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall initiate a proceeding to define the term vehicles and loads that cannot be easily dismantled or divided as used in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/a subsection (a) and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 31112] of title 49] .

==={{anchor|H8352FF07FAB44EA48EACE5B9B340BC11}}(3) List of commodities– ===



===={{anchor|H79E74A18295446C79F4FC533171E6400}}(A) In general– ====

The definition developed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 subparagraph (A)] shall include a list of commodities (or classes or types of commodities) that do not qualify as nondivisible loads.

===={{anchor|H53C691B25BE945CB86FF11D19E16753C}}(B) Limitation– ====

The list of commodities developed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 paragraph (2)] shall not be interpreted to be a comprehensive list of commodities that do not qualify as nondivisible loads.

==={{anchor|H6ABE0CCB28B640EFA82DB6449F9E4001}}(4) Regulations– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall—
:(A) promulgate final regulations setting forth the determination of the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 paragraph (2)] not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013; and
:(B) update such regulations, as necessary.

==={{anchor|HF4D1542F1CA54455AA86BE0380B0B49E}}(5) Applicability– ===

Regulations promulgated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 paragraph (4)] shall apply to all vehicles and loads operating on the National Highway System.

==={{anchor|H8A0B96E95D6945889114E3DEE0378B26}}(6) State requirements– ===

A State may establish any requirement that is not inconsistent with regulations promulgated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 paragraph (4)] ..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|HB6BC2544F6F6400BBD644878C4DE516C}}Sec. 6. Waivers of weight limitations during periods of national emergency=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 127] of title 23, United States Code] , as amended by sections 4and 5, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HFE98B6E155824CCD81E611B79A703C0E}}(k) Waivers during periods of national emergency– ==



==={{anchor|H05180F5CA0BC47B2A3AFAE2C3827053F}}(1) In general– ===

Notwithstanding any other provision of this section or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A section 127A] , the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] , in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] , may waive or limit the application of any vehicle weight limit established under this section or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A section 127A] with respect to a highway route during a period of national emergency in order to respond to the effects of the national emergency.

==={{anchor|H3F93B3FDC2EE4E70A7B68F4860C013FC}}(2) Applicability– ===

Emergency limits established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 paragraph (1)] shall preempt any inconsistent State vehicle weight limits..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H27C1908AB4E9411BB3134E4ECB81D49B}}Sec. 7. Vehicle weight limitations—National Highway System=



=={{anchor|HDBF770B466C44D739AED6748E0C0CFCC}}(a) In general– ==

Chapter 1of title 23, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 section 127(d)(4)] , by inserting“and in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A section 127A] ”after“this section”; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 section 127] the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H4CF0688AED004B9F8B118796595A87FC}}Sec. 127A. Vehicle weight limitations—National Highway System===



===={{anchor|HB83C761C9676481F8A6D29C7E53FC47B}}(a) Non-Interstate highways on National Highway System– ====



====={{anchor|H5FBA822220454F7CA60D121A409DE5B5}}(1) In general– =====

Beginning 90 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, any Interstate weight limit that applies to vehicles and combinations (other than longer combination vehicles) operating on the Interstate System in a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 section 127] shall apply to vehicles and combinations (other than longer combination vehicles) operating on non-Interstate segments of the National Highway System in the State unless the segments are subject to lower State weight limits as provided for under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subsection (d)] .

====={{anchor|H35FD737E905349718892BEBB8AE57E06}}(2) Existing highways– =====



======{{anchor|HA77F2196BBA94061AF361F2A345B6C74}}(A) In general– ======

Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A paragraph (1)] , in the case of a non-Interstate segment of the National Highway System that is open to traffic on June 1, 2008, a State may allow the operation of any vehicle or combination (other than a longer combination vehicle) on the segment that the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] determines under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subsection (b)] could have been lawfully operated on the segment on June 1, 2008.

======{{anchor|H4CF1EC6EE0F349C4ACCFFD5D0CDD6F14}}(B) Applicability of state laws– ======

All operations described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subparagraph (A)] shall continue to be subject to all State laws, limitations, and conditions, including routing-specific, commodity-specific, and configuration-specific designations and all other restrictions, in effect as of June 1, 2008.

====={{anchor|H2C6AA05F108A4D3DA60107EF3D4B263F}}(3) New highways– =====

Subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subsection (d)(1)] , the gross vehicle weight limitations and axle loading limitations applicable to all vehicles and combinations (other than longer combination vehicles) on a non-Interstate segment of the National Highway System that was not open to traffic on June 1, 2008, shall be the Interstate weight limit.

===={{anchor|HC319B68809E7437899F4A927EC32FEFD}}(b) Listing of vehicles and combinations– ====



====={{anchor|H9CAC853B352C454D8338560BBF8F4BBA}}(1) In general– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall initiate a proceeding to determine and publish a list of vehicles and combinations (other than longer combination vehicles), otherwise exceeding an Interstate weight limit, that could be lawfully operated on a non-Interstate segment of the National Highway System on June 1, 2008.

====={{anchor|H1F812065B2CA4BCFA389F89C49ED2F57}}(2) Requirements– =====

In publishing a list of vehicles and combinations under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A paragraph (1)] , the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall identify—
:(A) the gross vehicle weight limitations and axle loading limitations in each State applicable, as of June 1, 2008, to vehicles and combinations (other than longer combination vehicles) on non-Interstate segments of the National Highway System; and
:(B) operations of vehicles and combinations (other than longer combination vehicles), exceeding State gross vehicle weight limitations and axle loading limitations identified under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subparagraph (A)] , that were in actual and lawful operation on a regular or periodic basis (including seasonal operations) as of June 1, 2008.

====={{anchor|H93323FF870A74A7F9D50837A473BA546}}(3) Limitation– =====

An operation of a vehicle or combination may not be included on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A paragraph (1)] on the basis that a State law (including a regulation) could have authorized the operation on a prior date by permit or otherwise.

====={{anchor|H44C2BC80783441D4B670A8D28D6C596A}}(4) Publication of final list– =====

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall publish a final list of vehicles and combinations described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A paragraph (1)] .

====={{anchor|HB0A49384A4D74B6AAF4F202F53E1EE2A}}(5) Updates– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall update the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A paragraph (4)] as necessary to reflect new designations made to the National Highway System.

===={{anchor|H48B199AD424546519719E2830448EECF}}(c) Applicability of limitations– ====

The limitations established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subsection (a)] shall—
:(1) apply to any new designation made to the National Highway System; and
:(2) remain in effect on those non-Interstate highways that cease to be designated as part of the National Highway System.

===={{anchor|HABEAF7303FD94E7E9BD6C11321418292}}(d) Limitations on statutory construction– ====



====={{anchor|HDDA6E55343C040F7A7457EE48D551982}}(1) State enforcement of more restrictive weight limits– =====

Nothing in this section may be construed to prevent a State from maintaining or imposing a weight limitation that is more restrictive than the Interstate weight limit on vehicles or combinations (other than longer combination vehicles) operating on a non-Interstate segment of the National Highway System.

====={{anchor|HB90425C2FC0F4DA4A1AC4BE3958F6520}}(2) State actions to reduce weight limits– =====

Nothing in this section may be construed to prevent a State from reducing the gross vehicle weight limitation, single or tandem axle weight limitations, or the overall maximum gross weight on two or more consecutive axles of the State on any non-Interstate segment of the National Highway System.

===={{anchor|H33917B1014AA4B119516CBDFFA8D2E1D}}(e) Longer combination vehicles– ====



====={{anchor|H3E3D41347546495DBF64405BA450506D}}(1) Prohibition– =====



======{{anchor|H3A66E72B13944FA49FF6170E8B221F35}}(A) In general– ======

Beginning 90 days after the date of the enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, a longer combination vehicle may continue to operate on a non-Interstate segment of the National Highway System only if the operation of the longer combination vehicle configuration type was—
:(i) authorized by State officials pursuant to State law (including a regulation) as of June 1, 2008; and
:(ii) in actual and lawful operation on a regular or periodic basis (including seasonal operations) on or before June 1, 2008.

======{{anchor|H59747D99C2B242BBA8053E0E2BA14324}}(B) Applicability of state laws– ======

All operations described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subparagraph (A)] shall continue to be subject to all State laws, limitations, and conditions, including routing-specific, commodity-specific, and configuration-specific designations and all other restrictions, in effect as of June 1, 2008.

====={{anchor|HE2084B1B050E49FE922329B0ED220F8C}}(2) Listing of vehicles and combinations– =====



======{{anchor|H139342AF86CC45039A053F5060EB2186}}(A) In general– ======

Not later than 30 days after the date of enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall initiate a proceeding to determine and publish a list of longer combination vehicles that could be lawfully operated on non-Interstate segments of the National Highway System as of June 1, 2008.

======{{anchor|H3395C45B6020440388EF4FA30E2A91FA}}(B) Limitation– ======

A longer combination vehicle may not be included on the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subparagraph (A)] on the basis that a State law (including a regulation) could have authorized the operation of the vehicle on a prior date by permit or otherwise.

======{{anchor|HFECA52A466F44F06AD1F2D1131E87895}}(C) Publication of final list– ======

Not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of the Safe Highways and Infrastructure Preservation Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall publish a final list of longer combination vehicles described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subparagraph (A)] .

======{{anchor|H7CA964FAF3F442DBBC395B3D8D49D39E}}(D) Updates– ======

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall update the list published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127A subparagraph (C)] as necessary to reflect new designations made to the National Highway System.

====={{anchor|H9196AF8C104A41B38962FCADEB5EC794}}(3) Limitation on statutory construction– =====

Nothing in this subsection may be construed to prevent a State from further restricting in any manner or prohibiting the operation of a longer combination vehicle, except that any such restriction or prohibition shall be consistent with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/127 section 127 of this title] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31112..31114 sections 31112 through 31114 of title 49] .

===={{anchor|H36F4709DB90345EE8C18DD806AA6A571}}(f) Model schedule of fines– ====



====={{anchor|HA9A0429E8A3949F99C9DE0FFDEB7F2AA}}(1) In general– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] , in consultation with the States, shall establish a model schedule of fines to be assessed for violations of this section.

====={{anchor|H000C1CFC8DC0452DB2BA30B60C6C13EE}}(2) Purposes– =====

The purposes of the schedule of fines shall be—
:(A) to ensure that fines are sufficient to deter violations of this section; and
:(B) to permit States to recover costs associated with damage caused to the National Highway System by the operation of vehicles.

====={{anchor|H77C541B69AFA4B4494E9AB9E7F644EBA}}(3) Adoption by states– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall encourage States to adopt the schedule of fines..
</blockquote>
