{{Center|A BILL}}

To amendchapter 77of title 5, United States Code, to clarify certain due process rights of Federal employees serving in sensitive positions, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Amendments=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7701 Section 7701of title 5, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7701 subsection (k)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7701 subsection (l)] ; and
:(2) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7701 subsection (j)] the following:

<blockquote>
::(k) 
:::(1) The [[w:Merit Systems Protection Board|Board]] has authority to review on the merits an appeal by an employee or applicant for employment of an action arising from a determination that the employee or applicant for employment is ineligible for a sensitive position if—
::::(A) the sensitive position does not require a security clearance or access to classified information; and
::::(B) such action is otherwise appealable.
:::(2) In this subsection, the term sensitive position means a position designated as a sensitive position under Executive Order 10450 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7311 5 U.S.C. 7311note] ), or any successor thereto..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 2. Effective date=

The amendments made by this Act shall apply to any appeal that is pending on, or commenced on or after, the date of enactment of this Act.
