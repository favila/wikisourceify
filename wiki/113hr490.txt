{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend section 217 of the Immigration and Nationality Act to modify the visa waiver program, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HF46F0BA6215D4D2D93EC5EA5A78BAC4F}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Visa Waiver Program Enhanced Security and Reform Act”.

={{anchor|H6928F21A093149ED9855B40D904B27A7}}Sec. 2. Visa waiver program enhanced security and reform=



=={{anchor|H29CA76D19DDA44B8BDA71FF70A6D2375}}(a) Definitions– ==

 Section 217(c)(1) of the Immigration and Nationality Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 8 U.S.C. 1187(c)(1)] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H9DF42A2D93B546F581D299F764567598}}(1) Authority to designate; definitions– ===



===={{anchor|H95D4CEDFF2C84D0CAB04FA0F8386CE62}}(A) Authority to designate– ====

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] , in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]] , may designate any country as a program country if that country meets the requirements under paragraph (2).

===={{anchor|HCE0369D730894BA597839950AE9D3223}}(B) Definitions– ====

In this subsection:

====={{anchor|H6FFDA12EE89E431AAC64FAE58AF477FB}}(i) Appropriate congressional committees– =====

The term appropriate congressional committees means—
:(I) theCommittee on Foreign Relations, theCommittee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs, and theCommittee on the Judiciary of the Senate; and
:(II) theCommittee on Foreign Affairs, theCommittee on Homeland Security, and theCommittee on the Judiciary of the House of Representatives.

====={{anchor|HE758A751F70B4CC29C9C978DC25040A4}}(ii) Overstay rate– =====



======{{anchor|H72B80D9491B14330A67F2F21F796036C}}(I) Initial designation– ======

The term overstay rate means, with respect to a country being considered for designation in the program, the ratio of—
:(aa) the number of nationals of that country who were admitted to the United States on the basis of a nonimmigrant visa under section 101(a)(15)(B)whose periods of authorized stay ended during a fiscal year but who remained unlawfully in the United States beyond such periods; to
:(bb) the number of nationals of that country who were admitted to the United States on the basis of a nonimmigrant visa under section 101(a)(15)(B)whose periods of authorized stay ended during that fiscal year.

======{{anchor|HAF89523303804994BE82AEA0B2F78E55}}(II) Continuing designation– ======

The term overstay rate means, for each fiscal year after initial designation under this section with respect to a country, the ratio of—
:(aa) the number of nationals of that country who were admitted to the United States under this section or on the basis of a nonimmigrant visa under section 101(a)(15)(B)whose periods of authorized stay ended during a fiscal year but who remained unlawfully in the United States beyond such periods; to
:(bb) the number of nationals of that country who were admitted to the United States under this section or on the basis of a nonimmigrant visa under section 101(a)(15)(B)whose periods of authorized stay ended during that fiscal year.

======{{anchor|HD789F721EBD44CF8AA785B33A40DAD69}}(III) Computation of overstay rate– ======

In determining the overstay rate for a country, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] may utilize information from any available databases to ensure the accuracy of such rate.

====={{anchor|H918F42CC6365477FAFFDD1F9186F2EC0}}(iii) Program country– =====

The term program country means a country designated as a program country under subparagraph (A)..
</blockquote>

=={{anchor|H81485C49B02548FD98B1015AFCE53A98}}(c) Designation of program countries based on overstay rates– ==



==={{anchor|H096E23D8C1C24E7689322FCA3F9F5431}}(1) In general– ===

 Section 217(c)(2)(A) of the Immigration and Nationality Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 8 U.S.C. 1187(c)(2)(A)] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|HE9A9821566F0440F98929BA0BF518610}}(A) General numerical limitations– ====



====={{anchor|H9B2EBC1F2C404B419328621A2027B3A7}}(i) Low nonimmigrant visa refusal rate– =====

The percentage of nationals of that country refused nonimmigrant visas under section 101(a)(15)(B)during the previous full fiscal year was not more than 3 percent of the total number of nationals of that country who were granted or refused nonimmigrant visas under such section during such year.

====={{anchor|HB4B9D687235E4E7195F9365BB84CD6AE}}(ii) Low nonimmigrant overstay rate– =====

The overstay rate for that country was not more than 3 percent during the previous fiscal year..
</blockquote>


==={{anchor|HDC4404D7F5CE42F5AD31DAF132FB6ABD}}(2) Qualification criteria– ===

===={{anchor|HA61D894CEE23401791D79B633876BC7D}}(3) Qualification criteria– ====

==={{anchor|HF4EA16BDE6594406B20ACA33800E3856}}(5) Computation of visa refusal rates; judicial review– ===

 Section 217(c)(6) of such Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 8 U.S.C. 1187(c)(6)] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|HAA840B0DFA5842AD9E9CBFBDA72BF8FB}}(6) Computation of visa refusal rates and judicial review– ====



====={{anchor|H1BEA7F6E6B2C421BAA2F6C35B0C2D027}}(A) Computation of visa refusal rates– =====

For purposes of determining the eligibility of a country to be designated as a program country, the calculation of visa refusal rates shall not include any visa refusals which incorporate any procedures based on, or are otherwise based on, race, sex, or disability, unless otherwise specifically authorized by law or regulation.

====={{anchor|HEC7F2DFA75384D8594BDC84FC8509E4D}}(B) Judicial review– =====

No court shall have jurisdiction under this section to review any visa refusal, the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State’s]] computation of a visa refusal rate, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security’s]] computation of an overstay rate, or the designation or nondesignation of a country as a program country..
</blockquote>

:(A) by striking subparagraphs (B)through (D); and
:(B) by striking“waiver information.—”and all that follows through“In refusing”and inserting“waiver information.—In refusing”.

==={{anchor|HF42AADD319264324B92B106B5FAC37CA}}(7) Waiver authority– ===

 Section 217(c)(8) of such Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 8 U.S.C. 1187(c)(8)] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H529008CC52284291B38E2B7795C85075}}(8) Waiver authority– ====

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] , in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]] , may waive the application of paragraph (2)(A)(i)for a country if—
:(A) the country meets all other requirements of paragraph (2);
:(B) the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] determines that the totality of the country's security risk mitigation measures provide assurance that the country's participation in the program would not compromise the law enforcement, security interests, or enforcement of the immigration laws of the United States;
:(C) there has been a general downward trend in the percentage of nationals of the country refused nonimmigrant visas under section 101(a)(15)(B);
:(D) the country consistently cooperated with the Government of the United States on counterterrorism initiatives, information sharing, preventing terrorist travel, and extradition to the United States of individuals (including the country's own nationals) who commit crimes that violate United States law before the date of its designation as a program country, and the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] and the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]] assess that such cooperation is likely to continue; and
:(E) the percentage of nationals of the country refused a nonimmigrant visa under section 101(a)(15)(B)during the previous full fiscal year was not more than 10 percent of the total number of nationals of that country who were granted or refused such nonimmigrant visas..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H40255B9253E5472CA82872C4500A9ED3}}(d) Termination of designation; probation– ==

 Section 217(f) of the Immigration and Nationality Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 8 U.S.C. 1187(f)] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|HD5D297400D5B4EA08644DACBEC5E892B}}(f) Termination of designation; probation– ===



===={{anchor|H4879BBDA653D4D6D9C8862D8F141A863}}(1) Definitions– ====

In this subsection:

====={{anchor|H181889E00A534C248CD869D940200985}}(A) Probationary period– =====

The term probationary period means the fiscal year in which a probationary country is placed in probationary status under this subsection.

====={{anchor|H83C17B20844C432B8D14DDDAD8844D46}}(B) Program country– =====

The term program country has the meaning given that term in subsection (c)(1)(B).

===={{anchor|HCB1F5F0C59B04039AEAC93EF253A8F44}}(2) Determination, notice, and initial probationary period– ====



====={{anchor|HAC8378234FB94F669EBFFD42BF222AA1}}(A) Determination of probationary status and notice of noncompliance– =====

As part of each program country’s periodic evaluation required by subsection (c)(5)(A), the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] shall determine whether a program country is in compliance with the program requirements under subparagraphs (A)(ii) through (F) of subsection (c)(2).

====={{anchor|H6B77AD71BC0841B3AC87B474DF87FFD3}}(B) Initial probationary period– =====

If the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] determines that a program country is not in compliance with the program requirements under subparagraphs (A)(ii) through (F) of subsection (c)(2), the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] shall place the program country in probationary status for the fiscal year following the fiscal year in which the periodic evaluation is completed.

===={{anchor|H00710D3808C04497AE7898507CCDC896}}(3) Actions at the end of the initial probationary period– ====

At the end of the initial probationary period of a country under paragraph (2)(B), the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] shall take 1 of the following actions:

====={{anchor|H221D93AAA7AB477AAB1063475458E013}}(A) Compliance during initial probationary period– =====

If the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] determines that all instances of noncompliance with the program requirements under subparagraphs (A)(ii) through (F) of subsection (c)(2)that were identified in the latest periodic evaluation have been remedied by the end of the initial probationary period, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall end the country’s probationary period.

====={{anchor|H03D60F06D896452B8DFCC8B0D48901AF}}(B) Noncompliance during initial probationary period– =====

If the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] determines that any instance of noncompliance with the program requirements under subparagraphs (A)(ii) through (F) of subsection (c)(2)that were identified in the latest periodic evaluation has not been remedied by the end of the initial probationary period—
:(i) the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] may terminate the country’s participation in the program; or
:(ii) on an annual basis, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] may continue the country’s probationary status if the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] , in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]] , determines that the country’s continued participation in the program is in the national interest of the United States.

===={{anchor|HE43CEA5A43BF4A4CA10C426299558364}}(4) Actions at the end of additional probationary periods– ====

At the end of all probationary periods granted to a country pursuant to paragraph (3)(B)(ii), the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall take 1 of the following actions:

====={{anchor|H8C728FFD64BF45E1A32D4EEC949F678D}}(A) Compliance during additional period– =====

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall end the country’s probationary status if the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] determines during the latest periodic evaluation required by subsection (c)(5)(A)that the country is in compliance with the program requirements under subparagraphs (A)(ii) through (F) of subsection (c)(2).

====={{anchor|H1CB1039694CF43BCBC4FB07D826AD9F3}}(B) Noncompliance during additional periods– =====

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall terminate the country's participation in the program if the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] determines during the latest periodic evaluation required by subsection (c)(5)(A)that the program country continues to be in noncompliance with the program requirements under subparagraphs (A)(ii) through (F) of subsection (c)(2).

===={{anchor|H9CC284CE75574C55AF0833B699F73E5C}}(5) Effective date– ====

The termination of a country's participation in the program under paragraph (3)(B)or (4)(B)shall take effect on the first day of the first fiscal year following the fiscal year in which the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] determines that such participation shall be terminated. Until such date, nationals of the country shall remain eligible for a waiver under subsection (a).

===={{anchor|HF4D5941CBAA24D85957FE11AC603C153}}(6) Treatment of nationals after termination– ====

For purposes of this subsection and subsection (d)—
:(A) nationals of a country whose designation is terminated under paragraph (3)or (4)shall remain eligible for a waiver under subsection (a)until the effective date of such termination; and
:(B) a waiver under this section that is provided to such a national for a period described in subsection (a)(1)shall not, by such termination, be deemed to have been rescinded or otherwise rendered invalid, if the waiver is granted prior to such termination.

===={{anchor|H0A3E93BD9F32406B85D1DFECD3C0017A}}(7) Consultative role of the secretary of state– ====

In this subsection, references to subparagraphs (A)(ii) through (F) of subsection (c)(2)and subsection (c)(5)(A)carry with them the consultative role of the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]] as provided in those provisions..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H5DB79444C81D41D1AFF25530C0345B2A}}(e) Review of overstay tracking methodology– ==

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]] shall conduct a review of the methods used by the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] —
:(1) to track aliens entering and exiting the United States; and
:(2) to detect any such alien who stays longer than such alien's period of authorized admission.

=={{anchor|HE882A31DDD2A4D8AB60DA60EF46C3227}}(f) Evaluation of electronic system for travel authorization– ==

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] —
:(1) an evaluation of the security risks of aliens who enter the United States without an approved Electronic System for Travel Authorization verification; and
:(2) a description of any improvements needed to minimize the number of aliens who enter the United States without the verification described in paragraph (1).

=={{anchor|H02885FFACC144C7C94B6183B15318861}}(g) Sense of Congress on priority for review of program countries– ==

It is the sense of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] , in the process of conducting evaluations of countries participating in the visa waiver program under section 217 of the Immigration and Nationality Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 8 U.S.C. 1187] ), should prioritize the reviews of countries in which circumstances indicate that such a review is necessary or desirable.
