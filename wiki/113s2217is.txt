{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 10, United States Code, to enhance the participation of mental health professionals in boards for the correction of military records and boards for the review of the discharge or dismissal of members of the Armed Forces.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Military Mental Health Review Board Improvement Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Enhancement of participation of mental health professionals in boards for correction of military records and boards for review of discharge or dismissal of members of the Armed Forces=



==(a) Boards for correction of military records– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1552  Section 1552of title 10, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1552 subsection (g)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1552 subsection (h)]; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1552 subsection (f)]the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1552 subsection (g)]:

<blockquote> 
::(g) Any medical advisory opinion issued to a board established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1552 subsection (a)(1)]with respect to a member or former member of the armed forces who was diagnosed while serving in the armed forces as experiencing a mental health disorder shall include the opinion of a clinical psychologist or psychiatrist if the request for correction of records concerned relates to a mental health disorder. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Boards for review of discharge or dismissal– ==



===(1) Review for certain former members with PTSD or TBI– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1553 Subsection (d)(1) of section 1553 of such title]is amended by striking“physician, clinical psychologist, or psychiatrist”the second place it appears and inserting“clinical psychologist or psychiatrist, or a physician with training on mental health issues connected with post traumatic stress disorder or traumatic brain injury (as applicable)”.

===(2) Review for certain former members with mental health diagnoses– ===

Such section is further amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 
:(e) In the case of a former member of the armed forces (other than a former member covered by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1553 subsection (d)]) who was diagnosed while serving in the armed forces as experiencing a mental health disorder, a board established under this section to review the former member's discharge or dismissal shall include a member who is a clinical psychologist or psychiatrist, or a physician with special training on mental health disorders. . 
</blockquote>
