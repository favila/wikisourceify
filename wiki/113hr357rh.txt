{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to require courses of education provided by public institutions of higher education that are approved for purposes of the educational assistance programs administered by the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to charge veterans tuition and fees at the in-State tuition rate.

==(a) Short title– ==

This Act may be cited as the “GI Bill Tuition Fairness Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. References to title 38, United States Code=

Except as otherwise expressly provided, whenever in this Act an amendment or repeal is expressed in terms of an amendment to, or a repeal of, a section or other provision, the reference shall be considered to be made to a section or other provision of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/ title 38, United States Code] .

=Sec. 3. Approval of courses of education provided by public educational institutions for purposes of educational assistance programs administered by Secretary of Veterans Affairs conditional on in-State tuition rate for veterans=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3679 Section 3679] is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>
:(c) Notwithstanding any other provision of this subtitle, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] or the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary’s]] designee shall disapprove a course of education provided by a public educational institution unless the institution charges tuition and fees for a veteran at up to the same rate as the institution charges for residents of the State in which the institution is located, regardless of the veteran’s State of residence. The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall notify the State approving agency of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary’s]] disapproval of a course of education under this subsection..
</blockquote>


==(b) Treatment of enrolled benefits recipients– ==



===(1) In general– ===

In the case of a veteran or eligible person who is enrolled before July 1, 2015, in a course of education provided by a public educational institution that was approved underchapter 36of title 38, United States Code, at the time of the initial enrollment of the veteran or eligible person but that is disapproved by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] or the Secretary’s designee by reason of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3679 subsection (c) of section 3679 of such title] , as added by subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall treat such institution as an institution that is approved under such chapter for purposes of the laws administered by the Secretary with respect to the veteran or eligible person until the veteran or eligible person completes the program of education in which the veteran or eligible person is enrolled.

===(2) Termination– ===



====(A) Limitation– ====

Except as provided in subparagraph (B), the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] may not treat such an institution as an approved institution under paragraph (1)after July 1, 2019.

====(B) Waiver– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may waive the limitation under subparagraph (A)if the Secretary determines such a waiver is appropriate.

==(c) Effective date– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3679 Subsection (c) ofsection 3679of title 38, United States Code] , as added by subsection (a)shall apply with respect to educational assistance provided after July 1, 2015.

=Sec. 7. Extension of eligibility period for vocational rehabilitation programs=

==(b) Effective date– ==

=Sec. 8. Work-study allowance=

=Sec. 9. Responsibilities of the Directors of Veterans’ Employment and Training=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 Section 4103] is amended—
:(1) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (b)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (c)] ; and
:(2) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (a)] the following new [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (b)] :

<blockquote>

===(b) Responsibilities– ===

Each Director assigned to a State under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (a)] shall carry out the following responsibilities:
:(1) Monitoring the performance of veterans’ training and employment programs in the State, with special emphasis on services to disabled veterans.
:(2) Monitoring the performance of the State workforce agency in complying with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4212 section 4212 of this title] .
:(3) Suggesting to the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary of Labor for Veterans’ Employment and Training]] corrective actions that could be taken by the State workforce agency to address deficiencies in the performance of veterans’ training and employment programs in the State.
:(4) Annually negotiating with the State workforce agency to establish performance goals for veterans’ training and employment programs in the State.
:(5) Reviewing the State’s requests for funding for veterans’ training and employment programs and providing advice to the State workforce agency and the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]] regarding such funding requests.
:(6) Forwarding complaints regarding possible violations ofchapter 43 of this titleto the appropriate Regional Administrator or to the to the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]] , as required.
:(7) Carrying out grant officer technical representative responsibilities for grants issued under programs administered by the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department]] .
:(8) Providing advice to the State workforce agency on strategies to market veterans to employers.
:(9) Supervising and managing all support staff, including Assistant Directors, establishing workload priorities, managing all personnel actions, and evaluating all assigned personnel.
:(10) Submitting to the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]] regular reports on the matters described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 paragraphs (1)] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 (2)] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 (4)] , and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 (8)] , and any other matters the Assistant Secretary determine appropriate.
:(11) Performing such other related duties as directed by the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]] ..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 10. Contents of Transition Assistance Program=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 Section 1144 of title 10, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsection (b)] , by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>
::(9) Provide information about disability-related employment and education protections..
</blockquote>

:(2) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsections (c), (d), and (e)] , as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsections (d), (e), and (f)] , respectively; and
:(3) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsection (b)] the following new [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsection (c)] :

<blockquote>

====(c) Additional elements of program– ====

The mandatory program carried out by this section shall include—
:(1) for any such member who plans to use the member’s entitlement to educational assistance under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/ title 38] —
::(A) instruction providing an overview of the use of such entitlement; and
::(B) testing to determine academic readiness for post-secondary education, courses of post-secondary education appropriate for the member, courses of post-secondary education compatible with the member’s education goals, and instruction on how to finance the member’s post-secondary education; and
:(2) instruction in the benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] and in other subjects determined by the Secretary concerned..
</blockquote>


==(b) Deadline for implementation– ==

The program carried out under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 section 1144 of title 10, United States Code] , shall comply with the requirements of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsections (b)(9)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 (c)] of such section, as added by subsection (a), by not later than April 1, 2015.

==(c) Feasibility study– ==

Not later than 270 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]] and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]] the results of a study carried out by the Secretary to determine the feasibility of providing the instruction described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1142 subsection (b) ofsection 1142of title 10, United States Code] , at all overseas locations where such instruction is provided by entering into a contract jointly with the Secretary of Labor for the provision of such instruction.

=Sec. 11. Three-month extension of Veterans Retraining Assistance Program=



==(a) Extension– ==

 Section 211 of the VOW to Hire Heroes Act of 2011([[Public Law 112-56|Public Law 112–56]] ;125 Stat. 713; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4100 38 U.S.C. 4100 note] )is amended—
:(1) in subsection (a)(2)(B), by striking “March 31, 2014” and inserting “June 30, 2014” ; and
:(2) in subsection (k), by striking “March 31, 2014” and inserting “June 30, 2014” .

==(b) Interim report– ==



===(1) Report required– ===

Not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] , in collaboration with the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]] , shall submit to the appropriate committees of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] an interim report on the retraining assistance provided under section 211 of the VOW to Hire Heroes Act of 2011([[Public Law 112-56|Public Law 112–56]] ;125 Stat. 713; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4100 38 U.S.C. 4100 note] ).

===(2) Elements– ===

The report required by paragraph (1)shall include the following:
:(A) The total number of—
::(i) eligible veterans who have participated in the program established under such section as of the date of the enactment of this Act; and
::(ii) associates degrees or certificates awarded (or other similar evidence of the completion of the program of education or training earned) to veterans participating in the program established under such section as of such date.
:(B) Data related to the employment status of eligible veterans who participated in such program.

=Sec. 12. Increase in rates of disability compensation and dependency and indemnity compensation=



==(a) Rate adjustment– ==

Effective on December 1, 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall increase, in accordance with subsection (c), the dollar amounts in effect on November 30, 2013, for the payment of disability compensation and dependency and indemnity compensation under the provisions specified in subsection (b).

==(b) Amounts To be increased– ==

The dollar amounts to be increased pursuant to subsection (a)are the following:

===(1) Wartime disability compensation– ===

Each of the dollar amounts under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1114 section 1114 of title 38, United States Code] .

===(2) Additional compensation for dependents– ===

Each of the dollar amounts under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1115 section 1115(1) of such title] .

===(3) Clothing allowance– ===

The dollar amount under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1162 section 1162 of such title] .

===(4) Dependency and indemnity compensation to surviving spouse– ===

Each of the dollar amounts under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1311 subsections (a) through (d) of section 1311 of such title] .

===(5) Dependency and indemnity compensation to children– ===

Each of the dollar amounts under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1313 sections 1313(a)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1314 1314 of such title] .

==(c) Determination of Increase– ==



===(1) Percentage– ===

Except as provided in paragraph (2), each dollar amount described in subsection (b)shall be increased by the same percentage as the percentage by which benefit amounts payable under title II of the Social Security Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/401 42 U.S.C. 401 et seq.] )are increased effective December 1, 2012, as a result of a determination under section 215(i) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/415 42 U.S.C. 415(i)] ).

===(2) Rounding– ===

Each dollar amount increased under paragraph (1), if not a whole dollar amount, shall be rounded to the next lower whole dollar amount.

==(d) Special rule– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] may adjust administratively, consistent with the increases made under subsection (a), the rates of disability compensation payable to persons under[[Public Law 85-857|section 10 of Public Law 85–857]] (72 Stat. 1263)who have not received compensation underchapter 11 of title 38, United States Code.

==(e) Publication of adjusted rates– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall publish in the Federal Register the amounts specified in subsection (b), as increased under that section, not later than the date on which the matters specified in section 215(i)(2)(D) of the Social Security Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/415 42 U.S.C. 415(i)(2)(D)] )are required to be published by reason of a determination made under section 215(i) of such Actduring fiscal year 2014.

=Sec. 13. Performance awards in the senior executive service=

For each of fiscal years 2014 through 2018, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] may not pay any performance awards under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5384 section 5384of title 5, United States Code] .Amend the title so as to read: “A bill to amend [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/ title 38, United States Code] , to require courses of education provided by public institutions of higher education that are approved for purposes of the educational assistance programs administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] to charge veterans tuition and fees at the in-State tuition rate, to make other improvements in the laws relating to benefits administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] , and for other purposes.” .
