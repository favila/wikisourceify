{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to clarify the conditions under which certain persons may be treated as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Veterans Second Amendment Protection Act” .

=Sec. 2. Conditions for treatment of certain persons as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes=



==(a) In general– ==

Chapter 55of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 5511. Conditions for treatment of certain persons as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes==

In any case arising out of the administration by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] of laws and benefits under this title, a person who is mentally incapacitated, deemed mentally incompetent, or experiencing an extended loss of consciousness shall not be considered adjudicated as a mental defective under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subsection (d)(4)] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (g)(4) ofsection 922of title 18] without the order or finding of a judge, magistrate, or other judicial authority of competent jurisdiction that such person is a danger to himself or herself or others..
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections at the beginning ofchapter 55 of such titleis amended by adding at the end the following new item:

<blockquote>**[[#id41655ED6AD2D43E9BF62F76C009BF1F7|5511. Conditions for treatment of certain persons as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes.]]
.
</blockquote>
