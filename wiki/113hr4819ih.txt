{{Center|A BILL}}

 To direct the Secretary of the Treasury to develop and submit class life recommendations for depreciable assets.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“  Jobs Investment Act of 2014  ”. 

=Sec. 2. Class life recommendations=



==(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]]shall, based on the monitoring and analysis of actual experience with respect to depreciable property under section 168(i)(1) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 , develop recommendations for prescribing to any depreciable property a class life, classification, or recovery period that more equitably provides for capital cost recovery across all industries without any significant effect on total tax revenue.

==(b) Proposal– ==

Not later than January 31, 2019, and every 5 years thereafter, the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Ways and Means|Committee on Ways and Means of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Finance|Committee on Finance of the Senate]]the recommendations developed under subsection (a) .
