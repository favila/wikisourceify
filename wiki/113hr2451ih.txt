{{Center|A BILL}}

To direct the Administrator of the Small Business Administration to establish and carry out a direct lending program for small business concerns, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Strengthening Entrepreneurs’ Economic Development Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Direct Lending Program for Small Business Concerns=



==(a) Establishment– ==

The [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator of the Small Business Administration]] shall—
:(1) establish and carry out a loan program (in this Act referred to as the “program” ); and
:(2) establish a process under which an eligible small business concern may submit an application to the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] for the purpose of securing a loan under the program.

==(b) Loan amount– ==

Each loan made to an eligible small business concern under the program shall be in an amount not to exceed $150,000.

==(c) Repayment period– ==

An eligible small business concern that receives a loan under the program shall repay the loan not later than 6 years after the date on which such loan is disbursed.

==(d) No prepayment penalty– ==

There shall be no prepayment penalty on a loan made under the program.

==(e) Interest rate– ==

The maximum legal rate of interest on any loan made under the program shall not exceed the sum of the rate prescribed by the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] pursuant to section 7(a)(4)(A) of the Small Business Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/636 15 U.S.C. 636(a)(4)(A)] )for direct loans plus 5 percent.

==(f) Borrower fees– ==

With respect to each loan made to an eligible small business concern under the program, the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] may collect a fee from the borrower using the formula established under section 7(a)(18) of the Small Business Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/636 15 U.S.C. 636(a)(18)] ).

==(g) Underwriting standards– ==

Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] shall issue guidance regarding prudent underwriting standards that must be used for loans made under the program.

==(h) Lender participation– ==



===(1) Lenders– ===



====(A) In general– ====

The [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] shall establish a process under which the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] makes available to lenders each loan application submitted for the purpose of such lenders originating, underwriting, closing, and servicing the loan for which the applicant applied.

====(B) Eligibility– ====

Lenders are eligible to receive a loan application described in subparagraph (A)if they participate in the program.

====(C) Local lenders– ====

The [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] shall first make available a loan application described in subparagraph (A)to lenders within 50 miles of the principal office of the loan applicant.

====(D) Preferred lenders– ====

If a lender described in subparagraph (C)does not agree to originate, underwrite, close, and service the loan applied for within 5 business days of receiving a loan application described in subparagraph (A), the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] shall subsequently make available such loan application to lenders in the [[w:Small Business Administration|Preferred Lenders Program]] under section 7(a)(2)(C)(ii) of the Small Business Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/636 15 U.S.C. 636(a)(2)(C)(ii)] ).

====(E) Authority of administration to lend– ====

If a lender described in subparagraphs (C)and (D)does not agree to originate, underwrite, close, and service the loan applied for within 10 business days of receiving a loan application described in subparagraph (A), the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] shall, in accordance with the underwriting standards promulgated under subsection (g), consider such loan for origination, underwriting, closing, and servicing by the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]] within 10 business days.

===(2) Asset sales– ===

The [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] shall offer to sell loans made by the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] under the program. Such sales shall be made through the semi-annual public solicitation (in the Federal Register and in other media) of offers to purchase. The [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] may contract with vendors for due diligence, asset valuation, and other services related to such sales. The [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] may not sell any loan under the program for less than 90 percent of the net present value of the loan, as determined and certified by a qualified third party.

===(3) Loans not sold– ===

The [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] shall maintain and service loans made by the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] under this paragraph that are not sold through the asset sales under this subsection.

==(i) Definitions– ==

In this Act:

===(1) Administrator– ===

The term “Administrator” means the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator of the Small Business Administration]] .

===(2) Eligible small business concern– ===

The term “eligible small business concern” means a small business concern that has less than 20 employees.

===(3) Small business concern– ===

The term “small business concern” has the same meaning given such term under section 3 of the Small Business Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/632 15 U.S.C. 632] ).

=Sec. 3. Fee for high-dollar 7(a)loans=

 Section 7(a) of the Small Business Actis amended—
:(1) by redesignating paragraphs (31) through (35)as paragraphs (32) through (36); and
:(2) by inserting the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

===(31) Fee for high-dollar loans– ===

With respect to each loan in excess of $2,000,000 approved under this subsection, the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]] shall assess, collect, and retain a fee not to exceed a certain percentage, as determined by the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]] , of the outstanding balance of the deferred participation share of the loan, as necessary to reduce to zero the cost to the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]] of making loans under this subsection. As used in the paragraph, the term “cost” has the meaning given that term in section 502 of the Federal Credit Reform Act of 1990( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/661a 2 U.S.C. 661a] )..
</blockquote>
