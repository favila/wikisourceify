{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to require the reporting of cases of infectious diseases at facilities of the Veterans Health Administration, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H2360D5B0CCA04CC0B690D42B650DB5C3}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Department of Veterans Affairs Disease Reporting and Oversight Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H20C78A7811CC486A90ADDC814E0AAEC5}}Sec. 2. Requirement that Veterans Health Administration report cases of infectious diseases at facilities of the Administration=



=={{anchor|H2F85942B0F384344A41FD65DEB9FB7C9}}(a) In general– ==

Subchapter II ofchapter 73of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H68E7C366FD36420A8465CFE09D934A11}}Sec. 7330B. Reporting of infectious diseases==



==={{anchor|HECDF8A9275EC45E0A87B1935B939FF37}}(a) Reporting– ===


:(1) Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B paragraph (2)] , not later than 24 hours after the director of a Veterans Integrated Service Network confirms the presence of a notifiable infectious disease at a facility under the jurisdiction of the director, the director shall submit notice of such presence to the following:
::(A) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Central Office of the Department]] .
::(B) The [[w:Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|Director of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention]] .
::(C) The State in which the facility is located and if the State has an agency or department that handles matters relating to such notifiable infectious disease, the head of such agency or department.
::(D) The county in which the facility is located and if the county has an agency or department that handles matters relating to such notifiable infectious disease, the head of such agency or department.
::(E) For each individual who has contracted the notifiable infectious disease at the facility or is at risk of contracting such notifiable infectious disease—
:::(i) the individual and the individual's next of kin;
:::(ii) the individual's primary health care provider; and
:::(iii) the county in which the individual resides and if the county has an agency or department that handles matters relating to such notifiable infectious disease, the head of such agency or department.
::(F) Each employee of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] who is employed at such facility.
:(2) If the State in which a facility described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B paragraph (1)] is located requires that a notifiable infectious disease confirmed at such facility be reported to the State more quickly than required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B paragraph (1)] or requires that a suspected presence of a notifiable infectious disease at such facility be reported before waiting for confirmation of such presence, the director concerned shall comply with such State requirement.
:(3) Not later than 24 hours after submitting a notice under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B paragraph (1)] , the director concerned shall confirm that such notice is received.

==={{anchor|H82658737B7E14143872594F2EFE4EAF5}}(b) Notifiable infectious disease– ===

For purposes of this section, a notifiable infectious disease is any infectious disease that is—
:(1) on the list of nationally notifiable diseases published by the Council of State and Territorial Epidemiologists and the [[w:Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|Centers for Disease Control and Prevention]] ; or
:(2) covered by a provision of law of a State that requires the reporting of infectious diseases.

==={{anchor|H200CF9259080413EB4A509FBF148F3B3}}(c) Plan To prevent spread– ===


:(1) Not later than seven days after the director of a Veterans Integrated Service Network confirms the presence of a notifiable infectious disease at a facility under the jurisdiction of such director, the director shall develop and implement an action plan to manage and control the potential spread of the notifiable infectious disease.
:(2) The plan developed and implemented under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B paragraph (1)] shall also provide details on the role of partnering Federal, State, and local government entities in the management and control of the potential spread of the notifiable infectious disease.

==={{anchor|H7E53D2ED3DED460FA461015AE1C599BE}}(d) Record keeping– ===

The director of each Veterans Integrated Service Network shall keep records of each notice submitted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B subsection (a)(1)] for a period of not less than 10 years.

==={{anchor|HFD71EC39032941FC8DB69E2C86223426}}(e) Annual reports by Inspector General– ===

Not less frequently than once each year, the [[w:|Inspector General of the Department]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the compliance of the directors of the Veterans Integrated Service Networks with the requirements of this section.

==={{anchor|H94480A35EE164BF39FC57FA78C5E461E}}(f) Enforcement and disciplinary action– ===


:(1) In any case in which the [[w:|Inspector General of the Department]] suspects that a director of a Veterans Integrated Service Network has failed to comply with an applicable provision of this section, the Inspector General shall conduct an investigation to determine whether such director failed to comply with an applicable provision of this section.
:(2) If the [[w:|Inspector General]] determines under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B paragraph (1)] that a director has failed to comply with a provision of this section, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall suspend such director for such period as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] considers appropriate undersubchapter Iorsubchapter II of chapter 75 of title 5, as the case may be.
:(3) [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B Paragraph (2)] shall not be construed to prevent the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] from imposing, in addition to suspension under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B paragraph (2)] , such other disciplinary action on the director as the Secretary considers appropriate and for which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] is otherwise authorized..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H9F1B35FF7EC14C8C8CB178F792413851}}(b) Internal communication– ==

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Under Secretary for Health of the Veterans Health Administration]] shall issue a directive to the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Pathology Team]] , the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Infection Prevention Team]] , and the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Facilities Management Team of the Veterans Health Administration]] and such other groups within the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Administration]] as the Under Secretary considers appropriate on the actions that should be taken in any case in which a notifiable infectious disease (as such term is used in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330B section 7330B of title 38, United States Code] , as added by subsection (a)) is discovered in a facility of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]] .

=={{anchor|HCB1D860B161E4629AC63FC64096BA04B}}(c) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections at the beginning ofchapter 73 of such titleis amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7330A section 7330A] the following new item:

<blockquote>7330B. Reporting of infectious diseases..
</blockquote>
