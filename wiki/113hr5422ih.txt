{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 to require the EEOC to approve commencing or intervening in certain litigation, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Litigation Oversight Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Approval of EEOC litigation commencement or intervention=

   Section 705 of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/2000e–4 42 U.S.C. 2000e–4] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(l) 
::(1) The [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]shall approve or disapprove by majority vote whether the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]shall commence or intervene in litigation involving multiple plaintiffs, or an allegation of systemic discrimination or a pattern or practice of discrimination.
::(2) A member of the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]shall have the power to require the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]to approve or disapprove by majority vote whether the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]shall commence or intervene in any litigation.
::(3) Neither the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]nor a member of the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]may delegate the authority provided under paragraph (1) or (2) to any other person.
::(4) Not later than 30 days after the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]commences or intervenes in litigation pursuant to approval under this subsection, the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]shall post and maintain the following information on its public website with respect to the litigation:
:::(A) The court in which the case was brought.
:::(B) The name and case number of the case.
:::(C) The nature of the allegation.
:::(D) The causes of action brought.
:::(E) Each [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commissioner’s]]vote on commencing or intervening in the litigation.
::(5) The [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]shall issue, in a manner consistent with section 713 , procedural regulations to carry out this subsection. . 
</blockquote>
