{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to permit certain
		  veterans who were discharged or released from the Armed Forces by reason of
		  service-connected disability to transfer benefits under the Post-9/11
		  Educational Assistance Program, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H5C44215DD552451ABE7D352E5F5498A3}}Section 1. Service-connected death,
			 disability, or injury exception for transferability of entitlement to
			 educational assistance under the Post-9/11 Educational Assistance
			 program=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 Section 3319 of title
			 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 subsection (b)]—
::(A) by striking
			 “(b)
			 Eligible
			 individuals.—An
			 individual,” and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|HFAE6CAAE02964C4EA93D6388D37C80B5}}(b) Eligible
				Individuals– ====



====={{anchor|H47576FB656454401BAFA6956EC9E3812}}(1) In
				general– =====

An
				individual;
</blockquote>

::(B) by redesignating
			 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 paragraphs (1)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 (2)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 subparagraphs (A)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 (B)], respectively, and moving
			 them two ems to the right; and
::(C) by adding at the
			 end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H733A6DA58C714CD89DAB0F16F451C481}}(2) Approval after
				death in line of duty– ====

Notwithstanding the service requirements
				under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 paragraph (1)], an individual referred to in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 subsection (a)] shall also
				include an individual who elected to transfer educational assistance as
				described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 subsection (a)] but whose application for such transfer was not
				approved before such individual died in line of
				duty.;
				and
</blockquote>

:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 subsection
			 (f)]—
::(A) by striking
			 “(1)
			 Time for
			 transfer.—Subject to”
			 and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H36B99B90AD184F988080ACB55C701B34}}(1) Time for
				transfer– ====



====={{anchor|H17F15552DCCC44A084AC6D3E1203EA9E}}(A) In
				general– =====

Except as provided in
				[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 subparagraph (B)] and subject to; and
				
</blockquote>

::(B) by adding at the
			 end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H15E299841B7C4F2FA23F5452D2F09B12}}(B) Exception– ====



====={{anchor|HFE820DBC11194C29B761955AE6710594}}(i) Time for
				transfer for service-connected disability or injury in line of
				duty– =====

The limitation under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 subparagraph (A)] with respect to the
				time to transfer entitlement benefits under this section shall not apply if an
				individual is discharged or released from active duty in the Armed Forces in
				accordance with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3311 section 3311(c) of this title]—
:(I) due to a
				service-connected disability; or
:(II) after sustaining
				an injury in line of duty that requires hospital, nursing home, or domiciliary
				care or treatment.

====={{anchor|H4D9AE36245AB42739CDDEE223B8E916F}}(ii) Extended period
				of eligibility– =====

An individual
				referred to in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 clause (i)] may transfer entitlement benefits under this
				section—
:(I) before the end of the 36-month period
				beginning on the date of the individual’s discharge or release from active duty
				in the Armed Forces; or
:(II) before the end of the 48-month period
				beginning on the date of such discharge or release if the Secretary concerned
				determines special circumstances exist.

====={{anchor|HD3037273AA274FC0B41B1C01234EC7E7}}(iii) Alternative
				period of eligibility– =====

In the case of an individual described in
				[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 clause (i)] who is discharged or released from active duty in the Armed Forces
				on or after August 1, 2009, but before the date of the enactment of this
				subparagraph, the extended period of eligibility described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 clause (ii)] shall
				begin on the date of the enactment of this subparagraph.

===={{anchor|H2EE43E216B304D0EABD3944EFA28165C}}(C) Additional
				opportunity to make election– ====

An individual described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 subparagraph
				(B)(i)] may elect to transfer the individual’s entitlement under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/3319 subsection (a)],
				or to make changes to the dependent or dependents designated as the transferee
				or transferees of the individual’s entitlement under this section, one or more
				times during the time period referred to in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 subparagraph
				(B)(ii)]..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H782DCAC59AB24BDCA4BCBE0DC8F79CB5}}Sec. 2. Effective
			 Date=

The amendments made by
			 this Act to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3319 section 3319 of title 38, United States Code], shall apply with
			 respect to individuals who became eligible to transfer benefits under such
			 section on or after August 1, 2009.
