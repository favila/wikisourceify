{{Center|A BILL}}

To address equity capital requirements for financial institutions, bank holding companies, subsidiaries, and affiliates, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|idCFA49B333CA240C2B7B438BE820E3CAA}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Terminating Bailouts for Taxpayer Fairness Act of 2013”or the“TBTF Act”.

={{anchor|idAD9BF0229A3C4C83B87B5E80AEC31005}}Sec. 2. Definitions=



=={{anchor|ideea9f21a4b90435dacd885d75ce73b2e}}(a) In general– ==

As used in this Act—
:(1) the terms affiliate , appropriate Federal banking agency ,“Federal banking agency”, foreign bank , and insured depository institution have the same meanings as in section 3 of the Federal Deposit Insurance Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 12 U.S.C. 1813] );
:(2) the terms bank holding company and subsidiary have the same meanings as in section 2 of the Bank Holding Company Act of 1956([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1841 12 U.S.C. 1841] );
:(3) the term“Board”means the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System]] ;
:(4) the term“Corporation”means the [[w:Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation|Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation]] ;
:(5) the term financial institution means an insured depository institution, bank holding company, a savings and loan holding company, and a foreign bank subject to the Bank Holding Company Act of 1956;
:(6) the term“nonbank financial company”has the same meaning as in the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 12 U.S.C. 5311] ); and
:(7) the term savings and loan holding company has the same meaning as in section 10 of the Home Owners’ Loan Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 10 U.S.C. 1467a] ), except that such term does not include any savings and loan holding company described in section 10(c)(9)(C) of the Home Owners’ Loan Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1467a 12 U.S.C. 1467a(c)(9)(C)] ] ).

={{anchor|id9fcfc7a8dba448338a6242d001ea9aa5}}Sec. 3. Equity capital requirements=



=={{anchor|id8fa0c052128845ebb96340cf5f3ff77e}}(a) Equity capital requirements for bank holding companies, subsidiaries, and affiliates– ==



==={{anchor|id2e0383efdfff4153a92e9c94a90cce77}}(1) Equity capital requirements– ===



===={{anchor|idaf9ab737be524a94a0e8beaabd2bdb1c}}(A) In general– ====

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the appropriate Federal banking agency, in consultation with the other Federal banking agencies, shall, by rule, establish capital requirements for the ratio of equity capital to total consolidated assets for all financial institutions.

===={{anchor|id94ce5f9f63fb4a12b7d56d2153b5b4cb}}(B) Limitations– ====



====={{anchor|id25B5DFFF1F234F47BE7119C24599EB4C}}(i) In general– =====

In no case may the requirements issued under this subsection require any financial institution with more than $50,000,000,000 in total consolidated assets to have a ratio of less than 8 percent of equity capital to total consolidated assets.

====={{anchor|id89b6f73f3f4140b8a92809b0d87e2b01}}(ii) Comparability– =====

The equity capital requirement issued under this subsection for any financial institution with $50,000,000,000 or less in total consolidated assets shall be comparable to the requirements established by the appropriate Federal banking agencies under the prompt corrective actions regulations implementing section 38 of the Federal Deposit Insurance Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 12 U.S.C. 1831o] )and under the capital adequacy regulations implementing section 5 of the Bank Holding Company Act of 1956([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 12 U.S.C. 1844] ), that were in effect as of May 1, 2013.

==={{anchor|idf9912f43a6de4938857cbaa12e9b7364}}(2) Capital surcharge for the largest financial institutions– ===



===={{anchor|id0f8a5c88f15d44779432b10ddc7f8ab9}}(A) Study required– ====



====={{anchor|idD1D28BAD816244BFB339AD822A16BE3F}}(i) In general– =====

The [[w:Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation|Corporation]] shall study historical equity capital ratios chosen by large depository institutions before the advent of the Federal Reserve System, Federal deposit insurance, and the Federal income tax encouraged depositories to favor more highly leveraged deposit and debt funding.

====={{anchor|idD45FF09013074C9BB914A812A558B053}}(ii) Report to Congress– =====

Not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation|Corporation]] shall issue a report to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs|Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senate]] and the [[w:United States House Committee on Financial Services|Committee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives]] regarding the study conducted under clause (i).

====={{anchor|ida5ed376a4c8e4f718902d25ed965a9ee}}(iii) Incorporation of findings– =====

The [[w:Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation|Corporation]] , in consultation with the other Federal banking agencies, shall structure the capital surcharge for financial institutions with at least $500,000,000,000 in total consolidated assets, such that the surcharge fully accounts for and offsets any distortion of capital levels by the Government policies described in clause (i).

===={{anchor|iddad8d7217d034310ba96a0c897607b6c}}(B) Rules– ====

Not later than 1 year after the date of completion of the study required by subparagraph (A), the appropriate Federal banking agency, in consultation with the other Federal banking agencies, shall, by rule, establish equity capital surcharges for each financial institution having at least $500,000,000,000 in total consolidated assets.

===={{anchor|id815b56a3901a44b6b0bfc507f557404f}}(C) Increases authorized– ====

Capital requirements established under this paragraph may increase continuously as a percentage of total consolidated assets as the total consolidated assets of a financial institution increase.

===={{anchor|id22ae1dab3e4842eb871270dcee166fdb}}(D) Required amount– ====

The surcharge imposed under the rules issued under this paragraph shall require any financial institution having at least $500,000,000,000 in total consolidated assets to have a ratio of not less than 15 percent of equity capital to total consolidated assets.

===={{anchor|ida1cced675140435a976f4d2a7e1e1af2}}(E) Anti-evasion– ====



====={{anchor|idb3a0ccaf426f4da58d9d3f24ec385d45}}(i) In general– =====

Any attempt by a financial institution to structure any activity, transaction, or affiliation for the purpose or effect of evading or attempting to evade the asset threshold that gives rise to the surcharge provided in subparagraph (A)shall be considered a violation of the Federal Deposit Insurance Act, section 24 of the Revised Statutes of the United States,and the Bank Holding Company Act of 1956, as applicable to such financial institution.

====={{anchor|idc82af4a134394ca0969cd05b7380a819}}(ii) Restricting activities– =====

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, if the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Board]] , the [[w:Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation|Corporation]] , or the [[w:Office of the Comptroller of the Currency|Comptroller of the Currency]] has reasonable cause to believe that a financial institution or any affiliate thereof has engaged in an activity, transaction, or affiliation in a manner that functions as an evasion of the asset threshold that gives rise to the surcharge provided in subparagraph (A)or otherwise violates such provision, the appropriate Federal banking agency shall order, after due notice and opportunity for hearing, the financial institution to restrict, restructure, or divest the offending activities, transactions, or investments.

==={{anchor|idffaed617573d48d1916a2f151eb8102f}}(3) Effective date– ===

The equity capital and surcharge rules issued under paragraphs (1)and (2)shall apply with respect to each financial institution not later than 5 years after the date on which final rules are published in the Federal Register with respect to that financial institution.

==={{anchor|id715f59624d6d4073a036648f9ec9c197}}(4) Well-capitalized status– ===



===={{anchor|id030B7D9DB8494CBFA191B4E44B80B676}}(A) Compliance with other provisions– ====

Any financial institution that meets the equity capital requirements established under paragraph (1)or surcharge requirements established under paragraph (2)shall be considered well capitalized for purposes of—
:(i)  section 38 of the Federal Deposit Insurance Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 12 U.S.C. 1831o] ); and
:(ii) the early remediation requirements established pursuant to section 166 of the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 12 U.S.C. 5366] ).

===={{anchor|id9E408B847C334F21A9AFB7B0228EEDCD}}(B) Agency actions– ====

Consistent with this section, the appropriate Federal banking agency, in consultation with the other Federal banking agencies, shall, by regulation, establish the appropriate capital categories for financial institutions under section 38 of the Federal Deposit Insurance Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1831o 12 U.S.C. 1831o] )and early remediation requirements established pursuant to section 166 of the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/5366 12 U.S.C. 5366] ).

==={{anchor|ide97c3f3665684477bc8b097ae0130d83}}(5) Enhanced prudential standards– ===

The equity capital and surcharge rules issued under paragraphs (1)and (2)shall be considered sufficient to satisfy the risk-based capital requirements and leverage limits for purposes of section 165 of the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/5365 12 U.S.C. 5365] ).

=={{anchor|id80928feb50b84db68e31f7578445d8b4}}(b) Equity capital requirements for affiliates and subsidiaries of bank holding companies– ==



==={{anchor|id2ac12366a01f41e8a22a48f28cf53fca}}(1) In general– ===

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, notwithstanding any other provision of law applicable to insured depository institutions, the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Board]] (subject to section 5(c)(3) of the Bank Holding Company Act of 1956([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1844 12 U.S.C. 1844(c)(3)] ] )and section 10(g) of the Home Owners' Loan Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1467a 12 U.S.C. 1467a(g)] ] )), the [[w:Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation|Corporation]] , and the [[w:Office of the Comptroller of the Currency|Comptroller of the Currency]] shall each promulgate regulations to establish capital requirements for each affiliate and subsidiary of a financial institution that are no less stringent than the equity capital requirements established under subsection (a)(1)or surcharge requirements established under subsection (a)(2).

==={{anchor|id8e7fb39c063549979db54b7bf563abef}}(2) Limitation– ===

 Paragraph (1)and the regulations issued under paragraph (1)do not apply in the case of any financial institution with less than $50,000,000,000 in total consolidated assets.

=={{anchor|id6810403e9a7543898ed96a8e0e1f0bc1}}(c) Specific elements of capital requirements– ==

For purposes of calculating—
:(1) equity capital requirements under this section, equity capital shall consist of tangible common equity (defined as common stockholders’ equity less goodwill), deferred tax assets, accumulated other comprehensive income, treasury stock, and intangible assets plus retained earnings; and
:(2) total consolidated assets under this section, derivative exposures shall include—
::(A) the fair value of the derivative exposures without recognizing the benefits of any netting arrangement, unless—
:::(i) the netting arrangement is documented under a formal master netting agreement or other formal arrangement with a derivatives clearing organization; and
:::(ii) the financial institution, as a matter of ongoing business practice, exchanges collateral on a daily basis for the fulfillment of variation margin requirements on a net basis, and fulfills all contractual payment requirements, including payments for contract termination, on a net basis, with such net exchange of collateral and payments encompassing all derivative exposures covered by the formal arrangement; and
::(B) off-balance sheet assets—
:::(i) defined as any assets in which the financial institution has guaranteed performance by another party or provided a liquidity backstop should another party be unable to perform under the contractual obligation; and
:::(ii) excluding commitments to lend, whereby certain provisions and or covenants exist that limit the risk to the bank holding company with respect to future draws of liquidity.

=={{anchor|id3ef19d02f0c34a12ad2c062f5843c7d4}}(d) Risk-Based capital requirements permitted– ==



==={{anchor|id8628CC08A08F42AAA9B4A1AD67C513F0}}(1) Rule of construction– ===

Except as provided in paragraph (2), nothing in this section shall be interpreted to prevent any appropriate Federal banking agency from establishing supplemental risk-based capital requirements for any financial institution with more than $20,000,000,000 in total consolidated assets, or any affiliate or subsidiary of such institutions for the purpose of measuring the relative risk of certain assets and preventing investment in excessive amounts of riskier assets.

==={{anchor|idae2f288807f84d02b95afdddc280ce99}}(2) Limitation– ===



===={{anchor|idEDE68678413B4F509CB5C3F91FBC3B44}}(A) Joint determination– ====

An appropriate Federal banking agency may not implement risk-based capital requirements with respect to a financial institution with more than $20,000,000,000, unless all appropriate Federal banking agencies agree that bank supervision is insufficient to prevent the excessive concentration of riskier assets.

===={{anchor|id638BF63233864EB890FDD2184766EFEA}}(B) Report to congress– ====

Before proposing risk based capital rules described in this subsection, the appropriate Federal banking agencies shall submit a joint report to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs|Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senate]] and the [[w:United States House Committee on Financial Services|Committee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives]] detailing the deficiency in supervisory tools in preventing investment in excessive amounts of riskier assets and how risk based capital will be used. The appropriate Federal banking agencies may establish supplemental risk-based capital requirements that do not replace the equity capital requirements required by this Act not earlier than 90 days after the date of submission of the report under this subparagraph.

=={{anchor|id00686f8e0c8f448a83358bf465e9e9a7}}(e) Treatment of basel III international accord– ==

The [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Board]] , the [[w:Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation|Corporation]] , and the [[w:Office of the Comptroller of the Currency|Comptroller of the Currency]] shall be prohibited from any further implementation of any rules of the Federal banking agencies regarding“Basel III: A Global Regulatory Framework for More Resilient Banks and Banking Systems”.

={{anchor|id0b849693552e4936933d7813ea2bb75a}}Sec. 5. Limitation on the Federal safety net=

:(3) the Deposit Insurance Fund established under section 11(a)(4) of the Federal Deposit Insurance Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1821 12 U.S.C. 1821(a)(4)] ] );

=={{anchor|id0a506382c7114ef29e62d2bf4ce17a41}}(e) Examination ombudsman– ==



==={{anchor|ida8b5f775559f409aa2d6c048199776fe}}(1) In general– ===

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|id34b036d2af284d9ea0d52c3541651230}}Sec. 1012. Office of Examination Ombudsman===



===={{anchor|ida98ca1c200f84fb9bc23f9c136bea86a}}(a) Establishment– ====

There is established in the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Council]] an [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Office of Examination Ombudsman]] .

===={{anchor|id3a330f1972a4429e82f76e8a68c0e0ab}}(d) Duties– ====

The [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] shall—
:(1) receive and, at the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman's]] discretion, investigate complaints from financial institutions, their representatives, or another entity acting on behalf of such institutions, concerning examinations, examination practices, or examination reports;

:(3) review examination procedures of the Federal financial institutions regulatory agencies to ensure that the written examination policies of those agencies are being followed in practice and adhere to the standards for consistency established by the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Council]] ;
