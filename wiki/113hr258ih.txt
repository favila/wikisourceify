{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 18, United States Code, with respect to fraudulent representations about having received military declarations or medals.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Stolen Valor Act of 2013”.

=Sec. 2. Fraudulent representations about receipt of military decorations or medals=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 Section 704 of title 18, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 subsection (a)] , by striking“wears,”; and
:(2) so that [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 subsection (b)] reads as follows:

<blockquote>

====(b) Fraudulent representations about receipt of military decorations or medals– ====

Whoever, with intent to obtain money, property, or other tangible benefit, fraudulently holds oneself out to be a recipient of a decoration or medal described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 subsection (c)(2)] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 (d)] shall be fined under this title, imprisoned not more than one year, or both..
</blockquote>


==(b) Addition of certain other medals– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 Section 704(d) of title 18, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by striking“If a decoration”and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

====(1) In general– ====

If a decoration;
</blockquote>

:(2) by inserting“a combat badge,”after“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1129 1129 of title 10] ,”; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

====(2) Combat badge defined– ====

In this subsection, the term“combat badge”means a Combat Infantryman’s Badge, Combat Action Badge, Combat Medical Badge, Combat Action Ribbon, or Combat Action Medal..
</blockquote>


==(c) Conforming amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 Section 704 of title 18, United States Code] , is amended in each of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 subsections (c)(1)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 (d)] by striking“or (b)”.
