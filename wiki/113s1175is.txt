{{Center|A BILL}}

To require the Secretary of the Treasury to establish a program to provide loans and loan guarantees to enable eligible public entities to acquire interests in real property that are in compliance with habitat conservation plans approved by the Secretary of the Interior under the Endangered Species Act of 1973, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Infrastructure Facilitation and Habitat Conservation Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Conservation loan and loan guarantee program=



==(a) Definitions– ==

In this section:

===(1) Eligible public entity– ===

The term eligible public entity means a political subdivision of a State, including—
:(A) a duly established town, township, or county;
:(B) an entity established for the purpose of regional governance;
:(C) a special purpose entity; and
:(D) a joint powers authority, or other entity certified by the Governor of a State, to have authority to implement a habitat conservation plan pursuant to section 10(a) of the Endangered Species Act of 1973( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1539 16 U.S.C. 1539(a)] ).

===(2) Program– ===

The term program means the conservation loan and loan guarantee program established by the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] under subsection (b)(1).

===(3) Secretary– ===

The term Secretary means the Secretary of the Treasury.

==(b) Loan and loan guarantee program– ==



===(1) Establishment– ===

As soon as practicable after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall establish a program to provide loans and loan guarantees to eligible public entities to enable eligible public entities to acquire interests in real property that are acquired pursuant to habitat conservation plans approved by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] under section 10 of the Endangered Species Act of 1973( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1539 16 U.S.C. 1539] ).

===(2) Application; approval process– ===



====(A) Application– ====



=====(i) In general– =====

To be eligible to receive a loan or loan guarantee under the program, an eligible public entity shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] an application at such time, in such form and manner, and including such information as the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] may require.

=====(ii) Solicitation of applications– =====

Not less frequently than once per calendar year, the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall solicit from eligible public entities applications for loans and loan guarantees in accordance with this section.

====(B) Approval process– ====



=====(i) Submission of applications to Secretary of the Interior– =====

As soon as practicable after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] receives an application under subparagraph (A), the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall submit the application to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] for review.

=====(ii) Review by Secretary of the Interior– =====



======(I) Review– ======

As soon as practicable after the date of receipt of an application by the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] under clause (i), the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall conduct a review of the application to determine whether—
:(aa) the eligible public entity is implementing a habitat conservation plan that has been approved by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] under section 10 of the Endangered Species Act of 1973( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1539 16 U.S.C. 1539] );
:(bb) the habitat acquisition program of the eligible public entity would very likely be completed; and
:(cc) the eligible public entity has adopted a complementary plan for sustainable infrastructure development that provides for the mitigation of environmental impacts.

======(II) Report to Secretary– ======

Not later than 60 days after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] receives an application under subclause (I), the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] a report that contains—
:(aa) an assessment of each factor described in subclause (I); and
:(bb) a recommendation regarding the approval or disapproval of a loan or loan guarantee to the eligible public entity that is the subject of the application.

======(III) Consultation with Secretary of Commerce– ======

To the extent that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] considers to be appropriate to carry out this clause, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] may consult with the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] .

=====(iii) Approval by Secretary– =====



======(I) In general– ======

Not later than 120 days after receipt of an application under subparagraph (A), the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall approve or disapprove the application.

======(II) Factors– ======

In approving or disapproving an application of an eligible public entity under subclause (I), the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] may consider—
:(aa) whether the financial plan of the eligible public entity for habitat acquisition is sound and sustainable;
:(bb) whether the eligible public entity has the ability to repay a loan or meet the terms of a loan guarantee under the program;
:(cc) any factor that the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] determines to be appropriate; and
:(dd) the recommendation of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] .

======(III) Preference– ======

In approving or disapproving applications of eligible public entities under subclause (I), the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall give preference to eligible public entities located in biologically rich regions in which rapid growth and development threaten successful implementation of approved habitat conservation plans, as determined by the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] in cooperation with the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] .

====(C) Administration of loans and loan guarantees– ====



=====(i) Report to Secretary of the interior– =====

Not later than 60 days after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] approves or disapproves an application under subparagraph (B)(iii), the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] a report that contains the decision of the Secretary to approve or disapprove the application.

=====(ii) Duty of Secretary– =====

As soon as practicable after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] approves an application under subparagraph (B)(iii), the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall—
:(I) establish the loan or loan guarantee with respect to the eligible public entity that is the subject of the application (including such terms and conditions as the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] may prescribe); and
:(II) carry out the administration of the loan or loan guarantee.

==(c) Authorization of appropriations– ==

There are authorized to be appropriated to the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] to carry out this sectionsuch sums as are necessary.

==(d) Termination of authority– ==

The authority under this section shall terminate on the date that is 10 years after the date of enactment of this Act.
