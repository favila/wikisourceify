{{Center|A BILL}}

To improve master plans for major military installations.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Military Installations Enhancement Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Sense of Congress on United States military installations=



==(a) Findings– ==

 [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] makes the following findings:
:(1) The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] is one of the largest Federal holders of real estate, with a global portfolio covering more than 27,700,000 acres.
:(2) The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] maintains more than 5,000 military sites, with 122 sites considered major military installations, each valued at more than $1,794,000,000 in fiscal year 2013 dollars.
:(3) The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] is working to maximize range and training areas on its military installations in an effort to promote military readiness.
:(4) The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] is committed to ensuring that it is delivering cost-effective, safe, and environmentally sound capabilities and capacities to support the national defense mission.

==(b) Sense of Congress– ==

It is the sense of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that—
:(1) military installations are a critical component of the United States force capabilities;
:(2) military installations should be developed to preserve or expand range and training areas on bases in order to enhance military readiness;
:(3) military installations create communities of identity for servicemembers and their families, and are important to overall wellness and quality of life;
:(4) the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] should take into consideration opportunities to reduce military construction costs on installations through cost-effective construction and planning; and
:(5) the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] and [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] should endeavor to promote quality of life for servicemembers and military families living on military installations.

=Sec. 3. Improving master plans for major military installations=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2864 Section 2864of title 10, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2864 subsection (a)] —
::(A) by striking “At a time” and inserting “(1) At a time” ; and
::(B) by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>
:::(2) To address the requirements under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2864 paragraph (1)] , each installation master plan shall include consideration of—
::::(A) planning for redevelopment and infill development to reduce consumption of undeveloped land on installations;
::::(B) horizontal and vertical mixed-use development;
::::(C) the full lifecycle costs of planning decisions; and
::::(D) capacity planning through the establishment of growth boundaries around cantonment areas to focus development towards the core and preserve range and training space.;
</blockquote>

:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2864 subsection (b)] —
::(A) by striking “The transportation” and inserting “(1) The transportation” ; and
::(B) by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>
:::(2) To address the requirements under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2864 subsection (a)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2864 paragraph (1)] , each installation master plan shall include consideration of ways to diversify and connect transit systems and increase safety for all road users.;
</blockquote>

:(3) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2864 subsection (c)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2864 subsection (d)] ; and
:(4) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2864 subsection (b)] the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

===(c) Savings clause– ===

Nothing in this section shall supersede the requirements of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2859 section 2859(a)] of this title..
</blockquote>
