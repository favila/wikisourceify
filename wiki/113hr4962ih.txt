{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide for enhanced border security, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   National Border and Homeland Security Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Priority distributions under the State Criminal Alien Assistance Program=

   Section 241(i) of the Immigration and Nationality Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1231 8 U.S.C. 1231(i)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(7) In distributing amounts under this subsection to a State or political subdivision of a State, including a municipality, for a fiscal year, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]shall prioritize compensating—
::(A) States that are on the northern or southern border; or
::(B) political subdivisions of States, including municipalities, that, in the determination of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]], have one of the four largest populations of aliens unlawfully present in the United States for the preceding fiscal year. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Fencing along and operational control of the southwest border=



==(a) Fencing– ==

   Section 102(b)(1) of the Illegal Immigration Reform and Immigrant Responsibility Act of 1996 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1103 8 U.S.C. 1103 note])is amended—
:(1) in subparagraph (A) , by inserting“, not later than December 31, 2015,”before“construct”; and
:(2) in subparagraph (B) —
::(A) in clause (i) , by striking“370 miles, or other mileage determined by the Secretary, whose authority to determine other mileage shall expire on December 31, 2008,”and inserting“areas”; and
::(B) in clause (ii) , by striking“2008”and inserting“2015”.

==(b) Operational control– ==

   Subsection (a) of section 2 the Secure Fence Act of 2006 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1701  8 U.S.C. 1701note]; [[Public Law 109-367|Public Law 109–367]]  )is amended, in the matter preceding paragraph (1) —
:(1) by striking“18 months after the date of the enactment of this Act,”and inserting“December 31, 2015,”; and
:(2) by inserting“, in consultation with State and local officials along the international border between the United States and Mexico, including border State governors, mayors of border towns and cities, and border sheriffs,”before“shall”. 

=Sec. 4. Border Patrol agents=

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall increase the number of positions for full-time, active-duty [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Border Patrol]]agents over the number of such agents for the preceding fiscal year as follows:
:(1) Three thousand such agents for fiscal year 2015 (with 2,500 such agents deployed to the southern border and 500 such agents deployed to the northern border).
:(2) One thousand such agents for fiscal year 2016 (with 800 such agents deployed to the southern border and 200 such agents deployed to the northern border).
:(3) One thousand such agents for fiscal year 2017 (with 800 such agents deployed to the southern border and 200 such agents deployed to the northern border).
:(4) One thousand such agents for fiscal year 2018 (with 800 such agents deployed to the southern border and 200 such agents deployed to the northern border). 

=Sec. 5. Customs and Border Protection=

For each of fiscal years 2015, 2016, 2017, and 2018, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall increase by not fewer than 200 the number of [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]officers at United States ports of entry over the number of such officers at such ports for the preceding fiscal year. 

=Sec. 6. Biometric entry and exit data system=



==(a) In general– ==

Not later than 18 months after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall establish the biometric entry and exit data system required by  section 7208 of the Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act of 2004 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1365b 8 U.S.C. 1365b] ), notwithstanding any other dates specified in such section.

==(b) Requirements– ==

In addition to the features required by the biometric entry and exit data system in accordance with  section 7208 of the Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act of 2004 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1365b 8 U.S.C. 1365b] ), the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall ensure that such system is established and in operation at each port of entry in the United States. 

=Sec. 7. Prohibition on impeding certain activities of the Secretary of Homeland Security related to border security=

On public lands of the United States, neither the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]]nor the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]]may impede, prohibit, or restrict activities of the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]to achieve operational control (as defined in  section 2(b) of the Secure Fence Act of 2006 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1701  8 U.S.C. 1701note];[[Public Law 109-367|Public Law 109–367]] ). 

=Sec. 8. Global nuclear detection architecture=

   Section 1902(a) of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/6/592 6 U.S.C. 592(a)] )is amended—
:(1) in paragraph (1) , by inserting before the semicolon at the end the following:“, particularly with respect to potential smuggling routes in land border areas between ports of entry, railcars entering the United States from Canada or Mexico, and private aircraft or small vessels”;
:(2) by redesignating paragraphs (13) and (14) as paragraphs (14) and (15) , respectively;
:(3) by inserting after paragraph (12) the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
::(13) develop objectives to be accomplished to carry out this subsection, identify roles and responsibilities for meeting such objectives, ensure that the funding necessary to achieve such objectives is available, and employ monitoring mechanisms to determine progress toward achieving such objectives; ; and 
</blockquote>

:(4) in paragraph (14) , as so redesignated, by striking“paragraphs (10), (11), and (12)”and inserting“this subsection”. 

=Sec. 9. Portable radiation detectors and radioactive isotope identification devices=

Not later than one year after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall determine the number of next generation portable radiation detectors (PRD) and radioactive isotope identification devices (RIID) required by [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Border Patrol]]agents patrolling the southern and northern borders of the United States and procure such detectors and devices. 

=Sec. 10. Strategic plan to detect and interdict biological and chemical weapons=



==(a) In general– ==



===(1) Development– ===

Not later than one year after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall develop a strategic plan (in this section referred to as the“Plan”) to detect and interdict biological and chemical weapons entering the United States.

===(2) Implementation– ===

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall complete implementation of the Plan not later than two years after the development of the Plan under paragraph (1) .

==(b) Reports to Congress– ==



===(1) Initial report– ===

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report that describes the Plan.

===(2) Annual report– ===

Not later than two years after the submission of the initial report under paragraph (1) and annually thereafter, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall submit reports to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]on the implementation of the Plan.
