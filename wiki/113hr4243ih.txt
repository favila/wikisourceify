{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 40, United States Code, to permit commercial filmmaking and photography on the United States Capitol grounds, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Permitting commercial filmmaking and photography on United States Capitol Grounds=



==(a) In General– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/5106  Section 5106of title 40, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 

===(d) Commercial Filmmaking and Photography– ===



====(1) Authorization by Capitol Police– ====

Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/5104 section 5104(c)]or any other provision of this chapter which restricts commercial activity on the United States Capitol Grounds, the Chief of the United States Capitol Police (hereafter in this subsection referred to as the“Chief”)—
:(A) may issue a permit authorizing a person to engage in appropriate commercial filmmaking and photography activity on the United States Capitol Grounds (other than any Capitol Buildings described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/5101 section 5101]); and
:(B) under the terms and conditions of such a permit, may require the person to whom the permit is issued to pay an appropriate fee to cover any costs incurred by the [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]]as a result of the issuance of the permit.

====(2) Types of activities and fees permitted– ====

For purposes of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/5106 paragraph (1)]—
:(A) commercial filmmaking and photography activity shall be considered appropriate only if the activity is similar to the types of commercial activity permitted in Union Square prior to the transfer of jurisdiction and control of Union Square to the [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]]under  section 1202 of the Legislative Branch Appropriations Act, 2012 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/1811  2 U.S.C. 1811note]); and
:(B) a fee shall be considered appropriate only if the fee is similar to the fee collected by the [[w:National Park Service|Director of the National Park Service]]for commercial activity permitted in Union Square prior to such transfer of jurisdiction and control.

====(3) Use of fees– ====

Immediately upon receiving any fees collected under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/5106 paragraph (1)(B)], the Chief shall transfer the fees to the Capitol Trust Account established under  section 213(c) of the Legislative Branch Appropriations Act, 2014 ( [[Public Law 113-76|Public Law 113–76]]  ).

====(4) Regulations– ====

The Chief shall carry out this subsection in accordance with such regulations as the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]]may promulgate pursuant to the Board's authority under section 14 of the Act of July 31, 1946 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/1969 2 U.S.C. 1969] ), except that the Board shall promulgate the regulations in consultation with the [[w:United States House Committee on House Administration|Committee on House Administration of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Rules and Administration|Committee on Rules and Administration of the Senate]].

====(5) Union Square defined– ====

In this subsection, the term Union Square means the area for which jurisdiction and control was transferred to the [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]]under  section 1202 of the Legislative Branch Appropriations Act, 2012 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/1811 2 U.S.C. 1811 note]). . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Conforming Amendment Relating to Capitol Trust Account– ==

   Section 213(c) of the Legislative Branch Appropriations Act, 2014 ( [[Public Law 113-76|Public Law 113–76]]  )is amended by striking“subsection (b)(2)”each place it appears and inserting“subsection (b)(2) or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/5106  section 5106(d)of title 40, United States Code] ”.
