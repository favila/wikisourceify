{{Center|A BILL}}

 To direct the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to meet with certain advisory committees to receive administrative and policy recommendations to improve the health care system of the Department of Veterans Affairs, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Our Vets Deserve Better Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Administrative and policy recommendations to improve the health care system of the Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) Meetings– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall meet with the advisory committees described in paragraph (2) to receive administrative and policy recommendations to improve the health care system of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

===(2) Advisory committees described– ===

The advisory committees described in this paragraph are the following:
:(A) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Advisory Committee on Prosthetics and Special-Disabilities Programs]]established by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/543 section 543 of title 38], United States Code.
:(B) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Genomic Medicine Program Advisory Committee]]established pursuant to the  Federal Advisory Committee Act (5 U.S.C. App.).
:(C) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Geriatrics and Gerontology Advisory Committee]]established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7315  section 7315of title 38, United States Code].
:(D) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Health Services Research and Development Service Scientific Merit Review Board]]established pursuant to the  Federal Advisory Committee Act (5 U.S.C. App.).
:(E) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Research Advisory Committee on Gulf War Veterans' Illnesses]]established by  section 707 of the Veterans Health Care Act of 1992 ( [[Public Law 102-585|Public Law 102–585]]  ;[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/527  38 U.S.C. 527note]).
:(F) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Special Medical Advisory Group]]established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7312  section 7312of title 38, United States Code].
:(G) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans' Advisory Committee on Rehabilitation]]established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3121  section 3121of title 38, United States Code].
:(H) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans' Rural Health Advisory Committee]]established pursuant to the  Federal Advisory Committee Act (5 U.S.C. App.).
:(I) Any other advisory committee the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines appropriate.

==(b) Report– ==

Not later than 30 days after carrying out subsection (a)(1) , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall—
:(1) submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Senate]]a report on the administrative and policy recommendations received under subsection (a)(1) , including any administrative or legislative actions required to carry out such recommendations; and
:(2) publish such report on the public Internet website of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
