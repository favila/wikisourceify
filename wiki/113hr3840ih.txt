{{Center|A BILL}}

 To establish the Office of Net Assessment within the Department of Defense.   

=Section 1. Office of Net Assessment=



==(a) Policy– ==

It is the policy of the United States to maintain an independent organization within the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] to develop and coordinate net assessments of the standing, trends, and future prospects of the military capabilities and potential of the United States in comparison with the military capabilities and potential of other countries or groups of countries so as to identify emerging or future threats or opportunities for the United States.

==(b) Establishment– ==



===(1) In general– ===

  Chapter 4of title 10, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

===Sec. 145. Office of Net Assessment===



====(a) In general– ====

There is in the [[w:Office of the Secretary of Defense|Office of the Secretary of Defense]] an office known as the [[w:Office of the Secretary of Defense|Office of Net Assessment]] .

====(b) Head– ====


:(1) The head of the [[w:Office of the Secretary of Defense|Office of Net Assessment]] shall be appointed by the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] . The head shall be a member of the Senior Executive Service.
:(2) The head of the [[w:Office of the Secretary of Defense|Office of Net Assessment]] may communicate views on matters within the responsibility of the head directly to the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] without obtaining the approval or concurrence of any other official within the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] .
:(3) The head of the [[w:Office of the Secretary of Defense|Office of Net Assessment]] shall report directly to the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] . The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] may not delegate the authority under this paragraph.

====(c) Responsibilities– ====

The [[w:Office of the Secretary of Defense|Office of Net Assessment]] shall develop and coordinate net assessments with respect to the standing, trends, and future prospects of the military capabilities and potential of the United States in comparison with the military capabilities and potential of other countries or groups of countries to identify emerging or future threats or opportunities for the United States.

====(d) Budget– ====

In the budget materials submitted to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] by the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] in connection with the submittal to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] , pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105  section 1105of title 31], of the budget for any fiscal year after fiscal year 2014, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] shall ensure that a separate, dedicated program element is assigned for the [[w:Office of the Secretary of Defense|Office of Net Assessment]] .

====(e) Net assessment defined– ====

In this section, the term net assessment means the comparative analysis of military, technological, political, economic, and other factors governing the relative military capability of nations. . 
</blockquote>
