{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to expand eligibility for reimbursement for emergency medical treatment and to require that the Department of Veterans Affairs be treated as a participating provider for the recovery of the costs of certain medical care, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Veterans Emergency Health Care Safety Net Expansion Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Expansion of emergency medical treatment reimbursement for certain veterans=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1725  Section 1725(b)of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1725 paragraph (1)], by striking“an active Department health-care participant”and inserting“enrolled in the health care system established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705 section 1705(a) of this title] ”;
:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1725 paragraph (2)]; and
:(3) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1725 paragraph (3)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1725 paragraph (2)]. 

=Sec. 3. Treatment of Department of Veterans Affairs as participating provider for recovery of costs of certain medical care=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1729  Section 1729(f)of title 38, United States Code], is amended by striking“(f) No law”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 
:(f) 
::(1) In the case of a veteran who is entitled to care (or payment of the expenses of care) under a health-plan contract, for purposes of reimbursement for providing care or services under this chapter to that veteran for a non-service-connected disability described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1729 subparagraph (D)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1729 (E) of subsection (a)(2)], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]shall be treated as a participating provider under that health-plan contract.
::(2) No law . 
</blockquote>
