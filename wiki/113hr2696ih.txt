{{Center|A BILL}}

To increase transparency of agencies by requiring a report describing any proposed conference.

=Section 1. Reports on Conference Attendance by Federal Agencies=



==(a) In General– ==

Not later than 30 days before any conference, the head of the agency hosting the conference and the head of any agency that is sending an employee of such agency to the conference shall submit to the relevant congressional committee of jurisdiction a report on the following:
:(1) An itemized statement of the cost of attending the conference, including the total cost.
:(2) In the case of the agency hosting the conference, the total cost of the conference.
:(3) The names of keynote speakers.
:(4) A list of conference attendees from such agency and the titles of such attendees.
:(5) The location of the conference.
:(6) A summary of the purposes and goals of the conference.
:(7) A conference itinerary.

==(b) Definitions– ==

In this section:

===(1) Agency– ===

The term agency has the meaning given that term in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 section 551 of title 5, United States Code] .

===(2) Conference– ===

The term “conference” means a meeting, retreat, seminar, symposium, training, or other event that involves travel for 50 or more attendees.

===(3) Employee– ===

The term employee has the meaning given that term in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2105 section 2105 of title 5, United States Code] .
