{{Center|A BILL}}

To restore certain authorities of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives to administer the firearms laws, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H9AE95439312B425781051AA47EF795AB}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Gun Transparency and Accountability (Gun TRAC) Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HB31305A08CDD449CA599FE4387D6C999}}Sec. 2. Use of firearm trace information in civil proceedings=

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the contents of the Firearms Trace System database maintained by the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|National Trace Center of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives]] shall not be immune from legal process, shall be subject to subpoena or other discovery, shall be admissible as evidence, and may be used, relied on, or disclosed in any manner, and testimony or other evidence may be permitted based on the data, on the same basis as other information, in a civil action in any State (including the District of Columbia) or Federal court or in an administrative proceeding.
