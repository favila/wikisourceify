{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Controlled Substances Act to provide enhanced penalties for marketing controlled substances to minors.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Saving Kids From Dangerous Drugs Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Offenses involving controlled substances marketed to minors=

 Section 401 of the Controlled Substances Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/841 21 U.S.C. 841] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(i) Offenses involving controlled substances marketed to minors– 
::(1) Unlawful act– Except as authorized under this title, including paragraph (3), it shall be unlawful for any person at least 18 years of age to—
:::(A) knowingly or intentionally manufacture or create a controlled substance listed in schedule I or II that is—
::::(i) combined with a beverage or candy product;
::::(ii) marketed or packaged to appear similar to a beverage or candy product; or
::::(iii) modified by flavoring or coloring; and
:::(B) know, or have reasonable cause to believe, that the combined, marketed, packaged, or modified controlled substance will be distributed, dispensed, or sold to a person under 18 years of age.
::(2) Penalties– Except as provided in section 418, 419, or 420, any person who violates paragraph (1)of this subsection shall be subject to—
:::(A) an additional term of imprisonment of not more than 10 years for a first offense involving the same controlled substance and schedule; and
:::(B) an additional term of imprisonment of not more than 20 years for a second or subsequent offense involving the same controlled substance and schedule.
::(3) Exceptions–  Paragraph (1)shall not apply to any controlled substance that—
:::(A) has been approved by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] under section 505 of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/355 21 U.S.C. 355] ), if the contents, marketing, and packaging of the controlled substance have not been altered from the form approved by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] ; or
:::(B) has been altered at the direction of a practitioner who is acting for a legitimate medical purpose in the usual course of professional practice..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Sentencing Guidelines=

Pursuant to its authority under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/994 section 994 of title 28, United States Code] , and in accordance with this section, the [[w:United States Sentencing Commission|United States Sentencing Commission]] shall review its guidelines and policy statements to ensure that the guidelines provide an appropriate additional penalty increase to the sentence otherwise applicable in Part D of the Guidelines Manual if the defendant was convicted of a violation of section 401(i) of the Controlled Substances Act, as added by section 2of this Act.
