        =={{section|H61D10793F42F4F5CB17797F15A8AA651|
      1.
      Short title
      This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Federal Hiring Freeze Act of
			 2013
        .
		}}==
    
    
        =={{section|H30E518A999CB4A598FBFB51F69950448|
      2.
      Reducing the
			 number of Federal employees
      
        Section 3101 of title 5, United States Code,
			 is amended—
			
        (1)
        by striking
			 Each Executive agency and inserting (a)
			 In
			 general.—Subject to subsection (b), each Executive
			 agency; and
			
      
        (2)
        by adding at the
			 end the following:
				
					
            (b)
            Reduction in
				number of employees
						
              (1)
              Hiring
				freeze
              During each fiscal
				year which begins after the date of the enactment of this subsection and for
				which the 
                [[Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]]
             projects that there
				will be a Federal budget deficit, the head of an Executive agency may not
				appoint an individual to any position in the agency, except as otherwise
				provided in this subsection.
						
            
              (2)
              Exemptions
              The 
                [[Executive Office of the President|President]]
             may waive the application of
				paragraph (1) in any of the following circumstances or for any of the following
				reasons:
							
                (A)
                The existence of a
				state of war or other vital national security concern.
							
              
                (B)
                To make an
				appointment (including to a position within the 
                [[Federal Bureau of Prisons|Bureau of Prisons]]
            ) for purposes
				relating to Federal law enforcement.
							
              
                (C)
                To carry out a
				written commitment made by the Government on or before the date of the
				enactment of this subsection.
							
              
                (D)
                To make 1 or more
				appointments involving only the reassignment of personnel within the same
				agency.
							
              
                (E)
                The appointment is
				to a position under the Executive Schedule.
							
              
                (F)
                The appointment is
				of a short-term, seasonal, or intermittent nature and consistent with the
				agency’s hiring pattern and personnel levels of the previous fiscal
				year.
							
              
                (G)
                The appointment is
				to a position in the 
                [[Executive Office of the President|Executive Office of the President]]
             and necessary to
				facilitate the orderly transition and operation of a new Presidential
				administration.
							
              
                (H)
                The appointment is
				to a position within the 
                [[United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]]
             or the Postal Regulatory
				Commission.
							
              
                (I)
                The appointment is
				to a position within the 
                [[Federal Aviation Administration|Federal Aviation Administration]]
             as an air traffic
				controller.
							
            
            
              (3)
              Reports to
				Congress
							
                (A)
                In
				general
                Not later than 90 days after the end of each quarter of a
				fiscal year, the 
                [[Executive Office of the President|President]]
             shall provide 
                [[United States Congress|Congress]]
             with a report containing a
				list of each appointment approved by the 
                [[Executive Office of the President|President]]
             under this subsection during
				the quarter, broken down by Executive agency.
							
              
                (B)
                Information
				included
                The list contained in a report provided to 
                [[United States Congress|Congress]]
            
				under subparagraph (A) shall include a description of the position associated
				with each approved appointment, but shall not provide any information that may
				be used to identify the individual appointed to the position.
							
              
                (C)
                Public
				dissemination
                The 
                [[Executive Office of the President|President]]
             shall make each report provided to
				
                [[United States Congress|Congress]]
             under subparagraph (A) available to the public by posting the report
				on the official public website of the 
                [[Executive Office of the President|Executive Office of the President]]
             in a
				searchable, sortable, and down­load­able manner.
							
            
            
              (4)
              Termination
              
                Paragraph
				(1) shall cease to apply after the date as of which the 
                [[Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office
				of Management and Budget]]
             determines that a Federal budget deficit no longer
				exists.
						
          
          .
