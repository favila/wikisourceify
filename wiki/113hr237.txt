{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 5, United States Code, to reduce the
		  number of civil service positions within the executive branch, and for other
		  purposes.

={{anchor|H61D10793F42F4F5CB17797F15A8AA651}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Federal Hiring Freeze Act of
			 2013”.

={{anchor|H30E518A999CB4A598FBFB51F69950448}}Sec. 2. Reducing the
			 number of Federal employees=

Section 3101 of title 5, United States Code,
			 is amended—
:(1) by striking
			 “Each Executive agency” and inserting “(a)
			 In
			 general.—Subject to subsection (b), each Executive
			 agency”; and
:(2) by adding at the
			 end the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H21C65DC247D74732B6867E439103E111}}(b) Reduction in
				number of employees– ===



===={{anchor|H7FB53BBE1FCF472184FC38506BF5B28E}}(1) Hiring
				freeze– ====

During each fiscal
				year which begins after the date of the enactment of this subsection and for
				which the [[Wikipedia:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] projects that there
				will be a Federal budget deficit, the head of an Executive agency may not
				appoint an individual to any position in the agency, except as otherwise
				provided in this subsection.

===={{anchor|H4890BB7A8E8F4B9FAB1D48C2ADAD68A2}}(2) Exemptions– ====

The [[Wikipedia:Executive Office of the President|President]] may waive the application of
				paragraph (1) in any of the following circumstances or for any of the following
				reasons:
:(A) The existence of a
				state of war or other vital national security concern.
:(B) To make an
				appointment (including to a position within the [[Wikipedia:Federal Bureau of Prisons|Bureau of Prisons]]) for purposes
				relating to Federal law enforcement.
:(C) To carry out a
				written commitment made by the Government on or before the date of the
				enactment of this subsection.
:(D) To make 1 or more
				appointments involving only the reassignment of personnel within the same
				agency.
:(E) The appointment is
				to a position under the Executive Schedule.
:(F) The appointment is
				of a short-term, seasonal, or intermittent nature and consistent with the
				agency’s hiring pattern and personnel levels of the previous fiscal
				year.
:(G) The appointment is
				to a position in the [[Wikipedia:Executive Office of the President|Executive Office of the President]] and necessary to
				facilitate the orderly transition and operation of a new Presidential
				administration.
:(H) The appointment is
				to a position within the [[Wikipedia:United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]] or the Postal Regulatory
				Commission.
:(I) The appointment is
				to a position within the [[Wikipedia:Federal Aviation Administration|Federal Aviation Administration]] as an air traffic
				controller.

===={{anchor|H11CA5CE4E52E4ABB8F3CC75120ED8A13}}(3) Reports to
				Congress– ====



====={{anchor|H592BB86AD1F140EEBFA1B3EA4E24DD4E}}(A) In
				general– =====

Not later than 90 days after the end of each quarter of a
				fiscal year, the [[Wikipedia:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall provide [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress]] with a report containing a
				list of each appointment approved by the [[Wikipedia:Executive Office of the President|President]] under this subsection during
				the quarter, broken down by Executive agency.

====={{anchor|HEF356E3C6DB841C8A60146997990592C}}(B) Information
				included– =====

The list contained in a report provided to [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress]]
				under subparagraph (A) shall include a description of the position associated
				with each approved appointment, but shall not provide any information that may
				be used to identify the individual appointed to the position.

====={{anchor|HC8BBD09DF8474B1D896E7BAF6D921E37}}(C) Public
				dissemination– =====

The [[Wikipedia:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall make each report provided to
				[[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress]] under subparagraph (A) available to the public by posting the report
				on the official public website of the [[Wikipedia:Executive Office of the President|Executive Office of the President]] in a
				searchable, sortable, and down­load­able manner.

===={{anchor|H8BFD8CDE5C9541FDAA7B923227BE0AF2}}(4) Termination– ====

Paragraph
				(1) shall cease to apply after the date as of which the [[Wikipedia:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office
				of Management and Budget]] determines that a Federal budget deficit no longer
				exists..
</blockquote>
