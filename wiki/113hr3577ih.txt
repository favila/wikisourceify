{{Center|A BILL}}

To establish the Commission on Health Care Savings through Innovative Wireless Technologies.

=Section 1. Establishment=

There is established in the [[w:United States Congress|legislative branch]] a commission to be known as the “ [[w:United States Congress|Commission on Health Care Savings through Innovative Wireless Technologies]] ” (in this Act referred to as the “Commission” ).

=Sec. 2. Duties of Commission=

The duties of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] shall be the following:
:(1) Examine the cost savings to the United States health care system, if any, that can be achieved by increasing the use of wireless health information technologies (including technologies related to digital health, mobile health (mHealth), telehealth, telemedicine, e-Care, remote patient monitoring, and the collection of patient-generated health data) by patients, caregivers, and health care providers.
:(2) Examine existing scientific research studying the medical effectiveness of wireless health information technologies that deliver health care.
:(3) Examine existing payment models and incentive payment programs that provide Federal financial reimbursement or funding for the use of wireless health information technologies.
:(4) Examine options for [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] and for appropriate Federal agencies to incentivize and promote innovation and technological advancements in the area of wireless health information technologies.
:(5) Examine barriers to marketplace entry, whether technical or systemic, that impede efforts by persons and entities to develop new wireless health information technologies and to improve existing wireless health information technologies.
:(6) Identify appropriate situations for the integration of wireless health information technologies into Federal health care programs, and recommend methods for integrating such technologies into such programs.
:(7) Develop a proposal based on the findings of its examinations under this section for the establishment, implementation, and financing of a comprehensive program to encourage the further integration of wireless health information technologies into existing Federal health care programs.
:(8) Develop cost estimate approaches that the [[w:Congressional Budget Office|Congressional Budget Office]] can consider utilizing in order to more accurately assess the cost savings that the Federal Government can achieve by increasing the use of wireless health information technologies by patients, caregivers, and health care providers in the United States.

=Sec. 3. Membership=



==(a) Number and appointment– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] shall be composed of 19 individuals (such as the individuals described in subsection (b)) who have demonstrated experience or expertise with respect to wireless health information technologies and that are appointed not later than 45 days after the date of the enactment of this Act as follows:
:(1) Three members appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] .
:(2) Two members appointed by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives]] .
:(3) Two members appointed by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|minority leader of the House of Representatives]] .
:(4) Two members appointed by the [[w:United States Senate|majority leader of the Senate]] .
:(5) Two members appointed by the [[w:United States Senate|minority leader of the Senate]] .
:(6) Four members appointed by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] .
:(7) Four members appointed by the [[w:Federal Communications Commission|Chairman of the Federal Communications Commission]] .

==(b) Examples of individuals– ==

For purposes of subsection (a), individuals described in this subsection are—
:(1) representatives of—
::(A) health care providers;
::(B) group health plans, health insurance coverage offered in the group or individual market, and other third-party payers;
::(C) health information technology vendors;
::(D) small businesses or startup companies in the wireless health information technologies industry;
::(E) major research and academic institutions; and
::(F) patient advocacy groups;
:(2) health care professionals;
:(3) venture capital investors; and
:(4) individuals who have received health care treatment that included the use of wireless health information technologies and caregivers of such individuals.

==(c) Prohibition on Federal officers, employees, and Members of Congress serving as members– ==

No members appointed under subsection (a)may be officers or employees of the Federal Government or Members of Congress.

==(d) Terms– ==

Each member shall be appointed for the life of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] .

==(e) Basic pay and travel expenses– ==

Members shall serve without pay and without receipt of travel expenses.

==(f) Quorum– ==

Nine members of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] shall constitute a quorum but a lesser number may hold hearings.

==(g) Chairperson– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Chairperson of the Commission]] shall be elected by the members.

==(h) Meetings– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] shall meet at the call of the Chairperson and may not meet fewer than nine times.

=Sec. 4. Director and staff of Commission; experts and consultants=



==(a) Director– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] shall have a Director who shall be appointed by the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] to the extent or in the amounts provided in advance in appropriation Acts, the Director shall be paid at the rate of basic pay for level 4 of the Executive Schedule.

==(b) Staff– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] may appoint personnel as it considers appropriate.

==(c) Applicability of certain civil service laws– ==

The Director and staff of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] shall be appointed subject to the provisions of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/ title 5, United States Code] , governing appointments in the competitive service, and shall be paid in accordance with the provisions ofchapter 51andsubchapter III of chapter 53 of that titlerelating to classification and General Schedule pay rates.

==(d) Experts and consultants– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] may procure temporary and intermittent services under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3109 section 3109(b)of title 5, United States Code] .

==(e) Staff of Federal agencies– ==

Upon request of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] , the head of any Federal department or agency may detail, on a reimbursable basis, any of the personnel of that department or agency to the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] to assist it in carrying out its duties under this Act.

=Sec. 5. Powers of Commission=



==(a) Hearings and sessions– ==

Subject to rules prescribed by the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] , the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] may, for the purpose of carrying out this Act, hold hearings, sit and act at times and places, take testimony, and receive evidence as the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] considers appropriate.

==(b) Powers of members and agents– ==

Any member or agent of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] may, if authorized by the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] , take any action which the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] is authorized to take by this section.

==(c) Obtaining official data– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] may secure directly from any department or agency of the United States information necessary to enable it to carry out this Act. Upon request of the [[w:United States Congress|Chairperson of the Commission]] , the head of that department or agency shall furnish that information to the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] .

==(d) Gifts, bequests, and devises– ==

To the extent or in the amounts provided in advance in appropriation Acts, the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] may accept, use, and dispose of gifts, bequests, or devises of services or property, both real and personal, for the purpose of aiding or facilitating the work of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] . Gifts, bequests, or devises of money and proceeds from sales of other property received as gifts, bequests, or devises shall be deposited in the Treasury and shall be available for disbursement upon order of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] .

==(e) Mails– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] may use the United States mails in the same manner and under the same conditions as other departments and agencies of the United States.

==(f) Administrative support services– ==

Upon the request of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] , the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] shall provide to the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] , on a reimbursable basis, the administrative support services necessary for the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] to carry out its responsibilities under this Act.

==(g) Contract authority– ==

To the extent or in the amounts provided in advance in appropriation Acts, the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] may contract with and compensate government and private agencies or persons for services, without regard to section 3709 of the Revised Statutes( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/41/5 41 U.S.C. 5] ).

=Sec. 6. Reports=



==(a) Interim reports– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] shall submit to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] , the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] , and such agencies as the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] determines to be appropriate an interim report not later than 9 months after the final member is appointed to the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] and such other interim reports as the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] considers appropriate.

==(b) Final report– ==

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] shall transmit a final report to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] , the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] , and such agencies as the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] determines to be appropriate not later than 18 months after the final member is appointed to the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] . The final report shall contain a detailed statement of the findings and conclusions of the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] , together with its recommendations for legislation and administrative actions the [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] considers appropriate.

=Sec. 7. Termination=

The [[w:United States Congress|Commission]] shall terminate on the date that is 60 days after submitting its final report pursuant to section 6(b).
