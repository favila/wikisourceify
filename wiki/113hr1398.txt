{{Center|A BILL}}

To facilitate the development of offshore wind energy resources.

={{anchor|H9A6A84796E364EDC93147AA28041C0D0}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited at the“Advancing Offshore Wind Production Act”.

={{anchor|HF11B2B21729745F19C365F21B5E53B2C}}Sec. 2. Offshore meteorological site testing and monitoring projects=



=={{anchor|H61F3F735D78F4E8DBD08CC13EC5857C5}}(a) Definition of an offshore meteorological site testing and monitoring project– ==

In this section, the term offshore meteorological site testing and monitoring project means a project carried out on or in the waters of the Outer Continental Shelf administered by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Department of the Interior]] to test or monitor weather (including wind, tidal, current, and solar energy) using towers, buoys, or other temporary ocean infrastructure, that—
:(1) causes—
::(A) less than 1 acre of surface or seafloor disruption at the location of each meteorological tower or other device; and
::(B) not more than 5 acres of surface or seafloor disruption within the proposed area affected by for the project (including hazards to navigation);
:(2) is decommissioned not more than 5 years after the date of commencement of the project, including—
::(A) removal of towers, buoys, or other temporary ocean infrastructure from the project site; and
::(B) restoration of the project site to approximately the original condition of the site; and
:(3) provides meteorological information obtained by the project to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] .

=={{anchor|H8D56606C51994DA6979F5C198729467A}}(b) Offshore meteorological project permitting– ==



==={{anchor|H5B2B1B185B464EAAA6D4CF0C2DCF8519}}(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall by regulation require that any applicant seeking to conduct an offshore meteorological site testing and monitoring project on the outer Continental Shelf (as that term is defined in the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1331 43 U.S.C. 1331 et seq.] ] )) must obtain a permit and right of way for the project in accordance with this subsection.

==={{anchor|H205653EBF30948349A58504F72939051}}(2) Permit and right of way timeline and conditions– ===



===={{anchor|H72FCBE7F0C734A579BFBBA11725F3608}}(A) Deadline for approval– ====

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall decide whether to issue a permit and right of way for an offshore meteorological site testing and monitoring project within 30 days after receiving an application.

===={{anchor|HEC1FFFD454EC45D9928B3B2D7BB49BBF}}(B) Public comment and consultation– ====

During the period referred to in subparagraph (A), the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall—
:(i) provide an opportunity for submission of comments by the public; and
:(ii) consult with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] , the [[w:United States Coast Guard|Commandant of the Coast Guard]] , and the heads of other Federal, State, and local agencies that would be affected by issuance of the permit and right of way.

===={{anchor|H86C0F5787E914806B7BBC2D138E547AD}}(C) Denial of permit; opportunity to remedy deficiencies– ====

If the application is denied, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall provide the applicant—
:(i) in writing, clear and comprehensive reasons why the application was not approved and detailed information concerning any deficiencies in the application; and
:(ii) an opportunity to remedy such deficiencies.

=={{anchor|HC6587677DE314A569CAF1C686EA34172}}(c) NEPA exclusion– ==

 Section 102(2)(C) of the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4332 42 U.S.C. 4332(2)(C)] ] )shall not apply with respect to an offshore meteorological site testing and monitoring project.

=={{anchor|HC10D5075E48E496C98B6FA19F601B9D6}}(d) Protection of information– ==

The information provided to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] pursuant to subsection (a)(3)shall be treated by the Secretary as proprietary information and protected against disclosure.
