{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 39, United States Code, to provide that the United States Postal Service may provide certain basic financial services, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “   Providing Opportunities for Savings, Transactions, and Lending Act of 2014   ”or the“  POSTAL Act of 2014  ”.  

=Sec. 2. Authority for the Postal Service to offer certain financial services=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404  Section 404(a)of title 39, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 paragraph (7)], by striking“and”at the end;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 paragraph (8)], by striking the period at the end and inserting“; and”; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
::(9) to provide basic financial services, including—
:::(A) small-dollar loans;
:::(B) alone, or in partnership with depository institutions (as defined under  section 3 of the Federal Deposit Insurance Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/1813 12 U.S.C. 1813] )) and credit unions—
::::(i) checking accounts and interest-bearing savings accounts; and
::::(ii) services relating to international money transfers;
:::(C) such other basic financial services as the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]determines appropriate in the public interest; and
:::(D) the creation of a“Postal Card”that allows users to engage in the financial services described under this paragraph. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Technical and conforming amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404  Section 404(e)(2)of title 39, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following:“The preceding sentence shall not apply to any financial service offered by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 subsection (a)(9)].”.
