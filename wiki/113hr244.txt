{{Center|A BILL}}

To assure that the services of a nonemergency department
		  physician are available to hospital patients 24 hours a day, seven days a week
		  in all non-Federal hospitals with at least 100 licensed beds.

={{anchor|H144A0A5FAF3649748329B222639E2930}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Physician Availability Act of
			 2013”.

={{anchor|H6831EF5483F44EB09B1F4F0A4CE5A069}}Sec. 2. Requirement for
			 physician availability in acute care hospitals=



=={{anchor|H94DF864CF55441FD90AEA57FA9730DD8}}(a) In
			 General– ==

Each covered hospital shall have a qualified physician
			 available in the hospital 24 hours a day, seven days a week to attend to the
			 needs of inpatients of the hospital.

=={{anchor|HDDEC604C66FB45CDA09F0D15207504DE}}(b) Definitions– ==

For
			 purposes of this section:

==={{anchor|HCE879ED4C4454EE48D9053DAD8FB9896}}(1) Covered
			 hospital– ===



===={{anchor|HAE0E4A12522141219E17F0BC9CF9A925}}(A) In
			 general– ====

Subject to subparagraph (B), the term covered
			 hospital means a subsection (d) hospital (as defined in section
			 1886(d)(1)(B) of the Social Security
			 Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1395ww 42 U.S.C. 1395ww(d)(1)(B)])) that—
:(i) has
			 a participation agreement in effect under section 1866 of such Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1395cc 42 U.S.C.
			 1395cc]);
:(ii) is
			 participating in the program under title XIX of such Act; or
:(iii) is
			 receiving Federal funds under a grant or cooperative agreement.

===={{anchor|H5DF1A61B0E8849BD9CD5DC1F8EE19B4F}}(B) Exclusion for
			 federal facilities and small hospitals– ====

Such term does not include
			 a hospital that—
:(i) is a
			 facility of the Federal Government; or
:(ii) the
			 [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] determines has fewer than 100 licensed
			 beds (as defined by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]).

==={{anchor|HDD4F63E06C4D461BAB9661196C2F9F39}}(2) Physician;
			 qualified physician– ===


:(A) The term
			 physician means, with respect to a hospital, an individual who is
			 a doctor of medicine or osteopathy legally authorized under State law to
			 practice medicine and surgery in that hospital.
:(B) The term qualified physician
			 means, with respect to a hospital, an individual who is a physician and whose
			 credentials as such a physician have been verified by the administration of the
			 hospital (before providing any services at the hospital) through appropriate
			 means, including verification through the National Practitioner
			 Databank.

==={{anchor|H7E65BF3318764631903EE72B5BE5FE29}}(3) Physician
			 availability– ===

A physician is considered to be
			 “available” in a hospital if—
:(A) the physician is
			 physically present in the hospital;
:(B) the physician’s
			 primary responsibility is to be in attendance to serve the needs of the
			 hospital’s inpatients without delay; and
:(C) the physician is
			 not physically present in, assigned to, serving in, or expected to cover, the
			 hospital’s emergency room or emergency department.

=={{anchor|HC19EFC2EA52D451B93E670774CB4BB16}}(c) Enforcement– ==



==={{anchor|H476137938BF04667AB6B6C8CCEBB2B28}}(1) Warning– ===

If
			 the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] (in this section referred to as the
			 “
              Secretary
            ”) determines that a hospital has violated subsection
			 (a), in the first instance the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] shall provide a written warning
			 regarding such violation to the hospital and shall notify the [[w:|Inspector General
			 of the Department of Health and Human Services]] (in this section referred to as
			 the “
              HHS Inspector General
            ”) of such violation. Subsequently, the
			 [[w:|HHS Inspector General]] shall monitor the compliance of the hospital with the
			 requirement of subsection (a).

==={{anchor|HF4EB22107C48456799BFB0AE35729200}}(2) Second
			 violation– ===

After providing a warning to a hospital under paragraph
			 (1), if the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] determines that the hospital subsequently and knowingly
			 violates subsection (a)—
:(A) the hospital is
			 subject to a civil money penalty in an amount not to exceed $100,000,
			 and
:(B) the hospital shall
			 submit to the [[w:|HHS Inspector General]], by not later than 30 days after the date
			 of such a determination, a remedial plan to prevent future violations of the
			 requirement of such subsection.The
			 provisions of section 1128A of the Social Security Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1320a–7a 42 U.S.C. 1320a–7a]),
			 other than subsections (a) and (b) of such section, shall apply to civil money
			 penalties under subparagraph (A) in the same manner as they apply to a penalty
			 or proceeding under subsection (a) of such section.

==={{anchor|H9A736B05F52B4613B2BD3CB5EE406280}}(3) Subsequent
			 violations– ===

After imposing a civil money penalty under paragraph
			 (2) against a hospital, if the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] determines that the hospital
			 subsequently and knowingly violates subsection (a), the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] may issue an
			 order disqualifying the hospital from participation in the programs under
			 titles XVIII and XIX of the Social Security
			 Act and from receipt of Federal funds under any grant or cooperative
			 agreement for such period as the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] specifies and until the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]
			 receives satisfactory assurances that the hospital will be in substantial
			 compliance with the requirement of subsection (a).

==={{anchor|H08B4EAAF2278408F972584CD15B6935B}}(4) Failure to
			 submit or comply with remedial plan– ===

If the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] determines,
			 after consultation with the [[w:|HHS Inspector General]], that a hospital has failed
			 to submit a satisfactory remedial plan required under paragraph (2)(B) or is
			 failing to substantially carry out such a plan, the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] may suspend
			 payment of funds to the hospital under titles XVIII and XIX of the
			 Social Security Act and under Federal
			 grants or cooperative agreements until the Secretary receives satisfactory
			 assurances that such failures will not continue.

=={{anchor|HC32C4E0FE87545C98589E6688816DD0E}}(d) Effective
			 Date– ==

This section shall take effect on the first day of the first
			 month that begins more than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this
			 Act.
