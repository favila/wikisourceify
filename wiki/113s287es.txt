 To amend title 38, United States Code, to improve assistance to homeless veterans, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Helping Homeless Veterans Act of 2013 ”.

=Sec. 6. Requirement for Department of Veterans Affairs to assess comprehensive service programs for homeless veterans=


:(a) In general– Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall assess and measure the capacity of programs for which entities receive grants under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2011  section 2011of title 38, United States Code], or per diem payments under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 section 2012]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2061 2061 of such title].
:(b) Assessment at national and local levels– In assessing and measuring under subsection (a) , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall develop and use tools to examine the capacity of programs described in such subsection at both the national and local level in order to assess the following:
::(1) Whether sufficient capacity exists to meet the needs of homeless veterans in each geographic area.
::(2) Whether existing capacity meets the needs of the subpopulations of homeless veterans located in each geographic area.
::(3) The amount of capacity that recipients of grants under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2011 sections 2011]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2061 2061]and per diem payments under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 section 2012 of such]title have to provide services for which the recipients are eligible to receive per diem under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 section 2012(a)(2)(B)(ii) of title 38, United States Code], as added by section 4(5)(B) .
:(c) Use of information– The Secretary shall use the information collected under this section as follows:
::(1) To set specific goals to ensure that programs described in subsection (a) are effectively serving the needs of homeless veterans.
::(2) To assess whether programs described in subsection (a) are meeting goals set under paragraph (1) .
::(3) To inform funding allocations for programs described in subsection (a) .
::(4) To improve the referral of homeless veterans to programs described in subsection (a) .
:(d) Report– Not later than 180 days after the date on which the assessment required by subsection (b) is completed, the Secretary shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the Senate]] and the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the House of Representatives]] a report on such assessment and such recommendations for legislative and administrative action as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may have to improve the programs and per diem payments described in subsection (a) .

=Sec. 8. Partnerships with public and private entities to provide legal services to homeless veterans and veterans at risk of homelessness=


:(a) In general–   Chapter 20of title 38, United States Code, is amended by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2022 section 2022]the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 2022A. Partnerships with public and private entities to provide legal services to homeless veterans and veterans at risk of homelessness==


:(a) Partnerships authorized– Subject to the availability of funds for that purpose, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may enter into partnerships with public or private entities to fund a portion of the general legal services specified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2022A subsection (c)]that are provided by such entities to homeless veterans and veterans at risk of homelessness.
:(b) Locations– The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall ensure that, to the extent practicable, partnerships under this section are made with entities equitably distributed across the geographic regions of the United States, including rural communities, tribal lands of the United States, Native Americans, and tribal organizations (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3765  section 3765of title 38, United States Code]).
:(c) Legal services– Legal services specified in this subsection include legal services provided by public or private entities that address the needs of homeless veterans and veterans at risk of homelessness as follows:
::(1) Legal services related to housing, including eviction defense and representation in landlord-tenant cases.
::(2) Legal services related to family law, including assistance in court proceedings for child support, divorce, and estate planning.
::(3) Legal services related to income support, including assistance in obtaining public benefits.
::(4) Legal services related to criminal defense, including defense in matters symptomatic of homelessness, such as outstanding warrants, fines, and driver's license revocation, to reduce recidivism and facilitate the overcoming of reentry obstacles in employment or housing.
:(d) Consultation– In developing and carrying out partnerships under this section, the Secretary shall, to the extent practicable, consult with public and private entities—
::(1) for assistance in identifying and contacting organizations capable of providing the legal services described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2022A subsection (c)]; and
::(2) to coordinate appropriate outreach relationships with such organizations.
:(e) Reports– The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may require entities that have entered into partnerships under this section to submit to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] periodic reports on legal services provided to homeless veterans and veterans at risk of homelessness pursuant to such partnerships.

===(f) Sunset– ===

The authority of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] to enter into partnerships under this section as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2022A subsection (a)]shall expire on December 31, 2016. . 
</blockquote>

:(b) Clerical amendment– The table of sections at the beginning ofchapter 20 of such titleis amended by adding after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2022 section 2022]the following new item:

<blockquote>  2022A. Partnerships with public and private entities to provide legal services to homeless veterans and veterans at risk of homelessness.  . 
</blockquote>
