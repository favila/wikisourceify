{{Center|A BILL}}

To ensure orderly conduct of Nuclear Regulatory Commission actions.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Nuclear Regulatory Commission Reorganization Plan Codification and Complements Act” .

==Title I— Replacement of Reorganization Plan==



==Sec. 101. General functions==



===(a) Functions– ===

Those functions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Nuclear Regulatory Commission]] (in this title referred to as the Commission ) concerned with—
:(1) policy formulation;
:(2) rulemaking, as defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553 section 553of title 5 of the United States Code] , except that those matters set forth in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553 553 (a)(2)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553 (b)] which do not pertain to policy formulation orders or adjudications shall be reserved to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ]] ;
:(3) orders and adjudications, as defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 section 551 (6)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 (7) of title 5 of the United States Code] ; and
:(4) approving the distribution of appropriated funds according to programs and purposes proposed by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] ,shall remain vested in the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] . A majority of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may determine, in an area of doubt, whether any matter, action, question, or area of inquiry pertains to one of these functions. Any member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may request such a vote. Any member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may propose a policy matter for consideration by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] . All members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall have full, unfettered, timely, and equal access to information pertaining to its functions. The performance of any portion of these functions may be delegated by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] to a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , including the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman of the Commission]] (in this title referred to as the Chairman ) and to the staff.

===(b) Officers and employees– ===



====(1) Officers– ====

With respect to the following officers or successor officers duly established by statute or by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , the Chairman shall initiate the appointment, subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , and the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] or a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may initiate an action for removal, subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] by majority vote:
:(A)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] .
:(B)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chief]] and [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Deputy Chief Financial Officer]] .
:(C)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|General Counsel]] .
:(D)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Commission Appellate Adjudication]] .
:(E)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Secretary of the Commission]] .
:(F)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Public Affairs]] .
:(G)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Congressional Affairs]] .
:(H)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of International Programs]] .
:(I)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chief Administrative Judge]] and members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Atomic Safety and Licensing Board Panel]] .Any performance evaluation or rating of the officers listed in subparagraphs (A) through (I)shall be determined by a majority vote of the members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

====(2) Replacement of officers– ====


:(A) In the event of a vacancy in a position described in paragraph (1), the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may designate an acting officer for a maximum of 60 days, after which any further extension must be approved by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] . If, at the end of 60 days, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] has not approved the appointment of an officer proposed by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , or the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] has not proposed one, any Commissioner may initiate the appointment subject to approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .
:(B) With respect to the following officers or successor officers duly established by statute or by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , after consultation with the Executive Director for Operations, shall initiate the appointment, subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , and the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , or a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may initiate an action for removal, subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] by majority vote:
::(i)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Nuclear Reactor Regulation]] .
::(ii)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Nuclear Material Safety and Safeguards]] .
::(iii)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Nuclear Regulatory Research]] .
::(iv)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Nuclear Security and Incident Response]] .
::(v)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of New Reactors]] .
::(vi)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Federal and State Materials and Environmental Management Programs]] .
::(vii)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Investigations]] .
::(viii)  [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Director of the Office of Enforcement]] .

====(3) Appointment of Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards– ====

The Chairman or a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall initiate the appointment of the Members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards]] , subject to the approval of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] . The provisions for appointment of the Chairman of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards]] and the term of the members shall not be affected by the provisions of this title.

====(4) Delegation of staff supervision functions– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall delegate the function of appointing, removing, and supervising the staff of the following offices or successor offices to the respective heads of such offices: [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|General Counsel]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Secretary of the Commission]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chief Financial Officer]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of Commission Appellate Adjudication]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of Congressional Affairs]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of Public Affairs]] , and [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Office of International Programs]] . The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall delegate the functions of appointing, removing, and supervising the staff of the following panels and committee to the respective Chairmen thereof: [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Atomic Safety and Licensing Board Panel]] and [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards]] .

===(c) Commission member offices– ===

Each member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall appoint, remove, and supervise the personnel employed in his or her immediate office.

===(d) Performance of functions– ===

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall act as provided by section 201(a)(1) of the Energy Reorganization Act of 1974( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/5841 42 U.S.C. 5841(a)(1)] ] )in the performance of its functions as described in subsections (a)and (b) of this section.

==Sec. 102. Chairman==



===(a) Functions– ===

Except as otherwise provided in section 101, all functions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall rest with the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] . The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall be the official spokesman for the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] and, as such, shall represent the policies determined by a majority of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

===(b) Additional functions– ===

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall also be the principal executive officer of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , and shall be responsible to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] for assuring that the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] and the staff of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] (other than the officers and staff referred to in section 101 (b)(4)and (c)) are responsive to the requirements of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] in the performance of its functions; shall determine the use and expenditure of funds of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , in accordance with the distribution of appropriated funds according to programs and purposes approved by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ; shall present to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] for its consideration the proposals set forth in paragraph (3); and shall be responsible for the following functions, which the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall delegate, subject to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman’s]] direction and supervision, to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] unless otherwise provided by this Act:
:(1) Administrative functions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .
:(2) Distribution of business among such personnel and among administrative units and offices of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .
:(3) Preparation of proposals for the reorganization of the major offices of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .
:(4) Appointing and removing, without any further action by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , all officers and employees under the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] other than those whose appointment and removal are specifically provided for by section 101 (b)and (c).

===(c) Governing principles– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The Chairman as principal executive officer and the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] shall be governed by the general policies of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] and by such regulatory decisions, findings, and determinations, including those for reorganization proposals, budget revisions, and distribution of appropriated funds, as the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may by law, including this title, be authorized to make.

====(2) Full and current information– ====

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] and the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] shall have joint responsibility insuring that the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] is fully and currently informed about matters within its functions.

====(3) Failure to act in accordance– ====

If a majority of Commissioners determine that the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] has not acted in accordance with paragraph (1)or (2), such Commissioners shall provide written notice of the determination to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] and provide copies thereof to the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] .

==Sec. 103. Emergency authority==



===(a) In general– ===

Notwithstanding sections 101and 102, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] is authorized to exercise emergency authority described in paragraph (4), subject to the following limitations:
:(1) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may not exercise emergency authority unless and until the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] declares a specific emergency exists and, not later than 24 hours after such declaration, notifies—
::(A) the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] , in writing; and
::(B) the public.
:(2) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may only exercise emergency authority in response to—
::(A) an imminent safety threat pertaining to a facility or materials licensed or regulated by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ; or
::(B) a determination by the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] , the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]] , the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] , the [[w:Federal Bureau of Investigation|Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation]] , the [[w:Central Intelligence Agency|Director of the Central Intelligence Agency]] , or the [[w:|Director of National Intelligence]] of an imminent security threat to a facility or materials licensed or regulated by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .Where authority is exercised pursuant to this section, public notification may be delayed provided that the Chairman determines that prior public disclosure would constitute a risk to public health and safety and so notifies the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] .
:(3) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may only exercise emergency authority for the duration of the emergency or 30 days, whichever is less. The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may approve extensions of that time. Each extension is limited to 30 days and requires notification of the public, the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] .
:(4) The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman’s]] emergency authority includes the functions of responding to, issuing orders respecting, advising United States civil authorities and the United States public about, and directing and coordinating actions relative to such emergency incident.

===(b) Delegation– ===

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may delegate the authority to perform such emergency functions, in whole or in part, to any of the other members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] . Such authority may also be delegated or redelegated, in whole or in part, to the staff of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

===(c) Consultation– ===

To the extent practicable, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall consult with the full [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] on any regulatory or policy actions to be taken under an emergency. Such consultations shall be exempt from the requirements of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552b section 552bof title 5, United States Code] (commonly referred to as the Government in the Sunshine Act ).

===(d) Guidelines and notice– ===

In acting under this section, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , or other member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] delegated authority under subsection (b), shall conform to the policy guidelines of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] .

===(e) Termination of emergency– ===

Upon termination of the emergency, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] shall immediately notify the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , the public, the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] .

===(f) Report– ===

Within 30 days following the conclusion of the emergency, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , or the member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] or member of the staff delegated the emergency functions under subsection (b), shall render a complete report of all actions taken during the emergency, specifically delineating actions taken utilizing the authority provided in this section, to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , the [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] .

===(g) Commission procedures– ===

Not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall revise its procedures to comply with the requirements of this section. Such revision shall define the roles of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] ers during an emergency, specifying—
:(1) complete access to records and information relating to actions taken during the emergency;
:(2) complete access to [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] staff involved in the management of the emergency;
:(3) complete access to the location or locations where decisions are made during the emergency; and
:(4) participation in decisions that may affect [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] actions and policies beyond the response to a particular emergency to the extent practicable.

==Sec. 104. Reporting==



===(a) Delegation; direct communication– ===

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] may make such delegations and provide for such reporting as the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] deems necessary, subject to provisions of law. Any officer or employee under the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] may communicate directly to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , or to any member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , whenever in the view of such officer or employee a critical problem, or matter of public health and safety or common defense and security, is not being properly addressed.

===(b) Executive Director for Operations– ===

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] shall report for all matters to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] .

===(c) Functions– ===

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Directors of Nuclear Reactor Regulations]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Nuclear Material Safety and Safeguards]] , and [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Nuclear Regulatory Research]] shall report to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Executive Director for Operations]] .

===(d) Direct reporting– ===

The heads of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] level offices or successor offices, of [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|General Counsel]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Secretary of the Commission]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission Appellate Adjudication]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Congressional Affairs]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Public Affairs]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|International Programs]] , [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Atomic Safety and Licensing Board Panel]] , and [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Advisory Committee on Reactor Safeguards]] shall report directly to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] and the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall receive such reports.

==Sec. 105. Rescission of Reorganization Plan approval==

Approval of Reorganization Plan No. 1 of 1980(5 U.S.C. App. 1)is rescinded.

==Title II— Miscellaneous==



==Sec. 201. Certification of documents transmitted to Congress==

A letter or other document transmitted by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Nuclear Regulatory Commission]] , on behalf of the full [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] , to a member of Congress in his or her capacity as chairman or ranking minority member of a Committee of Congress, shall include a certification that the letter or document is being sent to both the Chairman and ranking minority member of that [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Committee]] in accordance with established [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] procedures.

==Sec. 202. Time limits for Commission review of Atomic Safety and Licensing Board decisions==

When reviewing the decisions and actions of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Atomic Safety and Licensing Board]] , the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall follow the following procedures:
:(1) Each Commissioner shall vote on the matter not later than 90 days after receipt of final briefs, after which time the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall not further delay a decision. Once a majority position is established, the Secretary shall notify in writing any Commissioners who have not voted that a majority position has been established. Any Commissioners who have not yet voted shall vote within three days of the Secretary’s notice or be considered by the Secretary as not participating.
:(2) Not later than 30 days after a majority position is established, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall publish any resulting decision, including adjudicatory orders and direction to agency staff. If a majority position is not established due to a tied vote, not later than 30 days after [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] voting is complete, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall publish any resulting decision, including adjudicatory orders and direction to agency staff.

==Sec. 203. Allegations of wrongdoing==



===(a) Referral to inspector general– ===

Not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Nuclear Regulatory Commission]] shall revise its procedures to ensure that any allegation of wrongdoing on the part of the Chairman of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] is immediately referred to the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Inspector General of the Commission]] .

===(b) Supervision of inspector general– ===

During the pendency of any investigation by the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Inspector General]] of the Chairman with respect to an allegation described in subsection (a), the Chairman shall delegate responsibility for supervising the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Inspector General]] to a member of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] other than the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] , consistent with the Inspector General Act of 1978.

==Sec. 204. Approval of Commissioner travel==

The [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman of the Nuclear Regulatory Commission]] shall authorize all international travel requested by other members of the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] for official business unless the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Chairman]] submits a notice of disapproval to the full Commission specifying the basis for the disapproval. The notice of disapproval shall be submitted within 5 days after the travel is requested or the travel shall be deemed approved.

==Sec. 205. Implementation==

Except as otherwise specified in this Act, the [[w:Nuclear Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall revise its procedures to conform to this Act within 180 days of its date of enactment.
