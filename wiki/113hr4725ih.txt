{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to ensure that all veterans are eligible to participate in hospice care programs of the Secretary of Veterans Affairs.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Care for our Heroes Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Determination of eligibility for hospice care=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1710  Section 1710of title 38, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 
:(i) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, and in accordance with the priorities specified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705 section 1705(a)]of this title, each veteran is eligible to participate in a hospice program (or a similar program) conducted by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]], regardless of—
::(1) the net worth or income of the veteran, spouse of the veteran, or child of the veteran; or
::(2) whether the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]has otherwise furnished to the veteran any benefit under the laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Effective date– ==

The amendment made by subsection (a) shall apply with respect to a veteran who seeks to participate in a hospice program (or similar program) conducted by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]on or after the date of the enactment of this Act.
