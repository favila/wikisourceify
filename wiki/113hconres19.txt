{{Center|CONCURRENT RESOLUTION}}

Authorizing the use of the Capitol Grounds
		  for the Greater Washington Soap Box Derby.

={{anchor|H832AC00D84F74BB999EE5E38FAB1CA90}}Section 1. Use of Capitol Grounds for
			 soap box derby races=



=={{anchor|H889D19BDDF824C41A31EFBC5DCF211D6}}(a) In
			 General– ==

The Greater
			 Washington Soap Box Derby Association (in this resolution referred to as the
			 sponsor) shall be permitted to sponsor a public event, soap box
			 derby races (in this resolution referred to as the event), on the
			 Capitol Grounds.

=={{anchor|H0C2DAF4F809F4DF19B3FCDE454D99952}}(b) Date of
			 Event– ==

The event shall be held
			 on June 15, 2013, or on such other date as the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of
			 Representatives]] and the Committee on Rules and Administration of the Senate
			 jointly designate.

={{anchor|H32E65B2AA6574B08AC9C31BBA645F1E2}}Sec. 2. Terms and
			 conditions=



=={{anchor|HC4BE2D2300D04351876EEE3B9FC1B658}}(a) In
			 General– ==

Under conditions to
			 be prescribed by the [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]] and the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]], the
			 event shall be—
:(1) free of admission charge and open to the
			 public; and
:(2) arranged not to interfere with the needs of
			 [[w:United States Congress|Congress]].

=={{anchor|H96A1BBC1B2CB4B50B1DEB46F2552AE62}}(b) Expenses and
			 Liabilities– ==

The sponsor shall
			 assume full responsibility for all expenses and liabilities incident to all
			 activities associated with the event.

={{anchor|H8EFC244BB5F74543A007A3DDC323CF20}}Sec. 3. Event
			 preparations=

Subject to the
			 approval of the [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]], the sponsor is authorized to erect
			 upon the Capitol Grounds such stage, sound amplification devices, and other
			 related structures and equipment as may be required for the event.

={{anchor|HD616FD4F252B4618B9A79EA9F7BA6F7C}}Sec. 4. Additional
			 arrangements=

The [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of
			 the Capitol]] and the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]] are authorized to make such additional
			 arrangements as may be required to carry out the event.

={{anchor|H1161AA65AF444900BFFC67C3A6DB7DFB}}Sec. 5. Enforcement of
			 restrictions=

The [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol
			 Police Board]] shall provide for enforcement of the restrictions contained in
			 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/5104 section 5104(c) of title 40, United States Code], concerning sales,
			 advertisements, displays, and solicitations on the Capitol Grounds, as well as
			 other restrictions applicable to the Capitol Grounds, with respect to the
			 event.
