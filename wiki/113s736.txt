{{Center|A BILL}}

To establish a maximum amount for special use permit fees applicable to certain cabins on National Forest System land in the State of Alaska.

={{anchor|S1}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Alaska Subsistence Structure Protection Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|id7B604C1D11A84F6C9144E37C2D5E5410}}Sec. 2. Definitions=

In this Act:

=={{anchor|id817F4097A20C4CD895C42E9539C32E0A}}(1) Covered cabin– ==

The term“covered cabin”means a cabin (including related structures) on [[w:United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land in the State of Alaska—
:(A) that is used for subsistence uses;
:(B) that is subject to section 1303(d) of the Alaska National Interest Lands Conservation Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/3193 16 U.S.C. 3193(d)] );
:(C) that is subject to section 1316(a) of the Alaska National Interest Lands Conservation Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/3204 16 U.S.C. 3204(a)] ); or
:(D) that is used for commercial fishing by a permittee that does not generate more than $15,000 in gross annual income from the fishery.

=={{anchor|id721F2E0778E144B6B9D74C623AF2F286}}(2) Subsistence uses– ==

The term“subsistence uses”has the meaning given the term in section 803 of the Alaska National Interest Lands Conservation Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 16 U.S.C. 3113] ).

={{anchor|idB1DA6872AD34418CBA699C7CB534AE47}}Sec. 3. Alaska subsistence use cabin permit fees=

The maximum amount of the fee for the issuance of a special use permit by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] for the use and occupancy of [[w:United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land for a covered cabin shall be not more than $250.
