{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for the collection of data on traffic stops,
		  and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H48C87B0012BD4CDFB72E2EC72E95996D}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Traffic Stops Along the Border
			 Statistics Study Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HF423B8CEC2A44D4F84BE91AAD496C4DB}}Sec. 2. Attorney general
			 study=



=={{anchor|H860E4B15CC8B4AABA0968860AC3FFB36}}(a) Study– ==



==={{anchor|H69C0E629E6AA4A12909D1697D663E677}}(1) In
			 general– ===

The [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall conduct a nationwide study of
			 stops for traffic violations by law enforcement officers.

==={{anchor|H83B1A312BE80471C993FF4339180EECF}}(2) Initial
			 analysis– ===

The [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall perform an initial analysis
			 of existing data, including complaints alleging, and other information
			 concerning, traffic stops motivated by race and other bias.

==={{anchor|HC2C3FF5F250E47BE93F7DBF510809574}}(3) Data
			 collection– ===

After completion of the initial analysis under
			 paragraph (2), the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall gather the following data on traffic
			 stops from a nationwide sample of jurisdictions, including jurisdictions
			 identified in the initial analysis:
:(A) The traffic
			 infraction alleged to have been committed that led to the stop.
:(B) Identifying
			 characteristics of the driver stopped, including the race, gender, ethnicity,
			 and approximate age of the driver.
:(C) Whether
			 immigration status was questioned, immigration documents were requested, or an
			 inquiry was made to the Immigration and Naturalization Service with regard to
			 any individual in the vehicle and whether any individual in the vehicle was
			 turned over to immigration officials.
:(D) The number of
			 stops conducted within 25 miles of the United States border with Mexico
			 compared with the number of stops conducted within 25 miles of the United
			 States border with Canada.
:(E) The number of
			 individuals in the stopped vehicle.
:(F) Whether a search
			 was instituted as a result of the stop and whether consent was requested for
			 the search.
:(G) Any alleged
			 criminal behavior by the driver that justified the search.
:(H) Any items seized,
			 including contraband or money.
:(I) Whether any
			 warning or citation was issued as a result of the stop.
:(J) Whether an arrest
			 was made as a result of either the stop or the search and the justification for
			 the arrest.
:(K) The duration of
			 the stop.

=={{anchor|HB298ABCF23A1400989F10AF6A07FEAD0}}(b) Reporting– ==



==={{anchor|HD49EE8DE032C4DD9B33AC77BF3BE0D9B}}(1) Initial
			 analysis– ===

Not later than 120 days after the date of enactment of
			 this Act, the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall report the results of the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney
			 General’s]] initial analysis under subsection (a)(2) to [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress]] and make such
			 report available to the public, and identify the jurisdictions for which the
			 study is to be conducted.

==={{anchor|H142719FCEDDF4F3BA1A4FE6D165B44DC}}(2) Data
			 collection– ===

Not later than 2 years after the date of enactment of
			 this Act, the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall report the results of the data collected
			 under subsection (a)(3) to [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress]], a copy of which shall also be published in
			 the Federal Register.

={{anchor|H113A8FA56F1E4058A48BEE800D79A74C}}Sec. 3. Grant
			 program=

In order to complete
			 the study described in section 2, the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may provide grants to
			 law enforcement agencies to collect and submit the data described in section 2
			 to the appropriate agency as designated by the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]].

={{anchor|HB8861DE4EC41438F948B3A0B372225DA}}Sec. 4. Limitation on use
			 of data=

Information released
			 pursuant to section 2 shall not reveal the identity of any individual who is
			 stopped or any law enforcement officer involved in a traffic stop.

={{anchor|H334205C6FD704F608449472CF367E0F3}}Sec. 5. Definition=

For purposes of this Act, the term
			 “law enforcement agency” means an agency of a State or political
			 subdivision of a State, authorized by law or by a Federal, State, or local
			 government agency to engage in or supervise the prevention, detection, or
			 investigation of violations of criminal laws.

={{anchor|HFA7F2C5EAEAF4D2DB2E0AA96C3CC6738}}Sec. 6. Authorization of
			 appropriations=

There are
			 authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be necessaryto carry out this
			 Act.
