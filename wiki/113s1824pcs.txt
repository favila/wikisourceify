{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Safe Drinking Water Act to exempt certain lead pipes, fittings, fixtures, solder, and flux that contain brass.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Drinking Water Supply Assistance Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Exempting certain uses of lead=

 Section 1417(a) of the Safe Drinking Water Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/300g–6 42 U.S.C. 300g–6(a)] )is amended—
:(1) in paragraph (4)—
::(A) in subparagraph (A), by striking “or” after the semicolon;
::(B) in subparagraph (B), by striking the period at the end and inserting “; or” ; and
::(C) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:::(C) except as provided in paragraph (5), any pipe, pipe or plumbing fitting or fixture, solder, or flux that contains brass that is not lead free if—
::::(i) a supplier of water owns the brass before the date of enactment of this subparagraph;
::::(ii) the brass is used to prevent or mitigate—
:::::(I) a deficit in the budget of a municipality; or
:::::(II) an interruption in service of a public water system; and
::::(iii) the supplier of water is otherwise in compliance with (as of the date on which the brass is used) all laws (including regulations), except any monitoring requirement, including subpart C of part 141 of title 40, Code of Federal Regulations, regarding lead in water.; and
</blockquote>

:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(5) Finding of unreasonable risk to public health– ===

The exemption in paragraph (4)(C)shall not apply to a municipality if the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] finds that the exemption, as applied to the municipality or all municipalities, would result in an unreasonable risk to public health..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Evaluation of sources of lead in water distribution systems and alternate routing systems=

The [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]] shall—
:(1) consult with and seek the advice of the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|National Drinking Water Advisory Council]] on potential changes to the regulations pertaining to lead under the Safe Drinking Water Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/300f 42 U.S.C. 300f et seq] .); and
:(2) request the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Council]] to consider sources of lead throughout drinking water distribution systems, including through components used to reroute drinking water during distribution system repairs.
