{{Center|A BILL}}

To designate the attack that occurred at a recruiting station in Little Rock, Arkansas, on June 1, 2009, in which Private William Long of the United States Army was killed and Private Quinton Ezeagwula of the United States Army was wounded, as an international terrorist attack for which the two soldiers are to be awarded the Purple Heart.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Honoring Ezeagwula and Long Act” or the “HEAL Act” .

=Sec. 2. Designation of Little Rock recruiting station attack as an international terrorist attack for purposes of awarding the Purple Heart=

The attack that occurred at a recruiting station in Little Rock, Arkansas, on June 1, 2009, in which Private William Long of the United States Army was killed and Private Quinton Ezeagwula of the United States Army was wounded, was an international terrorist attack carried out by an enemy of the United States, and pursuant to Army Regulations regarding the award of the Purple Heart (AR 600–8–22, chapter 2, section II, 2–8b), Privates Long and Ezeagwula are entitled to be awarded the Purple Heart.
