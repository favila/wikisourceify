{{Center|A BILL}}

 To restore the integrity of the Fifth Amendment to the Constitution of the United States, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “   Fifth Amendment Integrity Restoration Act of 2014   ”or the“   FAIR Act   ”.  

=Sec. 2. Civil forfeiture proceedings=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/983  Section 983of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/983 subsection (c)]—
::(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/983 paragraph (1)], by striking“a preponderance of the evidence”and inserting“clear and convincing evidence”;
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/983 paragraph (2)], by striking“a preponderance of the evidence”and inserting“clear and convincing evidence”; and
::(C) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/983 paragraph (3)]and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 
:::(3) if the Government's theory of forfeiture is that the property used to commit or facilitate the commission of a criminal offense, or was involved in the commission of a criminal offense, the Government shall establish, by clear and convincing evidence, that—
::::(A) there was a substantial connection between the property and the offense; and
::::(B) the owner of any interest in the seized property—
:::::(i) intentionally used the property in connection with the offense; or
:::::(ii) knowingly consented or was willfully blind to the use of the property by another in connection with the offense. ; and 
</blockquote>

:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/983 subsection (d)(2)(A)], by striking“an owner who”and all that follows through“upon learning”and inserting“an owner who upon learning”. 

=Sec. 3. Disposition of forfeited property=



==(a) Revisions to Controlled Substances Act– ==

   Section 511(e) of the Controlled Substances Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/881 21 U.S.C. 881(e)])is amended—
:(1) in paragraph (1) , by striking“civilly or”;
:(2) in paragraph (2)(B) , by striking“accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 section 524(c)]of title 28,”and inserting“the General Fund of the Treasury of the United States”; and
:(3) in paragraph (4)(B) , by striking“civilly or”.

==(b) Revisions to title 18– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981  Section 981(e)of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by striking“is authorized”and all that follows through“or forfeiture of the property;”and inserting“may forward to the Treasurer of the United States any proceeds of property forfeited pursuant to this section for deposit in the General Fund of the Treasury or transfer such property on such terms and conditions as such officer may determine—”;
:(2) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 paragraphs (3)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (4)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (5)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (6)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (7)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 paragraphs (1)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (2)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (3)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (4)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (5)], respectively; and
:(3) in the matter following[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 paragraph (5)], as so redesignated—
::(A) by striking the first, second, and third sentences; and
::(B) by striking“paragraphs (3), (4), and (5)”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 paragraphs (1)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (2)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/981 (3)] ”. 

=Sec. 4. Department of Justice Assets Forfeiture Fund deposits=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524  Section 524(c)(4)of title 28, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 subparagraphs (A)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 (B)]; and
:(2) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 subparagraphs (C)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 (D)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 subparagraphs (A)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 (B)], respectively. 

=Sec. 5. Applicability=

The amendments made by this Act shall apply to—
:(1) any civil forfeiture proceeding pending on or filed on or after the date of enactment of this Act; and
:(2) any amounts received from the forfeiture of property on or after the date of enactment of this Act.
