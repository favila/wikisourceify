{{Center|A BILL}}

To establish within the Smithsonian Institution the
		  Smithsonian American Latino Museum, and for other purposes.


{{section|H2B5FE14E4F4E457998365B8CF5393DEE|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Smithsonian American Latino Museum
			 Act

        
.

{{section|HF76D395358B24E2593BAB8564DBEF4A4|2. Establishment of museum}}
There is established
			 within the [[Smithsonian Institution|Smithsonian Institution]] a museum to be known as the
			 Smithsonian American Latino Museum
.

{{section|HA15E1F9FE50E483C8A4EEC239965ED0D|3. Location and authorization}}
(a)
Arts and
			 Industries Building
The Arts and Industries Building of the
			 [[Smithsonian Institution|Smithsonian Institution]], located on the National Mall at 900 Jefferson Drive,
			 Southwest, Washington, District of Columbia, including a new underground annex
			 facility, is designated as the location of the Smithsonian American Latino
			 Museum.

(b)
Planning and
			 Construction
The [[Smithsonian Institution|Board of
			 Regents of the Smithsonian Institution]], in consultation with the [[United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of
			 the Interior]], the [[United States Commission of Fine Arts|Commission of Fine Arts]], and the [[National Capital Planning Commission|National Capital Planning
			 Commission]], and with other appropriate Federal and local agencies, is
			 authorized to prepare plans, design, renovate, rehabilitate, and construct the
			 Smithsonian American Latino Museum facility, as referred to in the May 2011
			 Report to Congress of the Commission to Study the Potential Creation of a
			 National Museum of the American Latino.

(c)
Schedule and
			 funding
(1)
In
			 general
The [[Smithsonian Institution|Board of Regents]] is authorized to prepare a plan of
			 action for the Smithsonian American Latino Museum, and to identify and evaluate
			 viable funding models for both construction and operation of the Museum.

(2)
Timing
The
			 plan of action authorized in paragraph (1)
 shall be concluded not later than 18
			 months after the date of enactment of this Act.



{{section|HFCCDC3366F2D450A835A45548A358213|4. Agreement with Secretary of the interior}}
The
			 [[United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] and the [[Smithsonian Institution|Board of Regents of the Smithsonian
			 Institution]] shall enter into an agreement that—
(1)
allows for the
			 planning, design, and construction of the underground annex facility by the
			 [[Smithsonian Institution|Board of Regents]], in a manner harmonious with and to protect the open space and
			 visual sightlines of the Mall; and

(2)
provides a
			 timeline for the transfer of administrative jurisdiction, if necessary, of the
			 appropriate subsurface area from the [[United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] to the
			 [[Smithsonian Institution|Smithsonian Institution]].


{{section|HDC88D5A6DF994500A2ABF6451BE2E12E|5. Consideration of recommendations of commission}}
In carrying out its duties under this Act,
			 the [[Smithsonian Institution|Board of Regents of the Smithsonian Institution]] shall take into
			 consideration the reports and plans submitted by the Commission to Study the
			 Potential Creation of a National Museum of the American Latino under section
			 333 of the Consolidated Natural Resources Act of 2008
 (Public Law 110–229
; 122
			 Stat. 784
)
.
