{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide for advance appropriations for certain information technology accounts of the Department of Veterans Affairs, to include mental health professionals in training programs of the Department, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Rural Veterans Mental Health Care Improvement Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Advance appropriations for accounts for health-related information technology for the Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) Advance appropriations for information technology accounts– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/117 Section 117(c) of title 38, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(4) Accounts, including subaccounts of the Accounts referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/117 paragraphs (1) through (3)], providing funds for information technology. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Effective date– ==

The amendment made by subsection (a) shall take effect on October 1, 2014, and shall apply with respect to fiscal years beginning on or after that date. 

=Sec. 3. Inclusion of mental health professionals in the education and training program for health personnel of the Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) In general– ==

In carrying out the education and training program required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302  section 7302(a)(1)of title 38, United States Code], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall include education and training of marriage and family therapists and licensed professional mental health counselors.

==(b) Funding– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall apportion funding for the education and training program equally among the professions included in the program. 

=Sec. 4. Provision of mental health services for families of certain veterans at facilities of the Department=



==(a) Provision of mental health services at Department facilities– ==

   Subsection (e) of section 304 of the Caregivers and Veterans Omnibus Health Services Act of 2010 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1712A  38 U.S.C. 1712Anote]; [[Public Law 111-163|Public Law 111–163]]  )is amended—
:(1) by striking“ peer outreach and peer support services ”and inserting“ services ”; and
:(2) by striking“The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall carry out the services”and inserting “The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall carry out—

<blockquote> 
::(1) the services ; 
</blockquote>

:(3) by striking the period at the end and inserting“; and”; and
:(4) by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
::(2) the mental health services required by subsection (a)(2) at or through [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]medical centers. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Definition of mental health services– ==

Such section is further amended by striking subsection (f) and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

===(f) Definitions– ===

In this section:

====(1) Mental health services– ====

The term mental health services includes outpatient mental healthcare referred to insection 17.38(a)(1)(i)of title 38, Code of Federal Regulations (as in effect on the day before the date of enactment of this Act).

====(2) Vet center– ====

The term vet center means a center for readjustment counseling and related mental health services for veterans under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1712A  section 1712Aof title 38, United States Code]. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 5. Report on provision of telemedicine services=



==(a) In general– ==

Not later than 120 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and the [[w:|  [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans’ Affairs of the House of Representatives]] ]]a report on the following:
:(1) Issues that may be impeding the provision by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]of telemedicine services for veterans, including the following:
::(A) Statutory or regulatory restrictions.
::(B) Licensure or credentialing issues for any provider practicing telemedicine with veterans who live in a different State than the provider.
::(C) Limited broadband access in rural areas.
::(D) Limited information technology resources or capabilities.
::(E) Long distances veterans must travel to access a facility or clinic with telemedicine capabilities.
::(F) Insufficient liability protection for providers.
::(G) Reimbursement issues faced by providers.
::(H) Travel limitations for providers that are unaffiliated with the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]and are participating or seeking to participate in a telemedicine program of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
:(2) Actions taken to address the issues identified in paragraph (1) .
:(3) An update on efforts by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to carry out the initiative of teleconsultation for the provision of remote mental health and traumatic brain injury assessments required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1709A  section 1709Aof title 38, United States Code].
:(4) An update on efforts by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to offer training opportunities in telemedicine to medical residents, as required by  section 108(b) of the Janey Ensminger Act ( [[Public Law 112-154|Public Law 112–154]]  ;[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7406  38 U.S.C. 7406note]).
:(5) An update on efforts by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to, in partnership with primary care providers, install video cameras and instruments to monitor weight, blood pressure, and other vital statistics in the homes of patients.

==(b) Telemedicine defined– ==

In this section, the term telemedicine means the use by a health care provider of telecommunications to assist in the diagnosis or treatment of a patient’s medical condition.
