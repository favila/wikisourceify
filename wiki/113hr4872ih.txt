{{Center|A BILL}}

 To eliminate the use of the frank for mail transmitted by Members of Congress and Congressional officials, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Ending Special Mail Privileges for Congress Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Repeal of frank; requirements relating to the use of Congressional mail=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/3201  Section 3201of title 39, United States Code], is amended—

=Sec. 5. Effective date; regulations=



==(a) Effective Date– ==

The amendments made by this Act shall apply with respect to matter mailed during the One Hundred Fourteenth [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]and each succeeding [[w:United States Congress|Congress]].

==(b) Regulations– ==

In consultation with the [[w:United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]], the [[w:United States House Committee on House Administration|Committee on House Administration of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Rules and Administration|Committee on Rules and Administration of the Senate]]shall each revise the regulations governing the treatment of official mail of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House]]or [[w:United States Senate|Senate]](as the case may be) to take into account the amendments made by this Act.
