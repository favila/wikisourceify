{{Center|A BILL}}

To make the antitrust laws applicable to a political
		  committee under the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971 which is established
		  and administered by a separate segregated fund of a corporation pursuant to
		  section 316(b)(2)(C) of such Act.


{{section|H4F4521B71195452A89C9030467401495|1. Application of antitrust laws to corporate PACs}}
The
			 antitrust laws shall apply to a political committee under the Federal Election
			 Campaign Act of 1971
 which is established and administered by a separate
			 segregated fund of a corporation pursuant to section 316(b)(2)(C) of such Act
,
			 and in accordance with such laws, a political committee for multiple legal
			 entities in the same business shall be prohibited.

{{section|H75D7539A39A14AED8A6B4A3BFF7EC51C|2. Definition}}
For purposes of this Act, the term
			 antitrust laws
 has the meaning given such term in the first
			 section of the Clayton Act
, except that such term includes section 5 of the
			 Federal Trade Commission Act
 to the extent that such section 5
 applies to
			 unfair methods of competition.
