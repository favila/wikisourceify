{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to allow a credit for the purchase of heating and cooling equipment which meets the Energy Star program requirements and is used in certain high-cost energy communities, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Energy Efficient Heating and Cooling Tax Credit Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Credit for conversion of home heating using oil fuel to using natural gas or biomass feedstocks=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C Subsection (a) of section 25C of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986](relating to nonbusiness energy property) is amended by striking“and”at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C paragraph (1)], by striking the period at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C paragraph (2)]and inserting“, and”, and by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
:(3) the amount of the qualifying heating conversion expenditures paid or incurred by the taxpayer during such taxable year. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Dollar limitation– ==



===(1) In general– ===



====(A) Limitation– ====

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C Subsection (b) of section 25C of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986]is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 

=====(4) Limitation on qualifying heating conversion expenditures– =====

The amount of the credit allowed under this section by reason of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C paragraph (3) of subsection (a)]for any taxable year with respect to any taxpayer shall not exceed $5,000. . 
</blockquote>


====(B) Conforming amendment– ====

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C Paragraph (1) of section 25C(b) of such Code]is amended by inserting“by reason of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C paragraphs (1)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C (2) of subsection (a)] ”after“The credit allowed under this section”.

===(2) No double counting– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C Section 25C(e) of such Code](relating to special rules) is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 

====(4) No double counting– ====

No amount taken into account for purposes of determining a credit under this section by reason of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C paragraph (3) of subsection (a)]shall be taken into account for purposes of determining a credit under this section by reason of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C paragraphs (1)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C (2) of subsection (a)]. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Qualifying heating conversion expenditures– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C Section 25C of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986](relating to residential energy property expenditures) is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 

===(h) Qualifying heating conversion expenditures– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The term qualifying heating conversion expenditures means expenditures made by the taxpayer for qualified heating conversion property which—
:(A) meets the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C subparagraphs (A)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C (B) of subsection (d)(1)], and
:(B) is used as a heating or cooling system on a building or structure located in a community (as determined under section 19(a)(1) of the Rural Electrification Act of 1936 ) in which the average residential expenditure for home energy is more than 200 percent of the national average residential expenditure for home energy (as determined by the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Energy Information Agency]]using the most recent data available).

====(2) Amounts included– ====

The term qualifying heating conversion expenditures includes expenditures—
:(A) for labor costs properly allocable to the onsite preparation, assembly, or original installation of property described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C paragraph (1)], including fuel service connection installation costs specifically related to fuel service to the qualified energy property used in such conversion, and
:(B) the removal of the fuel oil equipment (including any storage tank) for such a building or structure.

====(3) Exclusions– ====

Such term does not include expenditures for soil cleanup.

====(4) Qualified heating conversion property– ====

For purposes of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C paragraph (1)], the term qualified heating conversion property means property which—
:(A) is placed in service before January 1, 2019,
:(B) meets the performance and quality standards described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C subsection (d)(2)(B)], and
:(C) is a product which qualifies under the Energy Star program and meets the requirements for such property under such program. . 
</blockquote>


==(d) Conforming amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C Subsection (g) of section 25C of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986](relating to termination) is amended by striking“This section”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C Paragraphs (1)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/25C (2) of subsection (a)] ”.

==(e) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to property placed in service after the date of the enactment of this Act.
