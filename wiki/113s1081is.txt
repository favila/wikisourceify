{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 10, United States Code, to expand and enhance authorities on protected communications of members of the Armed Forces, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Military Whistleblower Protection Enhancement Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Expansion and enhancement of authorities relating protected communications of members of the Armed Forces and prohibited retaliatory actions=



==(a) Expansion of prohibited retaliatory personnel actions– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 Subsection (b) ofsection 1034of title 10, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (1)]—
::(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (A)], by striking“or”at the end;
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (B)]—
:::(i) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 clause (i)], by inserting“or a representative of a Member of Congress”after“a Member of Congress”;
:::(ii) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 clause (iv)], by striking“or”at the end;
:::(iii) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 clause (v)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 clause (vi)];
:::(iv) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 clause (v)]the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 clause (v)]:

<blockquote> 
::::(v) a court, grand jury, or court-martial proceeding, or an authorized official of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]or another law enforcement agency; or ; and 
</blockquote>

:::(v) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 clause (vi)], as redesignated by clause (iii) of this subparagraph, by striking the period at the end and inserting“; or”; and
::(C) by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote> 
:::(C) testimony, or otherwise participating in or assisting in an investigation or proceeding related to a communication under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (A)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (B)], or filing, causing to be filed, participating in, or otherwise assisting in an action brought under this section. ; and 
</blockquote>

:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (2)], by inserting after“any favorable action”the following:“, or a significant change in a member's duties, responsibilities, or working conditions”.

==(b) Inspector General investigations of allegations– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 Subsection (c) of such section]is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (1)], by striking“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (3)] ”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (4)] ”;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (2)], by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (A)]and inserting the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (A)]:

<blockquote> 
::(A) Any violation of any law, rule, or regulation, including a law or regulation prohibiting rape, sexual assault, or other sexual misconduct in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/920..920c sections 920 through 920c of this title](articles 120 through 120c of the Uniform Code of Military Justice), sexual harassment or unlawful discrimination. ; 
</blockquote>

:(3) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraphs (3)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (4)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (5)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraphs (4)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (5)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (6)], respectively;
:(4) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (2)]the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (3)]:

<blockquote> 
::(3) A communication described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (2)]shall not be excluded from the protections provided in this section because—
:::(A) the communication was made to a person who participated in an activity that the member reasonably believed to be covered by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (2)];
:::(B) the communication revealed information that had previously been communicated;
:::(C) of the member’s motive for making the communication;
:::(D) the communication was not made in writing;
:::(E) the communication was made while the member was off duty;
:::(F) the communication was made during the normal course of duties of the member. ; 
</blockquote>

:(5) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (D) of paragraph (4)], as redesignated by paragraph (3) of this subsection, by inserting before the period at the end of the second sentence the following:“, with the consent of the member”; and
:(6) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (5)], as so redesignated—
::(A) by striking“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (3)(A)] ”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (4)(A)] ”;
::(B) by striking“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (3)(D)] ”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (4)(D)] ”; and
::(C) by striking“60 days”and inserting“one year”.

==(c) Inspector General investigations of underlying allegations– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 Subsection (d) of such section]is amended by striking“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (A)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (B) of subsection (c)(2)] ”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (A)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (B)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (C) of subsection (c)(2)] ”.

==(d) Reports on investigations– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 Subsection (e) of such section]is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (1)]—
::(A) by striking“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (c)(3)(E)] ”both places it appears and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (c)(4)(E)] ”;
::(B) by striking“the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] ”and inserting“the Secretary of the military department concerned”; and
::(C) by striking“to the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]],”and inserting“to such Secretary,”;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (3)], by striking“the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] ”and inserting“the Secretary of the military department concerned”; and
:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (4)], by striking the second sentence and inserting the following new sentence:“The report shall include an explicit determination as to whether a personnel action prohibited by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (b)]has occurred and a recommendation as to the disposition of the complaint, including appropriate corrective action for the member.”.

==(e) Action in case of violations– ==

Such section is further amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsections (f)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (g)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (h)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (i)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsections (g)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (h)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (j)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 (k)], respectively; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (e)]the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (f)]:

<blockquote> 

====(f) Action in case of violations– ====


:(1) If an Inspector General reports under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (e)]that a personnel action prohibited by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (b)]has occurred, not later than 30 days after receiving such report from the Inspector General, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]or the Secretary of the military department concerned, as applicable, shall order such action as is necessary to correct the record of a personnel action prohibited by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (b)], taking into account the recommendations in the report by the Inspector General. Such Secretary shall take any appropriate disciplinary action against the individual who committed such prohibited personnel action.
:(2) If the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]or the Secretary of the military department concerned, as applicable, determines that an order for corrective or disciplinary action is not appropriate, not later than 30 days after making the determination, such Secretary shall—
::(A) provide to the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Armed Services|Committees on Armed Services of the Senate]]and the [[w:United States House Committee on Armed Services|House of Representatives]], and the member or former member, a notice of the determination and the reasons for not taking action; or
::(B) refer the report to the appropriate board for the correction of military records for further review under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (g)]. . 
</blockquote>


==(f) Correction of records– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 Subsection (g) of such section], as redesignated by subsection (e)(1) of this section , is further amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (1)], by striking“may review”and inserting“shall review”;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (2)(C)], by striking“may”and inserting“upon the request of the member or former member, after an initial determination that a complaint is not frivolous and has not previously been addressed by the board, shall”; and
:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 paragraph (3)]—
::(A) in the matter preceding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (A)], by striking“board elects to hold”and inserting“board holds”; and
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subparagraph (A)]—
:::(i) by striking“may be provided”and inserting“shall be provided”; and
:::(ii) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 clause (ii)], by striking“the case is unusually complex or otherwise requires”and inserting“the member or former member would benefit from”.

==(g) Review– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 Subsection (h) of such section], as redesignated by subsection (e)(1) of this section , is further amended by striking“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (f)] ”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (g)] ”.

==(h) Burdens of proof– ==

Such section is further amended by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (h)], as so redesignated, the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 subsection (i)]:

<blockquote> 

===(i) Burdens of proof– ===

The burdens of proof specified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1221 section 1221(e) of title 5]shall apply in any investigation conducted by an Inspector General, and any review conducted by the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]], and any board for the correction of military records, under this section. . 
</blockquote>


==(i) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall take effect on the date that is 30 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, and shall apply with respect to allegations pending or submitted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1034 section 1034 of title 10, United States Code], on or after that date.
