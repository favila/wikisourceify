{{Center|A BILL}}

To establish minimum standards for States that allow the carrying of concealed firearms.

={{anchor|S1}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Common Sense Concealed Firearms Permit Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|id02A2D0FAD93E401A9FE67B281B58B045}}Sec. 2. Concealed firearms permits=



=={{anchor|id954EB322DBCA4C30B44DBFFE38D7B664}}(a) In general– ==

Chapter 44 of title 18, United States Code, is amended by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926C section 926C] the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|id2E06B99519C844E6A1D69C7D7B7521B4}}Sec. 926D. Concealed firearms permits==



==={{anchor|id2EFC46542BA4422DA34822DAD1C45E20}}(a) In general– ===

Each State that allows residents of the State to carry concealed firearms shall—
:(1) establish a process to issue permits to residents of the State to carry concealed firearms; and
:(2) require that each resident of the State seeking to carry a concealed firearm in the State obtain a permit through the process established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D paragraph (1)] .

==={{anchor|id804FD0B54A544EA98369BC100FBDF9D2}}(b) Requirements– ===

In establishing a process to issue permits to carry concealed firearms under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D subsection (a)] , a State shall—
:(1) ensure that a local law enforcement agency participates in the process; and
:(2) at a minimum, require that an applicant for a permit to carry a concealed firearm—
::(A) be a legal resident of the United States;
::(B) be not less than 21 years of age;
::(C) demonstrate good cause for requesting a concealed firearm permit; and
::(D) demonstrate that the applicant is worthy of the public trust to carry a concealed firearm in public.

==={{anchor|id0D567036D452430D99FD9F9389DE2CB8}}(c) Law enforcement agency report– ===

If a State establishes a process under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D subsection (a)] that allows for an agency other than a law enforcement agency to issue permits to carry concealed firearms, the process shall require that—
:(1) a local law enforcement agency submit to the agency responsible for issuing permits a written report that describes whether the applicant meets the standards of the State to carry a concealed firearm; and
:(2) the agency responsible for issuing permits maintain a report submitted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D paragraph (1)] in the file of the applicant.

==={{anchor|id237BB1B920EF44A696ADC3C3190BAD7B}}(d) Definition– ===

In this section, the term local law enforcement agency means a law enforcement agency of the unit of local government with jurisdiction of the area in which the applicant for a permit to carry a concealed firearm resides.

==={{anchor|id9022EB9DA06848B9B96CA4D3274A8031}}(e) Compliance– ===

Not later than 270 days after the date of enactment of this section, each State described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D subsection (a)] shall be in compliance with this section..
</blockquote>
