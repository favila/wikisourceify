{{Center|A BILL}}

To enable concrete masonry products manufacturers to establish, finance, and carry out a coordinated program of research, education, and promotion to improve, maintain, and develop markets for concrete masonry products.

={{anchor|H2184C9B8CF3A4BD88A59EF0F5F6F2C3D}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Concrete Masonry Products Research, Education, and Promotion Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HE4E612FDED1F4790881D33F50C235C0A}}Sec. 2. Findings and declaration of policy=



=={{anchor|HAED211455CC5456B8904FB271844C27C}}(a) Findings– ==

 [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] finds the following:
:(1) The production of concrete masonry products plays a significant role in the economy of the United States.
:(2) Concrete masonry products are produced by hundreds of concrete manufacturers and utilized throughout the United States and foreign countries.
:(3) Concrete masonry products move in the channels of interstate and foreign commerce, and concrete masonry products that do not move in such channels of commerce directly burden or affect interstate commerce of concrete masonry products.
:(4) The concrete masonry products industry employs thousands of workers and positively impacts economic stability throughout the United States.
:(5) The maintenance and expansion of existing markets for concrete masonry products and the development of new markets is vital to the welfare of concrete manufacturers in the United States and those concerned with marketing and using concrete masonry products as well as the general economic welfare of the United States.
:(6) The concrete masonry products industry plays a vital role in providing safe, environmentally sustainable, and economical construction for citizens of the United States and abroad.
:(7) Concrete masonry products are used for the construction of structures that protect and provide shelter for citizens during disasters (earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, tornados, fire, et cetera).
:(8) Concrete masonry structures provide energy efficiencies to reduce American energy demands.
:(9) Concrete masonry provides for durable structures that support sustainability objectives, reducing long-term demands on natural resources.
:(10) Concrete masonry products are made using locally available resources throughout the United States, reducing transportation and infrastructure demands.
:(11) Concrete masonry products are used to support the construction of durable and cost-efficient, low-income housing.

=={{anchor|H139D4C14197A4DF1B6AF0053D3682E60}}(b) Purpose– ==

The purpose of this Act is to authorize the establishment of an orderly program for developing, financing, and carrying out an effective, continuous, and coordinated program of research, education, and promotion, including funds for marketing and market research activities, that is designed to—
:(1) strengthen the position of the concrete masonry products industry in the domestic marketplace;
:(2) maintain, develop, and expand markets and uses for concrete masonry products in the domestic and foreign marketplaces; and
:(3) promote the use of concrete masonry products in environmentally sustainable construction and building.

=={{anchor|H71647988603E4DD7A37513E78ABA5097}}(c) Limitation– ==

Nothing in this Act may be construed to provide for the control of production or otherwise limit the right of any person to manufacture concrete masonry products.

={{anchor|H1AABA81ECDA2440E871665BF0C9D8BF6}}Sec. 3. Definitions=

For the purposes of this Act:

=={{anchor|H8BDAD0092C7F4E6090C7E3B4FE290521}}(a) Block machine– ==

The term block machine means a piece of equipment that utilizes vibration and compaction to form concrete masonry products.

=={{anchor|H12596C8568F64AFDB0752D02C1EEA688}}(b) Board– ==

The term Board means the [[w:|Concrete Masonry Products Board]] established under section 5.

=={{anchor|H16EE05181C1D4E80B5224D4EC318B56B}}(c) Cavity– ==

The term cavity means the open space in the mold of a block machine capable of forming a single concrete masonry unit having nominal plan dimensions of 8 inches by 16 inches.

=={{anchor|H26E90D9B2B9345D6819F9A13665139AE}}(d) Commerce– ==

The term commerce includes interstate, foreign, and intrastate commerce.

=={{anchor|H7DC642D5B635456098CAE0F66EBE9224}}(e) Concrete masonry products– ==

The term concrete masonry products refers to a broader class of products, including concrete masonry units as well as hardscape products such as concrete pavers and segmental retaining wall units, manufactured on a block machine using dry-cast concrete.

=={{anchor|H84F637E9515C4F65A36928A127459028}}(f) Concrete masonry unit– ==

The term concrete masonry unit means a concrete masonry product that is a manmade masonry unit having an actual width of 3 inches or greater and manufactured from dry-cast concrete using a block machine. Such term includes concrete block and related concrete units used in masonry applications.

=={{anchor|H8E82D815171547F6AF879E7D2CE0EDD4}}(g) Department– ==

The term Department means the Department of Commerce.

=={{anchor|H5E0F98ACA02548339768CCD8F87B491E}}(h) Dry-Cast concrete– ==

The term dry-cast concrete means a composite material that is composed essentially of aggregates embedded in a binding medium composed of a mixture of cementitious materials (including hydraulic cement, pozzolans, or other cementitious materials) and water of such a consistency to maintain its shape after forming in a block machine.

=={{anchor|HE5D744AE5C7F4F7592CC095A802E798C}}(i) Education– ==

The term education means programs that will educate or communicate the benefits of concrete masonry products in safe and environmentally sustainable development, advancements in concrete masonry product technology and development, and other information and programs designed to generate increased demand for commercial, residential, multi-family, and institutional projects using concrete masonry products and to generally enhance the image of concrete masonry products.

=={{anchor|HD531EEBDCD3445A4BAC6B1AFCE174C88}}(j) Machine cavities– ==

The term machine cavities means the cavities with which a block machine could be equipped.

=={{anchor|H505CA9F615704ADC888CD804E6435358}}(k) Machine cavities in operation– ==

The term machine cavities in operation means those machine cavities associated with a block machine that have produced concrete masonry units within the last 6 months of the date set for determining eligibility and is fully operable and capable of producing concrete masonry units.

=={{anchor|H7E5292EDE0A744D787FE1B1580D14900}}(l) Masonry unit– ==

The term masonry unit means a noncombustible building product intended to be laid by hand or joined using mortar, grout, surface bonding, post-tensioning or some combination of these methods.

=={{anchor|HD71206AFCA774C5DBBCA0DFCE4BBBB94}}(m) Manufacturer– ==

The term manufacturer means any person engaged in the manufacturing of commercial concrete masonry products.

=={{anchor|H3C266AB4526246E3AE56340678EC0D15}}(n) Order– ==

The term order means an order issued under section 4.

=={{anchor|H02EFC5569B85488A92A3B53381DF70A1}}(o) Person– ==

The term person means any individual, group of individuals, partnership, corporation, association, cooperative, or any other entity.

=={{anchor|H7D8DC7FE56024EB3B1B41376BD995814}}(p) Promotion– ==

The term promotion means any action, including paid advertising, to advance the image and desirability of concrete masonry products with the express intent of improving the competitive position and stimulating sales of concrete masonry products in the marketplace.

=={{anchor|H5238A40A85B0484A87F6614C05F732A8}}(q) Research– ==

The term research means studies testing the effectiveness of market development and promotion efforts, studies relating to the improvement of concrete masonry products and new product development, and studies documenting the performance of concrete masonry.

=={{anchor|H18085F97280442EDA8D08D7F92F9257E}}(r) Secretary– ==

The term Secretary means the Secretary of Commerce.

=={{anchor|H83F85CF1D0E9460EB2DD80BAC8D71758}}(s) United States– ==

The term United States means the several States and the District of Columbia.

={{anchor|H18746D982DEB42A6B7A582021C143339}}Sec. 4. Issuance of orders=



=={{anchor|HA118369D7BA1410D8042327F8DCB361A}}(a) In general– ==



==={{anchor|H19685A1EE31544C4B00AAD5E5B96F7AF}}(1) Issuance– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] , subject to the procedures provided in subsection (b), shall issue orders under this Act applicable to manufacturers of concrete masonry products.

==={{anchor|H5CB51C65667D4DE58101646BFD3074A8}}(2) Scope– ===

Any order shall be national in scope.

==={{anchor|HAC8C4B5908F14C008E440A5E82754271}}(3) One order– ===

Not more than one order shall be in effect at any one time.

=={{anchor|H020FD06185FD4289801A23EBB815626D}}(b) Procedures– ==



==={{anchor|H7DF28F06944241CF858492E2ACE0DA58}}(1) Development or receipt of proposed order– ===

A proposed order with respect to concrete masonry products may be—
:(A) prepared by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] at any time; or
:(B) requested by or submitted to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] by—
::(i) an existing national organization of concrete masonry product manufacturers; or
::(ii) any person that may be affected by the issuance of an order with respect to concrete masonry products.

==={{anchor|H7D73CF1B191548D8871CF3AB43A0BC5F}}(2) Publication of proposed order– ===

Not later than 60 days after receiving a proposed order or a request for a proposed order in accordance with subparagraph (B) of paragraph (1), the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall publish a proposed order in the Federal Register and give 30 days notice and opportunity for public comment on the proposed order.

==={{anchor|H577C6A6F313343FF8515D246FDE6633A}}(3) Issuance of order– ===



===={{anchor|H7FA0B1B088A946FCB6283A066401E534}}(A) In general– ====

After notice and opportunity for public comment are provided in accordance with paragraph (2), the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall issue the order, taking into consideration the comments received and including in the order such provisions as are necessary to ensure that the order is in conformity with this Act.

===={{anchor|HF711DD2065324E9289ABF9BD62D6C04C}}(B) Effective date– ====

The order shall be issued and become effective only after an affirmative vote in a referendum as provided in section 7, but not later than 120 days after publication of the proposed order.

=={{anchor|HAA7DAD1D577746DE8506E13892ED8EDE}}(c) Amendments– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may, from time to time, amend an order. The provisions of this Act applicable to an order shall be applicable to any amendment to an order.

={{anchor|HD84CC13F3A584ED2869F734EDE3D87DC}}Sec. 5. Required terms in orders=



=={{anchor|HCFD6A7896CB941DC8C7CD3673A0A1D7B}}(a) In general– ==

Any order issued under this Act shall contain the terms and provisions specified in this section.

=={{anchor|H6AE991424E1441C9BE0F48738E14DB2B}}(b) Concrete Masonry Products Board– ==



==={{anchor|H0C97300A6E504CE8897AD3C7ADE3B3A1}}(1) Establishment and membership– ===



===={{anchor|HD5E4D6C8ADEC4D5BB68052AA14688703}}(A) Establishment– ====

The order shall provide for the establishment of a [[w:|Concrete Masonry Products Board]] to carry out a program of generic promotion, research, and information regarding concrete masonry products.

===={{anchor|H21AFC6739DE6404CBFD9772F21AA6968}}(B) Membership– ====



====={{anchor|HA92B039F76B241FDBFC81C4EEA0AF0B1}}(i) Number of members– =====

The board shall consist of not more than 25 members.

====={{anchor|H6892D23A03D74011BB242E20F176FB1F}}(ii) Appointment– =====

The members of the [[w:|Board]] shall be appointed by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] from nominations submitted as provided in this subsection.

====={{anchor|H6093F6FCDB3645EB8EEEE4BC43C5745E}}(iii) Composition– =====

The [[w:|Board]] shall consist of manufacturers.

==={{anchor|H8740BD866B954BF3B6C62B5995BFE78A}}(2) Distribution of appointments– ===



===={{anchor|H22F221EEBFC54FF0B065FA5B841CABA1}}(A) Geographical representation– ====

To ensure fair and equitable representation of the concrete masonry products industry, the composition of the [[w:|Board]] shall reflect the geographical distribution of the manufacture of concrete masonry products in the United States and the types of concrete masonry products manufactured.

===={{anchor|H736BD3AF4C294C38AD7AD9EA0214762C}}(B) Adjustment in board representation– ====

Three years after the assessment of concrete masonry products commences pursuant to an order, and at the end of each 3-year period thereafter, the [[w:|Board]] , subject to the review and approval of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] , shall, if warranted, recommend to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] the reapportionment of the [[w:|Board]] membership to reflect changes in the geographical distribution of the manufacture of concrete masonry products and the types of concrete masonry products manufactured.

==={{anchor|H8E4D145CDFF54B71BD1BEB2BE3C1BD66}}(3) Nominations Process– ===

The order shall provide the following:

===={{anchor|HD09A9FC7A3B44DD5BCEC518DDAF6685E}}(A) Number of nominations– ====

Two nominees shall be submitted for each appointment to the [[w:|Board]] .

===={{anchor|HDC22E646F6A24936A9B9250B9826FE01}}(B) Procedure– ====

Nominations for each appointment of a manufacturer shall be made by manufacturers in accordance with procedures specified in the order.

===={{anchor|H8A55C96082304833BC6F948387D07E75}}(C) Failure to nominate– ====

In any case in which manufacturers fail to nominate individuals for an appointment to the [[w:|Board]] , the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may appoint an individual to fill the vacancy on a basis provided in the order or other regulations of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] .

===={{anchor|H7AA98DE73B7F4F70A3A28A06F7E4A7CE}}(D) Failure to appoint– ====

If the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] fails to make an appointment to the [[w:|Board]] within 30 days of receiving nominations for such appointment, the first nominee for such appointment shall be deemed appointed.

==={{anchor|HECFA8539380640858C10538CBF208780}}(4) Alternates– ===

The order shall provide for the selection of alternate members of the [[w:|Board]] by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] in accordance with procedures specified in the order.

==={{anchor|H0691759DC6ED4FF996C5D39ACBFD6583}}(5) Terms– ===



===={{anchor|H0F3C50ACAB504682996FCC707BCCDEF3}}(A) In general– ====

The members and any alternates of the [[w:|Board]] shall each serve for a term of 3 years, except that members and any alternates initially appointed to the [[w:|Board]] shall serve for terms of not more than 2, 3, and 4 years, as specified by the order.

===={{anchor|HDA980C2657704A788EC54336460AD49E}}(B) Limitation on consecutive terms– ====

A member or alternate may serve not more than 2 consecutive terms.

===={{anchor|H14F38D8F3D1640CEA3BF48767FD9E4BD}}(C) Continuation of term– ====

Notwithstanding subparagraph (B), each member or alternate shall continue to serve until a successor is appointed by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] .

===={{anchor|HBF21C7FAAE69465289022D1AE5485CA2}}(D) Vacancies– ====

A vacancy arising before the expiration of a term of office of an incumbent member or alternate of the [[w:|Board]] shall be filled in a manner provided for in the order.

==={{anchor|H0B1EDEFE9D534D7E9C65C607B1599FC9}}(6) Disqualification from Board service– ===

The order shall provide that if a member or alternate of the [[w:|Board]] who was appointed as a manufacturer ceases to qualify as a manufacturer, such member or alternate shall be disqualified from serving on the [[w:|Board]] .

==={{anchor|H51D247D0BFC14FAC918E6CF920C1E58A}}(7) Compensation– ===



===={{anchor|H77E1CB3DFD5C41BF8ACFDBE41911B3D0}}(A) In general– ====

Members and any alternates of the [[w:|Board]] shall serve without compensation.

===={{anchor|H2F525C1E77804C79AEDA5ACC0E1B854D}}(B) Travel expenses– ====

If approved by the [[w:|Board]] , members or alternates shall be reimbursed for reasonable travel expenses, which may include per diem allowance or actual subsistence incurred while away from their homes or regular places of business in the performance of services for the [[w:|Board]] .

=={{anchor|H4D8F26AA25A64A7394EBEA06BD23F004}}(c) Powers and duties of the Board– ==

The order shall specify the powers and duties of the [[w:|Board]] , which shall include the power and duty—
:(1) to administer the order in accordance with its terms and conditions and to collect assessments;
:(2) to develop and recommend to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] for approval such bylaws as may be necessary for the functioning of the board and such rules as may be necessary to administer the order, including activities authorized to be carried out under the order;
:(3) to meet, organize, and select from among members of the [[w:|Board]] a chairperson, other officers, and committees and subcommittees, as the [[w:|Board]] determines appropriate;
:(4) to establish regional organizations or committees to administer regional initiatives;
:(5) to establish working committees of persons other than [[w:|Board]] members;
:(6) to employ such persons, other than the members, as the board considers necessary, and to determine the compensation and specify the duties of the persons;
:(7) to prepare and submit for the approval of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] , before the beginning of each fiscal year, rates of assessment under section 6and an annual budget of the anticipated expenses to be incurred in the administration of the order, including the probable cost of each promotion, research, and information activity proposed to be developed or carried out by the [[w:|Board]] ;
:(8) to borrow funds necessary for the startup expenses of the order;
:(9) to carry out research, education, and promotion programs and projects, and to pay the costs of such programs and projects with assessments collected under section 6;
:(10) subject to subsection (e), to enter into contracts or agreements to develop and carry out programs or projects of research, education, and promotion relating to concrete masonry products;
:(11) to keep minutes, books, and records that reflect the actions and transactions of the [[w:|Board]] , and promptly report minutes of each [[w:|Board]] meeting to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] ;
:(12) to receive, investigate, and report to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] complaints of violations of the order;
:(13) to furnish the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] with such information as the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may request;
:(14) to recommend to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] such amendments to the order as the [[w:|Board]] considers appropriate; and
:(15) to provide the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] with advance notice of meetings.

=={{anchor|H93115B138AFA496082B20F022C1B06CB}}(d) Programs and projects; budgets; expenses– ==



==={{anchor|H943DEC9B3289459FA16A3196F662AE11}}(1) Programs and projects– ===

The order shall require the [[w:|Board]] to submit to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] for approval any program or project of research, education, or promotion.

==={{anchor|HD1237544D6974E929F5408488FF6C7C7}}(2) Budgets– ===



===={{anchor|H9DF274AC12684CB8B53029D65D83D5BB}}(A) Submission– ====

The order shall require the [[w:|Board]] to submit to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] for approval a budget of the anticipated expenses and disbursements of the [[w:|Board]] in the implementation of the order, including the projected costs of concrete masonry products research, education, and promotion programs and projects.

===={{anchor|HCC779C0273784F488216631768622828}}(B) Timing– ====

The budget shall be submitted before the beginning of a fiscal year and as frequently as may be necessary after the beginning of the fiscal year.

===={{anchor|HA03B660DBDFC4E13B5A721D7BE1F058A}}(C) Approval– ====

If the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] fails to approve or reject a budget within 30 days of receipt, such budget shall be deemed approved.

==={{anchor|H48A946F6B50A499097513CAE83579E5A}}(3) Administrative Expenses– ===



===={{anchor|HA09F8D27F0FA4B728D5F5FD04C677FD3}}(A) Incurring expenses– ====

A board may incur the expenses described in paragraph (2)and other expenses for the administration, maintenance, and functioning of the [[w:|Board]] as authorized by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] .

===={{anchor|H997C40BCE1874918879A9812C4415793}}(B) Payment of expenses– ====

Expenses incurred under subparagraph (A)shall be paid by the [[w:|Board]] using assessments collected under section 6, earnings obtained from assessments, and other income of the [[w:|Board]] . Any funds borrowed by the [[w:|Board]] shall be expended only for startup costs and capital outlays.

===={{anchor|H972400433F7C404D96366B06E5658A9A}}(C) Limitation on spending– ====

For fiscal years beginning 3 or more years after the date of the establishment of the [[w:|Board]] , the [[w:|Board]] may not expend for administration (except for reimbursement to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] required under subparagraph (D)), maintenance, and functioning of the [[w:|Board]] in a fiscal year an amount that exceeds 10 percent of the assessment and other income received by the [[w:|Board]] for the fiscal year.

===={{anchor|H11DBB8AF08FD4B77BC3E1E9F742CD606}}(D) Reimbursement of Secretary– ====

The order shall require that the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] be reimbursed from assessments for all expenses incurred by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] in the implementation, administration, and supervision of the order, including all referenda costs incurred in connection with the order.

=={{anchor|HC839F1A8DB4A4686AC6A5F8AF8105C4D}}(e) Contracts and agreements– ==



==={{anchor|H9C28DB69897347ACB74540011D54E975}}(1) In general– ===

The order shall provide that, with the approval of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] , the [[w:|Board]] may—
:(A) enter into contracts and agreements to carry programs and projects of research, education, and promotion activities relating to concrete masonry products, including contracts and agreements with manufacturer associations or other entities as considered appropriate by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] ;
:(B) enter into contracts and agreements for administrative services; and
:(C) pay the cost of approved research, education, and promotion programs and projects using assessments collected under section 6, earnings obtained from assessments, and other income of the [[w:|Board]] .

==={{anchor|H11C8BDA0605C4C8EBE9F27F53811147B}}(2) Requirements– ===

Each contract or agreement shall provide that any person who enters into the contract or agreement with the [[w:|Board]] shall—
:(A) develop and submit to the [[w:|Board]] a proposed activity together with a budget that specifies the cost to be incurred to carry out the activity;
:(B) keep accurate records of all of transactions relating to the contract or agreement;
:(C) account for funds received and expended in connection with the contract or agreement;
:(D) make periodic reports to the [[w:|Board]] of activities conducted under the contract or agreement; and
:(E) make such other reports as the [[w:|Board]] or the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] considers relevant.

==={{anchor|H223AF6B745DC4F17AF83A029DCB38B65}}(3) Failure to approve– ===

If the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] fails to approve or reject a contract or agreement entered into under paragraph (1)within 30 days of receipt, the contract or agreement shall be deemed approved.

=={{anchor|H9A25132999FE4DF592D881A02D82AAD9}}(f) Books and records of Board– ==



==={{anchor|HDC82B9C3BFD74850A985BDEC0966736D}}(1) In general– ===

The order shall require the [[w:|Board]] to—
:(A) maintain such books and records (which shall be available to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] for inspection and audit) as the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may require;
:(B) collect and submit to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] , at any time the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may specify, any information the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may request; and
:(C) account for the receipt and disbursement of all funds in the possession, or under the control, of the [[w:|Board]] .

==={{anchor|HFCEAB232CA2C48438D31164731FA019A}}(2) Audits– ===

The order shall require the [[w:|Board]] to have—
:(A) the books and records of the [[w:|Board]] audited by an independent auditor at the end of each fiscal year; and
:(B) a report of the audit submitted directly to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] .

=={{anchor|HC6B7C905456F49B799A03EB700C24751}}(g) Prohibited activities– ==



==={{anchor|HF3AAC058844B4F5D9B3FDFC73120E84B}}(1) In general– ===

Subject to paragraph (2), the [[w:|Board]] shall not engage in any action to, nor shall any funds received by the [[w:|Board]] under this Act be used to—
:(A) influence legislation or governmental action;
:(B) engage in an action that would be a conflict of interest; or
:(C) engage in advertising that is false or misleading.

==={{anchor|HAB771243F9F54DAF82C149D8B23FA04C}}(2) Exceptions– ===

 Paragraph (1)does not preclude—
:(A) the development and recommendation of amendments to the order;
:(B) the communication to appropriate government officials of information relating to the conduct, implementation, or results of research, education, and promotion activities under the order; or
:(C) any action designed to market concrete masonry products directly to a foreign government or political subdivision of a foreign government.

=={{anchor|HE6549FAC065841FDB93D1296EB0A1005}}(h) Periodic evaluation– ==

The order shall require the [[w:|Board]] to provide for the independent evaluation of all research, education, and promotion activities undertaken under the order.

=={{anchor|H2B3D6CB519BE4173B6D0EE918F2E1471}}(i) Books and records of persons covered by order– ==



==={{anchor|HC5270B6FEE2D40A7B42273D4AFC757D3}}(1) In general– ===

The order shall require that manufacturers and importers of concrete masonry products shall—
:(A) maintain records sufficient to ensure compliance with the order and regulations;
:(B) submit to the [[w:|Board]] any information required by the [[w:|Board]] to carry out its responsibilities; and
:(C) make the records described in subparagraph (A)available, during normal business hours, for inspection by employees or agents of the [[w:|Board]] or the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Department]] , including any records necessary to verify information required under subparagraph (B).

==={{anchor|H8DA250E6618D4512A3402E42B71C8F72}}(2) Time requirement– ===

Any record required to be maintained under paragraph (1)shall be maintained for such time period as the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may prescribe.

==={{anchor|HAAA0A2EB3F8049C3987F017D0D935EAC}}(3) Confidentiality of information– ===



===={{anchor|H970033B76F8F4FEF8E1F52A6D4BC6BDF}}(A) In general– ====

Except as otherwise provided in this Act, all information obtained under paragraph (1)or as part of a referendum under section 7shall be kept confidential by all officers, employees, and agents of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Department]] and of the [[w:|Board]] .

===={{anchor|H4537C7140A824D4E92790CD2E8D2F488}}(B) Suits and hearings– ====

Information referred to in subparagraph (A)may be disclosed only if—
:(i) the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] considers the information relevant; and
:(ii) the information is revealed in a judicial proceeding or administrative hearing brought at the direction or on the request of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] or to which the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] or any officer of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Department]] is a party.

===={{anchor|H3FA45AA863DA4BF8B8D3C30050AA37BE}}(C) General statements and publications– ====

This paragraph does not prohibit—
:(i) the issuance of general statements based on reports or on information relating to a number of persons subject to an order if the statements do not identify the information furnished by any person; or
:(ii) the publication, by direction of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] , of the name of any person violating any order and a statement of the particular provisions of the order violated by the person.

===={{anchor|H33CC3CFB1609409E94118716CE9A329B}}(D) Penalty– ====

Any person who willfully violates this subsection shall be fined not more than $5,000, imprisoned not more than 1 year, or both.

==={{anchor|H179A8710598F4D0597F621958B0FA189}}(4) Withholding information– ===

This subsection does not authorize the withholding of information from [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] .

={{anchor|HD654FE7EE3AE4C52B6CF88C3A917251D}}Sec. 6. Assessments=



=={{anchor|HCA34D8BA88C4464394B67470EE1150DB}}(a) Assessments– ==

The order shall provide that assessments shall be paid by manufacturers with respect to concrete masonry products manufactured and marketed in the United States.

=={{anchor|HB51B8BEE00B94741A0A7326BDEDEF645}}(b) Collection– ==



==={{anchor|H61D40CE7BCD24221B844B633A4484B22}}(1) In general– ===

Assessments required under the order shall be remitted by the manufacturer to the [[w:|Board]] in the manner prescribed by the order.

==={{anchor|H8B25699FAD1C42A8881C4FE720CF1A2D}}(2) Timing– ===

The order shall provide that assessments required under the order shall be remitted to the [[w:|Board]] not less frequently than quarterly.

==={{anchor|H321332AE3D834EFF88B0C20D11C39237}}(3) Records– ===

As part of the remittance of assessments, manufacturers shall identify the total amount due in assessments on all sales receipts, invoices or other commercial documents of sale as a result of the sale of concrete masonry units in a manner as prescribed by the [[w:|Board]] to ensure compliance with the order.

=={{anchor|HB08A011ADDF34429B56CE9CCCC419724}}(c) Assessment rates– ==

With respect to assessment rates, the order shall contain the following terms:

==={{anchor|HD6EB96E68812470DBDA0E938B5554698}}(1) Initial rate– ===

The assessment rate on concrete masonry products shall be $0.01 per concrete masonry unit sold.

==={{anchor|H92BED6221E034749AEAC0969E1E01A66}}(2) Changes in the rate– ===



===={{anchor|H005737A6E6394C4A9AA27325CB4EC52E}}(A) Authority to change rate– ====

The [[w:|Board]] shall have the authority to change the assessment rate. A two-thirds majority of voting members of the [[w:|Board]] shall be required to approve a change in the assessment rate.

===={{anchor|HB60B8A172D9E4A0FB29F6128EABEE2DA}}(B) Limitation on increases– ====

An increase or decrease in the assessment rate with respect to concrete masonry products may not exceed $0.01 per concrete masonry unit sold.

===={{anchor|HF8859FFCC7D94C4E80A8163C8AA87337}}(C) Maximum rate– ====

The assessment rate shall not be in excess of $0.05 per concrete masonry unit.

===={{anchor|HFC6F01BC5CD44004AFD34CC081B027D3}}(D) Limitation on frequency of changes– ====

The assessment rate may not be increased or decreased more than once annually.

=={{anchor|H65CA0606B26A4352A3127E65FF957195}}(d) Late-Payment and interest charges– ==



==={{anchor|HB720066FA9BD4BF399C87E0C96047C31}}(1) In general– ===

Late-payment and interest charges may be levied on each person subject to the order who fails to remit an assessment in accordance with subsection (b).

==={{anchor|HBE3B9DF70A064D0988D5A4F8D5FF66DE}}(2) Rate– ===

The rate for late-payment and interest charges shall be specified by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] .

=={{anchor|HD13F5B336B7343CFA95DBFAE6341B776}}(e) Investment of assessments– ==

Pending disbursement of assessments under a budget approved by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] , a board may invest assessments collected under this section in—
:(1) obligations of the United States or any agency of the United States;
:(2) general obligations of any State or any political subdivision of a State;
:(3) interest-bearing accounts or certificates of deposit of financial institutions that are members of the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Federal Reserve System]] ; or
:(4) obligations fully guaranteed as to principal and interest by the United States.

=={{anchor|H658A5896C8FF4A2CB726CA1F5B9D44D8}}(f) Assessment funds for Regional Initiatives– ==



==={{anchor|H09831F2AA1104C348091984867076451}}(1) In general– ===

The order shall provide that no less than 50 percent of the assessments (less administration expenses) paid by a manufacturer shall be used to support research, education, and promotion plans and projects in support of the geographic region of the manufacturer.

==={{anchor|H411C5AA13AA549BFAF120EF1853D6C16}}(2) Geographic regions– ===

The order shall provide for the following geographic regions:
:(A) Region I shall comprise Connecticut, Delaware, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Vermont, and West Virginia.
:(B) Region II shall comprise Alabama, Florida, Georgia, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, and Virginia.
:(C) Region III shall comprise Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kentucky, Michigan, Minnesota, Nebraska, North Dakota, Ohio, South Dakota, and Wisconsin.
:(D) Region IV shall comprise Arizona, Arkansas, Kansas, Louisiana, Missouri, New Mexico, Oklahoma, and Texas.
:(E) Region V shall comprise Alaska, California, Colorado, Hawaii, Idaho, Montana, Nevada, Oregon, Utah, Washington, and Wyoming.

==={{anchor|H3928BF1C3DC8464DB2204D33C352F161}}(3) Adjustment of geographic regions– ===

The order shall provide that the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may, upon recommendation of the [[w:|Board]] , modify the composition of the geographic regions described in paragraph (2).

={{anchor|HFF1F482941454F35B707DF3534306B8A}}Sec. 7. Referenda=



=={{anchor|H80AC7AA412C8405A854A949DAA647578}}(a) Initial Referendum– ==



==={{anchor|H5AC0E3121F3A44E791E6652A0073E0E2}}(1) Referendum required– ===

During the 60-day period immediately preceding the proposed effective date of the order issued under section 4, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall conduct a referendum among manufacturers required to pay assessments under the order, as provided in section 6.

==={{anchor|H80547F99BD74437599B4EEB08AA5729A}}(2) Approval of order needed– ===

The order shall become effective only if the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] determines that the order has been approved by a simple majority of all votes cast in the referendum.

=={{anchor|HACAC40A6521B4213B823F0B9459E63D6}}(b) Votes permitted– ==



==={{anchor|HADB5E8EF490F43E3BC0CA8C176FB7047}}(1) In general– ===

Each manufacturer eligible to vote in a referendum conducted under this section shall be entitled to cast one vote for each machine cavity in operation that is operated by such manufacturer if they satisfy the eligibility requirements as defined in paragraph (2).

==={{anchor|HC62B82B836F2434DA291FE1905E5CF86}}(2) Eligibility– ===

For purposes of paragraph (1), manufacturers shall be considered to be eligible to vote if they have manufactured concrete masonry products during a period of at least 180 days prior to the referendum.

=={{anchor|H5057777429364C618354FC77DEB027DE}}(c) Manner of conducting referenda– ==



==={{anchor|HDAEB8891655142D9B988E507C77047F2}}(1) In general– ===

Referenda conducted pursuant to this section shall be conducted in a manner determined by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] .

==={{anchor|H10BBEDBA0CD94FF5AD5883E9F47A04D7}}(2) Advance registration– ===

A manufacturer who chooses to vote in any referendum conducted under this section shall register with the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] prior to the voting period, after receiving notice from the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] concerning the referendum under paragraph (4).

==={{anchor|H7B5E937977BD41219421C76D6589BB87}}(3) Voting– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall establish procedures for voting in any referendum conducted under this section. The ballots and other information or reports that reveal or tend to reveal the identity or vote of voters shall be strictly confidential.

==={{anchor|H30C9B6C2744B456C85D1EFC98444DC6D}}(4) Notice– ===

Not later than 30 days before a referendum is conducted under this section with respect to an order, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall notify all manufacturers, in such a manner as determined by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] , of the period during which voting in the referendum will occur. The notice shall explain any registration and voting procedures established under this subsection.

=={{anchor|H98E099CF501B44509D69518F16312D22}}(d) Subsequent referenda– ==

If an order is approved in a referendum conducted under subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall conduct a subsequent referendum—
:(1) at the request of the [[w:|Board]] , subject to the voting requirements of subsections (b)and (c), to ascertain whether eligible manufacturers favor suspension, termination, or continuance of the order; or
:(2) effective beginning on the date that is 5 years after the date of the approval of the order, and at 5-year intervals thereafter, at the request of 25 percent or more of the number of persons eligible to vote under subsection (b).

=={{anchor|H88A3899068DD4F0EBA856EBAC405394E}}(e) Suspension or termination– ==

If, as a result of a referendum conducted under subsection (d), the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] determines that suspension or termination of the order is favored by a simple majority of all votes cast in the referendum, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall—
:(1) not later than 180 days after the referendum, suspend or terminate, as appropriate, collection of assessments under the order; and
:(2) suspend or terminate, as appropriate, activities under the order as soon as practicable and in an orderly manner.

=={{anchor|HFBCE042F26B74C5B9E563CD649D43537}}(f) Costs of referenda– ==

The [[w:|Board]] established under an order with respect to which a referendum is conducted under this section shall reimburse the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] from assessments for any expenses incurred by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] to conduct the referendum.

={{anchor|HFA8B8171494A4E23B302CED7C5792987}}Sec. 8. Petition and review=



=={{anchor|HBE83076554CD4539895D6264BACE3367}}(a) Petition– ==



==={{anchor|H2516BB3D9EBA40229F44E30608C30B14}}(1) In general– ===

A person subject to an order issued under this Act may file with the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] a petition—
:(A) stating that the order, any provision of the order, or any obligation imposed in connection with the order, is not established in accordance with law; and
:(B) requesting a modification of the order or an exemption from the order.

==={{anchor|H77418B03671C4F20BBA7E726CE821205}}(2) Hearing– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall give the petitioner an opportunity for a hearing on the petition, in accordance with regulations issued by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] .

==={{anchor|H8489165B8FEC466EAEF2DA84833657A6}}(3) Ruling– ===

After the hearing, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall make a ruling on the petition. The ruling shall be final, subject to review as set forth in subsection (b).

==={{anchor|HCE90E18917EC4242B1AFE7F7C9A4E6E8}}(4) Limitation on petition– ===

Any petition filed under this subsection challenging an order, any provision of the order, or any obligation imposed in connection with the order, shall be filed within 2 years after the effective date of the order, provision, or obligation subject to challenge in the petition.

=={{anchor|HBB580E4BFE0842958EFEE7ED73CC5D80}}(b) Review– ==



==={{anchor|H77937A0742814934BC3467239B8E72CC}}(1) Commencement of action– ===

The district courts of the United States in any district in which a person who is a petitioner under subsection (a)resides or conducts business shall have jurisdiction to review the ruling of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] on the petition of the person, if a complaint requesting the review is filed no later than 20 days after the date of the entry of the ruling by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] .

==={{anchor|H39FD58D820E541AAB9BBCCE8669ED440}}(2) Process– ===

Service of process in proceedings under this subsection shall be conducted in accordance with the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure.

==={{anchor|H54BFEB8C6C8F4AFCB838276E553B24FB}}(3) Remands– ===

If the court in a proceeding under this subsection determines that the ruling of the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] on the petition of the person is not in accordance with law, the court shall remand the matter to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] with directions—
:(A) to make such ruling as the court shall determine to be in accordance with law; or
:(B) to take such further action as, in the opinion the court, the law requires.

=={{anchor|HAB2C126ADDDB4AA4A3A5D5E33A619D2D}}(c) Enforcement– ==

The pendency of proceedings instituted under this section shall not impede, hinder, or delay the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] or the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] from obtaining relief under section 9.

={{anchor|H93E3CF064DAC4744B8AC74C9D42C31C7}}Sec. 9. Enforcement=



=={{anchor|H99C9CE5F8B7E4EF7A1EB74434BD478B3}}(a) Jurisdiction– ==

A district court of the United States shall have jurisdiction to enforce, and to prevent and restrain any person from violating, this Act or an order or regulation issued by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] under this Act.

=={{anchor|H3514FC6E86C04E60AB3D6D609D4A5BE0}}(b) Referral to Attorney General– ==

A civil action authorized to be brought under this section shall be referred to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General of the United States]] for appropriate action.

=={{anchor|H07BDB6A4B7F547CE9C2B04B030A6D441}}(c) Civil penalties and orders– ==



==={{anchor|H48FD5723CE0946DD9FB367298B9D80BD}}(1) Civil penalties– ===

A person who willfully violates an order or regulation issued by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] under this Act may be assessed by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] a civil penalty of not more than $5,000 for each violation.

==={{anchor|HFBA86695CEF545E5BB067A93E7233B54}}(2) Separate offense– ===

Each violation and each day during which there is a failure to comply with an order or regulation issued by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall be considered to be a separate offense.

==={{anchor|HEDD35203E1EE4E5CAB38505B21CFC581}}(3) Cease-and-desist orders– ===

In addition to, or in lieu of, a civil penalty, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may issue an order requiring a person to cease and desist from violating the order or regulation.

==={{anchor|H9917CE8E4D674F8D8AA67A6969F7C76D}}(4) Notice and hearing– ===

No order assessing a penalty or cease-and-desist order may be issued by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] under this subsection unless the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] provides notice and an opportunity for a hearing on the record with respect to the violation.

==={{anchor|H1FD339D046F34BCA979748CA8E3AC261}}(5) Finality– ===

An order assessing a penalty or a cease-and-desist order issued under this subsection by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall be final and conclusive unless the person against whom the order is issued files an appeal from the order with the appropriate district court of the United States, as provided in subsection (d).

=={{anchor|H1AAEB8E088A14E97BD86B2532357833E}}(d) Additional remedies– ==

The remedies provided in this Act shall be in addition to, and not exclusive of, other remedies that may be available.

={{anchor|H3C80E1CFC4DD41A9AFEC962C76FBCB6C}}Sec. 10. Investigation and power to subpoena=



=={{anchor|H1108D05E126F4E2A98F9A9CB46EAA7D2}}(a) Investigations– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may conduct such investigations as the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] considers necessary for the effective administration of this Act, or to determine whether any person has engaged or is engaging in any act that constitutes a violation of this Act or any order or regulation issued under this Act.

=={{anchor|H56B4B51FAB6A4EC6B544E598ED991EE8}}(b) Subpoenas, oaths, and affirmations– ==



==={{anchor|H33406A5CC845490BAC6B43F59BF67564}}(1) Investigations– ===

For the purpose of conducting an investigation under subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may administer oaths and affirmations, subpoena witnesses, compel the attendance of witnesses, take evidence, and require the production of any records that are relevant to the inquiry. The production of the records may be required from any place in the United States.

==={{anchor|HAF1ABA09F3304B9AB14DBF58508813B6}}(2) Administrative hearings– ===

For the purpose of an administrative hearing held under section 8(a)(2)or section 9(c)(4), the presiding officer may administer oaths and affirmations, subpoena witnesses, compel the attendance of witnesses, take evidence, and require the production of any records that are relevant to the inquiry. The attendance of witnesses and the production of the records may be required from any place in the United States.

=={{anchor|HA8C61939EE84491AABD174BDC714DD95}}(c) Aid of courts– ==



==={{anchor|HD8288AF181D84968B40404C7D09587D9}}(1) In general– ===

In the case of contumacy by, or refusal to obey a subpoena issued under subsection (b)to, any person, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may invoke the aid of any court of the United States within the jurisdiction of which the investigation or proceeding is conducted, or where the person resides or conducts business, in order to enforce a subpoena issued under subsection (b).

==={{anchor|HAA788954A4DF4CD3A649A57A590DAD2C}}(2) Order– ===

The court may issue an order requiring the person referred to in a paragraph (1)to comply with a subpoena referred to in paragraph (1).

==={{anchor|H1E37699199A94406BD6AC5005A4984E2}}(3) Failure to obey– ===

Any failure to obey the order of the court may be punished by the court as a contempt of court.

==={{anchor|HC31B83AACE9A4093A55A8DCC4041B0F1}}(4) Process– ===

Process in any proceeding under this subsection may be served in the United States judicial district in which the person being proceeded against resides or conducts business, or wherever the person may be found.

={{anchor|HF012C962546445109014D8E4A0E1C1DD}}Sec. 11. Suspension or termination=



=={{anchor|H230E9022643D424E9AB3BFEA74D97F9B}}(a) Mandatory suspension or termination– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall suspend or terminate an order or a provision of an order if the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] finds that an order or provision of an order obstructs or does not tend to effectuate the purpose of this Act, or if the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] determines that the order or a provision of an order is not favored by persons voting in a referendum conducted under section 7.

=={{anchor|H34DADDED00514BF899B70E7197EC99A8}}(b) Implementation of suspension or termination– ==

If, as a result of a referendum conducted under section 7, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] determines that the order is not approved, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall—
:(1) not later than 180 days after making the determination, suspend or terminate, as the case may be, collection of assessments under the order; and
:(2) as soon as practicable, suspend or terminate, as the case may be, activities under the order in an orderly manner.

={{anchor|HE2AD094D540246B1835EE325E8E22431}}Sec. 12. Confidentiality=

Nothing in this Act shall be construed to require the [[w:|Board]] to disclose information or records under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 section 552 of title 5, United States Code] .

={{anchor|HAAE51E838DC440A29E36AC0BD2F26A25}}Sec. 13. Amendments to orders=

The provisions of this Act applicable to the order shall be applicable to any amendment to the order, except that section 8shall not apply to an amendment.

={{anchor|H3868C33C30C149188FB64AA32649BEF8}}Sec. 14. Effect on other laws=

This Act shall not affect or preempt any other Federal or State law authorizing research, education, and promotion relating to concrete masonry products.

={{anchor|H8F6A7DDF5A114FC2B255F17275278529}}Sec. 15. Regulations=

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may issue such regulations as may be necessary to carry out this Act and the power vested in the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] under this Act.

={{anchor|H2BA9BCAAB7D2409F99FBDD342D207EA5}}Sec. 16. Limitation on expenditures for administrative expenses=

Funds appropriated to carry out this Act may not be used for the payment of the expenses or expenditures of the [[w:|Board]] in administering the order.
