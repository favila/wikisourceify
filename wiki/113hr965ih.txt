{{Center|A BILL}}

 To prohibit the possession or transfer of junk guns, also known as Saturday Night Specials.   

=Section 1. Prohibition against possession or transfer of junk guns, also known as saturday night specials=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Section 922 of title 18, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(aa) 
::(1) It shall be unlawful for any person to possess or transfer a junk gun which has been shipped or transported in interstate or foreign commerce.
::(2)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Paragraph (1)]shall not apply to the continuous and otherwise lawful possession of a junk gun by a person during any period that began before the effective date of this subsection. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Junk gun defined– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 Section 921(a) of such title]is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(36) 
::(A) The term junk gun means—
:::(i) a handgun that is not a sporting handgun; and
:::(ii) any combination of parts from which a handgun described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 clause (i)]can be assembled.
::(B) The term sporting handgun means a handgun which the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]]has determined, using the criteria applied in making determinations under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/925 section 925(d)(3)], to be of a type generally recognized as particularly suitable for or readily adaptable to sporting purposes. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Penalty– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 Section 924(a)(1)(B)]of such title is amended by inserting“or (aa)”before“of”.
