{{Center|A BILL}}

 To temporarily suspend the collection of entrance fees at units of the National Park System and the National Wildlife Refuge System.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Entrance Fee Suspension Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Suspension of entrance fees at units of the national park system and units of the national wildlife refuge system=



==(a) In general– ==

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, during the 16-day period beginning on April 10, 2014, and ending on April 25, 2014, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] (acting through the [[w:National Park Service|Director of the National Park Service]] and the [[w:U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service|Director of the United States Fish and Wildlife Service]] ) shall suspend the collection of fees for admission to units of the National Park System and units of the National Wildlife Refuge System, respectively.

==(b) Report– ==

Not later than 60 days after the date on which the 16-day period described in subsection (a) expires, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] and the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] a report that includes, if the collection was not suspended under subsection (a) —
:(1) the total amount of fees that would have been collected for admission to units of the National Park System; and
:(2) the total amount of fees that would have been collected for admission to units of the National Wildlife Refuge System. 

=Sec. 3. Funding=



==(a) In general– ==

During fiscal year 2014, the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] shall transfer from discretionary unobligated balances that are not designated as emergency or overseas contingency operations—
:(1) the amount described in section 2(b)(1) to the [[w:National Park Service|National Park Service]] account within the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Department of the Interior]] ; and
:(2) the amount described in section 2(b)(2) to the [[w:U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service|United States Fish and Wildlife Service]] account within the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Department of the Interior]] .

==(b) Offset– ==

   Section 13031(j)(3)(B)(i) of the Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act of 1985 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/19/58c 19 U.S.C. 58c(j)(3)(B)(i)] )is amended by striking“October 29, 2021”and inserting“November 5, 2021”.

==(c) Receipt and acceptance– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall be entitled to receive, shall accept, and shall use to carry out this Act the funds transferred under subsection (a) , without further appropriation. 

=Sec. 4. Budgetary effects=

The budgetary effects of this Act, for the purpose of complying with the Statutory Pay-As-You-Go Act of 2010 , shall be determined by reference to the latest statement titled“Budgetary Effects of PAYGO Legislation”for this Act, submitted for printing in the Congressional Record by the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Budget|Chairman of the Senate Budget Committee]] , provided that such statement has been submitted prior to the vote on passage.
