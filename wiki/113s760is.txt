{{Center|A BILL}}

To require the establishment of Federal customer service standards and to improve the service provided by Federal agencies.

={{anchor|S1}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Government Customer Service Improvement Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|id1035286875f44172be5f9a70bc1c3535}}Sec. 2. Definitions=

In this Act:

=={{anchor|id24c5f11f948e4ca0a152486d053bdaba}}(1) Agency– ==

The term agency —
:(A) means an Executive agency (as defined under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/105 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 105] of title 5, United States Code] ) that provides significant services directly to the public or other entity; and
:(B) does not include an Executive agency if the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] determines that this Act should not apply to the Executive agency for national security reasons.

=={{anchor|id3d490c08cf9043cc802fd07784edeeb6}}(2) Customer– ==

The term customer , with respect to an agency, means any individual or entity that is directly served by an agency.

={{anchor|ide35e577f752d4632803a0ff45146eb64}}Sec. 3. Development of customer service standards=



=={{anchor|ide2ea753f531644aba8d3822247313598}}(a) Government-Wide standards– ==



==={{anchor|id4D62D0C2412042A18BB75B8A1497C402}}(1) In general– ===

The Director of the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] shall develop Government-wide standards for customer service delivery, which shall be included in the Federal Government Performance Plan required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1115 section 1115 of title 31, United States Code] .

==={{anchor|id82C6F468CB8C45B18630CD1C75E93190}}(2) Requirements– ===

The standards developed under paragraph (1)shall include—
:(A) Government-wide goals for continuous service improvements and efforts to modernize service delivery; and
:(B) where appropriate, Government-wide target response times for telephone calls, electronic mail, mail, benefit processing, and payments.

=={{anchor|id44bf08ee7c2547dc860c126a82cbd99f}}(b) Agency standards– ==



==={{anchor|idD38080D69D7E4FB5A1FE179A59940C07}}(1) In general– ===

The Chief Performance Officer for each agency shall establish customer service standards in accordance with the Government-wide standards established under subsection (a), which shall be included in the Agency Performance Plans required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1115 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 1115] of title 31, United States Code] .

==={{anchor|idB34E6F3FE1EC4DADA6594CC22D5F973E}}(2) Requirements– ===

Agency standards established under paragraph (1)shall include, if appropriate—
:(A) target call wait times during peak and non-peak hours;
:(B) target response times for correspondence, both by mail and electronic mail;
:(C) procedures for ensuring all applicable metrics are incorporated into service agreements with nongovernmental individuals and entities;
:(D) target response times for processing benefits and making payments; and
:(E) recommendations for effective publication of customer service contact information, including a mailing address, telephone number, and email address.

=={{anchor|id9773711bce8e48afbdd4fef9d3e2197c}}(c) Customer service input– ==

The Performance Improvement Officer for each agency shall collect information from customers of the agency regarding the quality of customer service provided by the agency. Each agency shall include the information collected under this subsection in the performance report made available by the agency under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1116 section 1116 of title 31, United States Code] .

=={{anchor|id6d57544200964d88a591145eeafac53e}}(d) Annual performance update– ==

The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall include achievements by agencies in meeting the customer service performance measures and standards developed under subsection (a)in each update on agency performance required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1116 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 1116] of title 31, United States Code] .

={{anchor|id9c1281d754e7453f9738b315e6738f89}}Sec. 4. Service improvement unit pilot program=



=={{anchor|id85f1dc65f4c44797b57f15e9287197a7}}(a) Established– ==

The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall establish a pilot program, to be known as the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Service Improvement Unit Pilot Program]] (in this section referred to as the pilot program ), to provide assistance to agencies that do not meet the Government-wide standards established under section 3.

=={{anchor|id1907e945b5ba461a842d0d25c61fae9d}}(b) Personnel– ==

The heads of agencies with expertise in change management, process improvement, and information technology innovation shall detail employees to the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] to work on the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|pilot program]] , based on the expertise and skills required to address service improvement goals.

=={{anchor|id8d083b4c5566461ab23e1bd453de32ce}}(c) Responsibilities– ==

Under the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|pilot program]] , the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] shall work with agencies that are not meeting the customer service standards and performance measures established under section 3to improve and modernize service delivery to develop solutions, including—
:(1) evaluating the efforts of the agency to improve service delivery;
:(2) developing a plan to improve within existing resources and by drawing on expertise and assistance from other agencies (including the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] ) where necessary;
:(3) monitoring implementation by the agency of the plan developed under paragraph (2)until the customer service standards and performance measures are met; and
:(4) submitting to the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] monthly reports on the prog­ress being made to improve service at the agency until the customer service standards are met.

=={{anchor|id4910c132b2314ac4987181c4e1e8c19d}}(d) Report– ==

Not later than 2 years after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the accomplishments and outcomes of the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|pilot program]] and any recommendations relating to achieving the customer service standards and performance measures established under section 3.

=={{anchor|id0c896aa6b1e647e7bb48f1a442a960f7}}(e) Support– ==

The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] shall provide administrative and other support in order to implement the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|pilot program]] under this section. The heads of agencies shall, as appropriate and to the extent permitted by law, provide at the request of the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] up to 2 personnel authorizations who have expertise in change management, process improvement, and information technology innovation to support the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|pilot program]] .

=={{anchor|id361a16c1f62845108ae38c106e7e0217}}(f) Termination– ==

The authority to carry out the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|pilot program]] shall terminate 2 years after the date of enactment of this Act.

={{anchor|id8fc526abc4ae4cd8a1592711b28c2481}}Sec. 5. Retirement reporting=



=={{anchor|id6ffe32edfd024288b87dcb210b0f245e}}(a) Definition– ==

In this section, the term agency has the meaning given that term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 551] of title 5, United States Code] .

=={{anchor|id62b3dc074caa40ae9cb97eefd596b06f}}(b) Reports– ==



==={{anchor|id5EA2EB20EAC846FABAD37C14F043A28E}}(1) In general– ===

Except as provided in paragraph (2)and not later than 30 days after the date of enactment of this Act, and every month thereafter, the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of Personnel Management]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] and the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]] , and issue publicly (including on the Web site of the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] ), a report that—
:(A) for each agency, evaluates the timeliness, completeness, and accuracy of information submitted by the agency relating to employees of the agency who are retiring; and
:(B) indicates—
::(i) the total number of applications for retirement benefits, lump sum death benefits, court ordered benefits, phased retirement, and disability retirement that are pending action by the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] ; and
::(ii) the number of months each such application has been pending.

==={{anchor|id3263C364302945C5AD876DDA477EB355}}(2) Suspension of reporting requirement– ===

 Paragraph (1)shall not apply to the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of Personnel Management]] for any month immediately following a 3-year period in which there are no applications described in paragraph (1)(B)that have been pending for more than 60 days.

=={{anchor|id8cccf3cd2a3b4d499cbd9bc329de6f00}}(c) Modernization timeline– ==

The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of Personnel Management]] shall establish—
:(1) a timetable for the completion of each component of the retirement systems modernization project of the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] , including all data elements required for accurate completion of adjudication; and
:(2) the date by which all Federal payroll processing entities will electronically transmit all personnel data to the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] .

=={{anchor|id0a45bf55848f44ddb9b3af68b7a86201}}(d) Budget request– ==

The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] shall include a detailed statement regarding the progress of the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] in completing the retirement systems modernization project of the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] and recommendations to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] regarding the additional resources needed to fully implement the retirement systems modernization project of the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] in each budget request of the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] submitted as part of the preparation of the budget of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105(a) of title 31, United States Code] .

={{anchor|id7174fe99b887413a8f15ae9572cdd85d}}Sec. 6. No increase in expenditures=

It is the sense of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that no additional funds should be appropriated to carry out this Act.
