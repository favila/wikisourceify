{{Center|A BILL}}

To improve the response to and prevention of sexual assaults involving members of the Armed Forces.

=Section 1. Review and policy regarding Department of Defense investigative practices in response to allegations of sex-related offenses=



==(a) Review– ==

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] shall conduct a review of the practices of the military criminal investigative organizations (Army Criminal Investigation Command, Naval Criminal Investigative Service, and Air Force Office of Special Investigation) regarding the investigation of alleged sex-related offenses involving members of the Armed Forces, including the extent to which the military criminal investigative organizations make a recommendation regarding whether an allegation of a sex-related offense appears founded or unfounded.

==(b) Policy– ==

After conducting the review required by subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] shall develop a uniform policy for the Armed Forces, to the extent practicable, regarding the use of case determinations to record the results of the investigation of a sex-related offense. In developing the policy, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] shall consider the feasibility of adopting case determination methods, such as the uniform crime report, used by nonmilitary law enforcement agencies.

==(c) Sex-Related offense defined– ==

In this section, the term sex-related offense includes—
:(1) any offense covered by [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/920 section 920] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/920a 920a] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/920b 920b] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/920c 920c] , or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/925 925 of title 10, United States Code] (article 120, 120a, 120b, 120c, or 125 of the Uniform Code of Military Justice); or
:(2) an attempt to commit an offense specified in a paragraph (1) as punishable under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/880 section 880 of such title] (article 80 of the Uniform Code of Military Justice).

=Sec. 2. Development of selection criteria for assignment as Sexual Assault Response and Prevention Program Managers, Sexual Assault Response Coordinators, and Sexual Assault Victim Advocates=



==(a) Qualifications for assignment– ==

 Section 1602(e)(2) of the Ike Skelton National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2011([[Public Law -|[[Public Law 111-383|Public Law 111–383]] ]] ; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1561 10 U.S.C. 1561note] ;124 Stat. 4431)is amended—
:(1) by redesignating subparagraph (B)as subparagraph (C); and
:(2) by striking subparagraph (A)and inserting the following new subparagraphs:

<blockquote>
::(A) the qualifications necessary for a member of the Armed Forces or a civilian employee of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] to be selected for assignment to duty as a Sexual Assault Response and Prevention Program Manager, Sexual Assault Response Coordinator, or Sexual Assault Victim Advocate, whether assigned to such duty on a full-time or part-time basis;
::(B) consistent with section 584(c) of the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2012([[Public Law -|[[Public Law 112-81|Public Law 112–81]] ]] ; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1561 10 U.S.C. 1561 note] ;125 Stat. 1433), the training, certification, and status of members of the Armed Forces and civilian employees of the department assigned to duty as Sexual Assault Response and Prevention Program Managers, Sexual Assault Response Coordinators, and Sexual Assault Victim Advocates for the Armed Forces; and.
</blockquote>
