{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide effective criminal prosecutions for certain identity thefts, and for other purposes.    

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “   Stopping Tax Offenders and Prosecuting Identity Theft Act of 2014   ”or the“   STOP Identity Theft Act of 2014   ”.  

=Sec. 2. Use of Department of Justice resources with regard to tax return identity theft=



==(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]should make use of all existing resources of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]], including any appropriate task forces, to bring more perpetrators of tax return identity theft to justice.

==(b) Considerations To be taken into account– ==

In carrying out this section, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]should take into account the following:
:(1) The need to concentrate efforts in those areas of the country where the crime is most frequently reported.
:(2) The need to coordinate with State and local authorities for the most efficient use of their laws and resources to prosecute and prevent the crime.
:(3) The need to protect vulnerable groups, such as veterans, seniors, and minors (especially foster children) from becoming victims or otherwise used in the offense. 

=Sec. 3. Victims of identity theft may include organizations=

  Chapter 47of title 18, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028 section 1028]—
::(A) in subsection (a)(7), by inserting“(including an organization)”after“another person”; and
::(B) in subsection (d)(7), in the matter preceding subparagraph (A), by inserting“or other person”after“specific individual”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028A section 1028A(a)(1)], by inserting“(including an organization)”after“another person”. 

=Sec. 4. Identity theft for purposes of tax fraud=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028  Section 1028(b)(3)of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in subparagraph (B), by striking“or”at the end;
:(2) in subparagraph (C), by inserting“or”after the semicolon; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
::(D) during and in relation to a felony under section 7206 or 7207 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 ; . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 5. Reporting requirement=

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on the Judiciary|Committees on the Judiciary of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary|Senate]]a report that contains the following information:
:(1) Information readily available to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]about trends in the incidence of tax return identity theft.
:(2) Recommendations on additional statutory tools that would aid in the effective prosecution of tax return identity theft.
:(3) The status on implementing the recommendations of the Department’s March 2010 Audit Report 10-21 entitled“The Department of Justice’s Efforts to Combat Identity Theft”.
