{{Center|A BILL}}

To establish and clarify that Congress does not authorize persons convicted of dangerous crimes in foreign courts to freely possess firearms in the United States.

={{anchor|id0DD2EA2CD51C40E7BC425A5F7858DB29}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“No Firearms for Foreign Felons Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|idBFC3737FC1C7496FB0351E5339933952}}Sec. 2. No firearms for foreign felons=



=={{anchor|idD13731E7A21B4D488948A18D36A8FF02}}(a) Definitions– ==



==={{anchor|id0C9DAD7519BE40EDA6D72D450746D315}}(1) Courts– ===

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 Section 921(a) of title 18, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(36) The term any court includes any Federal, State, or foreign court..
</blockquote>


==={{anchor|idBE68C00A3A0F4DE1A16AEB5D46D0E6ED}}(2) Exclusion of certain felonies– ===

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 Section 921(a)(20) of title 18, United States Code] , is amended—
:(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (A)] , by striking“any Federal or State offenses”and inserting“any Federal, State, or foreign offenses”;
:(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (B)] , by striking“any State offense classified by the laws of the State”and inserting“any State or foreign offense classified by the laws of that jurisdiction”; and
:(C) in the matter following[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (B)] , in the first sentence, by inserting before the period the following:“, except that a foreign conviction shall not constitute a conviction of such a crime if the convicted person establishes that the foreign conviction resulted from a denial of fundamental fairness that would violate due process if committed in the United States or from conduct that would be legal if committed in the United States”.

=={{anchor|id93A7AB699E164F66B4821011614B7D5B}}(b) Domestic violence crimes– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 Section 921(a)(33) of title 18, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (A)] —
::(A) in the matter preceding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 clause (i)] , by striking“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (C)] ”and inserting“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (B)] ”; and
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 clause (i)] —
:::(i) by inserting“(I)”after“(i)”;
:::(ii) by striking“and”and inserting“or”; and
:::(iii) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::::(II) is a crime under foreign law that is punishable by imprisonment for a term of not more than 1 year; and; and
</blockquote>

:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (B)(ii)] , by striking“if the conviction has”and inserting the following:

<blockquote>if the conviction—
::(I) occurred in a foreign jurisdiction and the convicted person establishes that the foreign conviction resulted from a denial of fundamental fairness that would violate due process if committed in the United States or from conduct that would be legal if committed in the United States; or
::(II) has.
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|id02C5788A84D54130B0C46098C32F6450}}(c) Penalties– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 Section 924(e)(2)(A)(ii) of title 18, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by striking“an offense under State law”and inserting“an offense under State or foreign law”; and
:(2) by inserting before the semicolon the following:“, except that a foreign conviction shall not constitute a conviction of such a crime if the convicted person establishes that the foreign conviction resulted from a denial of fundamental fairness that would violate due process if committed in the United States or from conduct that would be legal if committed in the United States”.
