{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 5, United States Code, to enhance accountability within the Senior Executive Service, and for other purposes.    

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Senior Executive Service Accountability Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Biennial justification of positions=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3133  Section 3133(a)(2)of title 5, United States Code], is amended by inserting after“positions”the following:“, with a justification for each position (by title and organizational location) and the specific result expected from each position, including the impact of such result on the agency mission,”. 

=Sec. 3. Extension of probationary period=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3393  Section 3393(d)of title 5, United States Code], is amended by striking“1-year”and inserting“2-year”.

==(b) Conforming amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3592 Section 3592(a)(1) of such title]is amended by striking“1-year”and inserting“2-year”. 

=Sec. 4. Modification of pay retention for Senior Executive Service members removed for under performance=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3594  Section 3594(c)(1)(B)of title 5, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 
:(B) 
::(i) any career appointee placed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3594 subsection (a)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3594 (b)(2) of this section]shall be entitled to receive basic pay at the highest of—
:::(I) the rate of basic pay in effect for the position in which placed;
:::(II) the rate of basic pay in effect at the time of the placement for the position the career appointee held in the civil service immediately before being appointed to the Senior Executive Service; or
:::(III) the rate of basic pay in effect for the career appointee immediately before being placed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3594 subsection (a)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3594 (b) of this section]; and
::(ii) any career appointee placed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3594 subsection (b)(1) of this section]shall be entitled to receive basic pay at the rate of basic pay in effect for the position in which placed; and . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 5. Requirement that performance requirements be established in advance=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4312  Section 4312(b)(1)of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by striking“on or”and inserting“not later than 30 calendar days”; and
:(2) by inserting“in writing”after“communicated”. 

=Sec. 6. Amendments to adverse action provisions with respect to career appointees in the Senior Executive Service=



==(a) Suspension for 14 days or less for senior executive service employee– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7501 Paragraph (1) ofSection 7501of title 5, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 
:(1)  “employee”means—
::(A) an individual in the competitive service who is not serving a probationary period or trial period under an initial appointment or who has completed 1 year of current continuous employment in the same or similar positions under other than a temporary appointment limited to 1 year or less; or
::(B) a career appointee in the Senior Executive Service who—
:::(i) has completed the probationary period prescribed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3393 section 3393(d)]; or
:::(ii) was covered by the provisions of subchapter II of this chapter immediately before appointment to the Senior Executive Service; and . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Modification of cause and procedure for suspension and termination– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7543  Section 7543of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7543 subsection (a)], by striking“misconduct,”and inserting“such cause as would promote the efficiency of the service, misconduct,”; and
:(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7543 subsection (b)(1)], by striking“30”and inserting“15”.

===(2) Conforming amendments– ===

 Subchapter V ofchapter 35of title 5, United States Code, is amended—
:(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3593 section 3593]—
::(i) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3593 subsection (a)(2)], by striking“misconduct,”and inserting“such cause as would promote the efficiency of the service, misconduct,”; and
::(ii) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3593 subsection (b)], by striking“misconduct,”and inserting“such cause as would promote the efficiency of the service, misconduct,”; and
:(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3594 section 3594(a)], by striking“misconduct,”and inserting“such cause as would promote the efficiency of the service, misconduct,”. 

=Sec. 7. Mandatory leave for career appointees in the Senior Executive Service=



==(a) In general– ==

 Subchapter II ofchapter 63of title 5, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 6329. Mandatory leave for Senior Executive Service career appointees==


:(a) In this section—
::(1) the term“employee”means—
:::(A) a career appointee in the Senior Executive Service who—
::::(i) has completed the probationary period prescribed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3393 section 3393(d) of this title]; or
::::(ii) was covered by the provisions of subchapter II of chapter 75 of this title immediately before appointment to the Senior Executive Service; and
:::(B) who has received written notice of removal from the civil service undersubchapter V of chapter 75 of this title; and
::(2) the term“mandatory leave”means, with respect to an employee, an absence with pay but without duty during which such employee—
:::(A) shall be charged accrued annual leave for the period of such absence; and
:::(B) may not accrue any annual leave under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6303 section 6303]for the period of such absence.
:(b) Under regulations prescribed by the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]], an agency may place an employee on mandatory leave for misconduct, neglect of duty, malfeasance, or such cause as would promote the efficiency of the service.
:(c) If an agency determines that an employee should be placed on mandatory leave under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6329 subsection (b)], such leave shall begin no earlier than the date on which the employee received written notice of a removal undersubchapter V of chapter 75.
:(d) If a final order or decision is issued in favor of such employee with respect to removal undersubchapter V of chapter 75by the agency, the [[w:Merit Systems Protection Board|Merit Systems Protection Board]], or the [[w:U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit|United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit]], any annual leave that is charged to an employee by operation of this section shall be restored to the applicable leave account of such employee. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections of chapter 63of title 5, United States Code, is amended by adding after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6328 section 6328]the following new item:

<blockquote>  6329. Mandatory leave for Senior Executive Service career appointees.  . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Regulations– ==

Not later than 6 months after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of Personnel Management]]shall prescribe regulations with respect to the leave provided by the amendment in subsection (a) .
