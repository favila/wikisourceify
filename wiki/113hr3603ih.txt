{{Center|A BILL}}

 To limit the construction on United States soil of satellite positioning ground monitoring stations of foreign governments, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Limitation on construction on United States soil of satellite positioning ground monitoring stations of foreign governments=



==(a) Certification– ==

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] may not authorize or permit the construction of a satellite positioning ground monitoring station directly or indirectly controlled by a foreign government on United States soil until the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] and the [[w:|Director of National Intelligence]] jointly certify to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that such monitoring station will not possess the capability or potential to be used for the purpose of gathering intelligence or improving any foreign weapons system.

==(b) Report– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] , the [[w:|Director of National Intelligence]] , and the [[w:|Commander of the United States Strategic Command]] shall jointly submit to the appropriate committees of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the use of satellite positioning ground monitoring stations by foreign governments for the purpose of gathering intelligence or improving the accuracy of missile guidance systems.

===(2) Elements– ===

The report required by paragraph (1) shall include the following:
:(A) A description and assessment of the current and potential use of satellite ground monitoring stations under the control of foreign governments for the purpose of gathering intelligence.
:(B) A description of the role of positioning satellites in ballistic and tactical missile guidance systems.
:(C) A description and assessment of the current and potential future use of satellite positioning ground monitoring stations as a means of improving the accuracy of satellite guided missiles.

===(3) Form– ===

The report required by paragraph (1) shall be submitted in unclassified form, but may include a classified annex.

===(4) Appropriate committees of Congress defined– ===

In this subsection, the term appropriate committees of Congress means—
:(A) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services]] , the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Foreign Relations|Committee on Foreign Relations]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee on Intelligence of the Senate]] ; and
:(B) the [[w:United States House Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services]] , the [[w:United States House Committee on Foreign Affairs|Committee on Foreign Affairs]] , and the [[w:United States House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence|Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence of the House of Representatives]] .
