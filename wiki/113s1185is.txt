{{Center|A BILL}}

To enhance penalties for violations of securities protections that involve targeting seniors.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Senior Investor Protections Enhancement Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Definitions=



==(a) In general– ==

In this Act, the following definitions shall apply:

===(1) Senior– ===

The term senior means an individual who is 62 years of age or older.

===(2) Securities laws– ===

The term securities laws means the Securities Act of 1933( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 77b et seq.] ), the Securities Exchange Act of 1934( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/78a 15 U.S.C. 78a et seq.] ), the Investment Company Act of 1940( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/80a 15 U.S.C. 80a et seq.] ), and the Investment Advisers Act of 1940( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/80b 15 U.S.C. 80b et seq.] ] ).

==(b) Application of senior definition– ==



===(1) Securities Act of 1933– ===

 Section 2(a) of the Securities Act of 1933( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/77b 15 U.S.C. 77b(a)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(20) The term senior means an individual who is 62 years of age or older..
</blockquote>


===(2) Securities Exchange Act of 1934– ===

 Section 3(a) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/78c 15 U.S.C. 78c(a)] ] )is amended—
:(A) by redesignating the first paragraph designated as (80), as added by 101(b)(2) of the Jumpstart Our Business Startups Act(126 Stat. 307)(relating to emerging growth companies), as paragraph (81); and
:(B) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(82) The term senior means an individual who is 62 years of age or older..
</blockquote>


===(3) Investment Company Act of 1940– ===

 Section 2(a) of the Investment Company Act of 1940( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/80a–2 15 U.S.C. 80a–2(a)] ] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(55) The term senior means an individual who is 62 years of age or older..
</blockquote>


===(4) Investment Advisers Act of 1940– ===

 Section 202(a) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/80b–2 15 U.S.C. 80b–2(a)] ] )is amended—
:(A) by redesignating the second paragraph designated as paragraph (29), as added by section 770 of the Wall Street Transparency and Accountability Act of 2010(124 Stat. 1801), as paragraph (31)and moving such paragraph to the end; and
:(B) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(32) The term senior means an individual who is 62 years of age or older..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Enhanced penalties for violations of Securities Act of 1933=



==(a) Civil actions– ==

 Section 20(d)(2) of the Securities Act of 1933( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/77t 15 U.S.C. 77t(d)(2)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(D) Special rule for seniors– ===

Notwithstanding subparagraphs (A), (B), and (C), if a person commits a violation described in paragraph (1), and the violation is directed toward, targets, or is committed against a person who, at the time of the violation, is a senior, the [[w:U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|Commission]] , in addition to any other applicable civil penalty, may impose a civil penalty of not more than $50,000 for each such violation..
</blockquote>


==(b) Other violations– ==

 Section 24 of the Securities Act of 1933( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/77x 15 U.S.C. 77x] )is amended—
:(1) by inserting “(a)In general.—” before “Any person” ; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

====(b) Special rule for seniors– ====

Notwithstanding subsection (a), if a person commits a violation described in subsection (a), and the violation is directed toward, targets, or is committed against a person who, at the time of the violation is a senior, the [[w:U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|Commission]] , in addition to any other applicable civil penalty, may impose a civil penalty of not more than $50,000 for each such violation..
</blockquote>

=Sec. 7. Directive to the United States Sentencing Commission=



==(a) In general– ==

Pursuant to its authority under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/994 section 994(p) of title 28, United States Code] , and in accordance with this section, the [[w:United States Sentencing Commission|United States Sentencing Commission]] shall review and amend the Federal sentencing guidelines and policy statements to ensure that the guideline offense levels and enhancements appropriately punish violations of the securities laws against seniors.

==(b) Requirements– ==

In carrying out this section, the [[w:United States Sentencing Commission|United States Sentencing Commission]] shall—
:(1) ensure that section 2B1.1 and 2C1.1 of the Federal sentencing guidelines (and any successors thereto) apply to and punish offenses in which the victim of a violation of the securities laws is a senior;
:(2) ensure reasonable consistency with other relevant directives, provisions of the Federal sentencing guidelines, and statutory provisions;
:(3) make any necessary and conforming changes to the Federal sentencing guidelines, in accordance with the amendments made by this Act; and
:(4) ensure that the Federal sentencing guidelines adequately meet the purposes of sentencing set forth in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3553 section 3553(a)(2)of title 18, United States Code] .
