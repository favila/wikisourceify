{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 5, United States Code, to prohibit the transfer or reprogramming of discretionary appropriations made available to the Internal Revenue Service, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ The Stop Internal Resource Slush Fund Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Amendment to prohibit the transfer or reprogramming of funds made available to the IRS=



==(a) In general– ==

  Chapter 95of title 5, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 9511. Prohibition on transfer or reprogramming of funds==



===(a) Prohibition– ===

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, and in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/9511 subsection (b)], none of the funds made available to the [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]] shall be eligible for transfer or reprogramming if such funds are derived from—
:(1) fees for services provided by the [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]] ;
:(2) discretionary appropriations for salaries and expenses or other personnel and hiring programs; or
:(3) reimbursable programs.

===(b) Deficit reduction– ===

On the date that is 90 days after the last day of the fiscal year in which such funds were collected or made available, any unobligated amounts derived from[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/9511 subsection (a)(1)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/9511 (a)(2)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/9511 (a)(3)]are rescinded and shall be returned to the general fund of the Treasury for the sole purpose of deficit reduction. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Technical and conforming amendments– ==


:(1) The table of sections for chapter 95of title 5, United States Code, is amended by inserting after the item relating to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/9510 section 9510] the following:

<blockquote>  9511. Internal Revenue Service personnel limitations.  . 
</blockquote>

:(2) The heading for chapter 95of title 5, United States Code, is amended by inserting“ and requirements ”after“ flexibilities ”.
