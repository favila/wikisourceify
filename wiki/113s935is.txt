{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to prohibit the Secretary of Veterans Affairs from requesting additional medical examinations of veterans who have submitted sufficient medical evidence provided by non-Department medical professionals and to improve the efficiency of processing certain claims for disability compensation by veterans, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Quicker Veterans Benefits Delivery Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Treatment of medical evidence provided by non-Department of Veterans Affairs medical professionals in support of claims disability compensation=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5103A Section 5103A(d) of title 38, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>
:(3) During the six-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of the Quicker Veterans Benefits Delivery Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may not request a medical examination under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5103A paragraph (1)] in the case of a claim for disability compensation in support of which a claimant submits medical evidence provided by a non-Department medical professional, including a non-Department medical opinion, that is competent, credible, probative, and otherwise adequate for purposes of making a decision on the claim..
</blockquote>


==(b) Effective date– ==

The amendment made by subsection (a)shall apply with respect to medical evidence submitted after the date that is 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act.

=Sec. 3. Improvement of disability claims processing=



==(a) Prestabilization rates– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 Section 1156 of title 38, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 subsections (c) and (d)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 subsections (e) and (f)] , respectively; and
:(2) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 subsection (b)] the following new [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 subsection (c)] :

<blockquote>

====(c) Prestabilization rates– ====

During the six-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of the Quicker Veterans Benefits Delivery Act of 2013, in assigning to a veteran a prestabilization rating pursuant tosection 4.28of title 38, Code of Federal Regulations, or successor regulation, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall assign such a rating at the level of total, 50 percent, or 30 percent, as determined appropriate by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] ..
</blockquote>


==(b) Temporary minimum rates– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 Section 1156of title 38, United States Code] , is further amended by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 subsection (c)] , as added by subsection (a), the following new [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 subsection (d)] :

<blockquote>

===(d) Temporary minimum rates– ===

During the six-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of the Quicker Veterans Benefits Delivery Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall assign a temporary minimum disability rating to a veteran who—
:(1) has one or more disabilities not covered under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 subsection (a)] ; and
:(2) submits a claim for such disability that has sufficient evidence to support a minimum disability rating..
</blockquote>


==(c) Reporting of claims backlog– ==

During the six-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall not include any veteran to whom the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] has assigned a pre-stabilization rating under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1156 subsection (c) of section 1156 of such title] , as added by subsection (a), or a temporary minimum disability rating under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1156 subsection (d) of such section] , as added by subsection (b), in any count of the backlog of disability ratings to be assigned by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] .

==(d) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall take effect on the date that is 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act and shall apply with respect to claims for disability compensation filed on or after that date.

=Sec. 4. Timing of monthly payments of benefits under the laws administered by the Secretary of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5120 Section 5120(e) of title 38, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by striking “Whenever” and inserting “(1) Whenever” ; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>
::(2) During the six-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of the Quicker Veterans Benefits Delivery Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may certify benefit payments for any calendar month in such a way that such payments will be delivered by mail, or transmitted for credit to the payee’s account pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5120 subsection (d) of this section] , before the first day of the calendar month for which such payments are issued..
</blockquote>


==(b) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by subsection (a)shall apply with respect to a calendar month that begins after the date that is 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act.
