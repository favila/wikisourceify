{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

Authorizing expenditures by the Committee on the Judiciary.

={{anchor|id2DEACF597B7F41BF9C7B51EA8AB77F78}}Section 1. General authority=

In carrying out its powers, duties, and functions under the Standing Rules of the Senate, in accordance with its jurisdiction under rule XXV of the Standing Rules of the Senate, including holding hearings, reporting such hearings, and making investigations as authorized by paragraphs 1 and 8 of rule XXVI of the Standing Rules of the Senate, theCommittee on the Judiciary(in this resolution referred to as the“committee”) is authorized from March 1, 2013 through September 30, 2013, in its discretion to—
:(1) make expenditures from the contingent fund of the Senate;
:(2) employ personnel; and
:(3) with the prior consent of the Government department or agency concerned and theCommittee on Rules and Administration, to use on a reimbursable or nonreimbursable basis the services of personnel of any such department or agency.

={{anchor|ID217b32e9fdd54f97a595910ad52ebe42}}Sec. 2. Expenses for period ending September 30, 2013=

The expenses of thecommitteefor the period March 1, 2013 through September 30, 2013 under this resolution shall not exceed $5,882,131, of which amount—
:(1) not to exceed $200,000 may be expended for the procurement of the services of individual consultants, or organizations thereof (as authorized by section 202(i) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/72a 2 U.S.C. 72a(i)] )); and
:(2) not to exceed $20,000 may be expended for the training of the professional staff of the committee (under procedures specified by section 202(j) of that Act).

={{anchor|ID478da0c840a34fe48e2e25ce9878db1c}}Sec. 3. Expenses and agency contributions=



=={{anchor|ID15785b847a7246dea1d99f0aba018389}}(a) Expenses of the committee– ==



==={{anchor|ID6bdb3418963c4980b390eb04801c6da0}}(1) In general– ===

Except as provided in paragraph (2), expenses of the committee under this resolution shall be paid from the contingent fund of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] upon vouchers approved by thechairman of the committee.

==={{anchor|ID8c915fe43d664ee19c05aa93185762c3}}(2) Vouchers not required– ===

Vouchers shall not be required for—
:(A) the disbursement of salaries of employees paid at an annual rate;
:(B) the payment of telecommunications provided by the [[w:United States Senate|Office of the Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper]] ;
:(C) the payment of stationery supplies purchased through the [[w:United States Senate|Keeper of the Stationery]] ;
:(D) payments to the [[w:United States Senate|Postmaster of the Senate]] ;
:(E) the payment of metered charges on copying equipment provided by the [[w:United States Senate|Office of the Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper]] ;
:(F) the payment of Senate Recording and Photographic Services; or
:(G) the payment of franked and mass mail costs by the [[w:United States Senate|Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, United States Senate]] .

=={{anchor|ID0d031a12098648e88168c67a2ebe67d3}}(b) Agency contributions– ==

There are authorizedsuch sums as may be necessaryfor agency contributions related to the compensation of employees of thecommitteefrom March 1, 2013 through September 30, 2013, to be paid from theappropriations account for“Expenses of Inquiries and Investigations”of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] .
