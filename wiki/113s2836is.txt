{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide for an integrated plan for the space launch activities of the Federal Government.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Integrated Space Launch Policy Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Integrated plan on space launch activities of the Federal Government=



==(a) Plan required– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]and the [[w:National Aeronautics and Space Administration|Administrator of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration]]shall jointly, in coordination with the [[w:United States National Security Council|National Security Council]], the [[w:Office of Science and Technology Policy|Director of the Office of Science and Technology Policy]], and the heads of other appropriate agencies of the Federal Government, develop a plan (to be known as the“Integrated Federal Space Launch Plan”) to achieve the effective planning, coordination, and execution of the civilian and national security space launch activities of the Federal Government in order to ensure that the mission needs of the Federal Government for reliable, timely, and affordable access to space are met in a cost-effective manner.

==(b) Elements of plan– ==

The plan developed under subsection (a) shall include, at a minimum, the following:
:(1) An estimate of the anticipated annual space launch demand of the Federal Government during the 10 fiscal years beginning with the fiscal year beginning in the year in which the plan is developed.
:(2) A description of the capabilities required to meet the demand estimated for purposes of paragraph (1).
:(3) A description of the acquisition plans of each Federal agency covered by the plan for purposes of meeting the demand estimated for purposes of paragraph (1).
:(4) An identification and assessment of opportunities for coordination among Federal agencies in space launch acquisition efforts, and a summary of the lessons learned by the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]and the [[w:National Aeronautics and Space Administration|National Aeronautics and Space Administration]]regarding their launch service programs.
:(5) An assessment whether the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]is currently achieving assured access to space with its space launch in a manner consistent with the provisions of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2273  section 2273of title 10, United States Code].

==(c) Submittal of plan to Congress– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]]and the [[w:National Aeronautics and Space Administration|Administrator]]shall jointly submit the plan developed under subsection (a) to the appropriate committees of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]].
:(2) Form– The plan shall be submitted in unclassified form, but may include a classified annex.

==(d) Comptroller General of the United States assessment– ==

The [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]]shall submit to the appropriate committees of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report setting forth an assessment of the adequacy of the plan developed under subsection (a), including the extent to which the plan includes the launch needs and capabilities of the civilian agencies and the national security agencies of the Federal Government.

==(e) Appropriate committees of Congress defined– ==

In this section, the term appropriate committees of Congress means—
:(1) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services]], the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation]], the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Committee on Appropriations]], the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Budget|Committee on the Budget]], and the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee on Intelligence of the Senate]]; and
:(2) the [[w:United States House Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services]], the [[w:United States House Committee on Science, Space and Technology|Committee on Science, Space, and Technology]], the [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|Committee on Appropriations]], the [[w:United States House Committee on the Budget|Committee on the Budget]], and the [[w:United States House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence|Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence of the House of Representatives]].
