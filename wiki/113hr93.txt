{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend chapter 44 of title 18, United States Code, to restrict the ability of a person whose Federal license to import, manufacture, or deal in firearms has been revoked, whose application to renew such a license has been denied, or who has received a license revocation or renewal denial notice, to transfer business inventory firearms, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HCF19D3C697104ABC9E8F5627A91DCF43}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Fire Sale Loophole Closing Act”.

={{anchor|H0F2D9A78A3864A64BC546CCC4D802179}}Sec. 2. Restrictions on the ability of a person whose Federal license to import, manufacture, or deal in firearms has been revoked, whose application to renew such a license has been denied, or who has received a license revocation or renewal denial notice, to transfer business inventory firearms=



=={{anchor|H8E02D2FBA8274494A04D86DED35EAB67}}(a) Restrictions– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Section 922 of title 18, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(aa) 
::(1) 
:::(A) It shall be unlawful for a person who has been notified by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] that the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] has made a determination to revoke a license issued to the person under this chapter to import, manufacture, or deal in firearms, or to deny an application of the person to renew such a license, to—
::::(i) transfer a business inventory firearm of the person—
:::::(I) into a personal collection of the person; or
:::::(II) to an employee of the person, or to an individual described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/923 section 923(d)(1)(B)] with respect to the person; or
::::(ii) receive a firearm that was a business inventory firearm of the person as of the date the person received the notice.
:::(B) [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Subparagraph (A)] shall not apply with respect to a license revocation or denial determination that is rescinded.
::(2) 
:::(A) It shall be unlawful for a person, on or after the effective date of the revocation of a license issued to the person under this chapter to import, manufacture, or deal in firearms, or (in the case that the application of the person to renew such a license is denied) on or after the date the license expires, to—
::::(i) engage in conduct prohibited by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (1)] ; or
::::(ii) transfer to any other person (except a person licensed under this chapter or a Federal, State, or local law enforcement agency) a firearm that was a business inventory firearm of the person as of the effective date or expiration date, as the case may be.
:::(B) [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Subparagraph (A)] shall not apply with respect to a license revocation or denial determination that is reversed..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H2F1CC763FE744CDCAED56AC19D834BE0}}(b) Business inventory defined– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 Section 921(a) of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H7629467D904D4F818D2ABC197FE3E671}}(36) Business inventory firearm– ===

The term business inventory firearm means, with respect to a person, a firearm required by law to be recorded in the acquisition and disposition logs of any firearms business of the person..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H42C5E57EB1C34883A5F3852C3B503678}}(c) Conforming amendment– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/923 Section 923(c) of such title] is amended in the 2nd sentence by inserting“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(aa)] and to”after“subject only to”.

=={{anchor|H7C0F2103191C4365BB29F1F6BADD12DD}}(d) Penalties– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 Section 924(a) of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(8) Whoever knowingly violates[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(aa)] shall be fined under this title, imprisoned not more than 1 year (or, if the violation was willful, 5 years), or both..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H62C703B58D81435CA61453D91CDC1717}}(e) Requirement that license revocation or application denial notice include text of law prohibiting dealing in firearms without a Federal firearms license and restricting transfer of firearms after receipt of official license revocation or renewal application denial notice– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/923 Section 923(f)(1) of such title] is amended in the last sentence by inserting“, and shall set forth the provisions of Federal law and regulation which prohibit a person not licensed under this chapter from engaging in the business of dealing in firearms or are relevant in determining whether a person is doing so, and the provisions of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(aa)] ”before the period.
