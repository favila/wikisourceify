{{Center|A BILL}}

 To suspend from the visa waiver program any country that has identified passport holders fighting with an Islamist extremist organization, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Suspension from visa waiver program=



==(a) In general– ==

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]], in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]], immediately shall suspend from the visa waiver program established under  section 217 of the Immigration and Nationality Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1187 8 U.S.C. 1187] )any country that has identified passport holders fighting with Islamic extremist organizations, including such groups as the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL) and al-Qaeda affiliated organization.

==(b) Restoration of participation– ==

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]shall restore a suspended country’s participation in the visa waiver program upon a determination by the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]], the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]], and the [[w:|Director of National Intelligence]]of the ability of both the suspended country and the United States to identify, track, and negate the risk of any individual entering the United States from such country under the visa waiver program. 

=Sec. 2. Sense of Congress=

It is the sense of the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that—
:(1) the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]]should conduct a review of the national security risks associated with the visa waiver program and submit the results of that review to the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]];
:(2) the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]should take steps to strengthen the electronic travel authorization system described in  section 217(h)(3) of the Immigration and Nationality Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1187 8 U.S.C. 1187(h)(3)] ), identify any individual fighting with an Islamic extremist organization, and deny such individual the ability to enter the United States by means of such system; and
:(3) the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]should complete as expeditiously as possible the biometric entry and exit data system described in  section 7208 of the 9/11 Commission Implementation Act of 2004 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1365b 8 U.S.C. 1365b] ). 

=Sec. 3. Technical amendments=

   Section 217 of the Immigration and Nationality Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1187 8 U.S.C. 1187] )is amended by striking“  [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ”each place the term appears (except in subsection (c)(11)(B)) and inserting“  [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] ”.
