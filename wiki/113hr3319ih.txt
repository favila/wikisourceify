{{Center|A BILL}}

To modernize the Federal Employees Health Benefits Program, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Equal Healthcare Access Act” .

=Sec. 2. Access to Federal health insurance=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/ Subpart G of part III of title 5, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by redesignatingchapters 89Aand89Baschapters 89Band89C, respectively; and
:(2) by inserting afterchapter 89the following:

<blockquote>

====89A Health insurance for non-Federal employees– ====

**[[#H3B75DBE9F07043089AB2394870492D01|Sec.]]
8921. Definitions.**[[#HE5AC6107C2984AE183F41C86A651EF74|8922. Health insurance program.]]
**[[#H2E9AFE11294944B285C13AE3DA8F6870|8923. Contracting requirement.]]
**[[#H5D81CEF76EFD47898A4D012AB67F33D5|8924. Eligibility to enroll.]]
**[[#HFB8DFD919A9D42A187C80ED81F1EB8A5|8925. Exceptions to provisions incorporated by reference.]]
8926. Coordination and application provisions.

====Sec. 8921. Definitions====

For purposes of this chapter—
:(1) any term used in this chapter which is defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/8901 section 8901] shall have the same meaning as is given such term under such section; and
:(2) the term Office means the Office of Personnel Management.

====Sec. 8922. Health insurance program====



=====(a) In general– =====

The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office]] shall administer a health insurance program for non-Federal employees in accordance with this chapter.

=====(b) Regulations– =====

The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office]] shall prescribe regulations under which, except as otherwise provided in this chapter and to the maximum extent practicable, the provisions ofchapter 89shall be applied for purposes of carrying out this chapter.

====Sec. 8923. Contracting requirement====

The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office]] shall, not later than 9 months after the date of the enactment of the Equal Healthcare Access Act, enter into contracts with qualified carriers to make health benefits plans available under this chapter.

====Sec. 8924. Eligibility to enroll====



=====(a) In general– =====

Any qualified individual may enroll in a health benefits plan under this chapter.

=====(b) Qualified individual defined– =====

For purposes of this section, the term qualified individual means any individual other than an individual who is enrolled or eligible to be enrolled in a health benefits plan underchapter 89, including as a family member.

====Sec. 8925. Exceptions to provisions incorporated by reference====

Notwithstanding any other provision of law—
:(1) subscription charges for a health benefits plan under this chapter may differ between or among geographic regions; and
:(2) an employer may, under arrangements satisfactory to the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office]] —
::(A) offer coverage under this chapter to its employees; and
::(B) make a contribution toward the cost of such coverage.

====Sec. 8926. Coordination and application provisions====



=====(a) Coordination– =====

A health benefits plan under this chapter shall be deemed to be a health plan offered through an Exchange established under the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act([[Public Law -|[[Public Law 111-148|Public Law 111–148]] ]] ;124 Stat. 119)for purposes of section 1312 of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/18032 42 U.S.C. 18032] ).

=====(b) Application– =====



======(1) In general– ======

In the case of a qualified individual enrolled in a health benefits plan under this chapter—
:(A) for purposes of  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/36B section 36B] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, such plan shall be treated as a qualified health plan described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/8926 subsection (b)(2)(A)] thereof that was enrolled in through an Exchange established by a State under section 1311 of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act; and
:(B) for purposes of section 1402 of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/18071 42 U.S.C. 18071] ), such plan shall be treated as a qualified health plan in the silver level of coverage in the individual market offered through an Exchange.

======(2) Regulations– ======

The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] , in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] , shall prescribe regulations necessary to carry out this subsection..
</blockquote>
