{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for State approval of national monuments, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H0B332A92B557479AB9851236EB52934E}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Preserve Land Freedom For Americans Act”.

={{anchor|H04EEAD99DBF94C8CB5251BAB5663CDD3}}Sec. 2. Designation of national monuments=

The Act of June 8, 1906([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/431 16 U.S.C. 431 et seq.] )is amended—
:(1) in section 2([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/431 16 U.S.C. 431] ), by striking“Sec. 2.That the”and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HFB747BEAC6444468B5E3885B6A1B0C12}}Sec. 2. Designation of national monuments==

After obtaining approval from the Governor and the legislature of each State within the boundaries of which the proposed national monument would be located, the; and
</blockquote>

:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H8C267F9466504CF0B834A579513EAA14}}Sec. 5. Restrictions on public use==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall not implement any restrictions on the public use of a national monument until the expiration of an appropriate review period (as determined by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] ) providing for public input and State approval under section 2..
</blockquote>
