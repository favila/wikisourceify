{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to require the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to consider the resources of individuals applying for pension that were recently disposed of by the individuals for less than fair market value when determining the eligibility of such individuals for such pension, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Veterans Pension Protection Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Consideration by Secretary of Veterans Affairs of resources disposed of for less than fair market value by individuals applying for pension=



==(a) Veterans– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1522 Section 1522 of title 38, United States Code], is amended—

==(c) Effective date– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1522 Subsections (a)(2)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1522 (b)(2)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1522 (c) of section 1522 of title 38, United States Code], as added by subsection (a) , and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1543 subsections (a)(2)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1543 (a)(4)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1543 (b)(2)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1543 (c) of section 1543 of such title], as added by subsection (b) , shall take effect on the date that is one year after the date of the enactment of this Act and shall apply with respect to payments of pension and increased pension applied for after such date and to payments of pension and increased pension for which eligibility is redetermined after such date, except that no reduction in pension shall be made under such subsections because of any disposal of covered resources made before such date.

==(d) Annual reports– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than two years after the date of the enactment of this Act and not less frequently than once each year thereafter through 2018, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall submit to the appropriate committees of Congress a report on the administration of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1522 subsections (a)(2)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1522 (b)(2)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1522 (c) ofsection 1522of title 38, United States Code], as added by subsection (a) , and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1543 subsections (a)(2)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1543 (a)(4)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1543 (b)(2)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1543 (c) of section 1543 of such title], as added by subsection (b) , during the most recent 12-month period.

===(2) Elements– ===

Each report submitted under paragraph (1) shall include the following, for the period covered by the report:
:(A) The number of individuals who applied for pension under chapter 15 of such title.
:(B) The number of individuals who received pension under such chapter.
:(C) The number of individuals with respect to whom the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]denied or discontinued payment of pension under the subsections referred to in paragraph (1) .
:(D) A description of any trends identified by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]regarding pension payments that have occurred as a result of the amendments made by this section.
:(E) Such other information as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]considers appropriate.

===(3) Appropriate committees of Congress defined– ===

In this subsection, the term appropriate committees of Congress means—
:(A) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs]]and the [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|Select Committee on Aging of the Senate]]; and
:(B) the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the House of Representatives]].
