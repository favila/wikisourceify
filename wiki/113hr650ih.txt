{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 to establish a base minimum wage for tipped employees.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Working for Adequate Gains for Employment in Services Act ”or the“ WAGES Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Base minimum wage for tipped employees=

   Section 3(m)(1) of the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/203 29 U.S.C. 203(m)(1)])is amended by adding before the semicolon the following:

<blockquote> , except that, for purposes of this paragraph, the cash wage paid such employee shall be not less than— 
:(A) $3.75 an hour beginning 90 days after the date of enactment of the  Working for Adequate Gains for Employment in Services Act  ; 
:(B) $5.00 an hour beginning 1 year after the date on which the change required by subparagraph (A) takes effect; and 
:(C) beginning 2 years after the date on which the change required by subparagraph (A) takes effect and adjusted as necessary thereafter, 70 percent of the wage in effect under section 6(a)(1) but in no case less than $5.50 an hour. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Publication of notice=

   Section 6 of the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/206 29 U.S.C. 206])is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(h) Not later than 10 days prior to the effective date of any increase in the minimum wage for tipped employees in accordance with section 3(m)(1) , the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary]] shall publish (in the Federal Register and on the Internet website of the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department of Labor]] ) a notice announcing the adjusted required wage. . 
</blockquote>
