{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to improve the ability of veterans to receive health care at private medical facilities.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Veterans’ Health Care Flexibility Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Removal of barriers to veterans receiving care at private medical facilities=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703  Section 1703of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by amending[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsection (a)]to read as follows:

<blockquote> 
::(a) 
:::(1) Upon the election of a veteran enrolled in the health care system established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705 section 1705(a)], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall furnish to the veteran at a non-Department facility hospital care and medical services that the veteran is eligible to receive under this chapter.
:::(2) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall enter into contracts with non-Department facilities to furnish hospital care and medical services pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 paragraph (1)].
:::(3) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may not require a veteran to make more than one election under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 paragraph (1)].
:::(4) An election made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 paragraph (1)]may not be construed as affecting the ability of a veteran to receive hospital care and medical services at a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]. ; 
</blockquote>

:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsection (b)]; and
:(3) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsections (c)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 (d)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsections (b)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 (c)], respectively.
