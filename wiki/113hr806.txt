{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to make permanent the requirement for annual reports on Comptroller General reviews of the accuracy of Department of Veterans Affairs medical budget submissions, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H325F8EF19C0A43DE94C5C676D5F9E7B6}}Section 1. Permanent requirement for annual reports on Comptroller General reviews of the accuracy of Department of Veterans Affairs medical budget submissions=



=={{anchor|H5BB4EAAB548B4F8DBDB3A2679A3FAE20}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/117 Section 117 of title 38, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H35A3507CF404460F81EF7DAB568CF5B0}}(e) Comptroller General review of the accuracy of medical care budget submission in relation to baseline health care model projection– ===


:(1) The [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]] shall conduct a review of each budget of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] for a fiscal year that is submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105(a) of title 31] in order to assess whether or not the relevant components of the amounts requested in such budget for such fiscal year for the medical care accounts of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] specified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/117 subsection (c)] are consistent with estimates of the resources required by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] for the provision of medical care and services in such fiscal year, as forecast using the Enrollee Health Care Projection Model, or other methodologies used by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] .
:(2) 
::(A) Not later than 120 days after the date on which the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] submits a budget request for under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105 of title 31, United States Code] , the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General]] shall submit to theCommittees on Veterans’ Affairs,Appropriations, and theBudget of the Senateand theCommittees on Veterans’ Affairs,Appropriations, and theBudget of the House of Representativesand to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] a report on the review conducted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/117 paragraph (1)] for the year or years covered by such budget request.
::(B) Each report under this paragraph shall include, for the fiscal year covered by the report, the following:
:::(i) An assessment of the review conducted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/117 paragraph (1)] .
:::(ii) The basis for such assessment.
:::(iii) Such additional information as the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General]] determines appropriate.
::(C) Each report submitted under this subsection shall also be made available to the public..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H170C80E95DF44C97A202B685F90C6BA2}}(b) Conforming repeal– ==

 Section 4 of the Veterans Health Care Budget Reform and Transparency Act of 2009([[Public Law 111-81|Public Law 111–81]] )is hereby repealed.
