{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to establish the Office of the Medical Inspector within the Office of the Under Secretary for Health of the Department of Veterans Affairs.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Increasing Medical Oversight in the Department of Veterans Affairs Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Office of the Medical Inspector=



==(a) Establishment– ==

 Subchapter I ofchapter 73of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 7310. Office of the Medical Inspector==



===(a) In general– ===

There is established in the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]within the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Office of the Under Secretary for Health]]an office to be known as the“  [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Office of the Medical Inspector]] ”(in this section referred to as the“Office”).

===(b) Head– ===


:(1) The [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Medical Inspector]]shall be the head of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Office]].
:(2) The [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Medical Inspector]]shall be appointed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]from among individuals qualified to perform the duties of the position.
:(3) The [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Medical Inspector]]shall report directly to the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Under Secretary for Health]].

===(c) Functions– ===

The functions of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Office]]shall include the following:
:(1) To review the quality of health care provided to veterans—
::(A) by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]generally; and
::(B) by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]through contracts with non-Department health care providers.
:(2) To review offices of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]]that have an impact on the quality of health care provided to veterans by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]and the performance of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]in providing such care.
:(3) To review offices and facilities of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]]to ensure that policies and procedures of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]and the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]]are applied consistently at all such offices and facilities.
:(4) To investigate any systemic issues, as determined by the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Medical Inspector]], that arise within the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]], including the following:
::(A) Improper issuance of credentials and privileges to health care providers.
::(B) Impediments to the access of veterans to health care from the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
::(C) Wait times for appointments by veterans at medical facilities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]in excess of wait-time goals established by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
::(D) Intentional falsification by employees of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]of information or data with respect to wait times for such appointments.
:(5) To establish temporary investigative teams to carry out reviews or investigations described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7310 paragraphs (1)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7310 (2)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7310 (3)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7310 (4)]in response to specific incidents or inquiries, including the following:
::(A) Investigations of complaints by a veteran, a family member of a veteran, or another individual that may require a visit to a facility or facilities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
::(B) Assessments to examine potential systemic issues within the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]]that may require the conduct of surveys, the collection of data, and the analysis of databases of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
:(6) To recommend policies to promote economy and efficiency in the administration of, and to prevent and detect criminal activity, waste, abuse, and mismanagement in, programs and operations of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]].
:(7) To carry out any other tasks required of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Office]]by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]or the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Under Secretary for Health]]before, on, or after the date of the enactment of this section.

===(d) Reports– ===


:(1) Not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of this section, and periodically thereafter, the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Medical Inspector]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]], the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Under Secretary for Health]], and [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]reports on any problems or deficiencies encountered in programs and operations of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]], including any recommendations for corrective actions.
:(2) Each report required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7310 paragraph (1)]shall be made available to the public on an Internet website of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
:(3) Any other report prepared by the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Medical Inspector]]in carrying out the functions of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Office]]under this section shall be—
::(A) submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]; and
::(B) made available to the public on an Internet website of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].

===(e) Privacy matters– ===

Any medical or other personal information obtained by the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Office]]shall be protected from disclosure or misuse in accordance with the laws on privacy applicable to such information. . 
</blockquote>

==(d) Continuation in office– ==

The individual serving as the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Medical Inspector of the Department of Veterans Affairs]]on the day before the date of the enactment of this Act may serve as the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Medical Inspector of the Department of Veterans Affairs]]after that date until the date on which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]appoints an individual to be the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Medical Inspector]]pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7310  section 7310(b)(2)of title 38, United States Code], as added by subsection (a) .
