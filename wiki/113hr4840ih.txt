{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title II of the Social Security Act to preclude use of the social security account number on Government-issued identification cards issued in connection with Medicare, Medicaid, and SCHIP benefits, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Seniors’ Identity Protection Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Restrictions on use of the social security account number of Government-issued identification cards issued in connection with Medicare, Medicaid, and SCHIP benefits=



==(a) Provisions related to Medicare– ==

   Section 205(c)(2)(C) of the Social Security Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/405 42 U.S.C. 405(c)(2)(C)] )is amended by adding at the end the following new clause:

<blockquote> 
:(x) In the administration of title XVIII , the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]may not display an individual’s social security account number (or any derivative of such number) on any personal identification card issued for purposes of carrying out such title. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Provisions related to Medicaid or SCHIP– ==

   Section 205(c)(2)(C)(vi) of such Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/402 42 U.S.C. 402(c)(2)(C)(iv)] )is amended by adding at the end the following new subclause:

<blockquote> 
:(III) Any State or political subdivision thereof (and any person acting as an agent of such an agency or instrumentality), in the administration of any program established under title XIX or XXI , may not display an individual’s social security account number (or any derivative of such number) on any personal identification card issued for purposes of carrying out such program. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply with respect to personal identification cards issued after 1 year after the date of the enactment of this Act. 

=Sec. 3. Sense of the Congress relating to use by private entities of the social security account number=

It is the sense of the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that private entities should not use social security account numbers as a method of identification of individuals on documents made available to the public or on personal identification cards issued to individuals by such entities.
