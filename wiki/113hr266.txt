{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 28, United States Code, with respect to certain tort claims arising out of the criminal misconduct of confidential informants, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HB4452CB7025A44ACA4A251D4659D92DC}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Justice for Victims of Confidential Informant Crime Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H0794E60D34674562A2F061097FD78FCF}}Sec. 2. Tort claims arising out of the criminal misconduct of a confidential informant=



=={{anchor|HBDA7E73D44B543BC9C0A01A03BDDE217}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2401 Section 2401(b) of title 28, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by striking“A tort claim”and inserting“(1) Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2401 paragraph (2)] ”; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(2) In the case of a claim arising out of the conduct of an employee of the Government with respect to the criminal misconduct of a Government informant,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2401 paragraph (1)] shall be applied by substituting“three years and six months”for“two years”..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HFB80A12E59EC41458494C5C2EC7D1A69}}(b) Retroactive effect– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply with respect to any claim that—
:(1) accrued on or after May 1, 1981; and
:(2) in the case of a claim that accrued before the date of enactment of this Act, is presented not later than one year after that date.

=={{anchor|H61D6E4B5C0954192830D04BA2F0316A5}}(c) Preclusion of certain defenses or bars– ==

The Government may not assert a defense or a bar, based on the doctrine of res judicata or collateral estoppel, to a claim that—
:(1) accrued before the date of enactment of this Act; and
:(2) to which the amendments made by this section apply.
