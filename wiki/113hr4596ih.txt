{{Center|A BILL}}

 To limit investor and homeowner losses in foreclosures, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Limiting Investor and Homeowner Loss in Foreclosure Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Loss mitigation programs=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/105  Section 105of title 11, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(e) Without limiting the court’s authority under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/105 subsection (d)]or under any other statute or rule, the court, by local rule or order, may establish and maintain a loss mitigation program for the consideration and negotiation of consensual alternatives to avoid foreclosure between an individual debtor and the holder of a claim secured by a security interest in real property that is the debtor’s principal residence. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Conforming amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/362  Section 362(e)of title 11, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(3) If the party in interest requesting relief from the stay under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/362 subsection (d)]of this section participates in a loss mitigation program maintained pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/105 section 105(e) of this title], the time periods specified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/362 paragraphs (1)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/362 (2)]of this subsection shall be tolled during the time period commencing on the date on which such participation began and ending on the date on which notice of such termination is filed and served on the debtor. . 
</blockquote>
