{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 18, United States Code, to provide for increased supervision of persons required to wear an electronic monitoring device as a condition of release from prison, to criminalize disabling such an electronic monitoring device, to establish the Office of the Inspector General for Probation and Pretrial Services, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Federal Probation System Reform Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Supervision of a person assigned an electronic monitoring device as a condition of release; Penalty for disabling an electronic monitoring device=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/ Part II of title 18, United States Code], is amended by inserting afterchapter 237the following new chapter:

<blockquote> 

===239 Electronic Monitoring Devices– ===

 Sec. 3801. Supervision of a person assigned an electronic monitoring device as a condition of release. 3802. Penalty for disabling an electronic monitoring device. 

===Sec. 3801. Supervision of a person assigned an electronic monitoring device as a condition of release===



====(a) In general– ====

In the case of a person who was convicted of a Federal offense who has been sentenced to probation pursuant tosubchapter B of chapter 227, placed on probation pursuant to the provisions ofchapter 403, or placed on supervised release pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3583 section 3583], or a person accused of such an offense who has been released pending trial, sentence, or appeal pursuant tochapter 207, who is required to wear an electronic monitoring device as a condition of such probation or release, the [[w:Administrative Office of the United States Courts|Director of the Administrative Office of the United States Courts]]shall ensure that an appropriate probation officer or pretrial services officer supervises the person by doing the following:
:(1) Conducting a daily review of any data produced by the electronic monitoring device worn by the person.
:(2) In the case of an alert produced by an electronic monitoring system that the [[w:Administrative Office of the United States Courts|Director]]determines requires an investigation, conducting an investigation immediately following the alert, including—
::(A) contacting the person;
::(B) inspecting the electronic monitoring device; and
::(C) documenting the alert and the response taken.

====(b) Uniform Standards– ====

Not later than 60 days after the date of the enactment of this section, the [[w:Administrative Office of the United States Courts|Director of the Administrative Office of the United States Courts]]shall issue uniform standards in order to implement[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3801 subsection (a)].

===Sec. 3802. Penalty for disabling an electronic monitoring device===



====(a) Offense– ====

Whoever—
:(1) intentionally disables an electronic monitoring device that was assigned to a person as a condition of probation pursuant tosubchapter B of chapter 227orchapter 403, supervised release pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3583 section 3583], or release pending trial, sentence, or appeal pursuant tochapter 207; or
:(2) having been assigned an electronic monitoring device as a condition of probation pursuant tosubchapter B of chapter 227orchapter 403, supervised release pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3583 section 3583], or release pending trial, sentence, or appeal pursuant tochapter 207, intentionally allows another person to disable such device;shall be punished as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3802 subsection (b)].

====(b) Punishment– ====

The punishment for an offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3802 subsection (a)]is—
:(1) if the person to whom the electronic monitoring device was assigned commits a Federal, State, or local offense in addition to violating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3802 subsection (a)]upon the disabling of such device, a fine under this title or imprisonment for not more than 4 years; or
:(2) if the person to whom the electronic monitoring device was assigned does not commit a Federal, State or local crime in addition to violating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3802 subsection (a)]upon the disabling of such device, a fine under this title or imprisonment for not more than 1 year. . 
</blockquote>

=Sec. 3. Inspector General for Probation and Pretrial Services=



==(a) In general– ==

 Chapter 207 of part II of title 18, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3155 sections 3155]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3156 3156]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3156 sections 3156]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3157 3157], respectively;
:(2) after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3154 section 3154], by inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

===Sec. 3155. Inspector General for Probation and Pretrial Services===



====(a) Establishment– ====

There is established within pretrial services (commonly referred to as the [[w:|United States Probation and Pretrial Services System]]) the [[w:|Office of the Inspector General for Probation and Pretrial Services]](referred to in this section as the“Office”).

====(b) Appointment, term, and removal of Inspector General– ====



=====(1) Appointment– =====

The head of the [[w:|Office]]shall be the [[w:|Inspector General]], who shall be appointed by the [[w:Chief Justice of the United States|Chief Justice of the United States]]after consultation with the [[w:|majority]]and [[w:United States Senate|minority leaders of the Senate]]and the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker]]and [[w:United States House of Representatives|minority leader of the House of Representatives]].

=====(2) Term– =====

The [[w:|Inspector General]]shall serve for a term of four years and may be reappointed by the [[w:Chief Justice of the United States|Chief Justice of the United States]]for any number of additional terms.

=====(3) Removal– =====

The [[w:|Inspector General]]may be removed from office by the [[w:Chief Justice of the United States|Chief Justice of the United States]]. The [[w:Chief Justice of the United States|Chief Justice]]shall communicate the reasons for any such removal to both Houses of Congress.

====(c) Duties– ====

With respect to probation and pretrial services, the [[w:|Office]]shall—
:(1) conduct investigations of alleged misconduct;
:(2) conduct and supervise audits and investigations;
:(3) prevent and detect waste, fraud, and abuse; and
:(4) recommend changes in laws or regulations governing probation and pretrial services.

====(d) Powers– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

In carrying out the duties of the [[w:|Office]], the [[w:|Inspector General]]shall have the power—
:(A) to make investigations and reports;
:(B) to obtain information or assistance from any Federal, State, or local governmental agency, or other entity, or unit thereof, including all information kept in the normal course of business by probation and pretrial services in any judicial district;
:(C) to require, by subpoena or otherwise, the attendance and testimony of such witnesses, and the production of such books, records, correspondence memoranda, papers, and documents; which subpoena, in the case of contumacy or refusal to obey, shall be enforceable by civil action;
:(D) to administer to or take from any person an oath, affirmation, or affidavit;
:(E) to employ such officers and employees, subject to the provisions of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/ title 5], governing appointments in the competitive service, and the provisions ofchapter 51andsubchapter III of chapter 53 of such titlerelating to classification and General Schedule pay rates;
:(F) to obtain services authorized by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3109  section 3109of title 5]at daily rates not to exceed the equivalent rate prescribed for grade GS–18 of the General Schedule by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5332  section 5332of title 5]; and—
:(G) to the extent and in such amounts as may be provided in advance by appropriations Acts, to enter into contracts and other arrangements for audits, studies, analyses, and other services with public agencies and with private persons, and to make such payments as may be necessary to carry out the duties of the Office.

=====(2) Limitation– =====

The [[w:|Inspector General]]shall not have the authority to—
:(A) investigate or review any matter that is directly related to the merits of a decision or procedural ruling by any judge or court; or
:(B) punish or discipline any pretrial services officer or probation officer.

====(e) Reports– ====



=====(1) When to be made– =====

The [[w:|Inspector General]]shall—
:(A) make an annual report to the [[w:Administrative Office of the United States Courts|Director of the Administrative Office of the United States Courts]]and to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]relating to the activities of the [[w:|Office]]; and
:(B) make prompt reports to the [[w:Administrative Office of the United States Courts|Director]]and to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]on matters that may require action by them.

=====(2) Sensitive matter– =====

If a report contains sensitive matter, the [[w:|Inspector General]]may so indicate and [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]may receive that report in closed session.

=====(3) Duty to inform Attorney General– =====

In carrying out the duties of the [[w:|Office]], the [[w:|Inspector General]]shall report expeditiously to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]whenever the [[w:|Inspector General]]has reasonable grounds to believe there has been a violation of Federal criminal law.

====(f) Whistleblower protection– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

No officer, employee, agent, contractor, or subcontractor of pretrial services may discharge, demote, threaten, suspend, harass, or in any other manner discriminate against an employee in the terms and conditions of employment because of any lawful act done by the employee to provide information, cause information to be provided, or otherwise assist in an investigation regarding any possible violation of Federal law or regulation, or misconduct, by a pretrial services officer or probation officer, which may assist the [[w:|Inspector General]]in the performance of duties under this chapter.

=====(2) Civil action– =====

An employee injured in violation of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3155 paragraph (1)]may, in a civil action, obtain appropriate relief.

====(g) Authorization of appropriations– ====

 There is authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be necessary to carry out[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3155 this section] .  . 
</blockquote>
