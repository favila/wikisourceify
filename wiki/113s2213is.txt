{{Center|A BILL}}

 To replace the Director of the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection with a five-person Commission.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Consumer Financial Protection Commission Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Establishment of the Commission=

   Section 1011 of the Consumer Financial Protection Act of 2010 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/5491 12 U.S.C. 5491] )is amended—
:(1) by striking subsections (b) , (c) , and (d) ;
:(2) by redesignating subsection (e) as subsection (j) ; and
:(3) by inserting after subsection (a) the following new subsections:

<blockquote> 

===(b) Establishment of the Commission– ===



====(1) In general– ====

There is established a commission (referred to in this title as the“  [[w:|Commission]] ”) that shall serve as the head of the [[w:|Bureau]].

====(2) Authority to prescribe regulations– ====

The [[w:|Commission]]—
:(A) may prescribe such regulations and issue such orders in accordance with this title as the [[w:|Commission]]may determine to be necessary for carrying out this title and all other laws within the jurisdiction of the [[w:|Commission]]; and
:(B) shall exercise any authorities granted under this title and all other laws within the jurisdiction of the [[w:|Commission]]. 

===(c) Composition of the Commission– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:|Commission]]shall be composed of 5 members who shall be appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]], by and with the advice and consent of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]], from among individuals who—
:(A) are citizens of the United States; and
:(B) have strong competencies and experiences related to consumer financial protection.

====(2) Staggering– ====

The members of the [[w:|Commission]]shall serve staggered terms, which initially shall be established by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]for terms of 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 years, respectively.

====(3) Terms– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

Except as provided in paragraph (2) , each member of the [[w:|Commission]], including the [[w:|Chair]], shall serve for a term of 5 years.

=====(B) Removal for cause– =====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]may remove any member of the [[w:|Commission]]only for inefficiency, neglect of duty, or malfeasance in office.

=====(C) Vacancies– =====

Any member of the [[w:|Commission]]appointed to fill a vacancy occurring before the expiration of the term to which the predecessor of that member was appointed (including the [[w:|Chair]]) shall be appointed only for the remainder of the term.

=====(D) Continuation of service– =====

Each member of the [[w:|Commission]]may continue to serve after the expiration of the term of office to which that member was appointed until a successor has been appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and confirmed by the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]], except that a member may not continue to serve for more than 1 year after the date on which the term of that member would otherwise expire.

=====(E) Other employment prohibited– =====

No member of the [[w:|Commission]]shall engage in any other business, vocation, or employment during the term of service of that member on the [[w:|Commission]]. 

===(d) Affiliation– ===

With respect to members appointed pursuant to subsection (c) , not more than 3 shall be members of any one political party. 

===(e) Chair of the Commission– ===



====(1) Appointment– ====

The [[w:|Chair of the Commission]]shall be appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]from among the members of the [[w:|Commission]].

====(2) Authority– ====

The [[w:|Chair]]shall be the principal executive officer of the [[w:|Bureau]], and shall exercise all of the executive and administrative functions of the [[w:|Bureau]], including with respect to—
:(A) the appointment and supervision of personnel employed under the [[w:|Bureau]](other than personnel employed regularly and full time in the immediate offices of members of the [[w:|Commission]]other than the [[w:|Chair]]);
:(B) the distribution of business among personnel appointed and supervised by the [[w:|Chair]]and among administrative units of the Bureau; and
:(C) the use and expenditure of funds.

====(3) Limitation– ====

In carrying out any of the functions of the [[w:|Chair]]under the provisions of this subsection, the [[w:|Chair]]shall be governed by general policies of the [[w:|Commission]]and by such regulatory decisions, findings, and determinations as the [[w:|Commission]]may by law be authorized to make.

====(4) Requests or estimates related to appropriations– ====

Requests or estimates for regular, supplemental, or deficiency appropriations on behalf of the [[w:|Commission]]may not be submitted by the [[w:|Chair]]without the prior approval of the [[w:|Commission]]. 

===(f) No impairment by reason of vacancies– ===

No vacancy in the membership of the [[w:|Commission]]shall impair the right of the remaining members of the [[w:|Commission]]to exercise all the powers of the [[w:|Commission]]. Three members of the [[w:|Commission]]shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business, except that if there are only 3 members serving on the [[w:|Commission]]because of vacancies in the membership of the [[w:|Commission]], 2 members of the [[w:|Commission]]shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. If there are only 2 members serving on the [[w:|Commission]]because of vacancies in the membership of the [[w:|Commission]], 2 members shall constitute a quorum for the 6-month period beginning on the date of the vacancy which caused the number of [[w:|Commission]]members to decline to 2. 

===(g) Seal– ===

The [[w:|Commission]]shall have an official seal. 

===(h) Compensation– ===



====(1) Chair– ====

The [[w:|Chair]]shall receive compensation at the rate prescribed for level II of the Executive Schedule under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5313  section 5313of title 5, United States Code].

====(2) Other members of the Commission– ====

The 4 members of the [[w:|Commission]]other than the [[w:|Chair]]shall each receive compensation at the rate prescribed for level III of the Executive Schedule under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5314  section 5314of title 5, United States Code]. 

===(i) Initial quorum established– ===

During any time period prior to the date of confirmation of at least 2 members of the [[w:|Commission]], 1 member of the [[w:|Commission]]shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. Following the confirmation of at least 2 additional commissioners, the quorum requirements of subsection (f) shall apply. . 
</blockquote>
