{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to specify the responsibilities of the Directors and Assistant Directors of Veterans’ Employment and Training.   

=Section 1. Responsibilities of the Directors of Veterans’ Employment and Training=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103  Section 4103of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (b)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (c)]; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (a)]the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (b)]:

<blockquote> 

===(b) Responsibilities– ===

Each Director and Assistant Director assigned to a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 subsection (a)]shall carry out the following responsibilities:
:(1) Monitoring the performance of veterans’ training and employment programs in the State, with special emphasis on services to disabled veterans.
:(2) Monitoring the performance of the State workforce agency in complying with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4212 section 4212 of this title].
:(3) Suggesting to the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary of Labor for Veterans’ Employment and Training]]corrective actions that could be taken by the State workforce agency to address deficiencies in the performance of veterans’ training and employment programs in the State.
:(4) Annually negotiating with the State workforce agency to establish performance goals for veterans’ training and employment programs in the State.
:(5) Reviewing the State’s requests for funding for veterans’ training and employment programs and providing advice to the State workforce agency and the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]]regarding such funding requests.
:(6) Conducting outreach to inform employers in the State regarding their rights and responsibilities underchapter 43 of this titleand of veterans preferences and Federal contractors’ and subcontractors’ affirmative action programs to hire veterans.
:(7) Managing programs to administer and investigatechapter 43 of this title, veterans preferences under Federal and State laws, and federal contractors’ and subcontractors’ affirmative action programs to hire veterans.
:(8) Forwarding complaints regarding possible violations ofchapter 43 of this titleto the appropriate Regional Administrator or to the to the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]], as required.
:(9) Other duties as assigned by the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]]as directed.
:(10) Carrying out grant officer technical representative responsibilities for grants issued under programs administered by the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department]].
:(11) Providing advice to the State workforce agency on strategies to market veterans to employers.
:(12) Supervising and managing all support staff, including Assistant Directors, establishing workload priorities, managing all personnel actions, and evaluating all assigned personnel.
:(13) Submitting to the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]]regular reports on the matters described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 paragraphs (1)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 (2)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 (4)], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103 (10)], and any other matters the Director or [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]]determine appropriate.
:(14) Performing such other related duties as directed by the [[w:Veterans Employment and Training Services|Assistant Secretary]]. . 
</blockquote>
