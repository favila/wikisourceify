{{Center|A BILL}}

To permit business development companies to increase investments in small-and middle-market financial services companies and investment advisors.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Business Development Company Modernization Act” .

=Sec. 2. Investments of business development companies=

The Investment Company Act of 1940is amended—
:(1) in section 2(a)(46(B)( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/80a–2 15 U.S.C. 80a–2(46)(B)] )by striking “nor a company which would be an investment company except for the exclusion from the definition of investment company in section 3(c) of this title” ; and
:(2) in section 60( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/80a–59 15 U.S.C. 80a–59] )by striking “except that the [[w:U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|Commission]] shall not” and inserting

<blockquote>except that—
::(1)  section 12shall not apply to the purchasing, otherwise acquiring, or holding by a business development company of any security issued by, or any other interest in the business of, any person who is—
:::(A) an investment adviser registered under title II of this Actor who is an investment adviser to an investment company; or
:::(B) an eligible portfolio company as defined in section 2(a)(46)( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/80a–2 15 U.S.C. 80a–2(a)(46)] ); and
:::(C) the [[w:U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|Commission]] shall not.
</blockquote>
