{{Center|A BILL}}

To require poverty impact statements for certain legislation.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Poverty Impact Trigger Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Certain poverty impact legislation subject to point of order=

Rule XXI of the Rules of the House of Representatives is amended by adding at the end the following new clause:

<blockquote>
12. Certain legislation reported by committeesIt shall not be in order to consider a bill or joint resolution of a public nature authorizing an appropriation of $10,000,000 or more, unless—
(a) the committee report accompanying the bill or joint resolution includes a CBO Poverty Index Division impact statement; or
(b) the chair of the committee reporting the bill or joint resolution submits such statement to be published in the Congressional Record before consideration of the bill or joint resolution..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Congressional budget office poverty impact division=



==(a) In General– ==

 Section 202 of the Congressional Budget Act of 1974 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/602 (2 U.S.C. 602)] is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

===(h) CBO Poverty Impact Division– ===



====(1) Creation– ====

There is established within the [[w:Congressional Budget Office|Office]] the CBO Poverty Impact Division (hereinafter in this subsection referred to as the “Division” ).

====(2) Duties and functions– ====



=====(A) Preparation and submission of impact statement– =====

When a chair of a committee of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] submits a written request to the Division to prepare and submit to the committee a CBO Poverty Index Division impact statement, the Division shall prepare and submit such statement to the committee not later than 30 days after such request.

=====(B) Content of impact statement– =====

A CBO Poverty Index Division impact statement shall include the following:
:(i) A projected ratio equal to the amount of appropriations authorized in the bill or joint resolution that will benefit individuals and families below the poverty threshold over the total amount of appropriations authorized by the bill or joint resolution.
:(ii) A projection of the number of individual and family incomes—
::(I) that may decrease below the poverty threshold because of the bill or joint resolution; and
::(II) that may increase above the poverty threshold because of the bill or joint resolution.
:(iii) A projection as to how the legislation improves access to basic human services, including health care, housing, and education.

=====(C) Poverty threshold defined– =====

In this subsection, the term “poverty threshold” means an income level below 200 percent of the poverty line (as defined in section 673(2) of the Community Services Block Grant Act)..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 4. Exercise of rulemaking powers=

 Section 2 of this Actis enacted by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] —
:(1) as an exercise of the rulemaking power of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] and as such it shall be considered as part of the rules of the House of Representatives and such rules shall supersede other rules only to the extent that they are inconsistent therewith; and
:(2) with the full recognition of the constitutional right of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] to change such rules at any time, in the same manner, and to the same extent as in the case of any other rule of the House of Representatives.

=Sec. 5. Effective date=

The amendments made by this Act shall apply to any bill or joint resolution reported by a committee of the House of Representatives after the 90-day period beginning on the date of enactment of this Act.
