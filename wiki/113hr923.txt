{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to deny any deduction for direct-to-consumer advertisements of prescription drugs.

={{anchor|HB0F97B3A68D04E4EBF02FDF4F23883CF}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Say No to Drug Ads Act”.

={{anchor|H37E29179341B4837BA6BA6B6ABB3ADA8}}Sec. 2. Disallowance of deduction for direct-to-consumer advertisement of prescription drugs=



=={{anchor|HEC51590B8F4E4D2D9DA9133E315D9956}}(a) General rule– ==

 Part IX of subchapter B of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986(relating to items not deductible) is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H6C204FD179564BE69F6BFAC0E70E6287}}Sec. 280I. Direct-to-consumer advertisement of prescription drugs==

No deduction shall be allowed under this chapter for any amount paid or incurred for a direct-to-consumer advertisement of a prescription drug..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H1D19EABBC26242FBBAD52C3278E88600}}(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections for part IX of subchapter B of chapter 1 of such Codeis amended by adding at the end thereof the following new item:

<blockquote>__NOTOC__
**[[#H6C204FD179564BE69F6BFAC0E70E6287|Sec. 280I. Direct-to-consumer advertisement of prescription drugs.]]
.
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H79E42990C12F4F89AA1923BCC33509BF}}(c) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to amounts paid or incurred after December 31, 2013.
