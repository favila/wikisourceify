{{Center|A BILL}}

 To prohibit royalty incentives for deepwater drilling, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Deepwater Drilling Royalty Relief Prohibition Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Prohibition on royalty incentives for deepwater drilling=



==(a) In general– ==

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall not issue any oil or gas lease sale under the  Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1331 43 U.S.C. 1331 et seq.] )with royalty-based incentives in any tract located in water depths of 400 meters or more on the outer Continental Shelf.

==(b) Royalty relief for deepwater production– ==

   Section 345 of the Energy Policy Act of 2005 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/15905 42 U.S.C. 15905] )is repealed.

==(c) Royalty relief– ==

   Section 8(a)(3) of the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1337 43 U.S.C. 1337(a)(3)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

===(D) Prohibition– ===

Notwithstanding subparagraphs (A) through (C) or any other provision of law, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall not reduce or eliminate any royalty or net profit share for any lease or unit located in water depths of 400 meters or more on the outer Continental Shelf. . 
</blockquote>


==(d) Application– ==

This section and the amendments made by this section—
:(1) apply beginning with the first lease sale held on or after the date of enactment of this Act for which a final notice of sale has not been published as of that date; and
:(2) do not apply to a lease in effect on the date of enactment of this Act.
