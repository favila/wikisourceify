{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to improve the opportunity for veterans to use video conferencing for hearings before the Board of Veterans’ Appeals.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Veterans Access to Speedy Review Act” .

=Sec. 2. Board of Veterans' Appeals video hearings=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7107 Section 7107of title 38, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7107 subsection (d)] , by amending [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7107 paragraph (1)] to read as follows:

<blockquote>
::(1) 
:::(A) Upon request for a hearing, the [[w:|Board]] shall determine, for purposes of scheduling the hearing for the earliest possible date, whether a hearing before the [[w:|Board]] will be held at its principal location or at a facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] or other appropriate Federal facility located within the area served by a regional office of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] . The [[w:|Board]] shall also determine whether to provide a hearing through the use of the facilities and equipment described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7107 subsection (e)(1)] or by the appellant personally appearing before a [[w:|Board]] member or panel.
:::(B) The [[w:|Board]] shall notify the appellant of the determinations of the location and type of hearing made under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7107 subparagraph (A)] . Upon notification, the appellant may request a different location or type of hearing as described in such subparagraph. If so requested, the [[w:|Board]] may grant such request and, in doing so, shall ensure that the hearing is scheduled at the earliest possible date without any undue delay or other prejudice to the appellant.; and
</blockquote>

:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7107 subsection (e)(2)] , by striking the last sentence.
