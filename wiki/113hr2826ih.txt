{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title III of the Social Security Act to prevent the payment of unemployment benefits to incarcerated individuals.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Permanently Ending Receipt by Prisoners Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Preventing the payment of unemployment benefits to incarcerated individuals=



==(a) In general– ==

   Section 303 of the Social Security Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/503 42 U.S.C. 503] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(m) 
::(1) In order to obtain the information necessary to carry out the provisions of a State law under which an individual who is confined in a jail, prison, or other penal institution or correctional facility pursuant to his conviction of a criminal offense is ineligible for regular compensation on account of such individual’s inability to satisfy the requirement under subsection (a)(12) , the State agency charged with the administration of the State law shall seek such information—
:::(A) from the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner of Social Security]]under sections 202(x)(3)(B)(iv) and 1611(e)(1)(I)(iii) ; and
:::(B) through such additional means as the State agency considers appropriate.
::(2) If the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]], after reasonable notice and opportunity for hearing to the State agency charged with the administration of the State law, finds that there is a failure to comply substantially with the requirements of paragraph (1) , the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]]shall notify such State agency that further payments will not be made to the State until the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]]is satisfied that there is no longer any such failure. Until the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]]is so satisfied, such [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary]]shall make no future certification to the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]]with respect to such State. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Effective date– ==

The amendment made by subsection (a) shall take effect on the date that is 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, except that the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]]may delay the application of the requirement imposed by such amendment until a date that is not later than 3 years after such date of enactment with respect to any State for which the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary]]determines that additional time is necessary to satisfy such requirement.
