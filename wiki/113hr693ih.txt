={{anchor|H70E456D71AFC4C4199C640269D2EDA5F}}Section 1. Short title=

=={{anchor|H9A65A41608CA422BA51D0F9B10A77E36}}(c) Suspension or modification of provisions– ==

 Section 359j of the Agricultural Adjustment Act of 1938([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/7/1359jj 7 U.S.C. 1359jj] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H9181C9A0307340998E93B4959FC83AE7}}(c) Suspension or modification of provisions– ===

Notwithstanding any other provision of this part, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] may suspend or modify, in whole or in part, the application of any provision of this part if the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] determines that the action is appropriate, taking into account—
:(1) the interests of consumers, workers in the food industry, businesses (including small businesses), and agricultural producers; and
:(2) the relative competitiveness of domestically produced and imported foods containing sugar..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H6DC99228266B4C16877F8D1678621E0A}}(d) Administration of tariff rate quotas– ==

 Section 359k of the Agricultural Adjustment Act of 1938([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/7/1359kk 7 U.S.C. 1359kk] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H821169A963D94364827D21ABEDC3051A}}Sec. 359k. Administration of tariff rate quotas==



==={{anchor|H0F5AD301785E403589BEC86941174E2B}}(a) Establishment– ===

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, at the beginning of the quota year, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall establish the tariff-rate quotas for raw cane sugar and refined sugar at no less than the minimum level necessary to comply with obligations under international trade agreements that have been approved by [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] .

==={{anchor|HD149874EF5014AD694C371812F55CDE2}}(b) Adjustment– ===



===={{anchor|H2E45EFCFCEF6425CAF00F15300522BC0}}(1) In general– ====

Subject to subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall adjust the tariff-rate quotas for raw cane sugar and refined sugar to provide adequate supplies of sugar at reasonable prices in the domestic market.

===={{anchor|H35725DE715F14E5C8C74EE3490E8ED30}}(2) Ending stocks– ====

Subject to paragraphs (1)and (3), the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall establish and adjust tariff-rate quotas in such a manner that the ratio of sugar stocks to total sugar use at the end of the quota year will be approximately 15.5 percent.

===={{anchor|H762E799A4F5A48E4A0AB11D56A99E89D}}(3) Maintenance of reasonable prices and avoidance of forfeitures– ====



====={{anchor|HD036D1F073744FCEBBB876D013EAB282}}(A) In general– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] may establish a different target for the ratio of ending stocks to total use if, in the judgment of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] , the different target is necessary to prevent—
:(i) unreasonably high prices; or
:(ii) forfeitures of sugar pledged as collateral for a loan under section 156 of the Federal Agriculture Improvement and Reform Act of 1996([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/7/7272 7 U.S.C. 7272] ).

====={{anchor|HE990A46E19284644930753489D2A9CEC}}(B) Announcement– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall publicly announce any establishment of a target under this paragraph.

===={{anchor|H8953A7B715E34EC2BCC7104DA9EF44A4}}(4) Considerations– ====

In establishing tariff-rate quotas under subsection (a)and making adjustments under this subsection, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall consider the impact of the quotas on consumers, workers, businesses (including small businesses), and agricultural producers.

==={{anchor|H25925730DC884D82BF1A335332C4275E}}(c) Temporary transfer of quotas– ===



===={{anchor|H5AFC04212E5B45F98AAD2834F5D83AC1}}(1) In general– ====

To promote full use of the tariff-rate quotas for raw cane sugar and refined sugar, notwithstanding any other provision of law, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall promulgate regulations that provide that any country that has been allocated a share of the quotas may temporarily transfer all or part of the share to any other country that has also been allocated a share of the quotas.

===={{anchor|HD50858E274FA44EC92796BDD120AF3BE}}(2) Transfers voluntary– ====

Any transfer under this subsection shall be valid only on voluntary agreement between the transferor and the transferee, consistent with procedures established by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] .

===={{anchor|HAA2520B8DA70469EB7C27909456CCA06}}(3) Transfers temporary– ====



====={{anchor|H15DD583A3BA6411FB7F14BA4127978D4}}(A) In general– =====

Any transfer under this subsection shall be valid only for the duration of the quota year during which the transfer is made.

====={{anchor|H156D1B1210C24CC8A497CF7F6A8B0DC5}}(B) Following quota year– =====

No transfer under this subsection shall affect the share of the quota allocated to the transferor or transferee for the following quota year..
</blockquote>
