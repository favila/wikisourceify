{{Center|A BILL}}

To direct the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency to change the Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure rule with respect to certain farms.

={{anchor|H8A6870CDD8DD4DD3A2469534713F781F}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Farmers Undertake Environmental Land Stewardship Act”or the“FUELS Act”.

={{anchor|H6059EED8A94F4C27BB5C04F08EF22C1F}}Sec. 2. Applicability of Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure rule=



=={{anchor|HCB2A211B301B45AD9EABF52231F7710D}}(a) Definitions– ==

In this Act:

==={{anchor|HB7F56D50EDCB4AF8AAC1A1BCB81526E5}}(1) Administrator– ===

The term Administrator means the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency.

==={{anchor|H07DC046174F04E6F81D73DC5F71EB2B7}}(2) Farm– ===

The term farm has the meaning given the term in section 112.2 of title 40, Code of Federal Regulations (or successor regulations).

==={{anchor|HD3DEAF598532457E9BD6F19D36331C41}}(3) Gallon– ===

The term gallon means a United States liquid gallon.

==={{anchor|HB955E8ED284E4923BA0E508F82A273BF}}(4) Oil– ===

The term oil has the meaning given the term in section 112.2 of title 40, Code of Federal Regulations (or successor regulations).

==={{anchor|id6645EA1BC00A41EE9F0F5209B615A4B4}}(5) Oil discharge– ===

The term oil discharge has the meaning given the term“discharge”in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 112.2] of title 40, Code of Federal Regulations (or successor regulations).

==={{anchor|idD59A0522CC564436867156931A764CD3}}(6) Reportable oil discharge history– ===

The term reportable oil discharge history has the meaning as used to describe reportable discharge history insection 112.7(k)(1)of title 40, Code of Federal Regulations (or successor regulations).

==={{anchor|id981BC07D5B664A1E8EE4C31229C5D163}}(7) Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure rule– ===

The term Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure rule means the regulation, including amendments, promulgated by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] under part 112 of title 40, Code of Federal Regulations.

=={{anchor|idCA7F0738080049A1BC8640DD1DAB0227}}(b) Certification– ==

In implementing the Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure rule with respect to any farm, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall—
:(1) require certification of compliance with the rule by—
::(A) a professional engineer for a farm with—
:::(i) an individual tank with an aboveground storage capacity greater than 10,000 gallons;
:::(ii) an aggregate aboveground storage capacity greater than or equal to 42,000 gallons; or
:::(iii) a reportable oil discharge history; or
::(B) the owner or operator of the farm (via self-certification) for a farm with—
:::(i) an aggregate aboveground storage capacity greater than 10,000 gallons but less than 42,000 gallons; and
:::(ii) no reportable oil discharge history of oil; and
:(2) exempt from all requirements of the rule any farm—
::(A) with an aggregate aboveground storage capacity of less than or equal to 10,000 gallons; and
::(B) no reportable oil discharge history.

=={{anchor|H7B8CFD097E444412B47024BDF853C5CC}}(c) Calculation of aggregate aboveground storage capacity– ==

For purposes of subsection (b), the aggregate aboveground storage capacity of a farm excludes all containers on separate parcels that have a capacity that is less than 1,320 gallons.
