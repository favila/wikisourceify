{{Center|A BILL}}

 To allow postal patrons to contribute to funding for gang prevention programs through the voluntary purchase of certain specially issued postage stamps.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Stamp Out Violence In Our Communities Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Special postage stamp=



==(a) In general– ==

In order to afford a convenient way for members of the public to contribute to funding for gang prevention programs, the [[w:United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]]shall provide for a special postage stamp in accordance with subsection (b) .

==(b) Terms and conditions– ==

The issuance and sale of the stamp referred to in subsection (a) shall be governed by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/416 section 416 of title 39], United States Code, and regulations under such section, subject to the following:

===(1) Disposition of proceeds– ===

All amounts becoming available from the sale of such stamp shall be transferred to the [[w:Office of Justice Programs|Gang Resistance Education and Training]](G.R.E.A.T.) Program, administered by the [[w:Office of Justice Programs|Office of Justice Programs of the Department of Justice]], through payments which shall be made at least twice a year.

===(2) Duration– ===

Such stamp shall be made available to the public for a period of at least 2 years, beginning no later than 12 months after the date of the enactment of this Act.

===(3) Limitation– ===

Such stamp shall not be counted for purposes of applying any numerical limitation under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/416 subsection (e)(1)(C)]of such section.
