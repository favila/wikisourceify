{{Center|A BILL}}

To treat gold and silver coins used as legal tender in the same manner as United States currency for taxation purposes.

={{anchor|idEBE08CE894E64E03982F6C41FFFD9FB7}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Sound Money Promotion Act”.

={{anchor|IDD7D7D1C3322244779573B27884CD93F1}}Sec. 2. Gold and silver coins that are legal tender not subject to taxation=



=={{anchor|id52FB940ADBCD4517AE209335E7B8B71D}}(a) In general– ==

Gold and silver coins declared legal tender by the Federal Government or any State government shall not be subject to taxation.

=={{anchor|ID74E72F0C10F243AD849B740396FB67B5}}(b) Conforming amendment– ==

 Section 1(h)(5) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986is amended—
:(1) by striking“(as defined in section 408(m)without regard to paragraph (3) thereof)”in subparagraph (A), and
:(2) by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|ID16CD76E186B54717BDD646B2C5B7C9E8}}(C) Collectible– ====

For purposes of this paragraph, the term collectible has the meaning given such term by section 408(m), determined without regard to subparagraphs (A)(iii), (A)(iv), and (B)..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|ID84053FBF7F73400CBC016229669C8EFD}}(c) Effective date– ==

The provisions of, and amendments made by, this section shall take effect on the date of the enactment of this Act.
