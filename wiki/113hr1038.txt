{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide equal treatment for utility special entities
		  using utility operations-related swaps, and for other
		  purposes.

={{anchor|H29F562BC149C4A7E8479E19F5F416271}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Public Power Risk Management Act of
			 2013”.

={{anchor|H64E7C20168DA402F94CD34E24B0A6CDA}}Sec. 2. Transactions with
			 utility special entities=

Section 1a(49) of the Commodity Exchange Act
			 (7 U.S.C. 1a(49)) is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HBDA11FBFE385454184FA47D56DA514F2}}(E) Certain
				transactions with a utility special entity– ==


:(i) Transactions in
				utility operations-related swaps shall be reported pursuant to section
				4r.
:(ii) In making a determination to exempt
				pursuant to subparagraph (D), the [[Wikipedia:|Commission]] shall treat a utility
				operations-related swap entered into with a utility special entity, as defined
				in section 4s(h)(2)(D), as if it were entered into with an entity that is not a
				special entity, as defined in section
				4s(h)(2)(C)..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|HF034DE850A754729A9A99B6554D532B4}}Sec. 3. Utility special
			 entity defined=

Section
			 4s(h)(2) of the Commodity Exchange Act (7 U.S.C. 6s(h)(2)) is amended by adding
			 at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HF167A0E48B3E41E2A531325F11121594}}(D) Utility special
				entity– ==

For purposes of this Act, the term utility special
				entity means a special entity, or any instrumentality, department, or
				corporation of or established by a State or political subdivision of a State,
				that—
:(i) owns or operates
				an electric or natural gas facility or an electric or natural gas
				operation;
:(ii) supplies natural
				gas and or electric energy to another utility special entity;
:(iii) has public
				service obligations under Federal, State, or local law or regulation to deliver
				electric energy or natural gas service to customers; or
:(iv) is a Federal
				power marketing agency, as defined in section 3 of the Federal Power
				Act..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H63A30426B9604867A06DA563E0FB163E}}Sec. 4. Utility
			 operations-related swap=



=={{anchor|H7919825F50854AF8B26B5AEC8DDB6853}}(a) Swap further
			 defined– ==

Section 1a(47)(A)(iii) of the Commodity Exchange Act (7
			 U.S.C. 1a(47)(A)(iii)) is amended—
:(1) by striking
			 “and” at the end of subclause (XXI);
:(2) by adding
			 “and” at the end of subclause (XXII); and
:(3) by adding at the
			 end the following:

<blockquote>
::(XXIII) a utility
				operations-related
				swap;.
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H7252942D15574B0EA25257D1BA5A49D1}}(b) Utility
			 operations-Related swap defined– ==

Section 1a of such Act (7 U.S.C.
			 1a) is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H4F15E3505D244D28A5E7FB5AB39B19D7}}(52) Utility
				operations-related swap– ===

The term utility operations-related
				swap means a swap that—
:(A) is entered into to
				hedge or mitigate a commercial risk;
:(B) is not a contract,
				agreement, or transaction based on, derived on, or referencing—
::(i) an
				interest rate, credit, equity, or currency asset class; or
::(ii) a
				metal, agricultural commodity, or crude oil or gasoline commodity of any grade,
				except as used as fuel for electric energy generation; and
:(C) is associated
				with—
::(i) the generation,
				production, purchase, or sale of natural gas or electric energy, the supply of
				natural gas or electric energy to a utility, or the delivery of natural gas or
				electric energy service to utility customers;
::(ii) all fuel supply
				for the facilities or operations of a utility;
::(iii) compliance with
				an electric system reliability obligation;
::(iv) compliance with
				an energy, energy efficiency, conservation, or renewable energy or
				environmental statute, regulation, or government order applicable to a utility;
				or
::(v) any other electric
				energy or natural gas swap to which a utility is a
				party..
</blockquote>
