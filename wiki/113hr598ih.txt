{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 5, United States Code, to give members of the United States Capitol Police the option to delay mandatory retirement until age 60.

=Section 1. Optional delay in mandatory retirement age for members of capitol police=



==(a) Civil service retirement system– ==

The second sentence of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/8335 section 8335(c) of title 5, United States Code] , is amended to read as follows: “At the request of such a member, the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]] shall exempt the member from automatic separation under this subsection until that member becomes 60 years of age, so long as the member undergoes a physical examination during the 6-month period which ends on the day the member turns 57 years of age and, on the basis of the examination, meets appropriate physical fitness criteria (as determined by the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Board]] in consultation with the Chief of the Capitol Police and the exclusive representative of the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police]] underchapter 71 of title 5, United States Code, with respect to collective bargaining).” .

==(b) Federal employees retirement system– ==

The second sentence of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/8425 section 8425(c) of title 5, United States Code] , is amended to read as follows: “At the request of such a member, the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]] shall exempt the member from automatic separation under this subsection until that member becomes 60 years of age, so long as the member undergoes a physical examination during the 6-month period which ends on the day the member turns 57 years of age and, on the basis of the examination, meets appropriate physical fitness criteria (as determined by the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Board]] in consultation with the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Chief of the Capitol Police]] and the exclusive representative of the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police]] underchapter 71 of title 5, United States Code, with respect to collective bargaining).” .
