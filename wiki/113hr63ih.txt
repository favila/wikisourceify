{{Center|A BILL}}

To modify the boundary of the Shiloh National Military Park located in Tennessee and Mississippi, to establish Parker’s Crossroads Battlefield as an affiliated area of the National Park System, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H96917D8C22444636846C11354E3FB8A2}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Shiloh National Military Park Boundary Adjustment and Parker’s Crossroads Battlefield Designation Act”.

={{anchor|H721D00B313534CC6AF4D50986F8EC2B3}}Sec. 2. Definitions=

In this Act, the following definitions apply:

=={{anchor|H046D141B1616488FA7A04E38D38112AD}}(1) Affiliated area– ==

The term affiliated area means the Parker’s Crossroads Battlefield established as an affiliated area of the National Park System under section 4.

=={{anchor|H838DCCACA49C4C48A2D1BE71153660C0}}(2) Park– ==

The term Park means Shiloh National Military Park, a unit of the National Park System.

=={{anchor|H433A5029C2DB4AC69C91517911375E6F}}(3) Secretary– ==

The term Secretary means the Secretary of the Interior.

={{anchor|H817DCFF85AF74500A3E4C8C4E794975E}}Sec. 3. Areas to be added to shiloh national military park=



=={{anchor|H13463BD971AE42119F2DCA64DA789614}}(a) Additional areas– ==

The boundary of Shiloh National Military Park is modified to include the areas that are generally depicted on the map entitled“Shiloh National Military Park, Proposed Boundary Adjustment”, numbered 304/80,011, and dated April 2012 as follows:
:(1) Fallen Timbers Battlefield.
:(2) Russell House Battlefield.
:(3) Davis Bridge Battlefield.

=={{anchor|HDA93741C61A9499480FD95E075A0D232}}(b) Acquisition authority– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may acquire lands described in subsection (a)by donation, purchase from willing sellers with donated or appropriated funds, or exchange.

=={{anchor|HF193AB137E7A4D3F9F4603936A880A58}}(c) Administration– ==

Any lands acquired under this section shall be administered as part of the Park.

={{anchor|H955C42990ACC49309602A3FC1BB7D85C}}Sec. 4. Establishment of affiliated area=



=={{anchor|H8907C4C8AA534C3D81584FB5CA217386}}(a) In general– ==

Parker’s Crossroads Battlefield in the State of Tennessee is hereby established as an affiliated area of the [[w:National Park Service|National Park System]] .

=={{anchor|HFCC32B9B0A124EB28BE442FEC69EBBDD}}(b) Description– ==

The affiliated area shall consist of the area generally depicted within the Proposed Boundary on the map entitled Parker’s Crossroads Battlefield, Proposed Boundary , numbered 903/80,073, and dated April 2012.

=={{anchor|H9B4C4D4D69F945D3A50230D7E298C8AA}}(c) Administration– ==

The affiliated area shall be managed in accordance with this Actand all laws generally applicable to units of the [[w:National Park Service|National Park System]] .

=={{anchor|H24B40B571FD44B4CB2F447E54DC20B52}}(d) Management entity– ==

The City of Parkers Crossroads and the Tennessee Historical Commission shall jointly be the management entity for the affiliated area.

=={{anchor|HC3EC0F841E1A4A549B40AE5383FC6652}}(e) Cooperative agreements– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may provide technical assistance and enter into cooperative agreements with the management entity for the purpose of providing financial assistance with marketing, marking, interpretation, and preservation of the affiliated area.

=={{anchor|H5FA530199CE74B09A0583C947335C777}}(f) Limited role of the secretary– ==

Nothing in this Actauthorizes the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] to acquire property at the affiliated area or to assume overall financial responsibility for the operation, maintenance, or management of the affiliated area.

=={{anchor|H5FE8A641FBD84E7F92A2134EFDEE9597}}(g) General management plan– ==



==={{anchor|H66DFFFD8F8D3439CB52B49B57AF92F74}}(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] , in consultation with the management entity, shall develop a general management plan for the affiliated area. The plan shall be prepared in accordance with[[Public Law 91-383|section 12(b) of Public Law 91–383]] ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1a–1 16 U.S.C. 1a–1 et seq.] ; commonly known as the National Park System General Authorities Act).

==={{anchor|H66E60AC5F72041899C65BB70E39D1DAE}}(2) Transmittal– ===

Not later than 3 years after the date that funds are made available for this Act, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall provide a copy of the completed general management to the [[w:United States House Committee on Natural Resources|Committee on Natural Resources of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources|Committee on Energy and Natural Resources of the Senate]] .
