{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for the withdrawal of the United States from
		  the North American Free Trade Agreement.


{{section|H0511CBB365584B1FA3C8A3F3FF01E098|1. Withdrawal of the United States from the NAFTA}}
(a)
Withdrawal of
			 approval
Notwithstanding any
			 other provision of law, the approval of the NAFTA by the [[United States Congress|Congress]] provided for
			 in section 101(a) of the North American Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act

			 shall cease to be effective beginning on the date that is six months after the
			 date of the enactment of this Act.

(b)
Notification of
			 withdrawal
On the date of the
			 enactment of this Act, the [[Executive Office of the President|President]] shall provide to the Governments of Canada
			 and Mexico written notice of withdrawal of the United States from the NAFTA in
			 accordance with Article 2205 of the NAFTA.

(c)
NAFTA
			 defined
In this section, the term NAFTA
 means the
			 North American Free Trade Agreement entered into between the United States,
			 Canada, and Mexico on December 17, 1992.
