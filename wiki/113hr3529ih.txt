{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide exemptions from certain mortgage, servicing, and appraisal requirements for non-profit low-income housing providers, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Protecting Habitat Homeownership Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Exemptions=



==(a) Truth in Lending Act– ==

The  Truth in Lending Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1601 15 U.S.C. 1601 et seq.] )is amended—
:(1) in  section 128(f) ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1638 15 U.S.C. 1638(f)] )—
::(A) in paragraph (3) —
:::(i) by striking“apply to any fixed rate”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> apply to— 
::::(A) any fixed rate ; 
</blockquote>

:::(ii) by striking the period and inserting“; or”; and
:::(iii) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
::::(B) any residential mortgage loan originated by a non-profit low-income housing provider. ; and 
</blockquote>

::(B) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

=====(4) Non-profit low-income housing provider defined– =====

For purposes of this subsection, the term“non-profit low-income housing provider”means an organization that—
:(A) is exempt from taxation pursuant to  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/501 section 501(c)(3)]of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 ;
:(B) makes residential mortgage loans—
::(i) for the purpose of promoting or facilitating homeownership for poor or low-income, disabled, or other disadvantaged persons or families; and
::(ii) sets interest rates on such loans that—
:::(I) are lower than the bank prime loan rate, as determined under the Federal Reserve Statistical Release of selected interest rates (commonly referred to as the H.15) by the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System]] , for the last day of the most recent weekly release of such rates; or
:::(II) are, after adjusting for inflation, no-interest loans or loans with interest rates significantly below the interest rates for loans for purchase of single-family housing generally available in the market;
:(C) except as described under subparagraph (B) , does not engage in the business of a loan originator or mortgage broker;
:(D) conducts its activities in a manner that serves public or charitable purposes;
:(E) receives funding and revenue and charges fees in a manner that does not incentivize the organization or its employees to act other than in the best interests of its clients;
:(F) compensates employees in a manner that does not incentivize employees to act other than in the best interests of its clients; and
:(G) meets such other requirements as the [[w:|Bureau]] determines appropriate. . 
</blockquote>

:(2) in  section 129C(a) ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1639c 15 U.S.C. 1639c(a)] ), by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

====(10) Exemption for non-profit low-income housing providers– ====

This subsection shall not apply to a residential mortgage loan made by a non-profit low-income housing provider (as such term is defined under section 128(f)(4) ). ; 
</blockquote>

:(3) in  section 129E ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1638e 15 U.S.C. 1638e] ), by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

====(l) Exemption for non-Profit low-Income housing providers– ====

This section shall not apply to a residential mortgage loan made by a non-profit low-income housing provider (as such term is defined under section 128(f)(4) ), or any services provided with respect to such a mortgage loan. ; and 
</blockquote>

:(4) in  section 129H ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1638h 15 U.S.C. 1638h] ),by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

====(g) Exemption for non-Profit low-Income housing providers– ====

This section shall not apply to a residential mortgage loan made by a non-profit low-income housing provider (as such term is defined under section 128(f)(4) ). . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act of 1974– ==

   Section 6(k) of the Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act of 1974 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/2605 12 U.S.C. 2605(k)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

===(3) Exemption for non-profit low-income housing providers– ===

This subsection and subsections (l) and (m) shall not apply to the servicing of a residential mortgage loan made by a non-profit low-income housing provider (as such term is defined under section 128(f)(4) of the Truth in Lending Act ). . 
</blockquote>
