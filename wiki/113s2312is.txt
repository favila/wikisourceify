{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend titles 5, 10, and 32, United States Code, to eliminate inequities in the treatment of National Guard technicians, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   National Guard Technician Equity Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Titles 10 and 32, United States Code, amendments regarding National Guard technicians and related provisions=



==(a) Authority To employ technician as non-Dual status technician after 20 years of creditable service– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709 Subsection (c) ofsection 709of title 32, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 
:(c) A person shall have the right to be employed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709 subsection (a)]as a non-dual status technician (as defined by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/10217  section 10217of title 10]) if—
::(1) the technician position occupied by the person has been designated by the Secretary concerned to be filled only by a non-dual status technician; or
::(2) the person occupying the technician position has at least 20 years of creditable service as a military technician (dual status). . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Exception to dual-Status employment condition of membership in Selected Reserve– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/10216  Section 10216of title 10, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/10216 subsection (a)(1)(B)], by inserting“subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/10216 subsection (d)],”before“is required”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/10216 subsection (d)(1)], by striking“Unless specifically exempted by law”and inserting“Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709  section 709(c)(2)of title 32]or as otherwise specifically exempted by law”.

==(c) Continued compensation after loss of membership in Selected Reserve– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/10216 Subsection (e) of section 10216 of title 10, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

===(e) Continued compensation after loss of membership in Selected Reserve– ===

Funds appropriated for the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]may continue to be used to provide compensation to a military technician who was hired as a military technician (dual status), but who is no longer a member of the Selected Reserve. . 
</blockquote>


==(d) Exemption of military technicians from review by Army Qualitative Retention Program or Air Force Selective Retention Program– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/10216 Subsection (f)]of such section is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

===(f) Exemption from consideration by Armed Forces retention boards– ===

A military technician (dual status) who is fully qualified in the technician's military technician (dual status) position and is properly performing the technician's military technician duties in such position—
:(1) shall be retained in the armed forces;
:(2) may not be considered for involuntary separation by a retention board of the armed force concerned; and
:(3) shall be entitled to re-enlist as an enlisted member so as to maintain eligibility for continued employment as a military technician (dual status). . 
</blockquote>


==(e) Repeal of permanent limitations on number of non-Dual status technicians– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/10217  Section 10217of title 10, United States Code], is amended by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/10217 subsection (c)].

==(f) Technician restricted right of appeal and adverse actions covered– ==



===(1) Rights of grievance, arbitration, appeal, and review beyond AG– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709  Section 709of title 32, United States Code], is amended—
:(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709 subsection (f)]—
::(i) in the matter preceding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709 paragraph (1)], by striking“Notwithstanding any other provision of law and under”and inserting“Under”; and
::(ii) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709 paragraph (4)], by striking“a right of appeal”and inserting“subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709 subsection (j)], a right of appeal”; and
:(B) by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 
::(j) 
:::(1) Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709 subsection (f)(4)]or any other provision of law, a technician and a labor organization that is the exclusive representative of a bargaining unit including the technician shall have the rights of grievance, arbitration, appeal, and review extending beyond the adjutant general of the jurisdiction concerned and to the [[w:Merit Systems Protection Board|Merit Systems Protection Board]]and thereafter to the [[w:U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit|United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit]], in the same manner as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4303 sections 4303],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7121 7121], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7701..7703 7701–7703 of title 5], with respect to a performance-based or adverse action imposing removal, suspension for more than 14 days, furlough for 30 days or less, or reduction in pay or pay band (or comparable reduction).
:::(2) This subsection does not apply to a technician who is serving under a temporary appointment or in a trial or probationary period. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Adverse actions covered– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709 Section 709(g) of title 32, United States Code], is amended by striking“7511, and 7512”.

===(3) Conforming amendment– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7511 Section 7511(b) of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(A) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7511 paragraph (5)]; and
:(B) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7511 paragraphs (6) through (10)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7511 paragraphs (5) through (9)], respectively.

==(g) Repeal of prohibition against overtime pay for National Guard technicians– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/709  Section 709(h)of title 32, United States Code], is amended by striking the second sentence and inserting the following new sentence:“Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5542 section5542 ]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5543  5543of title 5]or any other provision of law, the Secretary concerned shall pay a technician for irregular or overtime work at a rate equal to the rate of basic pay applicable to the technician, except that, at the request of the technician, the Secretary may grant the technician, instead of such pay, an amount of compensatory time off from the technician’s scheduled tour of duty equal to the amount of time spent in such irregular or overtime work.”. 

=Sec. 3. Title 5, United States Code, amendment regarding National Guard technicians and related matters=



==(a) Leave– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323  Section 6323(a)of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323 paragraph (1)], by striking“paragraph (2)”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323 paragraphs (2)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323 (3)] ”;
:(B) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323 paragraph (3)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323 paragraph (4)]; and
:(C) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323 paragraph (2)]the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323 paragraph (3)]:

<blockquote> 
::(3) An employee described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323 paragraph (1)]who volunteers for active Guard and Reserve duty (as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/101  section 101(d)(6)of title 10]) or training or duty under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/502  section 502(f)of title 32]shall not accrue leave under this subsection. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Report– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report setting forth the following:
:(A) A description of the average number of hours per fiscal year that a Federal employee who is also a member of the National Guard spends in any type of leave status (including leave without pay) in order to cover periods of active duty for training or inactive-duty training (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/37/101  section 101of title 37, United States Code]), or to engage in other training under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/502..505 sections 502–505 of title 32, United States Code].
:(B) An assessment whether leave provided under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6323  section 6323(a)of title 5, United States Code](as amended by paragraph (1) ), is adequate to cover the operational tempo of the National Guard.

==(b) Comptroller General of the United States report on health care benefits– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report setting forth the following:
:(A) An evaluation of the feasibility of converting military technicians from FEHBP coverage to coverage provided under the TRICARE Reserve Select option of the TRICARE program.
:(B) A description of any problems associated with the conversion of military technicians from FEHBP coverage to coverage provided under chapter 55of title 10, United States Code, during contingency operations.

===(2) Definitions– ===

In this subsection:
:(A) The term contingency operation has the meaning given that term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/101 section 101(a)(13) of title 10, United States Code].
:(B) The term FEHBP coverage means coverage provided underchapter 89 of title 5, United States Code.
