{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 18, United States Code, to provide exceptions from the firearm prohibitions otherwise applicable in relation to marijuana if its possession is lawful under State law.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Protecting Individual Liberties and States' Rights Act” .

=Sec. 2. Exceptions from firearm prohibitions otherwise applicable in relation to marijuana if its possession is lawful under State law=



==(a) Prohibitions applicable to felons and persons subject to a felony indictment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 Section 921(a)(20)of title 18, United States Code] , is amended in the 1st sentence—
:(1) by striking “or” at the end of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (A)] ;
:(2) by striking the period at the end of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (B)] and inserting “, or” ; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(C) any offense pertaining to marijuana that occurs in a State if the conduct constituting the offense is lawful under the law of the State..
</blockquote>


==(b) Prohibitions applicable to unlawful users of, or persons addicted to, a controlled substance– ==



===(1) Sale or other disposition– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Section 922(d) of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following: “ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Paragraph (3)] of the 1st sentence of this subsection shall not apply with respect to a sale or other disposition to a person who is in a State if the controlled substance involved is marijuana, the possession of marijuana is lawful under the law of the State, and the person is a resident of the State.” .

===(2) Receipt, etc– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Section 922(g) of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following: “ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Paragraph (3)] of the preceding sentence shall not apply with respect to conduct of a person in a State if the controlled substance involved is marijuana, the possession of marijuana is lawful under the law of the State, and the person is a resident of the State.” .

==(c) Marijuana defined– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 Section 921(a) of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(36) The term marijuana has the meaning given the term marihuana in section 102(16) of the Controlled Substances Act..
</blockquote>
