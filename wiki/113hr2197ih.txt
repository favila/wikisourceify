{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act to designate segments of the York River and associated tributaries for study for potential inclusion in the National Wild and Scenic Rivers System.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “York River Wild and Scenic River Study Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Designation for study=

 Section 5(a) of the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1276 16 U.S.C. 1276(a)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==(_) York River, Maine– ==


:(A) The York River that flows 11.25 miles from its headwaters at York Pond to the mouth of the river at York Harbor, and all associated tributaries.
:(B) The study conducted under this paragraph shall—
::(i) determine the effect of the designation on—
:::(I) existing commercial and recreational activities, such as hunting, fishing, trapping, recreational shooting, motor boat use, bridge construction;
:::(II) the authorization, construction, operation, maintenance, or improvement of energy production and transmission infrastructure; and
:::(III) the authority of State and local governments to manage those activities; and
::(ii) identify—
:::(I) all authorities that will authorize or require the Secretary to influence local land use decisions (such as zoning) or place restrictions on non-Federal land if designated under this Act;
:::(II) all authorities that the Secretary may use to condemn property; and
:::(III) all private property located in the area studied under this paragraph..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Study and report=

 Section 5(b) of the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1276 16 U.S.C. 1276(b)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==(_) York River, Maine– ==

The study of the York River, Maine, named in paragraph (_) of subsection (a) shall be completed by the Secretary of the Interior and the report thereon submitted to [[w:|Congress]] not later than 3 years after the date on which funds are made available to carry out this paragraph..
</blockquote>
