{{Center|A BILL}}

To establish the United States Advisory Council on Human Trafficking to review Federal Government policy on human trafficking.

=Section 1. United States Advisory Council on Human Trafficking=



==(a) Establishment– ==

There is established the [[w:|United States Advisory Council on Human Trafficking]] (referred to in this section as the “Council” ), which shall provide advice and recommendations to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Senior Policy Operating Group]] (referred to in this section as the “Group” ) and the [[w:|President's Task Force to Monitor and Combat Trafficking in Persons]] .

==(b) Membership– ==



===(1) Composition– ===

The [[w:|Council]] shall be composed of not fewer than 8 individuals who are—
:(A) survivors of human trafficking; or
:(B) nongovernmental experts or professionals in the human trafficking field.

===(2) Equal representation– ===

To the extent practicable, the [[w:|Council]] be comprised of an equal number of survivors and nongovernmental experts.

===(3) Appointment– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall appoint the members of the [[w:|Council]] .

===(4) Term; reappointment– ===

 [[w:|Council]] members shall serve for terms of 2 years and may be reappointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] to serve additional 2-year terms.

==(c) Functions– ==

The [[w:|Council]] shall—
:(1) be a nongovernmental advisory body to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Group]] ;
:(2) meet, at its own discretion, not less frequently than annually or at the request of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Group]] to review Federal Government policy and programs intended to combat human trafficking, including programs related to the provision of services for victims;
:(3) formulate assessments and recommendations to ensure that United States policy and programming efforts conform, to the extent practicable, to the best practices in the field of human trafficking prevention; and
:(4) meet with the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Group]] not less frequently than annually to formally present the [[w:|Council's]] findings and recommendations.

==(d) Reports– ==

Every year beginning after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:|Council]] shall submit a report to the chair of [[w:|President’s Interagency Task Force to Monitor and Combat Trafficking]] that contains the findings derived from the reviews conducted pursuant to subsection (c)(2).

==(e) Employee status– ==

Members of the [[w:|Council]] —
:(1) shall not be considered employees of the United States Government for any purpose; and
:(2) shall not receive compensation other than reimbursement of travel expenses and per diem allowance in accordance with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5703 section 5703 of title 5, United States Code] .

==(f) Nonapplicability of FACA– ==

The [[w:|Council]] shall not be subject to the requirements under the Federal Advisory Committee Act(5 U.S.C. App.).

=Sec. 2. Sunset=

This section shall cease to be effective on September 30, 2020.
