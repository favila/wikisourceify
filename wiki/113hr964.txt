{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Controlled Substances Act to provide that
		  Federal law shall not preempt State law.


{{section|HDDA0C968A91F40D987CD570BBA49D984|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Respect States’ and Citizens’ Rights
			 Act of 2013

        
.

{{section|HD2FA2047A1FE4C7C9981D59465B9DE5D|2. In general}}
Section 708 of the
			 Controlled Substances Act
 (21 U.S.C. 903
)
 is amended—
(1)
by striking
			 No provision
 and inserting 
            (a)
 In
			 general.—
Except as provided in subsection (b)
, no
			 provision
; and

(2)
by adding at the
			 end the following:
(b)
Special rule
				regarding State marihuana laws
In the case of any State law that pertains
				to marihuana, no provision of this title shall be construed as indicating an
				intent on the part of the [[United States Congress|Congress]] to occupy the field in which that provision
				operates, including criminal penalties, to the exclusion of State law on the
				same subject matter, nor shall any provision of this title be construed as
				preempting any such State
				law.

.
