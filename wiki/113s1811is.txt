{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 49, United States Code, to prohibit voice communications through mobile communication devices on commercial passenger flights.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “ Commercial Flight Courtesy Act” .

=Sec. 2. Limitation on mobile communications devices=



==(a) In general– ==

Subchapter I ofchapter 417of title 49, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 41725. Limitation on mobile communications devices==



===(a) In general– ===

Except as provided in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/41725 subsection (b)] , an individual may not engage in voice communications through a mobile communication device while onboard an aircraft in scheduled passenger interstate or intrastate air transportation.

===(b) Exceptions– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/41725 Subsection (a)] shall not apply to—
:(1) individuals traveling on aircraft operated by a foreign carrier unless such travel is between airports located within the United States;
:(2) members of the flight crew or Federal Air Marshals who are conducting official business on behalf of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Transportation Security Administration]] ;
:(3) the use of in-flight phones that are affixed to the aircraft; or
:(4) chartered or private flights..
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections inchapter 417of title 49, United States Code, is amended by inserting after the item relating to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/41724 section 41724] the following new item:
