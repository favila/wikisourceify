{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 18, United States Code, to protect more victims of domestic violence by preventing their abusers from possessing or receiving firearms, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Lori Jackson Domestic Violence Survivor Protection Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Definitions of“intimate partner”and“misdemeanor crime of domestic violence”expanded=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921  Section 921(a)of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 paragraph (32)]—
::(A) by striking“and an individual”and inserting“an individual”; and
::(B) by inserting“, or a dating partner (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2266 section 2266]) or former dating partner”before the period at the end; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 paragraph (33)(A)(ii)]—
::(A) by striking“or by”and inserting“by”; and
::(B) by inserting“, or by a dating partner (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2266 section 2266]) or former dating partner of the victim”before the period at the end. 

=Sec. 3. Unlawful sale of firearm to a person subject to court order=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922  Section 922(d)(8)of title 18, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 
:(8) is subject to a court order described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subsection (g)(8)]; or . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 4. List of persons subject to a restraining or similar order prohibited from possessing or receiving a firearm expanded=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922  Section 922(g)(8)of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in the matter preceding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subparagraph (A)], by striking“that”;
:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subparagraphs (A)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (B)]and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 
::(A) 
:::(i) that was issued after a hearing of which such person received actual notice, and at which such person had an opportunity to participate; or
:::(ii) in the case of an ex parte order, relating to which notice and opportunity to be heard are provided—
::::(I) within the time required by State, tribal, or territorial law; and
::::(II) in any event within a reasonable time after the order is issued, sufficient to protect the person’s right to due process; 
::(B) that restrains such person from—
:::(i) harassing, stalking, threatening, or engaging in other conduct that would put an individual in reasonable fear of bodily injury to such individual, including an order that was issued at the request of an employer on behalf of its employee or at the request of an institution of higher education on behalf of its student; or
:::(ii) intimidating or dissuading a witness from testifying in court; and ; and 
</blockquote>

:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subparagraph (C)]—
::(A) by striking“intimate partner or child”each place it appears and inserting“individual described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subparagraph (B)] ”;
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 clause (i)], by inserting“that”before“includes”; and
::(C) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 clause (ii)], by inserting“that”before“by its”.
