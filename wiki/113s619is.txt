{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 18, United States Code, to prevent unjust and irrational criminal punishments.

={{anchor|H21FD29E9AD9D4BFA92856036E654BECE}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Justice Safety Valve Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H3209A3EB9B394D179A38BCB77F5747C7}}Sec. 2. Authority to impose a sentence below a statutory minimum=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3553 Section 3553 of title 18, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H6EC7A9599CC443EEBF4A00230C207874}}(g) Authority To impose a sentence below a statutory minimum To prevent an unjust sentence– ==



==={{anchor|HB53B4853011545C78BEEC40F615F024D}}(1) General rule– ===

Notwithstanding any provision of law other than this subsection, the court may impose a sentence below a statutory minimum if the court finds that it is necessary to do so in order to avoid violating the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3553 subsection (a)] .

==={{anchor|HB475A00966C449E58AE671FEF5EA23FE}}(2) Court to give parties notice– ===

Before imposing a sentence under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3553 paragraph (1)] , the court shall give the parties reasonable notice of the court’s intent to do so and an opportunity to respond.

==={{anchor|HD1B5315C5D1741BCA6A3BECC03096697}}(3) Statement in writing of factors– ===

The court shall state, in the written statement of reasons, the factors under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3553 subsection (a)] that require imposition of a sentence below the statutory minimum.

==={{anchor|H3509D321C48D4BB1B4BC3CD1E6D172B1}}(4) Appeal rights not limited– ===

This subsection does not limit any right to appeal that would otherwise exist in its absence..
</blockquote>
