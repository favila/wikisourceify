{{Center|A BILL}}

To improve the examination of depository institutions, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HA3589C2B387041EE8B011AD7E6426E66}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Financial Institutions Examination Fairness and Reform Act”.

={{anchor|H33AA1AD070D34AC7AAF25FA3B5D41471}}Sec. 2. Timeliness of examination reports=

The Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council Act of 1978([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/3301 12 U.S.C. 3301 et seq.] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

={{anchor|H3C412FF48094455CB24E13B5A57CE4D0}}Sec. 1012. Timeliness of examination reports=



=={{anchor|HE3811E87A8FA4E5CBDBB7E45DCAA9535}}(a) In general– ==



==={{anchor|HBCAA62525C8D43A3991B69B2F4022B3D}}(1) Final examination report– ===

A Federal financial institutions regulatory agency shall provide a final examination report to a financial institution not later than 60 days after the later of—
:(A) the exit interview for an examination of the institution; or
:(B) the provision of additional information by the institution relating to the examination.

==={{anchor|H41194F535F184865883BCECBDC78A77E}}(2) Exit interview– ===

If a financial institution is not subject to a resident examiner program, the exit interview shall occur not later than the end of the 9-month period beginning on the commencement of the examination, except that such period may be extended by the Federal financial institutions regulatory agency by providing written notice to the institution and the Office of Examination Ombudsman describing with particularity the reasons that a longer period is needed to complete the examination.

=={{anchor|H6BA97AE820204CD9AE700FA942020047}}(b) Examination materials– ==

Upon the request of a financial institution, the Federal financial institutions regulatory agency shall include with the final report under this section an appendix listing all examination or other factual information relied upon by the agency in support of a material supervisory determination..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|HB90954CBDB654416B16DAC3BD04C43D8}}Sec. 3. Examination standards=



=={{anchor|HE0B75D400BFF4225AA99FC5F71896E93}}(a) In general– ==

The Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council Act of 1978([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/3301 12 U.S.C. 3301 et seq.] ] ), as amended by this Act, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H8ACAE73C7A3D459A9C57964364F39FF4}}Sec. 1013. Examination standards==



==={{anchor|HF9ACA97B5B4A4EAF9F5453401A204989}}(a) In general– ===

In the examination of financial institutions—
:(1) a commercial loan shall not be placed in non-accrual status solely because the collateral for such loan has deteriorated in value;
:(2) a modified or restructured commercial loan shall be removed from non-accrual status if the borrower demonstrates the ability to perform on such loan over a maximum period of 6 months, except that with respect to loans on a quarterly, semiannual, or longer repayment schedule such period shall be a maximum of 3 consecutive repayment periods;
:(3) a new appraisal on a performing commercial loan shall not be required unless an advance of new funds is involved; and
:(4) in classifying a commercial loan in which there has been deterioration in collateral value, the amount to be classified shall be the portion of the deficiency relating to the decline in collateral value and repayment capacity of the borrower.

==={{anchor|HD701E41AE97A43EAAD71354D7BF1060D}}(b) Well capitalized institutions– ===

The Federal financial institutions regulatory agencies may not require a financial institution that is well capitalized to raise additional capital in lieu of an action prohibited under subsection (a).

==={{anchor|H2D31DC8A348A460788A56249E9D05C6F}}(c) Consistent loan classifications– ===

The Federal financial institutions regulatory agencies shall develop and apply identical definitions and reporting requirements for non-accrual loans..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H5CCAE0FCDFED4339A9ACE4201E2922F7}}(b) Definition of material supervisory determination– ==

 Section 309(f)(1)(A) of the Riegle Community Development and Regulatory Improvement Act of 1994([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/4806 12 U.S.C. 4806(f)(1)(A)] )is amended—
:(1) in clause (ii), by striking“and”at the end; and
:(2) by inserting after clause (iii)the following:

<blockquote>
::(iv) any issue specifically listed in an exam report as a matter requiring attention by the institution’s management or board of directors; and.
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H0B24DFD8227549E7B9027D83A3D1EC93}}Sec. 4. Examination Ombudsman=



=={{anchor|H781FF1D1A887448D848ADF2DBADBF551}}(a) In general– ==

The Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council Act of 1978([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/3301 12 U.S.C. 3301 et seq.] ] ), as amended by this Act, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HD66544E439244295ABF3FF7BD4D5F9D6}}Sec. 1014. Office of Examination Ombudsman==



==={{anchor|HAF3BD5E206404CB5A00144F8710A1EEC}}(a) Establishment– ===

There is established in the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Council]] an [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Office of Examination Ombudsman]] .

==={{anchor|H4BC83992835647EEA3004E0D4D18CCE2}}(b) Head of office– ===

There is established the position of the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] , who shall serve as the head of the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Office of Examination Ombudsman]] , and who shall be hired separately by the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Council]] and shall be independent from any member agency of the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Council]] .

==={{anchor|H251EDBC89F8E4B05B398FC3A6216009D}}(c) Staffing– ===

The [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] is authorized to hire staff to support the activities of the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Office of Examination Ombudsman]] .

==={{anchor|HDECA446267674D55A2B7F2FF19C66635}}(d) Duties– ===

The [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] shall—
:(1) receive and, at the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman’s]] discretion, investigate complaints from financial institutions, their representatives, or another entity acting on behalf of such institutions, concerning examinations, examination practices, or examination reports;
:(2) hold meetings, at least once every three months and in locations designed to encourage participation from all sections of the United States, with financial institutions, their representatives, or another entity acting on behalf of such institutions, to discuss examination procedures, examination practices, or examination policies;
:(3) review examination procedures of the Federal financial institutions regulatory agencies to ensure that the written examination policies of those agencies are being followed in practice and adhere to the standards for consistency established by the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Council]] ;
:(4) conduct a continuing and regular program of examination quality assurance for all examination types conducted by the Federal financial institutions regulatory agencies;
:(5) process any supervisory appeal initiated under section 1015or section 309(e) of the Riegle Community Development and Regulatory Improvement Act of 1994; and
:(6) report annually to theCommittee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives, theCommittee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senate, and the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Council]] , on the reviews carried out pursuant to paragraphs (3)and (4), including compliance with the requirements set forth in section 1012regarding timeliness of examination reports, and the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Council’s]] recommendations for improvements in examination procedures, practices, and policies.

==={{anchor|H1B4F5C3F6EA54C67AFAFB42104A0AF45}}(e) Confidentiality– ===

The [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] shall keep confidential all meetings, discussions, and information provided by financial institutions..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|H8AD1A2ABC90C49998FB33E3EAC99A335}}Sec. 5. Right to appeal before an independent administrative law judge=

The Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council Act of 1978([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/3301 12 U.S.C. 3301 et seq.] ] ), as amended by this Act, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

={{anchor|H27B241E285E94E19B159C038088E1A7C}}Sec. 1015. Right to appeal before an independent administrative law judge=



=={{anchor|HF7114C3C3E744442B1BDDC7196126F1F}}(a) In general– ==

A financial institution shall have the right to appeal a material supervisory determination contained in a final report of examination.

=={{anchor|H0D555C7E1BED409EB8D9F846D9ECF6BD}}(b) Notice– ==



==={{anchor|H1CCBA98471B44637A557D78AFAC60BDD}}(1) Timing– ===

A financial institution seeking an appeal under this section shall file a written notice with the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] within 60 days after receiving the final report or examination that is the subject of such appeal.

==={{anchor|HF68281EFCBC243DE8E865A50F77B6E7E}}(2) Identification of determination– ===

The written notice shall identify the material supervisory determination that is the subject of the appeal, and a statement of the reasons why the institution believes that the determination is incorrect or should otherwise be modified.

==={{anchor|H6CD397AB1CBF4BB5866BB08529DDD2E5}}(3) Information to be provided to institution– ===

Any information relied upon by the agency in the final report that is not in the possession of the financial institution may be requested by the financial institution and shall be delivered promptly by the agency to the financial institution.

=={{anchor|H41695319544241B78DEFC40213AAE583}}(c) Hearing before independent administrative law judge– ==



==={{anchor|HDB755068862E423694D9F2CC0A21163F}}(1) In general– ===

The [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] shall determine the merits of the appeal on the record, after an opportunity for a hearing before an independent administrative law judge.

==={{anchor|H2A48713B8B044AF4A9F626F52248B573}}(2) Hearing procedures– ===

If a hearing is requested by the financial institution, the hearing shall—
:(A) take place not later than 60 days after the notice of the appeal was received by the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] ; and
:(B) be conducted pursuant to the procedures set forth under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/556 sections[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 556] ] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/557 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 557] of title 5, United States Code] .

==={{anchor|H9E8CD759FE864393A8208B28C12548BD}}(3) Judge recommendation; standard of review– ===

In any hearing under this subsection—
:(A) the administrative law judge shall recommend to the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] what determination should be made; and
:(B) in making such recommendation, the administrative law judge shall not defer to the opinions of the examiner or agency, but shall independently determine the appropriateness of the agency’s decision based upon the relevant statutes, regulations, and other appropriate guidance.

=={{anchor|H4686AF30D3E24DA78882D38EC0714571}}(d) Final decision– ==

A decision by the [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] on an appeal under this section shall—
:(1) be made not later than 60 days after the record has been closed; and
:(2) be final agency action, and shall bind the agency whose supervisory determination was the subject of the appeal and the financial institution making the appeal.

=={{anchor|HA4A622C0F86F446AA77358EB3C779DC9}}(e) Report– ==

The [[w:Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council|Ombudsman]] shall report annually to theCommittee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives, theCommittee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senateon actions taken on appeals under this section, including the types of issues that financial institutions have appealed and the results of those appeals. In no case shall such a report contain information about individual financial institutions or any confidential or privileged information shared by financial institutions.

=={{anchor|H8D26F738C7B34C429904C578A5659AAF}}(f) Retaliation prohibited– ==

A Federal financial institutions regulatory agency may not—
:(1) retaliate against a financial institution, including service providers, or any institution-affiliated party, for exercising appellate rights under this section; or
:(2) delay or deny any agency action that would benefit a financial institution or any institution-affiliated party on the basis that an appeal under this section is pending under this section..
</blockquote>
