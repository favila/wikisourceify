{{Center|A BILL}}

 To prohibit the use of Federal funds for the costs of official portraits of members of Congress, heads of executive agencies, or heads of offices of the legislative branch.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Eliminating Government-funded Oil-painting Act ”or“EGO Act”. 

=Sec. 2. Prohibition on use of funds for official portraits=



==(a) Prohibition– ==

No funds appropriated or otherwise made available to the Federal Government may be used to pay for an official portrait of a member of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] , the head of an executive agency, or the head of an office of the legislative branch.

==(b) Definitions– ==

In this section—
:(1) the term“executive agency”has the meaning provided that term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/41/133 section 133 of title 41, United States Code]; and
:(2) the term“Member of Congress”includes a Delegate or Resident Commissioner to the Congress.
