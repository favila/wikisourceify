{{Center|A BILL}}

 To prohibit the indefinite detention of United States citizens and lawful resident aliens, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short Title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Restore Unalienable Rights Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Amendment of the National Defense Authorization Act=

   Subsection (b) of section 1021 of the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2012 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/801 10 U.S.C. 801 note])is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

==(b) Covered persons– ==

A covered person under this section is a person—
:(1) who—
::(A) planned, authorized, committed, or aided the terrorist attacks that occurred on September 11, 2001, or harbored those responsible for those attacks; or
::(B) was a part of or substantially supported al-Qaeda, the Taliban, or associated forces that are engaged in hostilities against the United States or its coalition partners, including any person who has committed a belligerent act or has directly supported such hostilities in aid of such enemy forces; and
:(2) who is not a United States citizen or lawful resident alien of the United States who was captured, detained, or arrested in the United States. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Prohibition on the indefinite detention of citizens and lawful permanent residents=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/4001  Section 4001of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/4001 subsection (b)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/4001 subsection (c)]; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/4001 subsection (a)]the following:

<blockquote> 
::(b) An authorization to use military force, a declaration of war, or any similar authority may not authorize the detention without charge or trial of a citizen or lawful permanent resident of the United States apprehended in the United States. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 4. Applicability=

The amendments made by this Act shall apply with respect to a person who is taken into custody or brought under the control of the United States after the date of the enactment of this Act.
