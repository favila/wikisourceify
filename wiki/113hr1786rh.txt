{{Center|A BILL}}

 To reauthorize the National Windstorm Impact Reduction Program, and for other purposes.    

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   National Windstorm Impact Reduction Act Reauthorization of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Definitions=



==(a) Director– ==

   Section 203(1) of the National Windstorm Impact Reduction Act of 2004 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/15702 42 U.S.C. 15702(1)] )is amended by striking“Director of the Office of Science and Technology Policy”and inserting“  [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology]] ”.

==(b) Lifelines– ==

   Section 203 of the National Windstorm Impact Reduction Act of 2004 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/15702 42 U.S.C. 15702] )is further amended—
:(1) by redesignating paragraphs (2) through (4) as paragraphs (3) through (5) , respectively; and
:(2) by inserting after paragraph (1) the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 

====(2) Lifelines– ====

The term“lifelines”means public works and utilities, including transportation facilities and infrastructure, oil and gas pipelines, electrical power and communication facilities and infrastructure, and water supply and sewage treatment facilities. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. National Windstorm Impact Reduction Program=

   Section 204 of the National Windstorm Impact Reduction Act of 2004 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/15703 42 U.S.C. 15703] )is amended—
:(1) by striking subsections (a) , (b) , and (c) and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

===(a) Establishment– ===

There is established the [[w:|National Windstorm Impact Reduction Program]], the purpose of which is to achieve major measurable reductions in the losses of life and property from windstorms through a coordinated Federal effort, in cooperation with other levels of government, academia, and the private sector, aimed at improving the understanding of windstorms and their impacts and developing and encouraging the implementation of cost-effective mitigation measures to reduce those impacts. 

===(b) Responsibilities of program agencies– ===



====(1) Lead agency– ====

The [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|National Institute of Standards and Technology]]shall have the primary responsibility for planning and coordinating the [[w:|Program]]. In carrying out this paragraph, the Director shall—
:(A) ensure that the [[w:|Program]]includes the necessary components to promote the implementation of windstorm risk reduction measures by Federal, State, and local governments, national standards and model building code organizations, architects and engineers, and others with a role in planning and constructing buildings and lifelines;
:(B) support the development of performance-based engineering tools, and work with appropriate groups to promote the commercial application of such tools, including through wind-related model building codes, voluntary standards, and construction best practices;
:(C) request the assistance of Federal agencies other than the [[w:|Program]]agencies, as necessary to assist in carrying out this Act;
:(D) coordinate all Federal post-windstorm investigations; and
:(E) when warranted by research or investigative findings, issue recommendations to assist in informing the development of model codes, and provide information to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]on the use of such recommendations.

====(2) National Institute of Standards and Technology– ====

In addition to the lead agency responsibilities described under paragraph (1) , the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|National Institute of Standards and Technology]]shall be responsible for carrying out research and development to improve model building codes, voluntary standards, and best practices for the design, construction, and retrofit of buildings, structures, and lifelines.

====(3) National science foundation– ====

The [[w:National Science Foundation|National Science Foundation]]shall support research in—
:(A) engineering and the atmospheric sciences to improve the understanding of the behavior of windstorms and their impact on buildings, structures, and lifelines; and
:(B) economic and social factors influencing windstorm risk reduction measures.

====(4) National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration– ====

The [[w:National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration|National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration]]shall support atmospheric sciences research to improve the understanding of the behavior of windstorms and their impact on buildings, structures, and lifelines.

====(5) Federal emergency management agency– ====

The [[w:Federal Emergency Management Agency|Federal Emergency Management Agency]]shall—
:(A) support—
::(i) the development of risk assessment tools and effective mitigation techniques;
::(ii) windstorm-related data collection and analysis;
::(iii) public outreach and information dissemination; and
::(iv) promotion of the adoption of windstorm preparedness and mitigation measures, including for households, businesses, and communities, consistent with the Agency’s all-hazards approach; and
:(B) work closely with national standards and model building code organizations, in conjunction with the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|National Institute of Standards and Technology]], to promote the implementation of research results and promote better building practices within the building design and construction industry, including architects, engineers, contractors, builders, and inspectors. ; 
</blockquote>

:(2) by redesignating subsection (d) as subsection (c) , and by striking subsections (e) and (f) ; and
:(3) by inserting after subsection (c) , as so redesignated, the following new subsections:

<blockquote> 

===(d) Budget activities– ===

The [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology]], the [[w:National Science Foundation|Director of the National Science Foundation]], the [[w:National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration|Director of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration]], and the [[w:Federal Emergency Management Agency|Director of the Federal Emergency Management Agency]]shall each include in their agency’s annual budget request to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a description of their agency’s projected activities under the [[w:|Program]]for the fiscal year covered by the budget request, along with an assessment of what they plan to spend on those activities for that fiscal year. 
::(e) Interagency Coordinating Committee on Windstorm Impact Reduction– 
:::(1) Establishment– There is established an [[w:|Interagency Coordinating Committee on Windstorm Impact Reduction]], chaired by the Director.
:::(2) Membership– In addition to the chair, the [[w:|Committee]]shall be composed of—
::::(A) the heads of—
:::::(i) the [[w:Federal Emergency Management Agency|Federal Emergency Management Agency]];
:::::(ii) the [[w:National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration|National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration]];
:::::(iii) the [[w:National Science Foundation|National Science Foundation]];
:::::(iv) the [[w:Office of Science and Technology Policy|Office of Science and Technology Policy]]; and
:::::(v) the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]]; and
::::(B) the head of any other Federal agency the chair considers appropriate.
:::(3) Meetings– The [[w:|Committee]]shall meet not less than 2 times a year at the call of the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology]].
:::(4) General purpose and duties– The [[w:|Committee]]shall oversee the planning and coordination of the [[w:|Program]].
:::(5) Strategic plan– The [[w:|Committee]]shall develop and submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]], not later than one year after the date of enactment of the  National Windstorm Impact Reduction Act Reauthorization of 2014  , a Strategic Plan for the [[w:|Program]]that includes—
::::(A) prioritized goals for the [[w:|Program]]that will mitigate against the loss of life and property from future windstorms;
::::(B) short-term, mid-term, and long-term research objectives to achieve those goals;
::::(C) a description of the role of each [[w:|Program]]agency in achieving the prioritized goals;
::::(D) the methods by which progress towards the goals will be assessed; and
::::(E) an explanation of how the [[w:|Program]]will foster the transfer of research results into outcomes, such as improved model building codes.
:::(6) Progress report– Not later than 18 months after the date of enactment of the  National Windstorm Impact Reduction Act Reauthorization of 2014  , the [[w:|Committee]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on the progress of the [[w:|Program]]that includes—
::::(A) a description of the activities funded under the [[w:|Program]], a description of how these activities align with the prioritized goals and research objectives established in the Strategic Plan, and the budgets, per agency, for these activities;
::::(B) the outcomes achieved by the [[w:|Program]]for each of the goals identified in the Strategic Plan;
::::(C) a description of any recommendations made to change existing building codes that were the result of [[w:|Program]]activities; and
::::(D) a description of the extent to which the [[w:|Program]]has incorporated recommendations from the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee on Windstorm Impact Reduction]].
:::(7) Coordinated budget– The [[w:|Committee]]shall develop a coordinated budget for the [[w:|Program]], which shall be submitted to the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]at the time of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President’s]]budget submission for each fiscal year. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 4. National Advisory Committee on Windstorm Impact Reduction=

   Section 205 of the National Windstorm Impact Reduction Act of 2004 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/15704 42 U.S.C. 15704] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

=Sec. 205. National Advisory Committee on Windstorm Impact Reduction=


:(a) In general– The [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology]]shall establish an [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee on Windstorm Impact Reduction]], which shall be composed of at least 7 members, none of whom may be employees of the Federal Government, including representatives of research and academic institutions, industry standards development organizations, emergency management agencies, State and local government, and business communities who are qualified to provide advice on windstorm impact reduction and represent all related scientific, architectural, and engineering disciplines. The recommendations of the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee]]shall be considered by Federal agencies in implementing the [[w:|Program]].
:(b) Assessments– The [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee on Windstorm Impact Reduction]]shall offer assessments on—
::(1) trends and developments in the natural, engineering, and social sciences and practices of windstorm impact mitigation;
::(2) the priorities of the Program’s Strategic Plan;
::(3) the coordination of the [[w:|Program]]; and
::(4) any revisions to the [[w:|Program]]which may be necessary.

==(c) Compensation– ==

The members of the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee]]established under this section shall serve without compensation.
:(d) Reports– At least every 2 years, the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee]]shall report to the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Director]]on the assessments carried out under subsection (b) and its recommendations for ways to improve the [[w:|Program]].

==(e) Charter– ==

Notwithstanding   section 14(b)(2)of the Federal Advisory Committee Act (5 U.S.C. App), the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee]]shall not be required to file a charter subsequent to its initial charter, filed under section 9(c) of such Act , before the termination date specified in subsection (f) of this section .

==(f) Termination– ==

The [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee]]shall terminate on September 30, 2016.

==(g) Conflict of interest– ==

An [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee]]member shall recuse himself from any [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Advisory Committee]]activity in which he has an actual pecuniary interest. . 
</blockquote>
