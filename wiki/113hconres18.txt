{{Center|CONCURRENT RESOLUTION}}

Authorizing the use of the Capitol Grounds for the National Peace Officers’ Memorial Service.

={{anchor|H109CAECF45094A289058E17F1C4DCDA8}}Section 1. Use of the Capitol Grounds for National Peace Officers’ Memorial Service=



=={{anchor|H8C3DDD4E7F9A4D1CADA39EB3BF2D062E}}(a) In general– ==

The Grand Lodge of the Fraternal Order of Police and its auxiliary (in this resolution referred to as the sponsor ) shall be permitted to sponsor a public event, the 32nd Annual National Peace Officers’ Memorial Service (in this resolution referred to as the event ), on the Capitol Grounds, in order to honor the law enforcement officers who died in the line of duty during 2012.

=={{anchor|H89755A29721240248ED80F4CEF7C29E5}}(b) Date of event– ==

The event shall be held on May 15, 2013, or on such other date as the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives]] and theCommittee on Rules and Administration of the Senatejointly designate.

={{anchor|H19361955FFDB4624949C80EB8FDA09D8}}Sec. 2. Terms and conditions=



=={{anchor|H32DD1B4931FC4D87AB0234D8A5FEDF67}}(a) In general– ==

Under conditions to be prescribed by the [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]] and the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]] , the event shall be—
:(1) free of admission charge and open to the public; and
:(2) arranged not to interfere with the needs of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] .

=={{anchor|H1DEB60D126E7463F911DBFB580AFA466}}(b) Expenses and liabilities– ==

The sponsor shall assume full responsibility for all expenses and liabilities incident to all activities associated with the event.

={{anchor|HD379AF318A8242ED94FD836CF6AC12F8}}Sec. 3. Event preparations=

Subject to the approval of the [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]] , the sponsor is authorized to erect upon the Capitol Grounds such stage, sound amplification devices, and other related structures and equipment, as may be required for the event.

={{anchor|HC82D83BA8F2B4147A1CD1D7E6DE4EBD7}}Sec. 4. Enforcement of restrictions=

The [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]] shall provide for enforcement of the restrictions contained in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/5104 section 5104(c) of title 40, United States Code] , concerning sales, advertisements, displays, and solicitations on the Capitol Grounds, as well as other restrictions applicable to the Capitol Grounds, in connection with the event.
