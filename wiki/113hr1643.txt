{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to allow penalty-free withdrawals from the Federal Thrift Savings Fund to employees who are furloughed as a result of the Federal budget sequester.

={{anchor|H3E465212844C4461A1AFE37FC14892FE}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Employee Hardship Financial Relief Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H896BF30718084885BB0C980281071F0B}}Sec. 2. Penalty-free withdrawals from Thrift Savings fund to Federal employees furloughed as a result of sequester=



=={{anchor|H36DCC5EA8C7B40CDA3298C16F342246A}}(a) In general– ==

 Paragraph (2) of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 72(t)] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986(relating to subsection not to apply to certain distributions) is amended by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|HAF7691D082FA495A902BF40724B75CB4}}(H) Distributions from thrift savings fund to furloughed Federal employees– ===

Distributions to an individual from the Thrift Savings Fund during the period—
:(i) beginning on the date that the individual is furloughed (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7811 section 7811(a)(5) of title 5, United States Code] ) because of lack of funds, and
:(ii) ending on the date which is 30 days after such furlough ceases for such individual,but only to the extent that the aggregate such distributions with respect to any furlough does not exceed the aggregate reduction in such individual’s compensation on account of such furlough..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H591526443A354638833E34609C40B16D}}(b) Effective date– ==

The amendment made by this section shall apply to distributions after the date of the enactment of this Act.
