{{Center|A BILL}}

To establish the First State National Historical Park in
		  the State of Delaware, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H93065703594C41A38AED9A7CB0EF3BD3}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “First State National Historical Park
			 Act”.

={{anchor|HCAECA0F8A4F844458572AA7A762E69D2}}Sec. 2. Definitions=

In this Act:

=={{anchor|HEE7248BE3C5C43A9ADF3B1150F444D24}}(1) Historical
			 park– ==

The term historical park means the First State
			 National Historical Park established by section 3(a)(1).

=={{anchor|H19A6FDAE9D1044AD89953CCE12F9FF67}}(2) Secretary– ==

The
			 term Secretary means the Secretary of the Interior.

=={{anchor|H892868DADAA640B5BDD846145242883F}}(3) State– ==

The
			 term State means—
:(A) the State of
			 Delaware; and
:(B) in the case of the
			 property described in section 3(b)(8), the States of Delaware and
			 Pennsylvania.

={{anchor|H5FB1998D0ED943E19E6D9BCD97294E1C}}Sec. 3. First State
			 National Historical Park=



=={{anchor|HAA9A516AA75E4E829AC82D8B0C28F365}}(a) Establishment– ==



==={{anchor|H51AB9A90C978461896AF7F36BDC97D5C}}(1) In
			 general– ===

Subject to paragraph (3), there is established the First
			 State National Historical Park, to be administered as a unit of the [[w:National Park Service|National
			 Park System]].

==={{anchor|H76679C0A75364439A72BC7834228B9FA}}(2) Purposes– ===

The
			 purposes of the historical park are to preserve, protect, and interpret the
			 nationally significant cultural and historic resources in the State that are
			 associated with—
:(A) early Dutch,
			 Swedish, and English settlement of the Colony of Delaware and portions of the
			 Colony of Pennsylvania; and
:(B) the role of
			 Delaware—
::(i) in
			 the birth of the United States; and
::(ii) as
			 the first State to ratify the Constitution.

==={{anchor|H1865FDA4A3F740FF8863239CD143CEC4}}(3) Determination by
			 secretary– ===



===={{anchor|H7C0AB4C5B1114CC0A634277DCBE161E4}}(A) In
			 general– ====

The historical park shall not be established until the
			 date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] determines that sufficient land or interests in
			 land have been acquired from among the sites described in subsection (b) to
			 constitute a manageable park unit.

===={{anchor|H61EAAB51BD04425E97465A4B012AB217}}(B) Notice– ====

Not
			 later than 30 days after making a determination under subparagraph (A), the
			 [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall publish a notice in the Federal Register of the establishment
			 of the historical park, including an official boundary map for the historical
			 park.

===={{anchor|H2707ED904A0F4C8E8E4B91BAB232A4BF}}(C) Availability of
			 map– ====

The map published under subparagraph (B) shall be on file and
			 available for public inspection in the appropriate offices of the [[w:National Park Service|National Park
			 Service]].

=={{anchor|H3E7A992F868F4BCBB5648568C9CCFF7F}}(b) Historic
			 sites– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may include the following sites within the
			 boundary of the historical park as generally depicted on the maps numbered 1
			 through 6, entitled “First State National Historical Park, New Castle,
			 Kent, Sussex Counties, DE and Delaware County, PA” and “First
			 State National Historical Park, Woodlawn”, numbered T19/80,000G, and
			 dated February 2013:
:(1) The Old Sherriff’s
			 House in New Castle County, Delaware, as depicted on map 4 of 6.
:(2) Fort Christina
			 National Historic Landmark in New Castle County, Delaware, as depicted on map 3
			 of 6.
:(3) Old Swedes Church
			 National Historic Landmark in New Castle County, Delaware, as depicted on map 3
			 of 6.
:(4) Old New Castle
			 Courthouse in New Castle, Delaware, as depicted on map 4 of 6.
:(5) John Dickinson
			 Plantation National Historic Landmark in Kent County, Delaware, as depicted on
			 map 5 of 6.
:(6) Dover Green in
			 Kent County, Delaware, as depicted on map 5 of 6.
:(7) Ryves Holt House
			 in Sussex County, Delaware, as depicted on map 6 of 6.
:(8) The Woodlawn
			 property in New Castle County, Delaware, and Delaware County, Pennsylvania, as
			 depicted on map 2 of 6.
:(9) Old New Castle
			 Green, in New Castle, Delaware, as depicted on map 4 of 6.

={{anchor|HA98DBF3AC01C40F297B1606AC821D7DF}}Sec. 4. Administration=



=={{anchor|H0BDDBAB1B89E4C5C91ABFE4F75D594D6}}(a) In
			 general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall administer the historical park in
			 accordance with—
:(1) this Act;
			 and
:(2) the laws generally
			 applicable to units of the [[w:National Park Service|National Park System]], including—
::(A) the National Park
			 System Organic Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1 16 U.S.C. 1 et seq.]); and
::(B) the Act of August
			 21, 1935 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/461 16 U.S.C. 461 et seq.]).

=={{anchor|H264E21FC286C4C2C987F808EE8BE2FD8}}(b) Land
			 acquisition– ==



==={{anchor|H3B9AF9E47BE544D3884D9B4BB5136A62}}(1) Methods– ===



===={{anchor|H59D0D4CB572D4A5592DF2C5D94280611}}(A) In
			 general– ====

Except as provided in subparagraph (B), the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may
			 acquire all or a portion of any of the sites described in section 3(b),
			 including easements or other interests in land, by purchase from a willing
			 seller, donation, or exchange.

===={{anchor|HEC165D5EF5A746D083B1C37978918E04}}(B) Donation
			 only– ====



====={{anchor|H4FCDD386BD134275AA691DB0D299797B}}(i) Proposed NPS
			 Site– =====

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may acquire only by donation all or a portion
			 of the property identified as “Proposed NPS Site” on map 2 of 6
			 entitled “First State National Historical Park, Woodlawn”,
			 numbered T19/80,000G, and dated February 2013, including easements or other
			 interests in land.

====={{anchor|H07BD5E958C934205A07F0CEBE2AFEF1A}}(ii) Area for
			 potential addition by donation– =====

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may acquire only by
			 donation all or a portion of the property identified as “Area for Potential
			 Addition by Donation” on map 2 of 6 entitled “First State National
			 Historical Park, Woodlawn”, numbered T19/80,000G, and dated February
			 2013.

==={{anchor|H5F65115304624E3F8A77655F5C1438C9}}(2) Boundary
			 adjustment– ===

On acquisition of land or an interest in land under
			 paragraph (1), the boundary of the historical park shall be adjusted to reflect
			 the acquisition.

=={{anchor|H39A313C4E7674A7EA86A9EF9C9400F9E}}(c) Interpretive
			 tours– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may provide interpretive tours to sites and
			 resources in the State that are located outside the boundary of the historical
			 park and associated with the purposes for which the historical park is
			 established, including—
:(1) Fort
			 Casimir;
:(2) DeVries
			 Monument;
:(3) Amstel
			 House;
:(4) Dutch House;
			 and
:(5) Zwaanendael
			 Museum.

=={{anchor|H84FAAF5927D94F479841595D685CCCFC}}(d) Cooperative
			 agreements– ==



==={{anchor|H40D7CB917B4B47988C1D14BC3227B887}}(1) In
			 general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may enter into a cooperative agreement with
			 the State, political subdivisions of the State, institutions of higher
			 education, nonprofit organizations, and individuals to mark, interpret, and
			 restore nationally significant historic or cultural resources within the
			 boundaries of the historical park, if the cooperative agreement provides for
			 reasonable public access to the resources.

==={{anchor|HB44E0D69804349CDB1DDE58AACE104A8}}(2) Cost-sharing
			 requirement– ===



===={{anchor|HE819AAEC051144A19942AB7CA4F5D220}}(A) Federal
			 share– ====

The Federal share of the total cost of any activity carried
			 out under a cooperative agreement entered into under paragraph (1) shall be not
			 more than 50 percent.

===={{anchor|H57F584C20E2F4EFEA3382F995E436FD8}}(B) Form of
			 non-Federal share– ====

The non-Federal share may be in the form of
			 in-kind contributions or goods or services fairly valued.

=={{anchor|H429A689FDCDB48A0BA01288C4B1F4CCE}}(e) Management
			 plan– ==



==={{anchor|H40932C7C683F4DD395433E1A41B7DC3E}}(1) In
			 general– ===

Not later than 3 fiscal years after the date on which
			 funds are made available to carry out this subsection, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall
			 complete a management plan for the historical park.

==={{anchor|HBFCE244B971040EAA508AD7D779A8C59}}(2) Applicable
			 law– ===

The management plan shall be prepared in accordance with
			 [[Public Law 91-383|section 12(b) of Public Law 91–383]] ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1a–7 16 U.S.C. 1a–7(b)]) and other applicable
			 laws.

={{anchor|H70F84EB118394B748CC19CE6147FABA9}}Sec. 5. National landmark
			 study=



=={{anchor|H06CABA2CD86D45BDB875FEB2AC902BE5}}(a) In
			 general– ==

Not later than 3
			 years after the date on which funds are made available to carry out this
			 section, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall complete a study assessing the historical
			 significance of additional properties in the State that are associated with the
			 purposes of historical park.

=={{anchor|HFE2ADA723C604D338B7724692908EFA3}}(b) Requirements– ==

The
			 study prepared under subsection (a) shall include an assessment of the
			 potential for designating the additional properties as National Historic
			 Landmarks.

={{anchor|H2676252597CB4A108DE18E4BC1EF6F6E}}Sec. 6. Authorization of
			 appropriations=

There are
			 authorized to be appropriated such sums as are necessaryto carry out this
			 Act.
