{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend section 112(r) of the Clean Air Act (relating to prevention of accidental releases).

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “General Duty Clarification Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Prevention of accidental releases=



==(a) Purpose and general duty– ==

 Paragraph (1) of section 112(r) of the Clean Air Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7412 42 U.S.C. 7412(r)] )is amended—
:(1) by striking “It shall be the objective” and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

====(A) In general– ====

It shall be the objective; and
</blockquote>

:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

====(B) Regulations– ====



=====(i) Regulations– =====

Not later than 12 months after the date of enactment of the General Duty Clarification Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall promulgate regulations establishing criteria defining, for purposes of this paragraph, the terms extremely hazardous substance , appropriate hazard assessment techniques , and design and maintain a safe facility .

=====(ii) Uniformity– =====

Not later than 18 months after the date of enactment of the General Duty Clarification Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall issue guidelines to ensure that enforcement of this paragraph is handled by the regional offices of the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] in a uniform and appropriate manner across all regions of the United States..
</blockquote>


==(b) Definition of accidental release– ==

 Subparagraph (A) of section 112(r)(2) of the Clean Air Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7412 42 U.S.C. 7412(r)(2)] )is amended by inserting before the period at the end the following: “, other than such an emission resulting from an act intended to cause harm” .

==(c) Designs, approaches or technologies– ==

 Subsection (r) of section 112 of the Clean Air Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7412 42 U.S.C. 7412(r)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(12) Designs, approaches, or technologies– ===

In exercising any authority under this subsection, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall not, directly or indirectly, impose any obligation on any owner or operator of any stationary source to consider or implement particular designs, approaches, or technologies relating to manufacturing, processing, handling, or storage..
</blockquote>
