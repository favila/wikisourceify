{{Center|A BILL}}

To posthumously award a Congressional gold medal to Shirley Chisholm.
1. Short title


This Act may be cited as the “Shirley Chisholm Congressional Gold Medal Act”.

2. Findings


The [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress ]]finds as follows:
: Shirley Chisholm was a pioneer: She was the first African-American woman elected to Congress in 1968 where she served until 1982.: Shirley Chisholm inspired and led the march of political achievement by African-Americans and women in the three decades since she ran for the Presidency of the United States.: Her election to [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress ]]and her candidacy for the Presidency raised the profile and aspirations of all African-Americans and women in the field of politics.: Shirley Chisholm was recognized for her activism, independence, and groundbreaking achievements in politics during and after the civil rights era.: Shirley Chisholm was born in Brooklyn, New York, on November 30, 1924, attended Brooklyn College, and earned a Master's degree from Columbia University.: Shirley Chisholm worked in education and social services before being elected to the New York State Assembly in 1964.: Shirley Chisholm established the Unity Democratic Club in 1960, which played a significant role in rallying Black and Hispanic voters in New York City.: In 1969, Shirley Chisholm began her service in the 91st Congress, representing Brooklyn's 12th Congressional District in the United States House of Representatives.: During her service in the [[Wikipedia:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives ]], Shirley Chisholm promoted the employment of women in Congress and was vocal in her support of civil rights, women's rights, and the poor, while fervently opposing the Vietnam War.: In 1972, Shirley Chisholm was the first African-American to seek the nomination of a major party for [[Wikipedia:President of the United States|President of the United States ]].: Shirley Chisholm was a co-founder of the National Organization for Women.: An historic figure in American political history, Shirley Chisholm died at the age of 80 in Ormond Beach, Florida, on New Year's Day 2005.
3. Congressional gold medal


=(a) Presentation Authorized=

The [[Wikipedia:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives ]]and the [[Wikipedia:United States Senate|President pro tempore of the Senate ]]shall make appropriate arrangements for the posthumous presentation, on behalf of the [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress ]], of a gold medal of appropriate design in commemoration of Shirley Chisholm, in recognition of her activism, independence, and groundbreaking achievements in politics, her election as the first African-American woman in the [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress ]], and her campaign to be the first African-American to gain the nomination of a major political party for [[Wikipedia:President of the United States|President of the United States ]].
=(b) Design and striking=

For purposes of the presentation referred to in subsection (a) , the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury ]](referred to in this Act as the “Secretary ”) shall strike a gold medal with suitable emblems, devices, and inscriptions, to be determined by the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary ]].
4. Duplicate medals


The [[Wikipedia:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary ]]may strike and sell duplicates in bronze of the gold medal struck pursuant to section 3 under such regulations as the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary ]]may prescribe, at a price sufficient to cover the cost thereof, including labor, materials, dies, use of machinery, and overhead expenses, and the cost of the gold medal.

5. Status of medals


=(a) National medals=

The medals struck pursuant to this Act are national medals for purposes of chapter 51 of title 31, United States Code .
=(b) Numismatic items=

For purposes of section 5134 of title 31, United States Code , all medals struck under this Act shall be considered to be numismatic items.
6. Authority To use fund amounts; proceeds of sale


=(a) Authority To use fund amounts=

There is authorized to be charged against the United States Mint Public Enterprise Fund , such amounts as may be necessary to pay for the costs of the medals struck pursuant to this Act.
=(b) Proceeds of sale=

Amounts received from the sale of duplicate bronze medals authorized under section 4 shall be deposited into the United States Mint Public Enterprise Fund.
