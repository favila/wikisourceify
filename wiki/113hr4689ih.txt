{{Center|A BILL}}

 To require a plan approved by the Surface Transportation Board for the long-term storage of rail cars on certain railroad tracks.   

=Section 1. Storage of rail cars=



==(a) Amendment– ==

  Chapter 109of title 49, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 10911. Storage of rail cars==



===(a) Requirement of storage plan– ===



====(1) General rule– ====

A rail carrier providing transportation subject to the jurisdiction of the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]under this part who has stored any rail cars, including rail cars that it owns or leases, on tracks described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 paragraph (2)]for a period of 3 years or more and continues to store such cars on such tracks on the date of enactment of this section shall submit to the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]a plan for such storage under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (b)].

====(2) Covered tracks– ====

Tracks referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 paragraph (1)]are tracks passing through an area zoned for commercial or residential use that were designed or previously used for through transportation of trains, and do not include rail yard or storage yard tracks.

===(b) Plan– ===



====(1) Contents– ====

A plan submitted to the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (a)(1)]shall contain—
:(A) an explanation of the rail carrier’s reasons for the storage of rail cars on the tracks specified in the plan, including an explanation of why no suitable alternative site for storage of the rail cars exists;
:(B) a description of the tracks on which the rail cars are stored or will be stored, including the proximity of such tracks to a home or school;
:(C) evidence that the rail carrier has worked with the relevant local community to develop measures described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subparagraphs (D)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 (E)];
:(D) a description of measures to be undertaken to ensure that the rail car storage will not jeopardize the public safety for the duration of the period to which the plan applies;
:(E) a description of measures to be undertaken to mitigate any impacts of the long-term storage of rail cars on the community through which the tracks pass; and
:(F) an estimated timeline for the final disposition of the rail cars to be stored on the specified tracks.

====(2) Approval– ====

Not later than 6 months after receiving a complete plan under this section, the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]], after public notice and an opportunity for public comment, shall approve or disapprove the plan. The [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]shall approve the plan unless the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]determines that the storage of rail cars covered by the plan jeopardizes public safety, including the safety of children. Any such determination shall be based on information provided in the plan, by the local community, or otherwise through public comment.

===(c) Authority– ===



====(1) General rule– ====

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 paragraph (2)], a rail carrier providing transportation subject to the jurisdiction of the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]under this part may store rail cars as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (a)]only in accordance with a plan for such storage approved by the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (b)(2)].

====(2) Interim authority– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subparagraph (B)], the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]shall authorize the temporary storage of rail cars as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (a)]for a period before a plan has been approved under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (b)(2)]if the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]determines that a plan for such storage has been submitted, or is being prepared for submittal in a timely manner, for approval under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (b)].

=====(B) Exception– =====

The [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]shall not authorize temporary storage under this paragraph if the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]finds, on its own initiative or pursuant to information provided by the local community, that such storage poses a significant safety hazard, including to the safety of children.

===(d) Enforcement– ===



====(1) Petitions– ====

The [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]shall establish procedures to enable a local governmental entity to petition the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]to enforce this section.

====(2) Penalties– ====

Each rail car with respect to which a violation of this section has occurred shall be considered a separate violation for purposes of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/11901 section 11901(a)]. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Table of sections– ==

The table of sections for such chapter is amended by adding at the end the following new item:

<blockquote>  10911. Storage of rail cars.  . 
</blockquote>
