{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

 Condemning the President of the United States and the executive branch of Government for continuous actions that violate the laws and Constitution of the United States.    Whereas the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]violated the Constitution by unilaterally changing sections of the  Affordable Care Act ( [[Public Law 111-148|Public Law 111–148]]  )23 times without congressional approval;   Whereas the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]], in direct violation of their constitutional responsibility, decided to not enforce Federal law with regards to the  Defense of Marriage Act ( [[Public Law 104-199|Public Law 104–199]]  );   Whereas the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]continues to choose not to enforce Federal drug laws ( [[Public Law 91-513|Public Law 91–513]]  , the Controlled Substances Act , and[[Public Law 100-690|Public Law 100–690]] , the Anti-Drug Abuse Act of 1986 );   Whereas the President violated the Constitution by making presidential appointments to the [[w:National Labor Relations Board|National Labor Relations Board]]and the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Consumer Financial Protection Bureau]]while [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]was not in session;   Whereas the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]abused executive privilege with regards to the Operation Fast and Furious scandal, by refusing to comply with a subpoena issued by the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives]]in violation of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/192  section 192of title 2, United States Code];   Whereas the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]violated the law ( [[Public Law 89-236|Public Law 89–236]]  ) when he unilaterally changed our Nation’s immigration laws with regards to Deferred Action;   Whereas the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Department of Health and Human Services]]failed to enforce Federal law ( [[Public Law 111-5|Public Law 111–5]]  ) by illegally waiving the work requirement for welfare recipients;   Whereas the [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]]violated the First Amendment to the United States Constitution by targeting non-profit organizations because of their religious or political beliefs;   Whereas the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]knowingly violated the  National Defense Authorization Act of 2014 ( [[Public Law 113-66|Public Law 113–66]]  )by not providing a 30-day notice to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]prior to transporting five Guantanamo detainees to Qatar in a prisoner swap;   Whereas article II, section 3 of the United States Constitution states in pertinent part that the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] “shall take care that the Laws be faithfully executed.”; and   Whereas the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]reiterates its authority to impeach any president for failing to faithfully enforce the laws of the land or for violating the law: Now, therefore, be it    

= =

That the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]—
:(1) condemns the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and the executive branch of Government for actions and acts that are beyond the clearly defined laws of the United States;
:(2) calls on the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and the executive branch of Government to adhere to the Constitution and halt all unlawful and illegal actions which circumvent [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]and violate the Constitution; and
:(3) calls on the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and the executive branch of Government to respect the constitutionally defined separation of powers.
