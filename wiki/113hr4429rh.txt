{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to permanently extend the subpart F exemption for active financing income.    

=Section 1. Permanent subpart F exemption for active financing income=



==(a) Banking, financing, or similar businesses– ==

Subsection (h) of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/954 section 954]of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (relating to special rule for income derived in the active conduct of banking, financing, or similar businesses) is amended by striking paragraph (9).

==(b) Insurance businesses– ==

Subsection (e) of section 953 of such Code (relating to exempt insurance income) is amended by striking paragraph (10) and by redesignating paragraph (11) as paragraph (10).

==(c) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to taxable years of a foreign corporation beginning after December 31, 2013, and to taxable years of United States shareholders with or within which such taxable years of such foreign corporation end.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Permanent Active Financing Exception Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Permanent subpart F exemption for active financing income=



==(a) Banking, financing, or similar businesses– ==

  Subsection (h) of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/954 section 954]of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (relating to special rule for income derived in the active conduct of banking, financing, or similar businesses) is amended by striking paragraph (9) .

==(b) Insurance businesses– ==

  Subsection (e) of section 953 of such Code (relating to exempt insurance income) is amended by striking paragraph (10) and by redesignating paragraph (11) as paragraph (10) .

==(c) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to taxable years of a foreign corporation beginning after December 31, 2013, and to taxable years of United States shareholders with or within which such taxable years of such foreign corporation end.
