{{Center|JOINT RESOLUTION}}

Proposing an amendment to the Constitution of the United States relating to United States citizenship. That the following article is proposed as an amendment to the Constitution of the United States, which shall be valid to all intents and purposes as part of the Constitution when ratified by the legislatures of three-fourths of the several States within seven years after the date of its submission for ratification by the Congress:

<blockquote>
 A person born in the United States shall not be a citizen of the United States unless—: at the time of the person’s birth, one parent of the person is—:: a citizen of the United States;:: an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence in the United States who resides in the United States; or:: an alien performing active service in the Armed Forces of the United States; or: the person is naturalized in accordance with the laws of the United States..
</blockquote>
