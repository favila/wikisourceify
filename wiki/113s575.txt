{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 28, United States Code, to provide an Inspector General for the judicial branch, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Judicial Transparency and Ethics Enhancement Act of 2013”.

=Sec. 2. Inspector general for the judicial branch=



==(a) Establishment and Duties– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/ Part III of title 28, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===60 INSPECTOR GENERAL FOR THE JUDICIAL BRANCH– ===

===Sec. 1021. Establishment===

There is established for the judicial branch of the Government the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Office of Inspector General for the Judicial Branch]] (in this chapter referred to as the“Office”).

===Sec. 1022. Appointment, term, and removal of Inspector General===



====(a) Appointment– ====

The head of the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Office]] shall be the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] , who shall be appointed by the [[w:|Chief Justice of the United States]] after consultation with the majority and minority leaders of the Senate and the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker]] and [[w:United States House of Representatives|minority leader of the House of Representatives]] .

====(b) Term– ====

The [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall serve for a term of 4 years and may be reappointed by the [[w:|Chief Justice of the United States]] for any number of additional terms.

====(c) Removal– ====

The [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] may be removed from office by the [[w:|Chief Justice of the United States]] . The [[w:|Chief Justice]] shall communicate the reasons for any such removal to both Houses of Congress.

===Sec. 1023. Duties===

With respect to the judicial branch, the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Office]] shall—
:(1) conduct investigations of alleged misconduct in the judicial branch (other than the [[w:Supreme Court of the United States|United States Supreme Court]] ) under chapter 16 that may require oversight or other action within the judicial branch or by [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] ;
:(2) conduct investigations of alleged misconduct in the [[w:Supreme Court of the United States|United States Supreme Court]] that may require oversight or other action within the judicial branch or by [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] ;
:(3) conduct and supervise audits and investigations;
:(4) prevent and detect waste, fraud, and abuse; and
:(5) recommend changes in laws or regulations governing the judicial branch.

===Sec. 1024. Powers===



====(a) Powers– ====

In carrying out the duties of the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Office]] , the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall have the power to—
:(1) make investigations and reports;
:(2) obtain information or assistance from any Federal, State, or local governmental agency, or other entity, or unit thereof, including all information kept in the course of business by the [[w:|Judicial Conference of the United States]] , the judicial councils of circuits, the [[w:Administrative Office of the United States Courts|Administrative Office of the United States Courts]] , and the [[w:United States Sentencing Commission|United States Sentencing Commission]] ;
:(3) require, by subpoena or otherwise, the attendance and testimony of such witnesses, and the production of such books, records, correspondence, memoranda, papers, and documents, which subpoena, in the case of contumacy or refusal to obey, shall be enforceable by civil action;
:(4) administer to or take from any person an oath, affirmation, or affidavit;
:(5) employ such officers and employees, subject to the provisions of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/ title 5] , governing appointments in the competitive service, and the provisions ofchapter 51andsubchapter III of chapter 53 of such titlerelating to classification and General Schedule pay rates;
:(6) obtain services as authorized by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3109 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 3109] of title 5] at daily rates not to exceed the equivalent rate for a position at level IV of the Executive Schedule under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5315 section 5315 of such title] ; and
:(7) the extent and in such amounts as may be provided in advance by appropriations Acts, to enter into contracts and other arrangements for audits, studies, analyses, and other services with public agencies and with private persons, and to make such payments as may be necessary to carry out the duties of the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Office]] .

====(b) Chapter 16 matters– ====

The [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall not commence an investigation under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1023 section 1023(1)] until the denial of a petition for review by the judicial council of the circuit under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/352 section 352(c) of this title] or upon referral or certification to the [[w:|Judicial Conference of the United States]] of any matter under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/354 section 354(b) of this title] .

====(c) Limitation– ====

The [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall not have the authority to—
:(1) investigate or review any matter that is directly related to the merits of a decision or procedural ruling by any judge, justice, or court; or
:(2) punish or discipline any judge, justice, or court.

===Sec. 1025. Reports===



====(a) When To Be Made– ====

The [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall—
:(1) make an annual report to the [[w:|Chief Justice]] and to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] relating to the activities of the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Office]] ; and
:(2) make prompt reports to the [[w:|Chief Justice]] and to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] on matters that may require action by the [[w:|Chief Justice]] or [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] .

====(b) Sensitive Matter– ====

If a report contains sensitive matter, the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] may so indicate and [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] may receive that report in closed session.

====(c) Duty To Inform Attorney General– ====

In carrying out the duties of the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Office]] , the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] shall report expeditiously to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] whenever the [[w:Federal government of the United States|Inspector General]] has reasonable grounds to believe there has been a violation of Federal criminal law.

===Sec. 1026. Whistleblower protection===



====(a) In General– ====

No officer, employee, agent, contractor, or subcontractor in the judicial branch may discharge, demote, threaten, suspend, harass, or in any other manner discriminate against an employee in the terms and conditions of employment because of any lawful act done by the employee to provide information, cause information to be provided, or otherwise assist in an investigation regarding any possible violation of Federal law or regulation, or misconduct, by a judge, justice, or any other employee in the judicial branch, which may assist the Inspector General in the performance of duties under this chapter.

====(b) Civil Action– ====

An employee injured by a violation of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1026 subsection (a)] may, in a civil action, obtain appropriate relief..
