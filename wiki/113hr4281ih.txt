{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to improve the oversight of contracts awarded by the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to small business concerns owned and controlled by veterans.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Protecting Business Opportunities for Veterans Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Contracts with small business concerns owned and controlled by veterans=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127  Section 8127of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subsection (l)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subsection (m)]; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subsection (k)]the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subsection (l)]:

<blockquote> 

===(l) Limitations on subcontracting– ===


:(1) 
::(A) The requirements applicable to a small business concern under  section 46 of the Small Business Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/657s 15 U.S.C. 657s] )shall apply with respect to a small business concern owned and controlled by a veteran with a service-connected disability or a small business concern owned and controlled by a veteran that is awarded a contract under this section.
::(B) For purposes of applying the requirements of  section 46 of the Small Business Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/657s 15 U.S.C. 657s] )pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subparagraph (A)]—
:::(i) a small business concern owned and controlled by a veteran shall be treated as a small business concern owned and controlled by a veteran with a service-connected disability; and
:::(ii) the term“similarly situated entity”used in such[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/46 section 46]means a subcontractor for a small business concern owned and controlled by a veteran with a service-connected disability or a small business concern owned and controlled by a veteran described in such[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subparagraph (A)].
:(2) Upon awarding a contract to a small business concern under this section, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall obtain from an offeror a certification that the offeror will comply with the requirements described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 paragraph (1)(A)]if awarded the contract. Such certification shall—
::(A) specify the exact performance requirements applicable under such paragraph; and
::(B) explicitly acknowledge that the certification is subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1001  section 1001of title 18].
:(3) If the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines that a small business concern that is awarded a contract under this section did not act in good faith with respect to the requirements described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 paragraph (1)(A)], the small business concern shall be subject to the penalties specified in—
::(A)    section 16(g)(1) of the Small Business Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/645 15 U.S.C. 645(g)(1)] ); and
::(B)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1001  section 1001of title 18].
:(4) 
::(A) The [[w:Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization|Director of the Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization]]established by  section 15(k) of the Small Business Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/644 15 U.S.C. 644(k)] )and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Chief Acquisition Officer]]established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/41/1702  section 1702of title 41]shall jointly implement a process using the systems described in  section 16(g)(2) of the Small Business Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/645 15 U.S.C. 645(g)(2)] ), or any other systems available, to monitor compliance with this subsection. The [[w:Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization|Director]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Chief Acquisition Officer]]shall jointly refer any violations of this subsection to the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs]].
::(B) The [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General]]shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Senate]]an annual report that includes—
:::(i) the number of referred violations received under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subparagraph (A)]; and
:::(ii) the disposition of such referred violations, including the number of small business concerns suspended or debarred from Federal contracting or referred to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]for prosecution. . 
</blockquote>
