{{Center|A BILL}}

To extend the Terrorism Risk Insurance Program of the Department of the Treasury for 10 years, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Fostering Resilience to Terrorism Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Certification of acts of terrorism=

 Section 102(1) of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701 15 U.S.C. 6701note] )is amended—
:(1) in subparagraph (A), in the matter preceding clause (i), by striking “Secretary, in concurrence with” and inserting the following: “ [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] , in concurrence with the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] ,” ;
:(2) in subparagraph (B), in the matter preceding clause (i), by striking “Secretary” and inserting “ [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] ” ; and
:(3) in subparagraph (D), by striking “Secretary” and inserting “ [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] ” .

=Sec. 3. 10-year extension of Terrorism Risk Insurance Program=



==(a) Program years– ==

 Subparagraph (G) of section 102(11) of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701 15 U.S.C. 6701 note] )is amended by striking “2014” and inserting “2024” .

==(b) Timing of mandatory recoupment– ==

 Subclause (III) of section 103(e)(7)(E)(i) of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701 15 U.S.C. 6701note] )is amended by striking “2017” and inserting “2024” .

==(c) Termination date– ==

 Subsection (a) of section 108 of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701 15 U.S.C. 6701note] )is amended by striking “2014” and inserting “2024” .

==(d) Ongoing reports regarding market conditions for terrorism risk insurance– ==

 Paragraph (2) of section 108(e) of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701 15 U.S.C. 6701note] )is amended by striking “and 2013” and inserting “2013, 2017, 2020, and 2023” .

=Sec. 4. Homeland security enhancements=



==(a) Provision of information to insureds– ==

 Section 103 of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701 15 U.S.C. 6701note] )is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

===(j) Provision of homeland security information and best practices to insureds– ===

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] shall provide to insureds under the Program, through the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] —
:(1) timely homeland security information, including terrorism risk information, at the appropriate classification level; and
:(2) information on best practices to foster resilience to an act of terrorism..
</blockquote>


==(b) Research on program participation and best practices– ==

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] shall conduct research to determine the extent to which insureds participating in the Terrorism Risk Insurance Program under title I of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701 15 U.S.C. 6701note] )—in particular, insureds in critical infrastructure sectors as identified by such [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] —incorporate information provided under section 103(j)(2) of such Actinto business operations. Not later than the expiration of the 12-month period that begins upon the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] commencing the provision of information pursuant to such section 103(j)(2), such [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall submit a report regarding the findings of the research conducted under this subsection to the [[w:United States House Committee on Homeland Security|Committees on Homeland Security]] and [[w:United States House Committee on Financial Services|Financial Services of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs|Committees on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs]] and [[w:United States Senate Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs|Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senate]] .
