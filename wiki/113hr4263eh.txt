{{Center|IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES}}

 
{{Center|AN ACT}}

 To amend the Homeland Security Act of 2002 to authorize the Department of Homeland Security to establish a social media working group, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Social Media Working Group Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Social media working group=



==(a) In general– ==

   Title III of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/6/181 6 U.S.C. 181 et seq.] )is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 318. Social media working group==



===(a) Establishment– ===

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]]shall establish within the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department]]a [[w:Department of Homeland Security|social media working group]](in this section referred to as the“Group”).

===(b) Purpose– ===

In order to enhance information sharing between the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department]]and appropriate stakeholders, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]]shall provide guidance and best practices to the emergency preparedness and response community on the use of social media technologies before, during, and after a terrorist attack or other emergency.

===(c) Membership– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Office of the Under Secretary for Science and Technology|Under Secretary for Science and Technology]]shall serve as the permanent chairperson of the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]], and shall designate, on a rotating basis, a representative from a State or local government who is a member of the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]]to serve as co-chairperson. The [[w:Office of the Under Secretary for Science and Technology|Under Secretary]]shall establish term limits for individuals appointed to the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]]pursuant to paragraph (2) . Membership of the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]]shall be composed of a cross section of subject matter experts from Federal, State, local, tribal, and nongovernmental organization practitioners, including representatives from the following entities:
:(A) The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Office of Public Affairs of the Department]].
:(B) The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Office of the Chief Information Officer of the Department]].
:(C) The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Privacy Office of the Department]].
:(D) The [[w:Federal Emergency Management Agency|Federal Emergency Management Agency]].
:(E) The [[w:Federal Emergency Management Agency|Office of Disability Integration and Coordination of the Federal Emergency Management Agency]].
:(F) The American Red Cross.
:(G) The [[w:United States Forest Service|Forest Service]].
:(H) The [[w:Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|Centers for Disease Control and Prevention]].
:(I) The [[w:United States Geological Survey|United States Geological Survey]].
:(J) The [[w:National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration|National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration]].

====(2) Additional members– ====

The [[w:Office of the Under Secretary for Science and Technology|Under Secretary for Science and Technology]]shall appoint, on a rotating basis, qualified individuals to the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]]. The total number of such additional members shall—
:(A) be equal to or greater than the total number of regular members under paragraph (1) ; and
:(B) include—
::(i) not fewer than three representatives from the private sector; and
::(ii) representatives from—
:::(I) State, local, and tribal entities, including from—
::::(aa) law enforcement;
::::(bb) fire services;
::::(cc) emergency management services; and
::::(dd) public health entities;
:::(II) universities and academia; and
:::(III) non-profit disaster relief organizations.

===(d) Consultation with non-members– ===

To the extent practicable, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]]shall work with existing bodies in the public and private sectors to carry out subsection (b) .

===(e) Meetings– ===



====(1) Initial meeting– ====

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of this section, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]]shall hold its initial meeting. Such initial meeting may be held virtually.

====(2) Subsequent meetings– ====

After the initial meeting under paragraph (1) , the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]]shall meet at least twice each year, or at the call of the Chairperson. Such subsequent meetings may be held virtually.

===(f) Nonapplicability of FACA– ===

The  Federal Advisory Committee Act (5 U.S.C. App.)shall not apply to the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]].

===(g) Reports– ===

Not later than March 30 of each year, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Group]]shall submit to the appropriate congressional committees a report that includes the following:
:(1) A review of current and emerging social media technologies being used to support preparedness and response activities related to terrorist attacks and other emergencies.
:(2) A review of best practices and lessons learned on the use of social media during the response to terrorist attacks and other emergencies that occurred during the period covered by the report at issue.
:(3) Recommendations to improve the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department’s]]use of social media for emergency management purposes.
:(4) Recommendations to improve public awareness of the type of information disseminated through social media, and how to access such information, during a terrorist attack or other emergency.
:(5) Recommendations to improve information sharing among the Department and its components.
:(6) Recommendations to improve information sharing among State and local governments.
:(7) A review of available training for Federal, State, local, and tribal officials on the use of social media in response to a terrorist attack or other emergency.
:(8) A summary of coordination efforts with the private sector to discuss and resolve legal, operational, technical, privacy, and security concerns. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of contents in section 1(b) of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 is amended by inserting after the item relating to section 317 the following new item:

<blockquote>  Sec. 318. Social media working group.  . 
</blockquote>
    Passed the House of Representatives July 8, 2014. Karen L. Haas, Clerk.
