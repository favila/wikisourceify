 To amend title 38, United States Code, to provide for advance appropriations for certain discretionary accounts of the Department of Veterans Affairs.    

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Putting Veterans Funding First Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Advance appropriations for certain discretionary accounts of the Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/117 Section 117]of title 38, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) by striking“ medical care accounts of the Department ”each place it appears and inserting“ discretionary accounts of the Department ”;
:(2) in subsection (c)—
::(A) by striking“ medical care accounts of the Veterans Health Administration, Department of Veterans Affairs account ”and inserting“ accounts of the Department of Veterans Affairs account ”;
::(B) in paragraph (1), by inserting“ Veterans Health Administration, ”after“ (1) ”;
::(C) in paragraph (2), by inserting“ Veterans Health Administration, ”after“ (2) ”;
::(D) in paragraph (3), by inserting“ Veterans Health Administration, ”after“ (3) ”;
::(E) by adding at the end the following new paragraphs:

<blockquote> 
:::(4) Veterans Health Administration, Medical Research and Support. 
:::(5) National Cemetery Administration. 
:::(6) Veterans Benefits Administration, Native American Veteran Housing Loan Program Account. 
:::(7) Departmental Administration, General Administration, General Operating Expenses. 
:::(8) Departmental Administration, General Operating Expenses, Veterans Benefits Administration. 
:::(9) Departmental Administration, Information Technology Systems. 
:::(10) Departmental Administration, Office of the Inspector General. 
:::(11) Departmental Administration, Construction, Major Projects. 
:::(12) Departmental Administration, Construction, Minor Projects. 
:::(13) Departmental Administration, Grants for Construction of State Extended Care Facilities. 
:::(14) Departmental Administration, Grants for Construction of Veterans Cemeteries. ; and 
</blockquote>

::(F) in the subsection heading, by striking“  Medical care accounts  ”and inserting“  Discretionary accounts  ”; and
:(3) in the section heading, by striking“  certain medical care accounts  ”and inserting“  certain discretionary accounts  ”.

==(b) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by subsection (a) shall apply with respect to fiscal year 2016 and each subsequent fiscal year.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Putting Veterans Funding First Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Scoring of budgetary effects=

The budgetary effects of this Act, for the purpose of complying with the Statutory Pay-As-You-Go Act of 2010 , shall be determined by reference to the latest statement titled“ Budgetary Effects of PAYGO Legislation ”for this Act, submitted for printing in the Congressional Record by the [[w:United States House Committee on the Budget|Chairman of the House Budget Committee]], provided that such statement has been submitted prior to the vote on passage.

=Sec. 4. Comptroller General review of Department of Veterans Affairs medical care budget submission=

   Section 4 of the Veterans Health Care Budget Reform and Transparency Act of 2009 ( [[Public Law 111-81|Public Law 111–81]]  ;[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/117  38 U.S.C. 117note])is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

=Sec. 4. Comptroller General review of Department of Veterans Affairs medical care budget submission=



==(a) Review of budget submission; reports– ==

For each of fiscal years 2015, 2016, and 2017, the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]]shall—
:(1) review the budget requests for the medical care accounts of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]in the President’s budget request submitted pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105  section 1105of title 31, United States Code]; and
:(2) submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees]]on [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Veterans’ Affairs]], [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Appropriations]], and the [[w:United States House Committee on the Budget|Budget]]of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Budget|Senate]]and [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|House of Representatives]]a report or reports on the results of each such review.

==(b) Timing and scope of reports– ==

In determining the timing and scope for the submittal of the report or reports required under subsection (a) , the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General]]shall consult with the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]].

==(c) Medical care accounts– ==

In this section, the term“ medical care accounts of the Department of Veterans Affairs ”means the following medical care accounts of the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]], [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]account:
:(1) Medical Services.
:(2) Medical Support and Compliance.
:(3) Medical Facilities. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 5. Department of Veterans Affairs budget planning reform=



==(a) Future-Years Veterans Program– ==



===(1) In general– ===

  Chapter 1of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

===Sec. 119. Future-Years Veterans Program===



====(a) Submission to Congress– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]each year, at or about the time that the President’s budget is submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105  section 1105(a)of title 31], a Future-Years Veterans Program reflecting the estimated expenditures and proposed appropriations included in that budget. Any such Future-Years Veterans Program shall cover the fiscal year with respect to which the budget is submitted and at least the four succeeding fiscal years.

====(b) Consistency– ====


:(1) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall ensure that amounts described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119 subparagraph (A) of paragraph (2)]for any fiscal year are consistent with amounts described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119 subparagraph (B) of such paragraph]for that fiscal year.
:(2) Amounts referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119 paragraph (1)]are the following:
::(A) The amounts specified in program and budget information submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]in support of expenditure estimates and proposed appropriations in the budget submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105  section 1105(a)of title 31]for any fiscal year, as shown in the Future-Years Veterans Program submitted pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119 subsection (a).] 
::(B) The total amounts of estimated expenditures and proposed appropriations necessary to support the programs, projects, and activities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]included pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 paragraph (5) ofsection 1105(a)of title 31]in the budget submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]under that section for any fiscal year.

====(c) Contents– ====

The Future-Years Veterans Program under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119 subsection (a)]shall set forth the five-year plan of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to address the commitment of the United States to veterans and the resources necessary to meet that commitment and shall be developed and updated, as appropriate, annually by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]. Each Future-Years Veterans Program shall include an explanation of—
:(1) the information that was used to develop program planning guidance for the Future-Years Veterans Program; and
:(2) how the resource allocations included in the Future-Years Veterans Program correlate to such five-year strategy. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Clerical amendment– ===

The table of sections at the beginning of such chapter is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/118 section 118]the following new item:

<blockquote>  119. Future-Years Veterans Program.  . 
</blockquote>


===(3) Effective Date– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119  Section 119of title 38, United States Code], as added by paragraph (1) , shall apply with respect to the preparation and submission of the fiscal year 2018 budget request for the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

==(b) Quadrennial Veterans Review– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Such chapter is further amended by adding after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119 section 119], as added by subsection (a)(1) , the following new section:

<blockquote> 

===Sec. 120. Quadrennial veterans review===



====(a) Requirement– ====


:(1) Not later than fiscal year 2017, and every fourth year thereafter, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall conduct a review of the strategy for meeting the commitment of the United States to veterans and the resources necessary to meet that commitment (in this section referred to as a quadrennial veterans review ).
:(2) Each quadrennial veterans review shall include a comprehensive examination of the policies and strategies of the United States with respect to veterans, including recommendations regarding the long-term strategy and priorities for programs, services, benefits, and outcomes regarding veterans and guidance on the programs, assets, capabilities, budget, policies, and authorities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
:(3) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall conduct each quadrennial veterans review in consultation with key officials of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], the heads of other Federal agencies, and other relevant governmental and nongovernmental entities, including State, local, and tribal government officials, members of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]], veterans service organizations, private sector representatives, academics, and other policy experts.
:(4) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall ensure that each quadrennial veterans review is coordinated with the Future-Years Veterans Program required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119 section 119 of this title].

====(b) Contents of review– ====

In each quadrennial veterans review, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall—
:(1) delineate a veterans strategy consistent with the commitment of the United States to veterans and refine a strategy for the types of, and provision of, programs, services, benefits, and outcomes consistent with current authorities and requirements;
:(2) outline and prioritize the full range of programs and capabilities regarding veterans provided by the Federal Government;
:(3) identify the budget plan required to provide sufficient resources to successfully execute the full range of such programs and capabilities;
:(4) include an assessment of the organizational alignment of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]with respect to the strategy referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/120 paragraph (1)]and the programs and capabilities referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/120 paragraph (2)];
:(5) review and assess the effectiveness of the mechanisms of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]for executing the process of turning the requirements identified in the quadrennial veterans review into a plan to meet such requirements, including an expenditure plan for the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]; and
:(6) identify emerging trends, problems, opportunities, and issues that could affect veterans or the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]during the ten-year period following the period covered by the review.

====(c) Submission to Congress– ====


:(1) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]]a report regarding each quadrennial veterans review. The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall submit the report in the year following the year in which the review is conducted, but not later than the date on which the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]submits the budget for the next fiscal year to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105  section 1105of title 31, United States Code].
:(2) Each report submitted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/120 paragraph (1)]shall include—
::(A) the results of the quadrennial veterans review;
::(B) a description of the challenges to, and opportunities for, the assumed or defined veterans-related interests of the Nation that were examined for the purposes of that review;
::(C) the strategy for meeting the Nation’s commitment to veterans, including a prioritized list of the missions of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]];
::(D) a description of the interagency cooperation, preparedness of Federal assets, infrastructure, budget plan, and other elements of the programs and policies of the Nation associated with the strategy referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/120 subsection (b)(1)]that are required to execute successfully the full range of programs and capabilities identified in such strategy and the programs and capabilities outlined under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/120 subsection (b)(2)];
::(E) an assessment of the organizational alignment of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]with the strategy referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/120 subsection (b)(1)]and the programs and capabilities outlined under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/120 subsection (b)(2)], including the Department's organizational structure, management systems, budget and accounting systems, human resources systems, procurement systems, and physical and technical infrastructure;
::(F) a discussion of the status of cooperation among Federal agencies in the effort to promote national support for veterans;
::(G) a discussion of the status of cooperation between the Federal Government and State, local, and tribal governments in supporting veterans and providing programs, services, benefits, and outcomes to assist veterans;
::(H) an explanation of any underlying assumptions used in conducting the review; and
::(I) any other matter the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]considers appropriate. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Clerical amendment– ===

The table of sections at the beginning of such chapter is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119 section 119], as added by subsection (a)(2) , the following new item:

==(c) Policy Guidance– ==



===(1) In general– ===

==(d) Chief strategy officer of the Department of Veterans Affairs– ==



===(1) In general– ===

  Chapter 3of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

===Sec. 323. Chief Strategy Officer===



====(a) In general– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall designate the Assistant Secretary whose functions include planning, studies, and evaluations as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Chief Strategy Officer of the Department]]. The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Chief Strategy Officer]]shall advise the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]on long-range strategy and implications.

====(b) Responsibilities– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Chief Strategy Officer]]is the principal advisor to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]and other senior officials of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], and shall provide independent analysis and advice to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]and such officials. The Chief Strategy Officer shall carry out the following responsibilities:
:(1) Conducting cost estimation and cost analysis for the programs of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
:(2) Establishing policies for, and overseeing the integration of, the planning, programming, budgeting and execution process for the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
:(3) Providing analysis and advice on matters relating to the planning and programming phase of the planning, programming, budgeting and execution process, and the preparation of materials and guidance for such process, as directed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]], working in coordination with the [[w:Assistant Secretary for Management|Assistant Secretary for Management]].
:(4) Developing and executing the Future-Years Veterans Program of the Department, as specified under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/119 section 119 of this title].
:(5) Developing resource discussions relating to requirements under consideration in the quadrennial veterans review under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/120 section 120 of this title].
:(6) Formulating study guidance for analysis of alternatives for programs and initiatives, including any necessary acquisitions, development, or procurement commensurate with such alternatives, and performance of such analysis as directed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]].
:(7) Reviewing, analyzing, and evaluating programs for executing approved strategies and policies, ensuring that information on programs and expected outcomes is presented accurately and completely.
:(8) Ensuring that the costs of programs and alternatives are presented accurately and completely by assisting in establishing standards, policies, and procedures for the conduct of cost estimation and cost analysis throughout the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], including guidance relating to the proper selection of confidence levels in cost estimates generally and for specific programs of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
:(9) Conducting studies at the request of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]regarding costs, policy assumptions, and strategic implications of current policies and possible alternatives.
:(10) Communicating directly to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Deputy Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]about matters for which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Chief Strategy Officer]]is responsible without obtaining the approval or concurrence of any other official within the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Clerical amendment– ===

The table of sections at the beginning of such chapter is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/322 section 322]the following new item:

<blockquote>  323. Chief Strategy Officer.  . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 6. Study on the functions and organizational structure of the Office of the Secretary of Veterans Affairs and of the Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) Secretary of Veterans Affairs Study– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall conduct a study of the functions and organizational structure of the [[w:United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs|Office of the Secretary]]and of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

==(b) Contents of Study– ==

In conducting the study under subsection (a) , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall consider whether the allocation of functions and the organizational structure of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], as of the date of the enactment of this Act, constitute the most effective, efficient, and economical allocation and structure to assist the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]in carrying out the duties and responsibilities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]. The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall also consider—
:(1) whether the organization of the [[w:United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs|Office]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]is—
::(A) optimally structured to assist the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]in the effective exercise of control over the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], including—
:::(i) policy development and strategic planning;
:::(ii) programming, planning, and budget development and policy, program, and budget execution; and
:::(iii) contingency planning; and
::(B) the most effective and efficient organization for the initiation, development, and articulation of veterans’ policy and the provision of benefits and services;
:(2) means of improving and strengthening the oversight and accountability within the [[w:United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs|Office]]and [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]];
:(3) factors inhibiting efficient and effective execution of the functions of the [[w:United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs|Office]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], including factors relating to—
::(A) any duplication of functions (both within and between the [[w:United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs|Office]]and [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]);
::(B) the availability to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]of sufficient and detailed information regarding the operation of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to enable effective planning, policy execution, and oversight; and
::(C) the sufficiency of resources, including personnel, to carry out current and projected requirements in a more effective and efficient manner; and
:(4) possible alternative allocations and realignments of authorities and functions within the [[w:United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs|Office]]and [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to improve the Department’s overall operation and better provide benefits and services.

==(c) Independent Contractor Study– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall enter into a contract with an appropriate entity under which the entity shall carry out an independent study of the same matters required to be considered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]under subsection (b) . The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall ensure that the entity has full access to such information as the contractor requires in order to conduct the study and that the contractor otherwise receives full cooperation from all officials and entities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

==(d) Report to Congress– ==

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]]a report on the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary’s]]study under subsection (a) . The report shall include—
:(1) the findings and conclusions of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]with respect to each of the matters set forth in subsection (b) ;
:(2) any recommendations of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for organizational changes in the [[w:United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs|Office of the Secretary]]and the overall [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]and a description of the means for implementing each recommendation; and
:(3) a copy of the report of the independent contractor under subsection (c) , together with such comments on such report as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]considers appropriate. 

=Sec. 7. Performance awards in the Senior Executive Service=

For each of fiscal years 2014 through 2018, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]may not pay any performance awards under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5384  section 5384of title 5, United States Code].  Amend the title so as to read:“ A bill to amend[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/ title 38, United States Code], to provide for advance appropriations for certain discretionary accounts of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]], to direct the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]to submit to Congress a Future-Years Veterans Program and a quadrennial veterans review, to establish in the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]a Chief Strategy Officer, and for other purposes. ”.
