{{Center|A BILL}}

 To prohibit the long-term storage of rail cars on certain railroad tracks unless the Surface Transportation Board has approved the rail carrier’s rail car storage plan.   

=Section 1. Storage of rail cars=



==(a) Amendment– ==

  Chapter 109of title 49, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 10911. Storage of rail cars==



===(a) Storage plan requirement– ===



====(1) In general– ====

A rail carrier providing transportation subject to the jurisdiction of the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]under this part shall submit a storage plan to the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (b)]if the rail carrier—
:(A) has stored any rail cars, including rail cars that the rail carrier owns or leases, on tracks described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 paragraph (2)]for a period of 3 years or more; and
:(B) continues to store such cars on such tracks on the date of the enactment of this section.

====(2) Covered tracks– ====

Tracks referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 paragraph (1)(A)]—
:(A) pass through an area zoned for commercial or residential use;
:(B) were designed or previously used for through transportation of trains; and
:(C) do not include rail yard or storage yard tracks.

===(b) Plan– ===



====(1) Contents– ====

A storage plan submitted to the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (a)(1)]shall contain—
:(A) an explanation of the rail carrier’s reasons for storing rail cars on the tracks specified in the plan, including an explanation of why a suitable alternative storage site does not exist;
:(B) a description of the tracks on which the rail cars are stored or will be stored, including the proximity of such tracks to a home or school;
:(C) evidence that the rail carrier has worked with the relevant local community to develop measures described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subparagraph (D)];
:(D) a description of measures to be undertaken—
::(i) to ensure that the rail car storage will not jeopardize the public safety for the duration of the period to which the plan applies; and
::(ii) to mitigate any impacts of the long-term storage of rail cars on the community through which the tracks pass; and
:(E) an estimated time line for the final disposition of the rail cars to be stored on the specified tracks.

====(2) Approval– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

Not later than 6 months after receiving a storage plan under this section, the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]], after public notice and an opportunity for public comment, shall—
:(i) approve the plan; or
:(ii) disapprove the plan if the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]determines that the storage of rail cars covered by the plan jeopardizes public safety, including the safety of children.

=====(B) Basis– =====

Any determination under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subparagraph (A)]shall be based on information provided in the storage plan, by the local community, or through public comment.

===(c) Authority– ===



====(1) General rule– ====

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 paragraph (2)], a rail carrier providing transportation subject to the jurisdiction of the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]under this part may store rail cars as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (a)]only in accordance with a storage plan approved by the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (b)(2)].

====(2) Interim authority– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subparagraph (B)], the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]shall authorize the temporary storage of rail cars as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (a)]before a storage plan has been approved under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (b)(2)]if the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]determines that a plan for such storage has been submitted, or is being prepared for submittal in a timely manner, for approval under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 subsection (b)].

=====(B) Exception– =====

The [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]may not authorize temporary storage under this paragraph if the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]determines, on its own initiative or pursuant to information provided by the local community, that such storage poses a significant safety hazard, including to the safety of children.

===(d) Enforcement– ===



====(1) Petitions– ====

The [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]shall establish procedures to enable a local governmental entity to petition the [[w:Surface Transportation Board|Board]]to enforce the provisions of this section.

====(2) Penalties– ====

Each rail car stored in violation of this section shall constitute a separate violation for purposes of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/10911 section 11901(a)]. . 
</blockquote>
