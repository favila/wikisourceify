{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to establish a Department of Veterans Affairs Medal for Distinguished Public Service to honor veterans who make remarkable and distinguished contributions to their communities.   

=Section 1. Department of Veterans Affairs Medal for Distinguished Public Service=



==(a) Establishment– ==

 Subchapter II of chapter 5 of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 533. Department of Veterans Affairs Medal for Distinguished Public Service==



===(a) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may award a decoration called the Department of Veterans Affairs Medal for Distinguished Public Service to a veteran described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/533 subsection (b)].

===(b) Veteran described– ===

A veteran described in this subsection is a veteran whom the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines has, during the period after the date of the veteran’s discharge or release from the active military, naval, or air service—
:(1) made remarkable and distinguished contributions to the community of the veteran; and
:(2) exemplified the virtues of honor, duty, and commitment to service that originally led the veteran to service in the Armed Forces.

===(c) Posthumous award– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may award the medal under this section to a veteran described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/533 subsection (b)]who is deceased.

===(d) Regulations– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may prescribe regulations to carry out this section. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections at the beginning of such chapter of such title is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/532 section 532]the following new item:

<blockquote>  533. Department of Veterans Affairs Medal for Distinguished Public Service.  . 
</blockquote>
