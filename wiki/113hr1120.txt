{{Center|A BILL}}

To prohibit the National Labor Relations Board from
		  taking any action that requires a quorum of the members of the Board until such
		  time as Board constituting a quorum shall have been confirmed by the Senate,
		  the Supreme Court issues a decision on the constitutionality of the
		  appointments to the Board made in January 2012, or the adjournment sine die of
		  the first session of the 113th Congress.


{{section|HBE2B61DD12714A96B9991B119A678E7D|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Preventing Greater Uncertainty in
			 Labor-Management Relations Act

        
.

{{section|HA652D1515C174700B81FE50E0FD156CC|2. Activities by the National Labor Relations Board prohibited}}
Effective on the date of enactment of this
			 Act, the [[National Labor Relations Board|National Labor Relations Board]] shall cease all activity that requires
			 a quorum of the members of the [[National Labor Relations Board|Board]], as set forth in the National Labor
			 Relations Act
 (29 U.S.C. 151 et seq.
)
. The [[National Labor Relations Board|Board]] shall not implement,
			 administer, or enforce any decision, rule, vote, or other action decided,
			 undertaken, adopted, issued, or finalized on or after January 4, 2012, that
			 requires a quorum of the members of the [[National Labor Relations Board|Board]], as set forth in such Act.

{{section|HA165004B515141D2A2D8970C7DDF2B04|3. Termination}}
The provisions of this Act shall terminate
			 on the date on whichâ€”
(1)
all members of the [[National Labor Relations Board|National Labor Relations
			 Board]] are confirmed with the advice and consent of the [[United States Senate|Senate]], in accordance
			 with clause 2 of section 2 of article II of the Constitution, in a number
			 sufficient to constitute a quorum, as set forth in the National Labor Relations
			 Act
 (29 U.S.C. 151 et seq.
)
;

(2)
the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]]
			 issues a decision on the constitutionality of the appointments to the [[National Labor Relations Board|Board]]
			 made in January 2012; or

(3)
the adjournment
			 sine die of the first session of the 113th Congress.


{{section|HE7642C9DD2F34FFAA2FBA356EB75B4BD|4. Effect of certain board actions}}
In the event
			 that this Act terminates pursuant to paragraphs (1)
 or (3) of section 3
, no
			 decision, rule, vote, or other action decided, undertaken, adopted, issued, or
			 finalized by the [[National Labor Relations Board|Board]] on or after January 4, 2012, that requires authorization
			 by not less than a quorum of the members of the [[National Labor Relations Board|Board]], as set forth in the
			 National Labor Relations Act
, may be implemented, administered, or enforced
			 unless and until it is considered and acted upon by a [[National Labor Relations Board|Board]] constituting a
			 quorum, as set forth in the National Labor Relations Act
, or the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]]
			 issues a decision on the constitutionality of the appointments to the [[National Labor Relations Board|Board]]
			 made in January 2012.
