{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 10, United States Code, to ensure that the Secretary of Defense provides each member of the Armed Forces, before the member separates from the Armed Forces, with an electronic copy of the medical records of the member and a physical examination.   

=Section 1. Transmittal of electronic medical records to separating members of the Armed Forces=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1142  Section 1142of title 10, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1142 subsection (c)]—
::(A) by striking“In the case”and inserting“(1) In the case”;
::(B) by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
:::(2) 
::::(A) The Secretary concerned shall provide to each member of the armed forces who is scheduled to be separated from the armed forces a copy of the medical records of the member (including the results of a Physical Evaluation Board or any other physical examination) in an electronic format.
::::(B) To the extent practicable, the Secretary concerned shall ensure that medical records provided to a member of the National Guard under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1142 subparagraph (A)]include medical records that are—
:::::(i) in connection with the service of the member in the National Guard, maintained by the Governor of the State, Commonwealth, territory, or possession of the United States, or in the case of the District of Columbia, the Commanding General of the National Guard of the District of Columbia; and
:::::(ii) provided to the Secretary for purposes of such subparagraph. ; and 
</blockquote>

::(C) in the heading by striking“ to Department of Veterans Affairs ”; and
:(2) in the heading by striking“ to Department of Veterans Affairs ”.

==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections at the beginning ofchapter 58 of such titleis amended by striking the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1142 section 1142]and inserting the following:

<blockquote>  1142. Preseparation counseling; transmittal of medical records  . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 2. Mandatory physical examinations of separating members of the Armed Forces=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1145  Section 1145of title 10, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1145 subsection (a)(5)]—
::(A) by striking“(A) The Secretary”and inserting“The Secretary”;
::(B) by inserting“comprehensive”after“undergo a”; and
::(C) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1145 subparagraph (B)]; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 

===(f) Mandatory physical examinations for members not otherwise covered by this section– ===


:(1) The Secretary concerned shall provide a comprehensive physical examination pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1145 subsection (a)(5)]to each member of the armed forces who is scheduled to be separated from the armed forces and does not otherwise receive such an examination under such subsection.
:(2) A member may not be entitled to health care benefits pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1145 subsection (a)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1145 (b)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1145 (c)]solely by reason of being provided a physical examination under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1145 paragraph (1)]. . 
</blockquote>
