{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide effective criminal prosecutions for certain identity thefts, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HAEE6CC69E8734562A0B2B0359C02DD4B}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Stopping Tax Offenders and Prosecuting Identity Theft Act of 2013”or the“STOP Identity Theft Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HB897BF26A230473A949D93B44B5A56E1}}Sec. 2. Use of Department of Justice resources with regard to tax return identity theft=



=={{anchor|H484AB2F4AB3A452EB27BBA5613798C4F}}(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] should make use of all existing resources of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] , including any appropriate task forces, to bring more perpetrators of tax return identity theft to justice.

=={{anchor|H208197FD6B3C48F2979ED17A1921D4C5}}(b) Considerations To be taken into account– ==

In carrying out this section, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] should take into account the following:
:(1) The need to concentrate efforts in those areas of the country where the crime is most frequently reported.
:(2) The need to coordinate with State and local authorities for the most efficient use of their laws and resources to prosecute and prevent the crime.
:(3) The need to protect vulnerable groups, such as veterans, seniors, and minors (especially foster children) from becoming victims or otherwise used in the offense.

={{anchor|HF8A60B6A16CF42218423BD491C330FEC}}Sec. 3. Victims of identity theft may include organizations=

Chapter 47 of title 18, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028 section 1028] —
::(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028 subsection (a)(7)] , by inserting“(including an organization)”after“another person”; and
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028 subsection (d)(7)] , in the matter preceding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028 subparagraph (A)] , by inserting“or other person”after“specific individual”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028A section 1028A(a)(1)] , by inserting“(including an organization)”after“another person”.

={{anchor|HEE65464D29EE45438BD8C8FF3BEEAC5D}}Sec. 5. Reporting requirement=



=={{anchor|H5D67817A8545477AB0E2E6931C26BDDA}}(a) Generally– ==

Beginning with the first report made more than 9 months after the date of the enactment of this Act under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1116 section 1116 of title 31, United States Code] , the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall include in such report the information described in subsection (b)of this section as to progress in implementing this Act and the amendments made by this Act.

=={{anchor|H22D5D9B50928457ABBE1C55787906500}}(b) Contents– ==

The information referred to in subsection (a)is as follows:
:(1) Information readily available to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] about trends in the incidence of tax return identity theft.
:(2) The effectiveness of statutory tools, including those provided by this Act, in aiding the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] in the prosecution of tax return identity theft.
:(3) Recommendations on additional statutory tools that would aid in removing barriers to effective prosecution of tax return identity theft.
:(4) The status on implementing the recommendations of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department’s]] March 2010 Audit Report 10–21 entitled The Department of Justice’s Efforts to Combat Identity Theft .
