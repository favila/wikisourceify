{{Center|A BILL}}

 To establish an emergency watershed protection disaster assistance fund to be available to the Secretary of Agriculture to provide assistance for any natural disaster.   

=Section 1. Emergency watershed protection disaster assistance fund=



==(a) Definitions– ==

In this section:

===(1) Emergency watershed protection program– ===

The term emergency watershed protection program means the emergency watershed protection program established under  section 403 of the Agricultural Credit Act of 1978 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/2203 16 U.S.C. 2203] ).

===(2) Fund– ===

The term Fund means the Emergency Watershed Protection Disaster Assistance Fund established by subsection (b) .

===(3) Natural disaster– ===

The term natural disaster means—
:(A) a natural disaster declared by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]under  section 321(a) of theConsolidated Farm and Rural Development Act  ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/7/1961 7 U.S.C. 1961(a)] ); or
:(B) a major disaster or emergency designated by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]under the  Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/5121 42 U.S.C. 5121 et seq].).

===(4) Secretary– ===

The term Secretary means the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]].

==(b) Establishment– ==

There is established in the Treasury of the United States a fund to be known as the“Emergency Watershed Protection Disaster Assistance Fund”, to be administered by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]to carry out activities under the emergency watershed protection program.

==(c) Purpose and availability of fund– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Subject to subsection (d) , amounts in the Fund shall be available to the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]], until expended, to provide additional amounts for authorized activities described in subsection (b) in areas affected by a natural disaster.

===(2) Maintenance of funding– ===

Amounts in the Fund shall supplement (and not supplant) other Federal funding for natural disasters.

==(d) Directly appropriated amounts– ==

Amounts appropriated directly to the Fund by this Act or any subsequent Act for a specific purpose shall be available only for that purpose until such time as the transfer authority provided by subsection (f) takes effect with regard to the amounts.

==(e) Transfer of prior appropriations to fund– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]may transfer to the Fund, and merge with other amounts generally appropriated to the Fund, the available unobligated balance of any amounts described in paragraph (2) if, in advance of the transfer, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]—
:(A) determines that the unobligated amounts are no longer needed to respond to the natural disaster for which the amounts were originally appropriated; and
:(B) provides a certification of that determination to the [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|Committees on Appropriations of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Senate]].

===(2) Amounts– ===



====(A) In general– ====

Amounts described in this paragraph are amounts for the emergency watershed protection program that—
:(i) except in the case of funds described in subparagraph (B) , remain unobligated as of the date of enactment of this Act; or
:(ii) 
::(I) are made available under the  Disaster Relief Appropriations Act, 2013 ([[Public Law 113-2|division A ofPublic Law 113–2;]]  127 Stat. 4); and
::(II) are not necessary to fulfill any eligible request for the funds made in accordance with that Act.

====(B) Emergency designations– ====



=====(i) Emergency Designation for Statutory PAYGO– =====

This subsection is designated as an emergency requirement pursuant to  section 4(g) of the Statutory Pay-As-You-Go Act of 2010 ( [[Public Law 111-139|Public Law 111–139]]  ; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/933 2 U.S.C. 933(g)] ).

=====(ii) Emergency Designation for Congressional Enforcement– =====

In the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]], this subsection is designated as an emergency requirement pursuant to sections 403(a) and 423(b) of S. Con. Res. 13 (111th Congress) , the concurrent resolution on the budget for fiscal year 2010.

===(3) Certain funds– ===



====(A) Preliminary deposit– ====

Not later than 30 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]shall transfer to the Fund all amounts described in paragraph (2)(A)(ii) that are in excess of amounts requested in applications received by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]on or before April 18, 2014, in accordance with the  Disaster Relief Appropriations Act, 2013 ([[Public Law 113-2|division A ofPublic Law 113–2;]]  127 Stat. 4).

====(B) Final deposit– ====

As soon as practicable after the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]has made final determinations on all applications described in subparagraph (A) , the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]shall transfer to the Fund all remaining unobligated amounts described in paragraph (2)(A)(ii) .

==(f) Transfer of other appropriations to fund– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Unless otherwise specifically provided in an appropriations Act, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]may transfer to or within the Fund, and merge with other amounts generally appropriated to the Fund, the available unobligated balance of any amounts that are appropriated for fiscal year 2014 or any subsequent fiscal year for the emergency watershed protection program to respond to a natural disaster or are designated by the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]as an emergency requirement if, in advance of the transfer, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]—
:(A) determines that the unobligated amounts are no longer needed to respond to the natural disaster for which the amounts were originally appropriated; and
:(B) provides a certification of that determination to the [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|Committees on Appropriations of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Senate]].

===(2) Timing– ===

A transfer of unobligated amounts with respect to a natural disaster may not be made under this subsection until after the end of the 2-year period beginning on the date on which the amounts were originally appropriated for that natural disaster.

==(g) Availability of funds– ==

Amounts transferred into the Fund under this section shall be available to the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]for obligation without further appropriation.

==(h) Administrative expenses– ==

In addition to any other funds available to the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]to cover administrative costs, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]may use up to 3 percent of the amounts allocated from the Fund for a specific natural disaster to cover administrative costs of the State and local offices of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Department of Agriculture]]in the areas affected by the natural disaster to carry out disaster-related activities.

==(i) Limitation on per disaster obligations– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Amounts in the Fund, except for amounts described in subsection (d) that are appropriated to the Fund and obligated in accordance with that subsection, may not be obligated in excess of $1,000,000 for a natural disaster until at least 15 days after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]notifies the [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|Committees on Appropriations of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Senate]]of the determination of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]to obligate additional amounts and the reasons for the determination.

===(2) Specific and extreme need– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]may not obligate more than 50 percent of the amounts in the Fund for any 1 natural disaster unless the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]declares that there is a specific and extreme need for additional funds to be provided in response to that natural disaster at time of the obligation.

==(j) Quarterly reports– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]]shall submit, on a quarterly basis, to the [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|Committees on Appropriations of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Senate]]a report describing the status of the Fund and any transactions that have affected the Fund since the previous report.
