{{Center|A BILL}}

To require the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency and the Secretary of Energy to conduct a fuel system requirements harmonization study, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Gas Accessibility and Stabilization Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Expansion of waiver authority=

 Section 211(c)(4)(C) of the Clean Air Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7545 42 U.S.C. 7545(c)(4)(C)] )is amended—
:(1) in clause (ii)(II), by inserting “a problem with distribution or delivery equipment that is necessary for the transportation or delivery of fuel or fuel additives,” after “equipment failure,” ;
:(2) in clause (iii)(II), by inserting “(except that the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] may extend the effectiveness of a waiver for more than 20 days if the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] determines that the conditions under clause (ii)supporting a waiver determination will exist for more than 20 days)” before the semicolon at the end;
:(3) by redesignating the second clause (v)(relating to the authority of the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] to approve certain State implementation plans) as clause (vi); and
:(4) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(vii) Presumptive approval– ===

Notwithstanding any other provision of this subparagraph, if the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] does not approve or deny a request for a waiver under this subparagraph within 3 days after receipt of the request, the request shall be considered to be approved as received by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] and the applicable fuel standards shall be waived for the period of time requested..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Fuel system requirements harmonization study=

 Section 1509 of the Energy Policy Act of 2005([[Public Law 109-58|Public Law 109–58]] ;119 Stat. 1083)is amended—
:(1) in subsection (a)—
::(A) in paragraph (1)(A), by inserting “biofuels,” after “oxygenated fuel,” ; and
::(B) in paragraph (2)(G), by striking “Tier II” and inserting “Tier III” ; and
:(2) in subsection (b)(1), by striking “2008” and inserting “2014” .
