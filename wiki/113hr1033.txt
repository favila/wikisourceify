{{Center|A BILL}}

To authorize the acquisition and protection of nationally
		  significant battlefields and associated sites of the Revolutionary War and the
		  War of 1812 under the American Battlefield Protection Program.

={{anchor|H4D7EFA17921940CF8D45582AEB388210}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “American Battlefield Protection
			 Program Amendments Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H5505B8F9C8644C4399939FC50A252495}}Sec. 2. Revolutionary War
			 and War of 1812 American battlefield protection=

Section 7301(c) of the Omnibus Public Land
			 Management Act of 2009 (Public Law 111–11) is amended as follows:
:(1) In paragraph
			 (1)—
::(A) by striking
			 subparagraph (A) and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H1535A68712FE47919947BC850DBF4FAE}}(A) Battlefield
				report– ====

The term battlefield report means,
				collectively—
:(i) the report
				entitled Report on the Nation’s Civil War Battlefields, prepared
				by the [[Wikipedia:National Park Service|Civil War Sites Advisory Commission]], and dated July 1993; and
:(ii) the report
				entitled Report to Congress on the Historic Preservation of Revolutionary
				War and War of 1812 Sites in the United States, prepared by the [[Wikipedia:National Park Service|National
				Park Service]], and dated September 2007.;
				and
</blockquote>

::(B) in subparagraph
			 (C)(ii), by striking “Battlefield Report” and inserting
			 “battlefield report”.
:(2) In paragraph (2),
			 by inserting “eligible sites or” after
			 “acquiring”.
:(3) In paragraph (3), by inserting “an
			 eligible site or” after “acquire”.
:(4) In paragraph (4), by inserting “an
			 eligible site or” after “acquiring”.
:(5) In paragraph (5),
			 by striking “An” and inserting “An eligible site or
			 an”.
:(6) By redesignating
			 paragraph (6) as paragraph (9).
:(7) By inserting after
			 paragraph (5) the following new paragraphs:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H94E2476F05CE4DB4B12D892685D7E6D1}}(6) Willing
				Sellers– ===

Acquisition of land
				or interests in land under this subsection shall be from willing sellers
				only.

==={{anchor|H61A0D797F6BC4467A11578250725E6B6}}(7) Report– ===

Not later than 5 years after the date of
				the enactment of this subsection, the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall submit to [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress]] a
				report on the activities carried out under this subsection, including a
				description of—
:(A) preservation
				activities carried out at the battlefields and associated sites identified in
				the battlefield report during the period between publication of the battlefield
				report and the report required under this paragraph;
:(B) changes in the
				condition of the battlefields and associated sites during that period;
				and
:(C) any other relevant
				developments relating to the battlefields and associated sites during that
				period.

==={{anchor|H15F0C77494294443ADDB2CADEB1FBE6D}}(8) Prohibition on
				lobbying– ===



===={{anchor|H747395144EF647C38E4F07A4CDCBFDC5}}(A) In
				general– ====

None of the funds provided pursuant to this section may
				be used for purposes of lobbying any person or entity regarding the
				implementation of this section or be granted, awarded, contracted, or otherwise
				be made available to any person, organization, or entity that participates in
				such lobbying.

===={{anchor|H1C71E0F87075422A8BCEA6C2D6BB0D5A}}(B) Lobbying
				defined– ====

For purposes of this paragraph, the term
				lobbying means to directly or indirectly pay for any personal
				service, advertisement, telegram, telephone call, letter, printed or written
				matter, or other device intended or designed to influence in any manner a
				Member of Congress, a jurisdiction, or an official of any government to favor,
				adopt, or oppose by vote or otherwise, any legislation, law, ratification,
				policy, land use plan (including zoning), or appropriation of funds before or
				after the introduction of any bill, resolution, or other measure proposing such
				legislation, law, ratification, policy, or
				appropriation..
</blockquote>

:(8) In paragraph (9)
			 (as redesignated by paragraph (6)), by striking “2013” and
			 inserting “2018”.
