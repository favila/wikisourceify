{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend section 112(r) of the Clean Air Act (relating to
		  prevention of accidental releases).

={{anchor|HF311D1C7C3394B6F9B58078D858CE65F}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “General Duty Clarification Act of
			 2013”.

={{anchor|H7088EF111E1F4F03B68DCCD5CCC5266A}}Sec. 2. Prevention of
			 accidental releases=



=={{anchor|H9276A60C47394339AE33721F5CFE47A9}}(a) Purpose and
			 general duty– ==

Paragraph (1) of
			 section 112(r) of the Clean Air Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7412 42 U.S.C. 7412(r)]) is amended—
:(1) by striking
			 “It shall be the objective” and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H5C8F2755843F44718DF5B8556D5160A4}}(A) In
				general– ====

It shall be the
				objective;
				and
</blockquote>

:(2) by adding at the
			 end the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|HAC3BC024240D4D32A54D476A00BD2C42}}(B) Regulations– ====



====={{anchor|HBDE0AA6E7BC24C4EB5BE84458E2FAA32}}(i) Regulations– =====

Not later than 12 months after the date of
				enactment of the General Duty Clarification
				Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall promulgate regulations
				establishing criteria defining, for purposes of this paragraph, the terms
				extremely hazardous substance, appropriate hazard assessment
				techniques, and design and maintain a safe facility.

====={{anchor|H2757555050BD4B39A38FFBFA7B902DC7}}(ii) Uniformity– =====

Not later than 18 months after the date of
				enactment of the General Duty Clarification
				Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall issue guidelines to ensure
				that enforcement of this paragraph is handled by the regional offices of the
				[[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] in a uniform and appropriate manner across all
				regions of the United
				States..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H6109E92FCE334D49B08C1B220C7C95EA}}(b) Definition of
			 accidental release– ==

Subparagraph (A) of section 112(r)(2) of the
			 Clean Air Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7412 42 U.S.C. 7412(r)(2)]) is amended by inserting before the period
			 at the end the following: “, other than such an emission resulting from
			 an act intended to cause harm”.

=={{anchor|H7BAA02C0FD8549609191F85ADD0396A0}}(c) Designs,
			 approaches or technologies– ==

Subsection (r) of section 112 of the Clean
			 Air Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7412 42 U.S.C. 7412(r)]) is amended by adding at the end the
			 following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H3D7F3064D8334BFDA298BAF3A0616159}}(12) Designs,
				approaches, or technologies– ===

In exercising any authority under
				this subsection, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall not, directly or indirectly, impose
				any obligation on any owner or operator of any stationary source to consider or
				implement particular designs, approaches, or technologies relating to
				manufacturing, processing, handling, or
				storage..
</blockquote>
