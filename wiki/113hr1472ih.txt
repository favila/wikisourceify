{{Center|A BILL}}

To restore the longstanding partnership between States and the Federal Government in managing the Medicaid program.

={{anchor|HD7DD81288AE04CA3B3256DA3AC1691DC}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“State Flexibility Act”.

={{anchor|H1C1C639007394022B44A7A2F94DAE27B}}Sec. 2. Repeal of Medicaid and CHIP maintenance of effort requirements under PPACA=



=={{anchor|H4B59BC85E694437DBDBAB43860603E53}}(a) Repeal of PPACA Medicaid MOE– ==

 Section 1902 of the Social Security Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1396a 42 U.S.C. 1396a] )is amended by striking subsection (gg).

=={{anchor|H4ADFEE778C5F4C49BEBA4A05210622AB}}(b) Repeal of PPACA CHIP MOE– ==

 Section 2105(d)(3) of the Social Security Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1397ee 42 U.S.C. 1397ee(d)(3)] )is amended—
:(1) by striking subparagraph (A);
:(2) by redesignating subparagraphs (B) and (C)as subparagraphs (A) and (B), respectively; and
:(3) in the paragraph heading, by striking“Continuation of eligibility standards for children until October 1, 2019”and inserting“Continuity of coverage”.

=={{anchor|H78AFAA72EA1A4DFD8C8DD4CF5FC830DB}}(c) Conforming amendments– ==


:(1)  Section 1902(a) of the Social Security Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1396a 42 U.S.C. 1396a(a)] )is amended by striking paragraph (74).
:(2) Effective January 1, 2014, the paragraph (14) of section 1902(e)added by[[Public Law 111-148|section 2002(a) of[[Public Law -|Public Law 111–148]] ]] is amended by striking the third sentence of subparagraph (A).

=={{anchor|HDA7DD2A1FE0749248073FBCCCC5301AC}}(d) Effective date– ==

Except as provided in subsection (c)(2), the amendments made by this section shall take effect on the date of the enactment of this Act.
