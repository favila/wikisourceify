{{Center|A BILL}}

 To expand project eligibility to certain public infrastructure projects under chapter 6 of title 23, United States Code.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Transit-Oriented Development Infrastructure Financing Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Infrastructure finance=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/601  Section 601(a)(12)of title 23, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/601 subparagraph (C)], by striking“and”at the end;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/601 subparagraph (D)], by striking the period at the end and inserting“; and”; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
::(E) a project to improve or construct public infrastructure that is located within walking distance of and accessible to a fixed guideway transit facility, passenger rail station, intercity bus station, or intermodal facility, including transportation, public utility, and capital projects described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/5302 section 5302(3)(G)(v) of title 49], and related infrastructure. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Eligible project costs– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/602  Section 602(a)(5)(B)of title 23, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by striking“(B)Intelligent transportation system projects.—In the case”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

====(B) Exceptions– ====



=====(i) Intelligent transportation systems– =====

In the case ; and 
</blockquote>

:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

====(ii) Transit-oriented development projects– ====

In the case of a project described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/601 section 601(a)(12)(E)], eligible project costs shall be reasonably anticipated to be equal to or exceed $10,000,000. . 
</blockquote>
