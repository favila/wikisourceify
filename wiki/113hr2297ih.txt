{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 40, United States Code, to authorize the National Capital Planning Commission to designate and modify the boundaries of the National Mall area in the District of Columbia reserved for the location of commemorative works of preeminent historical and lasting significance to the United States and other activities, to require the Secretary of the Interior and the Administrator of General Services to make recommendations for the termination of the authority of a person to establish a commemorative work in the District of Columbia and its environs, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “National Mall Revitalization and Designation Act” .

=Sec. 2. Establishment of commemorative works in the District of Columbia and its environs=



==(a) Modification of Area I and Area II boundaries– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8908 Section 8908of title 40, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8908 subsection (c)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8908 subsection (d)] ; and
:(2) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8908 subsection (b)] the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

====(c) Modification of Area I and Area II boundaries– ====



=====(1) Modification authority– =====

The [[w:National Capital Planning Commission|National Capital Planning Commission]] , in the sole exercise of its discretion, may modify the boundaries of Area I and Area II, as depicted on the map referred to in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8908 subsection (a)] . A decision of the [[w:National Capital Planning Commission|National Capital Planning Commission]] under this paragraph is not subject to review.

=====(2) Notification– =====

The [[w:National Capital Planning Commission|National Capital Planning Commission]] shall notify the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] and [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] , the [[w:|National Capital Memorial Advisory Commission]] , and the congressional committees specified in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8908 subsection (b)] of any boundary modification made under the authority of this subsection.

=====(3) Conforming changes to map– =====

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall revise the map referred to in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8908 subsection (a)] to reflect any boundary modification made under the authority of this subsection..
</blockquote>


==(b) Recommendations regarding termination of authority To establish commemorative work– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8903 Section 8903 of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

===(f) Recommendations regarding termination of legislative authority– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] or the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] may submit a recommendation to [[w:|Congress]] that the legislative authority for a commemorative work be terminated before the expiration date applicable to the work under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8903 subsection (e)] if the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] or the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] determines the person authorized to establish the work does not possess the capacity or resources to successfully complete the project before that expiration date..
</blockquote>


==(c) Recommendations for changes to commemorative works process– ==



===(1) Preparation of recommendations– ===

The [[w:National Capital Planning Commission|National Capital Planning Commission]] shall prepare recommendations to improve upon the process required bychapter 89of title 40, United States Code, for the selection, design, and subject matter of commemorative works in the District of Columbia and its environs.

===(2) Special considerations– ===

In the preparation of recommendations under paragraph (1), the [[w:National Capital Planning Commission|National Capital Planning Commission]] shall—
:(A) consult with the [[w:National Park Service|National Park Service]] and other appropriate Federal and local agencies, recognized national leaders in culture and development, and other interested persons; and
:(B) take into account the Memorials and Museums Master Plan prepared by National Capital Planning Commission in 2001 and the Legacy Plan prepared by National Capital Planning Commission in 1997.

===(3) Submission of recommendations– ===

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:National Capital Planning Commission|National Capital Planning Commission]] shall submit the recommendations prepared under paragraph (1)to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] , the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] , the [[w:|National Capital Memorial Advisory Commission]] , the [[w:United States House Committee on House Administration|Committee on House Administration]] , the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure]] , and the [[w:United States House Committee on Natural Resources|Committee on Natural Resources of the House of Representatives]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources|Committee on Energy and Natural Resources of the Senate]] .

==(d) Plan for National Mall visitor enhancement– ==

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall submit a plan to enhance visitor enjoyment, amenities, cultural experiences in, and the vitality of the National Mall, as defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8908 section 8908of title 40, United States Code] , to the [[w:United States House Committee on House Administration|Committee on House Administration]] , the [[w:United States House Committee on Natural Resources|Committee on Natural Resources]] , and the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources|Committee on Energy and Natural Resources of the Senate]] .
