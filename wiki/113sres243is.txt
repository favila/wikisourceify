{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

Authorizing expenditures by the Committee on Veterans' Affairs.

= =

That, in carrying out its powers, duties, and functions under the Standing Rules of the Senate, in accordance with its jurisdiction under Rule XXV of such rules, including holding hearings, reporting such hearings, and making investigations as authorized by paragraphs 1 and 8 of Rule XXVI of the Standing Rules of the Senate, the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs]] is authorized from October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2014, and October 1, 2014, through February 28, 2015, in its discretion (1) to make expenditures from the contingent fund of the [[w:|Senate]] , (2) to employ personnel, and (3) with the prior consent of the government department or agency concerned and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Rules and Administration|Committee on Rules and Administration]] , to use on a reimbursable or non-reimbursable basis the services of personnel of any such department or agency.

=2. =


:(a) The expenses of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|committee]] for the period October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2014, under this resolution shall not exceed $2,178,117, of which amount (1) not to exceed $50,000 may be expended for the procurement of the services of individual consultants, or organizations thereof (as authorized by section 202(i) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/72a 2 U.S.C. 72a(i)] )), and (2) not to exceed $9,500 may be expended for the training of the professional staff of such [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|committee]] (under procedures specified by section 202(j) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/72a 2 U.S.C. 72a(j)] )).
:(b) For the period October 1, 2014, through February 28, 2015, expenses of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|committee]] under this resolution shall not exceed $907,549, of which amount (1) not to exceed $21,000 may be expended for the procurement of the services of individual consultants, or organizations thereof (as authorized by section 202(i) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/72a 2 U.S.C. 72a(i)] )), and (2) not to exceed $3,500 may be expended for the training of the professional staff of such [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|committee]] (under procedures specified by section 202(j) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/72a 2 U.S.C. 72a(j)] )).

=3. =

The [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|committee]] shall report its findings, together with such recommendations for legislation as it deems advisable, to the [[w:|Senate]] at the earliest practicable date, but not later than February 28, 2015.

=4. =

Expenses of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|committee]] under this resolution shall be paid from the contingent fund of the [[w:|Senate]] upon vouchers approved by the chairman of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|committee]] , except that vouchers shall not be required (1) for the disbursement of salaries of employees paid at an annual rate, or (2) for the payment of telecommunications provided by the [[w:|Office of the Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, United States Senate]] , or (3) for the payment of stationery supplies purchased through the [[w:|Keeper of the Stationery, United States Senate]] , or (4) for payments to the [[w:|Postmaster, United States Senate]] , or (5) for the payment of metered charges on copying equipment provided by the [[w:|Office of the Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, United States Senate]] , or (6) for the payment of Senate Recording and Photographic Services, or (7) for payment of franked and mass mail costs by the [[w:|Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, United States Senate]] .

=5. =

There areauthorized such sums as may be necessaryfor agency contributions related to the compensation of employees of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|committee]] from October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2014, and October 1, 2014, through February 28, 2015, to be paid fromthe Appropriations account for “Expenses of Inquiries and Investigations” .
