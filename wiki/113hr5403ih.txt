{{Center|A BILL}}

 To nullify certain guidance of the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection and to provide requirements for guidance issued by the Bureau with respect to indirect auto lending.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Reforming CFPB Indirect Auto Financing Guidance Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Nullification of auto lending guidance=

Bulletin 2013–02 of the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection (published March 21, 2013) shall have no force or effect. 

=Sec. 3. Guidance requirements=

   Section 1022(b) of the Consumer Financial Protection Act of 2010 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/5512 12 U.S.C. 5512(b)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

==(5) Guidance on indirect auto financing– ==

In proposing and issuing guidance primarily related to indirect auto financing, the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]]shall—
:(A) provide for a public notice and comment period before issuing the guidance in final form;
:(B) make available to the public, including on the website of the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]], all studies, data, methodologies, analyses, and other information relied on by the Bureau in preparing such guidance;
:(C) redact any information that is exempt from disclosure under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 paragraph (3)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 (4)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 (6)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 (7)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 (8) ofsection 552(b)of title 5, United States Code];
:(D) consult with the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System]], the [[w:Federal Trade Commission|Federal Trade Commission]], and the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]; and
:(E) conduct a study on the costs and impacts of such guidance to consumers and women-owned, minority-owned, and small businesses. . 
</blockquote>
