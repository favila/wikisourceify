{{Center|A BILL}}

To rescind $45 billion of unobligated discretionary appropriations, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HE26092B74A95443DB01E083D15857499}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Decrease Spending Now Act”. 

={{anchor|H21E76C8AA8034A96A4B52749576C7C9A}}Sec. 2. Rescission of unobligated discretionary appropriations=



=={{anchor|H5FEE3B1385CB4B48ADDD264C8DDB7E69}}(a) In general– ==

Of the unobligated balances of discretionary appropriations on the date of enactment of this Act, $45,000,000,000 is rescinded.

=={{anchor|HFA1C320E9C9342F9AB715CE0265B9355}}(b) Implementation– ==



==={{anchor|HE8C91CC31B6043E3A6614F87A68C99AB}}(1) In general– ===

The [[Wikipedia:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall determine which appropriation accounts the rescission under subsection (a) shall apply to and the amount that each such account shall be reduced by pursuant to such rescission.

==={{anchor|H6CF31AFB700D4652B5CF64CDA0FE1B74}}(2) Report– ===

Not later than 60 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[Wikipedia:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall submit a report to the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] and [[Wikipedia:United States Congress|Congress]] listing the accounts reduced by the rescission in subsection (a) and the amounts rescinded from each such account. 

=={{anchor|H3622EB33D99449368FCBC23D6DE36DC6}}(c) Exceptions– ==

The rescission under subsection (a) shall not apply to the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]], the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]], or the [[Wikipedia:Social Security Administration|Social Security Administration]].
