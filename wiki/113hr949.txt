=={{anchor|H081A15EC35D3466797EA30D3D31C7397}}Sec. 101. Federal-aid highway Buy America provisions==



==={{anchor|H3D5BC52A2BFC48F480877100C9D53A5B}}(a) In general– ===

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 Section 313 of title 23, United States Code] , is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H8BD7C78F78834D738D4BD93CE4ACF43E}}Sec. 313. Buy America===



===={{anchor|HCCF520573D9C44EFA4996699D4AF49B3}}(a) Domestic source requirement for steel, iron, and manufactured goods– ====



====={{anchor|H8DBE0FC81B294CDD8306206ADE9F0379}}(1) In general– =====

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, funds made available to carry out this title may not be obligated for a project unless the steel, iron, and manufactured goods used for the project are produced in the United States.

====={{anchor|H24C3CB908CFF4057BDF99578EEDA4B92}}(2) Scope– =====

The requirements of this section apply to all contracts for a project carried out within the scope of the applicable finding, determination, or decision under the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] ), regardless of the funding source of such contracts, if at least one contract for the project is funded with amounts made available to carry out this title.

===={{anchor|H6911F1B42FF14CF4AF487042356FCEFC}}(b) Exceptions– ====



====={{anchor|H25AEFB4AC27C487492D8786F3DE6EDB0}}(1) Issuance of waivers– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] may waive the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 subsection (a)] only if the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] finds that—
:(A) applying[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 subsection (a)] would be inconsistent with the public interest, as determined in accordance with the regulations required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 paragraph (2)] ;
:(B) the steel, iron, or manufactured goods required for a project are not produced in the United States—
::(i) in sufficient and reasonably available quantities; or
::(ii) to a satisfactory quality; or
:(C) the use of steel, iron, and manufactured goods produced in the United States for a project will increase the total cost of the project by more than 25 percent.

====={{anchor|H8D801C8D8AFA4FB29F471FB0B1F1AF32}}(2) Regulations– =====

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of the Invest in American Jobs Act of 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall issue regulations establishing the criteria that the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall use to determine whether the application of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 subsection (a)] is inconsistent with the public interest for purposes of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 paragraph (1)(A)] .

====={{anchor|H94C340518500460F8869EA917D143B03}}(3) Labor costs– =====

For purposes of this subsection, labor costs involved in final assembly shall not be included in calculating the cost of components.

====={{anchor|H558E47D7AD6C47EF84E3D80E9E2D5771}}(4) Requests for waivers– =====

A recipient of assistance under this title seeking a waiver under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 paragraph (1)] shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] a request for the waiver in such form and containing such information as the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] may require.

===={{anchor|HD75BED75BE9E40A085F6857D585BDB15}}(c) Waiver requirements– ====



====={{anchor|H17C7413A363C48A882785BD4FC384C26}}(1) Public notification of and opportunity for comment on request for a waiver– =====



======{{anchor|HB4021D6365494957A6044A8238C32ADE}}(A) In general– ======

If the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] receives a request for a waiver under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 subsection (b)] , the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall provide notice of and an opportunity for public comment on the request at least 30 days before making a finding based on the request.

======{{anchor|H518312BABED14130AA9CEC4EF2F0F36A}}(B) Notice requirements– ======

A notice provided under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 subparagraph (A)] shall—
:(i) include the information available to the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] concerning the request, including whether the request is being made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 subsection (b)(1)(A)] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 (b)(1)(B)] , or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 (b)(1)(C)] ; and
:(ii) be provided by electronic means, including on the official public Internet Web site of the Department of Transportation.

====={{anchor|H2871D5EADB3F4F9CB5924E159C24ACEE}}(2) Detailed justification in Federal Register– =====

If the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] issues a waiver under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 subsection (b)] , the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall publish in the Federal Register a detailed justification for the waiver that—
:(A) addresses the public comments received under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 paragraph (1)(A)] ; and
:(B) is published before the waiver takes effect.

===={{anchor|HD24A8442402C4B21B6A46F1EF2A3D539}}(d) State requirements– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] may not impose a limitation or condition on assistance provided under this title that restricts—
:(1) a State from imposing requirements that are more stringent than those imposed under this section with respect to limiting the use of articles, materials, or supplies mined, produced, or manufactured in foreign countries for projects carried out with such assistance; or
:(2) any recipient of such assistance from complying with such State requirements.

===={{anchor|HA5D4A5163A0644D38541BD958DD72D89}}(e) Intentional violations– ====

Pursuant to procedures established under subpart 9.4 of chapter 1 of title 48, Code of Federal Regulations, a person shall be ineligible to receive a contract or subcontract funded with amounts made available to carry out this title if the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] , the head of any department, agency, or instrumentality of the United States, or a court determines that such person intentionally—
:(1) affixed a label bearing a“Made in America”inscription, or any inscription with the same meaning, to any steel, iron, or manufactured goods that—
::(A) were used in a project to which this section applies; and
::(B) were not produced in the United States; or
:(2) represented that any steel, iron, or manufactured goods were produced in the United States that—
::(A) were used in a project to which this section applies; and
::(B) were not produced in the United States.

===={{anchor|HBB38FBF788CA4925AE4BF2BAD47A0D52}}(f) Consistency with international agreements– ====



====={{anchor|HE5A06C5BFE994014998A74849CDA721A}}(1) In general– =====

This section shall be applied in a manner that is consistent with United States obligations under international agreements.

====={{anchor|H69C4B96749E14E208837E4F9DDB5D13C}}(2) Treatment of foreign countries in violation of international agreements– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall prohibit the use of steel, iron, and manufactured goods produced in a foreign country in a project funded with amounts made available to carry out this title, including any project for which the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] has issued a waiver under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/313 subsection (b)] , if the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] , in consultation with the [[w:Office of the United States Trade Representative|United States Trade Representative]] , determines that the foreign country is in violation of the terms of an agreement with the United States by discriminating against steel, iron, or manufactured goods that are produced in the United States and covered by the agreement..
</blockquote>


==={{anchor|HA9D90EE6D5A04DA294B5E0AABE454D81}}(b) Review of nationwide waivers– ===



===={{anchor|H468469B0FEDF45A19DAB727B9C120A1C}}(1) In general– ====

===={{anchor|HB9B7C10DEA5F428D8E7434481BAE6BB4}}(3) Notice requirement– ====

===={{anchor|H52073663F8044FA698CE0C57359D0BB1}}(4) Detailed justification in Federal Register– ====

If the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] finds it is necessary to continue a standing nationwide waiver after a review under paragraph (1), the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] shall publish in the Federal Register a detailed justification for such waiver that addresses the public comments received under paragraph (2).

===={{anchor|HA656AC9CA1F64F70825B4250C12BE4B7}}(j) Buy America– ====



====={{anchor|H3E1B5DFC0CE747658D614F088B2B6665}}(1) Domestic source requirement for steel, iron, and manufactured goods– =====



======{{anchor|H130C33BFC77342969C400301693B18A3}}(A) In general– ======

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, and except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/5323 subparagraph (B)] , funds made available to carry out this chapter may not be obligated for a project unless the steel, iron, and manufactured goods used for the project are produced in the United States.

======{{anchor|H961089C6BE6744C1A9EB780D23BA36BF}}(B) Special rules for rolling stock– ======

Funds made available to carry out this chapter may not be obligated for the procurement of rolling stock (including train control, communication, traction power equipment, and rolling stock prototypes) unless—
:(i) the cost of components and subcomponents produced in the United States—
::(I) for fiscal year 2013 is more than 60 percent of the cost of all components of the rolling stock;
::(II) for fiscal year 2014 is more than 70 percent of the cost of all components of the rolling stock;
::(III) for fiscal year 2015 is more than 80 percent of the cost of all components of the rolling stock;
::(IV) for fiscal year 2016 is more than 90 percent of the cost of all components of the rolling stock; and
::(V) for fiscal year 2017, and each fiscal year thereafter, is 100 percent of the cost of all components of the rolling stock; and
:(ii) final assembly of the rolling stock, including rolling stock prototypes, occurs in the United States.

The requirements of this subsection apply to all contracts for a public transportation project carried out within the scope of the applicable finding, determination, or decision under the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] ), regardless of the funding source of such contracts, if at least one contract for the public transportation project is funded with amounts made available to carry out this chapter.

====={{anchor|H1EFC489A9B4A4794B06327E38B394D68}}(1) Domestic source requirement for steel, iron, and manufactured goods– =====

:(D) provides the justification for each such waiver, including the specific law, treaty, or international agreement under which the waiver was granted;
:(E) summarizes the funds expended on—
::(i) steel, iron, and manufactured goods produced in the United States for projects with respect to which the Buy America requirement under this section applied during the preceding calendar year; and
::(ii) steel, iron, and manufactured goods produced outside the United States for projects with respect to which the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] issued a waiver under subsection (b)during the preceding calendar year; and
:(F) provides an employment impact analysis of the cumulative effect of all waivers under subsection (b)issued by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] during the preceding calendar year on manufacturing employment in the United States.

===={{anchor|HACE6C6760E9A49DC8906BAE18A8257B2}}(d) State requirements– ====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] may not impose a limitation or condition on assistance provided under a section specified in subsection (a)(1)that restricts—
:(1) a State from imposing requirements that are more stringent than those imposed under this section with respect to limiting the use of articles, materials, or supplies mined, produced, or manufactured in foreign countries for projects carried out with such assistance; or
:(2) any recipient of such assistance from complying with such State requirements.

===={{anchor|H7D0FDF055ECA4E2A8E67DF84959D3E3F}}(e) Intentional violations– ====

Pursuant to procedures established under subpart 9.4 of chapter 1 of title 48, Code of Federal Regulations, a person shall be ineligible to receive a contract or subcontract funded with amounts made available to carry out a section specified in subsection (a)(1)if the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] , the head of any department, agency, or instrumentality of the United States, or a court determines that such person intentionally—
:(1) affixed a label bearing a“Made in America”inscription, or any inscription with the same meaning, to any steel, iron, or manufactured goods that—
::(A) were used in a project to which this section applies; and
::(B) were not produced in the United States; or
:(2) represented that any steel, iron, or manufactured goods were produced in the United States that—
::(A) were used in a project to which this section applies; and
::(B) were not produced in the United States.

===={{anchor|HADB19BDF519143F79C63A25B658A7F0F}}(f) Consistency with international agreements– ====



====={{anchor|HC6E64E83361B4F88B101751E7CDBBD3C}}(1) In general– =====

====={{anchor|H30FD679A6A7F4B4B81CA62B9F4731DB1}}(2) Treatment of foreign countries in violation of international agreements– =====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall prohibit the use of steel, iron, and manufactured goods produced in a foreign country in a project funded with amounts made available to carry out a section specified in subsection (a)(1), including any project for which the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] has issued a waiver under subsection (b), if the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] , in consultation with the [[w:Office of the United States Trade Representative|United States Trade Representative]] , determines that the foreign country is in violation of the terms of an agreement with the United States by discriminating against steel, iron, or manufactured goods that are produced in the United States and covered by the agreement.

==={{anchor|H4E6A04B076D742BE9F5FAF07214DCB7B}}Sec. 14. Buy America===



===={{anchor|HD828EAD46E804C49B68AFD4F750DA942}}(a) Domestic source requirement for steel, iron, and manufactured goods– ====



====={{anchor|HEAA459E9197F4051B34BFFEC74DB00B7}}(1) In general– =====

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, funds made available to carry out this Act may not be used, in whole or in part, for a project for the alteration of a bridge unless the steel, iron, and manufactured goods used for the project are produced in the United States.

====={{anchor|HE1F176C96D744A57A74CA48172F4028A}}(2) Scope– =====

The requirements of this section apply to all contracts for a project carried out within the scope of the applicable finding, determination, or decision under the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] ), regardless of the funding source of such contracts, if at least one contract for the project is funded with amounts made available to carry out this Act.

===={{anchor|H90E7DD0F6D254CAFA56FA061C3EE8DA5}}(b) Exceptions– ====



====={{anchor|H2693570A5A9F419BAA1828D294E42237}}(1) Issuance of waivers– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] may waive the requirements of subsection (a)only if the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] finds that—
:(A) applying subsection (a)would be inconsistent with the public interest, as determined in accordance with the regulations required under paragraph (2);
:(B) the steel, iron, or manufactured goods required for a project are not produced in the United States—
::(i) in sufficient and reasonably available quantities; or
::(ii) to a satisfactory quality; or
:(C) the use of steel, iron, and manufactured goods produced in the United States for a project will increase the total cost of the project by more than 25 percent.

====={{anchor|HEA276501A77F4FA8B42AF2BAE241662C}}(2) Regulations– =====
