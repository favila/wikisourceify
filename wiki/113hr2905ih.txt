{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Energy Employees Occupational Illness Compensation Program Act of 2000 to strengthen the quality control measures in place for part B lung disease claims and to establish the Advisory Board on Toxic Substances and Worker Health for the contractor employee compensation program under subtitle E of such Act.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Nuclear Workers Health Advisory Board Act” .

=Sec. 2. Establishment of the Advisory Board on Toxic Substances and Worker Health=



==(a) Advisory Board on Toxic Substances and Worker Health– ==

 Subtitle B of the Energy Employees Occupational Illness Compensation Program Act of 2000( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7384l 42 U.S.C. 7384l et seq.] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 3632. Advisory Board on Toxic Substances and Worker Health==



===(a) Establishment– ===



====(1) In general– ====

Not later than 120 days after the date of the enactment of this section, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall establish and appoint an [[w:|Advisory Board on Toxic Substances and Worker Health]] (referred to in this section as the Board ).

====(2) Consultation on appointments– ====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall make appointments to the [[w:|Board]] in consultation with organizations with expertise on worker health issues in order to ensure that the membership of the [[w:|Board]] reflects a proper balance of perspectives from the scientific, medical, legal, worker, worker families, and worker advocate communities.

====(3) Chairperson– ====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall designate a [[w:|Chair of the Board]] from among its members.

===(b) Duties– ===

The [[w:|Board]] shall—
:(1) advise the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] concerning the review and approval of the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department of Labor]] site exposure matrix;
:(2) conduct periodic peer reviews of, and approve, medical guidance for part E claims examiners with respect to the weighing of a claimant’s medical evidence;
:(3) obtain periodic expert review of evidentiary requirements for part B claims related to lung disease regardless of approval;
:(4) provide oversight over industrial hygienists, [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department of Labor]] staff physicians, and Department of Labor’s consulting physicians and their reports to ensure quality, objectivity, and consistency; and
:(5) coordinate exchanges of data and findings with the Advisory Board on Radiation and Worker Health to the extent necessary (under section 3624).

===(c) Staff and powers– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall appoint a staff to facilitate the work of the [[w:|Board]] . The staff of the Board shall be headed by a Director who shall be appointed undersubchapter VIII ofchapter 33of title 5, United States Code.

====(2) Federal agency personnel– ====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] may authorize the detail of employees of Federal agencies to the [[w:|Board]] as necessary to enable the [[w:|Board]] to carry out its duties under this section. The detail of such personnel may be on a non-reimbursable basis.

====(3) Powers– ====

The [[w:|Board]] shall have same powers that the Advisory Board has under section 3624.

====(4) Contractors– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]] shall employ outside contractors and specialists selected by the [[w:|Board]] to support the work of the [[w:|Board]] .

===(d) Expenses– ===

Members of the [[w:|Board]] , other than full-time employees of the United States, while attending meetings of the [[w:|Board]] or while otherwise serving at the request of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] , and while serving away from their homes or regular place of business, shall be allowed travel and meal expenses, including per diem in lieu of subsistence (as authorized by [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5703 section 5703of title 5, United States Code] ) for individuals in the Federal Government serving without pay.

===(e) Security clearances– ===



====(1) Application– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]] shall ensure that the members and staff of the [[w:|Board]] , and the contractors performing work in support of the [[w:|Board]] , are afforded the opportunity to apply for a security clearance for any matter for which such a clearance is appropriate.

====(2) Determination– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]] should, not later than 180 days after receiving a completed application for a security clearance under this subsection, make a determination whether or not the individual concerned is eligible for the clearance.

====(3) Report– ====

For fiscal year 2015, and each fiscal year thereafter, the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]] shall include in the budget justification materials submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] in support of the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Department of Energy]] budget for that fiscal year (as submitted with the budget of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105(a)of title 31, United States Code] ) a report specifying the number of applications for security clearances under this subsection, the number of such applications granted, and the number of such applications denied.

===(f) Information– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]] shall, in accordance with law, provide to the [[w:|Board]] and the contractors of the [[w:|Board]] , access to any information that the [[w:|Board]] considers relevant to carry out its responsibilities under this section, including information such as restricted data (as defined in section 11(y) of the Atomic Energy Act of 1954( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/2014 42 U.S.C. 2014(y)] )) and information covered by the Privacy Act..
</blockquote>


==(b) Department of Labor response to the Office of the Ombudsman annual report– ==

 Section 3686 of the Energy Employees Occupational Illness Compensation Program Act of 2000( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7385s–15 42 U.S.C. 7385s–15] )is amended—
:(1) in subsection (e)(1), by striking “February 15” and inserting “July 30” ; and
:(2) by striking subsection (h)and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

====(h) Response to report– ====

Not later than 180 days after the publication of the annual report under subsection (e), the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department of Labor]] shall submit an answer in writing on whether the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department]] agrees or disagrees with the specific issues raised by the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Ombudsman]] , if the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department]] agrees, on the actions to be taken to correct the problems identified by the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Ombudsman]] , and if the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department]] does not agree, on the reasons therefore. The [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department of Labor]] shall post such answer on the public Internet website of the Department..
</blockquote>
