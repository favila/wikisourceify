{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Controlled Substances Act to exclude
		  industrial hemp from the definition of marihuana, and for other
		  purposes.


{{section|H2E4166A501D94F819B9C0DE463E8A4AA|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Industrial Hemp Farming Act of
			 2013

        
.

{{section|H64055DEA22574CB59EFD032473AFD0A8|2. Exclusion of industrial hemp from definition of marihuana}}

        Section 102 of the Controlled Substances Act

			 (21 U.S.C. 802
)
 is amended—
(1)
in paragraph
			 (16)
—
(A)
by striking
			 
              (16)
 The
 and inserting 
              (16)(A)
 The
; and

(B)
by adding at the
			 end the following:
(B)
The term marihuana
 does
				not include industrial hemp.

;
				and



(2)
by adding at the
			 end the following:
(57)
The term
				industrial hemp
 means the plant Cannabis sativa L. and any part of
				such plant, whether growing or not, with a delta-9 tet­ra­hy­dro­can­nab­i­nol
				concentration of not more than 0.3 percent on a dry weight
				basis.

.



{{section|HB9B8EFCEA5B4456B8125F858CF983107|3. Industrial hemp determination}}

        Section 201 of
			 the Controlled Substances Act
 (21 U.S.C. 811
)
 is amended by adding at the end
			 the following:
(i)
Industrial hemp
				determination
If a person grows or processes Cannabis sativa L.
				for purposes of making industrial hemp in accordance with State law, the
				Cannabis sativa L. shall be deemed to meet the concentration limitation under
				section
				102(57)
.

.
