{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 18, United States Code, to provide a penalty for knowingly selling advertising that offers certain commercial sex acts.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Stop Advertising Victims of Exploitation Act of 2014   ”or the“ SAVE Act of 2014 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Advertising that offers certain commercial sex acts=



<blockquote> 

=Sec. 1591A. Advertising that offers certain commercial sex acts=



==(a) Offense– ==

Whoever, in or affecting interstate or foreign commerce, knowingly benefits financially from, receives anything of value from, or distributes advertising that offers a commercial sex act in a manner prohibited under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1591 section 1591], shall be fined under this title, imprisoned not more than 5 years, or both.

==(b) Definitions– ==

In this section, the terms have the meanings given those terms in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1591 section 1591]. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Extra-territorial jurisdiction=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1596  Section 1596of title 18, United States Code], is amended by striking“or 1591”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1591 1591], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1591A 1591A] ”. 

=Sec. 4. Clerical amendment=

The table of sections at the beginning of chapter 77of title 18, United States Code, is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1591 section 1591]the following:

<blockquote>  1591A. Advertising that offers certain commercial sex acts.  . 
</blockquote>
