{{Center|A BILL}}

To require the Secretary of Health and Human Services to remove social security account numbers from Medicare identification cards and communications provided to Medicare beneficiaries in order to protect Medicare beneficiaries from identity theft.

={{anchor|ID575BBCDE0B2F4C5E80C4225FA6B4CB9B}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Social Security Number Protection Act of 2011”.

={{anchor|ID35325DB541444DC29008047FAEBB0D96}}Sec. 2. Requiring the Secretary of Health and Human Services to prohibit the display of social security account numbers on Medicare identification cards and communications provided to Medicare beneficiaries=



=={{anchor|IDa385fc72d009413f8355c2695e9b0010}}(a) In general– ==

Not later than 3 years after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] shall establish and begin to implement procedures to eliminate the unnecessary collection, use, and display of social security account numbers of Medicare beneficiaries.

=={{anchor|ID5a799ac718fa48828956606ac0d71ea8}}(b) Medicare cards and communications provided to beneficiaries– ==



==={{anchor|idFEF925ABFB9A49138A85A3A030490556}}(1) Cards– ===



===={{anchor|id3587B9731ECC422E98F8049390AE7336}}(A) New cards– ====

Not later than 3 years after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] shall ensure that each newly issued Medicare identification card meets the requirements described in subparagraph (C).

===={{anchor|id29685B84A35448919F51768EF3B4B39C}}(B) Replacement of existing cards– ====

Not later than 5 years after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] shall ensure that all Medicare beneficiaries have been issued a Medicare identification card that meets the requirements of subparagraph (C).

===={{anchor|id809B7F21CCEC4D3E91CFE26070768ACA}}(C) Requirements– ====

The requirements described in this subparagraph are, with respect to a Medicare identification card, that the card does not display or electronically store (in an unencrypted format) a Medicare beneficiary’s social security account number.

==={{anchor|id1B445E99659B48B594A912DB839B525F}}(2) Communications provided to beneficiaries– ===

Not later than 3 years after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] shall prohibit the display of a Medicare beneficiary’s social security account number on written or electronic communication provided to the beneficiary unless the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] determines that inclusion of social security account numbers on such communications is essential for the operation of the Medicare program.

=={{anchor|idD36C7784DE97480DA555426A40AB632B}}(c) Medicare beneficiary defined– ==

In this section, the term“Medicare beneficiary”means an individual who is entitled to, or enrolled for, benefits under part A of title XVIII of the Social Security Actor enrolled under part B of such title.

=={{anchor|H681EEE47D16846BE8225AF41DBF3203}}(d) Conforming reference in the Social Security Act– ==

 Section 205(c)(2)(C) of the Social Security Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/405 42 U.S.C. 405(c)(2)(C)] ] )is amended by adding at the end the following new clause:

<blockquote>
:(xii) For provisions relating to requiring the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] to prohibit the display of social security account numbers on Medicare identification cards and communications provided to Medicare beneficiaries, see section 2 of theSocial Security Number Protection Act of 2011..
</blockquote>
