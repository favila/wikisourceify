{{Center|May 7, 2014}}

  
{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

      That because Lois G. Lerner, former Director, Exempt Organizations, [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]], offered a voluntary statement in testimony before the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee]], was found by the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee]]to have waived her Fifth Amendment Privilege, was informed of the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee]]’s decision of waiver, and continued to refuse to testify before the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee]], Ms. Lerner shall be found to be in contempt of Congress for failure to comply with a congressional subpoena.   That pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/192 2 U.S.C. §§ 192]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/194 194], the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives]]shall certify the report of the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform]], detailing the refusal of Ms. Lerner to testify before the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform]]as directed by subpoena, to the United States Attorney for the District of Columbia, to the end that Ms. Lerner be proceeded against in the manner and form provided by law.   That the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House]]shall otherwise take all appropriate action to enforce the subpoena.     Karen L. Haas, Clerk.
