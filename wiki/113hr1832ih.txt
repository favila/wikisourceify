{{Center|A BILL}}

To amendchapter 21of title 5, United States Code, to provide that fathers of certain permanently disabled or deceased veterans shall be included with mothers of such veterans as preference eligibles for treatment in the civil service.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Gold Star Fathers Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Preference eligible treatment for fathers of certain permanently disabled or deceased veterans=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2108 Section 2108(3) of title 5, United States Code] , is amended by striking [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2108 subparagraphs (F)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2108 (G)] and inserting the following:

<blockquote>
:(F) the parent of an individual who lost his or her life under honorable conditions while serving in the armed forces during a period named by [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2108 paragraph (1)(A) of this section] , if—
::(i) the spouse of that parent is totally and permanently disabled; or
::(ii) that parent, when preference is claimed, is unmarried or, if married, legally separated from his or her spouse;
:(G) the parent of a service-connected permanently and totally disabled veteran, if—
::(i) the spouse of that parent is totally and permanently disabled; or
::(ii) that parent, when preference is claimed, is unmarried or, if married, legally separated from his or her spouse; and.
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Effective date=

The amendment made by this Act shall take effect 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act.
