{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Clean Air Act to eliminate certain requirements under the renewable fuel program, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H01C18657FE85486B84095592C7EF4641}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Renewable Fuel Standard Amendments Act”.

={{anchor|H35600216084946728EE9FD810D03F6E8}}Sec. 2. Amendments to the Clean Air Act=



=={{anchor|H5A7D89707BB145638A4E0270F26D1370}}(a) Revised definition of renewable fuel– ==

 Section 211(o)(1)(J) of the Clean Air Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7545 42 U.S.C. 7545(o)(1)(J)] ] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H123085A52CCF4ABEBF54A6F4D95BFD5A}}(J) Renewable fuel– ===

The term renewable fuel means fuel that—
:(i) is produced from renewable biomass;
:(ii) is used to replace or reduce the quantity of fossil fuel present in a transportation fuel; and
:(iii) beginning on January 1, 2014, is advanced biofuel..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|H318A004321274620B1DCAD3FF9F65CB2}}Sec. 3. Applicability and regulations=

The amendments made by this Act to section 211(o) of the Clean Air Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7545 42 U.S.C. 7545(o)] ] )shall apply only with respect to calendar years 2014 and after, except that the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]] shall promulgate regulations to carry out such amendments not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, and take any steps necessary to ensure such amendments may be carried out for calendar years 2014 and after.
