{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Migratory Bird Treaty Act to delegate to States the authorities of the Secretary of the Interior under that Act with respect to cormorants, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H6C4361ED55414952898A67A32B36F7D4}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Cormorant Management and Natural Resources Protection Act”.

={{anchor|HBD91233DD02F4696931C7AFAD5F5C6D8}}Sec. 2. Findings=

The [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] finds the following:
:(1) The current permitting system is not sufficient to achieve a streamlined control of excessive cormorant populations.
:(2) Excessive cormorant populations cause damage to ecosystems.
:(3) Excessive cormorant populations pose public health and safety concerns.
:(4) Excessive cormorant populations pose an unsightly, loud, and olfactory nuisance.
:(5) Excessive cormorant populations can have a detrimental effect on fish populations.
:(6) Excessive cormorant populations displace native species from their habitats.
:(7) Cormorant excrement in colonies often kills vegetation.

={{anchor|H4CEB5FC534CF4230BCBDB57B91447495}}Sec. 3. Delegation to States of authority under Migratory Bird Treaty Act with respect to cormorants=



=={{anchor|H996F074A3C464703B12CD7ABEB9DD098}}(a) Delegation of authority– ==

 Section 7 of the Migratory Bird Treaty Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 16 U.S.C. 708] )is amended—
:(1) by inserting“(a)Preservation of State authority.—”before the first sentence; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H85CB0796E3AF445B95D277FEFAB11D34}}(b) Delegation to States of authority with respect to cormorants– ====



====={{anchor|H056461728DC34F72A836E3F1ACC81963}}(1) In general– =====

The authority of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] under this Act with respect to cormorants in a State is hereby delegated to the governor of the State effective on the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] approves a management plan for cormorants in the State that is submitted by the governor.

====={{anchor|H187C001325A943E9828D5839EFF1B48E}}(2) Approval or disapproval of management plan– =====



======{{anchor|HCE7953195EA24AAE8DFF42A367813453}}(A) In general– ======

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall approve or disapprove a management plan submitted under this subsection by not later than the end of the 60-day period beginning on the date the plan is submitted.

======{{anchor|H19AB0E6366D14684A2A7329BF4ED9F77}}(B) Requirement to approve– ======

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall approve a management plan submitted under this subsection if the plan is in accordance with United States obligations under treaties and Federal law.

======{{anchor|HA937FF926B69482F8770FC372CECF0F2}}(C) Disapproval of plan– ======

If the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] disapproves a management plan under this subsection the Secretary shall provide to the governor who submitted the plan the reasons for the disapproval and an opportunity to revise and resubmit the plan.

======{{anchor|H132696CCC05E4899B8D0CAD4B8615687}}(D) Plan deemed approved– ======

Except as provided in subparagraph (E), if the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] does not approve or disapprove a management plan before the end of the period referred to in paragraph (1)the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] is deemed to have approved the plan.

======{{anchor|HD9E38307C3F149C6BA67D6CFA92667BB}}(E) Limitation on approval– ======

A management plan shall not be approved under this paragraph if the plan is found to be in violation of United States obligations under treaties and Federal law.

======{{anchor|H28EC969F25CD45E2886331329F0C11D1}}(F) Review of approved plans– ======

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] —
:(i) shall review every 5 years each management plan approved for a State under this subsection and the State governor’s exercise of authority delegated under this subsection; and
:(ii) may revoke such approval and delegation if, based on such review, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] determines that the plan or the governor’s exercise of authority delegated under this subsection is not in accordance with this Act or any treaty implemented by this Act.

====={{anchor|H0E1E73EE4D164521B79E46C9039F471E}}(3) Relationship between approved plan and regulations– =====

A management plan that is approved for a State under this subsection shall apply in that State with respect to management of cormorants, in lieu of regulations issued under this Act.

====={{anchor|HBA474F76561A46BC9C5CD6DD83FCB799}}(4) Compliance with treaties and Federal law– =====

In exercising authority delegated under this subsection the governor of a State shall comply with this Act and all treaties implemented by this Act.

====={{anchor|H40B34E886044441AA71F33F907DAC1B0}}(5) Relationship to other authority– =====

Nothing in this subsection limits the authority of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] or any Federal agency to exercise authority under any Federal law to assist a State, upon request by the governor of the State, with control of cormorants.

====={{anchor|HC733C2E1B2BA418585D9186ACFAD061A}}(6) Cormorant defined– =====

In this subsection the term cormorant means the double-crested cormorant (Phalacrocorax auritus)..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H590C5DC6ACA940EA9443AB08ADC62A1D}}(b) Cooperation To prevent cormorant proliferation– ==



==={{anchor|H48D5C0963EB145FCAACDA90350201ADB}}(1) Department of Interior cooperation– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] , acting in consultation with the [[w:National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration|National Aquaculture Information Center]] and the [[w:|Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service]] , shall conduct educational and informational activities for the owners and operators of aquaculture facilities to improve their efforts to prevent cormorants from consuming aquatic species being reared in aquaculture facilities, which contributes to the proliferation of cormorants.

==={{anchor|HB36C68F0624A4C09BF5D333C22AE2897}}(2) Other efforts– ===

Nothing in this subsection restricts the authority of other Federal or State wildlife or natural resource management agencies to cooperate with the owners and operators of aquaculture facilities regarding the management and control of cormorants to prevent their proliferation.

==={{anchor|H52A8C24C67CB418C9B74A73C1BD08062}}(3) Cormorant defined– ===

In this subsection, the term cormorant means the double-crested cormorant (Phalacrocorax auritus).
