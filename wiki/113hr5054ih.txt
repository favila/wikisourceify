{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to establish within the Department of Veterans Affairs an Office of Whistleblower and Patient Protection.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Veterans’ Whistleblower and Patient Protection Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Establishment of Office of Whistleblower and Patient Protection=



==(a) In general– ==

  Chapter 3of title 38, United States Code, is amended by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319 section 319] the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 319A. Office of Whistleblower and Patient Protection==



===(a) Establishment– ===


:(1) There is in the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]an [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office of Whistleblower and Patient Protection]](in this section referred to as the“Office”). There is at the head of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]]a [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]appointed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]solely on the basis of integrity and demonstrated ability in accounting, auditing, financial analysis, law, management analysis, public administration, or investigations.
:(2) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]shall be a career appointee in the Senior Executive Service.
:(3) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]reports directly to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]concerning matters within the responsibility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]].

===(b) Complaints– ===


:(1) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]shall establish a dedicated Internet website and toll-free telephone number for an individual, regardless of whether the individual is an employee of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], to file a covered complaint directly to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]].
:(2) 
::(A) In addition to covered complaints filed directly with the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]]pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A paragraph (1)], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall refer to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]any covered complaint that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]receives directly, including pursuant to the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Patient Advocacy Program of the Veterans Health Administration]]or other similar program, or that is transmitted to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1213  section 1213of title 5]. In accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A subsection (c)], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]shall investigate each such complaint.
::(B) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may not refer a covered complaint that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]receives as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A subparagraph (A)]to any element of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], including the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office of Medical Inspection]], other than the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]].
::(C) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall ensure that employees of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]who are located at a medical facility are able to efficiently refer to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]any covered complaints received at such medical facility.
:(3) The identity of any individual who files a covered complaint may not be disclosed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]without the consent of such individual unless the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]determines that the disclosure of the identity of the individual is necessary because of an imminent danger to public health or safety or imminent violation of any criminal law.

===(c) Investigation and determination– ===


:(1) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]shall investigate each covered complaint that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]]receives directly pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/319A paragraph (1) of subsection (b)]or that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]refers to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A paragraph (2)(A)]of such subsection to determine whether there is a substantial likelihood that the covered complaint discloses a violation of any law, rule, or regulation, or gross mismanagement, gross waste of funds, abuse of authority, or substantial and specific danger to public health and safety. The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]shall make such determination not later than 240 days after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]receives the covered complaint. The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]is the only official of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]who may conduct an investigation that is required to be conducted by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1213 section 1213 of title 5].
:(2) If the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]makes a positive determination under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/319A paragraph (1)]regarding a covered complaint, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]shall—
::(A) notify the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]of such determination, including the basis for such determination and recommendations for actions to address such covered complaint; and
::(B) as appropriate, refer the covered complaint to the head of the appropriate department or agency of the Federal Government, including the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]], [[w:Office of Special Counsel|Special Counsel]], or the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs]].
:(3) 
::(A) If the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]does not make a positive determination under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/319A paragraph (1)]regarding a covered complaint, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]—
:::(i) upon the consent of the individual who filed the complaint, may transmit the complaint to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]; and
:::(ii) if the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]does not transmit the complaint under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A clause (i)], shall inform the individual of—
::::(I) the reasons for such determination; and
::::(II) other offices of the Federal Government available for receiving such complaints if the individual wishes to pursue the matter further.
::(B) Within a reasonable time after a covered complaint is transmitted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A subparagraph (A)(i)], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall inform the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]in writing of what action has been or is being taken and when such action will be completed. The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]shall inform the individual who filed the complaint of the report of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]].

===(d) Staff and resources– ===


:(1) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]]shall employ a sufficient number of attorneys, investigators, and other personnel as are necessary to carry out the functions of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]], including personnel with expertise in health care matters. Attorneys shall be compensated at a level commensurate with attorneys employed by the [[w:General Counsel|Office of the General Counsel]].
:(2) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall ensure that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]is furnished sufficient resources in addition to personnel under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A paragraph (1)]to enable the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]to carry out the functions of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]]in a timely manner.

===(e) Coordination– ===

In carrying out the duties of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]shall coordinate with the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department]]and the [[w:Office of Special Counsel|Special Counsel]]to ensure that the actions of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]are not duplicative with the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General]]or the [[w:Office of Special Counsel|Special Counsel]].

===(f) Reports– ===


:(1) During each 90-day period, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]a report that includes the following:
::(A) The findings and recommendations made by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]]to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]during the previous 90-day period.
::(B) With respect to such recommendations, whether the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]has made any actions based on such findings during such period.
::(C) During such period—
:::(i) the number of covered complaints received by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Director]];
:::(ii) the number of investigations commenced;
:::(iii) the number of positive determinations made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A paragraph (2) of subsection (c)];
:::(iv) the number of covered complaints for which a positive determination was not made pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A paragraph (3)(A)]of such subsection; and
:::(v) the number of covered complaints transmitted to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]under such paragraph.
:(2) During each 180-day period, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Senate]]a report that includes each report described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319A paragraph (1)]submitted during the previous 180-day period and any legislative recommendations of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]to address problems or concerns regarding the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Office]].

===(g) Covered complaint defined– ===

In this section, the term covered complaint means a complaint regarding—
:(1) an alleged prohibited personnel practice committed by an officer or employee of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]and described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2302 section 2302(b)(8)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2302 2302(b)(9)(A)(i)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2302 (B)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2302 (C)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2302 (D) of title 5]; or
:(2) the safety of a patient at a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections at the beginning of such chapter is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/319 section 319]the following new item:

<blockquote>  319A. Office of Whistleblower and Patient Protection.  . 
</blockquote>
