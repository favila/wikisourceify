{{Center|A BILL}}

To further enhance the promotion of exports of United States goods and services, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H6BAC091AE3CD4676B9F01542D4BCD7F0}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“State Trade Coordination Act”.

={{anchor|H3644D8BA21144EF6A504D48AD6AFBFC8}}Sec. 2. Membership of representatives of State trade promotion agencies on Trade Promotion Coordinating Committee=

 Section 2312(d) of the Export Enhancement Act of 1988([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/4727 15 U.S.C. 4727(d)] ] )is amended—
:(1) by redesignating paragraph (2)as paragraph (3); and
:(2) by inserting after paragraph (1)the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H1B9B89E23B7D452FA287A8F933F79FB6}}(2) Representatives from State trade promotion agencies– ===

The TPCC shall also include 1 or more members appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] who are representatives of State trade promotion agencies..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H6D55472DAAC547C5BFE1A89E3342E848}}Sec. 3. Federal and State export promotion coordination plan=



=={{anchor|H96EFAB565E2D4D76BB8E6A52B494F4E1}}(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] , acting through the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Trade Promotion Coordinating Committee]] and in coordination with representatives of State trade promotion agencies, shall develop a comprehensive plan to integrate the resources and strategies of State trade promotion agencies into the overall Federal trade promotion program.

=={{anchor|H7BFB2787A1EE478DAA8A8750D0247D32}}(b) Matters To be included– ==

The plan required under subsection (a)shall include the following:
:(1) A description of the role of State trade promotion agencies in assisting exporters.
:(2) An outline of the role of State trade promotion agencies and how it is different from Federal agencies located within or providing services within the State.
:(3) A plan on how to utilize State trade promotion agencies into the Federal trade promotion program.
:(4) An explanation of how Federal and State agencies will share information and resources.
:(5) A description of how Federal and State agencies will coordinate education and trade events in the United States and abroad.
:(6) A description of the efforts to increase efficiency and reduce duplication.
:(7) A clear identification of where businesses can receive appropriate international trade information under the plan.

=={{anchor|H8B887A4E773643838AED7543AB014621}}(c) Deadline– ==

The plan required under subsection (a)shall be finalized and submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] not later than 12 months after the date of the enactment of this Act.

={{anchor|H99BBFD5FEDBC47039052DB80238B919B}}Sec. 4. Annual Federal-State export strategy=



=={{anchor|H221B5F3C70B14D308DA0A8910DF0DC83}}(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] , acting through the head of the [[w:International Trade Administration|United States Commercial Service]] , shall develop an annual Federal-State export strategy for each State that submits to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] its export strategy for the upcoming calendar year. In developing an annual Federal-State export strategy under this subsection, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] shall take into account the Federal and State export promotion coordination plan developed under section 3.

=={{anchor|HFCF8781C77A141529D4B3B1597735138}}(b) Matters To be included– ==

The Federal-State export strategy required under subsection (a)shall include the following:
:(1) The State’s export strategy and economic goals.
:(2) The State’s key sectors and industries of focus.
:(3) Possible foreign and domestic trade events.
:(4) Efforts to increase efficiencies and reduce duplication.

=={{anchor|HD8D823A6B3AA4809BD8BB864BAAD7F69}}(c) Report– ==

The Federal-State export strategy required under subsection (a)shall be submitted to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Trade Promotion Coordinating Committee]] not later than February 1 of each year.

={{anchor|H57B8F63D593243C3A2816163BA43DEF0}}Sec. 5. Coordinated metrics and information sharing=



=={{anchor|H4B2D82ADCABE45D4BDB7118F8E210AB0}}(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] , in coordination with representatives of State trade promotion agencies, shall develop a framework to share export success information, and develop a coordinated set of reporting metrics.

=={{anchor|H788EC3B0DAF04D8F9FE889CA4CE61C58}}(b) Report to Congress– ==

Not later than 1 year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report that contains the framework and reporting metrics required under subsection (a).

 Section 2312 of the Export Enhancement Act of 1988([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 4727] )is amended—

::(C) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:::(7) in coordination with State trade promotion agencies, include a survey and analysis regarding the overall effectiveness of Federal-State coordination and export promotion goals on an annual basis, to further include best practices, recommendations to better assist small businesses, and other relevant matters.; and
</blockquote>
