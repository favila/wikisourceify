{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to ensure that veterans who experience extended waiting times for appointments at medical facilities of the Department of Veterans Affairs may receive care at non-Department facilities.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Veterans Need Timely Access to Care Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Expansion of fee-basis care by Department of Veterans Affairs for patients who experience extended waiting times=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 Section 1703 of title 38, United States Code,]is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsections (b) through (d)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsections (c) through (e)], respectively;
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsection (a)]the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsection (b)]:

<blockquote> 
::(b) As authorized by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1710 section 1710]of this title, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall enter into contracts with such non-Department facilities as may be necessary in order to furnish hospital care or medical services to any veteran enrolled in the patient enrollment system under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705 section 1705]of this title who—
:::(1) has waited—
::::(A) seven days or longer for an appointment for primary care in a Department facility; or
::::(B) 14 days or longer for an appointment for speciality care in a Department facility; and
:::(2) elects to receive such care in a non-Department facility. ; and 
</blockquote>

:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsection (c)], as redesignated by paragraph (1) , by inserting“or (b)”after“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 subsection (a)] ”.
