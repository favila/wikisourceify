{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 23, United States Code, to establish requirements relating to marijuana impaired driving, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Lucid Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Safety incentives to prevent operation of motor vehicles by intoxicated persons=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/163  Section 163of title 23, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

===(g) Marijuana impaired driving– ===

In addition to the other requirements of this section, in the case of a State in which the use of marijuana is legal under the laws of the State with or without medical justification, the State shall be eligible for a grant under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/163 subsection (b)], and shall be exempt from withholding under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/163 subsection (e)], for a fiscal year only if the State—
:(1) has in effect a law that prohibits an individual from driving or being in actual physical control of a motor vehicle while impaired by marijuana as determined using measures established by the State; and
:(2) enforces that law using training and methods for determining cognitive or physical marijuana impairment. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Applicability– ==

The amendment made by subsection (a) shall apply to fiscal years beginning after the date of enactment of this Act. 

=Sec. 3. Minimum penalties for repeat offenders for driving while intoxicated or driving under the influence=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/164  Section 164(a)(2)of title 23, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

===(2) Driving while intoxicated; driving under the influence– ===

The terms driving while intoxicated and driving under the influence mean—
:(A) driving or being in actual physical control of a motor vehicle while having an alcohol concentration above the permitted limit as established by each State; and
:(B) in the case of a State in which the use of marijuana is legal under the laws of the State with or without medical justification, driving or being in actual physical control of a motor vehicle while impaired by marijuana as determined using measures established by the State. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Applicability– ==

The amendments made by subsection (a) shall apply to fiscal years beginning after the date of enactment of this Act.
