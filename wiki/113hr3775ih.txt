{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend titles 10 and 38, United States Code, to improve the treatment of members of the Armed Forces and veterans who are victims of military sexual assault.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Military Sexual Assault Victims Empowerment Act” or the “Military SAVE Act” .

=Sec. 2. Improvement of treatment for military sexual assault=



==(a) Veterans– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Chapter 17of title 38, United States Code, is amended by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D section 1720D] the following new section:

<blockquote>

===Sec. 1720D–1. Counseling and treatment for sexual trauma at non-Department facilities===



====(a) Establishment– ====

Notwithstanding [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 section 1703 of this title] , and in addition to the program under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D section 1720D] , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall operate a program under which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] ensures that veterans who are victims of military sexual trauma may receive treatment for such trauma from private providers.

====(b) Election– ====

A veteran may elect to receive treatment under this section by notifying the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] of such election at a facility or regional office of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] . Such notification shall include a written affidavit by the veteran, made under penalty of perjury under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1746 section 1746of title 28] , stating that the veteran is a victim of military sexual trauma.

====(c) Voucher– ====


:(1) Upon receiving a notification under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D–1 subsection (b)] , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall issue the veteran a voucher described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D–1 paragraph (3)] .
:(2) A veteran to whom a voucher is issued under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D–1 paragraph (1)] may use the voucher to receive treatment by a private provider who agrees to accept such voucher as payment for such treatment. Any amount charged by the private provider for such treatment that is in addition to the amount paid by the voucher shall be the sole responsibility of the veteran.
:(3) A voucher described in this paragraph is a voucher indicating that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] will reimburse a private provider for treatment provided by the provider to a veteran under this section.
:(4) The amount for which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] will reimburse a private provider pursuant to a voucher described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D–1 paragraph (3)] is equal to the amount that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] pays to a non-Department facility under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 section 1703 of this title] for the same treatment for which the voucher is used.
:(5) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall require that a veteran make a reelection under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D–1 subsection (b)] during each 120-day period in which the voucher is used following the initial 120-day period in which the voucher is valid.

====(d) Consultation– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall consult with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] to ensure that this section is carried out in a manner that allows veterans to receive similar treatment as is provided to members of the Armed Forces under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074n section 1074nof title 10] .

====(e) Definitions– ====

In this section:
:(1) The term military sexual trauma means an injury, illness, disability, or psychological trauma that directly resulted from a physical assault of a sexual nature, battery of a sexual nature, or sexual harassment which occurred while the veteran was serving on active duty or active duty for training.
:(2) The term private provider means a non-Department facility or licensed health care professional, regardless of whether the facility or professional is entered into a contract pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1703 section 1703 of this title] .
:(3) The term sexual harassment has the meaning given that term in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D section 1720D of this title] .
:(4) The term treatment means treatment, counseling, and appropriate other care and services to overcome military sexual trauma..
</blockquote>

==(b) Members of the Armed Forces– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Chapter 55of title 10, United States Code, is amended by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m section 1074m] the following new section:

<blockquote>

===Sec. 1074n. Counseling and treatment for sexual trauma at private facilities===



====(a) Establishment– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] shall operate a program under which the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] ensures that members of the armed forces who are victims of military sexual trauma may receive treatment for such trauma by private providers.

====(b) Election– ====

A member may elect to receive treatment under this section by notifying the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] of such election. Such notification shall include a written affidavit by the member, made under penalty of perjury under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1746 section 1746of title 28] , stating that the member is a victim of military sexual trauma.

====(c) Voucher– ====


:(1) Upon receiving a notification under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074n subsection (b)] , the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] shall issue the member a voucher described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074n paragraph (3)] .
:(2) A member to whom a voucher is issued under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074n paragraph (1)] may use the voucher to receive treatment by a private provider who agrees to accept such voucher as payment for such treatment. Any amount charged by the private provider for such treatment that is in addition to the amount paid by the voucher shall be the sole responsibility of the member.
:(3) A voucher described in this paragraph is a voucher indicating that the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] will reimburse a private provider for treatment provided by the provider to a member under this section.
:(4) The amount for which the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] will reimburse a private provider pursuant to a voucher described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074n paragraph (3)] is equal to the amount that the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] pays to a private provider under the TRICARE program for the same treatment for which the voucher is used.
:(5) The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] shall require that a veteran make a reelection under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074n subsection (b)] during each 120-day period in which the voucher is used following the initial 120-day period in which the voucher is valid.

====(d) Consultation– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] shall consult with the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] to ensure that this section is carried out in a manner that allows members to receive similar treatment as is provided to veterans under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D–1 section 1720D–1of title 38] .

====(e) Definitions– ====

In this section:
:(1) The term military sexual trauma means an injury, illness, disability, or psychological trauma that directly resulted from a physical assault of a sexual nature, battery of a sexual nature, or sexual harassment which occurred while the member was serving on active duty or active duty for training.
:(2) The term private provider means a private facility or licensed health care professional, regardless of whether the facility or professional is entered into a contract under this chapter to provide treatment under the TRICARE program.
:(3) The term sexual harassment has the meaning given that term in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D section 1720Dof title 38] .
:(4) The term treatment means treatment, counseling, and appropriate other care and services to overcome military sexual trauma..
</blockquote>
