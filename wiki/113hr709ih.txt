{{Center|A BILL}}

To authorize the Secretary of the Army to take actions to manage the threat of Asian carp traveling up the Mississippi River in the State of Minnesota, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HA10AA34F99444A57BF1168731488178E}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Upper Mississippi Conservation and River Protection Act of 2013”or the“Upper Mississippi CARP Act”.

={{anchor|H752FA01102714E7996F998B1B3392EC2}}Sec. 2. Definitions=

In this Act, the following definitions apply:

=={{anchor|HB96F09773D624960A5081E9910A01B15}}(1) Alternative technology barrier– ==

The term alternative technology barrier includes an electric barrier, acoustic barrier, bubble barrier, and such other barriers as the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] determines appropriate.

=={{anchor|HA9CB1E5DE41A4B38B86E425234321F63}}(2) Asian carp– ==

The term Asian carp means—
:(A) grass carp (Ctenopharyngodon idella);
:(B) silver carp (Hypophthalmichthys molitrix);
:(C) bighead carp (Hypophthalmichthys nobilis); and
:(D) black carp (Mylopharyngodon piceus).

=={{anchor|H5E88598DBE92449B94D86C4EF38F5955}}(3) Lock and Dam 1– ==

The term Lock and Dam 1 means the lock and dam located on Mississippi River mile 847.8 in Minneapolis, Minnesota.

=={{anchor|HEFBAF83A486B4E4AA22A39671D466EA0}}(4) Lock and Dam 2– ==

The term Lock and Dam 2 means the lock and dam located on Mississippi River mile 815.2 upstream of Hastings, Minnesota.

=={{anchor|HC425FBA5613D4AF2B335B57AC39D70C8}}(5) Lock and Dam 4– ==

The term Lock and Dam 4 means the lock and dam located on Mississippi River mile 752.8 in Alma, Wisconsin.

=={{anchor|HC7A105E6853B4196B710E59417E98AFD}}(6) Secretary– ==

The term Secretary means the Secretary of the Army, acting through the Chief of Engineers.

=={{anchor|H073173F8025947DEBD1FEA6FCC550A90}}(7) Upper St. Anthony Falls Lock and Dam– ==

The term Upper St. Anthony Falls Lock and Dam means the lock and dam located on Mississippi River mile 853.9 in Minneapolis, Minnesota.

={{anchor|HED2A541DA34A498E848BC1DFEC5C5006}}Sec. 3. Feasibility study on temporary closure of Upper St. Anthony Falls Lock=



=={{anchor|HBBD14C40E75D442C9C8CCC702C3BC541}}(a) Study– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall conduct a study on the feasibility of temporary closure of the lock at the Upper St. Anthony Falls Lock and Dam to manage the threat of Asian carp traveling up the Mississippi River in the State of Minnesota.

=={{anchor|H7BB0A1240D5B49D48584289AED09395B}}(b) Potential impacts– ==

In conducting the study, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall assess the potential impacts, including environmental and economic impacts of—
:(1) temporary closure of the lock; and
:(2) continuing to operate the lock.

=={{anchor|HECCF6040B48949FC94132266B6D88A31}}(c) Consultation– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall carry out the study in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] of the Interior and appropriate Federal, State, and local entities.

=={{anchor|H4904E98B43AE4EF8A7F0C8111C631A31}}(d) Public comment– ==

In conducting the study, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall provide an opportunity for, and take into consideration, public comment.

=={{anchor|HCE625673D36D4AD6B4E2FE175A1F0610}}(e) Report– ==

Not later than 6 months after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the results of the study.

={{anchor|HFDB6098481584D57A30863B2998958CF}}Sec. 4. Feasibility study on use of other Asian carp control measures=



=={{anchor|H446B4D2DA5E44872AFEE1BE11BF7668B}}(a) Study– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall conduct a study on the feasibility of implementing control measures at the Upper St. Anthony Falls Lock and Dam and at Lock and Dam 1 to manage the threat of Asian carp traveling up the Mississippi River in the State of Minnesota.

=={{anchor|H900A6039A31749E88CC01F29A17DB44E}}(b) Types of control measures– ==

The study shall include an examination of each of the following:
:(1) Permanent closure of the lock.
:(2) Modified lock operations.
:(3) Use of an alternative technology barrier.
:(4) Such other control measures as the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] determines appropriate.

=={{anchor|HCE6AF711CD0F4088A90E87C0E9E935D2}}(c) Potential impacts– ==

In conducting the study, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall assess the potential impacts, including environmental and economic impacts of—
:(1) implementing each of the control measures to be examined under subsection (b); and
:(2) not implementing any control measures.

=={{anchor|H49F8AE9868584B55A43224F886323AD7}}(d) Consultation– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall carry out the study in consultation with the Secretary of the Interior and appropriate Federal, State, and local entities.

=={{anchor|HC7289836AC2C42ADAD3A4D7BBC464F1E}}(e) Public comment– ==

In conducting the study, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall provide an opportunity for, and take into consideration, public comment.

=={{anchor|H1FDE74A285764B1DB2903E3AF0D0D8DA}}(f) Report– ==

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the results of the study.

={{anchor|H0406FF99FA724C5DB76F23101073B900}}Sec. 5. Closure of locks to prevent spread of Asian carp=



=={{anchor|H64C6586D2DB64D84AB837F13CA607FD1}}(a) Discretionary closure– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] may close the lock at the Upper St. Anthony Falls Lock and Dam if the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] determines in writing, based on the assessment of potential impacts under section 3(b), that closure of the lock is justified as a method to manage the threat of Asian carp traveling up the Mississippi River in the State of Minnesota.

=={{anchor|HF5E4FF217F6A412D973A8B77941E3112}}(b) Mandatory closure– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall close the lock at the Upper St. Anthony Falls Lock and Dam if the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] determines that—
:(1) one or more live adult Asian carp has been captured above Lock and Dam 2; or
:(2) one or more juvenile Asian carp has been captured above Lock and Dam 4.

=={{anchor|H727D8D7B08EF430EB3A598ED45BEBA49}}(c) Determinations by Secretary of the Interior– ==



==={{anchor|H933A3C62855547D2A5826E08EF9E381C}}(1) Petitions– ===

If the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] determines that an Asian carp has been captured that meets the criteria described in subsection (b)(1)or (b)(2), the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] may transmit to the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] a petition for closure of the lock at the Upper St. Anthony Falls Lock and Dam.

==={{anchor|HA465DF09FC544DF8B4535E569FDC99AD}}(2) Consideration– ===

After receiving a petition under paragraph (1), the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] shall—
:(A) consider the recommendation contained in the petition and prepare a written response to the recommendation; and
:(B) transmit the petition and written response to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] and make the petition and written response available to the public by electronic means, including the Internet.

=={{anchor|H110ABDC33A124344B9CBFDB5C1B5B109}}(d) Period of closure– ==

If the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] closes the lock under this section, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] may reopen the lock after the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] determines in writing that adequate measures are in place to manage the threat of Asian carp moving upstream of the Upper St. Anthony Falls Lock and Dam.

=={{anchor|HE369961BDB38423E9AE83108731A41C9}}(e) Emergency operations– ==

Nothing in this section shall prevent the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]] from carrying out emergency lock operations necessary to mitigate flood damage.

={{anchor|HBB6BD2C7C1F844388CB8BB58DA36C544}}Sec. 6. Asian Carp Control Strategy Framework=

The [[w:Council on Environmental Quality|Council on Environmental Quality]] shall incorporate the Upper Mississippi River and tributaries, the Minnesota River, and the St. Croix River into the Asian Carp Control Strategy Framework of the Council.

={{anchor|HFF298184308348649A6F5E146835028F}}Sec. 7. Sense of Congress=

It is the sense of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that, to the maximum extent practicable, Federal agencies researching Asian carp control technologies should partner with State and local shareholders, giving priority to those collaborative partnerships in which the State and local shareholders contribute to the cost of the research.
