{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 to
		  establish a base minimum wage for tipped employees.


{{section|H7CF7A9F96FA14D31A3085C235028DDDA|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Working for Adequate Gains for
			 Employment in Services Act

        
 or the 
          WAGES
			 Act

        
.

{{section|H01CA4CAF793B48269AFFE77D2E0F204D|2. Base minimum wage for tipped employees}}

        Section
			 3(m)(1) of the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938
 (29 U.S.C. 203(m)(1)
)
 is
			 amended by adding before the semicolon the
			 following:
, except that, for purposes of this
			 paragraph, the cash wage paid such employee shall be not less
			 than—
(A)
$3.75 an hour beginning 90 days after the
				date of enactment of the Working for Adequate
				Gains for Employment in Services Act

;

(B)
$5.00 an hour
				beginning 1 year after the date on which the change required by subparagraph
				(A)
 takes effect; and

(C)
beginning 2 years after the date on which
				the change required by subparagraph (A)
 takes effect and adjusted as necessary
				thereafter, 70 percent of the wage in effect under section 6(a)(1)
 but in no
				case less than $5.50 an
				hour.

.


{{section|H30127C8766E84E1AA6C989DD49281C8F|3. Publication of notice}}

        Section 6 of the Fair
			 Labor Standards Act of 1938
 (29 U.S.C. 206
)
 is amended by adding at the end the
			 following:
(h)
Not later than 10 days prior to the
				effective date of any increase in the minimum wage for tipped employees in
				accordance with section 3(m)(1)
, the [[United States Department of Labor|Secretary]] shall publish (in the Federal
				Register and on the Internet website of the [[United States Department of Labor|Department of Labor]]) a notice
				announcing the adjusted required
				wage.

.
