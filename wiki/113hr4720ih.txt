{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to increase the priority for enrollment of medal of honor recipients in the health care system of the Department of Veterans Affairs.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Medal of Honor Priority Care Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Priority of medal of honor recipients in health care system of Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705  Section 1705of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705 subsection (a)(1)], by striking the period at the end and inserting the following:“and veterans who were awarded the medal of honor under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/3741 section 3741],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/6241 6241], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/8741 8741 of title 10]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/14/491  section 491of title 14].”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705 paragraph (3)], by striking“veterans who were awarded the medal of honor under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/3741 section 3741],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/6241 6241], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/8741 8741 of title 10]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/14/491  section 491of title 14],”.

==(b) Application– ==

The priority of enrollment of medal of honor recipients pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705  section 1705(a)(1)of title 38, United States Code], as amended by subsection (a) , shall apply to each such recipient, regardless of the date on which the medal is awarded.
