{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act to provide, because of problems relating to the operation of Exchanges, for a hardship exemption from the individual mandate for months of noncoverage, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Fairness In Failed Federal Exchange Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Waiver of individual mandate through application of hardship exemption=

 Section 1311(d) of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/18031 42 U.S.C. 18031(d)] )is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

==(8) Application of hardship exemption in cases of noncoverage– ==

An applicable individual (as defined in subsection (d) of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/5000A section 5000A] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986) who is not covered by minimum essential coverage and who is eligible to enroll through an Exchange for coverage under a qualified health plan for a month—
:(A) in 2014 is deemed (for purposes of this Act and the amendments made by this Act) to have had a certification by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] under paragraph (4)(H)with respect to the hardship exemption described in subsection (e)(5)(H) of such sectionfor such month; and
:(B) in a subsequent year is deemed (for such purposes) to have had a certification by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] under such paragraph with respect to such hardship exemption if the individual executes an attestation that the individual has been unable to enroll in such a plan through such Exchange for a month in such year..
</blockquote>
