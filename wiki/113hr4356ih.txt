{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to make certain improvements in the information security of the Department of Veterans Affairs, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Department of Veterans Affairs Information Security Protection Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Department of Veterans Affairs information security improvements=



==(a) Submittal of quarterly information security report to Congress– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5723 Paragraph (14) of subsection (b) ofsection 5723of title 38, United States Code], is amended by inserting“and to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]] ”after“to the Secretary”.

==(b) Plan for addressing known information security vulnerabilities– ==

Such subsection is further amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
:(17) Submitting to the Chairs and Ranking Members of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]], by not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of this paragraph, and quarterly thereafter, a plan of action to address critical known information security vulnerabilities that includes—
::(A) specific milestones regarding time­lines to address such vulnerabilities;
::(B) a summary of any reports provided to the [[w:Assistant Secretary for Information and Technology|Assistant Secretary for Information and Technology]]pursuant to subsection (e)(3) during the period covered by the report;
::(C) a discussion of any risk assessment analysis undertaken by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]that led to the inclusion of any such vulnerability; and
::(D) a summary of such plan of action that could be made publicly available. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Plan for replacing outdated operating systems– ==

Such subsection is further amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
:(18) Submitting to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]], by not later than January 1 of each year, a plan for identifying and replacing operating systems of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]that are out-of-date or unsupported and that includes—
::(A) requirements that such an operating system be removed from the network of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]no later than 15 days after the date on which the operating system was identified as being out-of-date or unsupported; and
::(B) information concerning the number of systems so identified during the year preceding the year in which the report is submitted, when each such system was so identified, and when each system so identified was removed from the network of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]. . 
</blockquote>


==(d) Software security– ==

Such subsection is further amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
:(19) Ensuring that any software or Internet applications used on systems by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]are secure from vulnerabilities that could affect the confidentiality of sensitive personal information of veterans. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Information technology reporting requirements=



==(a) In general– ==

  Chapter 57of title 38, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) by redesignatingsections 5727 and 5728assections 5729 and 5730, respectively; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5726 section 5726]the following new sections:

<blockquote> 

===Sec. 5727. Reporting requirements===

Not later than 30 days after the last day of each fiscal quarter, the Secretary shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]]a report that includes the following information for that fiscal quarter:
:(1) A detailed description of any incidents of failure to comply with established information security policies that occurred during that quarter.
:(2) Any actions taken in response to such an incident.
:(3) Any reports made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5723 paragraphs (8) through (10) of subsection (b) of section 5723 of this title]during that quarter.
:(4) Written certification that the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5722/c section 5722(c)]of this title were followed during that quarter.
:(5) A detailed discussion of whether each recommendation made by the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|National Institute of Standards and Technology]], the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]], or the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]]relating to information security have been implemented by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], and if not, an explanation of why such recommendation was not implemented.
:(6) Steps taken to ensure the security of the Veterans Health Information Systems and Technology Architecture of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]that allows for an integrated inpatient and outpatient electronic health record for patients and provides administrative tools to employees of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]taken during that quarter. 

===Sec. 5728. Information security strategic plan===



====(a) Plan required– ====

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this section, the Secretary, in consultation with the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]], the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]], the Secretary of Defense, the [[w:National Institute of Standards and Technology|Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology]], the heads of other appropriate Federal agencies, veterans groups, and appropriate industry specialists, shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]]a strategic plan for improving the information security of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]. Such plan shall address—
:(1) methods of protecting the sensitive personal information of veterans while not unduly interfering with the ability of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to provide benefits and services to veterans and their dependents;
:(2) how the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]can improve its compliance with information security requirements;
:(3) training and recruitment of employees with the necessary expertise and abilities in information security; and
:(4) the institutional capability of the Department to address information security threats and to implement best practices related to information security.

====(b) Biannual updates– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]]and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]]biannual updates to the plan required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5728 subsection (a)]. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendments– ==

The table of sections at the beginning of such chapter is amended by striking the items relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5727 sections 5727]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5728 5728]and inserting the following new items:

<blockquote>  5727. Reporting requirements. 5728. Information security strategic plan. 5729. Definitions. 5730. Authorization of appropriations.  . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 4. Requirements for Department of Veterans Affairs contracts for data processing or maintenance=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5725  Section 5725(a)of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5725 paragraph (2)], by striking the period and inserting“; and”; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
::(3) the contractor shall provide protective measures to safeguard from possible information security threats any information provided by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]that will be resident on or transiting through information systems controlled by the contractor. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Applicability– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5725 Paragraph (3) ofsection 5725(a)of title 38, United States Code], shall apply with respect to a contract entered into after the date of the enactment of this Act.
