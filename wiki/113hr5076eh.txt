{{Center|2d Session}}

 
{{Center|H. R. 5076}}

 
{{Center|IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES}}

 
{{Center|AN ACT}}

 To amend the Runaway and Homeless Youth Act to increase knowledge concerning, and improve services for, runaway and homeless youth who are victims of trafficking.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Enhancing Services for Runaway and Homeless Victims of Youth Trafficking Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Amendments=

The  Runaway and Homeless Youth Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/5701 42 U.S.C. 5701 et seq.] )is amended—
:(1) in section 343(b)(5) —
::(A) in subparagraph (A) by inserting“, severe forms of trafficking in persons (as defined in  section 103(9) of the Trafficking Victims Protection Act of 2000 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/7102 22 U.S.C. 7102(9)] )), and sex trafficking (as defined in  section 103(10) of such Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/7102 22 U.S.C. 7102(10)] ))”before the semicolon at the end;
::(B) in subparagraph (B) by inserting“, severe forms of trafficking in persons (as defined in  section 103(9) of the Trafficking Victims Protection Act of 2000 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/7102 22 U.S.C. 7102(9)] )), and sex trafficking (as defined in  section 103(10) of such Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/7102 22 U.S.C. 7102(10)] ))”after“assault”; and
::(C) in subparagraph (C) by inserting“, including such youth who are victims of trafficking (as defined in  section 103(15) of the Trafficking Victims Protection Act of 2000 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/7102 22 U.S.C. 7102(15)] ))”before the semicolon at the end; and
:(2) in section 351(a) by striking“or sexual exploitation”and inserting“sexual exploitation, severe forms of trafficking in persons (as defined in  section 103(9) of the Trafficking Victims Protection Act of 2000 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/7102 22 U.S.C. 7102(9)] )), or sex trafficking (as defined in  section 103(10) of such Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/7102 22 U.S.C. 7102(10)] ))”.    Passed the House of Representatives July 23, 2014. Karen L. Haas, Clerk.
