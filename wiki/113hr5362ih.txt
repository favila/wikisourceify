{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide that the Social Security Administration pay fees associated with obtaining birth certificate or State identification card for purposes of obtaining a replacement social security card for certain victims of domestic violence, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Responsibility of Social Security Administration to pay fees associated with obtaining birth certificate or State identification card for purposes of obtaining a replacement social security card for certain victims of domestic violence=



==(a) In general– ==

   Section 205(c)(2)(G) of the Social Security Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/405 42 U.S.C. 405(c)(2)(G)] )is amended—
:(1) by inserting“(i)”before“The Commissioner”; and
:(2) by adding after and below the end the following new clause:

<blockquote> 
::(ii) 
:::(I) In the case of a person described in subclause (II) who has submitted to the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner]]an application to replace a social security card issued to the person, the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner]]shall pay any fees charged by any governmental authority for furnishing the person with any of the following documents required to be submitted as part of the application:
::::(aa) A birth certificate of the person.
::::(bb) An identification card issued to the person by a State, that shows that the person is a resident of the State.
:::(II) A person described in this subclause is a person who—
::::(aa) is verified by the staff of a shelter (as defined in section 302(9) of the Family Violence Prevention and Services Act ) to be a resident of the shelter and is a victim of domestic violence (as defined in section 302(3) of such Act ) or is a dependent of such a resident; or
::::(bb) is verified by the staff of an organization providing supportive services (as defined in section 302(12) of such Act ) to be a recipient of such services or is a dependent of such a person.
:::(III) A provision by a person described in subclause (I) of authorization to release information about the person, and the provision of information pursuant to the authorization, shall not be construed to be a waiver of any privilege of the person, under Federal or State law, relating to the confidentiality of communications between a domestic violence victim and an advocate of such a victim. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by subsection (a) shall apply to applications submitted after the 90-day period that begins with the date of the enactment of this Act.
