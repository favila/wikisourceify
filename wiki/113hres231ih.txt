{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

Establishing a Select Committee on POW and MIA Affairs.

=Section 1. Establishment=

There is established a select committee to be known as the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Select Committee on POW and MIA Affairs]] (hereafter referred to as the select committee ).

=Sec. 2. Functions=

The [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] shall conduct a full investigation of all unresolved matters relating to any United States personnel unaccounted for from the Vietnam era, the Korean conflict, World War II, Cold War Missions, Persian Gulf War, Operation Iraqi Freedom, or Operation Enduring Freedom, including MIAs and POWs missing and captured.

=Sec. 3. Appointment and membership=



==(a) Members– ==

The [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] shall be composed of 10 Members of the House, who shall be appointed by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker]] . Not more than half of the members of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] shall be of the same political party.

==(b) Vacancy– ==

Any vacancy occurring in the membership of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] shall be filled in the same manner in which the original appointment was made.

==(c) Chairperson– ==

The [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker]] shall designate one member of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] to be its chairperson.

=Sec. 4. Authority and procedures=



==(a) Authority– ==

For purposes of carrying out this resolution, the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] (or any subcommittee of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] authorized to hold hearings) may sit and act during the present [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] at any time or place within the United States (including any Commonwealth or possession of the United States, or elsewhere, whether the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House]] is in session, has recessed, or has adjourned) and to hold such hearings as it considers necessary.

==(b) Rules of procedure– ==

Clauses 1, 2, and 4 of rule XI of the Rules of the House of Representatives shall apply to the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] .

==(c) Prohibition against meeting at certain times– ==

 Subsection (a)may not be construed to limit the applicability of clause 2(i) of rule XI of the Rules of the House of Representatives to the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] .

=Sec. 5. Administrative provisions=



==(a) Expenses– ==

There shall be paid out of the applicable accounts of the House such sums as may be necessary for the expenses of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] . Such payments shall be made on vouchers signed by the chairman of the select committee and approved in the manner directed by the [[w:United States House Committee on House Administration|Committee on House Administration]] . Amounts made available under this paragraph shall be expended in accordance with regulations prescribed by the [[w:United States House Committee on House Administration|Committee on House Administration]] .

==(b) Staff and travel– ==

In carrying out its functions under this resolution, the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] may—
:(1) appoint, either on a permanent basis or as experts or consultants, any staff that the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] considers necessary;
:(2) prescribe the duties and responsibilities of the staff;
:(3) fix the compensation of the staff at a single per annum gross rate that does not exceed the highest rate of basic pay, as in effect from time to time, of level V of the Executive Schedule in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5316 section 5316of title 5, United States Code] ;
:(4) terminate the employment of any such staff as the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] considers appropriate; and
:(5) reimburse members of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] and of its staff for travel, subsistence, and other necessary expenses incurred by them in the performance of their functions for the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] , other than expenses in connection with any meeting of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] , or a subcommittee thereof, held in the District of Columbia.

==(c) Expiration– ==

The [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] and all authority granted in this resolution shall expire 30 days after the filing of the final report of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] with the House.

=Sec. 6. Reports and records=



==(a) Final report– ==

As soon as practicable during the present [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] , the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] shall submit to the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House]] a final report setting forth its findings and recommendations as a result of its investigation.

==(b) Filing of reports– ==

Any report made by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] when the House is not in session shall be filed with the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Clerk of the House]] .

==(c) Referral of reports– ==

Any report made by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] shall be referred to the committee or committees that have jurisdiction over the subject matter of the report.

==(d) Records, files, and materials– ==

Following the termination of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] , the records, files, and materials of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] shall be transferred to the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Clerk of the House]] . If the final report of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] is referred to only one committee under the provisions of subsection (c), the records, files, and materials of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|select committee]] shall be transferred instead to the committee to which the final report is referred.

=Sec. 7. Definitions=

As used in this resolution:
:(1) The term Member of the House means any Representative in, or Delegate or Resident Commissioner to, the Congress.
:(2) The term MIA means any United States personnel that is unaccounted for and missing in action.
:(3) The term POW means any United States personnel that is unaccounted for and known to be a prisoner of war.
