{{Center|A BILL}}

 To authorize the ground burial at Arlington National Cemetery of members of the United States Army who served honorably in the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier platoon, Third Infantry Regiment (Old Guard), United States Army.   

=Section 1. Burial of deceased former members of the Tomb Guard Platoon at Arlington National Cemetery=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/4722  Section 4722(a)of title 10, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
:(3) 
::(A) Subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/985 section 985(b) of this title], an individual who served as a Tomb Guard described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/4722 subparagraph (B)]is eligible for interment of casketed remains or cremated remains at Arlington National Cemetery.
::(B)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/4722 Subparagraph (A)]applies with respect to an individual who—
:::(i) served honorably in the Army;
:::(ii) served at the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier as a Tomb Guard; and
:::(iii) was awarded the Tomb Guard Identification Badge or a certificate of participation if service occurred prior to the introduction of the badge.
::(C)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/4722 Subparagraph (A)]shall not apply to an individual otherwise described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/4722 subparagraph (B)]whose Tomb Guard Identification Badge or certificate was revoked before the burial of the individual.
::(D) The Society of the Honor Guard, Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, shall be responsible for verifying the eligibility requirements specified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/4722 subparagraph (B)]. . 
</blockquote>
