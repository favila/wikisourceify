{{Center|A BILL}}

To direct the Federal Trade Commission to prescribe rules prohibiting deceptive advertising of abortion services, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H76DE812EA1D14472A51388A9F0F2F3B1}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Stop Deceptive Advertising for Women’s Services Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H1669FACFD9B7412CA4E547FB9FBE8374}}Sec. 2. Prohibition of deceptive advertising of abortion services=



=={{anchor|HA017E8276EB14E7B96F28B8D3DC0B505}}(a) Conduct Prohibited– ==



==={{anchor|id13BDBDE41E9D4D91BE55373AD42E07B7}}(1) In general– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Federal Trade Commission|Federal Trade Commission]] shall promulgate rules to prohibit, as an unfair and deceptive act or practice, any person from advertising with the intent to deceptively create the impression that—
:(A) such person is a provider of abortion services if such person does not provide abortion services; and
:(B) such person is not a provider of abortion services if such person does provide abortion services.

==={{anchor|id7DD722CF358C4B89896A9DD61B9E096A}}(2) Rulemaking– ===

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the [[w:Federal Trade Commission|Commission]] shall promulgate a rule under this subsection in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 553] of title 5, United States Code] .

=={{anchor|idF03137C4B17449FABF8F7D48C8CF2E54}}(b) Enforcement– ==



==={{anchor|idF5CCE3E0362C4BC18F50836A7C03BFC0}}(1) Unfair or deceptive acts or practices– ===

A violation of a rule promulgated under subsection (a)(1)shall be treated as a violation of a rule defining an unfair or deceptive act or practice described under section 18(a)(1)(B) of the Federal Trade Commission Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/57a 15 U.S.C. 57a(a)(1)(B)] ] ).

==={{anchor|id1EDF806D9B08474693E10D7BC74B3B30}}(2) Powers of Commission– ===



===={{anchor|id7AAB61279418434591F51FE00E5DB745}}(A) In general– ====

Except as provided in subparagraph (C), the [[w:Federal Trade Commission|Federal Trade Commission]] shall enforce this Act in the same manner, by the same means, and with the same jurisdiction, powers, and duties as though all applicable terms and provisions of the Federal Trade Commission Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/41 15 U.S.C. 41 et seq.] )were incorporated into and made a part of this Act.

===={{anchor|id22A746F97C78497FA3E2653CB518BCD9}}(B) Privileges and immunities– ====

Except as provided in subparagraph (C), any person who violates this Act shall be subject to the penalties and entitled to the privileges and immunities provided in the Federal Trade Commission Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/41 15 U.S.C. 41 et seq.] ).

===={{anchor|idA1D31FFB7ED243D9B22BFC74EE22F4F5}}(C) Nonprofit organizations– ====

The [[w:Federal Trade Commission|Federal Trade Commission]] shall enforce this Act with respect to an organization that is not organized to carry on business for its own profit or that of its members as if such organization were a person over which the [[w:Federal Trade Commission|Commission]] has authority pursuant to section 5(a)(2) of the Federal Trade Commission Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/45 15 U.S.C. 45(a)(2)] ] ).

=={{anchor|HCB3E67CEC0AD4544BD1D2EC27FA36EB4}}(c) Definitions– ==

In this section:

==={{anchor|HA35F6188A2364841A8EE356B067E135C}}(1) Advertise– ===

The term advertise means offering of goods or services to the public, regardless of whether such goods or services are offered for payment or result in a profit.

==={{anchor|H46C28F95D8534B1FA2462FC20B3E9559}}(2) Abortion services– ===

The term abortion services means providing surgical and non-surgical procedures to terminate a pregnancy, or providing referrals for such procedures.

==={{anchor|H7ED1696AA4D347AD807AAB437343B992}}(3) Person– ===

The term person has the meaning given such term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 section 551(2) of title 5, United States Code] .
