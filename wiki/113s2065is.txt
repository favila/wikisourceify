{{Center|A BILL}}

 To create incentives for the development of alternative fuel vehicles.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Alternative Fuel Vehicle Development Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Alternative fuel vehicles=



==(a) Maximum fuel economy increase for alternative fuel automobiles– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32906 Section 32906(a) of title 49, United States Code], is amended by striking“(except an electric automobile)”and inserting“(except an electric automobile or, beginning with model year 2016, an alternative fueled automobile that does not use a fuel described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 subparagraph (A)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (B)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (C)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (D) of section 32901(a)(1)])”.

==(b) Minimum driving ranges for dual fueled passenger automobiles– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 Section 32901(c)(2) of title 49], United States Code, is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 subparagraph (B)], by inserting“, except that beginning with model year 2016, alternative fueled automobiles that do not use a fuel described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 subparagraph (A)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (B)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (C)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (D) of subsection (a)(1)]shall have a minimum driving range of 150 miles”after“at least 200 miles”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 subparagraph (C)], by adding at the end the following:“Beginning with model year 2016, if the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]prescribes a minimum driving range of 150 miles for alternative fueled automobiles that do not use a fuel described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 subparagraph (A)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (B)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (C)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (D) of subsection (a)(1)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 subparagraph (A)]shall not apply to dual fueled automobiles (except electric automobiles).”.

==(d) Electric dual fueled automobiles– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905  Section 32905of title 49, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 subsections (e)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 (f)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 subsections (f)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 (g)], respectively; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 subsection (d)]the following:

<blockquote> 

====(e) Electric dual fueled automobiles– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

At the request of the manufacturer, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]may measure the fuel economy for any model of dual fueled automobile manufactured after model year 2015 that is capable of operating on electricity in addition to gasoline or diesel fuel, obtains its electricity from a source external to the vehicle, and meets the minimum driving range requirements established by the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]for dual fueled electric automobiles, by dividing 1.0 by the sum of—
:(A) the percentage utilization of the model on gasoline or diesel fuel, as determined by a formula based on the model’s alternative fuel range, divided by the fuel economy measured under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32904 section 32904(c)]; and
:(B) the percentage utilization of the model on electricity, as determined by a formula based on the model’s alternative fuel range, divided by the fuel economy measured under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32904 section 32904(a)(2)].

=====(2) Alternative utilization– =====

The [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]may adapt the utility factor established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 paragraph (1)]for alternative fueled automobiles that do not use a fuel described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 subparagraph (A)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (B)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (C)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 (D) of section 32901(a)(1)].

=====(3) Alternative calculation– =====

If the manufacturer does not request that the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]calculate the manufacturing incentive for its electric dual fueled automobiles in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 paragraph (1)], the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]shall calculate such incentive for such automobiles manufactured by such manufacturer after model year 2015 in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 subsection (b)]. . 
</blockquote>

=Sec. 4. Study=

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]], after consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]], shall submit a report to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that—
:(1) describes options to incentivize the development of public compressed natural gas fueling stations; and
:(2) analyzes a variety of possible financing tools, which could include—
::(A) Federal grants and credit assistance;
::(B) public-private partnerships; and
::(C) membership-based cooperatives.
