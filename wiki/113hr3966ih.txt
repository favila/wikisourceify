{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend titles XVIII and XIX of the Social Security Act to provide for equitable treatment of residents of the territories with respect to low-income subsidies under the Medicare prescription drug benefit program, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Territories Medicare Prescription Drug Assistance Equity Act of 2014 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Equitable treatment of residents of territories in premium and cost-sharing subsidies under Medicare prescription drug program=



==(a) Medicare assistance– ==

   Section 1860D–14(a)(3) of the Social Security Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1395w–114 42 U.S.C. 1395w–114(a)(3)] )is amended by striking subparagraph (F).removed territorial ineligibility for low-income part D assistance —so 1860D-14 now made applicable in territories

=====(4) Increased amount beginning with second quarter of fiscal year 2016– =====

==(c) Conforming amendments– ==


:(1)    Section 1108(f) of the Social Security Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1308 42 U.S.C. 1308(f)] )is amended by striking“1935(e)(1)(B)”and inserting“  1935(e)(1)  ”.
:(2)    Section 1860D–14(a)(3)(C) of the Social Security Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1395w–114 42 U.S.C. 1395w–114(a)(3)(C)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:“The poverty line to be applied in the territories shall be the same as the poverty line applied to States in the continental United States.”.

==(d) Effective dates– ==

The amendments made by subsections (a) and (c)(2) shall be effective as of January 1, 2016, and the amendments made by subsections (b) and (c)(1) shall take effect on the date of the enactment of this Act.
