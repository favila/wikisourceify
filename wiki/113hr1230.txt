{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act to designate
		  segments of the Molalla River in the State of Oregon, as components of the
		  National Wild and Scenic Rivers System, and for other
		  purposes.


{{section|H54A0D228ADC346DEB1E92C0EBC7F9639|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Molalla River Wild and Scenic Rivers
			 Act

        
.

{{section|HE622C25CF26B4C83AF338985315C4FE8|2. Designation of wild and scenic river segments, Molalla River, Oregon}}
Section 3(a) of the Wild and Scenic Rivers
			 Act
 (16 U.S.C. 1274(a)

)
 is amended by adding at the end the following:
(___)
Molalla river,
				Oregon
(A)
In
				general
The following segments in the State of Oregon, to be
				administered by the Secretary of the Interior as a recreational river:
(i)
Molalla
				river
The approximately 15.1-mile segment from the southern
				boundary line of T. 7 S., R. 4 E., sec. 19, downstream to the edge of the
				[[Bureau of Land Management|Bureau of Land Management]] boundary in T. 6 S., R. 3 E., sec. 7.

(ii)
Table Rock Fork
				Molalla River
The approximately 6.2-mile segment from the
				easternmost Bureau of Land Management boundary line in the
				NE1/4
 sec. 4, T. 7 S., R. 4 E., downstream to the
				confluence with the Molalla River.


(B)
Withdrawal
Subject
				to valid existing rights, the Federal land within the boundaries of the river
				segments designated by subparagraph (A)
 is withdrawn from all forms of—
(i)
entry,
				appropriation, or disposal under the public land laws;

(ii)
location, entry,
				and patent under the mining laws; and

(iii)
disposition
				under all laws relating to mineral and geothermal leasing or mineral
				materials.



.
