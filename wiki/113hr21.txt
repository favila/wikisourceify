={{anchor|H90FBDC01643A46AEA2F54F2B7777BF60}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“NRA Members’ Gun Safety Act of 2013”.

=={{anchor|H67D23A1E7F7543B39262918D14AC115A}}Title I— Requiring a background check for every firearm sale==



=={{anchor|H838E05C3AEF2407B8ECD9780520094F6}}Sec. 101. Purpose==

The purpose of this title is to extend the Brady Lawbackground check procedures to all sales and transfers of firearms.

=={{anchor|H3634D24A52FE42D3989E7340B505F0C8}}Sec. 102. Firearms transfers==



==={{anchor|H24295F8EEB4C4C569371B4AE36D63266}}(a) In general– ===

Chapter 44 of title 18, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H590F9F99368A45A68317395438BCA3A2}}Sec. 932. Background checks for firearm transfers by unlicensed persons===



===={{anchor|H217E2ED795A449B199BB2742FA6A6BB4}}(a) Definitions– ====

In this section—
:(1) the term unlicensed transferee means a person who—
::(A) is not licensed under this chapter; and
::(B) desires to receive a firearm from an unlicensed transferor; and
:(2) the term unlicensed transferor means a person who—
::(A) is not licensed under this chapter; and
::(B) desires to transfer a firearm to an unlicensed transferee.

===={{anchor|H84B47FCAB13348A6B450005CAC1B793C}}(b) Responsibilities of transferors other than licensees– ====



====={{anchor|H589C916FEAF64B5E9B264DE07FBACA42}}(1) In general– =====

It shall be unlawful for an unlicensed transferor to transfer a firearm to an unlicensed transferee, unless the firearm is transferred—
:(A) 
::(i) through a licensed dealer under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)] ;
::(ii) through a law enforcement agency under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)] ;
::(iii) after inspecting a permit that confirms a background check under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (f)] ; or
::(iv) in accordance with an exception described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (g)] ; and
:(B) in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 paragraph (2)] .

====={{anchor|H0FA43B79A9DD4BECB18DC78BF2125799}}(2) Criminal background checks– =====

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (g)] , an unlicensed transferor—
:(A) subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subparagraph (B)] , may not transfer a firearm to an unlicensed transferee until—
::(i) the licensed dealer through which the transfer is made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)] makes a notification described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)(3)(A)] ;
::(ii) the law enforcement agency through which the transfer is made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)] makes a notification described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)(4)(A)] ; or
::(iii) the unlicensed transferee has presented a permit that confirms that a background check has been conducted, as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (f)] ; and
:(B) may not transfer a firearm to an unlicensed transferee if—
::(i) the licensed dealer through which the transfer is made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)] makes the notification described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)(3)(B)] ; or
::(ii) the law enforcement agency through which the transfer is made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)] makes the notification described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)(3)(B)] .

====={{anchor|HB50B0ABB160E4A0482D7496A591080F7}}(3) Absence of recordkeeping requirements– =====

Nothing in this section shall permit or authorize the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] to impose recordkeeping requirements on any unlicensed transferor.

===={{anchor|H1B42229717C8494E9C363B7284A61B53}}(c) Responsibilities of transferees other than licensees– ====



====={{anchor|H823FAF0372A8416BA3F6664DF6ED2CFD}}(1) In general– =====

It shall be unlawful for an unlicensed transferee to receive a firearm from an unlicensed transferor, unless the firearm is transferred—
:(A) 
::(i) through a licensed dealer under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)] ;
::(ii) through a law enforcement agency under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)] ;
::(iii) after inspecting a permit that confirms a background check in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (f)] ; or
::(iv) in accordance with an exception described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (g)] ; and
:(B) in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 paragraph (2)] .

====={{anchor|H1B126EFFC620474291D49663B70D6B2E}}(2) Criminal background checks– =====

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (g)] , an unlicensed transferee—
:(A) subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subparagraph (B)] , may not receive a firearm from an unlicensed transferor until—
::(i) the licensed dealer through which the transfer is made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)] makes a notification described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)(3)(A)] ;
::(ii) the law enforcement agency through which the transfer is made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)] makes a notification described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)(4)(A)] ; or
::(iii) the unlicensed transferee has presented a permit that confirms that a background check described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (f)] has been conducted; and
:(B) may not receive a firearm from another unlicensed transferor if—
::(i) the licensed dealer through which the transfer is made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)] makes a notification described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (d)(3)(B)] ; or
::(ii) the law enforcement agency through which the transfer is made under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)] makes a notification described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)(4)(B)] .

===={{anchor|H74B900E9A4CB43CE8B1E1B8A51942451}}(d) Background checks through licensed dealers– ====

A licensed dealer who agrees to assist in the transfer of a firearm between unlicensed transferor and an unlicensed transferee shall—
:(1) enter such information about the firearm as the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may require by regulation into a separate bound record;
:(2) record the transfer on a form specified by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ;
:(3) comply with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(t)] as if transferring the firearm from the inventory of the licensed dealer to the unlicensed transferee (except that a licensed dealer assisting in the transfer of a firearm under this subsection shall not be required to comply again with the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(t)] in delivering the firearm to the unlicensed transferee) and notify the unlicensed transferor and unlicensed transferee—
::(A) of such compliance; and
::(B) if the transfer is subject to the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(t)(1)] , of any receipt by the licensed dealer of a notification from the national instant criminal background check system that the transfer would violate[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922] or State law;
:(4) not later than 31 days after the date on which the transfer occurs, submit to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] a report of the transfer, which—
::(A) shall be on a form specified by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] by regulation; and
::(B) shall not include the name of or other identifying information relating to the unlicensed transferor or unlicensed transferee;
:(5) if the licensed dealer assists an unlicensed transferor in transferring, at the same time or during any 5 consecutive business days, two or more pistols or revolvers, or any combination of pistols and revolvers totaling two or more, to the same unlicensed transferee, in addition to the reports required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 paragraph (4)] , prepare a report of the multiple transfers, which shall—
::(A) be prepared on a form specified by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ; and
::(B) not later than the close of business on the date on which the transfer requiring the report under this paragraph occurs, be submitted to—
:::(i) the office specified on the form described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subparagraph (A)] ; and
:::(ii) the appropriate State law enforcement agency of the jurisdiction in which the transfer occurs; and
:(6) retain a record of the transfer as part of the permanent business records of the licensed dealer.

===={{anchor|HD71DCF1611DA4B98A907988EAF2E5D3D}}(e) Background checks through law enforcement agencies– ====

A State or local law enforcement agency that agrees to assist an unlicensed transferor in carrying out the responsibilities of the unlicensed transferor under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (b)] with respect to the transfer of a firearm shall—
:(1) 
::(A) contact the national instant criminal background check system under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(t)] ; and
::(B) 
:::(i) receive an identification number as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(t)(1)(B)(i)] ; or
:::(ii) wait the period described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 922(t)(1)(B)(ii)] ;
:(2) conduct such other checks as the agency considers appropriate to determine whether the receipt or possession of the firearm by the unlicensed transferee would violate[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922] or State law;
:(3) verify the identity of the unlicensed transferee by—
::(A) examining a valid identification document (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028 section 1028(d)] ) of the unlicensed transferee containing a photograph of the unlicensed transferee; or
::(B) confirming that the unlicensed transferor has examined a valid identification document described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subparagraph (A)] ;
:(4) notify the unlicensed transferor and transferee—
::(A) of the compliance by the law enforcement agency with the requirements under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 paragraphs (1)] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 (2)] , and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 (3)] ; and
::(B) of any receipt by the law enforcement agency of a notification from the national instant criminal background check system or other information that the transfer would violate[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922] or would violate State law;
:(5) not later than 31 days after the date on which the transfer occurs, submit to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] a report of the transfer, which—
::(A) shall be on a form specified by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] by regulation; and
::(B) shall not include the name of or other identifying information relating to the unlicensed transferor or unlicensed transferee;
:(6) if the law enforcement agency assists an unlicensed transferor in transferring, at the same time or during any 5 consecutive business days, two or more pistols or revolvers, or any combination of pistols and revolvers totaling two or more, to the same unlicensed transferee, in addition to the reports required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 paragraph (5)] , prepare a report of the multiple transfers, which shall be—
::(A) prepared on a form specified by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ; and
::(B) not later 24 hours after the transfer requiring the report under this paragraph occurs, submitted to—
:::(i) the office specified on the form described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subparagraph (A)] ; and
:::(ii) the appropriate State law enforcement agency of the jurisdiction in which the transfer occurs; and
:(7) maintain records of the transfer at such place, and in such form, as the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may prescribe.

===={{anchor|HA6E48CFBDA5B4638B8C7A66B02B9F2D1}}(f) Purchase permits confirming background checks– ====

An unlicensed transferor may transfer a firearm to an unlicensed transferee if the unlicensed transferor verifies that—
:(1) the unlicensed transferee has presented to a licensed dealer or a law enforcement official a permit or license that allows the unlicensed transferee to possess, acquire, or carry a firearm, and the licensed dealer or law enforcement official, as the case may be, has verified to the unlicensed transferor that the permit or license is valid;
:(2) the permit or license was issued not more than 5 years before the date on which the permit or license is presented under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 paragraph (1)] by a law enforcement agency in the State in which the transfer is to take place;
:(3) the law of the State provides that the permit or license is to be issued only after a law enforcement official has verified that neither the national instant criminal background check system nor other information indicates that possession of a firearm by the unlicensed transferee would be in violation of Federal, State, or local law; and
:(4) if the permit or license does not include a photograph of the unlicensed transferee, the unlicensed transferor has examined a valid identification document (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028 section 1028(d)] ) of the unlicensed transferee containing a photograph of the unlicensed transferee.

===={{anchor|H989439DA3C01422BB50EEC3BEF93D2C4}}(g) Exceptions– ====

Unless prohibited by any other provision of law,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsections (b)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 (c)] shall not apply to any transfer of a firearm between an unlicensed transferor and unlicensed transferee, if—
:(1) the transfer is a bona fide gift between immediate family members, including spouses, parents, children, siblings, grandparents, and grandchildren;
:(2) the transfer occurs by operation of law, or because of the death of another person for whom the unlicensed transferor is an executor or administrator of an estate or a trustee of a trust created in a will;
:(3) the transfer is temporary and occurs while in the home of the unlicensed transferee, if—
::(A) the unlicensed transferee is not otherwise prohibited from possessing firearms; and
::(B) the unlicensed transferee believes that possession of the firearm is necessary to prevent imminent death or great bodily harm to the unlicensed transferee;
:(4) the transfer is approved by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] under section 5812 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986; or
:(5) upon application of the unlicensed transferor, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] determined that compliance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (b)] is impracticable because—
::(A) the ratio of the number of law enforcement officers of the State in which the transfer is to occur to the number of square miles of land area of the State does not exceed 0.0025;
::(B) the location at which the transfer is to occur is extremely remote in relation to the chief law enforcement officer (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(s)(8)] ); and
::(C) there is an absence of telecommunications facilities in the geographical area in which the unlicensed transferor is located; or
:(6) the transfer is a temporary transfer of possession without transfer of title that takes place—
::(A) at a shooting range located in or on premises owned or occupied by a duly incorporated organization organized for conservation purposes or to foster proficiency in firearms;
::(B) at a target firearm shooting competition under the auspices of or approved by a State agency or nonprofit organization; or
::(C) while hunting, fishing, or trapping, if—
:::(i) the activity is legal in all places where the unlicensed transferee possesses the firearm; and
:::(ii) the unlicensed transferee holds any required license or permit.

===={{anchor|HA0A24C65ED5241E8893C1AE5CE3757A6}}(h) Processing fees– ====

A licensed dealer or law enforcement agency that processes the transfer of a firearm under this section may assess and collect a fee, in an amount not to exceed $15, with respect to each firearm transfer processed.

===={{anchor|HE3F6B939EB534FAB9E6B2B40A717AEA3}}(i) Records– ====

Nothing in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/932 subsection (e)(7)] shall be construed to authorize the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] to inspect records described in such subsection or to require that the records be transferred to a facility owned, managed, or controlled by the United States..
</blockquote>

=={{anchor|H260D0082966F4971AF5F3EC0642AB645}}Title III— Prevention of terrorists from obtaining firearms or explosives==



=={{anchor|H7E2C1B7DB56F4CFD9431B68C6BFB77FF}}Sec. 301. Granting the attorney general the authority to deny the sale, delivery, or transfer of a firearm or the issuance of a firearms or explosives license or permit to dangerous terrorists==

<blockquote>

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may deny the transfer of a firearm pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(t)(1)(B)(ii)] if the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] determines that the transferee is known (or appropriately suspected) to be or have been engaged in conduct constituting, in preparation for, in aid of, or related to terrorism, or providing material support thereof, and the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] has a reasonable belief that the prospective transferee may use a firearm in connection with terrorism.;
</blockquote>

:(2) by inserting the following new section after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922A section 922A] :

<blockquote>

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may determine that an applicant for a firearm permit which would qualify for an exemption under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(t)] is known (or appropriately suspected) to be or have been engaged in conduct constituting, in preparation for, in aid of, or related to terrorism, or providing material support thereof, and the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] has a reasonable belief that the applicant may use a firearm in connection with terrorism.; and

=={{anchor|H27BEEDDB83484514A4EBB8AD98824586}}Sec. 501. Concealed firearms permits==



==={{anchor|HDA049E3F14CE450F919492E0BC1B67A5}}(a) In general– ===

Chapter 44 of title 18, United States Code, is amended by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926C section 926C] the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H9F95C5FA2F2F45129797D7FF6CB98075}}Sec. 926D. Concealed firearms permits===



===={{anchor|H071D46CACF4B45DCA31DB249B53B5549}}(a) In general– ====

Each State that allows residents of the State to carry concealed firearms in or affecting interstate or foreign commerce shall—
:(1) establish a process to issue permits to residents of the State to carry concealed firearms in or affecting interstate or foreign commerce; and
:(2) require that each resident of the State seeking to carry a concealed firearm in or affecting interstate or foreign commerce in the State obtain a permit through the process established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D paragraph (1)] .

===={{anchor|H46EDAC25CAE0414896C18F87D64B1C1E}}(b) Requirements– ====

In establishing a process to issue permits to carry concealed firearms under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D subsection (a)] , a State shall—
:(1) ensure that a local law enforcement agency participates in the process; and
:(2) at a minimum, require that an applicant for a permit to carry a concealed firearm in or affecting interstate or foreign commerce—
::(A) be a legal resident of the United States;
::(B) be not less than 21 years of age;
::(C) demonstrate good cause for requesting a concealed firearm permit;
::(D) demonstrate that the applicant is worthy of the public trust to carry a concealed firearm in public;
::(E) complete a firearm safety training course certified by the State; and
::(F) not have been convicted of a crime of violence.

===={{anchor|HB851BCFD548F4828AD5CF00C71698789}}(c) Law enforcement agency report– ====

If a State establishes a process under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D subsection (a)] that allows for an agency other than a law enforcement agency to issue permits to carry concealed firearms, the process shall require that—
:(1) a local law enforcement agency submit to the agency responsible for issuing permits a written report that describes whether the applicant meets the standards of the State to carry a concealed firearm; and
:(2) the agency responsible for issuing permits maintain a report submitted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D paragraph (1)] in the file of the applicant.

===={{anchor|H50F973C2BDD345148BA3C37B12F9F9DF}}(d) Definition– ====

In this section, the term local law enforcement agency means a law enforcement agency of the unit of local government with jurisdiction of the area in which the applicant for a permit to carry a concealed firearm resides.

===={{anchor|HE973A359DC4A49209290D2A5E8B82C0D}}(e) Compliance– ====

Not later than 270 days after the date of enactment of this section, each State described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926D subsection (a)] shall be in compliance with this section..
</blockquote>
