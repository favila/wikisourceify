{{Center|A BILL}}

 To require certain preconditions for allowing premium tax credits, reductions in cost-sharing, and funding of Navigators and related Exchange enrollment activities, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Preconditions for premium tax credits, reductions in cost-sharing, and funding of Navigators and related Exchange enrollment activities=



==(a) In general– ==

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, no premium tax credit shall be allowed under section 36B of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 , no reduction in cost-sharing shall be allowed under  section 1402 of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/18071 42 U.S.C. 18071] ), and no funds may be awarded under  section 1311 of such Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/18031 42 U.S.C. 18031] )for the operation of a Navigator program under subsection (i) of such section or for facilitation or advocacy for enrollment under subsections (d)(6)(B) and (E) of such section , until—
:(1) 1 year after the date the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]certifies to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that the ACA verification methods (as defined in subsection (c)(1) )—
::(A) have been tested to verify their accuracy; and
::(B) have safeguards in place to protect personally identifiable information; and
:(2) the [[w:Office of the Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department of Health and Human Services]]reviews such verification methods and certifies to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]—
::(A) the quality, accuracy, response time, and integrity of such verification methods; and
::(B) that the level of improper subsidy payments is not likely to exceed 3 percent of the level of the total subsidy payments (as defined in subsection (c)(3) ).

==(b) Subsequent annual certification of verification methods– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:Office of the Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department of Health and Human Services]]shall annually review the quality, accuracy, response time, and integrity of the ACA verification methods in order to determine the level of improper subsidy payments as a percentage of the level of total subsidy payments.

===(2) Suspension if excessive improper subsidy payments– ===

If the [[w:Office of the Inspector General|Inspector General]]determines for a year as a result of such review that the level of improper subsidy payments is projected to exceed 3 percent of the level of total subsidy payments, for years beginning after the date of such determination awards shall be suspended until such time as the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]and the [[w:Office of the Inspector General|Inspector General]]certify that the level of improper subsidy payments is not likely to exceed 3 percent of the level of total subsidy payment.

==(c) Definitions– ==

In this section:
:(1) The term ACA verification methods means the verification methods required under subsections (c)(4) and (d) of  section 1411 of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/18081 42 U.S.C. 18081]).
:(2) The term Secretary means the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]], acting in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]]and other appropriate Federal officials in implementing  section 1411 of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/18081 42 U.S.C. 18081] ).
:(3) The term subsidy payments means premium tax credits allowed under section 36B of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 and reductions in cost-sharing effected under  section 1402 of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/18071 42 U.S.C. 18071] ).
