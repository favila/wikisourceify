{{Center|A BILL}}

To require zero-based budgeting for departments and
		  agencies of the Government.

={{anchor|HB800A6206D35462EA968AA39B3B524AF}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Zero-based Budgeting Ensures
			 Responsible Oversight (ZERO) Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H9F3BF82F336D45E5BCFF956823DDA6DD}}Sec. 2. Zero-based
			 budgeting=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 Section 1105 of
			 title 31, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new
			 subsection:

<blockquote>
:(i) 
::(1) The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall
				submit with materials related to each budget transmitted under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 subsection (a)]
				on or after January 1, 2015, a budget for each department and agency which
				contains the following information:
:::(A) A description of each activity for
				which the department or agency receives an appropriation in the current fiscal
				year or for which the department or agency requests an appropriation for the
				budget year.
:::(B) The legal basis for each activity.
:::(C) For each activity, three alternative
				funding levels for the budget year, and a summary of the priorities that would
				be accomplished within each level, and the additional increments of value that
				would be added by the higher funding levels. At least two of these funding
				levels shall be below the funding level for the current fiscal year.
:::(D) For each activity, one or more
				measures of its cost efficiency and effectiveness.
::(2) As soon as practicable, the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director
				of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall publish guidelines to carry out
				[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 this subsection]. The guidelines shall require that the baseline budget of each
				department or agency is assumed to be zero and each proposed expenditure shall
				be justified as if it were a new
				expenditure..
</blockquote>
