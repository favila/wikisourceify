{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978 to provide for the designation of Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court judges by the President, majority of the Supreme Court, Speaker and minority leader of the House of Representatives, and majority leader and minority leader of the Senate, and to provide for the public disclosure of Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court decisions.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “FISA Court Oversight Underscoring Responsibility and Transparency Act” or the “FISA COURT Act” .

=Sec. 2. Designation of Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court judges=

 Section 103 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1803 50 U.S.C. 1803(a)] )is amended—
:(1) in subsection (a), by striking paragraph (1)and inserting the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>
::(1) 
:::(A) There is established a court which shall have jurisdiction to hear applications for and grant orders approving electronic surveillance anywhere within the United States under the procedures set forth in this Act.
:::(B) The court established under paragraph (1)shall consist of 11 publicly designated district court judges, of whom—
::::(i) 2 judges shall be designated by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] ;
::::(ii) 1 judge shall be designated by a majority of the [[w:Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]] ;
::::(iii) 2 judges shall be designated by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives]] ;
::::(iv) 2 judges shall be designated by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|minority leader of the House of Representatives]] ;
::::(v) 2 judges shall be designated by the [[w:United States Senate|majority leader of the Senate]] ; and
::::(vi) 2 judges shall be designated by the [[w:United States Senate|minority leader of the Senate]] .
:::(C) No judge designated under this subsection (except when sitting en banc under paragraph (2)) shall hear the same application for electronic surveillance under this Act which has been denied previously by another judge designated under this subsection. If any judge so designated denies an application for an order authorizing electronic surveillance under this Act, such judge shall provide immediately for the record a written statement of each reason for his decision and, on motion of the United States, the record shall be transmitted, under seal, to the court of review established in subsection (b).; and
</blockquote>

:(2) in subsection (b), by striking “The Chief Justice” and inserting “A majority of the [[w:Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]] ” .

=Sec. 3. Public availability of significant opinions of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court=

 Section 601 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1871 50 U.S.C. 1871] )is amended—
:(1) in subsection (c)—
::(A) in the heading, by striking “Submissions to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] ” and inserting “Submissions to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] and public availability of certain opinions” ; and
::(B) in the matter preceding paragraph (1)—
:::(i) by striking “The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ” and inserting “Subject to subsection (d), the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ” ; and
:::(ii) by inserting “and make publicly available” after “in subsection (a)” ; and
:(2) in subsection (d)—
::(A) by striking “Protection of National Security.—The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ” and inserting the following:

<blockquote>Protection of National Security.—

====(1) Redaction of materials– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ;
</blockquote>

::(B) in paragraph (1), as designated under subparagraph (A), by inserting “and made publicly available” after “in subsection (a)” ; and
::(C) by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

====(2) Waiver of public availability– ====



=====(A) Waiver– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] , in consultation with the [[w:|Director of National Intelligence]] , may waive the requirement to make a decision, order, or opinion described in subsection (c)publicly available under such subsection if the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] determines the public disclosure of such decision, order, or opinion (including public disclosure in redacted form) would cause harm to the national security of the United States.

=====(B) Report– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall quarterly make publicly available an estimate of the number of decisions, orders, or opinions for which the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] waived under subparagraph (A)the requirement under subsection (c)to make such decisions, orders, or opinions publicly available and the reasons for waiving such requirement..
</blockquote>
