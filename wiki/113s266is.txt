{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for the inclusion of Israel in the visa waiver program, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Visa Waiver for Israel Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Israel designated as a program country for visa waiver program=



==(a) In general– ==

Beginning on the date described in section 3, Israel shall be deemed a program country for purposes of section 217 of the Immigration and Nationality Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1187 8 U.S.C. 1187] ).

==(b) Exemption from application of certain requirements– ==

In the case of Israel, section 217(c)(2)(A) of the Immigration and Nationality Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1187 8 U.S.C. 1187(c)(2)(A)] )does not apply.

=Sec. 3. Effective date; compliance requirements=

The date described in this section is the date on which the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] , in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]] , determines that the Government of Israel has complied with the following:
:(1) The Government of Israel has complied with all of the requirements under section 217(c)(2) of the Immigration and Nationality Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1187 8 U.S.C. 1187] ), except the requirement referenced in section 2(b) of this Act.
:(2) The Government of Israel has made every reasonable effort, without jeopardizing the security of the State of Israel, to ensure that reciprocal privileges are extended to all United States citizens.
