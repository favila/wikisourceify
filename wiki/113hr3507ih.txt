{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend titles 10 and 38, United States Code, to expand the use of telehealth under the TRICARE program and in the Department of Veterans Affairs, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ 21st Century Care for Military and Veterans Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Telehealth under TRICARE program=



==(a) Care provided at military medical treatment facilities– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1077  Section 1077of title 10, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 
:(g) In providing health care to a covered beneficiary under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1076  section 1076of this title]at a military medical treatment facility, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]]may furnish an item or service to the covered beneficiary via a telecommunications system. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Care provided at private facilities– ==



===(1) Certain dependents– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079  Section 1079of title 10, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 
:(r) 
::(1) An item or service furnished to a covered beneficiary via a telecommunications system shall be covered by a plan described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 paragraph (2)]to the same extent the item or service would be covered if furnished in the same location of the covered beneficiary, and benefits shall not be denied under such a plan solely on the basis that the item or service is being furnished via a telecommunications system. For the purposes of reimbursement, licensure, professional liability, and other purposes under this section with respect to the provision of telehealth services, practitioners, suppliers, and providers of such services are considered to be furnishing such services at their location and not at the originating site.
::(2) A plan described in this paragraph is a plan for which the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]]enters into a contract under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 subsection (a)]to provide dependents with medical care. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Certain members and former members– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1086  Section 1086of title 10, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 
:(i) 
::(1) An item or service furnished to a covered beneficiary via a telecommunications system shall be covered by a plan described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1086 paragraph (2)]to the same extent the item or service would be covered if furnished in the same location of the covered beneficiary, and benefits shall not be denied under such a plan solely on the basis that the item or service is being furnished via a telecommunications system. For the purposes of reimbursement, licensure, professional liability, and other purposes under this section with respect to the provision of telehealth services, practitioners, suppliers, and providers of such services are considered to be furnishing such services at their location and not at the originating site.
::(2) A plan described in this paragraph is a plan for which the Secretary enters into a contract under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1086 subsection (a)]to provide persons covered by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1086 subsection (c)]with health benefits. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Health care provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) In general– ==

 Subchapter I ofchapter 17of title 38, United States Code, is amended by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1709A section 1709A] the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 1709B. Provision of health care via telecommunications system==



===(a) Direct care– ===

In providing health care directly to an individual under this chapter orchapter 18 of this title, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may furnish an item or service to the individual via a telecommunications system.

===(b) Contracted care– ===


:(1) An item or service furnished to an individual covered by a plan described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1709B paragraph (2)]via a telecommunications system shall be covered by such a plan to the same extent the item or service would be covered if furnished in the same location of the individual, and benefits shall not be denied under such a plan solely on the basis that the item or service is being furnished via a telecommunications system. For the purposes of reimbursement, licensure, professional liability, and other purposes under this chapter andchapter 18with respect to the provision of telehealth services, practitioners, suppliers, and providers of such services are considered to be furnishing such services at their location and not at the originating site.
:(2) A plan described in this paragraph is a plan for which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]enters into a contract or agreement under this chapter orchapter 18 of this titleto furnish health care to an individual. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections at the beginning of such chapter is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1709A section 1709A]the following new item:

<blockquote>  1709B. Provision of health care via telecommunications system.  . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 4. Effective date=

The amendments made by this Act shall take effect on January 1, 2015, and shall apply to items and services furnished on or after such date.
