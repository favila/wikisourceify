{{Center|A BILL}}

To establish a regulatory review process for rules that the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency plans to propose, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Regulatory review by the Secretary of Agriculture=



==(a) Definitions– ==

In this section:

===(1) Administrator– ===

The term “Administrator” means the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]] .

===(2) Agricultural entity– ===

The term “agricultural entity” means an entity that is involved in or relates to agricultural enterprise, including an enterprise that is engaged in the business of—
:(A) producing food and fiber;
:(B) ranching and raising of livestock;
:(C) aquaculture; and
:(D) any other farming- and agriculture-related activities.

===(3) Secretary– ===

The term “Secretary” means the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] .

==(b) Review of regulatory agenda– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall review each notice issued by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] and published in the Federal Register relating to the preparation of any guidance, policy, memorandum, regulation, or statement of general applicability and future effect that may have a significant impact on a substantial number of agricultural entities, including—
:(1) any regulatory agenda published by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/602 section 602of title 5, United States Code] ;
:(2) any regulation plan or agenda published by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] or the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] pursuant to an Executive order, including Executive Order 12866 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/601 5 U.S.C. 601note] ; relating to regulatory planning and review); and
:(3) any other publication issued by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] or the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] that may reasonably be foreseen to contain a notice of a plan by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] to prepare any guidance, policy, memorandum, regulation, or statement of general applicability and future effect that may have a significant impact on a substantial number of agricultural entities.

==(c) Information gathering– ==

For a publication item reviewed under subsection (b)that the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] determines may have a significant impact on a substantial number of agricultural entities, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall—
:(1) solicit from the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] any information the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] may provide to facilitate a review of the publication item;
:(2) acting through the [[w:|Chief Economist of the Department of Agriculture]] , produce an economic impact statement for the publication item that contains a detailed estimate of potential costs to agricultural entities;
:(3) identify individuals representative of potentially affected agricultural entities for the purpose of obtaining advice and recommendations from those individuals about the potential impacts of the publication item; and
:(4) convene a review panel for analysis of the publication item that includes the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] , any full-time Federal employee of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Department of Agriculture]] appointed to the panel by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] , and any employee of the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] or the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs of the Office of Management and Budget]] that accepts an invitation from the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] to participate in the panel.

==(d) Duties of the review panel– ==

A review panel convened for a publication item under subsection (c)(4)shall—
:(1) review any information or material obtained by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] and prepared in connection with the publication item, including any draft proposed guidance, policy, memorandum, regulation, or statement of general applicability and future effect;
:(2) collect advice and recommendations from agricultural entity representatives identified by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] after consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] ;
:(3) compile and analyze the advice and recommendations; and
:(4) make recommendations to the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] based on the information gathered by the review panel or provided by agricultural entity representatives.

==(e) Comments– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 60 days after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] convenes a review panel under subsection (c)(4), the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall submit to the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] comments on the planned or proposed guidance, policy, memorandum, regulation, or statement of general applicability and future effect for consideration and inclusion in any related administrative record, including—
:(A) a report by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] on the concerns of agricultural entities;
:(B) the findings of the review panel;
:(C) the findings of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] , including any adopted findings of the review panel; and
:(D) recommendations of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] .

===(2) Publication– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall publish the comments under paragraph (1)in the Federal Register and make the comments available to the public on the public Internet site of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Department of Agriculture]] .

==(f) Waivers– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] may waive initiation of the review panel under subsection (c)(4)as the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] determines appropriate.
