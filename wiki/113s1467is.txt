{{Center|A BILL}}

 To establish the Office of the Special Advocate to provide advocacy in cases before courts established by the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978 and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ FISA Court Reform Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Definitions=

In this Act:

==(1) Decision– ==

The term decision means a decision, order, or opinion issued by the FISA Court or the FISA Court of Review.

==(2) FISA– ==

The term FISA means the  Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1801 50 U.S.C. 1801 et seq.] ).

==(3) FISA Court– ==

The term    means the court established under  section 103(a) of FISA ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1803 50 U.S.C. 1803(a)] ).

==(4) FISA Court of Review– ==

The term    means the court of review established under  section 103(b) of FISA ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1803 50 U.S.C. 1803(b)] ).

==(5) Office– ==

The term Office mean the [[w:|Office of the Special Advocate]] established under section (3)(a) .

==(6) Significant construction or interpretation of law– ==

The term significant construction or interpretation of law means a significant construction or interpretation of a provision, as that term is construed under  section 601(c) of FISA ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1871 50 U.S.C. 1871(c)] ).

==(7) Special Advocate– ==

The term“Special Advocate”means the [[w:|Special Advocate]] appointed under section 3(b) . 

=Sec. 3. Office of the Special Advocate=



==(a) Establishment– ==

There is established in the executive branch as an independent establishment, as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/104  section 104of title 5, United States Code], an [[w:|Office of the Special Advocate]] .

==(b) Special Advocate– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The head of the [[w:|Office is the Special Advocate]] .

===(2) Appointment and term– ===



====(A) Appointment– ====

The presiding judge of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] shall appoint the [[w:|Special Advocate]] from the list of candidates submitted under subparagraph (B) .

====(B) List of candidates– ====

The [[w:|Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board]] shall submit to the presiding judge of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] a list of not less than 5 qualified candidates to serve as [[w:|Special Advocate]] .

====(C) Security Clearance– ====

An individual may be appointed [[w:|Special Advocate]] without regard to whether the individual possesses a security clearance on the date of the appointment.

====(D) Term and dismissal– ====

A [[w:|Special Advocate]] shall be appointed for a term of 5 years and may be fired only for good cause shown, including the demonstrated inability to qualify for an adequate security clearance.

====(E) Reappointment– ====

There shall be no limit to the number of consecutive terms served by a [[w:|Special Advocate]] . The reappointment of a [[w:|Special Advocate]] shall be made in the same manner as appointment of a [[w:|Special Advocate]] .

====(F) Acting Special Advocate– ====

If the position of [[w:|Special Advocate]] is vacant, the presiding judge of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] may appoint an Acting Special Advocate from among the qualified employees of the [[w:|Office]] . If there are no such qualified employees, the presiding judge of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] may appoint an Acting Special Advocate from the most recent list of candidates provided by the [[w:|Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board]] pursuant to subparagraph (B) . The Acting Special Advocate shall have all of the powers of a [[w:|Special Advocate]] and shall serve until a [[w:|Special Advocate]] is appointed.

===(3) Employees– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] is authorized, without regard to the civil service laws and regulations, to appoint and terminate employees of the [[w:|Office]] .

==(c) Security clearances– ==

The appropriate departments, agencies, and elements of the executive branch shall cooperate with the [[w:|Office]] , to the extent possible under existing procedures and requirements, to expeditiously provide the [[w:|Special Advocate]] and appropriate employees of the [[w:|Office]] with the security clearances necessary to carry out the duties of the [[w:|Special Advocate]] .

==(d) Duties and authorities of the Special Advocate– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] —
:(A) shall review each application to the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ;
:(B) shall review each decision of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] issued after the date of the enactment of this Act and all documents and other material relevant to such decision in a complete, unredacted form;
:(C) shall participate in a proceeding before the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] if appointed to participate by the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] under section 4(a) ;
:(D) may request to participate in a proceeding before the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] ;
:(E) shall participate in such a proceeding if such request is granted;
:(F) may request reconsideration of a decision of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] under section 4(b) ;
:(G) may appeal or seek review of a decision of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] under section 5 ; and
:(H) shall participate in such appeal or review.

===(2) Advocacy– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] shall protect individual rights by vigorously advocating before the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] , as appropriate, in support of legal interpretations that minimize the scope of surveillance and the extent of data collection and retention.

===(3) Utilization of outside counsel– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] —
:(A) may delegate to a competent outside counsel any duty or responsibility of the [[w:|Special Advocate]] with respect to participation in a matter before the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] , the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] , or the [[w:Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court of the United States]] ; and
:(B) may not delegate to outside counsel any duty or authority set out in subparagraph (A) , (B) , (D) , (F) , or (G) of paragraph (1) .

===(4) Availability of documents and material– ===

The [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] , as appropriate, shall order any agency, department, or entity to make available to the [[w:|Special Advocate]] , or appropriate outside counsel if utilized by the [[w:|Special Advocate]] under paragraph (3) , any documents or other material necessary to carry out the duties described in paragraph (1) . 

=Sec. 4. Advocacy before the FISA Court=



==(a) Appointment To participate– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] may appoint the [[w:|Special Advocate]] to participate in a [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] proceeding.

===(2) Standing– ===

If the [[w:|Special Advocate]] is appointed to participate in a [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] proceeding pursuant to paragraph (1) , the [[w:|Special Advocate]] shall have standing as a party before the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] in that proceeding.

==(b) Reconsideration of a FISA Court decision– ==



===(1) Authority to move for reconsideration– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] may move the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] to reconsider any decision of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] made after the date of the enactment of this Act by petitioning the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] not later than 30 days after the date on which all documents and materials relevant to the decision are made available to the [[w:|Special Advocate]] .

===(2) Discretion of the FISA Court– ===

The [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] shall have discretion to grant or deny a motion for reconsideration made pursuant to paragraph (1) .

==(c) Amicus curiae participation– ==



===(1) Motion by the Special Advocate– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] may file a motion with the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] to permit and facilitate participation of amicus curiae, including participation in oral argument if appropriate, in any proceeding. The [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] shall have the discretion to grant or deny such a motion.

===(2) Facilitation by the FISA Court– ===

The [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] may, sua sponte, permit and facilitate participation by amicus curiae, including participation in oral argument if appropriate, in proceedings before the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] .

===(3) Regulations– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] shall promulgate rules to provide the public with information sufficient to allow interested parties to participate as amicus curiae. 

=Sec. 5. Appellate review=



==(a) Appeal of FISA Court decisions– ==



===(1) Authority to appeal– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] may appeal any decision of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] issued after the date of the enactment of this Act not later than 90 days after the date the decision is issued, unless it would be apparent to all reasonable jurists that such decision is dictated by statute or by precedent handed down after such date of enactment.

===(2) Standing as appellant– ===

If the [[w:|Special Advocate]] appeals a decision of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] pursuant to paragraph (1) , the [[w:|Special Advocate]] shall have standing as a party before the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] in such appeal.

===(3) Mandatory review– ===

The [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] shall review any [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] decision appealed by the [[w:|Special Advocate]] and issue a decision in such appeal.

===(4) Standard of review– ===

The standards for a mandatory review of a [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] decision pursuant to paragraph (3) shall be—
:(A) de novo with respect to issues of law; and
:(B) clearly erroneous with respect to determination of facts.

===(5) Amicus curiae participation– ===



====(A) In general– ====

The [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] shall accept amicus curiae briefs from interested parties in all mandatory reviews pursuant to paragraph (3) and shall provide for amicus participation in oral argument if appropriate.

====(B) Regulations– ====

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] shall promulgate rules to provide the public with information sufficient to allow interested parties to participate as amicus curiae.

==(b) Review of FISA Court of Review decisions– ==



===(1) Authority– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] may seek a writ of certiorari from the [[w:Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court of the United States]] for review of any decision of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] .

===(2) Standing– ===

In any proceedings before the [[w:Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court of the United States]] relating to a petition of certiorari filed under paragraph (1) and any proceedings in a matter for which certiorari is granted, the [[w:|Special Advocate]] shall have standing as a party. 

=Sec. 6. Disclosure=



==(a) Requirement To disclose– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall publicly disclose—
:(1) all decisions issued by the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] after July 10, 2003, that include a significant construction or interpretation of law;
:(2) any decision of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] appealed by the [[w:|Special Advocate]] pursuant to this Act; and
:(3) any [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] decision that is issued after an appeal by the [[w:|Special Advocate]] .

==(b) Disclosure described– ==

For each disclosure required by subsection (a) with respect to a decision, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall make available to the public documents sufficient—
:(1) to identify with particularity each legal question addressed by the decision and how such question was resolved;
:(2) to describe in general terms the context in which the matter arises;
:(3) to describe the construction or interpretation of any statute, constitutional provision, or other legal authority relied on by the decision; and
:(4) to indicate whether the decision departed from any prior decision of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] .

==(c) Documents described– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall satisfy the disclosure requirements in subsection (b) by—
:(1) releasing a [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] decision in its entirety or as redacted;
:(2) releasing a summary of a [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] decision; or
:(3) releasing an application made to the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] , briefs filed before the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] , or other materials, in full or as redacted.

==(d) Extensive disclosure– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall release as much information regarding the facts and analysis contained in a decision described in subsection (a) or documents described in subsection (c) as is consistent with legitimate national security concerns.

==(e) Timing of disclosure– ==



===(1) Decisions issued prior to enactment– ===

A decision issued prior to the date of the enactment of this Act that is required to be disclosed under subsection (a)(1) shall be disclosed not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act.

===(2) FISA Court decisions– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall release [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] decisions appealed by the [[w:|Special Advocate]] not later than 30 days after the date the appeal is filed.

===(3) FISA Court of Review decisions– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall release [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] decisions appealed by the [[w:|Special Advocate]] not later than 90 days after the date the appeal is filed.

==(f) Petition by the Special Advocate– ==



===(1) Authority to petition– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] may petition the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] to order—
:(A) the public disclosure of a decision of such a Court, and documents or other material relevant to such a decision, previously designated as classified information; or
:(B) the release of an unclassified summary of such decisions and documents.

===(2) Contents of petition– ===

Each petition filed under paragraph (1) shall contain a detailed declassification proposal or a summary of the decision and documents that the [[w:|Special Advocate]] proposes to have released publicly.

===(3) Role of the Attorney General– ===



====(A) Copy of petition– ====

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] shall provide to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] a copy of each petition filed under paragraph (1) .

====(B) Opposition– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may oppose a petition filed under paragraph (1) by submitting any objections in writing to the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] , as appropriate, not later than 90 days after the date such petition was submitted.

===(4) Public availability– ===

Not less than 91 days after receiving a petition under paragraph (1) , and taking into account any objections from the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] made under paragraph (3)(B) , the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] , as appropriate, shall declassify and make readily available to the public any decision, document, or other material requested in such petition, if such decision, document, or other material pertain to a decision that contains a significant construction or interpretation of law, to the greatest extent possible, consistent with legitimate national security considerations.

===(5) Effective date– ===

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] may not file a petition under paragraph (1) until 181 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, except with respect to a decision appealed by the [[w:|Special Advocate]] . 

=Sec. 7. Annual report to Congress=



==(a) Requirement for annual report– ==

The [[w:|Special Advocate]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] an annual report on the implementation of this Act.

==(b) Contents– ==

Each annual report submitted under subsection (a) shall—
:(1) detail the activities of the [[w:|Office]] ;
:(2) provide an assessment of the effectiveness of this Act; and
:(3) propose any new legislation to improve the functioning of the [[w:|Office]] or the operation of the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] . 

=Sec. 8. Preservation of rights=

Nothing in this Act shall be construed—
:(1) to provide the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] with authority to prevent the [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court]] or [[w:Federal Judicial Center|FISA Court of Review]] from declassifying decisions or releasing information pursuant to this Act; and
:(2) to eliminate the public’s ability to secure information under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552  section 552of title 5, United States Code](commonly known as the“  Freedom of Information Act  ”)or any other provision of law.
