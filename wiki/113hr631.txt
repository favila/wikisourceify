{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 10, United States Code, to provide requirements for the contents of the Transition Assistance Program, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H26099B81584548CDB0B9A3DDB5335160}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Servicemembers’ Choice in Transition Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HD1105B6A369E42678E6B65D5E14A5619}}Sec. 2. Contents of Transition Assistance Program=



=={{anchor|HE3081504203D4F889AA7F506445698D4}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 Section 1144 of title 10, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|HD17A76607F4D4C0683352D2CAEF5A551}}(f) Program contents– ===

The program carried out by this section shall consist of at least five days of instruction as follows:
:(1) At least one day of service-specific pre-separation training, as determined by the Secretary concerned.
:(2) Not more than one day of instruction providing an overview of each of the following subjects:
::(A) Preparation for employment.
::(B) Preparation for education or career or technical training.
::(C) Preparation for entrepreneurship.
::(D) Other options determined by the Secretary concerned.
:(3) At least two days of in-depth instruction in the participants choice of the subjects described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 paragraphs (A) though (D) of paragraph (2)] .
:(4) Not more than one day of instruction in the benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] and in other subjects determined by the Secretary concerned..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H4711568D20FF4E0AA762E3ECCFC95241}}(b) Deadline for implementation– ==

The program carried out under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 section 1144 of title 10, United States Code] , shall comply with the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsection (f)] of such section, as added by subsection (a), by not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act.

=={{anchor|H641939DD868B4B0598738A3D5BB458C6}}(c) Feasibility study– ==

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall submit to theCommittees on Veterans’ Affairs of the SenateandHouse of Representativesthe results of a study carried out by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] to determine the feasibility of providing the instruction described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsection (f)(4) of section 1144 of title 10, United States Code] , as added by subsection (a), at all overseas locations where such instruction is provided by entering into a contract jointly with the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]] for the provision of such instruction.
