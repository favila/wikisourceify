{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 44, United States Code, to require preservation of certain electronic records by Federal agencies, to require a certification and reports relating to Presidential records, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H4F46BC4D0E45478BA431123BD15C67CD}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Electronic Message Preservation Act”.

={{anchor|H531AD0577BDF4A8BBA95701574E8418B}}Sec. 2. Records management=



=={{anchor|H5C9C5ED4C6024209A9A848B140AE7B6A}}(a) Requirement for Preservation of Electronic Messages– ==



==={{anchor|HCDE486CE05824500B27133A26302567B}}(1) In general– ===

Chapter 29of title 44, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|HDAA5EC66D08E46D38B2FF0E1F7D830AF}}Sec. 2911. Preservation of electronic messages and other records===



===={{anchor|H7ACAD6DD2CA94CAF978433D0FE22954C}}(a) Regulations Required– ====

Not later than 18 months after the date of the enactment of this section, the Archivist shall promulgate regulations governing Federal agency preservation of electronic messages that are determined to be records (as such term is defined under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/3301 section 3301 of this title] ). Such regulations shall, at a minimum—
:(1) require the electronic capture, management, and preservation of such electronic records in accordance with the records disposition requirements ofchapter 33 of this title;
:(2) require that such electronic records are readily accessible for retrieval through electronic searches;
:(3) establish mandatory minimum functional requirements for electronic records management systems to ensure compliance with the requirements in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2911 paragraphs (1)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2911 (2)] ;
:(4) establish a process to certify that Federal agencies’ electronic records management systems meet the functional requirements established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2911 paragraph (3)] ; and
:(5) include timelines for Federal agency compliance with the regulations that ensure compliance as expeditiously as practicable but not later than four years after the date of the enactment of this section.

===={{anchor|H3400854C003F47128DB682F949AEF6F6}}(b) Coverage of Other Electronic Records– ====

To the extent practicable, the regulations promulgated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2911 subsection (a)] shall also include requirements for the capture, management, and preservation of other electronic records.

===={{anchor|H0F1FC55287BF47E9B6892A70FD844371}}(c) Compliance by Federal Agencies– ====

Each Federal agency shall comply with the regulations promulgated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2911 subsection (a)] .

===={{anchor|H6406960A1A1E484DBA1DFB7F22930E11}}(d) Review of Regulations Required– ====

The Archivist shall periodically review and, as necessary, amend the regulations promulgated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2911 subsection (a)] .

===={{anchor|HCFED178DDEF1412CB6B23265812B3EFD}}(e) Reports on Implementation of Regulations– ====



====={{anchor|H2F550105058F4BABB3D7574B4184A16F}}(1) Agency report to archivist– =====

Not later than four years after the date of the enactment of this section, the head of each Federal agency shall submit to the Archivist a report on the agency’s compliance with the regulations promulgated under this section.

====={{anchor|H9F85013B50AF40FB95001B65B334AD0E}}(2) Archivist report to congress– =====

Not later than 90 days after receipt of all reports required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2911 paragraph (1)] , the Archivist shall submit to theCommittee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senateand theCommittee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representativesa report on Federal agency compliance with the regulations promulgated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2911 subsection (a)] ..
</blockquote>


==={{anchor|H8B94399C99494041AF71A45D4079467D}}(2) Clerical amendment– ===

The table of sections at the beginning ofchapter 29of title 44, United States Code, is amended by adding after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2910 section 2910] the following new item:

<blockquote>__NOTOC__
2911. Preservation of electronic messages and other records..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H0821BCAD3A044AA1AC21B7ED97A5CFFD}}(b) Definitions– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 2901] of title 44, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) by striking“and”at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2901 paragraph (14)] ; and
:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2901 paragraph (15)] and inserting the following new paragraphs:

<blockquote>
::(15) the term electronic messages means electronic mail and other electronic messaging systems that are used for purposes of communicating between individuals; and
::(16) the term electronic records management system means software designed to manage electronic records, including by—
:::(A) categorizing and locating records;
:::(B) ensuring that records are retained as long as necessary;
:::(C) identifying records that are due for disposition; and
:::(D) ensuring the storage, retrieval, and disposition of records..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H8EE2892A84F849CFAD8755CB569B9BD0}}Sec. 3. Presidential records=



=={{anchor|H2D7FCFC50FC84CCEA1A9EBD47D492090}}(a) Additional Regulations Relating to Presidential Records– ==



==={{anchor|H397E067A293141C882FB2F934410A0A9}}(1) In general– ===

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 2206] of title 44, United States Code, is amended—
:(A) by striking“and”at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2206 paragraph (3)] ;
:(B) by striking the period at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2206 paragraph (4)] and inserting“; and”; and
:(C) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(5) provisions for establishing standards necessary for the economical and efficient management of electronic Presidential records during the President’s term of office, including—
:::(A) records management controls necessary for the capture, management, and preservation of electronic messages;
:::(B) records management controls necessary to ensure that electronic messages are readily accessible for retrieval through electronic searches; and
:::(C) a process to certify the electronic records management system to be used by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] for the purposes of complying with the requirements in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2206 subparagraphs (A)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2206 (B)] ..
</blockquote>


==={{anchor|H2E259DC919DD4C48A977573579791086}}(2) Definitions– ===

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 2201] of title 44, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraphs:

<blockquote>
:(6) The term electronic messages has the meaning given that term under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2901 section 2901(15)] ] of this title.
:(7) The term electronic records management system has the meaning given that term under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2901 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 2901(16)] of this title] ..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H090C85E915384A8D807DA5C9E9838523}}(b) Certification of President’s Management of Presidential Records– ==



==={{anchor|H8D47484316A84CBE94A1F72224AE92E9}}(1) Certification required– ===

Chapter 22of title 44, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H0F0C97BC1FDE4FEBAFF38BE708C5EFF9}}Sec. 2208. Certification of the President’s management of Presidential records===



===={{anchor|H799177FB62F84344A7EA8B05D7E146E2}}(a) Annual Certification– ====

The Archivist shall annually certify whether the electronic records management controls established by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] meet requirements under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2203 sections 2203(a)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2206 2206(5) of this title] .

===={{anchor|HA4FB48D7EF1C4D8DAF2F428E618F3085}}(b) Report to Congress– ====

The Archivist shall report annually to theCommittee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senateand theCommittee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representativeson the status of the certification..
</blockquote>


==={{anchor|H354926E155C649F5A699BC9BC8B56980}}(2) Clerical amendment– ===

The table of sections at the beginning ofchapter 22of title 44, United States Code, as amended by subsection (a)(4), is further amended by adding at the end the following new item:

<blockquote>__NOTOC__
2208. Certification of the President’s management of Presidential records..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H3B67F3F829004EC1BFD9F5296CDBC0AB}}(c) Report to Congress– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2203 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 2203(f)] of title 44, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(4) One year following the conclusion of a President’s term of office, or if a President serves consecutive terms one year following the conclusion of the last term, the Archivist shall submit to theCommittee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senateand theCommittee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representativesa report on—
::(A) the volume and format of electronic Presidential records deposited into that President’s Presidential archival depository; and
::(B) whether the electronic records management controls of that [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] met the requirements under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2203 sections 2203(a)] ] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2206 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 2206(5)] of this title] ..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HB2466390E5D9445FAB4747978CA8DB70}}(d) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall take effect one year after the date of the enactment of this Act.
