{{Center|AN ACT}}

 To amend title 49, United States Code, to direct the Assistant Secretary of Homeland Security (Transportation Security Administration) to provide expedited air passenger screening to severely injured or disabled members of the Armed Forces and severely injured or disabled veterans, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Helping Heroes Fly Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Operations center program for severely injured or disabled members of the Armed Forces and severely injured or disabled veterans=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/449 Subchapter I ofchapter 449of title 49, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 44927. Expedited screening for severely injured or disabled members of the Armed Forces and severely injured or disabled veterans==



===(a) Passenger screening– ===

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]], in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]], and organizations that advocate on behalf of members of the Armed Forces and veterans, including organizations that advocate on behalf of severely injured or disabled members of the Armed Forces and severely injured or disabled veterans, shall develop and implement a process to facilitate the ease of travel and to the extent possible provide expedited passenger screening services for severely injured or disabled members of the Armed Forces, severely injured or disabled veterans, and their accompanying family members or nonmedical attendants. Such process shall be designed to protect the privacy of the individual being screened to the maximum extent practicable.

===(b) Operations center– ===

As part of the process required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44927 subsection (a)], the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]]shall maintain an operations center to provide support and facilitate the movement of severely injured or disabled members of the Armed Forces and severely injured or disabled veterans through screening prior to boarding a passenger aircraft operated by an air carrier or foreign air carrier in air transportation or intrastate air transportation. Such operations center shall be operational at all times.

===(c) Protocols– ===

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]]shall—
:(1) establish and publish protocols, in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]], and organizations that advocate on behalf of members of the Armed Forces and veterans, including organizations that advocate on behalf of severely injured or disabled members of the Armed Forces and severely injured or disabled veterans, under which a severely injured or disabled member of the Armed Forces or severely injured or disabled veteran, or the family member or other representative of such a member or veteran, may contact the operations center maintained under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44927 subsection (b)]and request expedited screening services described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44927 subsection (a)]for the member or veteran; and
:(2) upon receipt of such a request, require such operations center to notify the appropriate Federal security director of the request to facilitate the expedited passenger screening services described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44927 subsection (a)]for the member or veteran.

===(d) Training– ===

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]]shall integrate training on the protocols established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44927 subsection (c)]into the training provided to all employees who will provide the screening services described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44927 subsection (a)].

===(e) Rule of construction– ===

Nothing in this section affects the authority of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]]to require additional screening of a severely injured or disabled member of the Armed Forces, a severely injured or disabled veteran, or their accompanying family members or nonmedical attendants, if intelligence, law enforcement, or other information indicates that additional screening is necessary.

===(f) Report– ===

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this section, and annually thereafter, the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on the implementation of this section. Each such report shall include each of the following:
:(1) Information on the training provided under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44927 subsection (d)].
:(2) Information on the consultations between the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]]and organizations that advocate on behalf of members of the Armed Forces and veterans as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44927 subsection (a)].
:(3) The number of people who accessed the operations center during the period covered by the report.
:(4) Such other information as the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]]determines is appropriate. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections at the beginning of such chapter is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44926 section 44926]the following new item:

<blockquote>  44927. Expedited screening for severely injured or disabled members of the Armed Forces and severely injured or disabled veterans.  . 
</blockquote>
