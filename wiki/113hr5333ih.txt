{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to ensure that a service animal of a patient receiving inpatient medical care at a medical facility of the Department of Veterans Affairs is able to access the room of the patient.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Expanding Access for Veterans’ Service Animals Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Access of service animals at medical facilities of Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) Sense of Congress– ==

It is the sense of Congress that—
:(1) certified therapeutic animals should be provided the same access to facilities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]as service animals; and
:(2) to ensure such access, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]should create and implement a certification process for therapeutic animals that mitigate the effects of a medically diagnosed mental health condition.

==(b) Access of service animals to medical facilities– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/901  Section 901(f)of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating paragraph (2) as paragraph (3); and
:(2) by inserting after paragraph (1) the following new paragraph (2):

<blockquote> 
::(2) 
:::(A) Except as provided by subparagraph (B), the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may not prohibit a covered service dog, or any other service animal that is allowed to access medical facilities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], of a patient who is receiving inpatient care at a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]from accessing the room of the patient, including with respect to staying overnight.
:::(B) In carrying out subparagraph (A), the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may prescribe regulations to ensure the safety and health of employees of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], patients, and the public, including by requiring that a patient maintain responsibility for the covered service dog or service animal. . 
</blockquote>
