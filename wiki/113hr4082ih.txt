{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to extend the work opportunity tax credit and to provide such credit for hiring long-term unemployed individuals.   

=Section 1. Extension of work opportunity tax credit and provision of credit for hiring long-term unemployed individuals=



==(a) Extension of work opportunity tax credit– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51  Section 51(c)(4)(B)of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986]is amended by striking“December 31, 2013”and inserting“December 31, 2015”.

==(b) Credit allowed for hiring long-Term unemployed individuals– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51  Section 51(d)(1)of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986]is amended by striking“or”at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51 subparagraph (H)], by striking the period at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51 subparagraph (I)]and inserting“, or”, and by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote> 
:(J) a qualified long-term unemployed individual. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Qualified long-term unemployed individual– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51 Section 51(d) of such Code]is amended by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51 paragraph (14)], by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51 paragraphs (11), (12), and (13)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51 paragraphs (12), (13), and (14)], respectively, and by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51 paragraph (10)]the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 

====(11) Qualified long-term unemployed individual– ====

The term qualified long-term unemployed individual means any individual who is certified by the designated local agency as—
:(A) an individual in receipt of unemployment compensation under State or Federal law during the entire 26-week period ending on the hiring date, or
:(B) an individual unemployed for such period who would have received such compensation for such period but for having exhausted the right to such compensation. . 
</blockquote>


===(3) Credit limited to $1,000 for hiring of each long-term unemployed individual– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/51 Section 51(b)(3) of such Code]is amended by inserting“($2,500 in the case of any qualified long-term unemployed individual)”.

==(c) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to individuals who begin work for the employer after December 31, 2013.
