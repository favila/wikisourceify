{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

 Authorizing expenditures by the Senate Committee on Indian Affairs.   

= =

That, in carrying out its powers, duties, and functions imposed by section 105 of S. Res. 4, agreed to February 4, 1977 (95th Congress) , and in exercising the authority conferred on it by that section, the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Indian Affairs|Committee on Indian Affairs]] is authorized from October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2014, and October 1, 2014, through February 28, 2015, in its discretion (1) to make expenditures from the contingent fund of the Senate, (2) to employ personnel, and (3) with the prior consent of the Government department or agency concerned and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Rules and Administration|Committee on Rules and Administration]] , to use on a reimbursable, or non-reimbursable, basis the services of personnel of any such department or agency. 

=2. =


:(a) For the period October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2014, expenses of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Indian Affairs|committee]] under this resolution shall not exceed $2,009,768.00, of which amount (1) not to exceed $20,000 may be expended for the procurement of the services of individual consultants, or organizations thereof (as authorized by section 202(i) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946 , as amended), and (2) not to exceed $20,000 may be expended for the training of the professional staff of such [[w:United States Senate Committee on Indian Affairs|committee]] (under procedures specified by section 202(j) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946 ).
:(b) For the period October 1, 2014, through February 28, 2015, expenses of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Indian Affairs|committee]] under this resolution shall not exceed $837,403.00, of which amount (1) not to exceed $20,000 may be expended for the procurement of the services of individual consultants, or organizations thereof (as authorized by section 202(i) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946 , as amended), and (2) not to exceed $20,000 may be expended for the training of professional staff of such [[w:United States Senate Committee on Indian Affairs|committee]] (under procedures specified by section 202(j) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946 ). 

=3. =

The [[w:United States Senate Committee on Indian Affairs|committee]] shall report its findings, together with such recommendations for legislation as it deems advisable, to the Senate at the earliest practicable date, but not later than February 28, 2015. 

=4. =

Expenses of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Indian Affairs|committee]] under this resolution shall be paid from the contingent fund of the Senate upon vouchers approved by the Chairwoman of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Indian Affairs|committee]] , except that vouchers shall not be required (1) for the disbursement of the salaries of employees paid at an annual rate, or (2) for the payment of telecommunications provided by the [[w:United States Senate|Office of the Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, United States Senate]] , or (3) for the payment of stationery supplies purchased through the [[w:United States Senate|Keeper of the Stationery, United States Senate]] , or (4) for payments to the [[w:United States Senate|Postmaster, United States Senate]] , or (5) for the payment of metered charges on copying equipment provided by the [[w:United States Senate|Office of the Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, United States Senate]] , or (6) for the payment of [[w:United States Senate|Senate Recording and Photographic Services]] , or (7) for payment of franked and mass mail costs by the [[w:United States Senate|Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, United States Senate]] . 

=5. =

 There are authorized such sums as may be necessary for agency contributions related to the compensation of employees of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Indian Affairs|committee]]  from October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2014, and October 1, 2014, through February 28, 2015, to be paid from theAppropriations account for Expenses of Inquiries and Investigations.
