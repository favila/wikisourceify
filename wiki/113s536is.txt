{{Center|A BILL}}

To require a study and report by the Comptroller General of the United States regarding the costs of Federal regulations.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Regulation Costs to America Act” .

=Sec. 2. GAO study on the cost of federal regulations=



==(a) In general– ==

The [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]] shall conduct an annual study of the total costs of Federal regulations.

==(b) Requirement– ==

In conducting the study required under subsection (a), the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]] shall use a methodology substantially similar to the methodology used in conducting the study described in the report entitled “The Impact of Regulatory Costs on Small Firms” (September 2010), prepared on behalf of the [[w:Small Business Administration|Office of Advocacy of the Small Business Administration]] .

==(c) Report– ==

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, and every year thereafter, the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the findings of the study conducted under subsection (a), which shall include an estimate of the total annual costs of Federal regulations by agency, as such term is defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 section 551of title 5, United States Code] .

==(d) Funding– ==

The [[w:Government Accountability Office|General Accountability Office]] shall carry out this section using unobligated funds otherwise made available to the [[w:Government Accountability Office|General Accountability Office]] .

==(e) Sense of Congress– ==

It is the sense of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that no additional funds should be made available to carry out this section.
