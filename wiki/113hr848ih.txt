{{Center|A BILL}}

To direct the Attorney General to make grants to States to develop systems to retrieve firearms from armed prohibited persons.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Armed Prohibited Persons Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Grants authorized=

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may make grants to States to develop and operate systems to retrieve firearms from armed prohibited persons.

=Sec. 3. Applications=

A State seeking a grant under this Act shall submit an application to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] at such time and containing such information as the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may reasonably require, including assurances that—
:(1) any system that the State operates will provide an armed prohibited person with a reasonable opportunity to lawfully dispose of such person’s firearm before the State takes any action to retrieve such a firearm;
:(2) the State will contribute pertinent information to the national instant criminal background check system established under section 103(b) of the Brady Handgun Violence Prevention Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 18 U.S.C. 922 note] ); and
:(3) the State has a plan for the continued operation and maintenance of the system or proposed system to retrieve firearms from armed prohibited persons.

=Sec. 4. Definitions=

In this Act:
:(1) The term armed prohibited person means a person—
::(A) who possesses a firearm;
::(B) who lawfully purchased such firearm in accordance with all applicable Federal and State law; and
::(C) whose possession of such firearm, because of an act or ommission committed by such person, became unlawful under applicable Federal and State law (including [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(g) of title 18, United States Code] ).
:(2) The term firearm has the meaning given the term in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 section 921(a)(3) of title 18, United States Code] .
:(3) The term State means each of the several States, the District of Columbia, and each commonwealth, territory, or possession of the United States.

=Sec. 5. Authorization of appropriations=

There is authorized to be appropriatedsuch sums as may be necessaryto carry out this Actfor fiscal years 2014 through 2018.
