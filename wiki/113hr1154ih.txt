{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Clean Air Act to eliminate the exemption for aggregation of emissions from oil and gas sources, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Bringing Reductions to Energy’s Airborne Toxic Health Effects Act”or the“BREATHE Act”. 

=Sec. 2. Repeal of exemption for aggregation of emissions from oil and gas sources=

   Section 112(n) of the Clean Air Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7412 42 U.S.C. 7412(n)])is amended by striking paragraph (4) . 

=Sec. 3. Hydrogen sulfide as a hazardous air pollutant=

The [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]] shall—
:(1) not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, issue a final rule adding hydrogen sulfide to the list of hazardous air pollutants under  section 112(b) of the Clean Air Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7412 42 U.S.C. 7412(b)]); and
:(2) not later than 365 days after a final rule under paragraph (1) is issued, revise the list under  section 112(c) of such Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7412 42 U.S.C. 7412(c)] )to include categories and subcategories of major sources and area sources of hydrogen sulfide, including oil and gas wells.
