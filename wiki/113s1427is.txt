{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 11 of the United States Code to clarify the rule allowing discharge as a nonpriority claim of governmental claims arising from the disposition of farm assets under chapter 12 bankruptcies.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Family Farmer Bankruptcy Clarification Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Clarification of rule allowing discharge to governmental claims arising from the disposition of farm assets under chapter 12 bankruptcies=



==(a) In general– ==

Subchapter II ofchapter 12of title 11, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 1232. Claim by a governmental unit based on the disposition of property used in a farming operation==


:(a) Any unsecured claim of a governmental unit against the debtor or the estate that arises before the filing of the petition, or that arises after the filing of the petition and before the debtor’s discharge under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1228 section 1228] , as a result of the sale, transfer, exchange, or other disposition of any property used in the debtor’s farming operation—
::(1) shall be treated as an unsecured claim arising before the date on which the petition is filed;
::(2) shall not be entitled to priority under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/507 section 507] ;
::(3) shall be provided for under a plan; and
::(4) shall be discharged in accordance with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1228 section 1228] .
:(b) For purposes of applying sections [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1228 1225(a)(4)] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1228 1228(b)(2)] , and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1229 1229(b)(1)] to a claim described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1232 subsection (a) of this section] , the amount that would be paid on such claim if the estate of the debtor were liquidated in a case underchapter 7 of this titleshall be the amount that would be paid by the estate in achapter 7case if the claim were an unsecured claim arising before the date on which the petition was filed and were not entitled to priority under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/507 section 507] .
:(c) For purposes of applying [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/532 sections 523(a)] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1228 1228(a)(2)] , and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1228 1228(c)(2)] to a claim described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1232 subsection (a) of this section] , the claim shall not be treated as a claim of a kind specified in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/532 section 523(a)(1)] .
:(d) 
::(1) A governmental unit may file a proof of claim for a claim described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/532 subsection (a)] that arises after the date on which the petition is filed.
::(2) If a debtor files a tax return after the filing of the petition for a period in which a claim described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/532 subsection (a)] arises, and the claim relates to the tax return, the debtor shall serve notice of the claim on the governmental unit charged with the responsibility for the collection of the tax at the address and in the manner designated in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/505 section 505(b)(1)] . Notice under this paragraph shall state that the debtor has filed a petition under this chapter, state the name and location of the court in which the case under this chapter is pending, state the amount of the claim, and include a copy of the filed tax return and documentation supporting the calculation of the claim.
::(3) If notice of a claim has been served on the governmental unit in accordance with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1232 paragraph (2)] , the governmental unit may file a proof of claim not later than 180 days after the date on which such notice was served. If the governmental unit has not filed a timely proof of the claim, the debtor or trustee may file proof of the claim that is consistent with the notice served under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1232 paragraph (2)] . If a proof of claim is filed by the debtor or trustee under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1232 this paragraph] , the governmental unit may not amend the proof of claim.
::(4) A claim filed under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1232 this subsection] shall be determined and shall be allowed under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/502 subsection (a), (b), or (c) of section 502] , or disallowed under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/502 subsection (d) or (e) of section 502] , in the same manner as if the claim had arisen immediately before the date of the filing of the petition..
</blockquote>

==(c) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to any bankruptcy case that—
:(1) is pending on the date of enactment of this Actand relating to which an order of discharge under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/1228 section 1228of title 11, United States Code] , has not been entered; or
:(2) commences on or after the date of enactment of this Act.
