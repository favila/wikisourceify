{{Center|A BILL}}

 To require debarment of persons convicted of fraudulent use of“Made in America”labels.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Prohibiting False“Made in America”Claims Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Debarment required of persons convicted of fraudulent use of“Made in America”labels=



==(a) Debarment required– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f Subsection (a) of section 2410f of title 10, United States Code], is amended by striking“the Secretary shall”and all that follows through the period and inserting“the person shall be debarred from contracting with the [[w:|Department of Defense]]unless the [[w:|Secretary]]waives the debarment under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f subsection (b)].”.

==(b) Waiver authority and notification requirement– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f Section 2410f of such title]is further amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f subsection (b)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f subsection (d)]; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f subsection (a)]the following new subsections:

<blockquote> 

====(b) Waiver for national security– ====

The [[w:|Secretary]]may waive a debarment required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f subsection (a)]if the [[w:|Secretary]]determines that the exercise of such a waiver would be in the national security interests of the United States. 

====(c) Notification– ====

The [[w:|Secretary]]shall notify the congressional defense committees annually, not later than March 1 of each year, of any exercise of the waiver authority under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f subsection (b)]. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Technical amendments– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f Section 2410f of such title]is further amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f subsection (a)], by inserting“ Debarment required.— ”after“(a)”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2410f subsection (d)], as redesignated by subsection (b) , by inserting“ Definition.— ”before“In this section”.
