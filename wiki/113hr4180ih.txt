{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to permit rollovers from health savings accounts to Medicare Advantage MSAs.   

=Section 1. HSA rollover to Medicare Advantage MSA=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 Paragraph (2) ofsection 138(b)of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986]is amended by striking“or”at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 subparagraph (A)], by adding“or”at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 subparagraph (B)], and by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote> 
:(C) a HSA rollover contribution described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 subsection (c)(5)], . 
</blockquote>


==(b) HSA rollover contribution– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 Subsection (c) of section 138 of such Code]is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 

===(5) Rollover contribution– ===

An amount is described in this paragraph as a rollover contribution if it meets the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 subparagraphs (A)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 (B)].

====(A) In general– ====

The requirements of this subparagraph are met in the case of an amount paid or distributed from a health savings account to the account beneficiary to the extent the amount received is paid into a Medicare Advantage MSA of such beneficiary not later than the 60th day after the day on which the beneficiary receives the payment or distribution.

====(B) Limitation– ====

This paragraph shall not apply to any amount described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 subparagraph (A)]received by an individual from a health savings account if, at any time during the 1-year period ending on the day of such receipt, such individual received any other amount described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 subparagraph (A)]from a health savings account which was not includible in the individual’s gross income because of the application of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 section 223(f)(5)(A)]. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Conforming amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 Subparagraph (A) of section 223(f)(5) of such Code]is amended by inserting“, a Medicare Advantage MSA (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/138 section 138(b)]),”before“or a health savings account”.

==(d) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2013.
