{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 10, United States Code, to extend military commissary and exchange store privileges, without time-period limitation, to members of the Armed Forces who are involuntarily separated with a service-connected disability and also to extend such privileges to their dependents.

={{anchor|H3E3637ECEF6642F1A8D3C162633DABFB}}Section 1. Commissary and exchange store privileges for involuntarily separated members with a service-connected disability and for their dependents=



=={{anchor|HE9E4966998724CE1B24C93572FA9F21D}}(a) Extension of privileges– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1146 Section 1146 of title 10, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|HA90475248D8046438D21E32F993B8363}}(d) Members involuntarily separated with service-Connected disability; dependents– ===


:(1) A member of the armed forces who is involuntarily separated from active duty or the Selected Reserve and who has a service-connected disability shall be permitted to use commissary and exchange stores on the same basis as a member of the armed forces entitled to retired or retainer pay.
:(2) A dependent of a member described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1146 paragraph (1)] shall be permitted to use commissary and exchange stores on the same basis as a dependent of a member of the armed forces entitled to retired or retainer pay..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H60FF9CBBF4DF4A2C8131E789FAE02F8C}}(b) Implementation– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] shall ensure that[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1146 subsection (d) of section 1146 of title 10, United States Code] , as added by subsection (a), is fully operational before the end of the 90-day period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act.
