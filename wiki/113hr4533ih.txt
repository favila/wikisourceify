{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Inspector General Act of 1978 to provide for the Inspector General of the National Security Agency to be appointed by the President, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   NSA Internal Watchdog Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Inspector General of the National Security Agency=



==(a) Elevation of Inspector General status– ==

The  Inspector General Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. App.)is amended—
:(1) in section 8G(a)(2) , by striking“the National Security Agency,”; and
:(2) in section 12 —
::(A) in paragraph (1) , by inserting“the [[w:|National Security Agency]],”after“the [[w:Federal Emergency Management Agency|Federal Emergency Management Agency]],”; and
::(B) in paragraph (2) , by inserting“the [[w:|National Security Agency]],”after“the [[w:National Aeronautics and Space Administration|National Aeronautics and Space Administration]],”.

==(b) Date of appointment– ==

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]shall nominate a person for appointment, by and with the advice and consent of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]], as [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]under  section 3(a) of the Inspector General Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. App.)consistent with the amendments made by subsection (a) .

==(c) Transition rule– ==

An individual serving as [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]on the date of the enactment of this Act pursuant to an appointment made under  section 8G of the Inspector General Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. App.)—
:(1) may continue so serving until the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]makes an appointment under section 3(a) of such Act with respect to the [[w:|National Security Agency]]consistent with the amendments made by subsection (a) ; and
:(2) shall, while serving under paragraph (1) , remain subject to the provisions of section 8G of such Act that, immediately before the date of the enactment of this Act, applied with respect to the Inspector General of the [[w:|National Security Agency]]and suffer no reduction in pay. 

=Sec. 3. Annual review of mechanisms for reporting employee or contractor complaints=

   Section 8H(g)(1) of the Inspector General Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. App.)is amended by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote> 
:(E) In the case of the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]], a review of the mechanisms for submitting complaints that are available to an employee of or contractor to the [[w:|National Security Agency]]and any recommendations of the Inspector General for improving such mechanisms. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 4. Special provisions concerning the National Security Agency=

The  Inspector General Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. App.)is amended by inserting after section 8J the following new section:

<blockquote> 

=Sec. 8K. Special provisions concerning the National Security Agency=



==(a) General counsel to the Inspector General– ==



===(1) In general– ===

There is a [[w:|General Counsel]]to the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]], who shall be appointed by the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]].

===(2) Duties– ===

The [[w:Office of General Counsel|General Counsel]]to the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]shall—
:(A) serve as the chief legal officer of the [[w:|Office of the Inspector General of the National Security Agency]];
:(B) provide legal services only to the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]];
:(C) prescribe professional rules of ethics and responsibilities for employees and officers of, and contractors to, the [[w:|National Security Agency]];
:(D) perform such functions as the [[w:|Inspector General]]may prescribe; and
:(E) serve at the discretion of the [[w:|Inspector General]].

===(3) Office of the General Counsel– ===

There is an [[w:|Office of the General Counsel]]to the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]. The [[w:|Inspector General]]may appoint to the Office to serve as staff of the [[w:|General Counsel]]such legal counsel as the [[w:|Inspector General]]considers appropriate.

==(b) Testimony– ==



===(1) Authority to compel– ===

The [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]is authorized to require by subpoena the attendance and testimony of former employees of the [[w:|National Security Agency]]or contractors, former contractors, or former detailees to the [[w:|National Security Agency]]as necessary in the performance of functions assigned to the [[w:|Inspector General]]by this Act.

===(2) Refusal to obey– ===

A subpoena issued under this subsection, in the case of contumacy or refusal to obey, shall be enforceable by order of any appropriate United States district court.

===(3) Notification– ===

The [[w:|Inspector General]]shall notify the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]7 days before issuing any subpoena under this section.

==(c) Prohibitions on investigations for national security reasons– ==



===(1) Evaluations of prohibitions– ===

Not later than 7 days after the date on which the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]receives notice or a statement under section 8G(d)(2)(C) of the reasons the [[w:|Secretary of Defense]]is prohibiting the [[w:|Inspector General]]from initiating, carrying out, or completing any audit or investigation, the Inspector General shall submit to the [[w:United States House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence|Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence]]and the [[w:United States House Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee on Intelligence]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services of the Senate]]an evaluation of such notice or such statement.

===(2) Inclusion in semi-annual report– ===

The [[w:|Inspector General]]shall include in the semiannual report prepared by the [[w:|Inspector General]]in accordance with section 5(a) a description of the instances in which the [[w:|Secretary of Defense]]prohibited the [[w:|Inspector General]]from initiating, carrying out, or completing any audit or investigation during the period covered by such report.

==(d) Standard for audits and investigations– ==

In carrying out any audit or investigation of a surveillance or data collection program, the [[w:|Inspector General]]shall—
:(1) assess the impact of such program on civil rights and civil liberties;
:(2) assess the effectiveness and use, including any improper or illegal use, of such program; and
:(3) make any recommendations the [[w:|Inspector General]]considers appropriate to improve the protection of civil rights and civil liberties in the operation of such program.

==(e) Availability of reports– ==



===(1) Availability– ===

Each report to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]or a committee of Congress by the [[w:|Inspector General]]shall be made available to all Members of Congress.

===(2) Member of Congress defined– ===

In this subsection, the term“Member of Congress”means a Senator, a Member of the House of Representatives, or a Delegate or Resident Commissioner to the Congress. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 5. Audit of surveillance programs and background investigations=



==(a) Audit– ==

The [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]appointed under  section 3(a) of the Inspector General Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. App.)consistent with the amendments made by section 2(a) of this Act shall perform a comprehensive audit of—
:(1) the effectiveness and use, including any improper or illegal use, of surveillance and data collection programs of the [[w:|National Security Agency]], including programs conducted pursuant to sections 501 and 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1861 50 U.S.C. 1861] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1881a 1881a]);
:(2) the interactions between the [[w:|National Security Agency]]and the court established under  section 103(a) of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1803 50 U.S.C. 1803(a)]), including the accuracy of the information provided to such court by the [[w:|Agency]]and the compliance of the [[w:|Agency]]with orders of such court; and
:(3) the process for conducting background investigations of persons for purposes of employment or potential employment by the [[w:|National Security Agency]]or for receiving access to classified information.

==(b) Report– ==

Not later than 180 days after the date on which the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]is appointed under  section 3(a) of the Inspector General Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. App.)consistent with the amendments made by section 2(a) of this Act , the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]shall submit to the [[w:United States House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence|Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee on Intelligence of the Senate]]a report containing the results of the audit conducted under subsection (a) .

==(c) Rule of construction– ==

Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to alter the responsibility of the [[w:|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]]to conduct audits or investigations of the surveillance programs of the [[w:|National Security Agency]]and the background investigation process for employment or access to classified information on an ongoing basis in accordance with the  Inspector General Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. App.).
