{{Center|A BILL}}

 To prohibit the closure or reduced operation of military commissary stores and exchange stores before January 1, 2017.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Save our Military Shopping Benefits Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Prohibition on closure or reduced operation of commissary stores and exchange stores=



==(a) Prohibition– ==

Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2482 subsections (c)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2482 (d) ofsection 2482of title 10, United States Code], or any other provision of law, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]may not—
:(1) close any commissary store or exchange store located within the United States; or
:(2) develop or implement any plan that would result in the closure of all commissaries located in the United States as part of a cost-saving effort.

==(b) Definition of close– ==

In this section, the term close includes any action that has the effect of—
:(1) reducing the operating hours or size of a commissary store or exchange store below fiscal year 2014 levels; or
:(2) changing the location of a commissary store or exchange store.

==(c) Exception– ==

  Subsection (a) does not apply to a change in the size or location of a commissary store or exchange store if the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]determines that the change is required for upgrading or modifying an existing structure for safety or health reasons.

==(d) Duration– ==

The prohibition imposed by subsection (a) expires January 1, 2017.
