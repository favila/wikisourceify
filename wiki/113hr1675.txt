{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to permit health plans without a deductible for prenatal, labor and delivery, and postpartum care to be treated as high deductible plans with respect to health savings accounts.

={{anchor|H357A1C413F1C4AFA83D96AA2166C3732}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Ensuring Women’s Access to Free-Market Healthcare Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H4B45737300234219959FF93BA8B8C1C0}}Sec. 2. HSA high deductible plan safe harbor for absence of prenatal, labor and delivery, and postpartum care deductible=



=={{anchor|HEA961FBBFFDE48DF9E7454BCEFEAE4E1}}(a) In general– ==

 Paragraph (2) of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 223(c)] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986is amended by redesignating subparagraph (D)as subparagraph (E)and by inserting after subparagraph (C)the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H416C9F4F4DBC40B6B05C72B7C78FC9A8}}(D) Safe harbor for absence of prenatal, labor and delivery, and postpartum care deductible– ===



===={{anchor|HF470A1B9F6ED4837A3FC6BF791CEE496}}(i) In general– ====

A plan shall not fail to be treated as a high deductible health plan by reason of failing to have a deductible for prenatal, labor and delivery, and postpartum care.

===={{anchor|H838E51144B06430EB14F02683F2F8628}}(ii) Prenatal, labor and delivery, and postpartum– ====

The [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] , in consultation with the American Academy of Pediatrics and American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists, shall by regulation define prenatal, labor and delivery, and postpartum care for purposes of this subparagraph..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HA5F0A55F4961403C8F0D8C04E02B61DF}}(b) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by subsection (a)shall apply to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2013.
