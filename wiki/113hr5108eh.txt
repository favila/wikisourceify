 To establish the Law School Clinic Certification Program of the United States Patent and Trademark Office, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. USPTO Law School Clinic Certification Program=



==(a) Establishment– ==

The [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Law School Clinic Certification Program of the United States Patent and Trademark Office]], as implemented by the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Office]], is established as a program entitled the“  [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Law School Clinic Certification Program]] ”. The [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Program]]shall allow students enrolled in a participating law school’s clinic to practice patent and trademark law before the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Office]]by drafting, filing, and prosecuting patent or trademark applications, or both, on a pro-bono basis for clients that qualify for assistance from the law school’s clinic. The [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Director]]shall establish regulations and procedures for application to and participation in the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Program]]. All law schools accredited by the American Bar Association are eligible for participation in the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Program]], and shall be examined for acceptance using identical criteria established by the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Director]]. The [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Program]]shall be in effect for the 10-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act.

==(b) Report on the program– ==

The [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Director]]shall, not later than the last day of the 2-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act, submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on the Judiciary|Committees on the Judiciary of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary|Senate]]a report on the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Program]], describing the number of law schools and law students participating in the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Program]], the work done through the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Program]], the benefits of the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Program]], and any recommendations of the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Director]]for modifications to the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Program]].

==(c) Definitions– ==

In this section:

===(1) Office– ===

The term“Office”means the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|United States Patent and Trademark Office]].

===(2) Program– ===

The term“Program”means the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Law School Clinic Certification Program]]established in subsection (a).

===(3) Director– ===

The term“Director”means the [[w:Patent and Trademark Office/Under Secretary for Intellectual Property|Under Secretary of Commerce for Intellectual Property and Director of the United States Patent and Trademark Office]].    Passed the House of Representatives September 15, 2014. Karen L. Haas, Clerk.
