{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to increase the number of graduate medical education residency positions at hospitals administered by the Secretary of Veterans Affairs.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Underserved Veterans Access to Health Care Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Increase of graduate medical education residency positions=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302  Section 7302of title 38, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 
:(e) 
::(1) In carrying out this section, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall establish medical residency programs, or ensure a sufficient number of residency positions in medical residency programs, at medical facilities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines—
:::(A) are experiencing a shortage of physicians; and
:::(B) are located in communities that are designated as a health professional shortage area (as defined in  section 332 of the Public Health Service Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/254e 42 U.S.C. 254e] )).
::(2) In carrying out[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302 paragraph (1)], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall—
:::(A) determine the specialty of health care professionals that a location is experiencing a shortage of, including with respect to primary care and mental health;
:::(B) allocate the residency positions under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302 paragraph (1)]based on such determination; and
:::(C) notify [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]of the information used by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]to make such determination. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Five-Year goal– ==

During the five-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall establish not fewer than 2,000 graduate medical education residency positions pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302  section 7302(e)of title 38, United States Code], as added by subsection (a) . In establishing such positions, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall give priority to medical facilities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]that—
:(1) as of the date of the enactment of this Act, do not have medical residency programs; and
:(2) are located in communities that have a high concentration of veterans.
