{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Congressional Accountability Act of 1995 to provide enhanced enforcement authority for occupational safety and health protections applicable to the legislative branch, to provide whistleblower protections and other antidiscrimation protections for employees of the legislative branch, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HC53638B899E8444980AF8B5D3E107341}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Congress Leads by Example Act of 2013”.

=={{anchor|HF0ADEBEC81884C1F9500D0BA535B1A79}}(c) Prohibiting Retaliation– ==



==={{anchor|H118CC0F538D64AA39BF5F72C28D5CF36}}(1) In general– ===

 Section 215 of such Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 2 U.S.C. 1341] )is amended—
:(A) by redesignating subsections (d), (e), (f), and (g)as subsections (e), (f), (g), and (h); and
:(B) by inserting after subsection (c)the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

====={{anchor|HEE67D8F4DFC14CA6A4131C422A25BB7F}}(d) Prohibiting retaliation– =====



======{{anchor|HA6AD619658D34810BEA28CBDD19A14E9}}(1) In general– ======

An employing office may not discharge or in any manner discriminate against any covered employee because such employee has requested the General Counsel to take any action authorized under this section, or has instituted or caused to be instituted, or has testified or is about to testify in, any proceeding that arises from the application of this section to the employing office, or because of the exercise by such employee on behalf of himself or others of any right provided under this section.

======{{anchor|HA49720AFE5794996B34850E2AAF8D66C}}(2) Enforcement– ======



======={{anchor|H6B114293720C4E19BE9626B104F5CA3C}}(A) Charge filed with general counsel– =======

Any covered employee who believes that he has been discharged or otherwise discriminated against by an employing office in violation of paragraph (1)may file a charge against the employing office with the General Counsel not later than 180 days after the occurrence of the alleged violation. The General Counsel shall investigate the charge.

======={{anchor|H98EF9160C44948469D5BB63F41B5125C}}(B) Mediation– =======

If, upon investigation under subparagraph (A), the General Counsel believes that a violation of paragraph (1)may have occurred and that mediation may be helpful in resolving the dispute, the General Counsel may request mediation under subsections (b) through (d) of section 1403between the covered employee and the employing office.

======={{anchor|H46985F6BDC13493F96DB2F8468323589}}(C) Complaint; hearing; Board review– =======

If mediation under subparagraph (B)has not succeeded in resolving the dispute (or if the General Counsel does not request mediation under such subparagraph) and the General Counsel believes that a violation of paragraph (1)may have occurred, the General Counsel may file with the Office a complaint against the employing office. The complaint shall be submitted to a hearing officer for decision pursuant to subsections (b) through (h) of section 1405and any person who has filed a charge under subparagraph (A)may intervene as of right, with the full rights of a party. The decision of the hearing officer shall be subject to review by the Board pursuant to section 1406.

======={{anchor|HBE18A03C46354FDBABEA437E42F53B62}}(D) Judicial review– =======

An individual who is aggrieved by a final decision of the Board under subparagraph (C)may file a petition for review in the [[w:U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit|United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit]] , pursuant to section 1407..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|HC407722561E14AC0B65AFDC274D8CAD5}}Sec. 3. Application to legislative branch employees of whistleblower protection rules and restrictions on discharge by reason of garnishment and discriminatory treatment by reason of bankruptcy=



=={{anchor|HED165210D8024241AB34E36FAEFB617F}}(a) In General– ==

 Part A of title II of the Congressional Accountability Act of 1995([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/1311 2 U.S.C. 1311 et seq.] ] )is amended—
:(1) in the heading, by striking“fair labor standards,”and all that follows and inserting“and other protections and benefits”;
:(2) by redesignating section 207as section 209; and
:(3) by inserting after section 206the following new sections:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|HE2470A8575BB4208A2D0329D9767F064}}Sec. 207. Rights and protections under whistleblower protection rules===



===={{anchor|H8518AE0D6DF14FCCB2A7411BBEF3D21F}}(a) Rights and Protections Described– ====



====={{anchor|HE56B2DF749464037B14824148289D97C}}(1) In general– =====

No employing office may take or fail to take, or threaten to take or fail to take, a personnel action (within the meaning of chapter 23 of title 5, United States Code) with respect to any covered employee or applicant for employment because of—
:(A) any disclosure of information by a covered employee or applicant which the employee or applicant reasonably believes evidences—
::(i) a violation of any law, rule, or regulation, or
::(ii) gross mismanagement, a gross waste of funds, an abuse of authority, or a substantial and specific danger to public health or safety,if such disclosure is not specifically prohibited by law and if such information is not specifically required by Executive order or the rules of the House of Representatives or Senate to be kept secret in the interest of national defense or the conduct of foreign affairs; or
:(B) any disclosure to the General Counsel, or to the Inspector General of an executive agency or office of the legislative branch or another employee designated by the head of the agency or office to receive such disclosures, of information which the employee or applicant reasonably believes evidences—
::(i) a violation of any law, rule, or regulation, or
::(ii) gross mismanagement, a gross waste of funds, an abuse of authority, or a substantial and specific danger to public health or safety.

====={{anchor|HE45FFD992CBA4FE782C0A5D5A5690B75}}(2) Definitions– =====

For purposes of this section and for purposes of applying the procedures established under title IV for the consideration of alleged violations of this section—
:(A) the term covered employee includes an employee of the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Government Accountability Office]] or [[w:Library of Congress|Library of Congress]] ; and
:(B) the term employing office includes the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Government Accountability Office]] and the [[w:Library of Congress|Library of Congress]] .

===={{anchor|HCDD61827368F4CE68F06E60CB855D261}}(b) Remedy– ====

The remedy for a violation of subsection (a)shall be such remedy as would be appropriate if awarded underchapter 12of title 5, United States Code, with respect to a prohibited personnel practice described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2302 section 2302(b)(8) of such title] .

===={{anchor|H38CC5578100346E38C24D7C52ACAFF7E}}(c) Regulations To Implement Section– ====



====={{anchor|H82C6A7F122C74088A90FE494214B9C6F}}(1) In general– =====

The Board shall, pursuant to section 304, issue regulations to implement this section.

====={{anchor|HCFCB14DFF53547EAA200EC163A479028}}(2) Agency regulations– =====

The regulations issued under paragraph (1)shall be the same as the substantive regulations promulgated by the [[w:|Merit Systems Protection Board]] to implement[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/12 chapters 12] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/23 23 of title 5, United States Code] , except to the extent that the [[w:|Board of Directors of the Office of Compliance]] may determine, for good cause shown and stated together with the regulation, that a modification of such regulations would be more effective for the implementation of the rights and protections under this section.

==={{anchor|HD528A25E6AF0477BBBB177F802FDFD3D}}Sec. 208. Restriction on discharge from employment by reason of garnishment or discriminatory treatment by reason of bankruptcy===



===={{anchor|H08B2160E195D4D70BCEBA8286518F5A8}}(a) Garnishment– ====



====={{anchor|HDBD08F0D7EB240689FC4FBF33FC134D9}}(1) Rights and protections described– =====

No employing office may discharge any covered employee by reason of the fact that the employee’s earnings have been subjected to garnishment for any one indebtedness.

====={{anchor|HF98C2209987A42E0BB2FA5376E31C356}}(2) Remedy– =====

The remedy for a violation of paragraph (1)shall be such remedy as would be appropriate if awarded under section 304(b) of theConsumer Credit Protection Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1674 15 U.S.C. 1674(b)] ).

===={{anchor|HAFA78E1F85904449839EFF90FC7A7495}}(b) Bankruptcy– ====



====={{anchor|H9D497157576549B48717DF5D03540293}}(1) Rights and protections described– =====

No employing office may deny employment to, terminate the employment of, or discriminate with respect to employment against, a covered employee who is or has been a debtor under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/ title 11, United States Code] , or a bankrupt or a debtor under the Bankruptcy Act, or another covered employee with whom such bankrupt or debtor has been associated, solely because such bankrupt or debtor—
:(A) is or has been a debtor under such title or a debtor or bankrupt under such Act;
:(B) has been insolvent before the commencement of a case under such title or during the case but before the grant or denial of a discharge; or
:(C) has not paid a debt that is dischargeable in a case under such title or that was discharged under such Act.

====={{anchor|H1731408345374346A8BCC2E8B55D460A}}(2) Remedy– =====

The remedy for a violation of paragraph (1)would be such remedy as would be appropriate if awarded with respect to a violation of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/525 section 525(a)] or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/525 (b) of title 11, United States Code] .

===={{anchor|HC1AB59B9809C4B0AB156CB524E41752D}}(c) Definitions– ====

For purposes of this section and for purposes of applying the procedures established under title IV for the consideration of alleged violations of this section—
:(1) the term covered employee includes an employee of the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Government Accountability Office]] or the [[w:Library of Congress|Library of Congress]] ; and
:(2) the term employing office includes the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Government Accountability Office]] and the [[w:Library of Congress|Library of Congress]] .

===={{anchor|HC5BE7CDA86214AB5AAD4316382A1E4F9}}(d) Regulations To Implement Section– ====



====={{anchor|HA4B27E07DA6D4C2DAFBD513094B85773}}(1) In general– =====

====={{anchor|HCAEECB58BBDF4A0888FC74FE6C9999C5}}(2) Agency regulations– =====

The regulations issued under paragraph (1)shall be the same as the substantive regulations promulgated to implement section 304 of theConsumer Credit Protection Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 15 U.S.C. 1674] )and the substantive regulations promulgated to implement[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/525 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 525] of title 11, United States Code] , except to the extent that the [[w:|Board of Directors of the Office of Compliance]] may determine, for good cause shown and stated together with the regulation, that a modification of any such regulation would be more effective for the implementation of the rights and protections under this section..
