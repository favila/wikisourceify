{{Center|A BILL}}

To reauthorize the Undetectable Firearms Act of 1988 for 10 years and to close a loophole in the Act.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Undetectable Firearms Modernization Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Amendments to prohibition on un­de­tect­able firearms=



==(a) Extension of sunset provision– ==

 Section 2(f)(2) of the Undetectable Firearms Act of 1988([[Public Law -|[[Public Law 100-649|Public Law 100–649]] ]] ; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 18 U.S.C. 922note] )is amended in the matter preceding subparagraph (A)by striking “25” and inserting “35” .

==(b) Other amendments– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 Section 922(p)of title 18, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (1)] —
::(A) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subparagraph (A)] , by striking “grips, stocks, and magazines” and inserting “all parts other than major components” ; and
::(B) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subparagraph (B)] , by striking “when subjected to inspection by the types of x-ray machines commonly used at airports, does not generate” and inserting the following: “if subjected to inspection by the types of detection devices commonly used at airports for security screening, would not generate” ;
:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (2)] —
::(A) by striking [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subparagraph (B)] and inserting the following:

<blockquote>
:::(B) the term major component , with respect to a firearm—
::::(i) means the slide or cylinder, or the frame or receiver of the firearm; and
::::(ii) in the case of a rifle or shotgun, includes the barrel of the firearm; and; and
</blockquote>

::(B) by striking [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subparagraph (C)] and the proviso that follows and inserting the following:

<blockquote>
:::(C) the term Security Exemplar means an object, to be fabricated at the direction of the Attorney General, that is—
::::(i) constructed of 3.7 ounces of material type 17–4 PH stainless steel in a shape resembling a handgun; and
::::(ii) suitable for testing and calibrating metal detectors.;
</blockquote>

:(3) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (3)] —
::(A) in the first sentence, by inserting after “of a firearm” the following: “, including a prototype,” ; and
::(B) by striking the second sentence; and
:(4) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (5)] , by striking “shall not apply to any firearm which” and all that follows and inserting the following: “shall not apply to—

<blockquote>
::(A) any firearm received by, in the possession of, or under the control of the United States; or
::(B) the manufacture, importation, possession, transfer, receipt, shipment, or delivery of a firearm by a licensed manufacturer or licensed importer pursuant to an existing contract with the United States..
</blockquote>
