{{Center|A BILL}}

To ensure public access to the summit of Rattlesnake Mountain in the Hanford Reach National Monument for educational, recreational, historical, scientific, cultural, and other purposes.

={{anchor|HECB8CF6CF9B743A28B1946789614E97F}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Rattlesnake Mountain Public Access Act”.

={{anchor|H019302CC080545729A4E8515CCC7470A}}Sec. 2. Findings=

 [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] finds the following:
:(1) The Hanford Reach National Monument is public land that belongs to the American people.
:(2) The [[w:U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service|United States Fish and Wildlife Service]] ’s Comprehensive Conservation Plan (CCP) for the Monument restricts public access to large portions of the Monument, including the summit of Rattlesnake Mountain.
:(3) Public access to Rattlesnake Mountain is important for educational, recreational, historical, scientific, and cultural purposes.
:(4) Rattlesnake Mountain reaches an elevation of 3,660 feet above sea level—the highest elevation of the Monument, and provides unparalleled scenic views over the Monument, the Hanford Site, and the Columbia River.
:(5) Public access to Rattlesnake Mountain will increase tourism interest in the Monument and will provide economic benefits to local governments.

={{anchor|HD48D71971729427F9D228F7F61992956}}Sec. 3. Ensuring public access to the summit of rattlesnake mountain in the hanford reach national monument=



=={{anchor|H6455E048FBB44C2995F43F27592F6D78}}(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall provide public access to the summit of Rattlesnake Mountain in the Hanford Reach National Monument for educational, recreational, historical, scientific, cultural, and other purposes, including—
:(1) motor vehicle access; and
:(2) pedestrian and other nonmotorized access.

=={{anchor|H19327518C2534B5E89A05CD5E96DFCAD}}(b) Cooperative agreements– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] may enter into cooperative agreements to facilitate access to the summit of Rattlesnake Mountain—
:(1) with the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]] , the State of Washington, or any local government agency or other interested persons, for guided tours, including guided motorized tours to the summit of Rattlesnake Mountain; and
:(2) with the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]] , and with the State of Washington or any local government agency or other interested persons, to maintain the access road to the summit of Rattlesnake Mountain.
