{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act to designate segments of the York River and associated tributaries for study for potential inclusion in the National Wild and Scenic Rivers System.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “York River Wild and Scenic River Study Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. York river study=



==(a) Designation for study– ==

 Section 5(a) of the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1276 16 U.S.C. 1276(a)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(141) York River, Maine– ===

The segment of the York River that flows 11.25 miles from the headwaters of the York River at York Pond to the mouth of the river at York Harbor, and any associated tributaries..
</blockquote>


==(b) Study and report– ==

 Section 5(b) of the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1276 16 U.S.C. 1276(b)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(20) York River, Maine– ===



====(A) In general– ====

Not later than 3 years after the date on which funds are made available to carry out this paragraph, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall—
:(i) complete the study of York River, Maine, described in subsection (a)(141); and
:(ii) submit to the appropriate committees of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report that describes the results of the study.

====(B) Requirements– ====

In conducting the study referred to in subparagraph (A)(i), the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall—
:(i) determine the effect of the designation on—
::(I) existing commercial and recreational activities, such as hunting, fishing, trapping, recreational shooting, motor boat use, and bridge construction;
::(II) the authorization, construction, operation, maintenance, or improvement of energy production and transmission infrastructure; and
::(III) the authority of State and local governments to manage the activities described in subclause (I); and
:(ii) identify—
::(I) any authorities that would authorize or require the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] —
:::(aa) to influence local land use decisions, such as zoning; or
:::(bb) to place restrictions on non-Federal land if designated under this Act;
::(II) any authorities that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] may use to condemn property; and
::(III) any private property located in the area studied under subsection (a)(141)..
</blockquote>
