{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 40, United States Code, to improve the functioning and management of the Public Buildings Service.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Public Buildings Savings and Reform Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Required offsets for new space=



==(a) Offsets and freeze of space inventory– ==

Chapter 33 of title 40, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 3317. Offsets and freeze of space inventory==



===(a) Information in prospectus– ===

Any prospectus that proposes new space, whether leased or owned, transmitted pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 section 3307(b)] for each of fiscal years 2014, 2015, 2016, and 2017 shall contain information outlining the details of the elimination of at least a corresponding amount of space.

===(b) Freezing the real estate footprint– ===

On an annual basis, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall not increase the size or amount of its real property inventory, leased or owned, as compared to its fiscal year 2012 real property baseline, except in those years in which space offsets have been specifically identified or for emergency or national security purposes as determined by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] ..
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The analysis for such chapter is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>3317. Offsets and freeze of space inventory..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Minimizing costs of leased space=



==(a) Definitions– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3301 Section 3301(a) of title 40, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3301 paragraphs (5) and (6)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3301 paragraphs (6) and (7)] , respectively; and
:(2) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3301 paragraph (4)] the following:

<blockquote>

====(5) Maximum rental rate– ====

The term “maximum rental rate” means the maximum rate, by rentable square feet estimated by the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] for the leasing of commercial space for purposes of a public building in a given geographical location..
</blockquote>


==(b) Restrictions on below-Prospectus projects– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 Section 3307(g)of title 40, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(3) Restriction on below-prospectus level– ===



====(A) Restriction on rental rate– ====

The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] may not lease space at an amount below the average annual rental rate thresholds established pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 subsections (a)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 (h)] and that exceeds the maximum rental rate established by the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] for the respective geographical location, unless the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] are notified by the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] in writing at least 10 days before the execution of such lease.

====(B) Annual reports– ====

The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall submit, on an annual basis, to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] a report detailing the maximum rental rates established by the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] by geographical location.

===(4) Restriction on other below-prospectus level projects– ===

Not less than 30 days before entering into any lease-construction agreement, regardless of the threshold amounts established pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 subsections (a)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 (h)] , the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall notify the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] , in writing, of the intention to enter into such agreement and include in the notification details of the project and any associated information..
</blockquote>

=Sec. 6. Reducing costs related to courthouses=



==(a) Reducing costs related to courthouses– ==

Chapter 33of title 40, United States Code, as amended by this Act, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 3319. Reducing costs related to courthouses==



===(a) Limitation on new courthouses– ===

The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] may not commence construction of any new courthouse if—
:(1) construction has not begun on or before the date of enactment of this section; and
:(2) the design and construction of the new courthouse fails to comply, at a minimum, with the courtroom sharing requirements described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3319 subsection (b)] .

===(b) Courtroom sharing requirements defined– ===

The term “courtroom sharing requirements” means—
:(1) in courthouses with 10 or more active district judges, 2 courtrooms per 3 active district judges, except such courthouses may contain not less than 9 courtrooms for active district judges;
:(2) in courthouses with 3 or more bankruptcy judges, 1 courtroom per 2 bankruptcy judges;
:(3) in courthouses with 3 or more senior district judges, 1 courtroom per 2 senior district judges; and
:(4) in courthouses with 3 or more magistrate judges, 1 courtroom per 2 magistrate judges.

===(c) United states courts design guide– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this section, the Design Guide for courthouses shall be updated to incorporate courtroom sharing requirements to the maximum extent practicable.

===(d) Utilization– ===

If a new courthouse will add capacity in the inventory of the [[w:General Services Administration|General Services Administration]] , existing space in the same courthouse complex must be fully utilized or relinquished from the inventory of the [[w:General Services Administration|General Services Administration]] ..
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The analysis for such chapter, as amended by this Act, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>**[[#HEF46EE248A0D400D8E5C221DF2E1F01E|3319. Reducing costs related to courthouses.]]
.
</blockquote>


=Sec. 7. Inspector General review=

The [[w:Office of Inspector General|Inspector General of the General Services Administration]] shall review the Public Buildings Service’s internship program, recent graduate program, and any similar program and report to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] on the following:
:(1) The costs associated with each program.
:(2) Value added to the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] by the programs.
:(3) Recommendations on improving the programs.
:(4) Compliance with Federal hiring laws, practices, and standards.

=Sec. 8. Bonuses and conferences=



==(a) Moratorium on bonuses– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/592 Section 592of title 40, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(g) Moratorium on bonuses– ===

No funds in the Federal Buildings Fund may be available for bonuses, performance awards, or similar expenditures for a member of the Senior Executive Service, until the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] submits a report to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] detailing—
:(1) the findings and conclusions of a review of the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Building Service’s]] system of awarding bonuses and performance awards;
:(2) actions to be taken by the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] to improve the system and ensure efficiency and effectiveness; and
:(3) a description of how the actions identified will resolve any inefficiencies identified..
</blockquote>


==(b) Restrictions on conferences and travel– ==

Chapter 33of title 40, United States Code, as amended by this Act, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 3320. Restrictions on conferences and travel==



===(a) Reports regarding conferences– ===

Not later than the end of each fiscal year, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] a report on conferences proposed to be hosted by the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] for the subsequent fiscal year. The report shall include the following for each proposed conference:
:(1) The purpose of the conference.
:(2) A detailed budget for the conference, including related travel, lodging, and per diem costs.
:(3) The expected number of participants, including from the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] .
:(4) The location and length of the conference.
:(5) Any expected non-Federal funding or sponsorships.

===(b) Conference review– ===

Not later than 60 days after each fiscal year, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall submit a report to the congressional committees identified in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3320 subsection (a)] including the following:
:(1) The actual expenditures and costs of each conference and a comparison to the budget submitted pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3320 subsection (a)] .
:(2) The actual number of participants of the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] in each conference and associated travel, lodging, and per diem costs.
:(3) An agenda of each conference programming and events..
</blockquote>


==(c) Clerical amendment– ==

The analysis for such chapter, as amended by this Act, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>3320. Restrictions on conferences and travel..
</blockquote>

==(a) Administrative costs specified– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/592 Section 592(c)of title 40, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(5) Exception for administrative expenses– ===

Notwithstanding [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/592 paragraph (1)] , deposits in the Fund may not be used for administrative expenses of the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] unless an amount specified for such expenses is specifically authorized for a given fiscal year..
</blockquote>


==(b) Reporting of administrative expenses– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 Section 3307 of title 40, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===(i) Budget proposal of administrative expenses– ===



====(1) Proposed budget– ====

Not later than the date that the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] submits the budget request to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105of title 31, United States Code] , the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] shall submit a proposed budget for the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] , the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] , and the [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|Committees on Appropriations of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Senate]] detailing all proposed administrative expenses for such [[w:Public Buildings Service|Service]] for the following fiscal year.

====(2) Specifics of budget proposal– ====

Such proposed budget shall include the following:
:(A) A breakdown of proposed administrative expenses of the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] and the expenses of the previous fiscal year, including a breakdown of object classification and subclassification to include salaries, bonuses, travel and transportation, training, and conferences.
:(B) A breakdown as described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 subparagraph (A)] for the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] headquarters, Commissioner’s office, and each of the regional offices.
:(C) A breakdown of proposed staffing levels, including the number of full-time equivalent positions and the number of Federal employees by job classification and title of the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] , the Service headquarters, Commissioner’s office, and the regional offices, and the numbers for the same for the previous fiscal year.
:(D) A detailed justification for any proposed increase in administrative expenses or staffing.
:(E) A justification for the total amount of administrative expenses.

====(3) Administrative cost categories– ====

The budget proposal information described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 paragraph (2)] shall provide the required information within the following administrative cost categories:
:(A) Building Operations and Maintenance, including cleaning, utilities and fuels, and maintenance.
:(B) Salaries and Expenses, including other building services, space acquisition, staff support, and information technology services..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 10. Limitation on authorizations=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 Section 3307 of title 40, United States Code] , as amended by this Act, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

This subsection shall only apply to resolutions approved after the date of enactment of this subsection..

=Sec. 11. Limitation on certain leasing authorities=



==(a) Limitation on leasing authority of other agencies– ==

Chapter 33of title 40, United States Code, as amended by this Act, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 3321. Limitation on leasing authority of other agencies==



===(a) In general– ===

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, an executive agency may not lease space for the purposes of a public building as defined under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3301 section 3301] , except as provided under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/585 section 585] and the provisions of this chapter.

===(b) Public building– ===

For the purposes of this section, the term “public building” includes leased space.

===(c) Exclusions– ===

This section shall not apply to—
:(1) properties that are excluded for reasons of national security by the President; and
:(2) properties of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]] .

===(d) Construction– ===

Nothing in this section shall be construed as creating new authority for executive agencies to enter into leases.

===(e) Delegation pilot– ===

With respect to executive agencies covered by [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3321 subsection (a)] —
:(1) the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] may delegate authority to manage the procurement of leases to an appropriate executive agency if the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] determines that such agency has the appropriate expertise and ability to do so and such delegation promotes efficiency and economy;
:(2) the delegation under this subsection does not exempt the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] or the agency to which the delegation is made from any other provision of this chapter, including [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/592 sections 592] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 3307] ; and
:(3) the delegation authority under this subsection shall terminate on December 31, 2018.

===(f) Report– ===

Not later than 6 months before the termination date set forth in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3321 subsection (e)(3)] , the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall submit a report to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] . The report shall include an analysis of the effectiveness of the delegation authority provided in this section, including—
:(1) a comparison of the costs and terms associated with leases procured under the delegation authority to the leases procured by the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] ;
:(2) whether procurements delegated were consistent with authorization limitations and parameters; and
:(3) recommendations on improvements to the delegation authority and whether such authority should be extended..
</blockquote>


==(b) Small businesses– ==

When using commercial leasing services, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] shall adhere to the requirements of the Small Business Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/631 15 U.S.C. 631 et seq.] ).

=Sec. 12. Utilization rates=



==(a) Utilization rates– ==

Chapter 33of title 40, United States Code, as amended by this Act, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 3322. Utilization rates==



===(a) Measurement of space utilization– ===

Not later than 6 months after the date of enactment of this section, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] shall develop and implement a method of measuring actual utilization rates of its owned and leased inventory of public buildings space and a plan for incorporating such utilization rates into performance metrics for owned and leased buildings by region.

===(b) Space utilization– ===

Any method developed and implemented pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3322 subsection (a)] shall be based on actual utilization by rentable square feet per person.

===(c) Report– ===

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this section and annually thereafter, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall report to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] utilization rates measured pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3322 subsection (a)] and performance metrics for owned and leased buildings.

===(d) Certification– ===

Not later than one year after the date of enactment of this section and every year thereafter, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] and tenant Federal agencies shall certify, in writing, to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] the actual number of personnel housed in each building, leased or owned, in the inventory of the [[w:General Services Administration|General Services Administration]] . Such written certifications shall detail the number of Federal employees, temporary employees, contractors, and any other non-Federal employees.

===(e) Database– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall maintain a database of utilization rates and metrics developed pursuant to this section.

====(2) Accessibility– ====

The database described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3322 paragraph (1)] shall be made available upon request by congressional committees.

====(3) Exclusions– ====

For reasons of national security, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] may exclude any property from the requirements of this subsection.

===(f) Agency space plans– ===

Not later than 60 days after the date of enactment of this section, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] all agency Real Property Cost Savings and Innovation Plans submitted pursuant to the MANAGEMENT PROCEDURES MEMORANDUM NO. 2013–02 of the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] . The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall submit subsequent updates of the Real Property Cost Savings and Innovation Plans or any successor plans to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] not later than 60 days after their receipt by the Administrator..
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The analysis for such chapter, as amended by this Act, is further amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>**[[#H36B9A4CA95C8498AB8BB76DD4C25FD4D|3322. Utilization rates.]]
.
</blockquote>


=Sec. 13. Eliminating project escalations=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/3307 Section 3307(c) of title 40, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following: “The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall notify, in writing, the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] of any increase of more than 5 percent of an estimated maximum cost or of any increase or decrease in the scope or size of a project of 5 or more percent. Such notification shall include an explanation regarding any such increase or decrease. The scope or size of a project shall not increase or decrease by more than 10 percent unless an amended prospectus is submitted and approved pursuant to this section.” .

=Sec. 14. Report on public building service activities=

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of the General Services]] shall submit a report to the [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on Environment and Public Works of the Senate]] regarding how the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Buildings Service]] , through its acquisition activities—
:(1) conserves existing urban, suburban and rural resources;
:(2) encourages the development and redevelopment of urban, suburban, and rural areas; and
:(3) reduces costs to the [[w:Public Buildings Service|Public Building Service]] and improves the social, economic, environmental, and cultural conditions of the communities in urban, suburban, and rural areas.
