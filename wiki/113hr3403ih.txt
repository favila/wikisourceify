{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 49, United States Code, to allow motor carriers to use hair testing as a method of detecting the use of controlled substances by operators of commercial motor vehicles, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Drug Free Commercial Driver Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Hair testing as method of detecting use of controlled substances=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 Section 31306of title 49, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 subsections (d) through (j)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 subsections (e) through (k)] , respectively; and
:(2) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 subsection (c)] the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

====(d) Inclusion of hair testing as a method of testing for controlled substances– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] shall modify regulations issued under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 subsection (b)] to allow a motor carrier to use hair testing as a method of detecting the use of a controlled substance by an operator of a commercial motor vehicle.

=====(2) Limitations on use of hair testing– =====



======(A) Preemployment and random testing– ======

Regulations issued under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 paragraph (1)] shall limit the use of hair testing as a method of detecting the use of a controlled substance to—
:(i) preemployment testing; and
:(ii) subject to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 subparagraph (B)] , random testing.

======(B) Limitation on use for random testing– ======

A motor carrier may not use hair testing as a method of detecting the use of a controlled substance by an operator of a commercial motor vehicle for random testing unless the motor carrier tested such operator at preemployment using the same method.

=====(3) Applicability of general testing guidelines– =====

A motor carrier using hair testing as a method of detecting the use of a controlled substance by an operator of a commercial motor vehicle shall be subject to—
:(A) privacy and other testing and laboratory requirements under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 subsection (c)] ; and
:(B) clearinghouse requirements under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/1306a section 1306a] ..
</blockquote>


==(b) Effective date– ==

The regulations required by [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 section 31306of title 49, United States Code] , as added by subsection (a), shall be issued not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act.

=Sec. 3. Guidelines for hair testing=

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] shall issue scientific and technical guidelines for hair testing as a method of detecting the use of a controlled substance for purposes of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 section 31306of title 49, United States Code] , as amended by this Act.

=Sec. 4. Exemption during rulemaking process=



==(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] shall develop procedures to allow a motor carrier to apply for an exemption under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 section 31306of title 49, United States Code] , to use hair testing as a method of detecting the use of a controlled substance by an operator of a commercial motor vehicle instead of other methods of preemployment and random testing. The procedures shall remain in effect until the procedures required under the amendment made by section 2have been implemented.

==(b) Limitations on exemption– ==

A motor carrier shall be eligible to apply for an exemption under paragraph (1)only if—
:(1) the motor carrier demonstrates to the Secretary that the carrier has used hair testing as a method of detecting the use of a controlled substance by an operator of a commercial motor vehicle for at least 1 year before the date of enactment of this Act; and
:(2) the motor carrier uses a hair testing laboratory that—
::(A) has obtained laboratory accreditation from the College of American Pathologists; and
::(B) uses a testing method that has been cleared by the [[w:Food and Drug Administration|Food and Drug Administration]] under section 510(k) of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/360 21 U.S.C. 360(k)] ).

==(c) Applicability of general testing guidelines– ==

A motor carrier using hair testing as a method of detecting the use of a controlled substance by an operator of a commercial motor vehicle shall be subject to—
:(1) privacy and other testing and laboratory requirements under subsection (c); and
:(2) clearinghouse requirements under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/1306a section 1306a of such title] .

=Sec. 5. Report=

Not later than 1 year after the date of implementation of the regulations issued under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31306 section 31306(d)of title 49, United States Code] (as amended by this Act), and annually thereafter, the Secretary shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report containing an analysis of the efficacy of hair testing and urinalysis as methods of detecting the use of controlled substances.

=Sec. 6. Definitions=

In this Act, the following definitions apply:

==(1) Commercial motor vehicle– ==

The term “commercial motor vehicle” has the meaning given the term in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31301 section 31301of title 49, United States Code] .

==(2) Controlled substance– ==

The term “controlled substance” means any substance under section 102 of the Comprehensive Drug Abuse Prevention and Control Act of 1970( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/802 21 U.S.C. 802] )specified by the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] .

==(3) Motor carrier– ==

The term “motor carrier” has the meaning given the term in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/13102 section 13102of title 49, United States Code] .
