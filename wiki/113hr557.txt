{{Center|A BILL}}

To prevent certain individuals purportedly appointed to
		  the National Labor Relations Board from receiving salaries, and to prevent an
		  unconstitutional quorum of the Board from taking agency actions, until there is
		  a final decision in pending lawsuits regarding the constitutionality of certain
		  alleged recess appointments.

={{anchor|H37E306CDA94E43F181295920178B17B5}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Advice and Consent Restoration
			 Act”.

={{anchor|H51BBED7DB3444CD5B91543C3229829B1}}Sec. 2. No salaries for
			 unconstitutional NLRB appointments=



=={{anchor|H078502754E6E46C780188302215BB83B}}(a) In
			 general– ==

Notwithstanding any
			 other provision of law, an individual shall not receive any salary or payment
			 for services performed as a member of the [[Wikipedia:National Labor Relations Board|National Labor Relations Board]] unless
			 the individual has been appointed—
:(1) by and with the advice and consent of the
			 [[Wikipedia:United States Senate|Senate]], in accordance with clause 2 of section 2 of article II of the United
			 States Constitution; or
:(2) appointed appropriately in accordance with
			 clause 3 of section 2 of article II of the United States Constitution.

=={{anchor|HA862EE0F741649F9BACDB8117A6A0C1B}}(b) Effective
			 date– ==

This section shall take effect on the first day of the first
			 applicable pay period beginning on or after the date of enactment of this
			 Act.

={{anchor|HEE1911F0FE9B42698382D69BF26625C1}}Sec. 3. Prohibiting NLRB
			 actions or decisions=



=={{anchor|H33DD9302828C4ACD953EF48A87A89240}}(a) In
			 general– ==

Notwithstanding any
			 other provision of law, an unconstitutional quorum of the [[Wikipedia:National Labor Relations Board|National Labor
			 Relations Board]] shall not convene or take any agency action, as defined in
			 section 551 of title 5, United States Code, and including any determination,
			 hearing, investigation, direction of election, certification, order, rule,
			 regulation, or review of any determination, until the date on which final
			 judgment is entered in all cases challenging the constitutionality of the
			 purported appointment of individuals to such Board that are pending before a
			 Federal court on the date of enactment of this Act.

=={{anchor|HE6D7339C6D6B414C9552126BC7A9C180}}(b) Definition– ==

In
			 this section, the term unconstitutional quorum means a quorum of
			 the [[Wikipedia:National Labor Relations Board|National Labor Relations Board]] that includes 1 or more individuals who were
			 purportedly appointed to the [[Wikipedia:National Labor Relations Board|Board]] and who are the subject of a cause of action
			 relating to such purported appointment that is pending before a Federal court
			 on the date of enactment of this Act.
