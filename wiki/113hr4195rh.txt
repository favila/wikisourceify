{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amendchapter 15of title 44, United States Code (commonly known as the Federal Register Act), to modernize the Federal Register, and for other purposes.    

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Federal Register Modernization Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Federal register modernization=



==(a) References to printing– ==

  Chapter 15of title 44, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1502 section 1502]—
::(A) in the heading, by striking“ printing ”and inserting“ publishing ”; and
::(B) by striking“printing and distribution”and inserting“publishing”;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1507 section 1507]is amended—
::(A) by striking“the duplicate originals or certified copies of the document have”and inserting“the document has”; and
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1507 paragraph (2)], by striking“printed”and inserting“published”; and
:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1509 section 1509], in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1509 subsections (a)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1509 (b)]of, by striking“printing, reprinting, wrapping, binding, and distributing”and inserting“publishing”, each place it appears.

==(b) Publish defined– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1501  Section 1501of title 44, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by striking“; and”at the end of the definition for“person”and inserting a semicolon;
:(2) by inserting after the definition for“person”the following:

<blockquote> 
::   publish means to circulate for sale or distribution to the public; and . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Filing documents with Office amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1503  Section 1503of title 44, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 1503. Filing documents with Office; notation of time; public inspection; transmission for publishing==

The original document required or authorized to be published by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1505  section 1505of this title]shall be filed with the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of the Federal Register]]for publication at times established by the [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee of the Federal Register]]by regulation. The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist of the United States]]shall cause to be noted on the original of each document the day and hour of filing. Upon filing, the document shall be immediately available for public inspection in the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office]]. The original shall be retained by the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|National Archives and Records Administration]]and shall be available for inspection under regulations prescribed by the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]], unless such original is disposed of in accordance with disposal schedules submitted by the [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee]]and authorized by the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]]pursuant to regulations issued under chapter 33of this title; however, originals of proclamations of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and Executive orders shall be permanently retained by the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Administration]]as part of the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|National Archives of the United States]]. The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office]]shall transmit to the [[w:Government Printing Office|Government Printing Office]], as provided by this chapter, each document required or authorized to be published by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1505  section 1505of this title]. Every Federal agency shall cause to be transmitted for filing the original of all such documents issued, prescribed, or promulgated by the agency. . 
</blockquote>


==(d) Federal Register amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1504  Section 1504of title 44, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 1504.   Federal Register ; publishing; contents; distribution; price==

Documents required or authorized to be published by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1505  section 1505of this title]shall be published immediately by the [[w:Government Printing Office|Government Printing Office]]in a serial publication designated the Federal Register . The [[w:Government Printing Office|Public Printer]]shall make available the facilities of the [[w:Government Printing Office|Government Printing Office]]for the prompt publication of the Federal Register in the manner and at the times required by this chapter and the regulations prescribed under it. The contents of the daily issues shall constitute all documents, required or authorized to be published, filed with the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of the Federal Register]]up to the time of the day immediately preceding the day of publication fixed by regulations under this chapter. There shall be published with each document a copy of the notation, required to be made by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1503  section 1503of this title], of the day and hour when, upon filing with the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office]], the document was made available for public inspection. Distribution shall be made at a time in the morning of the day of distribution fixed by regulations prescribed under this chapter. The prices to be charged for the Federal Register may be fixed by the [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee of the Federal Register]]established by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1506  section 1506of this title]without reference to the restrictions placed upon and fixed for the sale of Government publications by [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1705 sections 1705] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1708  1708of this title]. . 
</blockquote>

==(f) Administrative Committee of the Federal Register amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1506  Section 1506of title 44, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 1506. Administrative Committee of the Federal Register; establishment and composition; powers and duties==

The [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee of the Federal Register]]shall consist of the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist of the United States]]or Acting [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]], who shall chair the committee, an officer of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]designated by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]], and the [[w:Government Printing Office|Public Printer]]or Acting [[w:Government Printing Office|Public Printer]]. The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Director of the Federal Register]]shall act as secretary of the [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|committee]]. The [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|committee]]shall prescribe, with the approval of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]], regulations for carrying out this chapter. The regulations shall provide for, among other things—
:(1) the documents which shall be authorized under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1505  section 1505(b)of this title]to be published in the Federal Register;
:(2) the manner and form in which the Federal Register shall be published;
:(3) the manner of distribution to Members of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]], officers and employees of the United States, or Federal agency, for official use, and the number which shall be available for distribution to the public;
:(4) the prices to be charged for individual copies of, and subscriptions to, the Federal Register and any reprints and bound volumes of it;
:(5) the manner and form by which the Federal Register may receive information and comments from the public, if practicable and efficient; and
:(6) special editions of the Federal Register. . 
</blockquote>


==(g) Code of Federal Regulations amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1510  Section 1510of title 44, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 1510. Code of Federal Regulations==



===(a) Special edition for codification of agency documents– ===

The [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee of the Federal Register]], with the approval of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]], may require, from time to time as it considers necessary, the preparation and publication in a special edition of the Federal Register a complete codification of the documents of each agency of the Government having general applicability and legal effect, issued or promulgated by the agency by publication in the Federal Register or by filing with the [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee]], and which are relied upon by the agency as authority for, or are invoked or used by it in the discharge of, its activities or functions, and are in effect as to facts arising on or after dates specified by the [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee]].

===(b) Code of Federal Regulations– ===

A codification prepared under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/1510 subsection (a) of this section]shall be published and shall be designated as the“Code of Federal Regulations”. The [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee]]shall regulate the manner and forms of publishing this codification.

===(c) Supplementation, collation, and republication– ===

The [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee]]shall regulate the supplementation and the collation and republication of the codification with a view to keeping the Code of Federal Regulations as current as practicable. Each unit of codification shall be supplemented and republished at least once each calendar year. The Office of the Federal Register may create updates of each unit of codification from time to time and make the same available electronically or may provide public access using an electronic edition that allows a user to select a specific date and retrieve the version of the codification in effect as of that date.

===(d) Preparation and publication by the Federal Register– ===

The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of the Federal Register]]shall prepare and publish the codifications, supplements, collations, and user aids authorized by this section.

===(e) Prima facie evidence– ===

The codified documents of the several agencies published in the Code of Federal Regulations under this section, as amended by documents subsequently filed with the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office]]and published in the daily issues of the Federal Register, shall be prima facie evidence of the text of the documents and of the fact that they are in effect on and after the date of publication.

===(f) Regulations– ===

The [[w:Administrative Committee of the Federal Register|Administrative Committee]], with approval of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]], shall issue regulations for carrying out this section.

===(g) Exception– ===

This section does not require codification of the text of Presidential documents published and periodically compiled in supplements to title 3 of the Code of Federal Regulations. . 
</blockquote>
