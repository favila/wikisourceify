{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 31, United States Code, to eliminate the
		  statutory cap on the public debt and to place limitations on the purposes for
		  which public debt may be issued.

={{anchor|H013EE6F6907C4F1793E6F257FD363AA0}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Full Faith and Credit Act of
			 2013”.

={{anchor|H10A7E25059CC4ED0A1700A4826BF6830}}Sec. 2. Limitation on
			 obligations=



=={{anchor|H3F722A90F136483B848F63238BF2B954}}(a) In
			 general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/3101 Section 3101 of title
			 31, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H73DB57F6EF2943058311635AAD457A63}}Sec. 3101. Limitation on
				obligations==

Obligations may
				only be issued under this chapter to fund commitments incurred by the Federal
				Government that require current
				funding..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HAC1AD2F6E42948EC83D84425ABD5173F}}(b) Conforming
			 amendments– ==


:(1) [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/3130 Section 3130(e)(2)
			 of title 31, United States Code], is amended by inserting “, as in effect
			 on the day before the date of the enactment of the Full Faith and Credit Act of
			 2011” before the period at the end.
:(2) The table of
			 contents for chapter 31 of title 31, United States Code, is amended by amending
			 the item relating to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/3101 section 3101] to read as follows:

<blockquote>__NOTOC__
3101. Limitation on
				obligations..
</blockquote>
