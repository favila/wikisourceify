{{Center|A BILL}}

 To encourage continued enlargement of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   NATO Alliance Recognition and Promotion Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Findings=

  [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]finds the following:
:(1) On March 12, 2014, the [[w:NATO|North Atlantic Treaty Organization]]( [[w:NATO|NATO]]) marks the 15th anniversary of enlargement through the accession of 3 Central European countries: the Czech Republic, Poland, and Hungary.
:(2) On March 29, 2014, [[w:NATO|NATO]]marks the 10th anniversary of enlargement through the accession of 7 Central European countries: Bulgaria, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Romania, Slovakia, and Slovenia.
:(3) On April 7, 2014, [[w:NATO|NATO]]marks the 5th anniversary of enlargement through the accession of Albania and Croatia.
:(4) The incorporation of these European countries into [[w:NATO|NATO]]has contributed toward a vision of Europe that is aimed at promoting stability and cooperation, at building a Europe whole and free, united in peace, democracy and common values.
:(5) Since joining the Alliance, these 12 member states have contributed to numerous [[w:NATO|NATO]]-led peace and security and stability operations, including participation in the International Security Assistance Force’s (ISAF) mission in Afghanistan. 

=Sec. 3. Statement of Congress=

  [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]declares that—
:(1)   [[w:NATO|NATO]]has been the cornerstone of transatlantic security cooperation and an enduring instrument for promoting stability in Europe and around the world for over sixty-five years;
:(2) the incorporation of the Czech Republic, Poland, Hungary, Bulgaria, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Albania, and Croatia has been essential to the success of [[w:NATO|NATO]]in this modern era;
:(3) the Czech Republic, Poland, Hungary, Bulgaria, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Albania and Croatia has over time added to and strengthened the list of key European allies of the United States;
:(4) since joining [[w:NATO|NATO]], these member states have remained committed to the collective defense of the Alliance and have demonstrated their will and ability to contribute to transatlantic solidarity and assume increasingly more responsibility for international peace and security;
:(5) these [[w:NATO|NATO]]member states have become reliable partners and supporters of aspiring members, and the United States recognizes their continued efforts to aid in further enlargement initiatives; and
:(6) the commitment by these [[w:NATO|NATO]]members to Alliance principles and active participation in Alliance initiatives shows the success of [[w:NATO|NATO]]’s Open-Door Policy. 

=Sec. 4. Sense of Congress=

It is the sense of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that—
:(1) the United States should remain committed to maintaining a military presence in Europe as a means of promoting allied interoperability and providing visible assurance to [[w:NATO|NATO]]allies in the region; and
:(2) at the September 2014 [[w:NATO|NATO]]Summit in Wales, the United States, along with [[w:NATO|NATO]]allies, should continue to pursue enlargement initiatives for the aspirant countries.
