{{Center|A BILL}}

To expand the Alpine Lakes Wilderness in the State of
		  Washington, to designate the Middle Fork Snoqualmie River and Pratt River as
		  wild and scenic rivers, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H7B88AA70181B4BE697D36DC582674DAC}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Alpine Lakes Wilderness Additions and
			 Pratt and Middle Fork Snoqualmie Rivers Protection
			 Act”.

={{anchor|HBD30447ACA644313B57E98A0B1DFDE44}}Sec. 2. Expansion of
			 alpine lakes wilderness=



=={{anchor|H842EEE7BDE0F43D09A27B5DB37D6C888}}(a) In
			 general– ==

There is designated as wilderness and as a component of
			 the National Wilderness Preservation System certain Federal land in the Mount
			 Baker-Snoqualmie National Forest in the State of Washington comprising
			 approximately 22,173 acres that is within the Proposed Alpine Lakes Wilderness
			 Additions Boundary, as generally depicted on the map entitled “Proposed
			 Alpine Lakes Wilderness Additions” and dated December 3, 2009, which is
			 incorporated in and shall be considered to be a part of the Alpine Lakes
			 Wilderness.

=={{anchor|H3899D9822A6C41349A02BAC6141B0854}}(b) Administration– ==



==={{anchor|H435D28DB36974251B77B54747FFA2741}}(1) Management– ===

Subject
			 to valid existing rights, the land designated as wilderness by subsection (a)
			 shall be administered by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] (referred to in this
			 section as the “Secretary”), in accordance with the Wilderness Act
			 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1131 16 U.S.C. 1131 et seq.]), except that any reference in that Act to the
			 effective date of that Act shall be considered to be a reference to the date of
			 enactment of this Act.

==={{anchor|H2BD8AC78DD0D44D98FCBB3B851D83591}}(2) Map and
			 description– ===



===={{anchor|H9613E5CE881A40F485992254600E3A27}}(A) In
			 general– ====

As soon as practicable after the date of enactment of
			 this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall file a map and a legal description of the land
			 designated as wilderness by subsection (a) with—
:(i) the
			 Committee on Natural Resources of the House of Representatives; and
:(ii) the
			 Committee on Energy and Natural Resources of the Senate.

===={{anchor|H8764BC662B1C42D690540FE754688D0E}}(B) Force of
			 law– ====

A map and legal description filed under subparagraph (A)
			 shall have the same force and effect as if included in this Act, except that
			 the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] may correct minor errors in the map and legal description.

===={{anchor|H50FA3456B12540FD8F40D759A191A76A}}(C) Public
			 availability– ====

The map and legal description filed under
			 subparagraph (A) shall be filed and made available for public inspection in the
			 appropriate office of the [[w:United States Forest Service|Forest Service]].

=={{anchor|H5FE7943E9EB84195AE8054A6D9836DDA}}(c) Incorporation of
			 acquired land and interests in land– ==

Any land or interests in land
			 within the Proposed Alpine Lakes Wilderness Additions Boundary, as generally
			 depicted on the map entitled “Proposed Alpine Lakes Wilderness
			 Additions” and dated December 3, 2009, that is acquired by the United
			 States shall—
:(1) become part of the
			 wilderness area; and
:(2) be managed in
			 accordance with subsection (b)(1).

={{anchor|HBF25FD3554E54023BDF2AFA7065E3360}}Sec. 3. Wild and scenic
			 river designations=

Section
			 3(a) of the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1274 16 U.S.C. 1274(a)]) is amended by adding
			 at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|id852874A47A32483E9F348F666FFAEC7A}}(__) Middle Fork
				Snoqualmie, Washington– ==

The 27.4-mile segment from the headwaters
				of the Middle Fork Snoqualmie River near La Bohn Gap in NE 1/4 sec. 20, T. 24
				N., R. 13 E., to the northern boundary of sec. 11, T. 23 N., R. 9 E., to be
				administered by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] in the following
				classifications:
:(A) The approximately
				6.4-mile segment from the headwaters of the Middle Fork Snoqualmie River near
				La Bohn Gap in NE 1/4 sec. 20, T. 24 N., R. 13 E., to the west section line of
				sec. 3, T. 23 N., R. 12 E., as a wild river.
:(B) The approximately
				21-mile segment from the west section line of sec. 3, T. 23 N., R. 12 E., to
				the northern boundary of sec. 11, T. 23 N., R. 9 E., as a scenic river.

=={{anchor|id03DB4EF08A9E409FADCF706B60F5EA1D}}(__) Pratt River,
				Washington– ==

The entirety of the Pratt River in the State of
				Washington, located in the Mount Baker-Snoqualmie National Forest, to be
				administered by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] as a wild
				river..
</blockquote>
