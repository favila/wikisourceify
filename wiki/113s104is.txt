{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for congressional approval of national monuments and restrictions on the use of national monuments.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “National Monuments Act of 2011” .

=Sec. 2. Designation of national monuments=

The Act of June 8, 1906( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/431 16 U.S.C. 431 et seq.] ), is amended—
:(1) in section 2( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/431 16 U.S.C. 431] ), by striking “Sec. 2.That the” and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 2. Designation of national monuments==

After obtaining congressional approval of the proposed national monument and certifying compliance with theNational Environmental Policy Act of 1969( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] )with respect to the proposed national monument, the; and
</blockquote>

:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 5. Restrictions on public use==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall not implement any restrictions on the public use of a national monument until the expiration of an appropriate review period (as determined by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] ) providing for public input and congressional approval..
</blockquote>
