{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to require recipients of per diem payments from the Secretary of Veterans Affairs for the provision of services for homeless veterans to comply with codes relevant to operations and level of care provided, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Ensuring Safe Shelter for Homeless Veterans Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Conditions on award of per diem payments by Secretary of Veterans Affairs for provision of housing or services to homeless veterans=



==(a) Condition– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 Section 2012(c)(1)of title 38, United States Code] , is amended by striking “unless the facilities” and all that follows through “may specify.” and inserting the following: “unless the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] certifies the following:

<blockquote>
:(A) That the building where the entity provides housing or services for which the entity would receive such payment is in compliance with the codes relevant to the operations and level of care provided, including applicable provisions of the most recently published version of the Life Safety Code of the National Fire Protection Association or such other comparable fire and safety requirements as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may specify.
:(B) That such building and such housing or services are in compliance with licensing requirements, fire and safety requirements, and any other requirements in the jurisdiction in which the building is located regarding the condition of the building and the provision of such housing or services..
</blockquote>


===(2) Effective date– ===

The amendment made by paragraph (1)shall apply with respect to an application for a per diem payment under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 section 2012of title 38, United States Code] , submitted on or after the date of the enactment of this Act.

==(b) Annual inspections required– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 Section 2012] of such title is amended by striking [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 subsection (b)] and inserting the following new [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 subsection (b)] :

<blockquote>
:(b) 
::(1) Not less frequently than once each fiscal year, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall inspect each facility of each grant recipient or entity eligible for payments under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 subsection (a)] at which the recipients and entities provide services under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2011 section 2011] of this title or this section.
::(2) Except as provided in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (1)] , inspections made under such paragraph shall be made at such times as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] considers necessary.
::(3) An inspection of a facility of a recipient or entity described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (1)] made under such paragraph may be made with or without prior notice to the recipient or entity, as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] considers appropriate.
::(4) No per diem payment may be provided to a grant recipient or eligible entity under this section unless the facilities of the grant recipient or eligible entity meet such standards as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall prescribe..
</blockquote>


==(c) Revocation of certification authorized– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 Subsection (c)] of such section is amended—
:(1) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraphs (2)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 (3)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraphs (4)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 (5)] , respectively;
:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (1)] , as amended by subsection (a)(1), by striking “in paragraph (2)” and inserting “in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (4)] ” ; and
:(3) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (1)] the following new [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (2)] :

<blockquote>
::(2) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may revoke any certification made under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (1)] if the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] determines that such certification is no longer accurate..
</blockquote>


==(d) Congressional notification of termination of per diem required– ==

Such subsection is further amended by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (2)] the following new [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (3)] :

<blockquote>
:(3) Not later than 30 days after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] terminates provision of per diem payment under this section because the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] has revoked a certification under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 paragraph (2)] , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the Senate]] and the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the House of Representatives]] notice of such termination..
</blockquote>


==(e) Annual report– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2065 Section 2065(b)] of such title is amended—
:(1) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2065 paragraph (6)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2065 paragraph (7)] ; and
:(2) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2065 paragraph (5)] the following new [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2065 paragraph (6)] :

<blockquote>
::(6) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary’s]] evaluation of the safety and accessibility of facilities used to provide services under programs established by grant recipients or eligible entities under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2011 section 2011] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 2012] of this title, including the number of such grant recipients or eligible entities who have been certified by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 section 2012(c)(1)] of this title..
</blockquote>


==(f) Treatment of current recipients of per diem payments– ==



===(1) Assessment– ===

In the case of the recipient of a per diem payment under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 section 2012of title 38, United States Code] , that receives such a payment during the year in which this Act is enacted for the provision of housing or services, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall assess whether the building where such housing or services are provided is and whether the housing and services are in compliance as required by [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 section 2012(c)(1)] of such title, as amended by subsection (a)(1).

===(2) Failure to comply– ===

In the case described in paragraph (1), if the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] does not certify the compliance of the building and the housing or services under such section before the date that is two years after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may not make any additional per diem payments to the recipient for the provision of such housing or services under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2012 section 2012] of such title until the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] certifies that such building is and such housing or services are in compliance.

==(g) Conforming condition on award of grants by Secretary of Veterans Affairs for comprehensive service programs– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2011 Section 2011(b)(5)(A)of title 38, United States Code] , is amended by inserting “, including housing and building codes,” .
