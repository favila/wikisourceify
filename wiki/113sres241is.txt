{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

Authorizing expenditures by the Special Committee on Aging.

=Section 1. General authority=

In carrying out its powers, duties, and functions imposed by section 104 of S. Res. 4, agreed to February 4, 1977 (95th Congress), and in exercising the authority conferred on it by such section, the [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|Special Committee on Aging]] (in this resolution referred to as the “committee” ) is authorized from October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2014, and October 1, 2014, through February 28, 2015, in its discretion to—
:(1) make expenditures from the contingent fund of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] ;
:(2) employ personnel; and
:(3) with the prior consent of the Government department or agency concerned and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Rules and Administration|Committee on Rules and Administration]] , use on a reimbursable or nonreimbursable basis the services of personnel of any such department or agency.

=Sec. 2. Expenses=



==(a) Period ending September 30, 2014– ==

The expenses of the [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|committee]] for the period October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2014, under this resolution shall not exceed $2,375,377, of which amount, not to exceed $10,000 may be expended for the training of the professional staff of the [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|committee]] (under procedures specified by section 202(j) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/72a 2 U.S.C. 72a(j)] ] )).

==(b) Period ending February 28, 2015– ==

The expenses of the [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|committee]] for the period October 1, 2014, through February 28, 2015, under this resolution shall not exceed $989,740, of which amount, not to exceed $4,000 may be expended for the training of the professional staff of the [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|committee]] (under procedures specified by section 202(j) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/72a 2 U.S.C. 72a(j)] ] )).

=Sec. 3. Reporting legislation=

The [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|committee]] shall report its findings, together with such recommendations for legislation as it deems advisable, to the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] at the earliest practicable date, but not later than February 28, 2015.

=Sec. 4. Expenses and agency contributions=



==(a) Expenses of the committee– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Except as provided in paragraph (2), expenses of the [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|committee]] under this resolution shall be paid from the contingent fund of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] upon vouchers approved by the chairman of the [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|committee]] .

===(2) Vouchers not required– ===

Vouchers shall not be required for—
:(A) the disbursement of salaries of employees paid at an annual rate;
:(B) the payment of telecommunications provided by the [[w:United States Senate|Office of the Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper]] ;
:(C) the payment of stationery supplies purchased through the [[w:United States Senate|Keeper of the Stationery]] ;
:(D) payments to the [[w:United States Senate|Postmaster of the Senate]] ;
:(E) the payment of metered charges on copying equipment provided by the [[w:United States Senate|Office of the Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper]] ;
:(F) the payment of Senate Recording and Photographic Services; or
:(G) the payment of franked and mass mail costs by the [[w:United States Senate|Sergeant at Arms and Doorkeeper, United States Senate]] .

==(b) Agency contributions– ==

There are authorizedsuch sums as may be necessaryfor agency contributions related to the compensation of employees of the [[w:United States Senate Special Committee on Aging|committee]] from October 1, 2013, through September 30, 2014, and October 1, 2014, through February 28, 2015, to be paid from theappropriations account for “Expenses of Inquiries and Investigations” of the Senate.
