{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Consumer Financial Protection Act of 2010 to provide requirements that must be followed by the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection when carrying out certain examinations.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Bureau Examination Fairness Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Examination requirements=



==(a) In general– ==

The Consumer Financial Protection Act of 2010 is amended by inserting after section 1026 the following:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 1026A. Examination requirements==



===(a) Non-Inclusion of enforcement attorneys– ===

The [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]]may not include enforcement attorneys when performing examinations pursuant to section 1024 , 1025 , or 1026 .

===(b) Data requests– ===

In any request for data from an entity supervised by the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]]pursuant to section 1024 , 1025 , or 1026 , the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]]shall—
:(1) ensure that the different divisions of the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]]coordinate with each other before the data is requested;
:(2) accompany each request with a written statement of intended use for the data being requested;
:(3) use peer-reviewed data sampling based on limited data sets rather than requesting full data sets, unless the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Director]]determines, in writing, that requesting full data sets is necessary; and
:(4) with respect to a data request with respect to which the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]]anticipates the cost of complying with such request will exceed $50,000 per company, provide the entity with the written approval of the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Director]]for such request.

===(c) Examination time period– ===

When respect to an examination conducted by the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]], the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]]shall—
:(1) complete the examination field work and conduct an exit interview with the entity being examined not later than the end of the 60-day period beginning on the date the examination begins; and
:(2) produce a report of examination or supervisory letter—
::(A) not later than the end of the 120-day period beginning on the date the examination is completed; or
::(B) if the [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Director]]authorizes an extension and provides a written justification for such extension, not later than end of the 180-day period beginning on the date the examination is completed.

===(d) Prohibition on concurrent examinations– ===

The [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Bureau]]may only perform one limited-scope examination of an entity at any one time. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of contents under section 1 of the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act is amended by inserting after the item relating to section 1026 the following:

<blockquote>  Sec. 1026A. Examination requirements.  . 
</blockquote>
