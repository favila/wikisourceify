{{Center|A BILL}}

 To require that, if the President’s fiscal year 2014 budget does not achieve balance in a fiscal year covered by such budget, the President shall submit a supplemental unified budget by April 1, 2013, which identifies a fiscal year in which balance is achieved, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Require Presidential Leadership and No Deficit Act”or the“Require a PLAN Act”. 

=Sec. 2. Purpose and findings=



==(a) Purpose– ==

The purpose of this Act is to require the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] to submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a supplemental unified budget if the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President’s]] budget for fiscal year 2014 does not achieve balance in a fiscal year covered by such budget.

==(b) Findings– ==

  [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] finds the following:
:(1) With this year’s expected failure to meet the statutory deadline for submission of his budget, as stated by the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] , the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] will have only met the statutory deadline in one of his five budget submissions.
:(2) Despite a promise to cut the deficit in half, the deficit doubled during the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President’s]] first year in office and has exceeded $1 trillion for four years now.
:(3) Since taking office, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] has allowed the Federal debt to grow by nearly $6 trillion and total debt now exceeds the size of the entire economy of the United States.
:(4) Under the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President’s]] most recent budget submission, the budget never achieves balance.
:(5) The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President’s]] fiscal year 2013 budget submission includes the admission that under his own policies the Federal Government’s“fiscal position gradually deteriorates”. 

=Sec. 3. Submission of a supplemental unified budget=



==(a) In general– ==

If the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President’s]] budget for fiscal year 2014, submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105(a) of title 31, United States Code], results in a projected deficit in every fiscal year for which estimates are provided in such budget, then the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall submit a supplemental unified budget pursuant to subsection (b) .

==(b) Contents of supplemental unified budget– ==

Not later than April 1, 2013, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a supplemental unified budget that includes—
:(1) the information required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105(a) of title 31, United States Code];
:(2) an estimate of the earliest fiscal year in which the supplemental budget is not projected to result in a deficit;
:(3) a detailed description of additional policies to be implemented in order to achieve such result; and
:(4) an explanation of the differences between the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President’s]] budget for fiscal year 2014 and the supplemental unified budget referred to in this subsection .

==(c) Definition– ==

The term unified budget means the total level of outlays, total level of receipts, and the resulting deficit or surplus of the United States Government for a fiscal year.
