{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

 Raising a question of the privileges of the House.    WhereasH.R. 1123, the Unlocking Consumer Choice and Wireless Competition Act , was introduced on March 13, 2013;   Whereas the [[w:United States House Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary]]marked up and ordered the bill reported, with strong bipartisan support, on July 31, 2013;   Whereas the suspension version of the bill included new language to prohibit the bulk unlocking of cellular phones;   Whereas the changes made to the bill fundamentally altered the substance of the underlying bill;   Whereas the report of the [[w:United States House Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary]]on the bill was filed in the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House]]on February 25, 2014; and   Whereas the bill was passed under suspension of the rules on the night of February 25, 2014: Now, therefore, be it    

= =

That the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]disapproves of the actions taken by the [[w:United States House Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary]]and the leadership of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]with respect toH.R. 1123and directs the leadership of each committee of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House]]and the leadership of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House]]to operate in a matter that maximizes transparency and public trust.
