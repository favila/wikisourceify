{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to direct the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to make available for purchase Department of Veterans Affairs memorial headstones and markers for members of reserve components who performed certain training.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Guard Units And Reservists Deserve Remembrance Act” .

=Sec. 2. Availability for purchase of Department of Veterans Affairs memorial headstones and markers for members of reserve components who performed certain training=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2306 Section 2306of title 38, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>
:(i) 
::(1) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall make available for purchase a memorial headstone or marker for the marked or unmarked grave of an individual described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2306 paragraph (2)] or for the purpose of commemorating such an individual whose remains are unavailable.
::(2) An individual described in this paragraph is an individual who—
:::(A) as a member of a United States National Guard or Reserve component performed inactive duty training or active duty for training for at least 6 years but did not serve on active duty; and
:::(B) is not otherwise ineligible for a memorial headstone or marker on account of the nature of the individual’s separation from the Armed Forces or other cause.
::(3) A headstone or marker for the grave of an individual may be purchased under this subsection by—
:::(A) the individual;
:::(B) the surviving spouse, child, sibling, or parent of the individual; or
:::(C) an individual other than the next of kin, as determined by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] .
::(4) In establishing the prices of the headstones and markers made available for purchase under this section, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall ensure the prices are sufficient to cover the costs associated with the production and delivery of such headstones and markers.
::(5) No person may receive any benefit under the laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] solely by reason of this subsection.
::(6) This subsection does not authorize any new burial benefit for any person or create any new authority for any individual to be buried in a national cemetery.
::(7) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall coordinate with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] in establishing procedures to determine whether an individual is an individual described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2306 paragraph (2)] ..
</blockquote>
