{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the State Department Basic Authorities Act of 1956 to establish a United States Ambassador at Large for Arctic Affairs, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   United States Ambassador at Large for Arctic Affairs Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. United States Ambassador at Large for Arctic Affairs=

   Title I of the State Department Basic Authorities Act of 1956 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/2651a 22 U.S.C. 2651a et seq].)is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

=Sec. 63. United States Ambassador at Large for Arctic Affairs=



==(a) Establishment– ==

There is established within the [[w:United States Department of State|Department of State]]an [[w:United States Department of State|Ambassador at Large for Arctic Affairs]](referred to in this section as the“Ambassador”), appointed in accordance with subsection (b) .

==(b) Appointment– ==

The [[w:United States Department of State|Ambassador]]shall be appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]], by and with the advice and consent of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]].

==(c) Duties– ==



===(1) Diplomatic representation– ===

Subject to the direction of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]], the [[w:United States Department of State|Ambassador]]is authorized to represent the United States in matters and cases relevant to Arctic affairs in—
:(A) contacts with foreign governments, intergovernmental organizations, and specialized agencies of the [[w:United Nations|United Nations]], the Arctic Council, and other international organizations of which the United States is a member; and
:(B) multilateral conferences and meetings relating to Arctic affairs.

===(2) Chair of the Arctic Council– ===

The [[w:United States Department of State|Ambassador]]shall serve as the Chair of the Arctic Council when the United States holds the Chairmanship of the Arctic Council from 2015 through 2017.

===(3) Policies and procedures– ===

The [[w:United States Department of State|Ambassador]]shall coordinate United States Arctic policies. . 
</blockquote>
