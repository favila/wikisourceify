{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Telecommunications Act of 1996 to restore the authority of the Federal Communications Commission to adopt certain rules relating to preserving the open Internet and to direct the Commission to take all actions necessary to restore to effect vacated portions of such rules.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Open Internet Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Authority for and restoration of 2010 open Internet rules=

   Section 706 of the Telecommunications Act of 1996 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/47/1302 47 U.S.C. 1302] )is amended—
:(1) by redesignating subsection (d) as subsection (e); and
:(2) by inserting after subsection (c) the following:

<blockquote> 

===(d) Authority for and restoration of 2010 open Internet rules– ===

Notwithstanding sections 3(51) and 332(c)(2) of the Communications Act of 1934 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/47/153 47 U.S.C. 153(51)] ;[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/47/332 332(c)(2)]), the [[w:Federal Communications Commission|Commission]]is authorized by this section to adopt the rules adopted on December 21, 2010, in the Report and Order in the matter of preserving the open Internet and broadband industry practices (FCC 10–201). Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of the  Open Internet Act of 2014  , the [[w:Federal Communications Commission|Commission]]shall take all actions necessary to restore to effect the portions of such rules that were vacated by the [[w:U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit|United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit]]in Verizon v. Federal Communications Commission (No. 11–1355; decided on January 14, 2014). . 
</blockquote>
