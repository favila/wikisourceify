{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide monetary awards to any individual who provides information pertaining to the electronic communications sent by Lois Lerner during her employment at the Internal Revenue Service, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Identify and Recover Sent E-Mails Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Awards in connection with Lois Lerner IRS e-mails=



==(a) Recovery of e-mails– ==

Upon receipt of certification from [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that an individual or group of individuals has retrieved and submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]the electronic communications sent by Lois Lerner during her employment with the [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]], the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]]shall pay a cash award of $1,000,000 to such individual or group of individuals.

==(b) Destruction of e-mails– ==

Upon receipt of certification from the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]that an individual or group of individuals has provided pertinent information sufficient for prosecution of the individuals involved in illegal activities (if any) with respect to the destruction of the electronic communications sent by Lois Lerner during her employment with the [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]], the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]]shall pay a cash award of $500,000 to any such individual or group of individuals.

==(c) Source of funds– ==

The award amounts authorized under this section shall be derived from the unobligated amounts available for fiscal year 2014 in [[Public Law 113-76|Public Law 113–76]]  under the heading“Department of the Treasury—Internal Revenue Service—Taxpayer Services”. 

=Sec. 3. Obligation limitation on IRS employee salaries until Lois Lerner IRS e-mails are recovered=



==(a) In general– ==

Of the amounts made available in [[Public Law 113-76|Public Law 113–76]]  for the salaries for employees of the [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]], not more than 80 percent of such funds may be obligated during fiscal year 2014 until the electronic communications sent by Lois Lerner during her employment with the [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]]have been recovered and submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]].

==(b) Application– ==

The limitation provided in subsection (a) shall cease to apply on the date that Congress certifies to the [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Commissioner of Internal Revenue]]and the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of Personnel Management]]that such e-mails have been recovered.
