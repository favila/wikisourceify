{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide for rates of pay for Members of Congress to be adjusted as a function of changes in Government spending.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Congressional Accountability Pay Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Change in method for adjusting pay=

   Section 601(a) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/31 2 U.S.C. 31])is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

=601. =


:(a) 
::(1) This subsection applies with respect to—
:::(A) each Senator, Member of the House of Representatives, and Delegate to the House of Representatives, and the Resident Commissioner from Puerto Rico ,
:::(B) the [[w:United States Senate|President pro tempore of the Senate]] , the majority leader and the minority leader of the Senate, and the majority leader and the minority leader of the House of Representatives, and
:::(C) the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives]] .
::(2) Whenever outlays for any fiscal year are greater than outlays for the preceding fiscal year, then, effective as of the first day of the first applicable pay period beginning on or after January 1 of the calendar year next beginning after the close of the second of those 2 fiscal years, the rate of pay for each position under paragraph (1) shall be decreased by the percentage (rounded to the nearest1/10of 1 percent) equal to the percentage by which outlays for the second of those 2 fiscal years are greater than outlays for the first of those 2 fiscal years, but in no event below $1.
::(3) The [[w:Congressional Budget Office|Congressional Budget Office]] shall prescribe any regulations necessary to carry out this subsection, including regulations governing the determination of total outlays for a fiscal year.
::(4) For purposes of this subsection, the term outlays means budget outlays, as defined by  section 3 of the Congressional Budget and Impoundment Control Act of 1974 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/622 2 U.S.C. 622]).
::(5) This subsection shall apply for purposes of determining rates of pay for pay periods beginning on or after January 1, 2015. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Coordination rule=



==(a) In general– ==

   Section 225(l)(3) of the Fe deral Salary Act of 1967 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/362 2 U.S.C. 362(3)])is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(C) Notwithstanding any other provision of this subsection, in the case of a position referred to in  section 601(a) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/31 2 U.S.C. 31]), no recommendation referred to in this subsection (in the matter before paragraph (1) ) may be made which, if enacted, would cause the rate of pay for any such position to differ from the rate of pay which would, as of any given time, then be payable with respect to such position under such section 601(a) . . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Exclusion of judges– ==

   Section 225(l)(3)(A) of such Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/362 2 U.S.C. 362(3)(A)])is further amended—
:(1) in clause (i) , by striking all that follows“recommended for”and inserting“the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representative]] s and the [[w:Office of the Vice President of the United States|Vice President]] of the United States, respectively, shall be equal.”; and
:(2) in clause (iii) , by striking all that follows“a Delegate to the House of Representatives,”and that precedes“and each office or position under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5313 section 5313 of title 5] ”.
