{{Center|A BILL}}

To make participation in the American Community Survey voluntary, except with respect to certain basic questions, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H46C73ED5677144B08F64CB07E1198941}}Section 1. Participation in American Community Survey=



=={{anchor|H14F3B00B7F024719855BDD9DE404B0A5}}(a) Optional questions– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/193 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 193] of title 13, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:“The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] shall include, in the instructions for completing any survey authorized under this section, a statement indicating that answering any question other than questions that solicit the information described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/221 section 221(c)(2)(A)] is optional.”.

=={{anchor|id29E630CD3BA64F6CA734734828D71C6F}}(b) Refusal To participate– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/221 Section 221 of title 13, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/221 subsection (a)] , by striking“Whoever, being over eighteen years of age,”and inserting“Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/221 subsection (c)] , any person older than 18 years of age who”; and
:(2) by amending[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/221 subsection (c)] to read as follows:

<blockquote>
::(c) 
:::(1) Notwithstanding any other provision under this title—
::::(A) no person may be compelled to disclose information relative to the person's religious beliefs or to membership in a religious body; and
::::(B) except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/221 paragraph (2)] , no person may be fined or otherwise compelled to answer questions in connection with the survey, conducted by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] , which is commonly referred to as the American Community Survey .
:::(2) [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/221 Paragraph (1)(B)] —
::::(A) shall not apply to any question that elicits—
:::::(i) the name of the respondent;
:::::(ii) contact information for the respondent;
:::::(iii) the date of the response; or
:::::(iv) the number of people living or staying at the same address; and
::::(B) does not waive any penalty imposed for conduct described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/221 subsection (b)] ..
</blockquote>
