{{Center|A BILL}}

 To direct the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission to maintain up-to-date information on its website regarding charges and actions brought by the Commission, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   EEOC Transparency and Accountability Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Availability of information about cases on the EEOC website=



==(a) In general– ==

Beginning not later than 30 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Equal Employment Opportunity Commission]]shall maintain the following up-to-date information on its public website:
:(1) A description of each case brought in court by the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]], not later than 30 days after a judgment is made with respect to any cause of action in the case, without regard to whether the judgment is final. Such description shall identify—
::(A) the court in which the case was brought;
::(B) the name and case number of the case, the nature of the allegation, the causes of action brought, and the outcome of each cause of action in the case;
::(C) each instance in which the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]was ordered to pay fees and costs, including the amount of such fees and costs ordered to be paid and, when applicable, the amount of fees and costs actually paid by the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]and the reason for the fee or cost award;
::(D) whether the case was authorized by a majority vote of the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]or was brought pursuant to the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]’s delegation of authority to the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|General Counsel of the Commission]], including the basis on which the [[w:Office of General Counsel|General Counsel]]determined that submission to the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]for authorization was not necessary and a justification of that decision;
::(E) any case in which a sanction was imposed on the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]], including the amount of such sanction and the reason for the sanction; and
::(F) any appeal and the outcome of the appeal.
:(2) The total number of charges of an alleged unlawful employment practice filed during the preceding fiscal year by a member of the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]], as authorized by the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commissioner]]charge authority under  section 706(b) of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/2000e–5 42 U.S.C. 2000e–5(b)] )and  section 107(a) of the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/12117 42 U.S.C. 12117(a)] ), and the total number of resolutions of such charges disaggregated by type of resolution.
:(3) The total number of charges of an alleged unlawful employment practice filed during the preceding fiscal year as a result of the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]’s use of its directed investigation authority under  section 7(a) of the Age Discrimination in Employment Act of 1967 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/626 29 U.S.C. 626(a)] )and  section 11(a) of the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/211 29 U.S.C. 211(a)] ), and the total number of resolutions of such charges disaggregated by type of resolution.
:(4) Each case of systemic discrimination (including pattern or practice discrimination) brought in court by the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]under section 706 or 707 of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/2000e–5 42 U.S.C. 2000e–5] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/2000e–6 2000e–6]) within the preceding 30 days, the court in which the case was brought, the name and case number of the case, the industry involved, the employment practice or practices at issue, the nature of the alleged discrimination, and the circumstances of the systemic discrimination alleged in the case.

==(b) Disaggregation– ==

With respect to the total number of charges of alleged unlawful employment practices provided under paragraphs (2) and (3) of subsection (a) , the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]shall, on its public website, disaggregate each such total number by the number of such charges filed in each State, and within each State, by the number of such charges alleging discrimination on the basis of, or filed under, each of the following:
:(1) Race.
:(2) Sex.
:(3) National Origin.
:(4) Religion.
:(5) Color.
:(6) Retaliation.
:(7) Age.
:(8) Disability.
:(9)    Section 6(d) of the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/206 29 U.S.C. 206(d)] ).

==(c) Annual performance report– ==

Beginning fiscal year 2015, the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]shall include in its annual performance report under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1116  section 1116of title 31, United States Code], the information described in paragraphs (1) through (4) of subsection (a) for the preceding fiscal year, except that such information shall not be disaggregated in accordance with subsection (b) .

==(d) Definition– ==

For purposes of this section, the term State has the meaning given the term in  section 701 of the Civil Rights Act of 1965 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/2000e 42 U.S.C. 2000e] ). 

=Sec. 3. Good faith conference, conciliation, and persuasion=

   Section 706(b) of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/2000e–5 42 U.S.C. 2000e–5(b)] )is amended—
:(1) in the sixth sentence—
::(A) by striking“shall endeavor”and inserting“shall use good faith efforts to endeavor”; and
::(B) by inserting“bona fide”after“conference,”;
:(2) in the seventh sentence—
::(A) by inserting“, good faith”after“such informal”; and
::(B) by striking“persons concerned”and inserting“employer, employment agency, or labor organization, except for the sole purpose of allowing a party to any pending litigation to present to the reviewing court evidence to ensure the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]’s compliance with its obligations under this section prior to filing suit”; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:“No action or suit may be brought by the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]under this title unless the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]has in good faith exhausted its conciliation obligations as set forth in this subsection. No action or suit shall be brought by the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]unless it has certified that conciliation is at impasse. The determination as to whether the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]engaged in bone fide conciliation efforts shall be subject to judicial review. The [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]’s good faith obligation to engage in bona fide conciliation shall include providing the employer, employment agency, or labor organization believed to have engaged in an unlawful employment practice with all information regarding the legal and factual bases for the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]’s determination that reasonable causes exist as well as all information that supports the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]’s requested monetary and other relief (including a detailed description of the specific individuals or employees comprising the class of persons for whom the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]is seeking relief and any additional information requested that is reasonably related to the underlying cause determination or necessary to conciliate in good faith).”. 

=Sec. 4. Reporting to Congress regarding cases in which the EEOC is ordered to pay fees and costs or sanctions=



==(a) Investigation and report of Inspector General– ==

In any case brought by the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Equal Employment Opportunity Commission]]in which a court orders the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]to pay fees and costs or imposes a sanction on the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]], the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Inspector General of the Commission]]shall—
:(1) notify the [[w:United States House Committee on Education and the Workforce|Committee on Education and the Workforce of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions|Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions of the Senate]]of the order or sanction within 14 days of the court’s decision, including the name of the case, the nature of the court’s determination, and the amount of fees and costs ordered or the amount of the sanction imposed by the court; and
:(2) conduct an investigation to determine why an order for sanction, fees, or costs was imposed by the court, and, not later than 90 days after the court’s decision, submit a report to the [[w:United States House Committee on Education and the Workforce|Committee on Education and the Workforce of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions|Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions of the Senate]]that includes—
::(A) information obtained from interviews and affidavits of each member and staff person of the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]involved in the case;
::(B) the amount of resources allocated to the case, including in terms of full-time equivalents;
::(C) a comparison of the case to other cases in which a court ordered fees and costs or imposed sanctions against the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]];
::(D) if the determination to bring the case was not by a vote of the full [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]], the reasons such a vote was not held; and
::(E) any other relevant information.

==(b) Report of the Commission– ==

For any case described in subsection (a) , the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]], in consultation with the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|General Counsel of the Commission]], shall—
:(1) not later than 60 days after the court’s decision, submit a report to the [[w:United States House Committee on Education and the Workforce|Committee on Education and the Workforce of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions|Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions of the Senate]]detailing the steps the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]is taking to reduce instances in which a court orders the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]to pay fees and costs or imposes a sanction on the [[w:Equal Employment Opportunity Commission|Commission]]; and
:(2) not later than 30 days after the report is submitted to the [[w:United States House Committee on Education and the Workforce|Committee on Education and the Workforce of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions|Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions of the Senate]]under paragraph (1) , post such report on its public website.
