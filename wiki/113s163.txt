{{Center|A BILL}}

To prohibit any regulation regarding carbon dioxide or other greenhouse gas emissions reduction in the United States until China, India, and Russia implement similar reductions.

={{anchor|id5EE293E6FC4F44B4A43BE292BC16B537}}Section 1. Definition of Administrator=

In this Act, the term Administrator means the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]] .

={{anchor|id200A0E060F404514B88955CA6A68C388}}Sec. 2. Findings=

 [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] finds that—
:(1) in 1997, the Senate adopted Senate Resolution 98, 105th Congress, agreed to July 25, 1997, which expressed the sense of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] that the United States should not accept any agreement that would mandate new commitments to limit or reduce greenhouse gas emissions by developed countries unless the agreement also mandated new specific scheduled commitments to limit or reduce greenhouse gas emissions by developing countries within the same compliance period; and
:(2) the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] continues to move forward with the regulation of carbon dioxide emissions, however, the People’s Republic of China, India, and the Russian Federation do not impose similar regulations on carbon dioxide emissions.

={{anchor|idB799327EAD7C4DC58BA6CE3C1A1C93AE}}Sec. 3. Carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gas emissions reductions in China, India, and Russia=

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] or the head of any other Federal agency or department shall not regulate or continue to implement or enforce any regulations, proposals, or actions establishing any carbon dioxide or greenhouse gas emissions reductions until the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] , the [[w:Energy Information Administration|Administrator of the Energy Information Agency]] , and the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] certify in writing that—
:(1) the People’s Republic of China, India, and the Russian Federation have proposed, implemented, and enforced measures requiring carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gas emissions reductions; and
:(2) the reductions described in paragraph (1)are substantially similar to the carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gas emission reductions proposed by the Administrator or the head of any other Federal agency or department for the United States.

={{anchor|idD179E5C6AE4241A99E9D616595BC3BCA}}Sec. 4. Repeal=

Any regulation, proposal, or action in effect before, on, or after the date of enactment of this Act, but before the date on which the certification under section 3is made, that requires any carbon dioxide or other greenhouse gas emissions reduction shall have no force or effect.
