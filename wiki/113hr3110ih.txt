{{Center|A BILL}}

To allow for the harvest of gull eggs by the Huna Tlingit people within Glacier Bay National Park in the State of Alaska.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Huna Tlingit Traditional Gull Egg Use Act” .

=Sec. 2. Limited authorization for collection of gull eggs=



==(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] (referred to in this Act as the Secretary ) may allow the collection by members of the Hoonah Indian Association of the eggs of glaucous-winged gulls (Laurus glaucescens) within Glacier Bay National Park (referred to in this Act as the Park ) not more frequently than twice each calendar year at up to 5 locations within the Park, subject to any terms and conditions that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] determines to be necessary.

==(b) Applicable law– ==

For the purposes of sections and 816 of the Alaska National Interest Lands Conservation Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/410hh–2 16 U.S.C. 410hh–2] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/3126 3126] ), the collection of eggs of glaucous-winged gulls within the Park in accordance with subsection (a)shall be considered to be a use specifically permitted by that Act.

==(c) Harvest plan– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall establish schedules, locations, and any additional terms and conditions that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] determines to be necessary for the harvesting of eggs of glaucous-winged gulls in the Park, based on an annual harvest plan to be prepared by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] and the Hoonah Indian Association.
