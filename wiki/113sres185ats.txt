{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

To authorize representation by the Senate Legal Counsel in the case ofR. Wayne Patterson v. United States Senate, et al.Whereas, the United States Senate, Vice President Joseph R. Biden, Jr., and Senate Parliamentarian Elizabeth C. MacDonough have been named as defendants in the case ofR. Wayne Patterson v. United States Senate, et al., No. 13–cv–2311, now pending in the United States District Court for the Northern District of California;Whereas, pursuant to sections 703(a)and 704(a)(1) of the Ethics in Government Act of 1978, [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/288b 2 U.S.C. §§ 288b(a)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/288c 288c(a)(1)] , the [[w:|Senate]] may direct its counsel to defend the [[w:|Senate]] and officers and employees of the Senate in civil actions relating to their official responsibilities: Now, therefore, be it

= =

That the [[w:|Senate Legal Counsel]] is authorized to represent the [[w:|United States Senate]] , Vice President Joseph R. Biden, Jr., and Senate Parliamentarian Elizabeth C. MacDonough in the case ofR. Wayne Patterson v. United States Senate, et al.
