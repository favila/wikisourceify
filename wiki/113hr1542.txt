{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Homeland Security Act of 2002 to establish weapons of mass destruction intelligence and information sharing functions of the Office of Intelligence and Analysis of the Department of Homeland Security and to require dissemination of information analyzed by the Department to entities with responsibilities relating to homeland security, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H26319FF4E1544C9886CDA349B4424A6D}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“WMD Intelligence and Information Sharing Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HCFA4C6571B7447939C2994A9A13358C9}}Sec. 2. Weapons of mass destruction intelligence and information sharing=



=={{anchor|HBD550B758EAF4D68A46CF19ECDC27744}}(a) In general– ==

 Subtitle A of title II of the Homeland Security Act of 2002([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/6/121 6 U.S.C. 121 et seq.] ] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H77BD1811B4C3441CA44DD1D22F2FC6FC}}Sec. 210G. Weapons of mass destruction intelligence and information sharing==



==={{anchor|H77EF3CE39A23457299B39C21664F0FE5}}(a) In general– ===

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Office of Intelligence and Analysis of the Department of Homeland Security]] shall—
:(1) support homeland security-focused intelligence analysis of terrorist actors, their claims, and their plans to conduct attacks involving chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear materials against the Nation;
:(2) support homeland security-focused intelligence analysis of global infectious disease, public health, food, agricultural, and veterinary issues;
:(3) support homeland security-focused risk analysis and risk assessments of the homeland security hazards described in paragraphs (1)and (2)by providing relevant quantitative and nonquantitative threat information;
:(4) leverage existing and emerging homeland security intelligence capabilities and structures to enhance prevention, protection, response, and recovery efforts with respect to a chemical, biological, radiological, or nuclear attack;
:(5) share information and provide tailored analytical support on these threats to State, local, and tribal authorities as well as other national biosecurity and biodefense stakeholders; and
:(6) perform other responsibilities, as assigned by the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] .

==={{anchor|H84B050DFA1F54A47A9C9F669B2EFEC2A}}(b) Coordination– ===

Where appropriate, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Office of Intelligence and Analysis]] shall coordinate with other relevant [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department]] components, others in the Intelligence Community, including the National Counter Proliferation Center, and other Federal, State, local, and tribal authorities, including officials from high-threat areas, and enable such entities to provide recommendations on optimal information sharing mechanisms, including expeditious sharing of classified information, and on how they can provide information to the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department]] .

==={{anchor|HE3E2845664B24640964F0FA897F7C04D}}(c) Report– ===



===={{anchor|HCB556F53A307420E8DDAA0B25FA24D56}}(1) In general– ====

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this section and annually thereafter, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall report to the appropriate congressional committees on—
:(A) the intelligence and information sharing activities under subsection (a) and of all relevant entities within the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department]] to counter the threat from weapons of mass destruction; and
:(B) the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department’s]] activities in accordance with relevant intelligence strategies.

===={{anchor|H515F48EDC76A400D88760CAEECC0CF92}}(2) Assessment of implementation– ====

The report shall include—
:(A) a description of methods established to assess progress of the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Office of Intelligence and Analysis]] in implementing this section; and
:(B) such assessment.

==={{anchor|HF50203F0CEFD4B24A5E33520FFA85214}}(d) Definitions– ===

In this section:
:(1) The term appropriate congressional committees means theCommittee on Homeland Security of the House of Representativesand any committee of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] or the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] having legislative jurisdiction under the rules of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] or [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] , respectively, over the matter concerned.
:(2) The term intelligence community has the meaning given that term in section 3(4) of the National Security Act of 1947([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/401a 50 U.S.C. 401a(4)] ).
:(3) The term national biosecurity and biodefense stakeholders means officials from the Federal, State, local, and tribal authorities and individuals from the private sector who are involved in efforts to prevent, protect against, respond to, and recover from a biological attack or other phenomena that may have serious health consequences for the United States, including wide-scale fatalities or infectious disease outbreaks..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|H4E81E73647A64ED09FE611D8C67F4475}}Sec. 3. Dissemination of information analyzed by the Department to State, local, tribal, and private entities with responsibilities relating to homeland security=

 Section 201(d)(8) of the Homeland Security Act of 2002([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/6/121 6 U.S.C. 121(d)(8)] ] )is amended by striking“and to agencies of State”and all that follows and inserting“to State, local, tribal, and private entities with such responsibilities, and, as appropriate, to the public, in order to assist in preventing, deterring, or responding to acts of terrorism against the United States.”.
