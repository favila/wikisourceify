{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend titles 5 and 28, United States Code, with respect to the award of fees and other expenses in cases brought against agencies of the United States, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Government Litigation Savings Act” .

=Sec. 2. Modification of equal access to justice provisions=



==(a) Agency proceedings– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 Section 504of title 5, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 subsection (a)] —
::(A) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 paragraph (1)] ——
:::(i) by inserting after the first sentence the following: “Fees and other expenses may be awarded under this subsection only to a prevailing party who has a direct and personal interest in the adversary adjudication because of medical costs, property damage, determination of benefits, unpaid disbursement, fees and other expenses incurred in defense of the adjudication, interest in a policy concerning such medical costs, property damage, determination of benefits, unpaid disbursement, fees and other expenses, or otherwise.” ; and
:::(ii) by adding at the end the following: “The agency conducting the adversary adjudication shall make any party against whom the adjudication is brought, at the time the adjudication is commenced, aware of the provisions of this section.” ; and
::(B) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 paragraph (3)] , in the first sentence—
:::(i) by striking “may reduce” and inserting “shall reduce” ; and
:::(ii) by striking “unduly and unreasonably” and inserting “unduly or unreasonably” ;
:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 subsection (b)(1)] —
::(A) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 subparagraph (A)(ii)] , by striking “$125 per hour” and all that follows through the end and inserting “$200 per hour.);” ; and
::(B) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 subparagraph (B)(ii)] , by striking “; except that” and all that follows through “section 601;” and inserting

<blockquote>except that—
:::(I) the net worth of a party (other than an individual or a unit of local government) shall include the net worth of any parent entity or subsidiary of that party; and
:::(II) for purposes of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 subclause (I)] —
::::(aa) a parent entity of a party is an entity that owns or controls the equity or other evidences of ownership in that party; and
::::(bb) a subsidiary of a party is an entity the equity or other evidences of ownership in which are owned or controlled by that party;;
</blockquote>

:(3) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 subsection (c)(1)] , by striking “, United States Code” ; and
:(4) by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>
::(g) The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall adjust the maximum hourly fee set forth in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 subsection (b)(1)(A)(ii)] for the fiscal year beginning October 1, 2015, and for each fiscal year thereafter, to reflect changes in the Consumer Price Index, as determined by the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]] ..
</blockquote>


==(b) Court cases– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 Section 2412(d)of title 28, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by amending [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 paragraph (1)(A)] to read as follows:

<blockquote>
::(A) Except as otherwise specifically provided by statute, a court, in any civil action (other than cases sounding in tort), including proceedings for judicial review of agency action, brought by or against the United States in any court having jurisdiction of that action, shall award to a prevailing party (other than the United States) fees and other expenses, in addition to any costs awarded pursuant to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 subsection (a)] , incurred by that party in the civil action, unless the court finds that the position of the United States was substantially justified or that special circumstances make an award unjust. Fees and other expenses may be awarded under this paragraph only to a prevailing party who has a direct and personal interest in the civil action because of medical costs, property damage, determination of benefits, unpaid disbursement, fees and other expenses incurred in defense of the civil action, interest in a policy concerning such medical costs, property damage, determination of benefits, unpaid disbursement, fees and other expenses, or otherwise.;
</blockquote>

:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 paragraph (1)(C)] —
::(A) by striking “court, in its discretion, may” and inserting “court shall” ; and
::(B) by striking “unduly and unreasonably” and inserting “unduly or unreasonably” ;
:(3) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 paragraph (2)] —
::(A) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 subparagraph (A)(ii)] , by striking “$125” and all that follows through the end and inserting “$200 per hour.);” ;
::(B) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 subparagraph (B)(ii)] , by striking “; except that” and all that follows through “ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/601 section 601 of Title 5] ;” and inserting

<blockquote>except that—
:::(I) the net worth of a party (other than an individual or a unit of local government) shall include the net worth of any parent entity or subsidiary of that party; and
:::(II) for purposes of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 subclause (I)] —
::::(aa) a parent entity of a party is an entity that owns or controls the equity or other evidences of ownership in that party; and
::::(bb) a subsidiary of a party is an entity the equity or other evidences of ownership in which are owned or controlled by that party;; and
</blockquote>

:(4) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(5) The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall adjust the maximum hourly fee set forth in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 paragraph (2)(A)(ii)] for the fiscal year beginning October 1, 2015, and for each fiscal year thereafter, to reflect changes in the Consumer Price Index, as determined by the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]] ..
</blockquote>


==(c) Clerical amendments– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 Section 2412of title 28, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 subsection (d)(3)] , by striking “United States Code,” ; and
:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 subsection (e)] —
::(A) by striking “of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 section 2412] of title 28, United States Code,” and inserting “of this section” ; and
::(B) by striking “of such title” and inserting “of this title” .

==(d) Effective date– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Subject to paragraph (2), this Act and the amendments made by this Act shall take effect on the date of the enactment of this Act.

===(2) Applicability of certain amendments– ===

The amendments made by subsections (a)and (b)shall first apply with respect to awards of fees and other expenses that are made under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/504 section 504of title 5, United States Code] , or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/2412 section 2412(d)of title 28, United States Code] , on or after the date of the enactment of this Act.
