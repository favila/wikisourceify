{{Center|A BILL}}

 To direct the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to reimburse non-Department of Veterans Affairs medical providers for the provision of certain hospital care and medical services to veterans, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Access to Care and Treatment Now for Veterans Act   ”or the“  ACT Now for Veterans Act  ”. 

=Sec. 2. Reimbursement of non-Department of Veterans Affairs medical providers for the provision of certain hospital care and medical services to veterans=



==(a) Reimbursement required– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall reimburse any non- [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]medical provider, including such a provider who has not been pre-approved by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]], who furnishes covered hospital care or medical services to an eligible veteran.

==(b) Definitions– ==

For purposes of this section:
:(1) The term“eligible veteran”means a veteran who—
::(A) is enrolled in the patient enrollment system under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705  section 1705of title 38, United States Code], and provides a [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]identification card or proof of such enrollment;
::(B) is eligible to receive hospital care or medical services furnished by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]; and
::(C) attempts or has attempted to schedule an appointment at a [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]facility to receive such care or services but has been unable to schedule such appointment within 30 days of the date preferred by the veteran.
:(2) The term“covered hospital care or medical services”means, with respect to an eligible veteran, hospital care or medical services (as such terms are defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1701  section 1701of title 38, United States Code]) for which the veteran is eligible under chapter 17of title 38, United States Code.

==(c) Termination– ==

The authority under this section shall terminate on—
:(1) in the case that, before the date that is one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]submits to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]certification that the [[w:Veterans Health Administration|Veterans Health Administration]]is meeting wait-time goals established by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]], the date that is one year after the date of the enactment of this Act; or
:(2) in the case that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]does not submit such certification before such date, the date that is two years after the date of the enactment of this Act.

==(d) Implementation– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall begin implementing this section on the date of the enactment of this Act.
