{{Center|A BILL}}

 To establish the Wildlife and Hunting Heritage Conservation Council Advisory Committee to advise the Secretaries of the Interior and Agriculture on wildlife and habitat conservation, hunting, recreational shooting, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Sportsmen’s Heritage And Recreational Enhancement (SHARE) Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Wildlife and Hunting Heritage Conservation Council Advisory Committee=

The  Fish and Wildlife Coordination Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/661 16 U.S.C. 661 et seq.])is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

=Sec. 10. Wildlife and Hunting Heritage Conservation Council Advisory Committee=



==(a) Establishment– ==

There is hereby established the [[w:|Wildlife and Hunting Heritage Conservation Council Advisory Committee]] (in this section referred to as the Advisory Committee ) to advise the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretaries of the Interior]] and [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Agriculture]] on wildlife and habitat conservation, hunting, and recreational shooting.

==(b) Duties of the advisory committee– ==

The [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall advise the Secretaries with regard to—
:(1) implementation of Executive Order No. 13443: Facilitation of Hunting Heritage and Wildlife Conservation, which directs Federal agencies“to facilitate the expansion and enhancement of hunting opportunities and the management of game species and their habitat”;
:(2) policies or programs to conserve and restore wetlands, agricultural lands, grasslands, forest, and rangeland habitats;
:(3) policies or programs to promote opportunities and access to hunting and shooting sports on Federal lands;
:(4) policies or programs to recruit and retain new hunters and shooters;
:(5) policies or programs that increase public awareness of the importance of wildlife conservation and the social and economic benefits of recreational hunting and shooting; and
:(6) policies or programs that encourage coordination among the public, the hunting and shooting sports community, wildlife conservation groups, and States, tribes, and the Federal Government.

==(c) Membership– ==



===(1) Appointment– ===



====(A) In general– ====

The [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall consist of no more than 16 discretionary members and 7 ex officio members.

====(B) Ex officio members– ====

The ex officio members are—
:(i) the [[w:U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service|Director of the United States Fish and Wildlife Service]] or a designated representative of the Director;
:(ii) the [[w:Bureau of Land Management|Director of the Bureau of Land Management]] or a designated representative of the Director;
:(iii) the [[w:National Park Service|Director of the National Park Service]] or a designated representative of the Director;
:(iv) the [[w:United States Forest Service|Chief of the Forest Service]] or a designated representative of the Chief;
:(v) the [[w:Natural Resources Conservation Service|Chief of the Natural Resources Conservation Service]] or a designated representative of the Chief;
:(vi) the [[w:Farm Service Agency|Administrator of the Farm Service Agency]] or a designated representative of the Administrator; and
:(vii) the Executive Director of the Association of Fish and Wildlife Agencies.

====(C) Discretionary members– ====

The discretionary members shall be appointed jointly by the Secretaries from at least one of each of the following:
:(i) State fish and wildlife agencies.
:(ii) Game bird hunting organizations.
:(iii) Wildlife conservation organizations.
:(iv) Big game hunting organizations.
:(v) The tourism, outfitter, or guiding industry.
:(vi) The firearms or ammunition manufacturing industry.
:(vii) The hunting or shooting equipment retail industry.
:(viii) Tribal resource management organizations.
:(ix) The agriculture industry.
:(x) The ranching industry.

====(D) Eligibility– ====

Prior to the appointment of the discretionary members, the Secretaries shall determine that all individuals nominated for appointment to the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] , and the organization each individual represents, actively support and promote sustainable-use hunting, wildlife conservation, and recreational shooting.

===(2) Terms– ===



====(A) In general– ====

Except as provided in subparagraph (B) , members of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall be appointed for a term of 4 years. Members shall not be appointed for more than 3 consecutive or nonconsecutive terms.

====(B) Terms of initial appointees– ====

As designated by the Secretary at the time of appointment, of the members first appointed—
:(i) 6 members shall be appointed for a term of 4 years;
:(ii) 5 members shall be appointed for a term of 3 years; and
:(iii) 5 members shall be appointed for a term of 2 years.

===(3) Preservation of public advisory status– ===

No individual may be appointed as a discretionary member of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] while serving as an officer or employee of the Federal Government.

===(4) Vacancy and removal– ===



====(A) In general– ====

Any vacancy on the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall be filled in the manner in which the original appointment was made.

====(B) Removal– ====

  [[w:|Advisory Committee]] members shall serve at the discretion of the Secretaries and may be removed at any time for good cause.

===(5) Continuation of service– ===

Each appointed member may continue to serve after the expiration of the term of office to which such member was appointed until a successor has been appointed.

===(6) Chairperson– ===

The [[w:|Chairperson of the Advisory Committee]] shall be appointed for a 3-year term by the Secretaries, jointly, from among the members of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] . An individual may not be appointed as Chairperson for more than 2 consecutive or nonconsecutive terms.

===(7) Pay and expenses– ===

Members of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall serve without pay for such service, but each member of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall be reimbursed for travel and lodging incurred through attending meetings of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] approved subgroup meetings in the same amounts and under the same conditions as Federal employees (in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5703  section 5703of title 5, United States Code]).

===(8) Meetings– ===



====(A) In general– ====

The [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall meet at the call of the Secretaries, the chairperson, or a majority of the members, but not less frequently than twice annually.

====(B) Open meetings– ====

Each meeting of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall be open to the public.

====(C) Prior notice of meetings– ====

Timely notice of each meeting of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall be published in the Federal Register and be submitted to trade publications and publications of general circulation.

====(D) Subgroups– ====

The [[w:|Advisory Committee]] may establish such workgroups or subgroups as it deems necessary for the purpose of compiling information or conducting research. However, such workgroups may not conduct business without the direction of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] and must report in full to the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] .

===(9) Quorum– ===

Nine members of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall constitute a quorum.

==(d) Expenses– ==

The expenses of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] that the Secretaries determine to be reasonable and appropriate shall be paid by the Secretaries.

==(e) Administrative support, technical services, and advice– ==

A designated Federal Officer shall be jointly appointed by the Secretaries to provide to the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] the administrative support, technical services, and advice that the Secretaries determine to be reasonable and appropriate.

==(f) Annual report– ==



===(1) Required– ===

Not later than September 30 of each year, the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall submit a report to the Secretaries, the [[w:United States House Committee on Natural Resources|Committee on Natural Resources]] and the [[w:United States House Committee on Agriculture|Committee on Agriculture of the House of Representatives]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources|Committee on Energy and Natural Resources]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Agriculture, Nutrition and Forestry|Committee on Agriculture, Nutrition, and Forestry of the Senate]] . If circumstances arise in which the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] cannot meet the September 30 deadline in any year, the Secretaries shall advise the Chairpersons of each such Committee of the reasons for such delay and the date on which the submission of the report is anticipated.

===(2) Contents– ===

The report required by paragraph (1) shall describe—
:(A) the activities of the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] during the preceding year;
:(B) the reports and recommendations made by the [[w:|Advisory Committee]] to the Secretaries during the preceding year; and
:(C) an accounting of actions taken by the Secretaries as a result of the recommendations.

==(g) Federal advisory committee act– ==

The [[w:|Advisory Committee]] shall be exempt from the  Federal Advisory Committee Act (5 U.S.C. App.).

==(h) Abolishment of the existing wildlife and hunting heritage conservation council advisory committee– ==

Effective on the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service|Wildlife and Hunting Heritage Conservation Council]] formed in furtherance of  section 441 of the Revised Statutes ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1457 43 U.S.C. 1457] ), the  Fish and Wildlife Act of 1956 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/742a 16 U.S.C. 742a] ), and other Acts applicable to specific bureaus of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Department of the Interior]] is hereby abolished. .
