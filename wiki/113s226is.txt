{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 to provide leave because of the death of a son or daughter.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Parental Bereavement Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Family leave because of the death of a son or daughter=



==(a) Family leave– ==



===(1) Entitlement to leave– ===

 Section 102(a)(1) of the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2612 29 U.S.C. 2612(a)(1)] )is amended by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote>
:(F) Because of the death of a son or daughter..
</blockquote>


===(2) Requirements relating to leave– ===



====(A) Schedule– ====

 Section 102(b)(1) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2612 29 U.S.C. 2612(b)(1)] )is amended by inserting after the third sentence the following new sentence: “Leave under subsection (a)(1)(F)shall not be taken by an employee intermittently or on a reduced leave schedule unless the employee and the employer of the employee agree otherwise.” .

====(B) Substitution of paid leave– ====

 Section 102(d)(2)(B) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2612 29 U.S.C. 2612(d)(2)(B)] )is amended, in the first sentence, by striking “(C) or (D)” and inserting “(C), (D), or (F)” .

====(C) Notice– ====

 Section 102(e) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2612 29 U.S.C. 2612(e)] )is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

=====(4) Notice for leave due to death of a son or daughter– =====

In any case in which the necessity for leave under subsection (a)(1)(F)is foreseeable, the employee shall provide such notice to the employer as is reasonable and practicable..
</blockquote>


====(D) Spouses employed by same employer– ====

 Section 102(f)(1)(A) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2612 29 U.S.C. 2612(f)(1)(A)] )is amended by striking “ subparagraph (A)or (B)” and inserting “ subparagraph (A), (B), or (F)” .

====(E) Certification requirements– ====

 Section 103 of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2613 29 U.S.C. 2613] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=====(g) Certification related to the death of a son or daughter– =====

An employer may require that a request for leave under section 102(a)(1)(F)be supported by a certification issued at such time and in such manner as the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary]] may by regulation prescribe. If the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary]] issues a regulation requiring such certification, the employee shall provide, in a timely manner, a copy of such certification to the employer..
</blockquote>


====(F) Failure to return from leave– ====

 Section 104(c) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2614 29 U.S.C. 2614(c)] )is amended—
:(i) in paragraph (2)(B)(i), by inserting before the semicolon the following: “, or a death that entitles the employee to leave under section 102(a)(1)(F)” ; and
:(ii) in paragraph (3)(A)—
::(I) in the matter preceding clause (i), by inserting “, or the death,” before “described” ;
::(II) in clause (ii), by striking “or” at the end;
::(III) by redesignating clause (iii)as clause (iv); and
::(IV) by inserting after clause (ii)the following:

<blockquote>
:::(iii) a certification that meets such requirements as the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary]] may by regulation prescribe, in the case of an employee unable to return to work because of a death specified in section 102(a)(1)(F); or.
</blockquote>


====(G) Employees of local educational agencies– ====

 Section 108 of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2618 29 U.S.C. 2618] )is amended—
:(i) in subsection (c)—
::(I) in paragraph (1)—
:::(aa) in the matter preceding subparagraph (A), by inserting after “medical treatment” the following: “, or under section 102(a)(1)(F)that is foreseeable,” ; and
:::(bb) in subparagraph (A), by inserting after “to exceed” the following: “(except in the case of leave under section 102(a)(1)(F))” ; and
::(II) in paragraph (2), by striking “ section 102(e)(2)” and inserting “ paragraphs (2)and (4) of section 102(e), as applicable” ; and
:(ii) in subsection (d), in paragraph (2) and (3), by striking “or (C)” each place it appears and inserting “ (C), or (F)” .

==(b) Family leave for civil service employees– ==



===(1) Entitlement to leave– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 Section 6382(a)(1) of title 5, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(F) Because of the death of a son or daughter..
</blockquote>


===(2) Requirements relating to leave– ===



====(A) Schedule– ====

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 Section 6382(b)(1) of such title] is amended by inserting after the third sentence the following new sentence: “Leave under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 subsection (a)(1)(F)] shall not be taken by an employee intermittently or on a reduced leave schedule unless the employee and the employing agency of the employee agree otherwise.” .

====(B) Substitution of paid leave– ====

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 Section 6382(d) of such title] is amended, in the first sentence, by striking “or (E)” and inserting “(E), or (F)” .

====(C) Notice– ====

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 Section 6382(e) of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>
:(4) In any case in which the necessity for leave under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/6382/e subsection (a)(1)(F)] is foreseeable, the employee shall provide such notice to the employing agency as is reasonable and practicable..
</blockquote>


====(D) Certification requirements– ====

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6383 Section 6383 of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(g) An employing agency may require that a request for leave under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 section 6382(a)(1)(F)] be supported by a certification issued at such time and in such manner as the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] may by regulation prescribe. If the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office]] issues a regulation requiring such certification, the employee shall provide, in a timely manner, a copy of such certification to the employer..
</blockquote>
