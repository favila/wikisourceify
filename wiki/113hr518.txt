{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Reclamation States Emergency Drought Relief Act of 1991 for the purposes of extending the Reclamation States Emergency Drought Relief Act of 1991 through 2018, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HF00BD1E9676147848207A4C02A7894BC}}Section 1. Drought relief=



=={{anchor|H21B49C2B76C541468092C3649EC3135F}}(a) Termination of authority– ==

 Section 104(c) of the Reclamation States Emergency Drought Relief Act of 1991([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/2214 43 U.S.C. 2214(c)] )is amended by striking“2012”and inserting“2018”.

=={{anchor|H0D5829C264F24F6E8EC2AFC788922ADD}}(b) Authorization of appropriations– ==

 Section 301 of the Reclamation States Emergency Drought Relief Act of 1991([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/2241 43 U.S.C. 2241] )is amended—
:(1) by striking“90,000,000”and inserting“$110,000,000”; and
:(2) by striking“2012”and inserting“2018”.

={{anchor|HC8477E8AA7434D28A60DF409764F80EE}}Sec. 2. Climate variability review=

 Subsection (e) of section 203 of the Reclamation States Emergency Drought Relief Act of 1991([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/2223 43 U.S.C. 2223(e)] )is amended as follows:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H9EE37FC479B347879DF52DA194FF85E4}}(e) Review– ==

The contingency plans shall include provisions for periodic review to assure adequacy of the contingency plan to respond to current conditions, as well as address projected long-term climate variability and change, and such plans may be modified accordingly..
</blockquote>
