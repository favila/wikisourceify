{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 5, United States Code, to reform the provisions of law commonly referred to as the“Hatch Act”.

={{anchor|H7563A10281484761BDB875AA1A147F33}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“State and Local Law Enforcement Hatch Act Reform Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H9BC4687E1A3F433983ED9F013D8C93D6}}Sec. 2. Political activities=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1502 Section 1502 of title 5, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(d) [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1502 Subsection (a)(1)] does not prohibit a sheriff from participating in political campaigns for, or endorsing, political candidates running for elective office by—
::(1) attending or speaking at political campaign rallies or events;
::(2) holding or sponsoring political fundraisers; or
::(3) appearing on political advertisements, including print, radio, television, or any other form of advertising..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H556D942C620744A09F4C0B438FB18208}}Sec. 3. Statute of limitations=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1504 Section 1504 of title 5, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by inserting“(a)In general.—”before“When”; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H5223BF4768504C779B6FE480035AF24D}}(b) Statute of limitations for law enforcement officers– ===

With respect to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1502 paragraphs (1)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1502 (3) of section 1502(a)] , the Special Counsel may not present any charges against a law enforcement officer under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1502 subsection (a)] after the end of the 6-month period beginning on the later of—
:(1) the date of the alleged violation of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1502 paragraph (1)] or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1502 (3) of section 1502(a)] , as the case may be; or
:(2) the date of the enactment of the State and Local Law Enforcement Hatch Act Reform Act of 2013..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H561F18A349164737BDF7C998FC3561A2}}Sec. 4. Definitions=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1501 Section 1501 of title 5, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1501 paragraph (3)] , by striking“and”after the semicolon;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1501 paragraph (4)] , by striking the period and inserting a semicolon; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(5) “sheriff”means an individual who holds the elected Office of Sheriff, as defined by State constitution or State statute, of a county, town, township, parish, village, or other general purpose political subdivision of a State; and
::(6) “law enforcement officer”means a State or local officer or employee whose duties are primarily the investigation, apprehension, or detention of individuals suspected or convicted of offenses against the criminal laws of a State or local jurisdiction, including an officer or employee engaged in this activity who is transferred to a supervisory or administrative position..
</blockquote>
