{{Center|A BILL}}

To make payments by the Department of Homeland Security to a State contingent on a State providing the Federal Bureau of Investigation with certain statistics, to require Federal agencies, departments, and courts to provide such statistics to the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and to require the Federal Bureau of Investigation to publish such statistics.

={{anchor|H12A95086025F46B796757FCE5E089B39}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Jamiel Shaw, Jr. Memorial Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H6127CE475D76457680008FE2BB28FBB9}}Sec. 2. Payments by the Department of Homeland Security contingent on State immigration reporting=



=={{anchor|HB3C6CDF3510B4838BE733C01E5B588D6}}(a) In general– ==

A State or any political subdivision thereof may not receive funds under any program or activity administered by the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]] unless the State—
:(1) for each person who is arrested, charged with a crime, convicted of a crime, or incarcerated after being convicted of a crime by the State or any political subdivision thereof, compiles statistics on such person, including—
::(A) the immigration status of such person;
::(B) the country of origin of such person; and
::(C) the crime associated with such arrest, charge, conviction, or incarceration after conviction;
:(2) reports such statistics monthly to the [[w:Federal Bureau of Investigation|Federal Bureau of Investigation]] ; and
:(3) monthly certifies compliance with paragraphs (1)and (2)to the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] .

=={{anchor|H9B1D872EEDEB4651B469A814E584E231}}(b) Effective date– ==

This section shall take effect with respect to funds made available under a program or activity administered by the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]] for fiscal years beginning on or after October 1, 2013.

={{anchor|H50FCB5BAEB634DE6B37FE578655A33C5}}Sec. 3. Federal immigration reporting requirement=



=={{anchor|H1348A47DF8AC460CA1EA18FEAC1176E7}}(a) In general– ==

Each Federal agency and department shall—
:(1) for any person such agency or department arrests, charges with a crime, prosecutes to conviction of a crime, or incarcerates after conviction of a crime, compile statistics on such person, including—
::(A) the immigration status of such person;
::(B) the country of origin of such person; and
::(C) the crime associated with such arrest, charge, conviction, or incarceration after conviction; and
:(2) report such statistics monthly to the [[w:Federal Bureau of Investigation|Federal Bureau of Investigation]] .

=={{anchor|H4BC54C237CAC4C2DA33B50D3C730EEF2}}(b) Effective date– ==

This section shall take effect with respect to any person arrested, charged with a crime, prosecuted to conviction of a crime, or incarcerated after conviction of a crime by a Federal agency or department on or after October 1, 2013.

={{anchor|H17C0DAACD6D047A980A60446C14A0CB6}}Sec. 4. Statistics to be published=

The [[w:Federal Bureau of Investigation|Federal Bureau of Investigation]] shall annually publish all statistics reported under this Act as a separate data collection of the Uniform Crime Reports issued by such [[w:Federal Bureau of Investigation|Bureau]] .

={{anchor|H5A5F785580C4437E859645477C743AF6}}Sec. 5. Definition=

In this Act, the term crime means an offense listed in Table 4 of the Uniform Crime Report of the Federal Bureau of Investigation, issued pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/534 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 534(a)] of title 28, United States Code] .
