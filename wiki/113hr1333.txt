{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to grant family of members of the uniformed services temporary annual leave during the deployment of such members, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HDCA7938646BA421EBCAD621F4B42558A}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Military Family Leave Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H8D8B2A0BBBC84B44833464EB4E934FD0}}Sec. 2. Annual leave for family of deployed members of the uniformed services=



=={{anchor|H51A5B99772E34BF3A063C0238424A37C}}(a) In general– ==

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|HE10EE99BB6A54FE29E8E753033F5F74F}}Sec. 4401. Definitions===

In this chapter:
:(1) The terms benefit , rights and benefits , employee , employer , and uniformed services have the meaning given such terms in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4303 section 4303 of this title] .
:(2) The term contingency operation has the same meaning given such term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/101 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 101(a)(13)] of title 10] .
:(3) The term eligible employee means an individual who is—
::(A) a family member of a member of a uniformed service; and
::(B) an employee of the employer with respect to whom leave is requested under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 section 4402 of this title] .
:(4) The term family member means an individual who is, with respect to another individual, one of the following:
::(A) The spouse of the other individual.
::(B) A son or daughter of the other individual.
::(C) A parent of the other individual.
:(5) The term reduced leave schedule means a leave schedule that reduces the usual number of hours per workweek, or hours per workday, of an employee.
:(6) The terms spouse , son or daughter , and parent have the meaning given such terms in section 101 of the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 29 U.S.C. 2611] ).

==={{anchor|HD356215A9E354205850048EE1CD7CF36}}Sec. 4402. Leave requirement===



===={{anchor|HBE6B781BD69046F891D28AD9E6AFFF16}}(a) Entitlement to leave– ====

In any 12-month period, an eligible employee shall be entitled to two workweeks of leave for each family member of the eligible employee who, during such 12-month period—
:(1) is in the uniformed services; and
:(2) 
::(A) receives notification of an impending call or order to active duty in support of a contingency operation; or
::(B) is deployed in connection with a contingency operation.
:(b) Leave taken intermittently or on reduced leave schedule– 
::(1) Leave under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 subsection (a)] may be taken by an eligible employee intermittently or on a reduced leave schedule as the eligible employee considers appropriate.
::(2) The taking of leave intermittently or on a reduced leave schedule pursuant to this subsection shall not result in a reduction in the total amount of leave to which the eligible employee is entitled under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 subsection (a)] beyond the amount of leave actually taken.
:(c) Paid leave permitted– Leave granted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 subsection (a)] may consist of paid leave or unpaid leave as the employer of the eligible employee considers appropriate.
:(d) Relationship to paid leave– 
::(1) If an employer provides paid leave to an eligible employee for fewer than the total number of workweeks of leave that the eligible employee is entitled to under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 subsection (a)] , the additional amount of leave necessary to attain the total number of workweeks of leave required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 subsection (a)] may be provided without compensation.
::(2) An eligible employee may elect, and an employer may not require the eligible employee, to substitute any of the accrued paid vacation leave, personal leave, or family leave of the eligible employee for leave provided under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 subsection (a)] for any part of the total period of such leave the eligible employee is entitled to under such subsection.
:(e) Notice for leave– In any case in which an eligible employee chooses to use leave under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 subsection (a)] , the eligible employee shall provide such notice to the employer as is reasonable and practicable.

==={{anchor|H9F31ACEDD9414C5CBF73B4EABCB32EDA}}Sec. 4403. Certification===



===={{anchor|H73B640037B374AFF824126340A0C2C00}}(a) In general– ====

An employer may require that a request for leave under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 section 4402(a) of this title] be supported by a certification of entitlement to such leave.

===={{anchor|H3F49C9A0334F4249BEC3D61EA32CDF9E}}(b) Timeliness of certification– ====

An eligible employee shall provide, in a timely manner, a copy of the certification required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4403 subsection (a)] to the employer.

===={{anchor|HF569C9F29EB84027872153C455DFF00B}}(c) Sufficient certification– ====

A copy of the notification, call, or order described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 section 4402(a)(2)] of this title shall be considered sufficient certification of entitlement to leave for purposes of providing certification under this section. The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may prescribe such additional forms and manners of certification as the Secretary considers appropriate for purposes of providing certification under this section.

==={{anchor|HE4EFE9576373463D852D88EBAE8F793A}}Sec. 4404. Employment and benefits protection===



===={{anchor|H8F426B2351EE40848D77EF0130581852}}(a) In general– ====

An eligible employee who takes leave under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 section 4402] of this title for the intended purpose of the leave shall be entitled, on return from such leave—
:(1) to be restored by the employer to the position of employment held by the eligible employee when the leave commenced; or
:(2) to be restored to an equivalent position with equivalent rights and benefits of employment.

===={{anchor|HABD4D1C3C88F4AFBB09E344B669963F0}}(b) Loss of benefits– ====

The taking of leave under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4402 section 4402] of this title shall not result in the loss of any employment benefit accrued prior to the date on which the leave commenced.

===={{anchor|H9D1D0B530F054647B1DD8F34ADF4B437}}(c) Limitations– ====

Nothing in this section shall be construed to entitle any restored employee to—
:(1) the accrual of any seniority or employment benefits during any period of leave; or
:(2) any right, benefit, or position of employment other than any right, benefit, or position to which the employee would have been entitled had the employee not taken the leave.

==={{anchor|H07089B71DFED47539838143B2203A3F1}}Sec. 4405. Prohibited acts===



===={{anchor|HA43B2E4E85CC46069E1BE0897BB772E7}}(a) Exercise of rights– ====

It shall be unlawful for any employer to interfere with, restrain, or deny the exercise of or the attempt to exercise, any right provided under this chapter.

===={{anchor|HCCA396229E35419F9E0139229E4E4BAF}}(b) Discrimination– ====

It shall be unlawful for any employer to discharge or in any other manner discriminate against any individual for opposing any practice made unlawful by this chapter.

==={{anchor|H99974A9DDB9D44E280AB75CF09E86468}}Sec. 4406. Enforcement===

The provisions ofsubchapter III of chapter 43 of this titleshall apply with respect to the provisions of this chapter as if such provisions were incorporated into and made part of this chapter.

==={{anchor|H10FC1A2F32D743809179187E145D0DDC}}Sec. 4407. Miscellaneous provisions===

The provisions ofsubchapter IV of chapter 43 of this titleshall apply with respect to the provisions of this chapter as if such provisions were incorporated into and made part of this chapter..
