{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 23, United States Code, to improve highway safety and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Improving Driver Safety Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Distracted driving incentive grants=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405  Section 405(e)of title 23, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 paragraph (1)], by inserting“includes distracted driving issues as part of the State’s driver’s license examination and”after“any State that”;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 paragraph (3)]—
::(A) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 subparagraph (B)], by inserting“and”at the end;
::(B) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 subparagraph (C)]; and
::(C) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 subparagraph (D)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 subparagraph (C)];
:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 paragraph (4)(C)], by striking“section 31152”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/31136 section 31136] ”; and
:(4) by amending[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 paragraph (6)]to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

===(6) Distracted driving enforcement grants– ===



====(A) In general– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]may use up to 50 percent of the amounts available for grants under this subsection to award grants to any State that—
:(i) in fiscal year 2015—
::(I) has a basic text messaging statute, as determined by the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]], that—
:::(aa) is applicable to drivers of all ages; and
:::(bb) makes violation of the basic text messaging statute a primary offense;
::(II) participates in the annual distracted driving law enforcement mobilization coordinated by the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]]; and
::(III) is otherwise ineligible for a grant under this subsection;
:(ii) in fiscal year 2016—
::(I) meets the requirements of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 clause (i)];
::(II) imposes increased fines for repeat violations; and
::(III) has a statute that prohibits a driver who is younger than 18 years of age from using a personal wireless communications device while driving.

====(B) Use of grant funds– ====



=====(i) In general– =====

Subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 clauses (ii)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 (iii)], amounts received by a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 subparagraph (A)]may be used for activities related to the enforcement of distracted driving laws.

=====(ii) Fiscal year 2015– =====

In fiscal year 2015, up to 15 percent of the amounts received by a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 subparagraph (A)]may be used for any eligible project or activity under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/402 section 402].

=====(iii) Fiscal year 2016– =====

In fiscal year 2016, up to 25 percent of the amounts received by a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/405 subparagraph (A)]may be used for any eligible project or activity under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/402 section 402]. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Barriers to data collection report=

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:National Highway Traffic Safety Administration|National Highway Traffic Safety Administration]]shall submit a report to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|  [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate]] ]], the [[w:|  [[w:United States House Committee on Energy and Commerce|Committee on Energy and Commerce of the House of Representatives]] ]], and the [[w:|  [[w:United States House Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure|Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure of the House of Representatives]] ]]that—
:(1) identifies any legal and technical barriers to capturing adequate data on the prevalence of wireless communications devices while driving; and
:(2) provides recommendations on how to address such barriers.
