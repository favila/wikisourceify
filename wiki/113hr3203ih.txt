{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide the people of the United States with an opportunity to make gifts to the United States Government to be used for the purpose of providing public tours of the White House.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Restore the Tour Act” .

=Sec. 2. Gifts to fund public tours of the White House=

Chapter 2of title 3, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/3/110 section 110] the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 110A. Gifts to fund public tours of the White House==



===(a) In general– ===

To provide the people of the United States with an opportunity to make gifts to the United States Government to be used for the purpose of providing public tours of the White House—
:(1) the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] may accept for the Government a gift of—
::(A) money made only on the condition that it be used to provide public tours of the White House;
::(B) other intangible personal property made only on the condition that the property is sold and the proceeds from the sale used to provide public tours of the White House; and
:(2) the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] may accept for the Government a gift of tangible property made only on the condition that it be sold and the proceeds from the sale be used to provide public tours of the White House.

===(b) Authority To reject certain gifts– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] and the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] each may reject a gift under this section when the rejection is in the interest of the Government.

===(c) Conversion of non-Money gifts to money– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] and the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] shall convert a gift either of them accepts under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/3/110A subsection (a)(1)(B)] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/3/110A (2) of this section] to money on the best terms available. If a gift accepted under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/3/110A subsection (a) of this section] is subject to a gift or inheritance tax, the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] or the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator]] may pay the tax out of the proceeds of the gift or the proceeds of the redemption or sale of the gift.

===(d) Special account– ===

The Treasury has an account into which money received as gifts and proceeds from the sale or redemption of gifts under this section shall be deposited.

===(e) Use of funds– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall make all funds in the account described under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/3/110A subsection (d)] available to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Executive Office of the President]] , without further appropriation, and such funds shall be used to provide public tours of the White House.; and
</blockquote>

:(2) in the table of analysis for such chapter, by inserting after the item relating to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/3/110 section 110] the following:

<blockquote>110A. Gifts to fund public tours of the White House..
</blockquote>
