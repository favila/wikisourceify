{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 10, United States Code, to ensure that every military chaplain has the prerogative to close a prayer outside of a religious service according to the dictates of the chaplain’s own conscience.

=Section 1. Protection of the religious freedom of military chaplains to close a prayer outside of a religious service according to the dictates of the chaplain’s conscience=



==(a) United States Army– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/3547 Section 3547 of title 10, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>
:(c) If called upon to lead a prayer outside of a religious service, a chaplain shall have the prerogative to close the prayer according to the dictates of the chaplain’s own conscience..
</blockquote>


==(b) United States Military Academy– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/4337 Section 4337 of such title] is amended—
:(1) by inserting“(a)”before“There”; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>
::(b) If called upon to lead a prayer outside of a religious service, the Chaplain shall have the prerogative to close the prayer according to the dictates of the Chaplain’s conscience..
</blockquote>


==(c) United States Navy and Marine Corps– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/6031 Section 6031 of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>
:(d) If called upon to lead a prayer outside of a religious service, a chaplain shall have the prerogative to close the prayer according to the dictates of the chaplain’s own conscience..
</blockquote>


==(d) United States Air Force– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/8547 Section 8547 of such title] is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>
:(c) If called upon to lead a prayer outside of a religious service, a chaplain shall have the prerogative to close the prayer according to the dictates of the chaplain’s own conscience..
</blockquote>


==(e) United States Air Force Academy– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/9337 Section 9337 of such title] is amended—
:(1) by inserting“(a)”before“There”; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>
::(b) If called upon to lead a prayer outside of a religious service, the Chaplain shall have the prerogative to close the prayer according to the dictates of the Chaplain’s conscience..
</blockquote>
