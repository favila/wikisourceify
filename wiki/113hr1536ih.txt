{{Center|A BILL}}

To establish the Office of Agriculture Inspection within the Department of Homeland Security, which shall be headed by the Assistant Commissioner for Agriculture Inspection, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short Title=

This Act may be cited as the “Safeguarding American Commerce and Agriculture Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Establishment of the office of agricultural inspection=

 Title IV of the Homeland Security Act of 2002( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/6/201 6 U.S.C. 201 et seq.] )is amended by inserting after section 421the following:

<blockquote>

=Sec. 421a. Office of agricultural inspection=



==(a) Establishment– ==

There is established within [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] an [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of Agriculture Inspection]] , which shall be headed by an [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Assistant Commissioner]] .

==(b) Agriculture specialist career track– ==

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] —
:(1) shall identify appropriate career paths for customs and border protection agriculture specialists, including the education, training, experience, and assignments necessary for career progression within [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] ;
:(2) shall publish information on the career paths identified under paragraph (1); and
:(3) may establish criteria by which appropriately qualified customs and border protection technicians may be promoted to customs and border protection agriculture specialists.

==(c) Education, training, and experience– ==

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall provide customs and border protection agriculture specialists the opportunity to acquire the education, training, and experience necessary to qualify for promotion within [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] .

==(d) Agriculture specialist recruitment and retention– ==

Not later than 270 days after the date of the enactment of this section, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall develop a plan to more effectively recruit and retain qualified customs and border protection agriculture specialists. The plan shall include—
:(1) numerical goals for recruitment and retention; and
:(2) the use of recruitment incentives, as appropriate and permissible under existing laws and regulations.

==(e) Equipment support– ==

Not later than 270 days after the date of the enactment of this section, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall—
:(1) determine the minimum equipment and other resources that are necessary at [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] agriculture inspection stations and facilities to enable customs and border protection agriculture specialists to fully and effectively carry out their mission;
:(2) complete an inventory of the equipment and other resources available at each [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] agriculture inspection station and facility;
:(3) identify the necessary equipment and other resources that are not currently available at agriculture inspection stations and facilities; and
:(4) develop a plan to address any resource deficiencies identified under paragraph (3).

==(f) Interagency rotation program– ==

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] and the [[w:Rural Utilities Service|Secretary of Agriculture]] are authorized to enter into an agreement that—
:(1) establishes an interagency rotation program; and
:(2) provides for personnel of the [[w:Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service|Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service of the Department of Agriculture]] to take rotational assignments within [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] and vice versa for the purposes of strengthening working relationships between agencies and promoting interagency experience.

==(g) Report– ==

Not later than 270 days after the date of the enactment of this section, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] shall submit a report to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs|Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate]] and the [[w:|Committee on Homeland Security of the House of Representatives]] that describes—
:(1) the status of the implementation of the action plans developed by the Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service-U.S. Customs and Border Protection Joint Task Force on Improved Agriculture Inspection;
:(2) the findings of the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] under paragraphs (1), (2), and (3) of subsection (e);
:(3) the plan described in paragraph (e)(4);
:(4) the implementation of the remaining requirements under such subsection (e); and
:(5) any additional legal authority that the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] determines to be necessary to effectively carry out the agriculture inspection mission of the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]] .

==(h) Administration– ==

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] shall carry out this section acting through the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner of U.S. Customs and Border Protection]] in consultation with the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Assistant Commissioner for Agricultural Inspection]] ..
</blockquote>
