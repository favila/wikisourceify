{{Center|A BILL}}

 To direct the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency to publish a health advisory and submit reports with respect to Microcystins in drinking water.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Safe and Secure Drinking Water Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Microcystins in drinking water=



==(a) Health advisory– ==

Not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]](in this Act referred to as the“Administrator”) shall develop and publish a health advisory including recommendations on—
:(1) 
::(A) the level of Microcystins in drinking water below which the water is expected to be safe for human consumption; and
::(B) feasible treatment techniques and other means for achieving such level; and
:(2) standardized procedures for testing for Microcystins in drinking water.

==(b) Periodic reports– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Until the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]makes a determination of whether or not to regulate Microcystins in drinking water under  section 1412(b) of the Safe Drinking Water Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/300g–1 42 U.S.C. 300g–1(b)]), the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]shall submit reports as required by paragraph (2).

===(2) Reporting requirements– ===

Not later than 120 days after the date of enactment of this Act, and every year thereafter, the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on the following:
:(A) The status of the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator’s]]efforts to determine whether to regulate drinking water with respect to the level of Microcystins.
:(B) The steps taken by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]to promote testing of drinking water for Microcystins in areas that have been affected by harmful algal blooms.
:(C) An analysis of available treatment techniques and other means for addressing Microcystins in drinking water.
