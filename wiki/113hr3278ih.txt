{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amendchapter 77of title 5, United States Code, to clarify certain due process rights of Federal employees serving in sensitive positions, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Amendments=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7701  Section 7701of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7701 subsection (k)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7701 subsection (l)]; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7701 subsection (j)]the following:

<blockquote> 
::(k) 
:::(1) An employee or applicant for employment appealing an action arising from a determination of ineligibility for a sensitive position may not be denied [[w:Merit Systems Protection Board|Board]]review of the merits of such determination if—
::::(A) the position is not one that requires a security clearance or access to classified information; and
::::(B) such action is otherwise appealable.
:::(2) For purposes of this subsection, the term sensitive position means any position so designated pursuant to Executive Order 10450 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/7311  5 U.S.C. 7311note]) or, if superseded, a successor Executive order. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 2. Effective date=

The amendments made by this Act shall apply to all appeals, without exception, that are pending on, or commenced on or after, the date of the enactment of this Act.
