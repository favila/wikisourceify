{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 5, United States Code, to reduce the number of civil service positions within the executive branch, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Federal Hiring Freeze Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Reducing the number of Federal employees=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3101 Section 3101 of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by striking“Each Executive agency”and inserting“(a)In general.—Subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3101 subsection (b)], each Executive agency”; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

===(b) Reduction in number of employees– ===



====(1) Hiring freeze– ====

During each fiscal year which begins after the date of the enactment of this subsection and for which the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] projects that there will be a Federal budget deficit, the head of an Executive agency may not appoint an individual to any position in the agency, except as otherwise provided in this subsection.

====(2) Exemptions– ====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] may waive the application of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3101 paragraph (1)]in any of the following circumstances or for any of the following reasons:
:(A) The existence of a state of war or other vital national security concern.
:(B) To make an appointment (including to a position within the [[w:Federal Bureau of Prisons|Bureau of Prisons]] ) for purposes relating to Federal law enforcement.
:(C) To carry out a written commitment made by the Government on or before the date of the enactment of this subsection.
:(D) To make 1 or more appointments involving only the reassignment of personnel within the same agency.
:(E) The appointment is to a position under the Executive Schedule.
:(F) The appointment is of a short-term, seasonal, or intermittent nature and consistent with the agency’s hiring pattern and personnel levels of the previous fiscal year.
:(G) The appointment is to a position in the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Executive Office of the President]] and necessary to facilitate the orderly transition and operation of a new Presidential administration.
:(H) The appointment is to a position within the [[w:United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]] or the Postal Regulatory Commission.
:(I) The appointment is to a position within the [[w:Federal Aviation Administration|Federal Aviation Administration]] as an air traffic controller.

====(3) Reports to Congress– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

Not later than 90 days after the end of each quarter of a fiscal year, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall provide [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] with a report containing a list of each appointment approved by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] under this subsection during the quarter, broken down by Executive agency.

=====(B) Information included– =====

The list contained in a report provided to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3101 subparagraph (A)]shall include a description of the position associated with each approved appointment, but shall not provide any information that may be used to identify the individual appointed to the position.

=====(C) Public dissemination– =====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall make each report provided to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3101 subparagraph (A)]available to the public by posting the report on the official public website of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Executive Office of the President]] in a searchable, sortable, and down­load­able manner.

====(4) Termination– ====

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3101 Paragraph (1)]shall cease to apply after the date as of which the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] determines that a Federal budget deficit no longer exists. . 
</blockquote>
