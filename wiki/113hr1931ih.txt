{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 5, United States Code, to enhance the authority under which Federal agencies may pay cash awards to employees for making cost saving disclosures, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Employees of America Streamlining for Your Savings Act of 2013”or the“EASY Savings Act of 2013”.

=Sec. 2. Enhancement of authority to make cash awards to employees for cost saving disclosures=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 4512] of title 5, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (a)] —
::(A) in the matter preceding [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 paragraph (1)] , by inserting“or identification of surplus funds or unnecessary budget authority”after“mismanagement”;
::(B) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 paragraph (2)] , by inserting“or identification”after“disclosure”; and
::(C) in the matter following [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 paragraph (2)] , by inserting“or identification”after“disclosure”; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(c) The Inspector General of an agency or other agency employee designated under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (b)] shall refer to the Chief Financial Officer of the agency any potential surplus funds or unnecessary budget authority identified by an employee under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (a)] , along with any recommendations of the Inspector General or other agency employee.
::(d) 
:::(1) If the Chief Financial Officer of an agency determines that rescission of potential surplus funds or unnecessary budget authority, identified by an employee under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (a)] , would not hinder the effectiveness of the agency, the head of the agency shall transfer the amount of the surplus funds or unnecessary budget authority from the applicable appropriations account to the general fund of the Treasury, except as provided in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (e)] .
:::(2)  Title X of the Congressional Budget and Impoundment Control Act of 1974( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/681 2 U.S.C. 681 et seq.] ] )shall not apply to transfers under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 paragraph (1)] .
:::(3) Any amounts transferred under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 paragraph (1)] shall be deposited in the Treasury and used for deficit reduction, except that in the case of a fiscal year for which there is no Federal budget deficit, such amounts shall be used to reduce the Federal debt, in such manner as the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] considers appropriate.
::(e) The head of an agency may retain not more than 10 percent of any amounts which (but for this subsection) would otherwise be transferred to the general fund of the Treasury under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (d)] , representing surplus funds or unnecessary budget authority identified by an employee under this section, for the purpose of paying a cash award to such employee in accordance with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (a)] .
::(f) 
:::(1) The head of each agency shall submit to the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of Personnel Management]] an annual report regarding—
::::(A) each disclosure of fraud, waste, or mismanagement or identification of surplus funds or unnecessary budget authority, made under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (a)] by an employee of the agency, which is determined by the agency to have merit;
::::(B) the total savings achieved through disclosures and identifications described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subparagraph (A)] ; and
::::(C) the number and amount of cash awards by the agency under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (a)] .
:::(2) The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of Personnel Management]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] and the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Government Accountability Office]] an annual report on Federal cost saving and awards based on the reports under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 paragraph (1)] .
:::(3) The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of Personnel Management]] shall—
::::(A) ensure that the cash award program of each agency complies with this section; and
::::(B) submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] an annual certification indicating whether the cash award program of each agency complies with this section.
::(g) 
:::(1) The head of each agency shall include the information described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4512 subsection (f)(1)] in each budget request of the agency submitted to the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] as part of the preparation of the budget of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 1105(a)] of title 31] .
:::(2) Not later than 3 years after the date of enactment of the EASY Savings Act of 2013, and every 3 years thereafter, the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the operation of the cost savings and awards program under this section, including recommendations for any legislative changes which the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General]] considers appropriate..
</blockquote>


==(b) Prohibition– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4509  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 4509] of title 5, United States Code] , is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

===Sec. 4509. Prohibition of cash award to certain officers===



====(a) Definition– ====

For purposes of this section, the term agency refers to any agency within the meaning of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 section 551(1)] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/4501 4501(1)] .

====(b) Prohibition– ====

An officer may not receive a cash award under this subchapter if such officer—
:(1) is the head of an agency;
:(2) serves in—
::(A) a position under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5312 section 5312] (relating to positions at level I of the Executive Schedule); or
::(B) a position for which the compensation is set in statute by reference to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5312 section 5312] or level I of the Executive Schedule; or
:(3) is a voting member of an independent establishment..
</blockquote>
