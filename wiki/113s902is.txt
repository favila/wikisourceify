{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act to apply the provisions of the Act to certain Congressional staff and members of the executive branch.

=Section 1. Health insurance coverage for certain Congressional staff and members of the executive branch=

 Section 1312(d)(3)(D) of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/18032 42 U.S.C. 18032(d)(3)(D)] ) is amended—
:(1) by striking the subparagraph heading and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

===(D) Members of Congress, congressional staff, and political appointees in the exchange– ===

;
</blockquote>

:(2) in clause (i)in the matter preceding subclause (I)—
::(A) by striking “congressional staff with” and inserting “congressional staff, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] , the [[w:Office of the Vice President of the United States|Vice President]] , and political appointees with” ; and
::(B) by striking “congressional staff shall” and inserting “congressional staff, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] , the [[w:Office of the Vice President of the United States|Vice President]] , or political appointee, shall” ; and
:(3) in clause (ii)—
::(A) in subclause (II), by inserting after “ [[w:|Congress]] ,” the following: “of a committee of [[w:|Congress]] , and of a leadership office of [[w:|Congress]] ,” ; and
::(B) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

====(III) Political appointee– ====

In this subparagraph, the term political appointee means any individual who—
:(aa) is employed in a position described under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5312..5316 sections 5312 through 5316 of title 5, United States Code] , (relating to the Executive Schedule);
:(bb) is a limited term appointee, limited emergency appointee, or noncareer appointee in the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Senior Executive Service]] , as defined under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3132 paragraphs (5)] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3132 (6)] , and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3132 (7)] , respectively, of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3132 section 3132(a)of title 5, United States Code] ; or
:(cc) is employed in a position in the executive branch of the Government of a confidential or policy-determining character under schedule C of subpart C of part 213 of title 5 of the Code of Federal Regulations..
</blockquote>
