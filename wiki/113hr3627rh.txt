{{Center|A BILL}}

To require the Attorney General to report on State law penalties for certain child abusers, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Kilah Davenport Child Protection Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Attorney General Report=

Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, and again 3 years thereafter, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall publish and submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary of the United States Senate]] a report on the penalties for violations of laws prohibiting child abuse in each of the 50 States, the District of Columbia, and each territory of the United States, including whether the laws of that State, District, or territory provides for enhanced penalties when the victim has suffered serious bodily injury, or permanent or protracted loss or impairment of any mental or emotional function.

=Sec. 3. Expansion of predicate for increased penalties for certain domestic assaults=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/117 Section 117(a)(1)of title 18, United States Code] , is amended by inserting “, or against a child of or in the care of the person committing the domestic assault” after “intimate partner” .
