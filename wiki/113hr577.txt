{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to clarify the conditions under which certain persons may be treated as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes.

={{anchor|HB1C0A07840E3473FAD423BD1A33F9006}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Veterans Second Amendment Protection Act”.

={{anchor|HA8A488F2D92D46A08B7CB8D1BD2676EB}}Sec. 2. Conditions for treatment of certain persons as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes=



=={{anchor|H6030649F0A0E45AA892689E6947A06A5}}(a) In general– ==

Chapter 55 of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HC8C2F72CA38841629510EA5C54E1052D}}Sec. 5511. Conditions for treatment of certain persons as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes==

In any case arising out of the administration by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] of laws and benefits under this title, a person who is mentally incapacitated, deemed mentally incompetent, or experiencing an extended loss of consciousness shall not be considered adjudicated as a mental defective under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subsection (d)(4)] or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (g)(4) of section 922 of title 18] without the order or finding of a judge, magistrate, or other judicial authority of competent jurisdiction that such person is a danger to himself or herself or others..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|idC4E6E7F77FAD47718B3DF0CA58037C44}}(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections at the beginning ofchapter 55 of such titleis amended by adding at the end the following new item:

<blockquote>__NOTOC__
5511. Conditions for treatment of certain persons as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes..
</blockquote>
