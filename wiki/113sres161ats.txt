{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

 Relative to the death of the Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg, Senator from the State of New Jersey.    Whereas the
[[w:Frank_Lautenberg|Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg]] served the people of the State of New Jersey for over 28 years in the United States Senate;   Whereas the
[[w:Frank_Lautenberg|Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg]] was the longest serving United States Senator from the State of New Jersey;   Whereas the
[[w:Frank_Lautenberg|Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg]] cast 9,267 roll call votes—more than any other United States Senator from the State of New Jersey and the 40th most in United States Senate history;   Whereas the
[[w:Frank_Lautenberg|Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg]] served on multiple Committees in the Senate including the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works|Committee on the Environment and Public Works]] ; the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation]] ; and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Committee on Appropriations]] ; and served as [[w:United States Senate Environment and Public Works Subcommittee on Superfund, Toxics and Environmental Health|Chairman of the Environment and Public Works Subcommittee on Superfund, Toxics, and Environmental Health]] ; the [[w:United States Senate Commerce Subcommittee on Surface Transportation and Merchant Marine Infrastructure, Safety, and Security|Commerce, Science, and Transportation Subcommittee on Surface Transportation and Merchant Marine Infrastructure, Safety, and Security]] ; the [[w:United States Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Transportation, Housing and Urban Development, and Related Agencies|Appropriations Subcommittee on Transportation]] ; and the [[w:United States Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Financial Services and General Government|Appropriations Subcommittee on Financial Services, and General Government]] ;   Whereas the
[[w:Frank_Lautenberg|Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg]] enlisted in the United States Army at the age of 18 and served in the European Theater during World War II;   Whereas the
[[w:Frank_Lautenberg|Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg]] was able to attend Columbia University as a result of G.I. Bill benefits following his military service;   Whereas the
[[w:Frank_Lautenberg|Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg]] co-founded the company Automatic Data Processing (ADP) and worked as its Chief Executive Officer, helping it become one of America's most successful companies;   Whereas the
[[w:Frank_Lautenberg|Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg]] dedicated his Senate career to improving the environment and public health, strengthening our nation's transportation systems, and working tirelessly on behalf of the people of New Jersey: Now, therefore, be it    

= =

That—
:(1) the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] has heard with profound sorrow and deep regret the announcement of the death of the
[[w:Frank_Lautenberg|Honorable Frank R. Lautenberg]] , Senator from the State of New Jersey;
:(2) the [[w:United States Senate|Secretary of the Senate]] shall transmit this resolution to the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] and transmit an enrolled copy thereof to the family of the deceased; and
:(3) when the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] adjourns today, it stand adjourned as a further mark of respect to the memory of the deceased Senator.
