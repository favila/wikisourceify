{{Center|IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES}}

 
{{Center|AN ACT}}

 To amend the Energy Policy and Conservation Act to establish the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy as the lead Federal agency for coordinating Federal, State, and local assistance provided to promote the energy retrofitting of schools.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Streamlining Energy Efficiency for Schools Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Coordination of energy retrofitting assistance for schools=

   Section 392 of the Energy Policy and Conservation Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/6371a 42 U.S.C. 6371a] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(e) Coordination of energy retrofitting assistance for schools– 
::(1) Definition of school– Notwithstanding section 391(6) , for the purposes of this subsection, the term school means—
:::(A) an elementary school or secondary school (as defined in  section 9101 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/7801 20 U.S.C. 7801] ));
:::(B) an institution of higher education (as defined in  section 102(a) of the Higher Education Act of 1965 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/1002 20 U.S.C. 1002(a)] );
:::(C) a school of the defense dependents’ education system under the  Defense Dependents’ Education Act of 1978 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/921 20 U.S.C. 921 et seq.] )or established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2164  section 2164of title 10, United States Code];
:::(D) a school operated by the [[w:Bureau of Indian Affairs|Bureau of Indian Affairs]];
:::(E) a tribally controlled school (as defined in  section 5212 of the Tribally Controlled Schools Act of 1988 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/2511 25 U.S.C. 2511] )); and
:::(F) a Tribal College or University (as defined in  section 316(b) of the Higher Education Act of 1965 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/1059c 20 U.S.C. 1059c(b)] )).
::(2) Establishment of clearinghouse– The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]], acting through the [[w:|Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy]], shall establish a clearinghouse to disseminate information regarding available Federal programs and financing mechanisms that may be used to help initiate, develop, and finance energy efficiency, distributed generation, and energy retrofitting projects for schools.
::(3) Requirements– In carrying out paragraph (2) , the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall—
:::(A) consult with appropriate Federal agencies to develop a list of Federal programs and financing mechanisms that are, or may be, used for the purposes described in paragraph (2) ; and
:::(B) coordinate with appropriate Federal agencies to develop a collaborative education and outreach effort to streamline communications and promote available Federal programs and financing mechanisms described in subparagraph (A) , which may include the development and maintenance of a single online resource that includes contact information for relevant technical assistance in the [[w:|Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy]]that States, local education agencies, and schools may use to effectively access and use such Federal programs and financing mechanisms. . 
</blockquote>
    Passed the House of Representatives June 23, 2014. Karen L. Haas, Clerk.
