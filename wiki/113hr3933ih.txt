{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 11 of the United States Code to include firearms in the types of property allowable under the alternative provision for exempting property from the estate.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “ Protecting Gun Owners in Bankruptcy Act of 2014” .

=Sec. 2. Exemptions=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/522 Section 522of title 11, the United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/522 subsection (d)] by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(13) The debtor's aggregate interest, not to exceed $3,000 in value, in a single firearm or firearms., and
</blockquote>

:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/522 subsection (f)(4)(A)] —
::(A) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/522 clause (xiv)] by striking “and” at the end,
::(B) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/522 clause (xv)] by striking the period at the end and inserting “; and” , and
::(C) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:::(xvi) The debtor's aggregate interest, not to exceed $3,000 in value, in a single firearm or firearms..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Effective date; application of amendments=



==(a) Effective date– ==

Except as provided in subsection (b), this Act and the amendments made by this Act shall take effect on the date of the enactment of this Act.

==(b) Application of amendments– ==

The amendments made by this Act shall apply only with respect to cases commenced under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/ title 11 of the United States Code] on or after the date of the enactment of this Act.
