=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “School Safety Enhancements Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 5. Accountability=

 Section 2701 of title I of the Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act of 1968( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/3797a 42 U.S.C. 3797a] ), as amended by section 2of this Act, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==(h) Accountability– ==

All grants awarded by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] under this part shall be subject to the following accountability provisions:

===(1) Audit requirement– ===



====(A) Definition– ====

In this paragraph, the term unresolved audit finding means a finding in the final audit report of the [[w:United States Department of Justice Office of the Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department of Justice]] that the audited grantee has utilized grant funds for an unauthorized expenditure or otherwise unallowable cost that is not closed or resolved within 12 months from the date when the final audit report is issued.

====(B) Audits– ====

Beginning in the first fiscal year beginning after the date of enactment of this subsection, and in each fiscal year thereafter, the [[w:United States Department of Justice Office of the Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department of Justice]] shall conduct audits of recipients of grants under this part to prevent waste, fraud, and abuse of funds by grantees. The [[w:United States Department of Justice Office of the Inspector General|Inspector General]] shall determine the appropriate number of grantees to be audited each year.

====(C) Mandatory exclusion– ====

A recipient of grant funds under this part that is found to have an unresolved audit finding shall not be eligible to receive grant funds under this part during the first 2 fiscal years beginning after the end of the 12-month period described in subparagraph (A).

====(D) Priority– ====

In awarding grants under this part, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall give priority to eligible applicants that did not have an unresolved audit finding during the 3 fiscal years before submitting an application for a grant under this part.

====(E) Reimbursement– ====

If an entity is awarded grant funds under this part during the 2-fiscal-year period during which the entity is barred from receiving grants under subparagraph (C), the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall—
:(i) deposit an amount equal to the amount of the grant funds that were improperly awarded to the grantee into the General Fund of the Treasury; and
:(ii) seek to recoup the costs of the repayment to the fund from the grant recipient that was erroneously awarded grant funds.

===(2) Nonprofit organization requirements– ===



====(A) Definition– ====

For purposes of this paragraph and the grant programs under this part, the term nonprofit organization means an organization that is described in  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/501 section 501(c)(3)] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986and is exempt from taxation under section 501(a) of such Code.

====(B) Prohibition– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may not award a grant under this part to a nonprofit organization that holds money in offshore accounts for the purpose of avoiding paying the tax described in section 511(a) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

====(C) Disclosure– ====

Each nonprofit organization that is awarded a grant under this part and uses the procedures prescribed in regulations to create a rebuttable presumption of reasonableness for the compensation of its officers, directors, trustees and key employees, shall disclose to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] , in the application for the grant, the process for determining such compensation, including the independent persons involved in reviewing and approving such compensation, the comparability data used, and contemporaneous substantiation of the deliberation and decision. Upon request, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall make the information disclosed under this subparagraph available for public inspection.

===(3) Conference expenditures– ===



====(A) Limitation– ====

No amounts authorized to be appropriated to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] under this part may be used by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] , or by any individual or entity awarded discretionary funds through a cooperative agreement under this part, to host or support any expenditure for conferences that uses more than $20,000 in funds made available by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] , unless the [[w:|Deputy Attorney General]] or such Assistant Attorney Generals, Directors, or principal deputies as the [[w:|Deputy Attorney General]] may designate, provides prior written authorization that the funds may be expended to host the conference.

====(B) Written approval– ====

Written approval under subparagraph (A)shall include a written estimate of all costs associated with the conference, including the cost of all food, beverages, audio-visual equipment, honoraria for speakers, and entertainment.

====(C) Report– ====

The [[w:|Deputy Attorney General]] shall submit an annual report to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary of the Senate]] and the [[w:United States House Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary of the House of Representatives]] on all conference expenditures approved under this paragraph.

===(4) Annual certification– ===

Beginning in the first fiscal year beginning after the date of enactment of this subsection, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall submit, to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Committee on Appropriations of the Senate]] and the [[w:United States House Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary]] and the [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|Committee on Appropriations of the House of Representatives]] , an annual certification—
:(A) indicating whether—
::(i) all audits issued by the [[w:United States Department of Justice Office of the Inspector General|Office of the Inspector General]] under paragraph (1)have been completed and reviewed by the appropriate Assistant Attorney General or Director;
::(ii) all mandatory exclusions required under paragraph (1)(C)have been issued; and
::(iii) all reimbursements required under paragraph (1)(E)have been made; and
:(B) that includes a list of any grant recipients excluded under paragraph (1)from the previous year..
</blockquote>

===Sec. 509. National Center for Campus Public Safety===



====(a) Definition of institution of higher education– ====

In this section, the term institution of higher education has the meaning given the term in section 101 of the Higher Education Act of 1965( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/1001 20 U.S.C. 1001] ).

====(b) Authority to establish and operate Center– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may establish and operate a [[w:United States Department of Justice|National Center for Campus Public Safety]] (referred to in this section as the Center ).

====(c) Functions of the Center– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Center]] shall—
:(1) provide quality education and training for public safety personnel of institutions of higher education and their collaborative partners, including campus mental health agencies;
:(2) foster quality research to strengthen the safety and security of institutions of higher education;
:(3) serve as a clearinghouse for the identification and dissemination of information, policies, protocols, procedures, and best practices relevant to campus public safety, including off-campus housing safety, the prevention of violence against persons and property, and emergency response and evacuation procedures;
:(4) coordinate with the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] , the [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary of Education]] , State, local and tribal governments and law enforcement agencies, private and nonprofit organizations and associations, and other stakeholders, to develop protocols and best practices to prevent, protect against and respond to dangerous and violent situations involving an immediate threat to the safety of the campus community;
:(5) promote the development and dissemination of effective behavioral threat assessment and management models to prevent campus violence;
:(6) identify campus safety information (including ways to increase off-campus housing safety) and identify resources available from the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] , the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]] , the [[w:United States Department of Education|Department of Education]] , State, local, and tribal governments and law enforcement agencies, and private and nonprofit organizations and associations;
:(7) promote cooperation, collaboration, and consistency in prevention, response, and problem-solving methods among public safety and emergency management personnel of institutions of higher education and their campus- and non-campus-based collaborative partners, including law enforcement, emergency management, mental health services, and other relevant agencies;
:(8) disseminate standardized formats and models for mutual aid agreements and memoranda of understanding between campus security agencies and other public safety organizations and mental health agencies; and
:(9) report annually to [[w:|Congress]] on activities performed by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Center]] during the previous 12 months.

====(d) Coordination with available resources– ====

In establishing the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Center]] , the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall—
:(1) coordinate with the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] , the [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary of Education]] , and appropriate State or territory officials;
:(2) ensure coordination with campus public safety resources within the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]] , including within the [[w:Federal Emergency Management Agency|Federal Emergency Management Agency]] , and the [[w:United States Department of Education|Department of Education]] ; and
:(3) coordinate within the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] and existing grant programs to ensure against duplication with the program authorized by this section.

====(e) Reporting and accountability– ====

At the end of each fiscal year, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall—
:(1) issue a report that assesses the impacts, outcomes and effectiveness of the grants distributed to carry out this section;
:(2) in compiling such report, assess instances of duplicative activity, if any, performed through grants distributed to carry out this section and other grant programs maintained by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] , the [[w:United States Department of Education|Department of Education]] , and the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]] ; and
:(3) make such report available on the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] website and submit such report to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary|Senate]] and [[w:United States House Committee on the Judiciary|House Judiciary Committees]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Senate]] and [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|House Appropriations Committees]] ..
</blockquote>


==(c) Rule of construction– ==

Nothing in this section shall preclude public elementary and secondary schools or their larger governing agencies from receiving the informational and training benefits of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|National Center for Campus Public Safety]] authorized under section 509 of the Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act of 1968, as added by this Act.
