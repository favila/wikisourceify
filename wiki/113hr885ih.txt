{{Center|A BILL}}

To expand the boundary of San Antonio Missions National Historical Park, to conduct a study of potential land acquisitions, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “San Antonio Missions National Historical Park Boundary Expansion Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Park boundary study=

[[Public Law 95-629|Section 201 of Public Law 95–629]] ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/410ee 16 U.S.C. 410ee] )is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[[Public Law 95-629|subsections (b), (c), (d), (e), and (f)]] as[[Public Law 95-629|subsections (c), (d), (e), (f), and (g)]] , respectively;
:(2) by inserting after[[Public Law 95-629|subsection (a)]] the following:

<blockquote>

===(b) Study– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall conduct a study of land in Bexar and Wilson Counties, Texas, to identify land that would be suitable for inclusion in the park.

====(2) Requirements– ====

In conducting the study under[[Public Law 95-629|paragraph (1)]] , the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall examine the natural, cultural, recreational, and scenic values and characteristics of the land.

====(3) Report– ====

Not later than 3 years after the date on which funds are made available for the study under[[Public Law 95-629|paragraph (1)]] , the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Natural Resources|Committee on Natural Resources of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources|Committee on Energy and Natural Resources of the Senate]] a report that describes the findings, conclusions, and recommendations of the study.; and
</blockquote>

:(3) in[[Public Law 95-629|paragraph (1)(D) of subsection (f)]] (as redesignated by paragraph (1)), by striking “subsection (b)(2)” and inserting “subsection (c)(2)” .

=Sec. 3. Boundary expansion=

[[Public Law 95-629|Section 201 of[[Public Law -|Public Law 95–629]] ]] ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/410ee 16 U.S.C. 410ee] )is amended—
:(1) by striking “Sec. 201.(a) In order” and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 201. San Antonio Missions Historical Park==



===(a) Establishment– ===



====(1) In general– ====

In order; and
</blockquote>

:(2) in[[Public Law 95-629|subsection (a)]] —
::(A) in the second sentence, by striking “The park shall also” and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

====(2) Additional land– ====

The park shall also;
</blockquote>

::(B) in the third sentence, by striking “After advising the” and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

====(4) Revisions– ====

After advising the; and
</blockquote>

::(C) by inserting after[[Public Law 95-629|paragraph (2)]] (as designated by subparagraph (A)) the following:

<blockquote>

====(3) Boundary modification– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

The boundary of the park is modified to include approximately 137 acres, as depicted on the map entitled “San Antonio Missions National Historical Park Proposed Boundary Addition” , numbered 472/113, 006A, and dated June 2012.

=====(B) Availability of map– =====

The map described in subparagraph (A)shall be on file and available for inspection in the appropriate offices of the [[w:National Park Service|National Park Service]] .

=====(C) Prohibition on acquisition of lands by condemnation– =====

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may not use condemnation authority to acquire lands or interests in lands under this paragraph..
</blockquote>
