==Title I— Benefits claims submission==



==Sec. 101. Modification of Transition Assistance Program to enable online participation==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144  Section 1144of title 10, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 

===(f) Online curriculum– ===


:(1) The [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]], in conjunction with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]], and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]], shall take such actions and make such modifications to the eBenefits Internet website as may be necessary to ensure that members of the armed forces and spouses described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsection (a)(1)]can participate in the program carried out under this section through such Internet website.
:(2) An individual subject to a requirement under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1144 subsection (c)]may not satisfy such requirement by participating in the program carried out under this section solely through an Internet website. . 
</blockquote>


==Sec. 102. Requirement to encourage claimants to use appropriate forms==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall encourage each individual who is seeking to file a claim with the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for a benefit under a law administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]to file such claim using an appropriate form established by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for such purpose.

==Sec. 103. Required communications to users of eBenefits Internet website==



===(a) Automated notification of resources– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall take such measures as may be necessary so that whenever an individual establishes an account on the eBenefits Internet website of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]and [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]an e-mail is sent to the individual that includes a description of the following:
:(1) The benefits of filing a fully developed claim.
:(2) Where the individual can go to obtain assistance in filing a fully developed claim, including the following:
::(A) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].
::(B) An organization recognized by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for the representation of veterans under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5902 section 5902 of title 38, United States Code].

===(b) Alternate communication– ===

Whenever the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]sends a message to an individual through the eBenefits Internet website described in subsection (a) , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall also send such message to the individual through the use of a second medium selected by the individual, such as by telephone or by sending an e-mail to a private e-mail address of the individual.

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5110  Section 5110(b)(2)(C)of title 38, United States Code], is amended by striking“the date that is three years after the date of the enactment of such Act”and inserting“September 30, 2020”.

==Sec. 106. Requirement to use Department of Veterans Affairs form 21–0958 in regional offices for filing of notices of disagreement==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall use Department of Veterans Affairs form 21–0958, or such other form as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may require, in each of the regional offices of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]for purposes of filing notices of disagreement under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7105  section 7105(b)of title 38, United States Code].

==Title II— Practices of regional offices==



==Sec. 201. Required Comptroller General audit of regional offices of Veterans Benefits Administration==



===(a) In general– ===

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]]shall complete an audit of the regional offices of the [[w:Veterans Benefits Administration|Veterans Benefits Administration]]—
:(1) to assess the consistency of decisions being made with respect to claims for benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]; and
:(2) to identify ways in which the consistency of such decisions can be improved.

===(b) Identification of factors and best practices– ===

The audit conducted under subsection (a) shall include the following:
:(1) Identification of the factors, including management practices, that distinguish higher performing regional offices from other regional offices.
:(2) Identification of best practices employed by higher performing regional offices that distinguish the performance of such offices from other regional offices.

===(c) Report– ===

Not later than 90 days after the date on which the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General]]completes the audit required by subsection (a) , the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on the results of the audit.

==Sec. 202. Study on service-connected disabilities that worsen with age==



===(a) In general– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall complete a study on covered disabilities using historical data regarding service-connected disabilities.

===(b) Covered disabilities– ===

For purposes of the study required by subsection (a) , a covered disability is a service-connected disability that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines is of a type or class of disability or condition that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines is a type or class of disability or condition that, for individuals with such type or class of disability, the average impairment of earning capacity resulting from such disability or condition increases as the individual with such disability or condition ages.

===(c) Matters covered– ===

The study required by subsection (a) shall include the following:
:(1) Analysis of historical statistics and information related to the progressive nature of covered disabilities, in terms of increased impairment of earning capacity caused by the disabilities.
:(2) Development of recommendations for legislative and administrative action that use statistics and information described in paragraph (1) to adjudicate more quickly claims for increased disability compensation and disability compensation claims of veterans who had specific military occupation specialties when serving in the Armed Forces.

===(d) Report– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on the study required by subsection (a) , including the findings of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]with respect to such study.

===(e) Public comment– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall—
:(1) make the report submitted under paragraph (1) available to the public; and
:(2) seek comments from the public, including from veterans service organizations, veterans, and medical professionals, on the recommendations developed under subsection (c)(2) .

===(f) Proposal– ===



====(1) In general– ====

Not later than 180 days after submitting the report under subsection (d) , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall develop and submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a proposal for conduct of a pilot program to assess the feasibility and advisability of carrying out the recommendations developed under subsection (c)(2) .

====(2) Input– ====

In developing the proposal required by paragraph (1) , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall consider the comments received under subsection (e)(2) .

====(3) Limitation– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may not conduct the pilot program proposed under paragraph (1) or any portion of such proposal except pursuant to provisions of law enacted after the date of the receipt by [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]of such proposal.

===(g) Veterans service organization defined– ===

In this section, the term veterans service organization means an organization recognized by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for the representation of veterans under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5902 section 5902 of title 38, United States Code].

:(A) by redesignating subsection (d) as subsection (e) ; and
:(B) by inserting after subsection (c) the following new subsection (d) :

<blockquote> 

======(d) Licensure of contract physicians– ======

  Subsection (b) of such section 504 is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

=====(1) Number– =====

==Sec. 204. Improvement of mail processing by Department of Veterans Affairs==

Not later than the date that is one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall develop and implement a plan to establish a uniform mail processing and scanning system throughout the regional offices of the [[w:Veterans Benefits Administration|Veterans Benefits Administration]].

==Sec. 205. Review of practices of regional offices regarding use of suspense dates==



===(a) Review required– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs]]shall conduct a review of the practices of regional offices of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]regarding the use of suspense dates during the disability claim assessment process.

===(b) Report– ===

Not later than 270 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on the findings of the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General]]with respect to the review conducted under subsection (a) .

==Sec. 206. Semiannual reports on progress in implementing Veterans Benefits Management System==



===(a) In general– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act and not less frequently than once every 180 days thereafter until the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs]]certifies to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that the Veterans Benefits Management System is implemented and fully functional, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on the progress of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]in implementing the Veterans Benefits Management System.

===(b) Contents– ===

The report required by subsection (a) shall include the following:
:(1) An assessment of the current functionality of the Veterans Benefits Management System, including whether each component of the system is fully functional, partially functional, or not functional.
:(2) For each component of the system that is partially functional or not functional, an estimate of the date by which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]expects the component to be fully functional.
:(3) Recommendations submitted to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]by employees of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]who are involved in processing claims for benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]], including veteran service representatives, rating veteran service representatives, and decision review officers, for such legislative or administrative action as the employees consider appropriate to improve the processing of such claims.

==Sec. 207. Annual report on capacity of Veterans Benefits Administration to process benefits claims==



===(a) In general– ===

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this Act and not less frequently than once each year thereafter, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the Senate]]and the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the House of Representatives]]a report on the capacity of the [[w:Veterans Benefits Administration|Veterans Benefits Administration]]to process claims for benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]during the next one-year period.

===(b) Contents– ===

Each report submitted under subsection (a) shall include, for the period covered by the report, the following:
:(1) The number of claims for benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]expects the [[w:Veterans Benefits Administration|Veterans Benefits Administration]]to process.
:(2) The number of full-time equivalent employees of the [[w:Veterans Benefits Administration|Veterans Benefits Administration]]who are dedicated to processing such claims.
:(3) An estimate of the number of such claims a single full-time equivalent employee of the [[w:Veterans Benefits Administration|Administration]]can process in a year.
:(4) An assessment of whether the [[w:Veterans Benefits Administration|Administration]]requires additional or fewer full-time equivalent employees to process such claims during the next 1-year, 5-year, and 10-year periods.

===(c) Public availability– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall make the report required by subsection (a) available to the public on an Internet website of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

==Sec. 208. Increased transparency in Monday Morning Workload Report==



===(a) Disaggregation of claims by decisions requiring disability rating– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall include in each Monday Morning Workload Report published by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]the number of claims for benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]that have been received by a regional office of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]and are pending a decision, disaggregated by—
:(1) whether the claim has been pending for more than 125 days; and
:(2) whether the claim requires a decision concerning a disability rating.

===(b) Inclusion of information on assignment of partial ratings– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall include in each Monday Morning Workload Report published by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]in the portion of the report entitled“Transformation”and in the portion of the report entitled“Aggregate”the number of partial ratings assigned.

===(c) Report on fully developed claims– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall include in each Monday Morning Workload Report published by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]the following:
:(1) The total number of fully developed claims for benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]that—
::(A) have been received by a regional office of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]; and
::(B) are pending a decision.
:(2) The total number of such claims that have been pending for more than 125 days, disaggregated by station.

==Sec. 209. Reports on appeals of decisions on benefits claims==



===(a) Public availability– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall make the reports of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]entitled“Appeals Pending”and“Appeals Workload By Station”available to the public on an Internet website of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].

===(b) Appeals granted by station– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall include in one of the reports described in subsection (a) the percentage of appeals granted by station. 

==Title III— Government response==



==Sec. 301. Increased cooperation across Government==



===(a) Appointment of liaisons– ===



====(1) Department of Defense– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]shall appoint individuals as follows:
:(A) At least one individual to act as a liaison under this section between the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].
:(B) At least one individual for each of the reserve components of the Armed Forces to act as a liaison under this section between the respective component of the Armed Forces and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

====(2) Social Security Administration– ====

The [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner of Social Security]]shall appoint at least one individual to act as a liaison under this section between the [[w:Social Security Administration|Social Security Administration]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

====(3) National Archives and Records Administration– ====

The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist of the United States]]shall appoint at least one individual to act as a liaison under this section between the [[w:Social Security Administration|Social Security Administration]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

===(b) Duties of liaisons– ===

Each individual acting as a liaison under this section shall expedite the timely provision to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]of such information as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]requires to process claims submitted to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]].

===(c) Procedures– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]], the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner of Social Security]], and the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist of the United States]]shall jointly develop and implement procedures to improve the timely provision to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]of such information as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]requires to process claims submitted to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]].

====(2) Timely provision– ====

The procedures developed and implemented under paragraph (1) shall ensure that the information provided to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]is provided to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]not later than 30 days after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]requests the information.

===(d) Annual reports– ===

Not less frequently than once each year, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on—
:(1) the requests for information made by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]during the most recent one-year period for information from the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner of Social Security]], and the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist of the United States]]required by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]to process claims submitted to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]; and
:(2) the timeliness of responses to such requests.

==Sec. 302. Report on interoperability between electronic health records systems of Department of Defense and Department of Veterans Affairs==

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall jointly submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report that sets forth a timeline with milestones for achieving interoperability between the electronic health records systems of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].
