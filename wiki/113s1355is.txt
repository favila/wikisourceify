{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide regulatory parity among alternative fuel vehicles, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Natural gas vehicles=



==(a) Maximum fuel economy increase for alternative fuel automobiles– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32906 Section 32906(a) of title 49, United States Code] , is amended by striking “(except an electric automobile)” and inserting “(except an electric or natural gas automobile)” .

==(b) Automobile fuel economy definitions– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 Section 32901(a)of title 49, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 paragraph (8)] , by inserting “, but the inclusion of a reserve gasoline tank for incidental or emergency use in the event of alternative fuel depletion shall not detract from the dedicated nature of the automobile” before the period at the end; and
:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 paragraph (9)(B)] , by striking “provides equal or superior energy efficiency” and inserting “provides reasonably comparable energy efficiency” .

==(c) Minimum driving ranges for dual fueled passenger automobiles– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 Section 32901(c)(2) of title 49, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 subparagraph (B)] , by striking “(except electric automobiles)” and inserting “(except electric or natural gas automobiles)” ; and
:(2) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32901 subparagraph (C)] , by striking “(except electric automobiles)” each place it appears and inserting “(except electric or natural gas automobiles)” .

==(d) Manufacturing provision for alternative fuel automobiles– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 Section 32905(d) of title 49, United States Code] , is amended by striking [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 paragraphs (1)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 (2)] and inserting the following:

<blockquote>
:(1) the percentage utilization of the model on gasoline or diesel fuel, as determined by a formula based on the model’s alternative fuel range, divided by the fuel economy measured under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32904 section 32904(c)] ; and
:(2) the percentage utilization of the model on gaseous fuel, as determined by a formula based on the model’s alternative fuel range, divided by the fuel economy measured under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/32905 subsection (c)] ..
</blockquote>
