{{Center|A BILL}}

To allow funds under title II of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 to be used to provide training to school personnel regarding how to recognize child sexual abuse.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Helping Schools Protect Our Children Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Training teachers to recognize child sexual abuse=



==(a) State activities– ==

 Section 2113(c) of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/6613 20 U.S.C. 6613(c)] ] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(19) Providing training for all school personnel, including teachers, principals, and pupil services personnel, regarding how to recognize child sexual abuse..
</blockquote>


==(b) Local educational agency activities– ==

 Section 2123(a) of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/6623 20 U.S.C. 6623(a)] ] )is amended by inserting after paragraph (8)the following:

<blockquote>
:(9) Providing training for all school personnel, including teachers, principals, and pupil services personnel, regarding how to recognize child sexual abuse..
</blockquote>


==(c) Eligible partnership activities– ==

 Section 2134(a) of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/6634 20 U.S.C. 6634(a)] ] )is amended—
:(1) in paragraph (1)(B), by striking “and” after the semicolon;
:(2) in paragraph (2)(C), by striking the period at the end and inserting “; and” ; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(3) providing training for school personnel, including teachers, principals, and pupil services personnel, regarding how to recognize child sexual abuse..
</blockquote>
