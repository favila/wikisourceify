{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

 Relating to the application of Article II, section 3, clause 5, of the Constitution of the United States.    That the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]]has failed to meet the requirement of Article II, section 3, clause 5, of the Constitution of the United States to take care that a law be faithfully executed, with respect to the policy of the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]]to exercise prosecutorial discretion to with respect to individuals who came to the United States as children, as announced by the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]]on June 15, 2012; and that the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]shall bring a civil action in the [[w:U.S. District and Territorial Courts|United States District Court for the District of Columbia]]for declaratory or injunctive relief to challenge the policy of the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]].
