 Amending Senate Resolution 400 (94th Congress) to clarify the responsibility of committees of the Senate in the provision of the advice and consent of the Senate to nominations to positions in the intelligence community.   

=Section 1. Responsibility of committees in advice and consent of Senate to intelligence appointments=

 Section 17 of Senate Resolution 400agreed to May 19, 1976 (94th Congress) is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

=17. =


:(a) 
::(1) Except as provided insubsections (b)and(c), the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee]]shall have jurisdiction to review, hold hearings, and report the nominations of civilian individuals for positions in the intelligence community for which appointments are made by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]], by and with the advice and consent of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]].
::(2) Except as provided insubsections (b)and(c), other committees with jurisdiction over the department or agency of the Executive Branch which contain a position referred to inparagraph (1)may hold hearings and interviews with individuals nominated for such position, but only the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee]]shall report such nomination.
::(3) In this subsection, the term intelligence community means an element of the intelligence community specified in or designated under  section 3(4) of the National Security Act of 1947 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/3003 50 U.S.C. 3003(4)] ).
:(b) 
::(1) With respect to the confirmation of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Assistant Attorney General for National Security]], or any successor position, the nomination of any individual by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]to serve in such position shall be referred to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary|Committee on the Judiciary]]and, if and when reported, to the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee]]for not to exceed 20 calendar days, except that in cases when the 20-day period expires while the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]is in recess, the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee]]shall have 5 additional calendar days after the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]reconvenes to report the nomination.
::(2) If, upon the expiration of the period described inparagraph (1), the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee]]has not reported the nomination, such nomination shall be automatically discharged from the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee]]and placed on the Executive Calendar.
:(c) 
::(1) With respect to the confirmation of appointment to the position of [[w:National Security Agency/Central Security Service|Director of the National Security Agency]], [[w:National Security Agency/Central Security Service|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]], [[w:|Director of the National Reconnaissance Office]], or [[w:|Inspector General of the National Reconnaissance Office]], or any successor position to such a position, the nomination of any individual by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]to serve in such position, who at the time of the nomination is a member of the Armed Forces on active duty, shall be referred to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services]]and, if and when reported, to the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee]]for not to exceed 30 calendar days, except that in cases when the 30-day period expires while the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]is in recess, the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee]]shall have 5 additional calendar days after the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]reconvenes to report the nomination.
::(2) With respect to the confirmation of appointment to the position of [[w:National Security Agency/Central Security Service|Director of the National Security Agency]], [[w:National Security Agency/Central Security Service|Inspector General of the National Security Agency]], [[w:|Director of the National Reconnaissance Office]], or [[w:|Inspector General or the National Reconnaissance Office]], or any successor position to such a position, the nomination of any individual by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]to serve in such position, who at the time of the nomination is not a member of the Armed Forces on active duty, shall be referred to the [[w:United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Select Committee]]and, if and when reported, to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services]]for not to exceed 30 calendar days, except that in cases when the 30-day period expires while the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]is in recess, the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services]]shall have an additional 5 calendar days after the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]reconvenes to report the nomination.
::(3) If, upon the expiration of the period of sequential referral described inparagraphs (1)and(2), the committee to which the nomination was sequentially referred has not reported the nomination, the nomination shall be automatically discharged from that committee and placed on the Executive Calendar. . 
</blockquote>
