{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amendchapter 44of title 18, United States Code, to prohibit the possession of a firearm by a person who is adjudicated to have committed a violent juvenile act.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Preventing Gun Violence Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Prohibition on possession of a firearm by a person who is adjudicated to have committed a violent juvenile act=



==(a) Possession ban– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922  Section 922(g)of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by striking“or”at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (8)];
:(2) by striking the comma at the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (9)]and inserting“; or”; and
:(3) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 paragraph (9)]the following:

<blockquote> 
::(10) who has been adjudicated by a court of the United States to have committed a violent juvenile act, . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Violent juvenile act defined– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 Section 921(a) of such title]is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(36) The term violent juvenile act means—
::(A) an act by a person before the person attains 18 years of age that, if committed by an adult, would be punishable by a term of imprisonment exceeding one year, and—
:::(i) has as an element the use, attempted use, or threatened use of physical force against another person; or
:::(ii) by its nature, involves a substantial risk that physical force against another person may be used in the course of committing the act; and
::(B) an act described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 subparagraph (A)]—
:::(i) by a person who has attained 18 years of age but not 21 years of age; and
:::(ii) for which the person is tried as a juvenile or youthful offender under State law. . 
</blockquote>
