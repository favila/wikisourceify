{{Center|A BILL}}

To designate certain land in the State of Oregon as wilderness, to make additional wild and scenic river designations in the State of Oregon, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|S1}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Oregon Treasures Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|id06D6D8DC91254977A8F8C2DBDFB3B9EB}}Sec. 2. Cathedral Rock and Horse Heaven Wilderness=



=={{anchor|ID3f6d14215414457499a225e1e5ca0639}}(a) Definitions– ==

In this section:

==={{anchor|ID8675f91bf14b414e99523589d30b6bcc}}(1) Land exchange map– ===

The term land exchange map means the map entitled“Antone Ranch Exchanges”and dated July 26, 2010.

==={{anchor|IDd29f3cd5a8454fe39430df123fcf05d8}}(2) Proposed wilderness map– ===

The term proposed wilderness map means the map entitled“Cathedral Rock and Horse Heaven Wilderness”and dated November 8, 2010.

==={{anchor|IDd0c48b585507406a97bf5737a1d3e06c}}(3) Secretary– ===

The term Secretary means the Secretary of the Interior.

==={{anchor|IDdf5ec5ec0f1540aba34a876db3c23262}}(4) State– ===

The term State means the State of Oregon.

=={{anchor|ID31a53d682b9d47da8ad6a2690486ddb8}}(b) Land exchanges– ==



==={{anchor|ID9a747d13165d40259b76f965c83ae8a7}}(1) Authorization– ===



===={{anchor|ID12459dd363af45f99951410682eaf613}}(A) Smith exchange– ====



====={{anchor|IDda0286d7d57e464c9b87e53c26b4a577}}(i) In general– =====

Subject to paragraphs (2) through (5), if the owner of the non-Federal land described in clause (ii)(I)offers to convey to the United States all right, title, and interest of the owner in and to the non-Federal land, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall—
:(I) accept the offer; and
:(II) convey to the owner of the non-Federal land all right, title, and interest of the United States in and to the Federal land described in clause (ii)(II).

====={{anchor|IDc2d2dd5979c64474b7410f31aa96ba8e}}(ii) Description of land– =====



======{{anchor|ID62486839516149d6abbb2719f7aeaa0b}}(I) Non-Federal land– ======

The non-Federal land referred to in clause (i)is the approximately 1,135 acres of non-Federal land generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Land transfer from Smith to BLM”.

======{{anchor|ID4fa42aafc03b4003b8816e3319403f77}}(II) Federal land– ======

The Federal land referred to in clause (i)(II)is the approximately 1,195 acres of Federal land generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Land transfer from BLM to Smith”.

===={{anchor|ID5c8688f6774f48b8a05320c43f92830d}}(B) Shrum exchange– ====



====={{anchor|IDcff84fa988d24c43affb20659d5057be}}(i) In general– =====

Subject to paragraphs (2) through (5), if the owner of the non-Federal land described in clause (ii)(I)offers to convey to the United States all right, title, and interest of the owner in and to the non-Federal land, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall—
:(I) accept the offer; and
:(II) convey to the owner of the non-Federal land all right, title, and interest of the United States in and to the Federal land described in clause (ii)(II).

====={{anchor|ID3af527dc61984fd687aba51cb0af1cfd}}(ii) Description of land– =====



======{{anchor|IDa1e76d68f2ce44c2915df9ba4983ce0a}}(I) Non-Federal land– ======

The non-Federal land referred to in clause (i)is the approximately 415 acres of non-Federal land generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Land transfer from Shrum to BLM”.

======{{anchor|ID6a935b5aa36d494b9704a9ae18458ae7}}(II) Federal land– ======

The Federal land referred to in clause (i)(II)is the approximately 555 acres of Federal land generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Land transfer from BLM to Shrum”.

===={{anchor|IDbee13f0af8c844aaa134a09fd1815265}}(C) Young life exchange– ====



====={{anchor|ID9c5ddbe538b7468895ffecf1851dd93d}}(i) In general– =====

Subject to paragraphs (2) through (5), if the owner of the non-Federal land described in clause (ii)(I)offers to convey to the United States all right, title, and interest of the owner in and to the non-Federal land, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] and the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] shall—
:(I) accept the offer; and
:(II) convey to the owner of the non-Federal land all right, title, and interest of the United States in and to the Federal land described in clause (ii)(II).

====={{anchor|ID46485b8788cf45608157a7aa51dfc934}}(ii) Description of land– =====



======{{anchor|IDdd4d3e0638594e92acd639daf1993db5}}(I) Non-Federal land– ======

The non-Federal land referred to in clause (i)is the approximately 10,290 acres of non-Federal land generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Land transfer from Young Life to BLM”.

======{{anchor|ID22340bba3f014501b9b750423f43fcb2}}(II) Federal land– ======

The Federal land referred to in clause (i)(II)is—
:(aa) the approximately 11,365 acres of Federal land generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Land transfer from BLM to Young Life”;
:(bb) the approximately 645 acres of Federal land generally depicted on the land exchange map as“Land transfer from BLM to Young Life”; and
:(cc) the approximately 690 acres of Federal land generally depicted on the land exchange map as“Land transfer from USFS to Young Life”.

==={{anchor|IDcc8fb32df22242f390abcc95c83a3d67}}(2) Applicable law– ===

Each land exchange under paragraph (1)shall be carried out in accordance with section 206 of the Federal Land Policy and Management Act of 1976([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1716 43 U.S.C. 1716] ), including the requirement that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] determine that the public interest will be well served by making the exchange.

==={{anchor|ID5e452874458d4d28aa97c9c0d68acf2f}}(3) Conditions– ===

Each land exchange under paragraph (1)shall be subject to—
:(A) valid existing rights;
:(B) the condition that the owner make the offer to convey all or part of the non-Federal land during the 3-year period beginning on the date of enactment of this Act;
:(C) the condition that the owner of the non-Federal land pay not less than 50 percent of all costs relating to the land exchange, including the costs of appraisals, surveys, and any necessary environmental clearances;
:(D) the condition that title to the non-Federal land be acceptable to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] and in conformance with the title approval standards applicable to Federal land acquisitions; and
:(E) such terms and conditions as the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] or the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] , as appropriate, may require.

==={{anchor|ID0e76b951f8e24022895ff07a78cdef86}}(4) Valuation, appraisals, and equalization– ===



===={{anchor|ID128f689dedf1432d8672ae0cebad95ed}}(A) In general– ====

The value of the Federal land and the non-Federal land to be conveyed in each land exchange under this subsection—
:(i) shall be equal, as determined by appraisals conducted in accordance with subparagraph (B); or
:(ii) if not equal, shall be equalized in accordance with subparagraph (C).

===={{anchor|IDbb1fe96ea4f948c7bf2fc56120682a5e}}(B) Appraisals– ====



====={{anchor|IDf0b5aed99ed74f2087dfae13eef6e2b3}}(i) In general– =====

The Federal land and the non-Federal land to be exchanged under this subsection shall be appraised by an independent, qualified appraiser that is agreed to by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] or the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] , as appropriate.

====={{anchor|IDe95cdeb6a58e40fa81248a48ad3cd3f6}}(ii) Requirements– =====

An appraisal under clause (i)shall be conducted in accordance with—
:(I) the Uniform Appraisal Standards for Federal Land Acquisitions; and
:(II) the Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice.

===={{anchor|IDcea4c0d7c59144a993ce365c9d8b983a}}(C) Equalization– ====



====={{anchor|ID5b55bae524014db1aca05a093c6b591b}}(i) In general– =====

If the value of the Federal land and the non-Federal land to be conveyed in a land exchange under this subsection is not equal, the value may be equalized by—
:(I) making a cash equalization payment to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] or to the owner of the non-Federal land, as appropriate, in accordance with section 206(b) of the Federal Land Policy and Management Act of 1976([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1716 43 U.S.C. 1716(b)] ); or
:(II) reducing the acreage of the Federal land or the non-Federal land to be exchanged, as appropriate.

====={{anchor|ID6844ff58dbe04fb1ac36bcc25d36b5a3}}(ii) Cash equalization payments– =====

Any cash equalization payments received by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] under clause (i)(I)shall be—
:(I) deposited in the Federal Land Disposal Account established by section 206(a) of the Federal Land Transaction Facilitation Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/2305 43 U.S.C. 2305(a)] ); and
:(II) used in accordance with that Act.

==={{anchor|IDf3786f573cc341298a8dfe7128834e0b}}(5) Surveys– ===

The exact acreage and legal description of the Federal land and non-Federal land to be exchanged under paragraph (1)shall be determined by surveys approved by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] .

==={{anchor|ID41506f9e998e4a8fa2abb486a7907c7a}}(6) Completion of land exchange– ===

It is the intent of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that the land exchanges under this subsection be completed not later than 5 years after the date of enactment of this Act.

==={{anchor|ID24ce0482250e438ba2fdb07022ab1e02}}(7) Transfer of administrative jurisdiction– ===



===={{anchor|ID9d357362569b4ccea058fcafeaf8721e}}(A) In general– ====

Administrative jurisdiction over the approximately 750 acres of Federal land managed by the [[w:Bureau of Land Management|Bureau of Land Management]] generally depicted on the land exchange map as“Land transfer from BLM to USFS”is transferred from the [[w:Bureau of Land Management|Bureau of Land Management]] to the [[w:United States Forest Service|Forest Service]] .

===={{anchor|IDb001f4b566744bb78822917fd939013e}}(B) Administration– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] shall administer the transferred land in accordance with—
:(i) the Act of March 1, 1911(commonly known as the“ Weeks Act”) ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/480 16 U.S.C. 480 et seq.] ); and
:(ii) the laws (including regulations) applicable to the [[w:United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] .

===={{anchor|ID045fbf15b21a41efb02c2c5c7791c262}}(C) Costs– ====

Any costs relating to the transfer under subparagraph (A), including any costs for surveys and other administrative costs, shall be paid by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] .

=={{anchor|IDaf9fa6ef27f04587b03a0933793a3d60}}(c) Potential wilderness areas– ==



==={{anchor|ID3c3b9baccc5f4af5a1e3ab1946f59a66}}(1) Designation of potential wilderness– ===



===={{anchor|ID8671aa64cd2747cb93fdb1f8b0a822d9}}(A) In general– ====

Subject to paragraph (4), in furtherance of the purposes of the Wilderness Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1131 16 U.S.C. 1131 et seq.] ), the following areas of Federal land managed by the [[w:Bureau of Land Management|Bureau of Land Management]] in the State are designated as potential wilderness areas:

====={{anchor|ID1328683f0b8042ac83db4df6f3793357}}(i) Cathedral rock– =====

Certain land comprising approximately 4,560 acres generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Proposed Cathedral Rock Wilderness”.

====={{anchor|IDf4e1a88780bd4317929f5a09b2d9e81b}}(ii) Horse heaven– =====

Certain land comprising approximately 2,815 acres generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Proposed Horse Heaven Wilderness”.

===={{anchor|ID8b28f7d78656428da1bca7c24a5077cf}}(B) Interim management– ====

Subject to paragraph (4), each potential wilderness area designated under paragraph (1)shall be managed in a manner that maintains or improves the wilderness character of the potential wilderness area and suitability of the potential wilderness area for designation in accordance with the Wilderness Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1131 16 U.S.C. 1131 et seq.] )until the date on which the potential wilderness area is designated as wilderness under paragraph (2).

==={{anchor|ID83ef400c92d24a0498a75925c4804f46}}(2) Designation of wilderness– ===



===={{anchor|ID7a4fd520dd164d13b2511c436a01f842}}(A) Cathedral rock wilderness– ====

Subject to paragraph (4), the Federal land within the boundaries of the area generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Proposed Cathedral Rock Wilderness”shall be designated as wilderness and as a component of the National Wilderness Preservation System, to be known as the“Cathedral Rock Wilderness”, on the earlier of—
:(i) the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] publishes in the Federal Register notice that sufficient inholdings within the boundaries of the Proposed Cathedral Rock Wilderness have been acquired to establish a manageable wilderness unit; or
:(ii) the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] acquires secs. 2, 11, and 23 in T. 9 S, R. 19 E.

===={{anchor|ID324ad4715655474cb2b3f4c1ebedb265}}(B) Horse heaven wilderness– ====

Subject to paragraph (4), the Federal land within the boundaries of the area generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Proposed Horse Heaven Wilderness”shall be designated as wilderness and as a component of the National Wilderness Preservation System, to be known as the“Horse Heaven Wilderness”, on the earlier of—
:(i) the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] publishes in the Federal Register notice that sufficient inholdings within the boundaries of the Proposed Horse Heaven Wilderness have been acquired to establish a manageable wilderness unit; or
:(ii) the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] acquires those portions of secs. 11, 12, 13, 23, and 24 in T. 10 S, R. 18 E. that are generally depicted as within the boundaries of the“Proposed Horse Heaven Wilderness”on the proposed wilderness map.

===={{anchor|IDe2051537ce8a43a7ae6e206c31704bf4}}(C) Maps; legal descriptions– ====



====={{anchor|IDcee1dcfc71be46db9871539dda83f4c0}}(i) In general– =====

As soon as practicable after the date on which a wilderness area is designated under subparagraph (A)or (B), the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall prepare a map and legal description of the wilderness area.

====={{anchor|ID71668e40113242afb7faa428a144fde1}}(ii) Force of law– =====

The maps and legal descriptions prepared under clause (i)shall have the same force and effect as if included in this section, except that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may correct minor errors in the maps and legal descriptions.

====={{anchor|ID1dba820b7c29421cac686dc1ec14c013}}(iii) Availability– =====

The maps and legal descriptions prepared under clause (i)shall be on file and available for public inspection in the appropriate offices of the [[w:Bureau of Land Management|Bureau of Land Management]] .

===={{anchor|IDdf3511c50fe74af7944dc1d959510b38}}(D) Administration of wilderness– ====



====={{anchor|ID76e9f6b713e34726a7873f94f0abd209}}(i) In general– =====

Subject to valid existing rights, each area designated as wilderness under subparagraph (A)or (B)shall be administered by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] in accordance with the Wilderness Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1131 16 U.S.C. 1131 et seq.] ), except that—
:(I) any reference in the Wilderness Actto the effective date of that Act shall be considered to be a reference to the date of enactment of this Act; and
:(II) any reference in the Wilderness Actto the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] shall be considered to be a reference to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] .

====={{anchor|IDb1d6a3e224bb49a38873dd6905caae8b}}(ii) Grazing– =====

The grazing of livestock in a wilderness area designated under subparagraph (A)or (B), if established before the date of enactment of this Act, shall be permitted to continue subject to such reasonable regulations as are considered necessary by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] , in accordance with—
:(I)  section 4(d)(4) of the Wilderness Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1133 16 U.S.C. 1133(d)(4)] ); and
:(II) the guidelines set forth in Appendix A of the report of theCommittee on Interior and Insular Affairs of the House of Representativesaccompanying H.R. 2570 of the 101st Congress (H. Rept. 101–405).

====={{anchor|ID7587a3cc1e9b4aff92d6f8da0f0a469e}}(iii) Tribal rights– =====

Nothing in this subsection alters, modifies, enlarges, diminishes, or abrogates the treaty rights of any Indian tribe, including the off-reservation reserved rights secured by the Treaty with the Tribes and Bands of Middle Oregon of June 25, 1855 (12 Stat. 963).

==={{anchor|ID920f3582e1724a4c9da4dbd2a0edb67b}}(3) Incorporation of acquired land and interests– ===

Subject to paragraph (4), any land or interest in land that is acquired by the United States within the boundaries generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Proposed Cathedral Rock Wilderness”and“Proposed Horse Heaven Wilderness”shall—
:(A) become part of the potential wilderness area or wilderness area, as applicable; and
:(B) be managed in accordance with—
::(i) this subsection; and
::(ii) any other applicable laws.

==={{anchor|id11ccefa718454aeb85434c3d1bf424b3}}(4) Termination of authority– ===



===={{anchor|id7a481c0341284191a12c34ecd2a15c3e}}(A) Cathedral rock– ====

If the Cathedral Rock Wilderness is not designated as wilderness in accordance with paragraph (2)(A)by the date that is 10 years after the date of enactment of this Act, paragraphs (1) through (3)shall cease to be effective with respect to the Federal land described in paragraph (1)(A)(i).

===={{anchor|id10673a88f9cd42aab0910fdeb8cc6789}}(B) Horse heaven– ====

If the Horse Heaven Wilderness is not designated as wilderness in accordance with paragraph (2)(A)by the date that is 10 years after the date of enactment of this Act, paragraphs (1) through (3)shall cease to be effective with regard to the Federal land described in paragraph (1)(A)(ii).

==={{anchor|IDf1a1d9a3c64d4f18bb3121b227ece6af}}(5) Withdrawal– ===

Subject to valid existing rights, within the boundaries generally depicted on the proposed wilderness map as“Proposed Cathedral Rock Wilderness”and“Proposed Horse Heaven Wilderness”, the Federal land and any land or interest in land that is acquired by the United States is withdrawn from all forms of—
:(A) entry, appropriation, and disposal under the public land laws;
:(B) location, entry, and patent under the mining laws; and
:(C) operation of the mineral leasing, mineral materials, and geothermal leasing laws.

={{anchor|id677DFD6D277541289765684535FD2A9B}}Sec. 3. Wild Rogue Wilderness Area=



=={{anchor|IDc215525799cd4b67b746f0a54afff885}}(a) Definitions– ==

In this section:

==={{anchor|id4FB22F18359A437F9B33EA2162ABCD47}}(1) Commission– ===

The term“Commission”means the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission.

==={{anchor|ID56fe0abbc0244f17be2ec0ec762e6e4b}}(2) Map– ===

The term map means the map entitled“Wild Rogue Wilderness Additions”and dated December 8, 2011.

==={{anchor|ID6f39d5aba775450d92b817a8e22224e1}}(3) Secretary– ===

The term Secretary means the Secretary of the Interior.

==={{anchor|IDe7e9d3cd4bb940ac8f92a17b4df35354}}(4) State– ===

The term State means the State of Oregon.

==={{anchor|ID46afd64d569b44faa06b2ecdc7881d04}}(5) Wilderness additions– ===

The term Wilderness additions means the land added to the Wild Rogue Wilderness by subsection (b)(1).

=={{anchor|idABFF1966639D454BBC60E8BE4C3EF72B}}(b) Expansion of wild rogue wilderness area– ==



==={{anchor|H5383954819CB4B66906FBB3071B7A100}}(1) Expansion– ===

In accordance with the Wilderness Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1131 16 U.S.C. 1131 et seq.] ), the approximately 60,000 acres of [[w:Bureau of Land Management|Bureau of Land Management]] land, as generally depicted on the map, is included in the Wild Rogue Wilderness, a component of the National Wilderness Preservation System.

==={{anchor|HB33239EBB0654FAB9A74BFFE57ECB7C3}}(2) Map; legal description– ===



===={{anchor|H05C42A5D41914A78AF321D7A3B3919B8}}(A) In general– ====

As soon as practicable after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall file a map and legal description of the wilderness area designated by paragraph (1), with—
:(i) theCommittee on Energy and Natural Resources of the Senate; and
:(ii) theCommittee on Natural Resources of the House of Representatives.

===={{anchor|HBEA30B4F469446478B99BD8A92A047E7}}(B) Force of law– ====

The map and legal description filed under subparagraph (A)shall have the same force and effect as if included in this section, except that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may correct typographical errors in the map and legal description.

===={{anchor|HB3B3526210FA444EA28C08677D474B98}}(C) Public availability– ====

The map and legal description filed under subparagraph (A)shall be on file and available for public inspection in the appropriate offices of the [[w:Bureau of Land Management|Bureau of Land Management]] .

==={{anchor|IDcdd2779a811548899f73b4546567dbae}}(3) Administration– ===

Subject to valid existing rights, the Wilderness additions shall be administered by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] in accordance with the Wilderness Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1131 16 U.S.C. 1131 et seq.] ), except that—
:(A) any reference in that Act to the effective date shall be considered to be a reference to the date of enactment of this Act; and
:(B) any reference in that Act to the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] shall be considered to be a reference to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] .

==={{anchor|ID6d3241f06a94456c9d2d938e2c2476ae}}(4) Fish and Wildlife– ===

Nothing in this subsection affects the jurisdiction or responsibilities of the State with respect to fish and wildlife in the State.

==={{anchor|IDa2f5a4b40207472f9930cac3bf54f2cf}}(5) Adjacent management– ===



===={{anchor|ID01f3789b5d7b468c8e29f118600764fe}}(A) In general– ====

Nothing in this subsection creates any protective perimeter or buffer zone around the Wilderness additions.

===={{anchor|ID8bf1e2b0e2224edfa86d6e7f79935b08}}(B) Activities outside wilderness– ====

The fact that a nonwilderness activity or use on land outside the Wilderness additions can be seen or heard within the Wilderness additions shall not preclude the activity or use outside the boundary of the Wilderness additions.

==={{anchor|ID5e86d14a3d2348e689a05cd4d9b1a771}}(6) Protection of Tribal Rights– ===

Nothing in this subsection diminishes any treaty rights of an Indian tribe.

==={{anchor|H7A990EA5EA8F41278BEBF018B9EBCBB6}}(7) Withdrawal– ===

Subject to valid existing rights, the Wilderness additions are withdrawn from all forms of—
:(A) entry, appropriation, or disposal under the public land laws;
:(B) location, entry, and patent under the mining laws; and
:(C) disposition under all laws pertaining to mineral and geothermal leasing or mineral materials.

=={{anchor|HF2B758071F9F4826894329B027D522DD}}(c) Wild and scenic river designations, Rogue River area– ==



==={{anchor|HC6BB5B26B3774F6A85F93ACAC2295DF2}}(1) Amendments– ===

 Section 3(a) of the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1274 16 U.S.C. 1274(a)] )is amended by striking paragraph (5)and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|id69C2BC64508B43E6877F468657DDED03}}(5) Rogue, Oregon– ====



====={{anchor|idD68157658E744428BC5D1923935F50A8}}(A) In general– =====

The segment of the river extending from the mouth of the Applegate River downstream to the Lobster Creek Bridge, to be administered by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] or the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] , as agreed to by the Secretaries of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Interior]] and [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Agriculture]] or as directed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] .

====={{anchor|id56F7B82C122B454DA001B09694F4DB08}}(B) Additions– =====

In addition to the segment described in subparagraph (A), there are designated the following segments in the Rogue River:

======{{anchor|HB39B3B865F6D45FEBD4C087BACE657A8}}(i) Kelsey creek– ======

The approximately 4.8-mile segment of Kelsey Creek from the east section line of T. 32 S., R. 9 W., sec. 34, Willamette Meridian, to the confluence with the Rogue River, as a wild river.

======{{anchor|H65ED76D66300448B9D98914137980E3D}}(ii) East fork kelsey creek– ======

The approximately 4.6-mile segment of East Fork Kelsey Creek from the Wild Rogue Wilderness boundary in T. 33 S., R. 8 W., sec. 5, Willamette Meridian, to the confluence with Kelsey Creek, as a wild river.

======{{anchor|H3405CFE3AC11408B93042E2B72626A1D}}(iii) Whisky creek– ======



======={{anchor|HB5DC1E1CECE5439886A225E9809909D6}}(I) Recreational river– =======

The approximately 0.6-mile segment of Whisky Creek from the confluence of the East Fork and West Fork to 0.1 miles downstream from road 33-8-23, as a recreational river.

======={{anchor|HCF8023F3BF3B44AF9BB36311956E7B6F}}(II) Wild river– =======

The approximately 1.9-mile segment of Whisky Creek from 0.1 miles downstream from road 33-8-23 to the confluence with the Rogue River, as a wild river.

==={{anchor|H0890AE1355A5467EA39DBB49B0E15F88}}(2) Management– ===

Each river segment designated by subparagraph (B) of section 3(a)(5) of the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1274 16 U.S.C. 1274(a)(5)] )(as added by paragraph (1)) shall be managed as part of the Rogue Wild and Scenic River.

==={{anchor|H1AE4E29482F947838722BACE4E5E7335}}(3) Withdrawal– ===

Subject to valid existing rights, the Federal land within the boundaries of the river segments designated under subparagraph (B) of section 3(a)(5) of the Wild and Scenic Rivers Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1274 16 U.S.C. 1274(a)(5)] )(as added by paragraph (1)) is withdrawn from all forms of—
:(A) entry, appropriation, or disposal under the public land laws;
:(B) location, entry, and patent under the mining laws; and
:(C) disposition under all laws pertaining to mineral and geothermal leasing or mineral materials.

=={{anchor|H7A7C309BF16448A194757243C6292B8E}}(d) Additional protections for Rogue River tributaries– ==



==={{anchor|H709F68FCA1C943828784B92B196A40AA}}(1) Licensing by commission– ===

The [[w:Federal Energy Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall not license the construction of any dam, water conduit, reservoir, powerhouse, transmission line, or other project works on or directly affecting any stream described in paragraph (4).

==={{anchor|id828FB0CA79E94B19B36AB11C7BD54F0D}}(2) Other agencies– ===



===={{anchor|id0393E3735D34409B9B48BA7B4F8DA938}}(A) In general– ====

No department or agency of the United States shall assist by loan, grant, license, or otherwise in the construction of any water resources project on or directly affecting any stream segment that is described in paragraph (4), except to maintain or repair water resources projects in existence on the date of enactment of this Act.

===={{anchor|idC180CF2E8057477697D150D0A3782F6C}}(B) Effect– ====

Nothing in this paragraph prohibits any department or agency of the United States in assisting by loan, grant, license, or otherwise, a water resources project—
:(i) the primary purpose of which is ecological or aquatic restoration; and
:(ii) that provides a net benefit to water quality and aquatic resources.

==={{anchor|H13ACBC0D6806443B9D519A71AD11E618}}(3) Withdrawal– ===

Subject to valid existing rights, the Federal land located within a1/4mile on either side of the stream segments described in paragraph (4), is withdrawn from all forms of—
:(A) entry, appropriation, or disposal under the public land laws;
:(B) location, entry, and patent under the mining laws; and
:(C) disposition under all laws pertaining to mineral and geothermal leasing or mineral materials.
