{{Center|CONCURRENT RESOLUTION}}

Authorizing the use of the Capitol Grounds for the Greater Washington Soap Box Derby.

=Section 1. Use of Capitol Grounds for soap box derby races=



==(a) In General– ==

The Greater Washington Soap Box Derby Association (in this resolution referred to as the sponsor ) shall be permitted to sponsor a public event, soap box derby races (in this resolution referred to as the event ), on the Capitol Grounds.

==(b) Date of Event– ==

The event shall be held on June 15, 2013, or on such other date as the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Rules and Administration|Committee on Rules and Administration of the Senate]] jointly designate.

=Sec. 2. Terms and conditions=



==(a) In General– ==

Under conditions to be prescribed by the [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]] and the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]] , the event shall be—
:(1) free of admission charge and open to the public; and
:(2) arranged not to interfere with the needs of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] .

==(b) Expenses and Liabilities– ==

The sponsor shall assume full responsibility for all expenses and liabilities incident to all activities associated with the event.

=Sec. 3. Event preparations=

Subject to the approval of the [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]] , the sponsor is authorized to erect upon the Capitol Grounds such stage, sound amplification devices, and other related structures and equipment as may be required for the event.

=Sec. 4. Additional arrangements=

The [[w:Architect of the Capitol|Architect of the Capitol]] and the [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]] are authorized to make such additional arrangements as may be required to carry out the event.

=Sec. 5. Enforcement of restrictions=

The [[w:Attending Physician of the United States Congress|Capitol Police Board]] shall provide for enforcement of the restrictions contained in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/5104 section 5104(c) of title 40, United States Code] , concerning sales, advertisements, displays, and solicitations on the Capitol Grounds, as well as other restrictions applicable to the Capitol Grounds, with respect to the event.
