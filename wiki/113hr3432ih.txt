{{Center|A BILL}}

To expedite the planning and implementation of salvage timber sales as part of Forest Service and Department of the Interior restoration and rehabilitation activities for lands within the Gila and Lincoln National Forests and for Bureau of Land Management lands in New Mexico adversely impacted by the 2012 and 2013 fire seasons, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “New Mexico Wildland Fire Emergency Salvage Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Expedited salvage timber sales in response to the Silver, Little Bear, and Whitewater-Baldy Complex Fires in New Mexico=



==(a) Salvage timber sales required– ==

As part of the restoration and rehabilitation activities undertaken on lands within the Gila National Forest and Lincoln National Forest and on Bureau of Land Management lands adversely impacted by the 2012 Little Bear and Whitewater-Baldy Complex Fires and the 2013 Silver Fire in New Mexico, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] , with respect to the affected National Forest System lands, and the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] , with respect to affected Bureau of Land Management lands, shall promptly plan and implement salvage timber sales of dead, damaged, or downed timber resulting from those wildfires.

==(b) Legal sufficiency– ==

Due to the extraordinary severity of the wildfires referred to in subsection (a), salvage timber sales conducted under such subsection shall proceed immediately and to completion notwithstanding any other provision of law, including the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] ] ), section 14 of the National Forest Management Act of 1976( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/472a 16 U.S.C. 472a] ] ), the Forest and Rangeland Renewable Resources Planning Act of 1974( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1600 16 U.S.C. 1600 et seq.] ] ), the Federal Land Policy and Management Act of 1976( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1701 43 U.S.C. 1701 et seq.] ] ), and laws related to the management of timber on the lands covered by such subsection.

==(c) Administrative and judicial review– ==

Salvage timber sales conducted under subsection (a)shall not be subject to—
:(1) administrative review, including, in the case of the [[w:United States Forest Service|Forest Service]] , the notice, comment, and appeal requirements of section 322 of the Department of the Interior and Related Agencies Appropriations Act, 1993([[Public Law -|[[Public Law 102-381|Public Law 102–381]] ]] ; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1612 16 U.S.C. 1612note] ); or
:(2) judicial review in any court of the United States.

==(d) Limitation; rule of construction– ==

Nothing in this section shall be construed in a manner that would affect the land management plan applicable to any area designated as a component of the National Wilderness Preservation System under section 3 of the Wilderness Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1132 16 U.S.C. 1132] ] )and containing lands covered by subsection (a).
