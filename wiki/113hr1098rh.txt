{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Public Health Service Act to reauthorize certain programs relating to traumatic brain injury and to trauma research.    

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Traumatic Brain Injury Reauthorization Act of 2013   ”. 

=Sec. 2. CDC programs for prevention and surveillance of traumatic brain injury=



==(a) Prevention– ==

   Section 393B(b)(3) of the Public Health Service Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/280b–1c 42 U.S.C. 280b–1c(b)(3)])is amended by striking“health-status goals for 2010, commonly referred to as Healthy People 2010”and inserting“health-status goals for 2020, commonly referred to as Healthy People 2020”.

==(b) Surveillance– ==

   Subsection (b) of section 393C of the Public Health Service Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/280b–1d 42 U.S.C. 280b–1d] )is amended—
:(1) by striking“(b) Not later than”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

====(b) Reports– ====



=====(1) Initial report– =====

Not later than ; and 
</blockquote>

:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

====(2) Subsequent report– ====

Not later than 24 months after the date of enactment of the  Traumatic Brain Injury Reauthorization Act of 2013  , the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]], acting through the [[w:Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|Director of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention]]and the [[w:National Institutes of Health|Director of the National Institutes of Health]]and in consultation with the [[w:|Secretary of Defense]]and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]], shall submit to the relevant committees of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report that—
:(A) identifies which recommendations in the report under paragraph (1) have been adopted and which recommendations in such report have not been adopted; and
:(B) includes a description of planned activities to address each recommendation in such report that has not been adopted. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Funding– ==

   Section 394A of the Public Health Service Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/280b–3 42 U.S.C. 280b–3] )is amended—
:(1) by striking“and”after“1994,”;
:(2) by striking the second period at the end; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:“ Of the amounts made available to carry out this part for each of fiscal years 2014 through 2018, there is authorized to be appropriated $6,100,000 to carry out sections 393B and 393C  . ”. 

=Sec. 3. State grants for projects regarding traumatic brain injury=

   Section 1252 of the Public Health Service Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/300d–52 42 U.S.C. 300d–52] )is amended—
:(1) in subsection (a) , by striking“, acting through the Administrator of the Health Resources and Services Administration,”;
:(2) in paragraphs (1)(A)(i) and (3)(E) of subsection (f) , by striking“brain injury”and inserting“traumatic brain injury”;
:(3) in subsection (h) , by striking the comma after“under this section”and inserting a comma before“including”; and
:(4) by amending subsection (j) to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

===(j) Authorization of appropriations– ===

  For carrying out this section and section 1253  , there is authorized to be appropriated $9,760,000 for each of fiscal years 2014 through 2018.  . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 4. State grants for protection and advocacy services=

   Section 1253 of the Public Health Service Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/300d–53 42 U.S.C. 300d–53] )is amended—
:(1) in subsection (a) , by striking“, acting through the Administrator of the Health Resources and Services Administration (referred to in this section as the“Administrator”),”;
:(2) in subsections (c) , (d)(1) , (e)(1) , (e)(4) , (g) , (h) , and (j)(1) , by striking“Administrator”each place it appears and inserting“Secretary”;
:(3) in subsection (h) —
::(A) by striking the subsection heading and inserting“ Reporting ”;
::(B) by striking“Each protection and advocacy system”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

====(1) Reports by systems– ====

Each protection and advocacy system ; and 
</blockquote>

::(C) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

====(2) Report by Secretary– ====

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of the  Traumatic Brain Injury Reauthorization Act of 2013  , the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]shall prepare and submit to the appropriate committees of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report describing the services and activities carried out under this section during the period for which the report is being prepared. . 
</blockquote>

:(4) in subsection (i) —
::(A) by striking“Administrator of the Health Resources and Services Administration”and inserting“  [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] ”; and
::(B) by striking“by the Administrator”and inserting“by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] ”;
:(5) in subsection (k) , by striking“subtitle C”and inserting“subtitle C of title I”;
:(6) by striking subsection (l) (relating to authorization of appropriations); and
:(7) by redesignating subsection (m) as subsection (l) .
