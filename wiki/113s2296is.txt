{{Center|A BILL}}

 To require the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to employ at least three decision review officers at each regional office of the Department of Veterans Affairs, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Veterans Appeals Improvement Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Findings=

  [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]makes the following findings:
:(1)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7101  Section 7101(a)of title 38, United States Code], requires that the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board of Veterans' Appeals]]have such number of members and staff as may be found necessary in order to conduct hearings and dispose of appeals properly before the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board]]in a timely manner.
:(2) The supporting information submitted along with the budget under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105  section 1105of title 31, United States Code], for fiscal year 2015 for the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]states that appeals received by the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board of Veterans' Appeals]]are expected to increase by 52 percent from the 47,763 appeals received in 2011 to 72,786 by the end of 2015.
:(3) The annual report of the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board of Veterans' Appeals]]for Fiscal Year 2012 indicates that with 64 members, the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board]]was able to issue 44,300 decisions in that fiscal year.
:(4) The [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board]]also noted in such report that there is a direct and proportional correlation between the number of members and staff of the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board]]and the number of decisions they can issue. 

=Sec. 3. Minimum number of decision review officers at regional offices of Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) In general– ==

Not later than one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall ensure that not fewer than three decision review officers are employed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]at each regional office of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].

==(b) Notice– ==



===(1) In general– ===

In any case in which the number of decision review officers employed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]at a regional office of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]declines to fewer than three after the date that is one year after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the Senate]]and the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the House of Representatives]]notice of such decline.

===(2) Contents– ===

Notice submitted under paragraph (1) with respect to a regional office of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]shall include the following:
:(A) The number of decision review officers employed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]at such regional office.
:(B) A plan to increase the number of decision review officers at such regional office to at least three, including an estimate of when at least three decision review officers will be employed at such regional office.
:(C) A description of the measures that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]plans to take to ensure that appeals properly before the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board of Veterans' Appeals]]that would normally be disposed of by decision review officers at such regional office are disposed of in a timely manner during the period in which fewer than three decision review officers are employed at such regional office. 

=Sec. 4. Report=



==(a) In general– ==

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the Senate]]and the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the House of Representatives]]a report on the ability of the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board of Veterans' Appeals]]to conduct hearings and dispose of appeals properly before the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Board]]in a timely manner.

==(b) Contents– ==

The report required by subsection (a) shall include the following:
:(1) Whether the number of members of the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board]]is sufficient to conduct hearings and dispose of appeals properly before the [[w:Board of Veterans Appeals|Board]]in a timely manner.
:(2) An assessment of the feasibility and advisability of increasing the number of decision review officers by 10, 15, and 25 percent from the number of review officers employed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]on the day before the date of the enactment of this Act.
