{{Center|A BILL}}

 To prohibit certain assistance to the Palestinian Authority.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Stand with Israel Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Prohibition on foreign assistance=



==(a) In general– ==

Except as provided under subsection (b) and notwithstanding any other provision of law, no amounts may be obligated or expended to provide any direct United States assistance, loan guarantee, or debt relief to the Palestinian Authority, or any affiliated governing entity or leadership organization.

==(b) Exception– ==

The prohibition under subsection (a) shall have no effect for a fiscal year if the President certifies to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]during that fiscal year that the Palestinian Authority has—
:(1) formally recognized the right of Israel to exist as a Jewish state;
:(2) publicly recognized the state of Israel;
:(3) renounced terrorism;
:(4) purged all individuals with terrorist ties from security services;
:(5) terminated funding of anti-American and anti-Israel incitement;
:(6) publicly pledged to not engage in war with Israel; and
:(7) honored previous diplomatic agreements. 

=Sec. 3. Effective date=

The prohibition under section 2 shall take effect on the later of—
:(1) June 1, 2014; or
:(2) the date of the enactment of this Act.
