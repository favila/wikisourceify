{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide for the issuance of a Victory for Veterans stamp, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Victory for Veterans Stamp Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Victory for Veterans stamp=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/ Title 39, United States Code], is amended by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/414 section 414]the following:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 414a. Victory for Veterans stamp==


:(a) In order to provide members of the public an opportunity to honor their veterans by supporting veterans’ employment programs, to reduce the deficit, and to preserve the vital role of the [[w:United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]], the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]shall issue a special stamp (referred to in this section as the“Victory for Veterans Stamp”) in accordance with the provisions of this section.
:(b) The Victory for Veterans Stamp—
::(1) shall be offered at a cost equal to $1; and
::(2) shall be valid for purposes of postage for first-class mail.
:(c) 
::(1) The amounts becoming available from the sale of the Victory for Veterans Stamp shall be used as follows:
:::(A) One-third of such amounts shall be transferred to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]for purposes of funding vocational rehabilitation programs for veterans underchapter 31 of title 38.
:::(B) One-third of such amounts shall be transferred to the general fund of the Treasury for purposes of deficit reduction.
:::(C) One-third of such amounts shall be used by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]to satisfy obligations incurred under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/2005 section 2005].
::(2) Amounts transferred under this subsection to an agency under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/414a paragraph (1)(A)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/414a (1)(B)]shall be made under such arrangements as the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]shall by mutual agreement with such agency establish in order to carry out the purposes of this section.
::(3) For purposes of this section, the term“amounts becoming available from the sale of the Victory for Veterans Stamp”means—
:::(A) the total amounts received by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]that it would not have received but for the enactment of this section, reduced by
:::(B) an amount sufficient to cover reasonable costs incurred by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]in carrying out this section, including those attributable to the printing, sale, and distribution of the Victory for Veterans Stamp under this section, as determined by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]under regulations that it shall prescribe.
:(d) Amounts transferred under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/414a subsection (c)(1)(A)]to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]shall not be taken into account in any decision relating to the level of appropriations or other Federal funding to be furnished in any year to the Department.
:(e) The Victory for Veterans Stamp shall be made available to the public beginning on such date as the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]]shall by regulation prescribe, but in no event later than 6 months after the date of the enactment of this section.
:(f) The [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]]shall include in each report rendered under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/2402 section 2402]information concerning the operation of this section. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Conforming amendment– ==

The table of sections for[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/ title 39, United States Code], is amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/414 section 414]the following:

<blockquote>  414a. Victory for Veterans stamp.  . 
</blockquote>
