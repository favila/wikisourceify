{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 28, United States Code, to exempt election
		  officials from Federal jury service.


{{section|HA27F7F19379C4B318A1589F342C952D2|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Uninterrupted Elections
			 Act

        
.

{{section|H9A582515AD53451C9A1554FC27229112|2. Exemption of election officials from Federal jury duty}}
Section 1863 of title 28, United States
			 Code
, is amended by adding at the end the following:
(e)
(1)
Each plan under this
				section shall provide that a election or registration official, if named as a
				prospective juror, is exempt from starting service as a juror during any
				election period.

(2)

(A)
the term election or registration
				official
 means an appointed or elected local election administrator, or
				a permanent full-time employee of such an administrator, whose duties include
				the registration of voters or the administration of elections; and

(B)
the term election period

				means the period beginning 60 days before and ending 30 days after an election
				with respect to which the election or registration official has any of the
				duties described in subparagraph
				(A)
.
