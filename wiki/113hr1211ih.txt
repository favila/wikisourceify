{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 552] of title 5, United States Code (commonly known as the Freedom of Information Act), to provide for greater public access to information, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HA3AC421398C847F298168EA8C5AA9292}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“FOIA Oversight and Implementation Act of 2013”or the“FOIA Act”.

===={{anchor|H55487091D7A741D5B1124E111636AEB2}}(h) The Office of Government Information Services– ====



====={{anchor|HCDDA6D6EE88D4348B1A33CAA23B6CD88}}(1) Establishment– =====

There is established the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of Government Information Services]] within the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|National Archives and Records Administration]] . The head of the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office]] is the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Director of the Office of Government Information Services]] .

====={{anchor|H11A04F53851B4B8197C85D7E647B4805}}(2) Review of FOIA policy, procedure, and compliance– =====

The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of Government Information Services]] shall—
:(A) review policies and procedures of agencies under this section;
:(B) review compliance with this section by agencies; and
:(C) identify methods that improve compliance under this section that may include—
::(i) the timely processing of requests submitted to agencies under this section;
::(ii) the system for assessing fees and fee waivers under this section; and
::(iii) the use of any exemption under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 subsection (b)] ; and
:(D) review and provide guidance to agencies on the use of fees and fee waivers.

====={{anchor|H03A9A081062B4A03A14F70316E23BF54}}(3) Mediation services– =====

The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of Government Information Services]] shall offer mediation services to resolve disputes between persons making requests under this section and agencies as a non-exclusive alternative to litigation and, at the discretion of the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office]] , may issue advisory opinions if mediation has not resolved the dispute.

:(ii) The [[w:United States Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs|Committees on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs and the Judiciary of the Senate]] .

====={{anchor|HAC0C3653EC784899B68795748B97CA3D}}(5) Submission of additional information– =====

The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Director of the Office of Government Information Services]] may submit additional information to Congress and the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] that the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Director]] determines to be appropriate.

====={{anchor|HCC4BED0A85964DF1B068295617910E18}}(6) Annual meeting required– =====

Not less than once a year, the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of Government Information Services]] shall hold a meeting that is open to the public on the review and reports by the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office]] and permit interested persons to appear and present oral or written statements at such meeting..
</blockquote>

::(i) inform the public of the operations and activities of the Government; and

=={{anchor|H5B6FE3C1B9AC4A4BB48BAF4A8EA8091F}}(h) Chief FOIA Officer responsibilities; Council; review– ==

===={{anchor|HA18E9C958BF04D5DBD1740A679FFF5E7}}(j) Chief FOIA Officer– ====



====={{anchor|H3C8E1025244240A3AD152731C1C1D2C8}}(1) Designation– =====

Each agency shall designate a Chief FOIA Officer who shall be a senior official of such agency (at the Assistant Secretary or equivalent level).

====={{anchor|H0DC5588E4F7641708C3727076A855C89}}(2) Duties– =====

The Chief FOIA Officer of each agency shall, subject to the authority of the head of the agency—
:(A) have agency-wide responsibility for efficient and appropriate compliance with this section;
:(B) monitor implementation of this section throughout the agency and keep the head of the agency, the chief legal officer of the agency, and the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] appropriately informed of the agency’s performance in implementing this section;
:(C) recommend to the head of the agency such adjustments to agency practices, policies, personnel, and funding as may be necessary to improve its implementation of this section;
:(D) review and report to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] , through the head of the agency, at such times and in such formats as the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may direct, on the agency’s performance in implementing this section;
:(E) facilitate public understanding of the purposes of the statutory exemptions of this section by including concise descriptions of the exemptions in both the agency’s handbook issued under subsection (g), and the agency’s annual report on this section, and by providing an overview, where appropriate, of certain general categories of agency records to which those exemptions apply; and
:(F) designate one or more FOIA Public Liaisons.

====={{anchor|HEEB277B8A7FC4D67920A46C8611F61AE}}(3) Compliance review required– =====

The Chief FOIA Officer of each agency shall—
:(A) review, not less than annually, all aspects of the agency’s administration of this section to ensure compliance with the requirements of this section, including—
::(i) agency regulations;
::(ii) disclosure of records required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 paragraphs (2)] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 (8)] , and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 (9) of subsection (a)] ;
::(iii) assessment of fees and determination of eligibility for fee waivers;
::(iv) the timely processing of requests for information under this section;
::(v) the use of exemptions under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 subsection (b)] ; and
::(vi) dispute resolution services with the assistance of the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of Government Information Services]] or the FOIA Public Liaison; and
:(B) make recommendations as necessary to improve agency practices and compliance with this section.

===={{anchor|H4966E2D83A6A4BBAB41A971ECCA3ADF5}}(k) Chief FOIA Officers Council– ====



====={{anchor|H90E18103C68946828DFD5A3E852B3E4E}}(1) Establishment– =====

There is established in the executive branch the [[w:|Chief FOIA Officers Council]] (in this subsection, referred to as the Council ).

====={{anchor|H5CBFF363C49B4EF59B6C126C9030842F}}(2) Members– =====

The [[w:|Council]] shall consist of the following members:
:(A) The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Deputy Director for Management of the Office of Management and Budget]] .
:(B) The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Director of the Office of Information Policy at the Department of Justice]] .
:(C) The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Director of the Office of Government Information Services at the National Archives and Records Administration]] .
:(D) The Chief FOIA Officer of each agency.
:(E) Any other officer or employee of the United States as designated by the Co-Chairs.

====={{anchor|H37109A66DD0C4A4FBD5548E98B04CC53}}(3) Co-Chairs– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Director of the Office of Information Policy at the Department of Justice]] and the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Director of the Office of Government Information Services at the National Archives and Records Administration]] shall be the Co-Chairs of the Council.

====={{anchor|H1894C85E922748B1A17DF89076A61EAA}}(4) Support services– =====

The [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] shall provide administrative and other support for the [[w:|Council]] .

====={{anchor|HA7640064253447AEA4B712205B27C906}}(5) Consultation– =====

In performing its duties, the [[w:|Council]] shall consult regularly with members of the public who make requests under this section.

====={{anchor|H87BAB5AD07A249289DD938E137DCA050}}(6) Duties– =====

The duties of the [[w:|Council]] include the following:
:(A) Develop recommendations for increasing compliance and efficiency under this section.
:(B) Disseminate information about agency experiences, ideas, best practices, and innovative approaches related to this section.
:(C) Identify, develop, and coordinate initiatives to increase transparency and compliance with this section.
:(D) Promote the development and use of common performance measures for agency compliance with this section.

====={{anchor|H9FDE4DB85510455BA5E3600F3548DB07}}(7) Meetings– =====



======{{anchor|HF84C026A321248AEB6FD3A88F3B68120}}(A) Regular meetings– ======

The [[w:|Council]] shall meet regularly and such meetings shall be open to the public unless the [[w:|Council]] determines to close the meeting for reasons of national security or to discuss information exempt under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 subsection (b)] .

======{{anchor|H5593C869998B427D9B0722271F9E868D}}(B) Annual meetings– ======

Not less than once a year, the [[w:|Council]] shall hold a meeting that shall be open to the public and permit interested persons to appear and present oral and written statements to the [[w:|Council]] .

======{{anchor|HC88E20DA3BD346E580F91EDE4C88F065}}(C) Notice– ======

Not later than 10 business days before a meeting of the [[w:|Council]] , notice of such meeting shall be published in the Federal Register.

======{{anchor|HED083DDB9D524615B45DDE7491F3C4EE}}(D) Public availability of Council records– ======

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 subsection (b)] , the records, reports, transcripts, minutes, appendixes, working papers, drafts, studies, agenda, or other documents that were made available to or prepared for or by the Council shall be made publicly available.

======{{anchor|HA38FAE062BA04ECA8FD1AA2FA0505448}}(E) Minutes– ======

Detailed minutes of each meeting of the [[w:|Council]] shall be kept and shall contain a record of the persons present, a complete and accurate description of matters discussed and conclusions reached, and copies of all reports received, issued, or approved by the [[w:|Council]] ..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HAA763EFB9EEF4C72A1B647B8B087813B}}(i) Regulations– ==



==={{anchor|H2CA1EA7C1741485AA26B48D88182FFFE}}(1) Revision of regulations– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the head of each agency shall review the regulations of such agency and shall issue regulations on procedures for the disclosure of records under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 552] of title 5, United States Code] , in accordance with the amendments made by this section. The regulations of each agency shall include—
:(A) procedures for engaging in dispute resolution; and
:(B) procedures for engaging with the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of Government Information Services]] .

==={{anchor|H42E58D0982794A46805B93F8AFDD6C01}}(2) Office of Government Information Services report– ===

Not later than 270 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of Government Information Services]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on agency compliance with the requirements of this subsection.

==={{anchor|HBDD8C370ADBD4C02A79665E6C337A357}}(3) Report on noncompliance– ===

The head of any agency that does not meet the requirements of paragraph (1)shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the reason for noncompliance not later than 270 days after the date of the enactment of this Act.

==={{anchor|H9385D44F55784CAC89E0DF5B663E9482}}(4) Inspector General review for noncompliance– ===

Any agency that fails to comply with the requirements of this subsection shall be reviewed by the Office of Inspector General of such agency for compliance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 552] of title 5, United States Code.

==={{anchor|HD8E8BC762ADF4D389F3DA4DBC2D08712}}(5) Agency defined– ===

In this section, the term agency has the meaning given such term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 552(f)] of title 5, United States Code] .

={{anchor|H398A97A9C7724698A660757C0C767BF3}}Sec. 3. Pilot program=



=={{anchor|H24908360DEAF4633A5FA534EFB0A3E05}}(a) Establishment– ==

The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall establish a pilot program for 3 years to review the benefits of FOIAonline to process requests and release information under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 552] of title 5, United States Code (commonly known as the Freedom of Information Act).

=={{anchor|HA7B0AA1F732B42CFBB66108CFE5D8664}}(b) Plan required– ==

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall establish a plan to evaluate FOIAonline at no less than 3 agencies that have not previously participated in FOIAonline, including at least one of the following:
:(1) An agency that receives more than 50,000 requests annually for information under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 552] of title 5, United States Code.
:(2) An agency that receives between 25,000 and 50,000 requests annually for information under such section.
:(3) An agency that receives 25,000 or fewer requests annually for information under such section.

=={{anchor|HA77EF3C4CF444A99820B256E8E56B992}}(c) Agency use of Web site– ==

Each agency selected under subsection (b)shall use FOIAonline to—
:(1) receive requests under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 section 552 of title 5, United States Code] ;
:(2) process requests received under such section;
:(3) track the status of requests submitted under such section; and
:(4) make records released available publicly on FOIAonline.

=={{anchor|H464E577E994A44BD9F6C771490A3F3F3}}(d) Review required– ==

The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]] shall, in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] , the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Office of Government Information Services]] , and the head of each agency participating in the pilot program, review the benefits of FOIAonline, including—
:(1) any cost saving, resource saving, or efficiency gained through the use of FOIAonline;
:(2) any change in the amount of requests received under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 552] of title 5, United States Code;
:(3) any increase in transparency and accessibility to Government information; and
:(4) any changes in the ability to access and compile information needed for agency annual reports required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552 section 552 of title 5, United States Code] .

=={{anchor|H004C0DCC5AD647CE8B8B8FD5840CB34C}}(e) Report required– ==

Not later than 3 months after the completion of the pilot program, the head of each agency participating in the program shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the impact of the pilot program on agency processes under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 552] of title 5, United States Code, whether the agency will continue to participate in FOIAonline, and any recommendations the head of the agency considers appropriate to continue the use of FOIAonline.
