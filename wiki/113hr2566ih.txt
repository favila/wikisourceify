{{Center|A BILL}}

 To modify the definition of armor piercing ammunition to better capture its capabilities.   

=Section 1. Armor piercing ammunition=



==(a) Expansion of definition– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 Section 921(a)(17)(B) of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 clause (I)], by striking“or”at the end;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 clause (ii)], by striking the period and inserting“; and”; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
::(iii) a projectile that—
:::(I) may be used in a handgun; and
:::(II) the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]determines, under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926 section 926(d)], to be capable of penetrating body armor. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Determination of capability of projectiles To penetrate body armor– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926 Section 926 of such title]is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(d) 
::(1) The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]], in consultation with [[w:|Director of Operational Test and Evaluation of the Department of Defense]], shall promulgate standards for the uniform testing of projectiles against the Body Armor Exemplar.
::(2) The standards promulgated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926 paragraph (1)]shall take into account, among other factors, variations in performance that are related to the type of handgun used, the length of the barrel of the handgun, the amount and kind of powder used to propel the projectile, and the design of the projectile.
::(3) In[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926 paragraph (1)], the term Body Armor Exemplar means body armor that the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]determines meets minimum standards for the protection of law enforcement officers. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Timing of promulgation of standards– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]shall promulgate the standards required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/926  section 926(d)of title 18, United States Code], within 1 year after the date of the enactment of this section.

===(3) Assessment and modification of standards– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]shall assess the standards every 3 years, or more frequently if the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]finds that technological advances warrant doing so, and shall modify the standards as appropriate.
