{{Center|A BILL}}

 To increase oversight of small business assistance programs provided by the Small Business Administration.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Transparency in Small Business Assistance Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Oversight of small business assistance programs=



==(a) Definitions– ==

In this Act—
:(1) the terms Administration and Administrator mean the [[w:Small Business Administration|Small Business Administration]]and the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]]thereof, respectively; and
:(2) the term small business concern has the same meaning as in  section 3 of the Small Business Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/632  15 U.S.C. 632 ]).

==(b) Oversight of small business assistance programs– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:Small Business Administration|Administrator]]shall collect information from small business concerns that receive assistance provided by the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]], including a loan, a loan guarantee, a grant, or counseling services.

===(2) Information to be collected– ===

The information collected under paragraph (1) shall include—
:(A) the operational status of each small business concern, to be collected 6 months after and 1 year after the date on which the small business concern receives assistance from the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]];
:(B) if a small business concern is no longer operational, the reason for the closure of the small business concern;
:(C) the number of part-time employees and full-time employees that each small business concern employs, to be collected—
::(i) 6 months after the date on which the small business concern receives assistance from the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]], and every 6 months thereafter, during the time the small business concern is receiving assistance from the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]]; and
::(ii) 6 months after and 1 year after the date on which the small business concern ceases to receive assistance from the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]]; and
:(D) if the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]]is unable to provide assistance to the small business concern, the number of referrals that the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]]makes to another assistance program within the [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]]or to another Federal agency.

===(3) Report– ===

The [[w:Small Business Administration|Administration]]shall include the information collected under this subsection in the annual report required under  section 10(a) of the Small Business Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/639 15 U.S.C. 639(a)]).

==(c) Effective date– ==

This Act shall take effect 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act.
