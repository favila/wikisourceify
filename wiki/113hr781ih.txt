{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title II of the Social Security Act to prohibit the inclusion of Social Security account numbers on Medicare cards.

={{anchor|H6244C3867E344DA5B0900354CD1566B6}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Medicare Identity Theft Prevention Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H938626F93F2F47259C69D6B998480342}}Sec. 2. Prohibition of inclusion of Social Security account numbers on Medicare cards=



=={{anchor|H340A779C08FB462BABDF1A6A9E3CDCDD}}(a) In general– ==

 Section 205(c)(2)(C) of the Social Security Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/405 42 U.S.C. 405(c)(2)(C)] )is amended—
:(1) by moving clause (x), as added by section 1414(a)(2) of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, 6 ems to the left;
:(2) by redesignating clause (x), as added by section 2(a)(1) of the Social Security Number Protection Act of 2010, and clause (xi)as clauses (xi)and (xii), respectively; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following new clause:

<blockquote>
::(xiii) The [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] , in consultation with the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner of Social Security]] , shall establish cost-effective procedures to ensure that a Social Security account number (or derivative thereof) is not displayed, coded, or embedded on the Medicare card issued to an individual who is entitled to benefits under part A of title XVIIIor enrolled under part B of title XVIIIand that any other identifier displayed on such card is not identifiable as a Social Security account number (or derivative thereof)..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HC516550ADF164D6881E792F3D57CAB84}}(b) Implementation– ==

In implementing clause (xiii) of section 205(c)(2)(C) of the Social Security Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/405 42 U.S.C. 405(c)(2)(C)] ), as added by subsection (a)(3), the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] shall establish a cost-effective process that involves the least amount of disruption to Medicare beneficiaries and health care providers. The [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] shall consider implementing a process, similar to the process involving Railroad Retirement Board beneficiaries, under which a Medicare beneficiary identifier which is not a Social Security account number (or derivative thereof) is used external to the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Department of Health and Human Services]] and is convertible over to a Social Security account number (or derivative thereof) for use internal to such [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Department]] and the [[w:Social Security Administration|Social Security Administration]] .

=={{anchor|HDE6BF49E0C1849A4B911A3254F8DCB62}}(c) Effective date– ==



==={{anchor|H09A8CB58ADDC4B20B24F0837B49DD4CE}}(1) In general– ===

 Clause (xiii) of section 205(c)(2)(C) of the Social Security Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/405 42 U.S.C. 405(c)(2)(C)] ), as added by subsection (a)(3), shall apply with respect to Medicare cards issued on and after an effective date specified by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] , but in no case shall such effective date be later than the date that is 3 years after the date of the enactment of this Act.

==={{anchor|HFBC13FDE270C4573B1C6CFF713670407}}(2) Reissuance– ===

The [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] —
:(A) shall provide for the reissuance of Medicare cards that comply with the requirements of such clause not later than 3 years after the effective date specified by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] under paragraph (1); and
:(B) may permit an individual to apply for the reissuance of a Medicare card that complies with such requirements before the date of reissuance otherwise provided under subparagraph (A)in such exceptional circumstances as the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]] may specify.
