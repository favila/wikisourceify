{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 18, United States Code, to require that the Director of the Bureau of Prisons ensure that each chief executive officer of a Federal penal or correctional institution provides a secure storage area located outside of the secure perimeter of the Federal penal or correctional institution for firearms carried by certain employees of the Bureau of Prisons, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Correctional Officer Self-Protection Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Findings=

  [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]finds that—
:(1) the  Law Enforcement Officers Safety Act of 2004 ( [[Public Law 108-277|Public Law 108–277]]  ;118 Stat. 865)gives certain law enforcement officers, including certain correctional officers of the [[w:Federal Bureau of Prisons|Bureau of Prisons]], the right to carry a concealed firearm in all 50 States for self-protection;
:(2) the purpose of that Act is to allow certain law enforcement officers to protect themselves while off duty;
:(3) correctional officers of the [[w:Federal Bureau of Prisons|Bureau of Prisons]]have been the targets of assaults and murders while off duty; and
:(4) while that Act allows certain law enforcement officers to protect themselves off duty, the [[w:Federal Bureau of Prisons|Director of the Bureau of Prisons]]allows correctional officers of the [[w:Federal Bureau of Prisons|Bureau of Prisons]]to securely store personal firearms at only 33 Federal penal and correctional institutions while at work. 

=Sec. 3. Secure firearms storage=



==(a) In general– ==

  Chapter 303of title 18, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 4049. Secure firearms storage==



===(a) Definitions– ===

In this section—
:(1) the term employee means a qualified law enforcement officer employed by the [[w:Federal Bureau of Prisons|Bureau of Prisons]]; and
:(2) the terms firearm and qualified law enforcement officer have the meanings given those terms undersection 926B.

===(b) Secure firearms storage– ===

The [[w:Federal Bureau of Prisons|Director of the Bureau of Prisons]]shall ensure that each chief executive officer of a Federal penal or correctional institution—
:(1) 
::(A) provides a secure storage area located outside of the secure perimeter of the institution for employees to store firearms; or
::(B) allows employees to store firearms in a vehicle lockbox approved by the [[w:Federal Bureau of Prisons|Director of the Bureau of Prisons]]; and
:(2) notwithstanding any other provision of law (including regulations), allows employees to carry concealed firearms on the premises outside of the secure perimeter of the institution. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Technical and conforming amendment– ==

The table of sections forchapter 303 of title 18, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>  4049. Secure firearms storage.  . 
</blockquote>
