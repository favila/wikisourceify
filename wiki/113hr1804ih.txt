{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to direct the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to submit to Congress semiannual reports on the cost of foreign travel made by employees of the Department of Veterans Affairs.

={{anchor|H73E90321C1814DFDAC85EFFC7B763185}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Foreign Travel Accountability Act”.

={{anchor|H0721D62407A54666B54C425994F36527}}Sec. 2. Semiannual reports to Congress on cost of foreign travel=



=={{anchor|H40FA2C2A127643069F858D3449DE5056}}(a) In general– ==

Subchapter I ofchapter 5of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HAF2AEC2ABD0A4BC4B8D112168F141996}}Sec. 518. Semiannual reports to Congress on cost of foreign travel==



==={{anchor|H279F1645DB7A4B6FAA3C199907644ECD}}(a) Semiannual reports– ===

Not later than June 30, 2014, and each 180-day period thereafter, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committee on Veterans' Affairs of the Senate]] a semiannual report on covered foreign travel made during the 180-day period covered by the report.

==={{anchor|HBECCF712DEAC411096542DB02419A8D0}}(b) Matters included– ===

Each report under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/518 subsection (a)] shall include the following:
:(1) With respect to each instance of covered foreign travel made during the period covered by the report—
::(A) the purpose of such travel;
::(B) the destination;
::(C) the name and title of each employee included on such travel;
::(D) the duration of such travel; and
::(E) the total cost to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] of such travel.
:(2) The final costs to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] with respect to all covered foreign travel made during the period covered by the report, including costs relating to—
::(A) transportation, including fares for travel by air, rail, bus, ferry, cruise ship, taxi, mass transit, or other mode of transportation;
::(B) expenses or reimbursements relating to operating and maintaining a car, including the costs of fuel and mileage;
::(C) passport and visa fees;
::(D) lodging;
::(E) per diem payments;
::(F) baggage charges;
::(G) computer rental fees;
::(H) rental of halls, auditoriums, or other spaces;
::(I) entertainment; and
::(J) contractors.

==={{anchor|H524682162E0D469BBC3BA8BBDF068134}}(c) Duplicative information– ===

Each report under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/518 subsection (a)] shall include the information described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/518 subsection (b)] regardless of whether such information is also included in a report under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/517 section 517 of this title] .

==={{anchor|HC72FDF3F0AEE4D49AD429ADC3308BABD}}(d) Covered foreign travel defined– ===

In this section, the term covered foreign travel means travel made by an employee of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]] , including an employee who is stationed in a foreign country, on official business to a location outside of—
:(1) the several States;
:(2) the District of Columbia;
:(3) a territory, commonwealth, or possession of the United States;
:(4) Indian lands (as defined in section 4(4) of the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/2703 25 U.S.C. 2703(4)] ] )); or
:(5) the territorial waters of the United States..
</blockquote>
