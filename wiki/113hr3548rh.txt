{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title XII of the Public Health Service Act to expand the definition of trauma to include thermal, electrical, chemical, radioactive, and other extrinsic agents.    

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Improving Trauma Care Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Trauma definition=

Paragraph (4) of section 1231 of the Public Health Service Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/300d-31 42 U.S.C. 300d–31]) is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

==(4) Trauma– ==

The term trauma means an injury resulting from exposure to—
:(A) a mechanical force; or
:(B) another extrinsic agent, including an extrinsic agent that is thermal, electrical, chemical, or radioactive. . 
</blockquote>
   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Improving Trauma Care Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Trauma definition=



==(a) Revised definition under trauma systems grants programs– ==

   Paragraph (4) of section 1231 of the Public Health Service Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/300d–31 42 U.S.C. 300d–31] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

===(4) Trauma– ===

The term trauma means an injury resulting from exposure to—
:(A) a mechanical force; or
:(B) another extrinsic agent, including an extrinsic agent that is thermal, electrical, chemical, or radioactive. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Revised definition under interagency program for trauma research– ==

   Paragraph (3) of section 1261(h) of the Public Health Service Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/300d–61 42 U.S.C. 300d–61(h)] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 
:(3) The term“trauma”means an injury resulting from exposure to—
::(A) a mechanical force; or
::(B) another extrinsic agent, including an extrinsic agent that is thermal, electrical, chemical, or radioactive. . 
</blockquote>
