{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 18, United States Code, to provide increased protections for consumer or subscriber password information, and to amend the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978 to provide that the Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation may not access password information pursuant to an order under section 501 of that Act, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Reasonable Expectation of American Privacy Act of 2013 ”or the“ REAP Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Increased protections relating to voluntary disclosure of password information=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702  Section 2702of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 subsection (a)]—
::(A) in the matter preceding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 paragraph (1)], by striking“subsection (b) or (c)”and inserting“subsection (b), (c), or (d)”;
::(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 paragraph (2)], by striking“and”at the end;
::(C) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 paragraph (3)]—
:::(i) by inserting after“(not including the contents of communications covered by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 paragraph (1)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 (2)] ”the following:“, or the password information covered by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 paragraph (4)] ”; and
:::(ii) by striking the period at the end and inserting“; and”; and
::(D) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 paragraph (3)]the following:

<blockquote> 
:::(4) a person or entity providing an electronic communication service or a remote computing service to the public shall not knowingly divulge to any person or entity the password information pertaining to a subscriber or customer of such service. ; 
</blockquote>

:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 subsection (c)], by inserting after“(not including the contents of communications covered by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 subsection (a)(1)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 (2)] ”the following:“, or the password information covered by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 subsection (a)(4)] ”;
:(3) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 subsection (d)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 subsection (e)];
:(4) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 subsection (c)]the following:

<blockquote> 

===(d) Exceptions for Disclosure of Password Information– ===

A provider described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 subsection (a)]may divulge the password information pertaining to a subscriber or customer of such service—
:(1) as otherwise authorized in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2703 2703 of this title];
:(2) with the lawful consent of the subscriber or customer;
:(3) as may be necessarily incident to the rendition of the service or to the protection of the rights or property of the provider of that service;
:(4) to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children, in connection with a report submitted thereto under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2258A section 2258A]; or
:(5) to a governmental entity, if the provider, in good faith, believes that an emergency involving danger of death or serious physical injury to any person requires disclosure without delay of password information relating to the emergency. ; and 
</blockquote>

:(5) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 subsection (e)], as so redesignated, by inserting after“subsection (b)(8)”each place it appears, the following:“or (d)(5)”. 

=Sec. 3. Increased protections relating to required disclosure of customer communications and password information=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2703  Section 2703of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2703 subsection (a)]—
::(A) in the first sentence, by inserting after“A governmental entity may require the disclosure by a provider of electronic communication service”the following:“or remote computing service”;
::(B) by striking“in an electronic communications system for one hundred and eighty days or less”; and
::(C) by striking the second sentence;
:(2) by amending[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2703 subsection (b)]to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

====(b) Password information– ====

A governmental entity may require a provider of electronic communication service or remote computing service to disclose the password information pertaining to a subscriber or customer of such service only pursuant to a warrant issued using the procedures described in the Federal Rules of Criminal Procedure (or in the case of a State court, issued using State warrant procedures) by a court of competent jurisdiction. ; 
</blockquote>

:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2703 subsection (c)(1)], by inserting after“(not including the contents of communications”the following:“or the password information pertaining to a subscriber or customer of such service”; and
:(4) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2703 subsection (d)], by striking“subsection (b) or (c)”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2703 subsection (c)] ”.

==(b) Conforming amendments– ==

  Chapter 121of title 18, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2701 section 2701(c)(3)], by striking“, 2704,”;
:(2) by repealing[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2704 sections 2704]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2705 2705]; and
:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2706 section 2706], by striking“section 2702, 2703, or 2704”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2702 section 2702]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2703 2703] ”. 

=Sec. 4. Password information excluded from transactional records available pursuant to a counterintelligence request=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2709  Section 2709of title 18, United States Code], is amended by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2709 subsection (f)], the following:

<blockquote> 

==(g) Password information– ==

A request made pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2709 subsection (b)]for electronic communication transactional records may not include a request for the password information pertaining to a subscriber or customer of a provider of electronic communication service or remote computing service. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 5. Password information excluded from tangible things required to be produced through a FISA court order=

   Section 501(a)(1) of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act of 1978 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/50/1861 50 U.S.C. 1861(a)(1)] )is amended by inserting after“tangible things (including books, records, papers, documents, and other items”the following:“, but not including password information pertaining to a customer or subscriber of a provider of electronic communication service or remote computing service (as such terms are defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2510 section 2510]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2711 2711 of title 18, United States Code], respectively)”.
