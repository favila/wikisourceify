{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to clarify that the estate of a deceased veteran may receive certain accrued benefits upon the death of the veteran, to ensure that substituted claims are processed timely, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Veteran Families Fairness Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Clarification of eligible recipients of certain accrued benefits upon death of beneficiary=



==(a) Treatment of estate– ==



===(1) Eligible recipient– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121  Section 5121(a)(2)of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(A) in the matter preceding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121 subparagraph (A)], by inserting“, or estate,”after“person”; and
:(B) by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote> 
::(D) The estate of the veteran (unless the estate will escheat). . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Substitution in case of death of claimant– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121A Section 5121A(a)(1) of such title]is amended—
:(A) by inserting“, or estate,”after“a living person”; and
:(B) by adding at the end the following new sentence:“The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall process a claim of such a substituted claimant by recognizing the original filing date of the claim and without penalty to the priority of the claim.”.

===(3) Outreach– ===


:(A) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall identify deceased veterans whom the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines meet the following criteria:
::(i) The veteran died before—
:::(I) the date of the enactment of this Act; and
:::(II) being able to receive periodic monetary benefits under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]to which the veteran was entitled or had filed a claim to receive.
::(ii) Such benefits were not paid to an individual described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121  section 5121of title 38, United States Code], or such claim was not substituted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121A section 5121A of such title].
::(iii) If the amendments made by paragraphs (1) and (2) were in effect on the date of the death of the individual, such benefits could have been paid to the estate of the veteran under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121  section 5121of title 38, United States Code], or such claim could have been substituted by the estate under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121A section 5121A of such title].
:(B) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall notify the estate of each veteran identified under subparagraph (A) of the ability of the estate to apply for payments under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121 section 5121 of title 38, United States Code], as amended by paragraph (1) or apply to act as a substituted claimant under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121A section 5121A of such title], as amended by paragraph (2) . In determining such applications, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall substitute“the date on which the estate was notified of the eligibility of the estate to apply under this section”for—
::(i)  “the date of death”in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121 subsection (c) of such section 5121]; and
::(ii)  “the date of the death of such claimant”in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121A subsection (a) of such section 5121A].

===(4) Application– ===

In accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121A paragraph (3)], the amendments made by subsection (a) and (b) shall apply with respect to the death of an individual regardless of the date of such death.

==(b) Reimbursements– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall—
:(1) reimburse a claimant who is awarded a claim for compensation under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/ title 38, United States Code], including an individual or estate under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121 section 5121]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5121A section 5121A of such title], as amended by this section, for costs that—
::(A) relate to the medical treatment of the claimant;
::(B) were charged to the claimant during the period beginning on the date on which the claim was filed and ending on the date of such award; and
::(C) would have been paid by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]], or such treatment would have been provided by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]], if such treatment occurred after the date of such award; and
:(2) recommend to each claimant, upon the filing of the claim, to retain receipts and other documentation relating to costs described in paragraph (1) .
