{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 10, United States Code, to authorize long-term contracts for the procurement of certain liquid transportation fuels for the Department of Defense.

={{anchor|idB66DC308E3C14B64BFF639679CC8073A}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Freedom Fuels Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|idA5FA66D91B8C41EA8E597ECDFB4AC8EA}}Sec. 2. Authority for long-term contracts for procurement of certain liquid transportation fuels for the Department of Defense=



=={{anchor|id6C71B130C5FE47B89E000B6EF0F20E31}}(a) Authority– ==

Subchapter II of chapter 173 of title 10, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|id23E8C04BCBF740679EA90DEA9F3B53DD}}Sec. 2922h. Liquid fuels: contracts for procurement of certain transportation fuels==



==={{anchor|idD1B35F8422B443A5A1E2245899ACB6FB}}(a) Authority To contract– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] may enter into one or more contracts for the procurement of fuels described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2922h subsection (b)] for the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] .

==={{anchor|idA9DD4A6C60994AECA681F97B3ECA8A9E}}(b) Covered fuels– ===

A fuel described in this subsection is a liquid transportation fuel, including jet fuel, that—
:(1) meets the requirements of section 526 of the Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/17142 42 U.S.C. 17142] );
:(2) uses a feedstock that is produced within a State; and
:(3) is refined, manufactured, or produced in a State.

==={{anchor|id8B3F310E8D0C43D6985F1BE027840651}}(c) Period of contract– ===

The period of a contract entered into under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2922h subsection (a)] may not exceed 10 years.

==={{anchor|id3D97930F2FE247F78832DE2B4EFF5E8F}}(d) Prohibition on options– ===

A contract under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2922h subsection (a)] may not include any options that would extend the overall period of the contract beyond 10 years.

==={{anchor|id978B281184CA49C08DD0ED82878AF3BC}}(e) Reports on contracts– ===

Not later than three years after entering into a contract under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2922h subsection (a)] , the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the contract. The report shall include the following:
:(1) A description of the performance of the contract, including an assessment whether the contract is yielding savings to the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department]] when compared with the procurement of fuels that meet the requirements referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2922h subsection (b)] but are not procured under the authority in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2922h subsection (a)] .
:(2) In consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]] , an assessment whether the contract has stimulated the development of domestic energy infrastructure, and an assessment whether lengthening the maximum authorized period of contract in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2922h subsection (c)] would further stimulate the development of domestic energy infrastructure.
:(3) An assessment whether the expanded use of contracts as authorized by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2922h subsection (a)] have reduced, or can reduce, the vulnerability of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department]] to disruptions in the global oil supply, and an assessment whether lengthening the maximum authorized period of contract in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/2922h subsection (c)] , could further reduce the vulnerability of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department]] to such disruptions.

==={{anchor|idCF40CF2D6D4C4DDCA7373342A7A07BB9}}(f) State defined– ===

In this section, the term State —
:(1) means each of the several States; and
:(2) includes any land under the jurisdiction of an Indian tribe located in any of the several States..
</blockquote>
