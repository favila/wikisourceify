{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 5, United States Code, to limit the number of local wage areas allowable within a General Schedule pay locality.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Locality Pay Equity Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Limiting the number of local wage areas defined within a General Schedule pay locality=



==(a) Local wage area limitation– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5343 Section 5343(a) of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5343 paragraph (1)(B)(i)], by striking“(but such”and all that follows through“are employed)”;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5343 paragraph (4)], by striking“and”after the semicolon;
:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5343 paragraph (5)], by striking the period after“Islands”and inserting“; and”; and
:(4) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
::(6) the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] shall define not more than 1 local wage area within a pay locality, except that this paragraph shall not apply to the pay locality designated as“Rest of United States”. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Pay locality defined– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5342  Section 5342(a)of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5342 paragraph (2)(C)], by striking“and”after the semicolon;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5342 paragraph (3)], by striking the period after“employee”and inserting“; and”; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
::(4)   pay locality has the same meaning as is given such term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5302 section 5302(5)]. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 3. Regulations=

The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of Personnel Management]] shall prescribe any regulations necessary to carry out the purpose of this Act, including regulations to ensure that the enactment of this Act shall not have the effect of reducing any rate of basic pay payable to any individual who is serving as a prevailing rate employee (as defined by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5342 section 5342(a)(2) of title 5, United States Code]). 

=Sec. 4. Effective date=

This Act and the amendments made by this Act shall take effect at the start of the first full pay period beginning at least 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act.
