To require the Inspector General of the Environmental Protection Agency to include certain assessments in reports.

=Section 1. Inclusions in EPA Inspector General reports=

Notwithstanding any other provision of law, in the reports required to be submitted by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Inspector General of the Environmental Protection Agency]] not later than April 30 and October 31 of each calendar year, the [[w:Office of the Inspector General|Inspector General]] shall include an assessment of whether the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] achieved compliance during the period covered by the report with the requirements ofâ€”
:(1) 
::(A) the portions of section 4 of Executive Order 12866 (58 Fed. Reg. 51735 (September 30, 1993)) and Executive Order 13563 (76 Fed. Reg. 3821 (January 18, 2011)) that are applicable to the uniform reporting of regulatory and deregulatory agendas; and
::(B) any successor Executive orders establishing requirements applicable to the uniform reporting of regulatory and deregulatory agendas;
:(2)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/602 section 602 of title 5, United States Code] ;
:(3) the portions of section 202 of the Unfunded Mandates Reform Act of 1995( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/1532 2 U.S.C. 1532] )that require agencies to publish certain written statements before promulgating any general notice of proposed rulemaking; and
:(4) section 8 of Executive Order 13132 (64 Fed. Reg. 43258 (August 4, 1999)).
