{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to direct the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to report cases of reportable infectious diseases at medical facilities of the Department of Veterans Affairs in accordance with State law.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Infectious Disease Reporting Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Report of infectious disease at medical facilities of Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7311 Section 7311 of title 38, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 
:(f) 
::(1) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall report to the appropriate entity each case of a reportable infectious disease that occurs at a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]in accordance with the laws of the State in which the facility is located.
::(2) In addition to reporting each case of a reportable infectious disease at a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7311 paragraph (1)], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall report each such case to the accrediting organization of such facility.
::(3) 
:::(A) If the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]fails to report a case of a reportable infectious disease at a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]in accordance with State law pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7311 paragraph (1)], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall pay to the State an amount equal to the amount that a medical facility not owned by the Federal Government that is located in the same State would pay as a penalty to such State for such failure.
:::(B) The State may file a civil action against the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]in the United States district court for the district in which the medical facility is located to recover from the United States the amount described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7311 subparagraph (A)].
::(4) In this subsection, the term reportable infectious disease means, with respect to a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]], a disease that the State in which the facility is located requires to be reported. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Effective date– ==

The reporting requirement under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7311  section 7311(f)of title 38, United States Code], as added by subsection (a) , shall apply with respect to a case of reportable infectious disease occurring at a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]on or after the date that is 60 days after the date of the enactment of this Act.
