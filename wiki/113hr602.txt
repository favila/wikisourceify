{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to clarify the conditions under which certain persons may be treated as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes.

={{anchor|H4CB7853416D44594A391FC1289D36569}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Veterans 2nd Amendment Protection Act”.

={{anchor|HC50B463A1273475A80C2343B53D08F4D}}Sec. 2. Conditions for treatment of certain persons as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes=



=={{anchor|H0FC74E269C5E43DA86D7F8D5AB1F0E97}}(a) In general– ==

Chapter 55 of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HDFA33A57AF304AA5B0E5A2568B1F0C5E}}Sec. 5511. Conditions for treatment of certain persons as adjudicated mentally incompetent for certain purposes==

In any case arising out of the administration by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] of laws and benefits under this title, a person who is mentally incapacitated, deemed mentally incompetent, or experiencing an extended loss of consciousness shall not be considered adjudicated as a mental defective under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/922/18 subsection (d)(4)] or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (g)(4) of section 922 of title 18] without the order or finding of a judge, magistrate, or other judicial authority of competent jurisdiction that such person is a danger to himself or herself or others..
</blockquote>
