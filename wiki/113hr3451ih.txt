{{Center|A BILL}}

To require the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to establish a veterans conservation corps, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Veterans Conservation Corps Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Veterans Conservation Corps=



==(a) Establishment– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall, in cooperation with the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] , the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] , the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] , the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] , the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] , the [[w:|Chief Executive Officer of the Corporation for National and Community Service]] , and the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] , establish a veterans conservation corps to assist veterans in the transition from service in the Armed Forces to civilian life and to employ veterans—
:(A) in conservation, resource management, firefighting, law enforcement, and historic preservation projects on public lands and maintenance and improvement projects for cemeteries under the jurisdiction of the [[w:National Cemetery Administration|National Cemetery Administration]] ; and
:(B) as firefighters, law enforcement officers, and disaster relief personnel.

===(2) Veteran eligibility– ===

To be eligible to participate in the veterans conservation corps, a veteran shall be unemployed.

==(b) Conservation, resource management, historic preservation, and cemetery maintenance and improvement projects– ==



===(1) In general– ===

As part of the veterans conservation corps, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] , the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] , the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] , the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] , the [[w:|Chief Executive Officer of the Corporation for National and Community Service]] , and the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] shall—
:(A) employ veterans to carry out projects described in subsection (a)(1); and
:(B) award grants to, or enter into contracts with, State governments, local governments, or nongovernmental entities to employ veterans to carry out projects described in subsection (a)(1).

===(2) Priority– ===

In employing or awarding grants or contracts to employ veterans under this subsection, the Secretaries referred to in paragraph (1)and the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] shall give priority towards the employment of veterans who served on active duty in the Armed Forces on or after September 11, 2001.

===(3) Coordination– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall coordinate the activities of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] , the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] , the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] , the [[w:|Chief Executive Officer of the Corporation for National and Community Service]] , and the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] to employ veterans as part of the veterans conservation corps.

===(4) Oversight of projects– ===

The Secretaries referred to in paragraph (1)and the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] shall each provide oversight of the projects for which they employ veterans under subparagraph (A) of such paragraphor award grants or enter into contracts under subparagraph (B) of such paragraph.

==(c) First responders– ==



===(1) Firefighters– ===

As part of the veterans conservation corps, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] shall award grants under section 34 of the Federal Fire Prevention and Control Act of 1974( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/2229a 15 U.S.C. 2229a] )to hire veterans as firefighters.

===(2) Law enforcement officers– ===

As part of the veterans conservation corps, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall award grants under part Q of title I of the Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act of 1968( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/3796dd 42 U.S.C. 3796dd et seq.] )to hire veterans as law enforcement officers.

===(3) Disaster relief personnel– ===

As part of the veterans conservation corps, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] shall provide funds to increase participation by veterans in the FEMA Corps program, which is a partnership between the [[w:|Corporation for National and Community Service]] and the [[w:Federal Emergency Management Agency|Federal Emergency Management Agency]] .

===(4) Priority– ===

In awarding grants or providing funds under this subsection to hire veterans, the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] and the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall give priority to the hiring of veterans who served on active duty in the Armed Forces on or after September 11, 2001.

==(d) Assistance– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] may provide assistance to the Secretaries referred to in subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] , the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] , and the [[w:|Chief Executive Officer]] to carry out the veterans conservation corps. Such assistance may take the form of a transfer under paragraph (2).

===(2) Transfers– ===

Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, of amounts appropriated or otherwise made available to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] to carry out this section, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] may transfer such amounts as the Secretary considers appropriate to carry out the veterans conservation corps to the following:
:(A) The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] .
:(B) The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] .
:(C) The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] .
:(D) The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] .
:(E) The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] .
:(F) The [[w:|Corporation for National and Community Service]] .
:(G) The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] .

===(3) Assistance for conservation, resource management, historic preservation, and cemetery maintenance and improvement projects– ===



====(A) Application– ====

If a Secretary referred to in subsection (b)(1)or the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] or the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief Executive Officer]] seeks assistance under paragraph (1)to employ a veteran to carry out a project under subparagraph (A) of subsection (b)(1)or to award a grant or contract to carry out a project under subparagraph (B) of such subsection, such Secretary or the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] an application therefor at such time, in such manner, and containing such information as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] may require.

====(B) Selection– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall, in consultation with the steering committee established under subparagraph (C), award assistance under this paragraph in accordance with such criteria as the steering committee establishes.

====(C) Steering committee– ====



=====(i) In general– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall establish a steering committee—
:(I) to establish selection criteria for the awarding of assistance under paragraph (1)to employ a veteran to carry out a project under subparagraph (A) of subsection (b)(1)or to award a grant or contract to carry out a project under subparagraph (B) of such subsection; and
:(II) to provide the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] with advice on awarding assistance under this subsection with respect to projects described in subsection (a)(1)and carrying out the requirements of the veterans conservation corps under subsection (b).

=====(ii) Composition– =====

The steering committee shall be composed of the following:
:(I) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] .
:(II) The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] .
:(III) The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] .
:(IV) The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] .
:(V) The [[w:|Corporation for National and Community Service]] .
:(VI) The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]] .

=====(iii) Chairperson– =====

The chairperson of the steering committee shall be the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] .

=====(iv) Advisory input– =====

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] , the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]] , and the [[w:|Chief Executive Officer of the Corporation for National and Community Service]] may provide advice to the steering committee.

===(4) Assistance for first responders– ===

Not more than 10 percent of amounts appropriated or otherwise made available to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] to carry out this section may be transferred to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] and the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] to employ veterans under subsection (c).

==(e) Reporting framework– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall establish a reporting framework to regularly monitor and evaluate the veterans conservation corps to ensure proper oversight and accountability of the veterans conservation corps.

==(f) Outreach– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] shall ensure that veterans employed under the veterans conservation corps are aware of benefits and assistance available to them under laws administered by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] .

==(g) Donations– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] may solicit, accept, hold, administer, use, and dispose of, in furtherance of the purpose of this Act, donations of any money or property, real, personal, or mixed, tangible or intangible, received by gift, devise, bequest, or otherwise. Donations accepted under this subparagraph shall be used as nearly as possible in accordance with the terms, if any, of such donation.

==(h) Authorization of appropriations– ==



===(1) In general– ===

There is available without further appropriationto the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] to carry out this section,$600,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2014 through 2018.

===(2) Limitation– ===

Of amounts appropriated or otherwise made available to carry out this section, not more than five percent may be spent to administer the veterans conservation corps.

==(i) Definition of veteran– ==

In this section, the term veteran has the meaning given the term in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/101 section 101of title 38, United States Code] .
