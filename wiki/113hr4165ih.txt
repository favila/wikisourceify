{{Center|A BILL}}

 To protect crime victims’ rights.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Justice for Crime Victims Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Crime victims' rights=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3771  Section 3771of title 18, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3771 subsection (a)], by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
::(9) The right to be informed in a timely manner of any plea bargain or deferred prosecution agreement. 
::(10) The right to be informed of the rights under this section and the services described in  section 503(c) of the Victims' Rights and Restitution Act of 1990 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/10607 42 U.S.C. 10607(c)])and provided contact information for the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Office of the Victims' Rights Ombudsman of the Department of Justice]]. ; 
</blockquote>

:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3771 subsection (d)(3)], in the fifth sentence, by inserting“, unless the litigants, with the approval of the court, have stipulated to a different time period for consideration”before the period; and
:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3771 subsection (e)]—
::(A) by striking“this chapter, the term”and inserting the following: “this chapter:

<blockquote> 

=====(1) Court of appeals– =====

The term court of appeals means—
:(A) the United States court of appeals for the judicial district in which a defendant is being prosecuted; or
:(B) for a prosecution in the Superior Court of the District of Columbia, the District of Columbia Court of Appeals. 

=====(2) Crime victim– =====



======(A) In general– ======

The term ; 
</blockquote>

::(B) by striking“In the case”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

=====(B) Minors and certain other victims– =====

In the case ; and 
</blockquote>

::(C) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

=====(3) District court; court– =====

The terms district court and court include the Superior Court of the District of Columbia. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Crime victims fund– ==

   Section 1402(d)(3) of the Victims of Crime Act of 1984 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/10601 42 U.S.C. 10601(d)(3)] )is amended—
:(1) by inserting“(A)”before“Of the sums”; and
:(2) by striking“available for the United States Attorneys Offices”and all that follows and inserting the following: ‘‘available only for—

<blockquote> 
::(i) the United States Attorneys Offices and the [[w:Federal Bureau of Investigation|Federal Bureau of Investigation]]to provide and improve services for the benefit of crime victims in the Federal criminal justice system (as described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3771 3771 of title 18, United States Code], and  section 503 of the Victims’ Rights and Restitution Act of 1990 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/10607 42 U.S.C. 10607] )) through victim coordinators, victims' specialists, and advocates, including for the administrative support of victim coordinators and advocates providing such services; and 
::(ii) a Victim Notification System. 
::(B) Amounts made available under subparagraph (A) may not be used for any purpose that is not specified in clause (i) or (ii) of subparagraph (A) . . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Appellate review of petitions relating to crime victims' rights– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3771  Section 3771(d)(3)of title 18, United States Code], as amended by subsection (a)(2) of this section , is amended by inserting after the fifth sentence the following:“In deciding such application, the court of appeals shall apply ordinary standards of appellate review.”.

===(2) Application– ===

The amendment made by paragraph (1) shall apply with respect to any petition for a writ of mandamus filed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3771  section 3771(d)(3)of title 18, United States Code], that is pending on the date of enactment of this Act.
