{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to improve the accountability of the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to the Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Demanding Accountability for Veterans Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Accountability of Secretary of Veterans Affairs to Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) In general– ==

Chapter 7of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 712. Accountability of Secretary to Inspector General.==



===(a) List of managers– ===


:(1) If the [[w:|Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs]] determines that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] has not appropriately responded with significant progress to a covered report by the date specified in the action plan of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] developed in response to such covered report—
::(A) the [[w:|Inspector General]] shall notify the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]] and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] of such failure to appropriately respond; and
::(B) not later than 15 days after such notification, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall submit to the [[w:|Inspector General]] a list of the names of each responsible manager and the matter in the action plan for which the manager is responsible.
:(2) The [[w:|Inspector General]] may not make public the names of responsible managers submitted under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/712 paragraph (1)(B)] .

===(b) Performance of responsible managers– ===


:(1) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall—
::(A) promptly notify each responsible manager of a covered issue by not later than seven days after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] submits to the [[w:|Inspector General]] the name of the manager under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/712 subsection (a)(2)] ;
::(B) direct such manager to resolve such issue; and
::(C) provide such manager with appropriate counseling and a mitigation plan with respect to resolving such issue.
:(2) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall ensure that any performance review of a responsible manager includes an evaluation of whether the manager took appropriate actions during the period covered by the review to respond to the covered issue for which a request was made under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/712 subsection (a)] .
:(3) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] may not pay to a responsible manager any bonus or award, including a performance award under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5384 section 5384of title 5, United States Code] , if the covered issue for which a request was made under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/712 subsection (a)] is unresolved.

===(c) Role of Inspector General– ===

Any authority of the [[w:|Inspector General]] provided under this section is in addition to any responsibility or authority provided to the [[w:|Inspector General]] in the Inspector General Act of 1978(5 U.S.C. App).

===(d) Definitions– ===

In this section:
:(1) The term covered issue means, with respect to a responsible manager, an issue described in a covered report for which the manager is or was responsible.
:(2) The term covered report means a report by the [[w:|Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs]] that recommends actions to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] (or other official or employee of the Department) to address an issue in the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] with respect to public health or safety.
:(3) The term responsible manager means an individual who—
::(A) is an employee of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] ;
::(B) is or was responsible for an issue included in a covered report; and
::(C) in being so responsible, is or was employed in a management position, regardless of whether the employee is in the competitive civil service, Senior Executive Service, or other type of civil service..
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections at the beginning of such chapter is amended by inserting after the item relating to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/711 section 711] the following new item:

<blockquote>712. Accountability of Secretary to Inspector General..
</blockquote>
