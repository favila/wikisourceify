{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the provisions of title 46, United States Code, related to the Board of Visitors to the United States Merchant Marine Academy, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   U.S. Merchant Marine Academy Board of Visitors Enhancement Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. United States Merchant Marine Academy Board of Visitors=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312  Section 51312of title 46, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

=Sec. 51312. Board of Visitors=



==(a) In general– ==

A [[w:|Board of Visitors to the United States Merchant Marine Academy]](referred to in this section as the“Board”and the“Academy”, respectively) shall be established to provide independent advice and recommendations on matters relating to the United States Merchant Marine Academy .

==(b) Appointment and membership– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 60 days after the date of the enactment of the  U.S. Merchant Marine Academy Board of Visitors Enhancement Act  , the [[w:|Board]]shall be composed of—
:(A) 2 Senators appointed by the chairman, in consultation with the ranking member, of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|  [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate]] ]];
:(B) 3 members of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]appointed by the chairman, in consultation with the ranking member, of the [[w:|  [[w:United States House Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services of the House of Representatives]] ]];
:(C) 1 Senator appointed by the [[w:Office of the Vice President of the United States|Vice President]], who shall be a member of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|  [[w:United States Senate Committee on Appropriations|Committee on Appropriations of the Senate]] ]];
:(D) 2 members of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]appointed by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives]], in consultation with the Minority Leader, at least 1 of whom shall be a member of the [[w:|  [[w:United States House Committee on Appropriations|Committee on Appropriations of the House of Representatives]] ]];
:(E) the [[w:Air Force Elements U.S. Transportation Command|Commander of the United States Transportation Command]];
:(F) the [[w:Military Sealift Command|Commander of the Military Sealift Command]];
:(G) the [[w:United States Coast Guard|Assistant Commandant for Prevention Policy of the United States Coast Guard]];
:(H) 4 individuals appointed by the President; and
:(I) as ex officio members—
::(i) the chairman of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|  [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate]] ]];
::(ii) the chairman of the [[w:|  [[w:United States House Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services of the House of Representatives]] ]];
::(iii) the chairman of the [[w:|Advisory Board]]to the Academy established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51313 section 51313]; and
::(iv) the member of the House of Representatives in whose congressional district the Academy is located, as a non-voting member, unless such member of the House of Representatives is appointed as a voting member of the [[w:|Board]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subparagraph (B)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (D)].

===(2) Presidential appointees– ===

Of the individuals appointed by the President under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 paragraph (1)(H)]—
:(A) at least 2 shall be graduates of the Academy;
:(B) at least 1 shall be a senior corporate officer from a United States maritime shipping company that participates in the Maritime Security Program, or in any [[w:United States Maritime Administration|Maritime Administration]]program providing incentives for companies to register their vessels in the United States, and this appointment shall rotate biennially among such companies; and
:(C) 1 or more may be a Senate-confirmed Presidential appointee, a member of the Senior Executive Service, or an officer of flag-rank who from the [[w:United States Coast Guard|United States Coast Guard]], the [[w:National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration|National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration]], or any of the military services that commission graduates of the Academy, exclusive of the [[w:|Board]]members described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subparagraph (E)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (F)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (G)]of paragraph (1).

===(3) Term of service– ===

Each member of the [[w:|Board]]shall serve for a term of 2 years commencing at the beginning of each [[w:United States Congress|Congress]], except that any member whose term on the [[w:|Board]]has expired shall continue to serve until a successor is designated.

===(4) Vacancies– ===

If a member of the [[w:|Board]]is no longer able to serve on the [[w:|Board]]or resigns, the Designated Federal Officer selected under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subsection (g)(2)]shall immediately notify the official who appointed such member. Not later than 60 days after that notification, such official shall designate a replacement to serve the remainder of such member's term.

===(5) Current members– ===

Each member of the [[w:|Board]]serving as a member of the [[w:|Board]]on the date of the enactment of the  U.S. Merchant Marine Academy Board of Visitors Enhancement Act  shall continue to serve on the [[w:|Board]]for the remainder of such member’s term.

===(6) Designation and responsibility of substitute Board members– ===



====(A) Authority to designate– ====

A member of the [[w:|Board]]described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subparagraph (E)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (F)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (G)]of paragraph (1) or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subparagraph (B)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (C)]of paragraph (2) may, if unable to attend or participate in an activity described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subsection (d)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (e)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (f)], designate another individual to serve as a substitute member of the [[w:|Board]], on a temporary basis, to attend or participate in such activity.

====(B) Requirements– ====

A substitute member of the [[w:|Board]]designated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subparagraph (A)]shall be—
:(i) an individual who has been appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]and confirmed by the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]];
:(ii) a member of the Senior Executive Service; or
:(iii) an officer of flag-rank who is employed by—
::(I) the [[w:United States Coast Guard|United States Coast Guard]];
::(II) the [[w:Air Force Elements U.S. Transportation Command|United States Transportation Command]]; or
::(III) the [[w:Military Sealift Command|Military Sealift Command]].

====(C) Participation– ====

A substitute member of the [[w:|Board]]designated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subparagraph (A)]—
:(i) shall be permitted to fully participate in the proceedings and activities of the [[w:|Board]];
:(ii) shall report back to the member on the [[w:|Board]]’s activities not later than 15 days following the substitute member’s participation in such activities; and
:(iii) shall be permitted to participate in the preparation of reports described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 paragraph (j)]related to any proceedings or activities of the [[w:|Board]]in which such substitute member participates.

==(c) Chairperson– ==



===(1) In general– ===

On a biennial basis, the [[w:|Board]]shall select from among its members, a member of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]or a [[w:United States Senate|Senator]]to serve as the Chairperson.

===(2) Rotation– ===

A member of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]and a member of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]shall alternately serve as the [[w:|Chair of the Board]]on a biennial basis.

===(3) Term– ===

An individual may not serve as Chairperson for more than 1 consecutive term.

==(d) Meetings– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:|Board]]shall meet several times each year as provided for in the Charter described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 paragraph (2)(B)], including at least 1 meeting held at the Academy.

===(2) Selection and consideration– ===

Not later than 60 days after the date of the enactment of the  U.S. Merchant Marine Academy Board of Visitors Enhancement Act  , the Designated Federal Officer selected under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subsection (g)(2)]shall organize a meeting of the [[w:|Board]]for the purposes of—
:(A) selecting a Chairperson; and
:(B) considering an official Charter for the Board, which shall provide for the meeting of the [[w:|Board]]several times each year.

==(e) Visiting the Academy– ==



===(1) Annual visit– ===

The [[w:|Board]]shall visit the Academy annually on a date selected by the [[w:|Board]], in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]]and the Superintendent of the Academy.

===(2) Other visits– ===

In cooperation with the Superintendent, the [[w:|Board]]or its members may make other visits to the Academy in connection with the duties of the [[w:|Board]].

===(3) Access– ===

While visiting the Academy under this subsection, members of the [[w:|Board]]shall have reasonable access to the grounds, facilities, midshipmen, faculty, staff, and other personnel of the Academy for the purpose of carrying out the duties of the [[w:|Board]].

==(f) Responsibility– ==

The [[w:|Board]]shall inquire into the state of morale and discipline, the curriculum, instruction, physical equipment, fiscal affairs, academic methods, and other matters relating to the Academy that the [[w:|Board]]decides to consider.

==(g) Department of Transportation support– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]]shall—
:(1) provide support as deemed necessary by the [[w:|Board]]for the performance of the [[w:|Board]]’s functions;
:(2) not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of the  U.S. Merchant Marine Academy Board of Visitors Enhancement Act  , select a Designated Federal Officer to support the performance of the [[w:|Board]]’s functions; and
:(3) in cooperation with the [[w:United States Maritime Administration|Maritime Administrator]]and the Superintendent of the Academy, advise the [[w:|Board]]of any institutional issues, consistent with applicable laws concerning the disclosure of information.

==(h) Staff– ==

Staff members may be designated to serve without reimbursement as staff for the [[w:|Board]]by—
:(1) the [[w:|Chairperson of the Board]];
:(2) the chairman of the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|  [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate]] ]]; and
:(3) the chairman of the [[w:|  [[w:United States House Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services of the House of Representatives]] ]].

==(i) Travel expenses– ==

While serving away from home or regular place of business, a member of the [[w:|Board]]or a staff member designated under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subsection (h)]shall be allowed travel expenses, including per diem in lieu of subsistence, as authorized under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5703 section 5703 of title 5, United States Code].

==(j) Reports– ==



===(1) Annual report– ===

Not later than 60 days after each annual visit required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subsection (e)(1)], the [[w:|Board]]shall submit to the President a written report of its actions, views, and recommendations pertaining to the Academy.

===(2) Other reports– ===

If the members of the [[w:|Board]]visit the Academy under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subsection (e)(2)], the [[w:|Board]]may—
:(A) prepare a report on such visit; and
:(B) if approved by a majority of the members of the [[w:|Board]], submit such report to the President not later than 60 days after the date of the approval.

===(3) Advisors– ===

The [[w:|Board]]may call in advisers—
:(A) for consultation regarding the execution of the [[w:|Board]]’s responsibility under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 subsection (f)]; or
:(B) to assist in the preparation of a report described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 paragraph (1)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (2)].

===(4) Submission– ===

A report submitted to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 paragraph (1)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/46/51312 (2)]shall be concurrently submitted to—
:(A) the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]];
:(B) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|  [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate]] ]]; and
:(C) the [[w:|  [[w:United States House Committee on Armed Services|Committee on Armed Services of the House of Representatives]] ]]. . 
</blockquote>
