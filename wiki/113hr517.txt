{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide that 4 of the 12 weeks of parental leave made
		  available to a Federal employee shall be paid leave, and for other
		  purposes.

={{anchor|HDBA4EBA368DC41E687E6A7988D48C00F}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Federal Employees Paid Parental Leave
			 Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HB515BDCF8600455A8BE6373AC4DFC753}}Sec. 2. Paid parental
			 leave under title 5=



=={{anchor|H13D87E8CE5694889B4C21F26715E554A}}(a) Amendment to
			 title 5– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 Subsection (d) of
			 section 6382 of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating such subsection as
			 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 subsection (d)(1)];
:(2) by striking
			 “[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 subparagraph (A)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 (B)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 (C)], or” and inserting “[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 subparagraph
			 (C)] or”; and
:(3) by adding at the
			 end the following:

<blockquote>
::(2) An employee may elect to substitute
				for any leave without pay under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 subparagraph (A)] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 (B) of subsection (a)(1)]
				any paid leave which is available to such employee for that purpose.
::(3) The paid leave that is available to
				an employee for purposes of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 paragraph (2)] is—
:::(A) subject to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 paragraph (6)], 4
				administrative workweeks of paid parental leave under this subparagraph in
				connection with the birth or placement involved; and
:::(B) any annual or sick leave accrued or
				accumulated by such employee under subchapter I.
                
::(4) Nothing in this subsection shall be
				considered to require that an employee first use all or any portion of the
				leave described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 subparagraph (B) of paragraph (3)] before being allowed to
				use the paid parental leave described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 subparagraph (A) of paragraph
				(3)].
::(5) Paid parental leave under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 paragraph
				(3)(A)]—
:::(A) shall be payable from any
				appropriation or fund available for salaries or expenses for positions within
				the employing agency;
:::(B) shall not be considered to be annual
				or vacation leave for purposes of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5551 section 5551] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5552 5552] or for any other
				purpose; and
:::(C) if not used by the employee before the
				end of the 12-month period (as referred to in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 subsection (a)(1)]) to which it
				relates, shall not accumulate for any subsequent use.
::(6) The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director of the Office of
				Personnel Management]]—
:::(A) may promulgate regulations to increase the
				amount of paid parental leave available to an employee under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 paragraph (3)(A)],
				to a total of not more than 8 administrative workweeks, based on the
				consideration of—
::::(i) the benefits provided to the Federal
				Government of offering increased paid parental leave, including enhanced
				recruitment and retention of employees;
::::(ii) the cost to the Federal Government
				of increasing the amount of paid parental leave that is available to
				employees;
::::(iii) trends in the private sector and
				in State and local governments with respect to offering paid parental
				leave;
::::(iv) the Federal Government’s role as a
				model employer;
::::(v) the impact of increased paid
				parental leave on lower-income and economically disadvantaged employees and
				their children; and
::::(vi) such other factors as the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Director]]
				considers necessary; and
:::(B) shall prescribe any regulations
				necessary to carry out this subsection, including, subject to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 paragraph (4)],
				the manner in which an employee may designate any day or other period as to
				which such employee wishes to use paid parental leave described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 paragraph
				(3)(A)]..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H8CCD536DD399448C861B34B276D6A66A}}(b) Effective
			 date– ==

The amendment made by this section shall not be effective
			 with respect to any birth or placement occurring before the end of the 6-month
			 period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act.

={{anchor|H2C804EB157CD4DD684A51F156302F9C1}}Sec. 3. Paid parental
			 leave for Congressional employees=



=={{anchor|H3290473A0BD54EFD94D39B66397EF919}}(a) Amendment to
			 Congressional Accountability Act– ==

Section 202 of the Congressional
			 Accountability Act of 1995 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/1312 2 U.S.C. 1312]) is amended—
:(1) in subsection
			 (a)(1), by adding at the end the following: “In applying section
			 102(a)(1) (A) and (B) of such Act to covered employees, subsection (d) shall
			 apply.”;
:(2) by redesignating
			 subsections (d) and (e) as subsections (e) and (f), respectively; and
:(3) by inserting after
			 subsection (c) the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|H019611A1978243EBA010EE2EBE790ACA}}(d) Special rule for
				paid parental leave for Congressional employees– ====



====={{anchor|HAAC7DBCBA95149EA9887A10E93DCB218}}(1) Substitution of
				paid leave– =====

A covered employee taking leave without pay under
				subparagraph (A) or (B) of section 102(a)(1) of the Family and Medical Leave
				Act of 1993 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2612 29 U.S.C. 2612(a)(1)]) may elect to substitute for any such leave
				any paid leave which is available to such employee for that purpose.

====={{anchor|HDFB014E0214C431EB56A393F88CD3B4D}}(2) Amount of paid
				leave– =====

The paid leave that is available to a covered employee for
				purposes of paragraph (1) is—
:(A) the number of
				weeks of paid parental leave in connection with the birth or placement involved
				that correspond to the number of administrative workweeks of paid parental
				leave available to Federal employees under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 section 6382(d)(3)(A) of title 5,
				United States Code]; and
:(B) any additional
				paid vacation or sick leave provided by the employing office to such
				employee.

====={{anchor|H4BD6113964634FDFAD449D81707D119A}}(3) Limitation– =====

Nothing in this subsection shall be
				considered to require that an employee first use all or any portion of the
				leave described in subparagraph (B) of paragraph (2) before being allowed to
				use the paid parental leave described in subparagraph (A) of paragraph
				(2).

====={{anchor|H7B1481F22EB84A8CB653E6AE7E3A4298}}(4) Additional
				rules– =====

Paid parental leave under paragraph (2)(A)—
:(A) shall be payable
				from any appropriation or fund available for salaries or expenses for positions
				within the employing office; and
:(B) if not used by the covered employee before
				the end of the 12-month period (as referred to in section 102(a)(1) of the
				Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2612 29 U.S.C. 2612(a)(1)])) to which it
				relates, shall not accumulate for any subsequent
				use..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HB110DE04793E4EBAB8FD2EA4EE946991}}(b) Effective
			 date– ==

The amendment made by this section shall not be effective
			 with respect to any birth or placement occurring before the end of the 6-month
			 period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act.

==={{anchor|HB814A7A303AA4F15AB7FA3C9767254F7}}(3) Special rule for

===={{anchor|HEB545323A1FE4549B195A89DDA5644CA}}(A) Substitution of
				paid leave– ====

An employee of an employer described in section
				101(4)(A)(iv) taking leave under subparagraph (A) or (B) of subsection (a)(1)
				may elect to substitute for any such leave any paid leave which is available to
				such employee for that purpose.

===={{anchor|H67829CDACEBF4E5B82F985B9E42AE376}}(B) Amount of paid
				leave– ====

The paid leave that is
				available to an employee of an employer described in section 101(4)(A)(iv) for
				purposes of subparagraph (A) is—
:(i) the number of weeks of paid parental leave
				in connection with the birth or placement involved that correspond to the
				number of administrative workweeks of paid parental leave available to Federal
				employees under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 section 6382(d)(3)(A) of title 5, United States Code];
				and
:(ii) any additional
				paid vacation or sick leave provided by such employer.

===={{anchor|H2D42193655534AC593AA709F7AA82655}}(C) Limitation– ====

Nothing in this paragraph shall be
				considered to require that an employee first use all or any portion of the
				leave described in clause (ii) of subparagraph (B) before being allowed to use
				the paid parental leave described in clause (i) of such subparagraph.

===={{anchor|H219651793AD047199E5DF89574B75A10}}(D) Additional
				rules– ====

Paid parental leave under subparagraph (B)(i)—
:(i) shall be payable from any appropriation or
				fund available for salaries or expenses for positions with the employer
				described in section 101(4)(A)(iv); and
:(ii) if not used by
				the employee of such employer before the end of the 12-month period (as
				referred to in subsection (a)(1)) to which it relates, shall not accumulate for
				any subsequent
				use..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|HE4EE0C5F6AA54D39B96579C87F9E1221}}Sec. 5. Clarification for
			 members of the National Guard and Reserves=



=={{anchor|H9CF3C0C37C6F4D48B9803F6A93DCA61E}}(a) Executive branch
			 employees– ==

For purposes of
			 determining the eligibility of an employee who is a member of the National
			 Guard or Reserves to take leave under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 paragraph (1) (A)] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 (B) of section
			 6382(a) of title 5, United States Code], or to substitute such leave pursuant to
			 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6382 paragraph (2) of such section] (as added by section 2), any service by such
			 employee on active duty (as defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6381 section 6381(7) of such title]) shall be
			 counted as service as an employee for purposes of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/6381 section 6381(1)(B) of such
			 title].

=={{anchor|H1E0DCA441A814055B9080D095AEEA1C9}}(b) Congressional
			 Employees– ==

For purposes of
			 determining the eligibility of a covered employee (as such term is defined in
			 section 101(3) of the Congressional Accountability Act) who is a member of the
			 National Guard or Reserves to take leave under subparagraph (A) or (B) of
			 section 102(a)(1) of the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 (pursuant to
			 section 202(a)(1) of the Congressional Accountability Act), or to substitute
			 such leave pursuant to subsection (d) of section 202 of such Act (as added by
			 section 3), any service by such employee on active duty (as defined in section
			 101(14) of the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993) shall be counted as time
			 during which such employee has been employed in an employing office for
			 purposes of section 202(a)(2)(B) of the Congressional Accountability
			 Act.

=={{anchor|HC3D4923B52D045DBBCBB46DD13B88ED6}}(c) GAO and Library
			 of Congress Employees– ==

For
			 purposes of determining the eligibility of an employee of the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Government
			 Accountability Office]] or [[w:Library of Congress|Library of Congress]] who is a member of the [[w:National Guard Bureau|National
			 Guard]] or Reserves to take leave under subparagraph (A) or (B) of section
			 102(a)(1) of the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993, or to substitute such
			 leave pursuant to paragraph (3) of section 102(d) of such Act (as added by
			 section 4), any service by such employee on active duty (as defined in section
			 101(14) of such Act) shall be counted as time during which such employee has
			 been employed for purposes of section 101(2)(A) of such Act.
