{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide effective criminal prosecutions for certain identity thefts, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HA6DD182E3D6A479296A180ED26E26BE2}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Stopping Tax Offenders and Prosecuting Identity Theft Act of 2013” or the “STOP Identity Theft Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H1F1FEAC186CF42009B110588A5780410}}Sec. 2. Use of Department of Justice resources with regard to tax return identity theft=



=={{anchor|H83DB7D4B87644EDB996EDDBDC683F066}}(a) In general– ==

The [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] should make use of all existing resources of the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]], including any appropriate task forces, to bring more perpetrators of tax return identity theft to justice.

=={{anchor|HB78D430FEEF84AAEA2D80BE4DB596372}}(b) Considerations To be taken into account– ==

In carrying out this section, the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] should take into account the following:
:(1) The need to concentrate efforts in those areas of the country where the crime is most frequently reported.
:(2) The need to coordinate with State and local authorities for the most efficient use of their laws and resources to prosecute and prevent the crime.
:(3) The need to protect vulnerable groups, such as veterans, seniors, and minors (especially foster children) from becoming victims or otherwise used in the offense.

={{anchor|H3C93BF80030E4FA494B9F03DB3146892}}Sec. 3. Victims of identity theft may include organizations=

Chapter 47 of title 18, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) in section 1028—
::(A) in subsection (a)(7), by inserting “(including an organization)” after “another person”; and
::(B) in subsection (d)(7), in the matter preceding subparagraph (A), by inserting “or other person” after “specific individual”; and
:(2) in section 1028A(a)(1), by inserting “(including an organization)” after “another person”.

={{anchor|H54A1FF890F2146BFA4877837EECF9CB8}}Sec. 4. Identity theft for purposes of tax fraud=

Section 1028(b)(3) of title 18, United States Code, is amended—
:(1) in subparagraph (B), by striking “or” at the end;
:(2) in subparagraph (C), by inserting “or” after the semicolon; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(D) during and in relation to a felony under section 7206 or 7207 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986;.
</blockquote>


={{anchor|HEEFCFF246C3A4654B44C91CD319E42F8}}Sec. 5. Reporting requirement=



=={{anchor|HBAA33C5808474917B648CFE7DEE32F8B}}(a) Generally– ==

Beginning with the first report made more than 9 months after the date of the enactment of this Act under section 1116 of title 31, United States Code, the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall include in such report the information described in subsection (b) of this section as to progress in implementing this Act and the amendments made by this Act.

=={{anchor|H13BCC2EECA474973BE92238AD0A6436D}}(b) Contents– ==

The information referred to in subsection (a) is as follows:
:(1) Information readily available to the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] about trends in the incidence of tax return identity theft.
:(2) The effectiveness of statutory tools, including those provided by this Act, in aiding the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] in the prosecution of tax return identity theft.
:(3) Recommendations on additional statutory tools that would aid in removing barriers to effective prosecution of tax return identity theft.
:(4) The status on implementing the recommendations of the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Justice|Department’s]] March 2010 Audit Report 10–21 entitled The Department of Justice’s Efforts to Combat Identity Theft.
