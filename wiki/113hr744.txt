{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide effective criminal prosecutions for certain identity thefts, and for other purposes.


{{section|HA6DD182E3D6A479296A180ED26E26BE2|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the 
          Stopping Tax Offenders and Prosecuting Identity Theft Act of 2013

        
 or the 
          STOP Identity Theft Act of 2013

        
.

{{section|H1F1FEAC186CF42009B110588A5780410|2. Use of Department of Justice resources with regard to tax return identity theft}}
(a)
In general
The [[United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] should make use of all existing resources of the [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]], including any appropriate task forces, to bring more perpetrators of tax return identity theft to justice.

(b)
Considerations To be taken into account
In carrying out this section, the [[United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] should take into account the following:
(1)
The need to concentrate efforts in those areas of the country where the crime is most frequently reported.

(2)
The need to coordinate with State and local authorities for the most efficient use of their laws and resources to prosecute and prevent the crime.

(3)
The need to protect vulnerable groups, such as veterans, seniors, and minors (especially foster children) from becoming victims or otherwise used in the offense.

{{section|HEEFCFF246C3A4654B44C91CD319E42F8|5. Reporting requirement}}
(a)
Generally
Beginning with the first report made more than 9 months after the date of the enactment of this Act under section 1116 of title 31, United States Code
, the [[United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall include in such report the information described in subsection (b)
 of this section as to progress in implementing this Act and the amendments made by this Act.

(b)
Contents
The information referred to in subsection (a)
 is as follows:
(1)
Information readily available to the [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] about trends in the incidence of tax return identity theft.

(2)
The effectiveness of statutory tools, including those provided by this Act, in aiding the [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] in the prosecution of tax return identity theft.

(3)
Recommendations on additional statutory tools that would aid in removing barriers to effective prosecution of tax return identity theft.

(4)
The status on implementing the recommendations of the [[United States Department of Justice|Department’s]] March 2010 Audit Report 10–21 entitled The Department of Justice’s Efforts to Combat Identity Theft
.
