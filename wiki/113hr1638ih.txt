{{Center|A BILL}}

To repeal the authority to conduct certain censuses, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HAADF810328DA43DC8E2AD3AC811B2ACD}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Census Reform Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H2929C32844294C9C938C063E0EA937D5}}Sec. 2. Repeal and cancellation of certain census activities under title 13, United States Code=



=={{anchor|H70A1E96670F1402C88605ECCEE9CE3AC}}(a) Repeal of census relating to agriculture– ==

Chapter 3of title 13, United States Code, is repealed.

=={{anchor|HAB5BC75F3F974702B85318AF3C841882}}(b) Repeal of economic census– ==

Subchapter I of chapter 5 of such titleis repealed.

=={{anchor|H195D5570100A4D6DA9EF5888F83F915A}}(c) Repeal of mid-Decade census– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 Section 141 of such title] is amended—
:(1) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 subsection (d)] ;
:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 subsection (e)] and inserting the following:

<blockquote>
::(e) If in the administration of any program established by or under Federal law which provides benefits to State or local governments or to other recipients, eligibility for or the amount of such benefits would (without regard to this subsection) be determined by taking into account data obtained in the most recent decennial census then in the determination of such eligibility or amount of benefits the most recent data available from either the mid-decade or decennial census shall be used.; and
</blockquote>

:(3) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 subsection (f)] , by striking“and mid-decade”and“or (d)”.

=={{anchor|HE5A2CE612C04409A8663E7FD6B1401B4}}(d) Repeal of census of governments– ==

Subchapter III ofchapter 5of title 13, United States Code, is repealed.

=={{anchor|H6FCAC531C4CE41B4AC390E49807AE1E3}}(e) Effect on census activities– ==

Beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] and the [[w:Bureau of the Census|Bureau of the Census]] —
:(1) are not authorized to carry out any activities with respect to—
::(A) a census conducted underchapter 3,subchapter Iorsubchapter III of chapter 5, or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 section 141(d) of such title] , as in effect on the day before the date of the enactment of this Act; or
::(B) a survey (including the survey, conducted by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] , which is commonly referred to as the“American Community Survey”), sampling, or other questionnaire conducted under such title;
:(2) shall terminate any activities being carried out with respect to any such census, survey, sampling, or questionnaire; and
:(3) may only conduct the decennial census of population, as authorized under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 section 141 of title 13, United States Code] .

=={{anchor|H14599EC47F6A4D599E3C497A41EF98E7}}(f) Rescission of unobligated amounts– ==

Any unobligated amounts available to carry out the American Community Survey or the following provisions of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/ title 13, United States Code] , are rescinded:
:(1) [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 Section 141(d)] .
:(2) Chapter 3.
:(3) [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/5 Subchapter I] andsubchapter III of chapter 5.

={{anchor|H4A5CD7D175C644CEB9C611766DE4081F}}Sec. 3. Prohibition on surveys or questionnaires; limitations on statistics collected in a decennial census=



=={{anchor|HECF250F6721948599F5A2F36F8A5687C}}(a) In general– ==

Subchapter I ofchapter 1of title 13, United States Code, is amended by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/5 section 5] the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H66DBD44934B74CB4801AEABA9FA25751}}Sec. 5a. Prohibition on surveys or questionnaires; limitation on statistics==


:(a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law—
::(1) the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] may not conduct any survey, sampling, or other questionnaire, and may only conduct a decennial census of population as authorized under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 section 141] ; and
::(2) any form used by the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] in such a decennial census may only collect information necessary for the tabulation of total population by States..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H2356F440645C49FA85CF6E5C7C5AE6E0}}(b) Repeal of survey, questionnaire, or sampling authority– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/182 Sections 182] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/193 193] , and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/195 195 of title 13, United States Code] , are repealed.

=={{anchor|H445D7A57FA7F4C4E9E04A164771AA0FC}}(c) Conforming amendments– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 Section 141 of such title] is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 subsection (a)] , by striking“The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] ”and inserting“Consistent with the limitations set forth in section 5a, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]] ”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/141 subsection (g)] , by striking“, housing, and matters relating to population and housing”.

={{anchor|HCB68450948884182A2C53BAB8E98B8AC}}Sec. 4. Repeal of Census of Agriculture Act of1997=



=={{anchor|H598D50FD82854F57871E652E4A59FFFE}}(a) In general– ==

The Census of Agriculture Act of 1997([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/7/2201 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 7 U.S.C. 2201] note] ;[[Public Law -|Public Law 105–113]] )is repealed.

=={{anchor|H881CEE673FB640D286C44FCB98874B25}}(b) Effect oncensus– ==

Beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] —
:(1) is not authorized to carry out any activities with respect to the census required to be conducted in 2013 under the Census of Agriculture Act of 1997([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/7/2201 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 7 U.S.C. 2201] note] ;[[Public Law -|Public Law 105–113]] ), as in effect on the day before the date of the enactment of this Act; and
:(2) shall terminate any activities being carried out with respect to such census.

=={{anchor|H5DAA70B346724D9DBB9CF13CF9B30677}}(c) Rescission of unobligated funds– ==

Any unobligated amounts available to carry out the Census of Agriculture Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/7/2201 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 7 U.S.C. 2201] note] ;[[Public Law -|Public Law 105–113]] )are rescinded.
