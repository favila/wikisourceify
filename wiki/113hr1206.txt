{{Center|A BILL}}

To grant the Secretary of the Interior permanent authority to authorize States to issue electronic duck stamps, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H99DB852FCBB441D681CAA484C07A9460}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Permanent Electronic Duck Stamp Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HCC467ACD7E1D4C799B31C2382A3CC7DA}}Sec. 2. Definitions=

In this Act:

=={{anchor|H6F0CFEF091B049BCA937B05D1D912DEB}}(1) Actual stamp– ==

The term actual stamp means a Federal migratory-bird hunting and conservation stamp required under the Act of March 16, 1934 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 16 U.S.C. 718a et seq.]) (popularly known as the “Duck Stamp Act”), that is printed on paper and sold through the means established by the authority of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] immediately before the date of enactment of this Act.

=={{anchor|H1AD0C88066CD48CE9C858C1F1E1F56DB}}(2) Automated licensing system– ==



==={{anchor|H96FABF4DCF034C0AA9AC1CAF5B5F9288}}(A) In general– ===

The term automated licensing system means an electronic, computerized licensing system used by a State fish and wildlife agency to issue hunting, fishing, and other associated licenses and products.

==={{anchor|H6BC4E050F84847B98FF3B8705C913360}}(B) Inclusion– ===

The term automated licensing system includes a point-of-sale, Internet, telephonic system, or other electronic applications used for a purpose described in subparagraph (A).

=={{anchor|H7D99CD152FB346AAB8818FF54EF434AE}}(3) Electronic stamp– ==

The term electronic stamp means an electronic version of an actual stamp that—
:(A) is a unique identifier for the individual to whom it is issued;
:(B) can be printed on paper or produced through an electronic application with the same indicators as the State endorsement provides;
:(C) is issued through a State automated licensing system that is authorized, under State law and by the Secretary under this Act, to issue electronic stamps;
:(D) is compatible with the hunting licensing system of the State that issues the electronic stamp; and
:(E) is described in the State application approved by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] under section 4(b).

=={{anchor|H9B7F24A22ED34AC68F75E5B9B9CA5ECA}}(4) Secretary– ==

The term Secretary means the Secretary of the Interior.

={{anchor|HD6D929C4E04A43649D0AD4C26FB81DAE}}Sec. 3. Authority to issue electronic duck stamps=



=={{anchor|H4D51B6B0E00D4F959089B7C3787D543A}}(a) In general– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may authorize any State to issue electronic stamps in accordance with this Act.

=={{anchor|HAF34B4A998614C828950BB76C2A85068}}(b) Consultation– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall implement this section in consultation with State management agencies.

={{anchor|H531687248C844B02A8C2FF23F2523FE7}}Sec. 4. State application=



=={{anchor|HBA1E5FE18961497DA5D9CC0BA0082465}}(a) Approval of Application required– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may not authorize a State to issue electronic stamps under this Act unless the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] has received and approved an application submitted by the State in accordance with this section. The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may determine the number of new States per year to participate in the electronic stamp program.

=={{anchor|HD3248D137C9449FB9B456E9CDC02B357}}(b) Contents of Application– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may not approve a State application unless the application contains—
:(1) a description of the format of the electronic stamp that the State will issue under this Act, including identifying features of the licensee that will be specified on the stamp;
:(2) a description of any fee the State will charge for issuance of an electronic stamp;
:(3) a description of the process the State will use to account for and transfer to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] the amounts collected by the State that are required to be transferred to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] under the program;
:(4) the manner by which the State will transmit electronic stamp customer data to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]];
:(5) the manner by which actual stamps will be delivered;
:(6) the policies and procedures under which the State will issue duplicate electronic stamps; and
:(7) such other policies, procedures, and information as may be reasonably required by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]].

=={{anchor|H41620CA1A6D64EE59F134976B8BF2DFC}}(c) Publication of Deadlines, Eligibility Requirements, and Selection Criteria– ==

Not later than 30 days before the date on which the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] begins accepting applications under this section, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall publish—
:(1) deadlines for submission of applications;
:(2) eligibility requirements for submitting applications; and
:(3) criteria for approving applications.

={{anchor|H59C06E244D314CD39BCC4C59A4EC02F1}}Sec. 5. State obligations and authorities=



=={{anchor|HAB4861852CFB4212AAD06893A58060A1}}(a) Delivery of Actual Stamp– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall require that each individual to whom a State sells an electronic stamp under this Act shall receive an actual stamp—
:(1) by not later than the date on which the electronic stamp expires under section 6(c); and
:(2) in a manner agreed upon by the State and [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]].

=={{anchor|HDEF9228028D84B709351F2A5D3DDDC57}}(b) Collection and transfer of electronic stamp revenue and customer information– ==



==={{anchor|HCD177749F913481EB863F76AF7F0124C}}(1) Requirement to transmit– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall require each State authorized to issue electronic stamps to collect and submit to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] in accordance with this section—
:(A) the first name, last name, and complete mailing address of each individual that purchases an electronic stamp from the State;
:(B) the face value amount of each electronic stamp sold by the State; and
:(C) the amount of the Federal portion of any fee required by the agreement for each stamp sold.

==={{anchor|H9D8FFA84F3C24E8CB87B4CEE8B8E6718}}(2) Time of transmittal– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall require the submission under paragraph (1) to be made with respect to sales of electronic stamps by a State according to the written agreement between the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] and the State agency.

==={{anchor|HE4B4FBB4EDB1411C8E12C6673DA251D7}}(3) Additional fees not affected– ===

This section shall not apply to the State portion of any fee collected by a State under subsection (c).

=={{anchor|H1C95DA4D2BE045F5BD0542B45ED6B715}}(c) Electronic Stamp Issuance Fee– ==

A State authorized to issue electronic stamps may charge a reasonable fee to cover costs incurred by the State and the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Department of the Interior]] in issuing electronic stamps under this Act, including costs of delivery of actual stamps.

=={{anchor|H08544E6E61324247AAE912027982E551}}(d) Duplicate Electronic Stamps– ==

A State authorized to issue electronic stamps may issue a duplicate electronic stamp to replace an electronic stamp issued by the State that is lost or damaged.

=={{anchor|HB4D8A1540E524BC69A9A747B8FCD27EA}}(e) Limitation on Authority To Require Purchase of State License– ==

A State may not require that an individual purchase a State hunting license as a condition of issuing an electronic stamp under this Act.

={{anchor|H271A1876EC104C76A4333C3BF7530DD9}}Sec. 6. Electronic stamp requirements; recognition of electronic stamp=



=={{anchor|H763E99B2A3F145EF9B630D21EFC940D5}}(a) Stamp Requirements– ==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall require an electronic stamp issued by a State under this Act—
:(1) to have the same format as any other license, validation, or privilege the State issues under the automated licensing system of the State; and
:(2) to specify identifying features of the licensee that are adequate to enable Federal, State, and other law enforcement officers to identify the holder.

=={{anchor|H94266D4F34A14D91B1927B410F6CE0C6}}(b) Recognition of Electronic Stamp– ==

Any electronic stamp issued by a State under this Act shall, during the effective period of the electronic stamp—
:(1) bestow upon the licensee the same privileges as are bestowed by an actual stamp;
:(2) be recognized nationally as a valid Federal migratory bird hunting and conservation stamp; and
:(3) authorize the licensee to hunt migratory waterfowl in any other State, in accordance with the laws of the other State governing that hunting.

=={{anchor|HD5F1ABACFCE64694972A9FF36A07889E}}(c) Duration– ==

An electronic stamp issued by a State shall be valid for a period agreed to by the State and the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]], which shall not exceed 45 days.

={{anchor|H96C09478C7994394A5420FBEFFF37EFD}}Sec. 7. Termination of State participation=

The authority of a State to issue electronic stamps under this Act may be terminated—
:(1) by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]], if the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]]—
::(A) finds that the State has violated any of the terms of the application of the State approved by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] under section 4; and
::(B) provides to the State written notice of the termination by not later than the date that is 30 days before the date of termination; or
:(2) by the State, by providing written notice to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] by not later than the date that is 30 days before the termination date.
