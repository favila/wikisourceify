{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Federal Election Campaign Act to extend through 2018 the authority of the Federal Election Commission to impose civil money penalties on the basis of a schedule of penalties established and published by the Commission, to expand such authority to certain other violations, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Extension of administrative penalty authority of Federal Election Commission through2018=

 Section 309(a)(4)(C)(iv) of the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/437g 2 U.S.C. 437g(a)(4)(C)(iv)] ] )is amended by striking “December 31, 2013” and inserting “December 31, 2018” .

=Sec. 2. Expansion of administrative penalty authority of Federal Election Commission=



==(a) Application to qualified disclosure requirements– ==

 Section 309(a)(4)(C)(i) of the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/437g 2 U.S.C. 437g(a)(4)(C)(i)] ] )is amended by striking “any requirement of section 304(a) of the Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 2 U.S.C. 434(a)] )” and inserting “a qualified disclosure requirement” .

==(b) Schedule of penalties for each violation– ==

 Section 309(a)(4)(C)(i)(II) of the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/437g 2 U.S.C. 437g(a)(4)(C)(i)(II)] ] )is amended by inserting “, for violations of each qualified disclosure requirement,” before “under a schedule of penalties” .

==(c) Definition of qualified disclosure requirement– ==

 Section 309(a)(4)(C) of the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text//  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/437g 2 U.S.C. 437g(a)(4)(C)] ] )is amended—
:(1) by redesignating clause (iv), as amended by section 1, as clause (v); and
:(2) by inserting after clause (iii)the following new clause:

<blockquote>
::(iv) In this subparagraph, the term “qualified disclosure requirement” means any requirement of—
:::(I)  subsections (a), (c), (e), (f), (g), or (i) of section 304; or
:::(II)  section 305..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Effective date=

The amendments made by this Act shall take effect on the earlier of—
:(1) December 31, 2013; or
:(2) the date of the enactment of this Act.
