{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide for a smart water management pilot program.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Smart Water Management Conservation and Efficiency Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Smart water management pilot program=



==(a) Definitions– ==

In this section:

===(1) Eligible entity– ===

The term“eligible entity”means—
:(A) a utility;
:(B) a municipality;
:(C) a water district; and
:(D) any other authority that provides drinking water, wastewater treatment, or water reuse services.

===(2) Secretary– ===

The term“Secretary”means the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary of Energy]].

===(3) Smart water management pilot program– ===

The term“smart water management pilot program”or“pilot program”means the pilot program established under subsection (b) .

==(b) Smart water management pilot program– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall establish and carry out a smart water management pilot program in accordance with this section.

===(2) Purpose– ===

The purpose of the smart water management pilot program is to award grants to eligible entities to demonstrate and deploy novel and innovative technology-based solutions that will—
:(A) increase the energy and water efficiency of drinking water, wastewater treatment, and water reuse systems;
:(B) improve drinking water, water reuse, and wastewater treatment systems to help communities across the United States make significant progress in conserving water, saving energy, and reducing costs; and
:(C) support the implementation of innovative processes and the installation of advanced automated systems that provide real-time data on energy and water.

===(3) Project selection– ===



====(A) In general– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall make competitive, merit-reviewed grants under the pilot program to not less than 3, but not more than 5, eligible entities.

====(B) Selection Criteria– ====

In selecting an eligible entity to receive a grant under the pilot program, the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall consider—
:(i) energy and cost savings;
:(ii) the novelty of the technology to be used;
:(iii) the degree to which the project integrates next-generation sensors, software, analytics, and management tools;
:(iv) the anticipated cost-effectiveness of the pilot project in terms of energy efficiency savings, water savings or reuse, and infrastructure costs averted;
:(v) whether the technology can be deployed in a variety of geographic regions and the degree to which the technology can be implemented on a smaller or larger scale; and
:(vi) whether the project will be completed in 5 years or less.

====(C) Applications– ====



=====(i) In General– =====

Subject to clause (ii) , an eligible entity seeking a grant under the pilot program shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]an application at such time, in such manner, and containing such information as the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]determines to be necessary.

=====(ii) Contents– =====

An application under clause (i) shall, at a minimum, include—
:(I) a description of the project;
:(II) a description of the technology to be used in the project;
:(III) the anticipated results, including energy and water savings, of the project;
:(IV) a comprehensive budget for the project;
:(V) the names of the project lead organization and any partners;
:(VI) the number of users to be served by the project; and
:(VII) any other information that the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]determines to be necessary to complete the review and selection of a grant recipient.

===(4) Administration– ===



====(A) In general– ====

Not later than 300 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall select grant recipients under this section.

====(B) Evaluations– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall annually carry out an evaluation of each project for which a grant is provided under this section that—
:(i) evaluates the progress and impact of the project; and
:(ii) assesses the degree to which the project is meeting the goals of the pilot program.

====(C) Technical assistance– ====

On the request of a grant recipient, the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall provide technical assistance.

====(D) Best practices– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall make available to the public—
:(i) a copy of each evaluation carried out under subparagraph (B) ; and
:(ii) a description of any best practices identified by the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]as a result of those evaluations.

====(E) Report to Congress– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report containing the results of each evaluation carried out under subparagraph (B) .

==(c) Funding– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall use not less than $7,500,000 of amounts made available to the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]to carry out this section.

===(2) Prioritization– ===

In funding activities under this section, the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]shall prioritize funding in the following manner:
:(A) Any unobligated amounts made available to the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]]to carry out the activities of the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy Office]].
:(B) Any unobligated amounts (other than those described in subparagraph (A) ) made available to the [[w:United States Department of Energy|Secretary]].
