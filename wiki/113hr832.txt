{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Lower Rio Grande Valley Water Resources Conservation and Improvement Act of 2000 to authorize additional projects and activities under that Act, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Lower Rio Grande Valley Water Resources Conservation and Improvement Act of 2013”.

==(b) Inclusion of activities To conserve water or improve supply; transfers among projects– ==

 Section 4 of such Act([[Public Law 106-576|Public Law 106–576]] ;114 Stat. 3067)is further amended by redesignating subsection (c)as subsection (e), and by inserting after subsection (b)the following:

<blockquote>

===(c) Inclusion of activities To conserve water or improve supply– ===

In addition to the activities identified in the engineering reports referred to in subsection (a), each project that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] conducts or participates in under subsection (a)may include any of the following:
:(1) The replacement of irrigation canals and lateral canals with buried pipelines.
:(2) The impervious lining of irrigation canals and lateral canals.
:(3) Installation of water level, flow measurement, pump control, and telemetry systems.
:(4) The renovation and replacement of pumping plants.
:(5) Other activities that will result in the conservation of water or an improved supply of water.

===(d) Transfers among projects– ===

Of amounts made available for a project referred to in any of paragraphs (20) through (36) of subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may transfer and use for another such project up to 10 percent..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Reauthorization of appropriations for lower rio grande construction=

 Section 4(e) of the Lower Rio Grande Valley Water Resources Conservation and Improvement Act of 2000([[Public Law 106-576|Public Law 106–576]] ;114 Stat. 3067), as redesignated by section 2(b) of this Act, is further amended by inserting before the period the following:“for projects referred to in paragraphs (1) through (19) of subsection (a), and $45,389,954 for projects referred to in paragraphs (20) through (36) of subsection (a)”.
