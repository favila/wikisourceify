{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 10, United States Code, to improve the mental health assessments provided to members of the Armed Forces deployed in support of a contingency operation.

={{anchor|H7F91CCE5786A4517A406DBF2D9F1A48E}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Military Suicide Reduction Act”.

={{anchor|H9BAC9830C8BB42C0B9EE3E7C521E87C9}}Sec. 2. Mental health assessments for members of the Armed Forces deployed in support of a contingency operation=



=={{anchor|H5A1ECFFA5792420FAFD076E43F149027}}(a) Increased assessments– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m Subsection (a) of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 1074m] of title 10, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m paragraph (1)] —
::(A) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraphs (B) and (C)] as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraphs (C) and (D)] , respectively; and
::(B) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraph (A)] the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraph (B)] :

<blockquote>
:::(B) Once during each 180-day period in which the member is so deployed.; and
</blockquote>

:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m paragraph (2)] , by striking“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraph (B) and (C)] ”and inserting“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraphs (C) and (D)] ”.

=={{anchor|H390E1012701041A3A0A7E8CF4B29D7AE}}(b) Deployment assessments by medical professionals– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m Subsection (c) of such section] is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m paragraph (1)] , by striking“The mental health”and inserting“Except as provided by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m paragraph (3)] , the mental health”; and
:(2) by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>
::(3) 
:::(A) If the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] determines that a mental health assessment conducted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subsection (a)(1)(B)] cannot be performed by an individual described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m clause (i)] or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m (ii) of paragraph (1)(A)] , the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] shall ensure that the mental health assessment is conducted using an Internet-based questionnaire.
:::(B) After a member completes a questionnaire conducted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraph (A)] , the immediate commanding officer of the member shall complete a separate Internet-based questionnaire regarding the observations of the commanding officer with respect to the mental health of the member. The commanding officer may not have access to the questionnaire of the member conducted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraph (A)] .
:::(C) The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] shall ensure that the questionnaires conducted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraphs (A)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m (B)] to assess a member are combined and such combined document is reviewed and acted upon by personnel described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m paragraph (1)(A)] in the same manner as an assessment conducted pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraph (A)] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m (C)] , or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m (D) of subsection (a)(1)] .
:::(D) Nothing in this paragraph may be construed to treat a questionnaire conducted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraph (A)] or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subparagraph (B)] as being exempt from Department of Defense Directive 6025.18, a successor directive, or any other relevant regulation regarding privacy prescribed pursuant to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996([[Public Law 104-191|Public Law 104–191]] )..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H7E20771534684A9C88DDBB9DA7B42058}}(c) Uniform standards assessments– ==

Such section is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subsection (c)(1)(D)] , by striking“consistent”and inserting“uniform”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1074m subsection (f)(1)] , by adding at the end the following new sentence:“Such regulations shall ensure that this section is carried out in a uniform manner among the military departments.”.
