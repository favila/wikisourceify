{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide for congressional approval of national monuments and restrictions on the use of national monuments, to establish requirements for the declaration of marine national monuments, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Improved National Monument Designation Process Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Designation of national monuments=

The Act of June 8, 1906(commonly known as the“  Antiquities Act of 1906  ”) ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/431 16 U.S.C. 431 et seq.] ), is amended—
:(1) in  section 2 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/431 16 U.S.C. 431] )—
::(A) by striking“ Sec. 2.That the President is hereby authorized, in his discretion to”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

===Sec. 2. Designation of national monuments===



====(a) In general– ====

After obtaining congressional approval of the proposed national monument and certifying compliance withthe  National Environmental Policy Act of 1969  ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] )with respect to the proposed national monument and subject to subsection (b) , the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]may ; and 
</blockquote>

::(B) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

====(b) Requirements for declaration of marine national monuments– ====



=====(1) Definition of exclusive economic zone– =====

In this subsection, the term“exclusive economic zone”means the zone established by Proclamation Number 5030, dated March 10, 1983 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1453 16 U.S.C. 1453 note]).

=====(2) Requirements– =====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]may not declare any area of the exclusive economic zone to be a national monument unless—
:(A) the declaration is specifically authorized by an Act of Congress;
:(B) the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]has submitted to the Governor of each State and each territory, any part of which is located within 100 nautical miles of the proposed national monument, a proposal to make the declaration;
:(C) the Governor of each State and territory described in subparagraph (B) submits to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]notice that the legislature of the State or territory has approved the proposal submitted under that paragraph; and
:(D) the declaration is substantially the same as the proposal submitted under subparagraph (B) . ; and 
</blockquote>

:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 5. Restrictions on public use==

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]], or the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]], with respect to any area of the exclusive economic zone (as defined in section 2(b)(1) ) designated as a national monument, shall not implement any restrictions on the public use of a national monument until the expiration of an appropriate review period (as determined by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]]or the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]], as applicable) providing for public input and congressional approval. . 
</blockquote>
