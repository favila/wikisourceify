{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 44, United States Code, to require information on contributors to Presidential library fundraising organizations, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H40A2550DE7A24F5198E7732D0E599201}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Presidential Library Donation Reform Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HC81DE1272DCF4181A2111BF69FB05C12}}Sec. 2. Presidential libraries=



=={{anchor|HC1DD514C197F47889A62ACF1A025E80C}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 2112] of title 44, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H551B96B3332D40119B2AD8ABE5944829}}(h) Presidential library fundraising organization reporting requirement– ===



===={{anchor|HCE6E9AB98D8346F99921E714D861F10B}}(1) Reporting requirement– ====

Not later than 15 days after the end of a calendar quarter and until the end of the requirement period described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 paragraph (2)] , each Presidential library fundraising organization shall submit to the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] information for that quarter in an electronic searchable and sortable format with respect to every contributor who gave the organization a contribution or contributions (whether monetary or in-kind) totaling $200 or more for the quarterly period.

===={{anchor|HFC030BE4BECA478EB1174461EF54223E}}(2) Duration of reporting requirement– ====

The requirement to submit information under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 paragraph (1)] shall continue until the later of the following occurs:
:(A) The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] has accepted, taken title to, or entered into an agreement to use any land or facility for the Presidential archival depository for the [[w:President of the United States|President]] for whom the Presidential library fundraising organization was established.
:(B) The [[w:President of the United States|President]] whose archives are contained in the deposit no longer holds the Office of President.

===={{anchor|H6AB88B6E140F470C9DD6FE29E16172F2}}(3) Information required to be published– ====

The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall publish on the website of the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|National Archives and Records Administration]] , within 30 days after each quarterly filing, any information that is submitted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 paragraph (1)] , without a fee or other access charge, in a searchable, sortable, and downloadable database.

===={{anchor|H4D2ABE1A3A6448928F37EECD83EE7814}}(4) Submission of false material information prohibited– ====



====={{anchor|H79B18E18AF134B4DB218AB79F8909454}}(A) Individual– =====



======{{anchor|HC20498CD39454FAD93269BE984278FDB}}(i) Prohibition– ======

It shall be unlawful for any person who makes a contribution described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 paragraph (1)] to knowingly and willfully submit false material information or omit material information with respect to the contribution to an organization described in such paragraph.

======{{anchor|HD696D06DD24446DC8347597DF0979676}}(ii) Penalty– ======

The penalties described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1001 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 1001] of title 18, United States Code] , shall apply with respect to a violation of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 clause (i)] in the same manner as a violation described in such section.

====={{anchor|HD890184EC5BA400A85790A1216578FFA}}(B) Organization– =====



======{{anchor|HDFB5C9CD78024202B4B24F0413AA309F}}(i) Prohibition– ======

It shall be unlawful for any Presidential library fundraising organization to knowingly and willfully submit false material information or omit material information under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 paragraph (1)] .

======{{anchor|H880FAD6A4E5C4CD99C98CC3807F189CE}}(ii) Penalty– ======

The penalties described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1001 section 1001 of title 18, United States Code] , shall apply with respect to a violation of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 clause (i)] in the same manner as a violation described in such section.

===={{anchor|H0BCA7BD0B7FA44E183C9A6D539861237}}(5) Prohibition on contribution– ====



====={{anchor|H38E00B8A3A0E4FEAAC93F7AD3D7DC1AC}}(A) In general– =====

It shall be unlawful for a person to knowingly and willfully—
:(i) make a contribution described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 paragraph (1)] in the name of another person;
:(ii) permit his or her name to be used to effect a contribution described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 paragraph (1)] ; or
:(iii) accept a contribution described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 paragraph (1)] that is made by one person in the name of another person.

====={{anchor|H04B5442F68604BB78C6AF7A4E38EE0EC}}(B) Penalty– =====

The penalties set forth in section 309(d) of the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/437g 2 U.S.C. 437g(d)] ] )shall apply to a violation of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 subparagraph (A)] in the same manner as if such violation were a violation of section 316(b)(3) of such Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/441b 2 U.S.C. 441b(b)(3)] ] ).

===={{anchor|H2357B1523DA14C83AA8B7D985F0E7DF8}}(6) Regulations required– ====

The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall promulgate regulations for the purpose of carrying out this subsection.

===={{anchor|HB7C1166225EC43F4A920CEE26B4F4116}}(7) Definitions– ====

In this subsection:

====={{anchor|H18BAC3B0B6C441E99A363B5485E2C4AD}}(A) Information– =====

The term“information”means the following:
:(i) The amount or value of each contribution made by a contributor referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 paragraph (1)] in the quarter covered by the submission.
:(ii) The source of each such contribution, and the address of the entity or individual that is the source of the contribution.
:(iii) If the source of such a contribution is an individual, the occupation of the individual.
:(iv) The date of each such contribution.

====={{anchor|H4BA82867833A4B01918855091A18607F}}(B) Presidential library fundraising organization– =====

The term“Presidential library fundraising organization”means an organization that is established for the purpose of raising funds for creating, maintaining, expanding, or conducting activities at—
:(i) a Presidential archival depository; or
:(ii) any facilities relating to a Presidential archival depository..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H608C64B8855D4290BFD37E52BAB9A4BF}}(b) Applicability– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2112 Section 2112(h) of title 44, United States Code] (as added by subsection (a))—
:(1) shall apply to an organization established for the purpose of raising funds for creating, maintaining, expanding, or conducting activities at a Presidential archival depository or any facilities relating to a Presidential archival depository before, on, or after the date of the enactment of this Act; and
:(2) shall only apply with respect to contributions (whether monetary or in-kind) made after the date of the enactment of this Act.
