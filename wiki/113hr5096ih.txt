{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 18, United States Code, to clarify and expand Federal criminal jurisdiction over Federal contractors and employees outside the United States, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “   Civilian Ex­tra­ter­ri­torial Jurisdiction Act of 2014   ”or the“   CEJA   ”.  

=Sec. 2. Clarification and expansion of Federal jurisdiction over Federal contractors and employees=



==(a) Extraterritorial jurisdiction over Federal contractors and employees– ==



===(1) In general– ===

  Chapter 212Aof title 18, United States Code, is amended—
:(A) by transferring the text of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272 section 3272]to the end of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3271 section 3271], redesignating such text as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3271 subsection (c) of section 3271], and, in such text, as so redesignated, by striking“this chapter”and inserting“this section”;
:(B) by striking the heading of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272 section 3272]; and
:(C) by adding after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3271 section 3271], as amended by this paragraph, the following new sections:

<blockquote> 

====Sec. 3272. Offenses committed by Federal contractors and employees outside the United States====


:(a) 
::(1) Whoever, while employed by any department or agency of the United States other than the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]or accompanying any department or agency of the United States other than the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]], knowingly engages in conduct (or conspires or attempts to engage in conduct) outside the United States that would constitute an offense enumerated in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272 paragraph (3)]had the conduct been engaged in within the special maritime and territorial jurisdiction of the United States shall be punished as provided for that offense.
::(2) A prosecution may not be commenced against a person under this subsection if a foreign government, in accordance with jurisdiction recognized by the United States, has prosecuted or is prosecuting such person for the conduct constituting the offense, except upon the approval of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]or the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Deputy Attorney General]](or a person acting in either such capacity), which function of approval may not be delegated.
::(3) The offenses covered by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272 paragraph (1)]are the following:
:::(A) Any offense underchapter 5 (arson) of this title.
:::(B) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/111 section 111 (assaulting, resisting, or impeding certain officers or employees)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/113 113 (assault within maritime and territorial jurisdiction)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/114 114 (maiming within maritime and territorial jurisdiction) of this title], but only if the offense is subject to a maximum sentence of imprisonment of one year or more.
:::(C) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/201 section 201 (bribery of public officials and witnesses) of this title].
:::(D) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/499 section 499 (military, naval, or official passes) of this title].
:::(E) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/701 section 701 (official badges, identification cards, and other insignia)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/702 702](uniform of armed forces and [[w:United States Public Health Service|Public Health Service]]),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/703 703 (uniform of friendly nation)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/704 704 (military medals or decorations) of this title].
:::(F) Any offense underchapter 41 (extortion and threats) of this title, but only if the offense is subject to a maximum sentence of imprisonment of three years or more.
:::(G) Any offense underchapter 42(extortionate credit transactions) of this title.
:::(H) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 section 924(c)](use of firearm in violent or drug trafficking crime) or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 924(o)](conspiracy to violate[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 section 924(c)) of this title].
:::(I) Any offense underchapter 50A(genocide) of this title.
:::(J) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1111 section 1111](murder),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1112 1112](manslaughter),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1113 1113](attempt to commit murder or manslaughter),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1114 1114](protection of officers and employees of the United States),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1116 1116](murder or manslaughter of foreign officials, official guests, or internationally protected persons),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1117 1117](conspiracy to commit murder), or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1119 1119](foreign murder of United States nationals) of this title.
:::(K) Any offense underchapter 55(kidnapping) of this title.
:::(L) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1503 section 1503](influencing or injuring officer or juror generally),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1505 1505](obstruction of proceedings before departments, agencies, and committees),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1510 1510](obstruction of criminal investigations),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1512 1512](tampering with a witness, victim, or informant), or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1513 1513](retaliating against a witness, victim, or an informant) of this title.
:::(M) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1951 section 1951](interference with commerce by threats or violence),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1952 1952](interstate and foreign travel or transportation in aid of racketeering enterprises),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1956 1956](laundering of monetary instruments),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1957 1957](engaging in monetary transactions in property derived from specified unlawful activity),[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1958 1958](use of interstate commerce facilities in the commission of murder for hire), or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1959 1959](violent crimes in aid of racketeering activity) of this title.
:::(N) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2111 section 2111](robbery or burglary within special maritime and territorial jurisdiction) of this title.
:::(O) Any offense underchapter 109A(sexual abuse) of this title.
:::(P) Any offense underchapter 113B(terrorism) of this title.
:::(Q) Any offense underchapter 113C(torture) of this title.
:::(R) Any offense underchapter 115(treason, sedition, and subversive activities) of this title.
:::(S) Any offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2442 section 2442](child soldiers) of this title.
:::(T) Any offense under section 401 (manufacture, distribution, or possession with intent to distribute a controlled substance) or 408 (continuing criminal enterprise) of the Controlled Substances Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/841 21 U.S.C. 841] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/848 848]), or under section 1002 (importation of controlled substances), 1003 (exportation of controlled substances), or 1010 (import or export of a controlled substance) of the Controlled Substances Import and Export Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/952 21 U.S.C. 952] ,[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/953 953],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/960 960]), but only if the offense is subject to a maximum sentence of imprisonment of 20 years or more.
:(b) In addition to the jurisdiction under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272 subsection (a)], whoever, while employed by any department or agency of the United States other than the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]and stationed or deployed in a country outside of the United States pursuant to a treaty or executive agreement in furtherance of a border security initiative with that country, engages in conduct (or conspires or attempts to engage in conduct) outside the United States that would constitute an offense for which a person may be prosecuted in a court of the United States had the conduct been engaged in within the special maritime and territorial jurisdiction of the United States shall be punished as provided for that offense.
:(c) In this section:
::(1) The term employed by any department or agency of the United States other than the  means—
:::(A) an individual is—
::::(i) employed as a civilian employee, a contractor (including a subcontractor at any tier), an employee of a contractor (or a subcontractor at any tier), a grantee (including a contractor of a grantee or a subgrantee or subcontractor at any tier), or an employee of a grantee (or a contractor of a grantee or a subgrantee or subcontractor at any tier) of any department or agency of the United States other than the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]];
::::(ii) present or residing outside the United States in connection with such employment; and
::::(iii) not a national of or ordinarily resident in the host nation; and
:::(B) in the case of an individual who is such a contractor, contractor employee, grantee, or grantee employee, such employment supports a program, project, or activity for a department or agency of the United States.
::(2) The term accompanying any department or agency of the United States other than the  means an individual is—
:::(A) a dependant, family member, or member of household of—
::::(i) a civilian employee of any department or agency of the United States other than the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]]; or
::::(ii) a contractor (including a subcontractor at any tier), an employee of a contractor (or a subcontractor at any tier), a grantee (including a contractor of a grantee or a subgrantee or subcontractor at any tier), or an employee of a grantee (or a contractor of a grantee or a subgrantee or subcontractor at any tier) of any department or agency of the United States other than the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]], which contractor, contractor employee, grantee, or grantee employee is supporting a program, project, or activity for a department or agency of the United States other than the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]];
:::(B) residing with such civilian employee, contractor, contractor employee, grantee, or grantee employee outside the United States; and
:::(C) not a national of or ordinarily resident in the host nation.
::(3) The term grant agreement means a legal instrument described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/6304 section6304 ]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/6305  6305of title 31], other than an agreement between the United States and a State, local, or foreign government or an international organization.
::(4) The term grantee means a party, other than the United States, to a grant agreement.
::(5) The term host nation means the country outside of the United States where the employee or contractor resides, the country where the employee or contractor commits the alleged offense at issue, or both. 

====Sec. 3273. Regulations====

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]], after consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]], the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]], and the [[w:|Director of National Intelligence]], shall prescribe regulations governing the investigation, apprehension, detention, delivery, and removal of persons described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3271 sections 3271]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272 3272 of this title]. . 
</blockquote>

:(A) employed as a civilian employee, a contractor (including a subcontractor at any tier), or an employee of a contractor (or a subcontractor at any tier) of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]](including a nonappropriated fund instrumentality of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department]]); .

==(b) Venue– ==

  Chapter 211of title 18, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 3245. Optional venue for offenses involving Federal employees and contractors overseas==

In addition to any venue otherwise provided in this chapter, the trial of any offense involving a violation of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3261 section 3261],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3271 3271], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272 3272 of this title]may be brought—
:(1) in the district in which is headquartered the department or agency of the United States that employs the offender, or any 1 of 2 or more joint offenders; or
:(2) in the district in which is headquartered the department or agency of the United States that the offender is accompanying, or that any 1 of 2 or more joint offenders is accompanying. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Suspension of statute of limitations– ==

  Chapter 213of title 18, United States Code, is amended by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3287 section 3287] the following new section:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 3287A. Suspension of limitations for offenses involving Federal employees and contractors overseas==

The statute of limitations for an offense under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272 section 3272 of this title]shall be suspended for the period during which the individual is outside the United States or is a fugitive from justice within the meaning of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3290 section 3290 of this title]. . 
</blockquote>

=Sec. 3. Investigative task forces for contractor and employee oversight=



==(a) Establishment of investigative task forces for contractor and employee oversight– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]], in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]], the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]], and the head of any other department or agency of the Federal Government responsible for employing contractors or persons overseas, shall assign adequate personnel and resources, including through the creation of task forces, to investigate allegations of criminal offenses under chapter 212Aof title 18, United States Code(as amended by section 2(a) of this Act ), and may authorize the overseas deployment of law enforcement agents and other employees of the Federal Government for that purpose.

==(b) Responsibilities of attorney general– ==



===(1) Investigation– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]shall have principal authority for the enforcement of this Act and the amendments made by this Act, and shall have the authority to initiate, conduct, and supervise investigations of any alleged offense under this Act or an amendment made by this Act.

===(2) Law enforcement authority– ===

With respect to violations of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3271 sections3271 ]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272  3272of title 18, United States Code](as amended by section 2(a) of this Act ), the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]may authorize any person serving in a law enforcement position in any other department or agency of the Federal Government, including a member of the [[w:United States Department of State|Diplomatic Security Service of the Department of State]]or a military police officer of the Armed Forces, to exercise investigative and law enforcement authority, including those powers that may be exercised under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3052 section 3052 of title 18, United States Code], subject to such guidelines or policies as the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]considers appropriate for the exercise of such powers.

===(3) Prosecution– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]may establish such procedures the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]considers appropriate to ensure that Federal law enforcement agencies refer offenses under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3271 section 3271]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272 3272 of title 18, United States Code](as amended by section 2(a) of this Act ), to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]for prosecution in a uniform and timely manner.

===(4) Assistance on request of attorney general– ===

Notwithstanding any statute, rule, or regulation to the contrary, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]may request assistance from the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]], or the head of any other department or agency of the Federal Government to enforce[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3271 section3271 ]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3272  3272of title 18, United States Code](as so amended). The assistance requested may include the following:
:(A) The assignment of additional employees and resources to task forces established by the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]under subsection (a) .
:(B) An investigation into alleged misconduct or arrest of an individual suspected of alleged misconduct by agents of the [[w:United States Department of State|Diplomatic Security Service of the Department of State]]present in the nation in which the alleged misconduct occurs.

===(5) Annual report– ===

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, and annually thereafter for 5 years, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]shall, in consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]], the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]], and the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]], submit to Congress a report containing the following:
:(A) The number of prosecutions under chapter 212Aof title 18, United States Code(as amended by section 2(a) of this Act ), including the nature of the offenses and any dispositions reached, during the previous year.
:(B) The actions taken to implement subsection (a) , including the organization and training of employees and the use of task forces, during the previous year.
:(C) Such recommendations for legislative or administrative action as the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]considers appropriate to enforce chapter 212Aof title 18, United States Code(as amended by section 2(a) of this Act ), and the provisions of this section.

==(c) Definitions– ==

In this section, the terms agency and department have the meanings given such terms in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/6  section 6of title 18, United States Code].

==(d) Rule of construction– ==

Nothing in this section shall be construed to limit any authority of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]or any Federal law enforcement agency to investigate violations of Federal law or deploy employees overseas. 

=Sec. 4. Effective date=



==(a) Immediate effectiveness– ==

This Act and the amendments made by this Act shall take effect on the date of enactment of this Act.

==(b) Implementation– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]and the head of any other department or agency of the Federal Government to which this Act or an amendment made by this Act applies shall have 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act to ensure compliance with this Act and the amendments made by this Act. 

=Sec. 5. Rules of construction=



==(a) In general– ==

Nothing in this Act or any amendment made by this Act shall be construed—
:(1) to limit or affect the application of ex­tra­ter­ri­tor­i­al jurisdiction related to any other Federal law; or
:(2) to limit or affect any authority or responsibility of a [[w:United States Department of State|Chief of Mission]]as provided in  section 207 of the Foreign Service Act of 1980 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/3927 22 U.S.C. 3927] ).

==(b) Intelligence activities– ==

Nothing in this Act or any amendment made by this Act shall apply to the authorized intelligence activities of the United States Government. 

=Sec. 6. Funding=

If any amounts are appropriated to carry out this Act or an amendment made by this Act, the amounts shall be from amounts which would have otherwise been made available or appropriated to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]].
