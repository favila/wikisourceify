{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 18, United States Code, to strengthen enforcement of spousal court-ordered property distributions, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H79B64EAC8BB14974A21D4B39F5373021}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Jane’s Law”.

={{anchor|H0D81BE3F0FC146DA98873D9EC38D1676}}Sec. 2. Spousal obligations=



=={{anchor|H03AEB8F27DD44DA0B837559C8382BEDA}}(a) In General– ==

Chapter 11A of title 18, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H4D374943C36B4FFA8A0CAA11CC195EFD}}Sec. 228A. Failure to pay legal spousal court-ordered property distribution==



==={{anchor|H28AE4D2A5C22466C92AFB822926A7308}}(a) Offense– ===

Whoever knowingly, travels in interstate or foreign commerce, with the intent to evade compliance with a court ordered property distribution as part of a separation or divorce settlement involving more than $5,000, with respect to a spouse or former spouse, shall be fined under this title or imprisoned for not more than two years or both.

==={{anchor|H8D0E3D2336A54DC9925CCDA4478B6724}}(b) Mandatory Restitution– ===

Upon a conviction under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/228A this section] , the court shall order restitution under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/3663A section 3663A] in an amount equal to the total unpaid property distribution.

==={{anchor|HF3CD99ABFD7248918A139207743926BE}}(c) Definition– ===

The term court ordered property distribution means any amount determined under a court order pursuant to the law of a State or of an Indian tribe to be due from a spouse or former spouse as an equitable or other distribution to a spouse or former spouse of assets in connection with a separation or divorce..
</blockquote>
