{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to establish a presumption of service connection for mental health conditions related to military sexual trauma.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Military Sexual Trauma Claims Administration Reform and Eligibility Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Presumption of service connection for mental health conditions related to military sexual trauma=



==(a) Presumption– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 Subchapter II ofchapter 11of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote> 

===Sec. 1119. Presumption of service connection for mental health conditions related to military sexual trauma===



====(a) In general– ====

For purposes of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1110 section 1110 of this title], and subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1113 section 1113 of this title], a mental health condition of a veteran that a veteran certifies to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]as being incurred in or aggravated by military sexual trauma shall be considered to have been incurred in or aggravated by the service of the veteran, notwithstanding that there is no record of evidence of such mental health condition or such military sexual trauma occurring during such service.

====(b) Military sexual trauma defined– ====

In this section, the term military sexual trauma means, with respect to a veteran, psychological trauma, which in the judgment of a mental health professional, resulted from a physical assault of a sexual nature, battery of a sexual nature, or sexual harassment (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1720D section 1720D of this title]) which occurred during a period of serving in the Armed Forces on active duty or active duty for training. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Clerical amendment– ===

The table of sections at the beginning ofchapter 11 of such titleis amended by inserting after the item relating to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1118 section 1118]the following new item:

<blockquote>  1119. Presumption of service connection for mental health conditions related to military sexual trauma.  . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Presumption rebuttable– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1113  Section 1113of title 38, United States Code], is amended by striking“or 1118”each place it appears and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1118 1118], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1119 1119] ”.

==(c) Presumption during peacetime service– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1137  Section 1137of title 38, United States Code], is amended by striking“and 1113”and inserting“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1113 1113], and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1119 1119] ”.
