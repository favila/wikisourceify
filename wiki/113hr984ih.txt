{{Center|A BILL}}

To direct the Secretary of Defense to establish a task force on urotrauma.

=Section 1. Task force on Urotrauma=



==(a) Establishment– ==

Subject to the availability of appropriations for such purpose, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] shall establish a task force to be known as the “ [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force on Urotrauma]] ” (in this section referred to as the “Task Force” ) to continue and expand the report of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary]] on urotrauma titled “Genitourinary Trauma in the Military” and dated December 27, 2011.

==(b) Consultation– ==

In carrying out this section, the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] shall consult with the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] and the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] .

==(c) Duties– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall conduct a study on urotrauma among members of the Armed Forces and veterans, including—
:(1) an analysis of the incidence, duration, morbidity rate, and mortality rate of urotrauma;
:(2) an analysis of the social and economic costs and effects of urotrauma;
:(3) with respect to the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Department of Defense]] and [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]] , an evaluation of the facilities, access to private facilities, resources, personnel, and research activities that are related to the diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of urotrauma;
:(4) an evaluation of programs (including such biological, behavioral, environmental, and social programs) that improve the prevention or treatment of urotrauma;
:(5) a long-term plan for the use and organization of the resources of the Federal Government to improve the prevention and treatment of urotrauma;
:(6) an analysis of shortfalls in research, expertise, and health care infrastructure for female victims of urotrauma;
:(7) an analysis of technical, administrative, and budgetary mechanisms to allow for enhanced reproductive services for members who have been affected by urotrauma or who are at high risk of urotrauma;
:(8) an assessment of opportunities to enhance the coordination of—
::(A) Federal resources used to research, prevent, and continuously improve the management of urotrauma; and
::(B) inter-agency efforts regarding the chronic physical, behavioral, and emotional care of victims of urotrauma; and
:(9) updates to the report referred to in subsection (a).

==(d) Membership– ==



===(1) Appointed members– ===

In addition to the ex officio members described in paragraph (2), the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall be composed of 19 members as follows:
:(A) Sixteen members appointed by the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] .
:(B) One member appointed by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]] from among officers or employees of the [[w:National Institutes of Health|National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases]] whose primary interest is in the field of urotrauma.
:(C) The [[w:21st Theater Army Area Command|Chief of the Department of Surgery of Walter Reed National Military Medical Center]] .
:(D) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Chief Medical Director of the Department of Veterans Affairs]] .

===(2) Ex officio members– ===

The nonvoting, ex officio members of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] are as follows:
:(A) The [[w:United States Department of the Navy|Surgeon General of the Navy]] .
:(B) The [[w:Surgeon General|Surgeon General of the Army]] .
:(C) The [[w:United States Department of the Air Force|Surgeon General of the Air Force]] .
:(D) The [[w:United States Marine Corps|Medical Officer of the Marine Corps]] .
:(E) The [[w:National Institutes of Health|Director of the National Institutes of Health]] .
:(F) The [[w:National Institutes of Health|Director of the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases]] .
:(G) The [[w:National Institutes of Health|Director of the Division of Kidney, Urologic, and Hematologic Diseases of the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive Kidney Diseases]] .
:(H) The [[w:National Institutes of Health|Director of the National Institute of Biomedical Imaging and Bioengineering]] .

===(3) Qualifications– ===

In appointing members under paragraph (1)(A), the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] shall appoint individuals with experience related to—
:(A) studying or researching urotrauma;
:(B) preventing or treating urotrauma; or
:(C) suffering from urotrauma.

===(4) Term– ===

Each member shall be appointed for the life of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] .

===(5) Vacancies– ===

A vacancy in the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall be filled in the manner in which the original appointment was made.

===(6) Pay– ===


:(A) Except as provided in subparagraph (C), members of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall serve without pay.
:(B) Except as provided in subparagraph (C), members of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] who are full-time officers or employees of the United States may not receive additional pay, allowances, or benefits by reason of their service on the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] .
:(C) Each member shall receive travel expenses, including per diem in lieu of subsistence, in accordance with applicable provisions undersubchapter I of chapter 57 of title 5, United States Code.

===(7) Quorum– ===

A majority of members of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall constitute a quorum but a lesser number may hold hearings.

===(8) Chairperson– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] shall designate a member as the chairperson of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] .

===(9) Meetings– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall meet at the call of the chairperson.

==(e) Staff– ==



===(1) Director– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall have a director who shall be appointed by the chairperson.

===(2) Staff– ===

Subject to rules prescribed by the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] , the chairperson may appoint additional personnel as the chairperson considers appropriate.

===(3) Applicability of certain civil service laws– ===

The director and staff of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall be appointed subject to the provisions of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/ title 5, United States Code] , governing appointments in the competitive service, and shall be paid in accordance with the provisions ofchapter 51andsubchapter III of chapter 53 of that titlerelating to classification and General Schedule pay rates.

===(4) Experts and consultants– ===

Subject to rules prescribed by the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] , the chairperson may procure temporary and intermittent services under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3109 section 3109(b) of title 5, United States Code] .

===(5) Staff to federal agencies– ===

Upon request of the chairperson, the head of any Federal department or agency may detail, on a reimbursable basis, any of the personnel of that department or agency to the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] to assist it in carrying out its duties under this section.

==(f) Powers of Task Force– ==



===(1) Hearings and sessions– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] may, for the purpose of carrying out this section, hold hearings, sit and act at times and places, take testimony, and receive evidence as the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] considers appropriate. The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] may administer oaths or affirmations to witnesses appearing before it.

===(2) Powers of Members and agents– ===

Any member or agent of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] may, if authorized by the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] , take any action which the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] is authorized to take by this section.

===(3) Obtaining official data– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] may secure directly from any department or agency of the United States information necessary to enable it to carry out this section. Upon request of the chairperson of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] , the head of that department or agency shall furnish that information to the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] .

===(4) Mails– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] may use the United States mails in the same manner and under the same conditions as other departments and agencies of the United States.

===(5) Administrative support services– ===

Upon the request of the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] , the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]] shall provide to the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] , on a reimbursable basis, the administrative support services necessary for the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] to carry out its responsibilities under this section.

==(g) Reports– ==



===(1) Interim report– ===

Not later than one year after the date on which the members are appointed under subsection (d)(1), the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall submit to the appropriate congressional committees an interim report on the study conducted under subsection (c).

===(2) Final report– ===

Not later than two years after the date on which the members are appointed under subsection (d)(1), the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall submit to the appropriate congressional committees a final report on the study conducted under subsection (c), including any recommendations the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] considers appropriate to improve the prevention and treatment of urotrauma among members of the Armed Forces and veterans.

==(h) Termination– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] shall terminate on the date that is 60 days after the date on which the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Task Force]] submits the final report under subsection (g)(2).

==(i) Definitions– ==

In this section:
:(1) The term appropriate congressional committees means—
::(A) the [[w:United States House Committee on Armed Services|Committees on Armed Services of the House of Representatives]] and [[w:United States Senate Committee on Armed Services|Senate]] ; and
::(B) the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the House of Representatives]] and [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Senate]] .
:(2) The term urotrauma means injury to the urinary tract (including the kidneys, ureters, urinary bladder, urethra, and female and male genitalia) from a penetrating, blunt, blast, thermal, chemical, or biological cause.

==(j) Authorization of Appropriations– ==



===(1) Authorization– ===

There is authorized to be appropriatedto carry out this section$1,000,000 for each of fiscal years 2014 through 2017.

===(2) Offset– ===

The amount otherwise authorized to be appropriated for operation and maintenance, Defense-wide, for the [[w:Office of the Secretary of Defense|Office of the Secretary of Defense]] for each of fiscal years 2014 through 2017 is reduced by $1,000,000.
