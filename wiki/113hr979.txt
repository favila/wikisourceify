{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Farm Security and Rural
		  Investment Act of 2002 to modify the definition of the term biobased
		  product.

={{anchor|H62D2ED81FF2B43F69D0E1EBE9E7A299A}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Forest Products Fairness Act of
			 2013”.

={{anchor|H5433C193B83B42EE89177F01C7F6B2A7}}Sec. 2. Definition of
			 biobased product=

Section 9001
			 of the Farm Security and Rural Investment Act of 2002 (7 U.S.C. 8101) is
			 amended—
:(1) by striking
			 paragraph (4) and inserting the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|id7A1FC3BDE70E42728F168B2C58769CF2}}(4) Biobased
				product– ===



===={{anchor|id96611FCC661C4D0296A47B0E6A045F84}}(A) In
				general– ====

The term biobased product means a product
				determined by the [[Wikipedia:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] to be a commercial or industrial product (other
				than food or feed) that is—
:(i) composed, in
				whole or in significant part, of biological products, including renewable
				domestic agricultural materials and forestry materials; or
:(ii) an intermediate
				ingredient or feedstock.

===={{anchor|id0D470B3B80E043E0BB859C006345F4B9}}(B) Inclusion– ====

The
				term biobased product, with respect to forestry materials,
				includes forest products that meet biobased content requirements,
				notwithstanding the market share the product holds, the age of the product, or
				whether the market for the product is new or
				emerging.;
</blockquote>

:(2) by redesignating
			 paragraphs (9) through (14) as paragraphs (10) through (15), respectively;
			 and
:(3) by inserting
			 after paragraph (8) the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|id8916D58B411540EE9029096C8BB995DB}}(9) Forest
				product– ===



===={{anchor|id5B9AB7477A21440481EA052926C1A34D}}(A) In
				general– ====

The term forest product means a product made
				from materials derived from the practice of forestry or the management of
				growing timber.

===={{anchor|idDCDDD7E63DA04CBCAD3E1F0A13886104}}(B) Inclusions– ====

The
				term forest product includes—
:(i) pulp, paper,
				paperboard, pellets, lumber and other wood products; and
:(ii) any recycled
				products derived from forest
				materials..
</blockquote>
