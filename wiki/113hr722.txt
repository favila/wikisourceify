={{anchor|HFB88297B8B804F8485F0482B932867C2}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the
			 “Detectives Nemorin and Andrews
			 Anti-Gun Trafficking Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HF2DBAFF0BBA74F9EA9005AEDB9E72BFC}}Sec. 2. Illegal gun
			 trafficking=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 Section 924 of
			 title 18, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the
			 following:

<blockquote>
:(q) Whoever, in or
				affecting interstate or foreign commerce in violation of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subsection (a)(1)(A)],
				[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (a)(3)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (a)(6)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (b)(2)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (b)(3)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (b)(5)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (d)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (g)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (i)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (j)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (k)], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (m)], or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (n) of
				section 922] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 subsection (c)] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 (h)] of this section—
::(1) offers for sale,
				transfer, or barter 2 or more firearms, at least 2 of which are handguns,
				semiautomatic assault weapons, short-barreled shotguns, short-barreled rifles,
				or machineguns; and
::(2) at least 1 of the
				firearms—
:::(A) is transported,
				received, or possessed by the person, and—
::::(i) is
				stolen; or
::::(ii) has had the
				importer’s or manufacturer’s serial number removed, obliterated, or altered;
				or
:::(B) is offered by the
				person for sale, transfer, or barter to another person who—
::::(i) is
				prohibited from possessing a firearm under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 subsection (g)] or [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 (n) of section
				922];
::::(ii) is prohibited by
				State law from possessing a firearm;
::::(iii) has not attained
				18 years of age, except as otherwise allowed under Federal or State law;
::::(iv) is in a school
				zone; or
::::(v) has travelled from
				any State into any other State, and acquires or attempts to acquire the firearm
				otherwise in violation of Federal or State law,shall be
				fined under this title, imprisoned not more than 20 years, or
				both..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|H471C6253331049C9AFE0875E6B17B44E}}Sec. 4. Report to the
			 Congress=

Beginning in calendar
			 year 2013, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall submit biennially to the [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a
			 written report, covering the preceding 2 years, which specifies—
:(1) the State of
			 origin for each firearm, used in a crime, that was traced by the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau of
			 Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives]], and the State in which the firearm
			 was recovered;
:(2) the total number
			 of firearms so traced, by manufacturer, model, and type of firearm;
:(3) the name of
			 Federal firearms licensees who have had more than 5 firearms, used in a crime,
			 traced back to them in a single year; and
:(4) the number of
			 prosecutions for each individual offense under sections [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 922], [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/923 923], and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 924 of
			 title 18, United States Code].

={{anchor|HD48A19A9867D4D06A3280BBAF8303A52}}Sec. 5. Additional
			 penalty for possession of a stolen firearm during the commission of a
			 felony=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/924 Section 924 of title
			 18, United States Code], as amended by section 2 of this Act, is amended by
			 adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(r) Whoever, during
				and in relation to the commission of a crime punishable by imprisonment for a
				term exceeding 1 year, receives, possesses, conceals, barters, sells, or
				disposes of any stolen firearm or stolen ammunition, in or affecting interstate
				or foreign commerce, whether or not the person is aware that the firearm or
				ammunition is stolen, shall, in addition to the punishment provided for the
				crime so punishable, be sentenced to a term of imprisonment of not more than 5
				years..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H50C3955ED3FB4B6EB2C49E71291D554E}}Sec. 6. National Crime
			 Information Center Stolen Gun File=



=={{anchor|H604E3D6DF4784338A2D32345D316087B}}(a) Availability– ==

The
			 [[w:Federal Bureau of Investigation|Federal Bureau of Investigation]] shall make available to the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau of Alcohol,
			 Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives]] the National Crime Information Center Gun
			 File for the purpose of enabling the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and
			 Explosives]] to access the file while completing a crime gun trace.

=={{anchor|H288175CFC8CB40EA8320A20F55586A3C}}(b) Use– ==

The
			 [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives]] shall conduct a search of
			 the National Crime Information Center Stolen Gun File with respect to each
			 firearm submitted to the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives]]
			 for tracing.

=={{anchor|H6E4F8A633D594D79ACB90505CD9D1699}}(c) Notification
			 regarding stolen firearms– ==

If a law enforcement agency requests
			 the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives]] to trace a firearm,
			 and the National Crime Information Center Stolen Gun File indicates that the
			 firearm is stolen, then the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and
			 Explosives]] shall notify the law enforcement agency of that information and
			 provide the law enforcement agency with any available information regarding the
			 owner of the firearm.

=={{anchor|HF706A1BC806C481E9C443FA067DB6E35}}(d) Return of stolen
			 firearms possessed by BATFE– ==

If the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco,
			 Firearms, and Explosives]] possesses a firearm which the National Crime
			 Information Center Stolen Gun File indicates is stolen, the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau]] shall return
			 the firearm to the person who reported the firearm stolen, when the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Bureau]]
			 determines that—
:(1) the firearm is no
			 longer needed for Federal, State, or local criminal investigation or
			 evidentiary purposes; and
:(2) the person is
			 entitled to possess the firearm.

=={{anchor|HEDC7463CBC2045E2BC1D6CC48CEECC06}}(e) National instant
			 stolen gun check system– ==



==={{anchor|HBF5D146553BC40AFB04B6E8D54017840}}(1) Establishment– ===

Within
			 2 years after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall
			 establish a national instant stolen gun check system that any licensee may
			 contact, by telephone or other electronic means, for information to be supplied
			 immediately on whether a firearm to be received by the licensee is
			 stolen.

==={{anchor|H33ACE12108014452A19E2253A78ECF19}}(2) Instant check of
			 status of firearms to be transferred to certain Federal firearms licensees by
			 non-licensees– ===

If the national
			 instant stolen gun check system is contacted by a licensee for information on
			 whether a firearm to be received by the licensee is stolen, the system shall,
			 as soon as is practicable—
:(A) conduct a search of the National Crime
			 Information Center Stolen Gun File for information about the firearm;
			 and
:(B) inform the
			 licensee whether the information available to the system indicates that the
			 firearm is stolen.

==={{anchor|H13E081DD6D0A474C95C867AA5E227D80}}(3) Notification of
			 licensees– ===

On establishment of
			 the national instant stolen gun check system under this subsection, the
			 [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall notify each licensee and the chief law enforcement
			 officer of each State of the existence and purpose of the system and the means
			 to be used to contact the system.

==={{anchor|HDF7DDD54ED4E482697B6AE0D80345FA2}}(4) Permanent
			 retention of records– ===

The
			 national instant stolen gun check system shall create and maintain permanently
			 a record of each contact of the system, and all information provided to or by
			 the system during the contact.

==={{anchor|H8631AFDA50584663B84C0BA97F9A0C75}}(5) Definitions– ===

In this section:

===={{anchor|H530EC65E850249028E48F88FFE6A05A0}}(A) Licensee– ====

The term licensee means a
			 licensed dealer (as defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 section 921(a)(11) of title 18, United States
			 Code]), licensed importer (as defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 section 921(a)(9) of such title]), or
			 licensed manufacturer (as defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 section 921(a)(10) of such title]).

===={{anchor|HBC5F3056B6034032AF72B4D549E00426}}(B) Firearm– ====

The
			 term firearm has the meaning given in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/921 section 921(a)(3) of title
			 18, United States Code].
