{{Center|A BILL}}

 To establish a grant program in the Department of Education to promote the involvement of female students in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Advancing Girls in STEM Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Findings=

  [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]finds the following:
:(1) According to a September 2013 report by the [[w:Bureau of the Census|United States Census Bureau]], only 26 percent of employees in STEM fields were women, while 74 percent were men.
:(2) A [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Department of Commerce]]report states that employees in STEM fields earn 26 percent more than their non-STEM counterparts.
:(3) Women in STEM jobs earn 33 percent more than comparable women in non-STEM jobs.
:(4) The United States ranks 25th in mathematics and 17th in science among industrialized nations.
:(5) According to the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department of Labor]], only 15 percent of the world’s scientists reside in the United States and only 5 percent of United States workers are employed in fields related to science and engineering. 

=Sec. 3. Definitions=

For purposes of this Act, the following definitions shall apply:

==(1) State– ==

The term State means each State of the United States and the District of Columbia.

==(2) STEM– ==

The term STEM means science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.

==(3) Elementary School– ==

The term elementary school has the meaning given such term by  section 9101 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/7801 20 U.S.C. 7801] ).

==(4) Secondary School– ==

The term secondary school has the meaning given such term by  section 9101 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/7801 20 U.S.C. 7801] ).

==(5) Secretary– ==

The term Secretary means the [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary of the United States Department of Education]]. 

=Sec. 4. Grant program=



==(a) Grants Authorized– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary]]shall award grants to States to carry out the programs described in subsection (b) .

==(b) Use of Funds– ==

A State that receives a grant under this section shall use the grant to carry out programs that promote the involvement of female students at the elementary school and secondary school levels in STEM fields.

==(c) Award Allocation and Amount– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary]]shall award grants to States, on a competitive basis, in accordance with the following criteria:

===(1) Geographic Allocation– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary]]shall award 1 grant to each of 2 States in each of the following geographic regions:

====(A) South– ====

Alabama, Florida, Georgia, Kentucky, Louisiana, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas, and Virginia.

====(B) Midwest– ====

Arkansas, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, Nebraska, Ohio, Oklahoma, South Dakota, and Wisconsin.

====(C) Northeast– ====

Connecticut, Delaware, the District of Columbia, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Vermont, and West Virginia.

====(D) West– ====

Alaska, Arizona, California, Colorado, Hawaii, Idaho, Montana, Nevada, New Mexico, North Dakota, Oregon, Utah, Washington, and Wyoming.

===(2) Amount– ===

The amount of each grant shall be $2,500,000.

===(3) Deadline for Distribution– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary]]shall distribute grant funds in accordance with this section not later than the date that is 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act.

==(d) Application– ==

To be eligible for a grant under this Act, a State shall submit an application to the [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary]]at such time, in such manner, and containing such information as the [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary]]may reasonably require.

==(e) Reports– ==



===(1) States– ===

As a condition of receiving a grant under this Act, a State shall agree to submit a report to the [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary]], on an annual basis, identifying how the grant funds were used and the number of students who participated in the programs carried out with the grant funds.

===(2) Secretary– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Education|Secretary]]shall, on an annual basis, and using the reports received under paragraph (1) , report to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]on the overall impact and effectiveness of the grant program under this Act. 

=Sec. 5. Transfer of Funds for Grant Program=

Of amounts made available to the [[w:National Nuclear Security Administration|Office of Nuclear Warhead Protection in the National Nuclear Security Administration]]not obligated as of the date of enactment of this Act, there is transferred $20,000,000 to the [[w:United States Department of Education|Department of Education]]to carry out this Act.
