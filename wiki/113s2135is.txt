{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to clarify that products derived from tar sands are crude oil for purposes of the Federal excise tax on petroleum, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Tar Sands Tax Loophole Elimination Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Clarification of tar sands as crude oil for excise tax purposes=



==(a) In general– ==

  Paragraph (1) of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/4612 section 4612(a)]of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

===(1) Crude oil– ===

The term crude oil includes crude oil condensates, natural gasoline, any bitumen or bituminous mixture, any oil derived from a bitumen or bituminous mixture (including oil derived from tar sands), and any oil derived from kerogen-bearing sources (including oil derived from oil shale). . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Regulatory Authority To Address Other Types of Crude Oil and Petroleum Products– ==

  Subsection (a) of section 4612 of such Code is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 

===(10) Regulatory Authority To Address Other Types of Crude Oil and Petroleum Products– ===

Under such regulations as the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]may prescribe, after consultation with the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]], the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]may include as crude oil or as a petroleum product subject to tax under section 4611 , any fuel feedstock or finished fuel product customarily transported by pipeline, vessel, railcar, or tanker truck if the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]determines that—
:(A) the classification of such fuel feedstock or finished fuel product is consistent with the definition of oil under the Oil Pollution Act of 1990 , and
:(B) such fuel feedstock or finished fuel product is produced in sufficient commercial quantities as to pose a significant risk of hazard in the event of a discharge. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Technical amendment– ==

  Paragraph (2) of section 4612(a) of such Code is amended by striking“from a well located”.

==(d) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall take effect on the date of the enactment of this Act.
