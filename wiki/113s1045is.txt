{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 5, United States Code, to provide that persons having seriously delinquent tax debts shall be ineligible for Federal employment.

={{anchor|HD700FAE7B9734AB0AEAD19BB125057F4}}Section 1. Ineligibility of persons having seriously delinquent tax debts for Federal employment=



=={{anchor|H1510357D47024D0AB155E81FC6061A46}}(a) In general– ==

Chapter 73 of title 5, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H39B64F42E5D94440BA8F91C0F035BBC3}}VIII Ineligibility of persons having seriously delinquent tax debts for Federal employment– ==



=={{anchor|HA8FB47DC7F6044858E032911E4407B7F}}Sec. 7381. Ineligibility of persons having seriously delinquent tax debts for Federal employment==



==={{anchor|H522346C1248242FF946302F3113DA9E6}}(a) Definitions– ===

For purposes of this section—
:(1) the term seriously delinquent tax debt means an outstanding debt under the Internal Revenue Code of 1986for which a notice of lien has been filed in public records pursuant to section 6323 of such Code, except that such term does not include—
::(A) a debt that is being paid in a timely manner pursuant to an agreement under section 6159or section 7122 of such Code; and
::(B) a debt with respect to which a collection due process hearing under section 6330 of such Code, or relief under subsection (a), (b), or (f) of section 6015 of such Code, is requested or pending; and
:(2) the term Federal employee means—
::(A) an employee, as defined by section 2105; and
::(B) an employee of the [[w:United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]] or of the [[w:|Postal Regulatory Commission]] .

==={{anchor|H98DED5F2CCE0495397AAA4B86E2D6DC4}}(b) Ineligibility for Federal employment– ===

An individual who has a seriously delinquent tax debt shall be ineligible to be appointed, or to continue serving, as a Federal employee.

==={{anchor|H966DC5C6AB6649A183E501BC6EF687F7}}(c) Regulations– ===

The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office of Personnel Management]] shall, for purposes of carrying out this section with respect to the executive branch, prescribe any regulations which the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office]] considers necessary, which shall include regulations authorizing each agency to establish an appeal process for affected individuals..
</blockquote>
