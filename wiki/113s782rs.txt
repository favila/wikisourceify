{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend[[Public Law -|Public Law 101–377]]to revise the boundaries of the Gettysburg National Military Park to include the Gettysburg Train Station, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Gettysburg National Military Park Expansion Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Gettysburg National Military Park expansion=



==(a) Gettysburg National Military Park boundary revision– ==

Section 1 of[[Public Law -|Public Law 101–377]](16 U.S.C. 430g–4) is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

===(d) Additional land– ===

In addition to the land described in subsections (a) and (b), the park shall include—
:(1) the property commonly known as the Gettysburg Train Station (including land located in close proximity to the Gettysburg Train Station) that is—
::(A) located in the Borough of Gettysburg; and
::(B) depicted on the map entitled Gettysburg National Military Park, Proposed Boundary , numbered 305/80,045, and dated September 2008; and
:(2) the property that is—
::(A) located adjacent to Plum Run in Cumberland Township; and
::(B) depicted on the map entitled Gettysburg National Military Park, Proposed Boundary Addition , numbered 305/80,046, and dated February 2009. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Acquisition and disposal of land– ==

Section 2(a) of[[Public Law -|Public Law 101–377]]([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/430g-5 16 U.S.C. 430g–5(a)]) is amended—
:(1) by striking“The Secretary”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

====(1) In general– ====

The Secretary ; and 
</blockquote>

:(2) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

====(2) Acquisition of publicly owned land– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

Subject to subparagraph (B), the Secretary may acquire only by purchase from a willing seller publicly owned property that is located within the property described in section 1(d)(1) if the Secretary determines that efforts to acquire the property without cost have been exhausted.

=====(B) Eminent domain– =====

The Secretary may not acquire by eminent domain property that is located within the property described in section 1(d)(1). . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 2. Gettysburg national military park expansion=



==(a) Boundary revision– ==

 Section 1(b) of [[Public Law 101-377|Public Law 101–377]]  ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/430g–4 16 U.S.C. 430g–4(b)] )is amended—
:(1) by striking“include the”and insert “include—

<blockquote> 
::(1) the ; 
</blockquote>

:(2) at the end of[[Public Law 101-377|paragraph (1)]] (as designated by paragraph (1) ), by striking the period and inserting“; and”; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
::(2) the properties depicted as“Proposed Addition”on the map entitled“Gettysburg National Military Park Proposed Boundary Addition”, numbered 305/80,045, and dated January, 2010 (2 sheets), including—
:::(A) the property commonly known as the“Gettysburg Train Station”; and
:::(B) the property located adjacent to Plum Run in Cumberland Township. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Acquisition of land– ==

 Section 2(a) of [[Public Law 101-377|Public Law 101–377]]  ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/430g–5 16 U.S.C. 430g–5(a)] )is amended—
:(1) in the first sentence, by striking "The Secretary" and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

====(1) Authority to acquire land– ====

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]]  ; 
</blockquote>

:(2) in the second sentence, by striking“In acquiring”and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

====(2) Minimum Federal interests– ====

In acquiring ; and 
</blockquote>

:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

====(3) Methods of acquisition for certain land– ====

Notwithstanding[[Public Law 101-377|paragraph (1)]] , the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]]may acquire the properties added to the park by[[Public Law 101-377|section 1(b)(2)]] only—
:(A) by donation; or
:(B) if the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]]determines that efforts to acquire the properties without cost have been exhausted, by purchase from a willing seller. . 
</blockquote>
