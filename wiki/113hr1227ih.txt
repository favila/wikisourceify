{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Immigration and Nationality Act to authorize certain aliens who have earned a Ph.D. degree from a United States institution of higher education in a field of science, technology, engineering, or mathematics to be admitted for permanent residence and to be exempted from the numerical limitations on H–1B nonimmigrants.

={{anchor|H36F9B050593448C9BA01C04053B0FEC3}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Stopping Trained in America Ph.D.s From Leaving the Economy Act of 2013”or the“STAPLE Act”.

={{anchor|H6BDDCE1B7DD64A628DE03B272DA67F9C}}Sec. 2. Exemption from numerical limitations on permanent residents for certain United States educated immigrants=



=={{anchor|H4CC48595D7B645A6A45DB01089D95393}}(a) Aliens not subject to direct numerical limitations– ==

 Section 201(b)(1) of the Immigration and Nationality Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1151 8 U.S.C. 1151(b)(1)] ] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(F) Aliens who have earned a Ph.D. degree from a United States institution of higher education (as defined in section 101(a) of the Higher Education Act of 1965([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/1001 20 U.S.C. 1001(a)] )) in a field of science, technology, engineering, or mathematics and who have an offer of employment from a United States employer in a field related to such degree..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|H23BA060D192442BAB38F1FFC65413421}}(b) Procedure for granting immigrant status– ==

 Section 204(a)(1)(F) of the Immigration and Nationality Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1154 8 U.S.C. 1154(a)(1)(F)] ] )is amended—
:(1) by striking“or”after“ 203(b)(2),”;
:(2) by inserting“, or 201(b)(1)(F)”after“ 203(b)(3)”; and
:(3) by striking“ [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] ”and inserting“ [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary of Homeland Security]] ”.

={{anchor|H2B751673E0E84AD6B5AE1D665C88B4FA}}Sec. 3. Exemption from H–1B numerical limitation for certain United States educated nonimmigrants=

 Section 214(g)(5) of the Immigration and Nationality Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1184 8 U.S.C. 1184(g)(5)] ] )is amended—
:(1) in subparagraph (B), by striking“or”at the end;
:(2) in subparagraph (C), by striking the period at the end and inserting a semicolon; and
:(3) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(D) has earned a Ph.D. degree from a United States institution of higher education (as defined in section 101(a) of the Higher Education Act of 1965([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/1001 20 U.S.C. 1001(a)] ] )) in a field of science, technology, engineering, or mathematics and with respect to whom the petitioning employer requires such education as a condition for the employment..
</blockquote>
