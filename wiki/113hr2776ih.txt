{{Center|A BILL}}

 To establish a regulatory review process for rules that the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency plans to propose, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Review EPA’s Language on Agriculture and Thoroughly Engage with the Farm Act of 2013 ”or the“ RELATE with the Farm Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Regulatory review by the Secretary of Agriculture=



==(a) Review of regulatory agenda– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] shall review publications that may give notice that the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] is preparing or plans to prepare any guidance, policy, memorandum, regulation, or statement of general applicability and future effect that may have a significant impact on a substantial number of agricultural entities, including—
:(1) any regulatory agenda of the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] published pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/602 section 602 of title 5, United States Code];
:(2) any regulatory plan or agenda published by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] or the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] pursuant to an Executive order, including Executive Order 12866; and
:(3) any other publication issued by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] or the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]] that may reasonably be foreseen to contain notice of plans by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] to prepare any guidance, policy, memorandum, regulation, or statement of general applicability and future effect that may have a significant impact on a substantial number of agricultural entities.

==(b) Information gathering– ==

For a publication item reviewed under subsection (a) that the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] determines may have a significant impact on a substantial number of agricultural entities, the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall—
:(1) solicit from the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]] any information the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] may provide to facilitate a review of the publication item;
:(2) utilize the [[w:USDA, Office of the Chief Economist|Chief Economist of the Department of Agriculture]] to produce an economic impact statement for the publication item that contains a detailed estimate of potential costs to agricultural entities;
:(3) identify individuals representative of potentially affected agricultural entities for the purpose of obtaining advice and recommendations from such individuals about the potential impacts of the publication item; and
:(4) convene a review panel for analysis of the publication item that includes the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] , any full-time Federal employee of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Department of Agriculture]] appointed to the panel by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] , and any employee of the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Environmental Protection Agency]] or the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs]] within the Office of Management and Budget that accepts an invitation from the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] to participate in the panel.

==(c) Duties of the review panel– ==

A review panel convened for a publication item under subsection (b)(4) shall—
:(1) review any information or material obtained by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] and prepared in connection with the publication item, including any draft proposed guidance, policy, memorandum, regulation, or statement of general applicability and future effect;
:(2) collect advice and recommendations from agricultural entity representatives identified by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] after consultation with the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] ;
:(3) compile and analyze such advice and recommendations; and
:(4) make recommendations to the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] based on the information gathered by the review panel or provided by agricultural entity representatives.

==(d) Comments– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 60 days after the date the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] convenes a review panel pursuant to subsection (b)(4) , the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall submit to the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] comments on the planned or proposed guidance, policy, memorandum, regulation, or statement of general applicability and future effect for consideration and inclusion in any related administrative record, including—
:(A) a report by the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] on the concerns of agricultural entities;
:(B) the findings of the review panel;
:(C) the findings of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] , including any adopted findings of the review panel; and
:(D) recommendations of the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] .

===(2) Publication– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall publish the comments in the Federal Register and make the comments available to the public on the public Internet website of the Department of Agriculture.

==(e) Waivers– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] may waive initiation of the review panel under subsection (b)(4) as the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] determines appropriate.

==(f) Definition of agricultural entity– ==

In this section, the term“agricultural entity”means any entity involved in or related to agricultural enterprise, including enterprises that are engaged in the business of production of food and fiber, ranching and raising of livestock, aquaculture, and all other farming and agricultural related industries.
