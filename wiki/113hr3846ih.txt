{{Center|A BILL}}

 To provide for the authorization of border, maritime, and transportation security responsibilities and functions in the Department of Homeland Security and the establishment of United States Customs and Border Protection, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   United States Customs and Border Protection Authorization Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Establishment of United States Customs and Border Protection=



==(a) In general– ==

   Section 411 of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/6/211 6 U.S.C. 211] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

==Sec. 411. Establishment of United States Customs and Border Protection; Commissioner, Deputy Commissioner, and operational offices==



===(a) In general– ===

There is established in the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Department]]an agency to be known as [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]].

===(b) Commissioner of United States Customs and Border Protection– ===

There shall be at the head of [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]a Commissioner of United States Customs and Border Protection (in this section referred to as the Commissioner ), who shall be appointed by the President, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate.

===(c) Duties– ===

The [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]]shall—
:(1) ensure the interdiction of persons and goods illegally entering or exiting the United States;
:(2) facilitate and expedite the flow of legitimate travelers and trade;
:(3) detect, respond, and interdict terrorists, drug traffickers, human traffickers, criminals, and other persons who may undermine the security of the United States;
:(4) safeguard the borders of the United States to protect against the entry of dangerous goods while facilitating and expediting the flow of legitimate trade and travel;
:(5) oversee the functions of the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of International Trade]]established under  section 402 of the Security and Accountability for Every Port Act of 2006 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/19/2072 19 U.S.C. 2072] ; [[Public Law 109-347|Public Law 109–347]]  );
:(6) enforce and administer all customs laws of the United States;
:(7) develop and implement screening and targeting capabilities, including the screening, reviewing, identifying, and prioritizing of passengers and cargo across all international modes of transportation, both inbound and outbound; and
:(8) carry out the duties and powers prescribed by law or delegated by the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]].

===(d) Deputy Commissioner– ===

There shall be in [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]a [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Deputy Commissioner]]who shall assist the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]]in the management of [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]].

===(e) Office of United States Border Patrol– ===



====(1) In general– ====

There is established in [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of United States Border Patrol.]] 

====(2) Chief– ====

There shall be at the head of the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of United States Border Patrol]]a [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Chief]], who shall be a uniformed law enforcement officer chosen from the ranks of the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Border Patrol]]and who shall report to the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]].

====(3) Duties– ====

The [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Border Patrol]]shall—
:(A) serve as the law enforcement office of [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]with primary responsibility for interdicting persons attempting to illegally enter or exit the United States or goods being illegally imported to or exported from the United States at a place other than a designated port of entry;
:(B) deter and prevent illegal entry of terrorists, terrorist weapons, persons, and contraband; and
:(C) carry out other duties and powers prescribed by the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]].

===(f) Office of Air and Marine Operations– ===



====(1) In general– ====

There is established in [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]an [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of Air and Marine Operations]].

====(2) Assistant commissioner– ====

There shall be at the head of the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of Air and Marine Operations]]an Assistant Commissioner, who shall report to the Commissioner.

====(3) Duties– ====

The [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of Air and Marine Operations]]shall—
:(A) serve as the law enforcement office of [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]with primary responsibility to detect, interdict, and prevent acts of terrorism and the unlawful movement of people, illicit drugs, and other contraband across the borders of the United States in the air and maritime environment;
:(B) oversee the acquisition, maintenance, and operational use of [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]integrated air and marine forces;
:(C) provide aviation and marine support for other Federal, State, and local law enforcement agency needs, as appropriate; and
:(D) carry out other duties and powers prescribed by the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]].

===(g) Office of Field Operations– ===



====(1) In general– ====

There is established in [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]an [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of Field Operations]].

====(2) Assistant commissioner– ====

There shall be at the head of the Office of Field Operations an Assistant Commissioner, who shall report to the Commissioner.

====(3) Duties– ====

The [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of Field Operations]]shall coordinate the enforcement activities of [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]at United States air, land, and sea ports of entry to—
:(A) deter and prevent terrorists and terrorist weapons from entering the United States at ports of entry;
:(B) conduct inspections at United States ports of entry to safeguard the United States from terrorism and illegal entry of persons;
:(C) prevent illicit drugs, agricultural pests, and contraband from entering the United States; and
:(D) carry out other duties and powers prescribed by the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]].

===(h) Office of Intelligence and Investigative Liaison– ===



====(1) In general– ====

There is established in [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]an [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of Intelligence and Investigative Liaison]].

====(2) Assistant commissioner– ====

There shall be at the head of the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of Intelligence and Investigative Liaison]]an Assistant Commissioner, who shall report to the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]].

====(3) Duties– ====

The [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of Intelligence and Investigative Liaison]]shall—
:(A) develop, provide, coordinate, and implement intelligence capabilities into a cohesive intelligence enterprise to support the execution of the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]duties and responsibilities;
:(B) collect and analyze advance traveler and cargo information;
:(C) establish intelligence-sharing relationships with Federal, State, local, and tribal agencies and intelligence agencies; and
:(D) carry out other duties and powers prescribed by the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]].

===(i) Office of International Affairs– ===



====(1) In general– ====

There is established in [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]an [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of International Affairs]].

====(2) Assistant commissioner– ====

There shall be at the head of the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of International Affairs]]an Assistant Commissioner, who shall report to the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]].

====(3) Duties– ====

The [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of International Affairs]]shall—
:(A) coordinate and support [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]’s foreign initiatives, policies, programs, and activities;
:(B) coordinate and support [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protectio]]n’s personnel stationed abroad;
:(C) maintain partnerships and information sharing agreements with foreign governments, international organizations, and United States agencies in support of [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]duties and responsibilities;
:(D) provide necessary capacity building, training, and assistance to foreign border control agencies to strengthen global supply chain and travel security;
:(E) coordinate mission support services to sustain [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]’s global activities;
:(F) lead and coordinate [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]’s engagement in international negotiations, in collaboration with the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Office of International Affairs]]of the Department; and
:(G) carry out other duties and powers prescribed by the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner]].

===(j) Other authorities– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Secretary]]may establish such other Assistant Commissioners, agents, officers, and other offices as the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Secretary]]determines necessary to carry out the missions, duties, functions, and authorities of [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]].

====(2) Notification– ====

If the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Secretary]]exercises the authority provided pursuant to paragraph (1) , the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Secretary]]shall notify the appropriate congressional committees not later than 30 days before exercising such authority. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Special rules– ==



===(1) Treatment– ===

  Section 411 of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 , as amended by subsection (a) of this section , shall be treated as if included in such Act as of the date of the enactment of such Act, and, in addition to the functions, missions, duties, and authorities specified in such amended section 411 , [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|United States Customs and Border Protection]]shall continue to perform and carry out the functions, missions, duties, and authorities under section 411 of such Act as in existence on the day before such date of enactment.

===(2) Rules of construction– ===



====(A) Rules and regulations– ====

Notwithstanding paragraph (1) , nothing in this Act may be construed as affecting in any manner any rule or regulation issued or promulgated pursuant to section 411 of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 as in existence on the day before the date of the enactment of this Act, and any such rule or regulation shall continue to have full force and effect on and after such date.

====(B) Other actions– ====

Notwithstanding paragraph (1) , nothing in this Act may be construed as affecting in any manner any action, determination, policy, or decision pursuant to section 411 of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 as in existence on the day before the date of the enactment of this Act, and any such action, determination, policy, or decision shall continue to have full force and effect on and after such date.

==(c) Continuation in office– ==



===(1) Commissioner– ===

The individual serving as the [[w:|Commissioner of Customs]]on the day before the date of the enactment of this Act may serve as the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner of United States Customs and Border Protection]]on and after such date of enactment until a [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner of United States Customs and Border Protection]]is appointed under section 411 of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 , as amended by subsection (a) of this section .

===(2) Other positions– ===

The individuals serving as Assistant Commissioners and other officers and officials under section 411 of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 on the day before the date of the enactment of this Act may serve as the appropriate Assistant Commissioners and other officers and officials under such section 411 as amended by subsection (a) of this section unless the [[w:|  [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner of United States Customs and Border Protection]] ]]determines that another individual should hold such position.

==(d) Reference– ==



===(1) Title 5– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5314  Section 5314of title 5, United States Code], is amended by striking“Commissioner of Customs, Department of Homeland Security”and inserting“Commissioner of United States Customs and Border Protection, Department of Homeland Security”.

===(2) Other references– ===

On and after the date of the enactment of this Act, any reference in law or regulations to the“Commissioner of Customs”or the“Commissioner of the Customs Service”shall be deemed to be a reference to the [[w:U.S. Customs and Border Protection|Commissioner of United States Customs and Border Protection]].

==(e) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of contents in  section 1(b) of the Homeland Security Act of 2002 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/6/101 6 U.S.C. 101 et seq].)is amended by striking the item relating to section 411 and inserting the following new item:

</blockquote>
