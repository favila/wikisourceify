{{Center|1st Session}}

 
{{Center|S. 1471}}

 
{{Center|IN THE SENATE OF THE UNITED STATES}}

 
{{Center|AN ACT}}

 To authorize the Secretary of Veterans Affairs and the Secretary of the Army to reconsider decisions to inter or honor the memory of a person in a national cemetery, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Alicia Dawn Koehl Respect for National Cemeteries Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Authority to reconsider decisions of Secretary of Veterans Affairs or Secretary of the Army to inter the remains or honor the memory of a person in a national cemetery=



==(a) Authority To reconsider prior decisions– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411  Section 2411of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subsection (d)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subsection (f)]; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subsection (c)]the following new subsections:

<blockquote> 
::(d) 
:::(1) In a case described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subsection (e)], the appropriate Federal official may reconsider a decision to—
::::(A) inter the remains of a person in a cemetery in the [[w:National Cemetery Administration|National Cemetery Administration]]or in Arlington National Cemetery; or
::::(B) honor the memory of a person in a memorial area in a cemetery in the [[w:National Cemetery Administration|National Cemetery Administration]](described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2403 section 2403(a)]of this title) or in such an area in Arlington National Cemetery (described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2409 section 2409(a)]of this title).
:::(2) 
::::(A) 
:::::(i) In a case described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subsection (e)(1)(A)], the appropriate Federal official shall provide notice to the deceased person’s next of kin or other person authorized to arrange burial or memorialization of the deceased person of the decision of the appropriate Federal official to disinter the remains of the deceased person or to remove a memorial headstone or marker memorializing the deceased person.
:::::(ii) In a case described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subsection (e)(1)(B)], if the appropriate Federal official finds, based upon a showing of clear and convincing evidence and after an opportunity for a hearing in a manner prescribed by the appropriate Federal official, that the person had committed a Federal capital crime or a State capital crime but had not been convicted of such crime by reason of such person not being available for trial due to death or flight to avoid prosecution, the appropriate Federal official shall provide notice to the deceased person’s next of kin or other person authorized to arrange burial or memorialization of the deceased person of the decision of the appropriate Federal official to disinter the remains of the deceased person or to remove a memorial headstone or marker memorializing the deceased person.
::::(B) Notice under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subparagraph (A)]shall be provided by the appropriate Federal official as follows:
:::::(i) By the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/5104 section 5104]of this title.
:::::(ii) By the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]in accordance with such regulations as the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]shall prescribe for purposes of this subsection.
:::(3) 
::::(A) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the next of kin or other person authorized to arrange burial or memorialization of the deceased person shall be allowed a period of 60 days from the date of the notice required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 paragraph (2)]to file a notice of disagreement with the Federal official that provided the notice.
::::(B) 
:::::(i) A notice of disagreement filed with the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subparagraph (A)]shall be treated as a notice of disagreement filed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7105 section 7105]of this title and shall initiate appellate review in accordance with the provisions ofchapter 71of this title.
:::::(ii) A notice of disagreement filed with the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subparagraph (A)]shall be decided in accordance with such regulations as the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]]shall prescribe for purposes of this subsection.
:::(4) When the decision of the appropriate Federal official to disinter the remains or remove a memorial headstone or marker of the deceased person becomes final either by failure to appeal the decision in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 paragraph (3)(A)]or by final disposition of the appeal pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 paragraph (3)(B)], the appropriate Federal official may take any of the following actions:
::::(A) Disinter the remains of the person from the cemetery in the [[w:National Cemetery Administration|National Cemetery Administration]]or in Arlington National Cemetery and provide for the reburial or other appropriate disposition of the disinterred remains in a place other than a cemetery in the [[w:National Cemetery Administration|National Cemetery Administration]]or in Arlington National Cemetery.
::::(B) Remove from a memorial area in a cemetery in the [[w:National Cemetery Administration|National Cemetery Administration]]or in Arlington National Cemetery any memorial headstone or marker placed to honor the memory of the person. 
::(e) 
:::(1) A case described in this subsection is a case in which the appropriate federal official receives—
::::(A) written notice of a conviction referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 subsection (b)(1)],[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 (b)(2)], or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 (b)(4)]of a person described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 paragraph (2)]; or
::::(B) information that a person described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 paragraph (2)]may have committed a Federal capital crime or a State capital crime but was not convicted of such crime by reason of such person not being available for trial due to death or flight to avoid prosecution.
:::(2) A person described in this paragraph is a person—
::::(A) whose remains have been interred in a cemetery in the [[w:National Cemetery Administration|National Cemetery Administration]]or in Arlington National Cemetery; or
::::(B) whose memory has been honored in a memorial area in a cemetery in the [[w:National Cemetery Administration|National Cemetery Administration]]or in such an area in Arlington National Cemetery. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Modification of exception To interment or memorialization prohibition– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/2411 Subsection (a)(2)]of such section is amended by striking“such official approves an application for”.

==(c) Applicability– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply with respect to any interment or memorialization conducted by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]or the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary of the Army]]in a cemetery in the [[w:National Cemetery Administration|National Cemetery Administration]]or in Arlington National Cemetery after the date of the enactment of this Act. 

=Sec. 3. Disinterment of remains of Michael LaShawn Anderson from Fort Custer National Cemetery=


:(a) Disinterment of remains– The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall disinter the remains of Michael LaShawn Anderson from Fort Custer National Cemetery.
:(b) Notification of Next-of-Kin– The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall—
::(1) notify the next-of-kin of record for Michael LaShawn Anderson of the impending disinterment of his remains; and
::(2) upon disinterment, relinquish the remains to the next-of-kin of record for Michael LaShawn Anderson or, if the next-of-kin of record for Michael LaShawn Anderson is unavailable, arrange for an appropriate disposition of the remains.    Passed the Senate November 18, 2013.  Secretary
