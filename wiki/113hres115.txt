{{Center|RESOLUTION}}

Providing for the expenses of certain committees of the House of Representatives in the One Hundred Thirteenth Congress.

={{anchor|H448252422DAC4FAFAB771463D18F37E6}}Section 1. Committee Expenses for the One Hundred Thirteenth Congress=



=={{anchor|H032893B6D9BA46568B9592D6F0E60B13}}(a) In General– ==

With respect to the One Hundred Thirteenth Congress, there shall be paid out of the applicable accounts of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] , in accordance with this primary expense resolution, not more than the amount specified in subsection (b)for the expenses (including the expenses of all staff salaries) of each committee named in such subsection.

=={{anchor|HF07CC7BE35704BA39D0DAC143C9E7B56}}(b) Committees and Amounts– ==

The committees and amounts referred to in subsection (a)are:Committee on Agriculture, $10,072,374;Committee on Armed Services, $13,127,070;Committee on the Budget, $10,277,648;Committee on Education and the Workforce, $13,905,526;Committee on Energy and Commerce, $19,041,032;Committee on Ethics, $6,040,918;Committee on Financial Services, $14,788,964;Committee on Foreign Affairs, $14,776,224;Committee on Homeland Security, $14,067,176;Committee on House Administration, $9,201,120;Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence, $8,779,516;Committee on the Judiciary, $14,154,032;Committee on Natural Resources, $13,111,658;Committee on Oversight and Government Reform, $17,880,874;Committee on Rules, $5,714,816;Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, $10,565,510;Committee on Small Business, $5,985,376;Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure, $16,364,614;Committee on Veterans’ Affairs, $6,097,092; andCommittee on Ways and Means, $16,846,822.

={{anchor|HDDBE6BF4A6C84579A339F4C758DF992A}}Sec. 2. First Session Limitations=



=={{anchor|H7D78A19381A84FEA9E98B9CBB8B23985}}(a) In General– ==

Of the amount provided for in section 1for each committee named in subsection (b), not more than the amount specified in such subsection shall be available for expenses incurred during the period beginning at noon on January 3, 2013, and ending immediately before noon on January 3, 2014.

={{anchor|HF735D41D8715437C858E1758A904EB34}}Sec. 3. Second Session Limitations=



=={{anchor|HD9EC676231C74A499E47B100502CB449}}(a) In General– ==

Of the amount provided for in section 1 for each committee named in subsection (b), not more than the amount specified in such subsection shall be available for expenses incurred during the period beginning at noon on January 3, 2014, and ending immediately before noon on January 3, 2015.

=={{anchor|H1A33E139F7504370BE227C37D353B082}}(c) Review of Use of Funds in First Session– ==



==={{anchor|H1729C19BCAA9422D908B5CDD6D90B51D}}(1) Review– ===

None of the amounts provided for in section 1for a committee named in subsection (b)may be available for expenses of the committee after March 15, 2014, unless the chair or ranking minority member of the committee appears and presents testimony at a hearing of theCommittee on House Administrationheld prior to such date to review the committee's use of the amounts provided for in section 1during the first session of the One Hundred Thirteenth [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] and to determine whether the amount specified in subsection (b)with respect to the committee should be updated on the basis of the review.

==={{anchor|HFE81A88FECC3458287FB859339019F2D}}(2) Waiver– ===

TheCommittee on House Administrationmay waive the application of paragraph (1)to any or all of the committees named in subsection (b).

={{anchor|H369C8B1B2F5648EE9D79CA6A25AEBB6A}}Sec. 4. Vouchers=

Payments under this resolution shall be made on vouchers authorized by the committee involved, signed by the chairman of such committee, and approved in the manner directed by theCommittee on House Administration.

={{anchor|H7BEC32314C1144ACB4367536FB88990F}}Sec. 5. Regulations=

Amounts made available under this resolution shall be expended in accordance with regulations prescribed by theCommittee on House Administration.

={{anchor|H85042B7D6EF34C1AA6A5E71E049498E1}}Sec. 6. Reserve fund for unanticipated expenses=



=={{anchor|HFE55968E2DE54010A08277EE47F4C75F}}(a) Establishment– ==

There is hereby established a reserve fund for unanticipated expenses of committees for the One Hundred Thirteenth Congress.

=={{anchor|HE3F1822166A5442AB07E8B90D9B78BD2}}(b) Balance– ==

The balance of the reserve fund under this section shall be equal to the sum of the following:
:(1) The amount by which the amount made available for“House of Representatives—Committee Employees, Standing Committees, Special and Select”for fiscal year 2013 exceeds the amount that would be made available for“House of Representatives—Committee Employees, Standing Committees, Special and Select”by division C of the Department of Defense, Military Construction and Veterans Affairs, and Full-Year Continuing Appropriations Act, 2013(H.R. 933, as passed by the House of Representatives on March 6, 2013), as reduced pursuant to the provisions of division D of such Act.
:(2) The amount by which the amount made available for“House of Representatives—Committee Employees, Standing Committees, Special and Select”for fiscal year 2014 exceeds the amount made available for“House of Representatives—Committee Employees, Standing Committees, Special and Select”for fiscal year 2013.

=={{anchor|HB3939AF4A2684175AA722D7E3AC4F21A}}(c) Allocation to Committees– ==

Amounts in the reserve fund under this section shall be paid to a committee pursuant to an allocation approved by theCommittee on House Administration.

={{anchor|H9287A221450D4682ADF0EC5877D2C57C}}Sec. 7. Adjustment authority=

TheCommittee on House Administrationshall have authority to make adjustments in amounts under section 1, if necessary to comply with an order of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] issued under section 251Aor 254 of the Balanced Budget and Emergency Deficit Control Act of 1985or to conform to any change in appropriations for the purposes of such section 1.
