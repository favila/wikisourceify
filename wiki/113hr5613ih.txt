{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act to ensure that liquid over-the-counter medications are packaged with appropriate dosage delivery devices and, in the case of such medications labeled for pediatric use, appropriate flow restrictors, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Protecting Our Kids’ Medicine Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Dosage delivery devices for liquid OTC drugs and flow restrictors for such drugs labeled for pediatric use=



==(a) In general– ==

   Section 502 of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/21/352 21 U.S.C. 352] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:(dd) 
::(1) If it is a liquid formulation of a drug that is not subject to section 503(b) and—
:::(A) it is not packaged with a dosage delivery device in accordance with specifications to be determined by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]by regulation;
:::(B) in the case of such a liquid formulation that is labeled for pediatric use, it is not packaged with a dosage delivery device, as described in subparagraph (A), and—
::::(i) a flow restrictor; or
::::(ii) another mechanism to reduce the frequency and volume of accidental ingestion that provides a level of safety that is equivalent to or greater than the level of safety that would be provided by a flow restrictor, as determined by the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]by regulation; or
:::(C) its labeling is in violation of subparagraph (2).
::(2) The [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]shall require that any measurement in the labeling of a liquid formulation of a drug that is not subject to section 503(b) , including any measurement in the labeling of a dosage delivery device packaged with the liquid formulation, be expressed exclusively in metric units. The [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]may waive the requirement in the preceding sentence with respect to one or more liquid formulations if the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]determines that, with respect to such formulations, implementation of such requirement would not benefit the public health.
::(3) In this paragraph:
:::(A) The term dosage delivery device —
::::(i) means an object that is designed to measure the dosage of a drug in liquid form and deliver that drug to an individual; and
::::(ii) includes calibrated cups, droppers, syringes, and spoons.
:::(B) The term flow restrictor has such meaning as the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary]]may prescribe by regulation. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Regulations– ==

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Department of Health and Human Services|Secretary of Health and Human Services]], acting through the [[w:Food and Drug Administration|Commissioner of Food and Drugs]], shall—
:(1) promulgate a final rule implementing the amendment made by subsection (a); and
:(2) include in such rule a definition of the term flow restrictor .

==(c) Applicability– ==

The amendment made by subsection (a) applies beginning on the date that is 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act.
