{{Center|A BILL}}

 To authorize the revocation or denial of passports and passport cards to individuals affiliated with foreign terrorist organizations, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   F.T.O. Passport Revocation Act   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Revocation or denial of passports and passport cards to individuals affiliated with foreign terrorist organizations=

The Act entitled “  An Act to regulate the issue and validity of passports, and for other purposes  ”, approved July 3, 1926 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/211a 22 U.S.C. 211a et seq.] ), commonly known as the“  Passport Act of 1926  ”, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 

=Sec. 4. Authority to deny or revoke passport and passport card=



==(a) Ineligibility– ==



===(1) Issuance– ===

Except as provided under subsection (b), the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]]may not issue a passport or passport card to any individual whom the Secretary has determined is a member of or is otherwise affiliated with, or is aiding, assisting, abetting, or is otherwise helping an organization the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary]]has designated as a foreign terrorist organization pursuant to  section 219 of the Immigration and Nationality Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/8/1189 8 U.S.C. 1189] ).

===(2) Revocation– ===

The [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]]shall revoke a passport or passport card previously issued to any individual described in paragraph (1).

==(b) Exceptions– ==



===(1) Emergency and humanitarian situations– ===

Notwithstanding subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]]may issue a passport or passport card, in emergency circumstances or for humanitarian reasons, to an individual described in paragraph (1) of such subsection.

===(2) Limitation for return to United States– ===

Notwithstanding subsection (a)(2), the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]], before revocation, may—
:(A) limit a previously issued passport or passport card only for return travel to the United States; or
:(B) issue a limited passport or passport card that only permits return travel to the United States.

==(c) Report– ==

If the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary of State]]issues or limits a passport or passport card under subsection (b), the [[w:United States Department of State|Secretary]]shall, not later than 30 days after such issuance or limitation, submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on such issuance or limitation, as the case may be. . 
</blockquote>
