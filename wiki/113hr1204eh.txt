{{Center|AN ACT}}

To amend title 49, United States Code, to direct the Assistant Secretary of Homeland Security (Transportation Security Administration) to establish an Aviation Security Advisory Committee, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “ Aviation Security Stakeholder Participation Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Aviation Security Advisory Committee=



==(a) In general– ==

Subchapter II ofchapter 449of title 49, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 44946. Aviation Security Advisory Committee==



===(a) Establishment– ===

The [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Assistant Secretary]] shall establish within the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Transportation Security Administration]] an advisory committee to be known as the “ [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Aviation Security Advisory Committee]] ” .

===(b) Duties– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall consult the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] on aviation security matters, including on the development, refinement, and implementation of policies, programs, rulemaking, and security directives pertaining to aviation security, while adhering to sensitive security guidelines.

====(2) Recommendations– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] shall develop, at the request of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] , recommendations for improvements to aviation security.

=====(B) Recommendations of subcommittees– =====

Recommendations agreed upon by the subcommittees established under this section shall be approved by the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] for transmission to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] .

====(3) Periodic reports– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] shall periodically submit to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] —
:(A) reports on matters identified by the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] ; and
:(B) reports on other matters identified by a majority of the members of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] .

====(4) Annual report– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] shall submit to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] an annual report providing information on the activities, findings, and recommendations of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] , including its subcommittees, for the preceding year.

====(5) Feedback– ====

Not later than 90 days after receiving recommendations transmitted by the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 paragraph (4)] , the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall respond in writing to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] with feedback on each of such recommendations, an action plan to implement any of such recommendations with which the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] concurs, and a detailed justification for why any of such recommendations have been rejected.

====(6) Congressional notification– ====

Not later than 30 days after providing written feedback to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] in accordance with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 paragraph (5)] , the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall brief the [[w:United States House Committee on Homeland Security|Committee on Homeland Security of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate]] on such feedback.

====(7) Report to Congress– ====

Prior to briefing the [[w:United States House Committee on Homeland Security|Committee on Homeland Security of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation|Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation of the Senate]] in accordance with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 paragraph (6)] , the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall submit to such committees a report containing information relating to the recommendations transmitted by the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] in accordance with [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 paragraph (4)] .

===(c) Membership– ===



====(1) Appointment– ====



=====(A) In general– =====

Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this section, the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall appoint the members of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee.]] 

=====(B) Composition– =====

The membership of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committe]] e shall consist of individuals representing not more than 32 member organizations. Each organization shall be represented by one individual (or the individual’s designee).

=====(C) Representation– =====

The membership of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] shall include representatives of air carriers, all cargo air transportation, indirect air carriers, labor organizations representing air carrier employees, aircraft manufacturers, airport operators, general aviation, privacy organizations, the travel industry, airport based businesses, including minority owned small businesses, businesses that conduct security operations at airports, aeronautical repair stations, passenger advocacy groups, the aviation technology security industry, including biometrics, victims of terrorist acts against aviation, and law enforcement and security experts.

====(2) Removal– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] may review the participation of a member of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] and remove such member for cause at any time.

====(3) Prohibition on compensation– ====

The members of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] shall not receive pay, allowances, or benefits from the Government by reason of their service on the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee.]] 

====(4) Meetings– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall require the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] to meet at least semiannually and may convene additional meetings as necessary.

===(d) Air cargo security subcommittee– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall establish within the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] an [[w:Transportation Security Administration|air cargo security subcommittee]] to provide recommendations on air cargo security issues, including the implementation of the air cargo security programs established by the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Transportation Security Administration]] to screen air cargo on passenger aircraft and all-cargo aircraft in accordance with established cargo screening mandates.

====(2) Meetings and reporting– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|subcommittee]] shall meet at least quarterly and submit to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] for inclusion in the annual report required under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 subsection (b)(4)] information, including recommendations, regarding air cargo security. Such submissions shall include recommendations to improve the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Transportation Security Administration]] ’s cargo security initiatives established to meet the requirements of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44901 section 44901(g)] .

====(3) Membership– ====

The subcommittee shall—
:(A) include members of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] with expertise in air cargo operations; and
:(B) be cochaired by a Government and industry official.

===(e) General aviation security subcommittee– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall establish within the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] a [[w:Transportation Security Administration|general aviation subcommittee]] to provide recommendations on transportation security issues for general aviation facilities, general aviation aircraft, and helicopter operations at general aviation and commercial service airports.

====(2) Meetings and reporting– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|subcommittee]] shall meet at least quarterly and submit to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] for inclusion in the annual report required under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 subsection (b)(4)] information, including recommendations, regarding aviation security at general aviation airports.

====(3) Membership– ====

The subcommittee shall—
:(A) include members of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] with expertise in general aviation; and
:(B) be cochaired by a Government and industry official.

===(f) Perimeter security, exit lane security, and access control subcommittee– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall establish within the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] an airport perimeter security, exit lane security, and access control subcommittee to provide recommendations on airport perimeter security, exit lane security at commercial service airports, and access control issues.

====(2) Meetings and reporting– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|subcommittee]] shall meet at least quarterly and submit to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] for inclusion in the annual report required under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 subsection (b)(4)] information, including recommendations, regarding improving perimeter security, exit lane security at commercial service airports, and access control procedures at commercial service and general aviation airports.

====(3) Membership– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|subcommittee]] shall—
:(A) include members of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] with expertise in airport perimeter security and access control issues; and
:(B) be co-chaired by a Government and industry official.

===(g) Risk-based subcommittee– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall establish within the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] a [[w:Transportation Security Administration|risk-based subcommittee]] to provide recommendations on passenger screening policies and cargo.

====(2) Meetings and reporting– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|subcommittee]] shall meet at least quarterly and submit to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] for inclusion in the annual report required under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 subsection (b)(4)] information, including recommendations, regarding the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Transportation Security Administration]] ’s risk-based security programs.

====(3) Membership– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|subcommittee]] shall—
:(A) include members of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] with expertise in passenger advocacy and airport security operations; and
:(B) be cochaired by a Government and industry official.

===(h) Security technology subcommittee– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] shall establish within the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] a [[w:Transportation Security Administration|security technology subcommittee]] to provide recommendations on security technology issues, including harmonization of security technology standards and requirements.

====(2) Meetings and reporting– ====

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|subcommittee]] shall meet at least quarterly and submit to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] for inclusion in the annual report required under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 subsection (b)(4)] information, including recommendations, regarding security technology. Such submissions shall include recommendations to improve the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Transportation Security Administration]] ’s utilization of security technology and harmonization of security technology standards and requirements. All recommendations shall be in furtherance of aviation security and technology neutral.

====(3) Membership– ====

The subcommittee shall—
:(A) include members of the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] with expertise in checkpoint, baggage, and air cargo security technology; and
:(B) be co-chaired by a Government and industry official.

===(i) Other subcommittees– ===

The [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] , in consultation with the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] , may establish within the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] any other subcommittee that the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Assistant Secretary]] and Advisory Committee determine necessary.

===(j) Subject matter experts– ===

Each subcommittee under this section shall include subject matter experts with relevant expertise who are appointed by the respective subcommittee chairperson.

===(k) Nonapplicability of FACA– ===

 The Federal Advisory Committee Act(5 U.S.C. App.)shall not apply to the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] and its subcommittees.

===(l) Definitions– ===

In this section, the following definitions apply:

====(1) Advisory Committee– ====

The term “ [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Advisory Committee]] ” means the [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Aviation Security Advisory Committee]] established under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/44946 subsection (a)] .

====(2) Assistant Secretary– ====

The term “ [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Assistant Secretary]] ” means the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Assistant Secretary of Homeland Security]] ( [[w:Transportation Security Administration|Transportation Security Administration]] ).

====(3) Perimeter security– ====

The term “perimeter security” —
:(A) means procedures or systems to monitor, secure, and prevent unauthorized access to an airport, including its airfield and terminal; and
:(B) includes the fence area surrounding an airport, access gates, and access controls..
</blockquote>
