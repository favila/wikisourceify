{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for a biennial appropriations process with the exception of defense spending and to enhance oversight and the performance of the Federal Government.

={{anchor|id0639A90FED134DBAAA3117DD035AEC74}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Biennial Appropriations Act”.

={{anchor|IDFFA7628DA2DC40A58147362192BBFE38}}Sec. 2. Revision of timetable=

 Section 300 of the Congressional Budget Act of 1974([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 2 U.S.C. 631] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

={{anchor|id01B7916D275F43B883B6CA63C96A0A15}}Sec. 300. Timetable=



=={{anchor|idA5F99353CB284231B17D2020A1DB5EDF}}(a) Timetable– ==



==={{anchor|idF7A88EF501714653B4943733D4D21288}}(1) In general– ===

The timetable with respect to the congressional budget process for any fiscal year is as follows:On or before:Action to be completed:First Monday in FebruaryPresident submits his budget.February 15Congressional Budget Office submits report to Budget Committees.Not later than 6 weeks after President submits budgetCommittees submit views and estimates to Budget Committees.April 1Budget Committees report concurrent resolution on the budget.April 15Congress completes action on concurrent resolution on the budget.May 15Biennial appropriation bills and the defense appropriation bill may be considered in the House as provided in subsection (b).June 10House Appropriations Committee reports last appropriation bill.June 15Congress completes action on reconciliation legislation.June 30House completes action on appropriation bills.August 1Congress completes action on appropriation bills.October 1Fiscal year begins.

==={{anchor|ID196A1F38A51B4ACF8BB9B0AEA71C034A}}(2) Special Rule– ===

In the case of any first session of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that begins in any year immediately following a leap year and during which the term of a [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] (except a [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] who succeeds himself or herself) begins, the following dates shall supersede those set forth in subsection (a):First SessionOn or before:Action to be completed:First Monday in AprilPresident submits his budget.April 15Congressional Budget Office submits report to Budget Committees.April 20Committees submit views and estimates to Budget Committees.May 15Budget Committees report concurrent resolution on the biennial budget.June 1Congress completes action on concurrent resolution on the biennial budget.July 1Biennial appropriation bills may be considered in the House.July 20Biennial appropriation bills and the defense appropriation bill may be considered in the House as provided in subsection (b).August 1Congress completes action on biennial appropriations bills and reconciliation legislation.October 1Biennium begins.

=={{anchor|id9E8E844DABAC457792454DE581AE8E85}}(b) Biennial appropriation bills and defense appropriation bill– ==

Appropriation bills shall be enacted as follows:

==={{anchor|id498C90E322A2454FB34E6D12DE9B7139}}(1) Odd-numbered years– ===

In odd-numbered years [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] shall consider pursuant to the budget process in this title and enact—
:(A) an annual defense appropriation bill; and
:(B) biennial appropriation bills for—
::(i) Agriculture;
::(ii) Transportation, HUD;
::(iii) Interior, Environment;
::(iv) Labor, HHS, Education; and
::(v) Military Construction, Veterans Affairs.

==={{anchor|idAF05FD0F0D57422683283030C35EAF88}}(2) Even-numbered years– ===

In even-numbered years [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] shall consider pursuant to the budget process in this title and enact—
:(A) an annual defense appropriation bill; and
:(B) biennial appropriation bills for—
::(i) Commerce, Justice, Science;
::(ii) Energy and Water;
::(iii) Homeland Security;
::(iv) Financial Services;
::(v) Legislative Branch; and
::(vi) State–Foreign Operations..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|IDE0D9A2FA1AD046DA85DD2038885D3C70}}Sec. 3. Amendments to the Congressional Budget and Impoundment Control Act of 1974=



=={{anchor|ID0DBD14227D344FD2862C89A6182AB8C9}}(a) Definitions– ==

<blockquote>
:(11) The term biennium means the period of 2 consecutive fiscal years beginning on October 1..
</blockquote>

::(C) inserting after“for the fiscal year of that resolution”the following:“for defense and for each fiscal year in the biennium”;

:(3) in subsection (f)(2)(A), by—

={{anchor|IDAB66C305360942DE87DA490399D09ED0}}Sec. 5. Two-year appropriations; title and style of appropriations Acts=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/105 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 105] of title 1, United States Code] , is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>

={{anchor|ID607D8EF4CC5143A4AF47C1B4260D43D8}}Sec. 105. Title and style of appropriations Acts=



=={{anchor|ID1CE101CFCD024229979E7A521B47AC1C}}(a) In General– ==



==={{anchor|idAAC3B9E3FD0F4CA4B861C7FB85E7E4C8}}(1) Nondefense– ===

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/105 paragraph (2)] , the style and title of all Acts making appropriations for the support of the Government shall be as follows:“An Act making appropriations (here insert the object) for each fiscal year in the biennium of fiscal years (here insert the fiscal years of the biennium).”.

==={{anchor|idC6F783ABE2964ACD92DEE390640149F7}}(2) Defense– ===

The style and title of Acts making appropriations for the support of defense shall be as follows:“An Act making appropriations for defense for fiscal year (here insert the fiscal year).”.

==={{anchor|ID63D95CBF4ACA4BCB9507DFDE85AB5254}}(3) Amounts– ===

All Acts making regular appropriations for the support of the Government shall specify the amount of appropriations provided for each fiscal year in such period.

=={{anchor|IDE8099B01DA7A408EA97E7A970756250F}}(b) Definitions– ==

In this section—
:(1) the term biennium has the same meaning as in section 3(11) of the Congressional Budget and Impoundment Control Act of 1974([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/622 2 U.S.C. 622(11)] ] ); and
:(2) Acts described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/105 subsection (a)(1)] shall be considered as provided in section 300(b) of the Congressional Budget Act of 1974([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/631 2 U.S.C. 631(b)] )..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|ID168DC8C2FD2043BDBFF443D8A089043C}}Sec. 6. Multiyear authorizations=



=={{anchor|IDB888467037754127A27498706037BD24}}(a) In General– ==

 Title III of the Congressional Budget Act of 1974is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|ID4B3D52E83112459A9A17219B5727ED52}}Sec. 316. Authorizations of appropriations==



==={{anchor|ID34B608C34F954DD3AEB42F27623C9CCD}}(a) Point of Order– ===

It shall not be in order in the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] or the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] to consider—
:(1) any bill, joint resolution, amendment, motion, or conference report that authorizes appropriations for a period of less than 2 fiscal years, unless the program, project, or activity for which the appropriations are authorized will require no further appropriations and will be completed or terminated after the appropriations have been expended; and
:(2) in any odd-numbered year, any authorization or revenue bill or joint resolution until [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] completes action on the biennial budget resolution, all regular biennial appropriations bills, and all reconciliation bills.

==={{anchor|ID1AA6297C2F084978879BD10B46E50875}}(b) Applicability– ===

In the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] , subsection (a)shall not apply to—
:(1) defense;
:(2) any measure that is privileged for consideration pursuant to a rule or statute;
:(3) any matter considered in Executive Session; or
:(4) an appropriations measure or reconciliation bill..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|idADCEEF3B60A44FD2A9718E89C3B7EE7C}}Sec. 7. Congressional oversight=



=={{anchor|idC1D33BA9A3FC4DFCB5B763B1B9C75B7F}}(a) In general– ==

In each year that the activities of an agency are not required to be funded pursuant to section 300(b) of the Congressional Budget Act of 1974, the committee of the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House]] and the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]] with legislative jurisdiction over that agency shall hold a joint oversight hearing with the corresponding subcommittee of the Committee on Appropriations of their respective [[w:United States House of Representatives|House]] with jurisdiction over the agency.

=={{anchor|id54933ECF03EB461988851FE0AA928F79}}(b) Hearing– ==

The hearing required by subsection (a)shall review—
:(1) the mission of the agency;
:(2) the impact of biennial budgeting on agency efficiency;
:(3) the cost savings associated with biennial budgeting;
:(4) new programs created in the off year of the agency budget; and
:(5) programs that were terminated in the off year of the agency budget.

={{anchor|id9D0C80CDC149430EA267C870926982D0}}Sec. 8. Report on two-year fiscal period=

Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of OMB]] shall—
:(1) determine the impact and feasibility of changing the definition of a fiscal year and the budget process based on that definition to a 2-year fiscal period with a biennial budget process based on the 2-year period; and
:(2) report the findings of the study to the [[w:United States House Committee on the Budget|Committees on the Budget of the House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on the Budget|Senate]] .

={{anchor|ID057FB9BA30F04AA9A53645AC1A6F3E23}}Sec. 9. Effective date=

Except as provided in section 7, this Act and the amendments made by this Act shall take effect on January 1, 2015, and shall apply to budget resolutions and appropriations for the biennium beginning with fiscal year 2016.
