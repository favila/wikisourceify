{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 39, United States Code, to cap rural post office closures at no more than 5 percent of total closures in any given year, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Securing Access to Rural Postal Services Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Treatment of closures and consolidations=



==(a) Rural post office annual closure limitation– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404a Section 404a(a)of title 39, United States Code] , is amended—
:(A) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404a paragraph (2)] , by striking “or” at the end;
:(B) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404a paragraph (3)] , by striking the period and inserting “; or” ; and
:(C) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
::(4) in any calendar year, close, consolidate, or suspend the operations of more than 5 percent of the postal facilities that—
:::(A) were operating as of the first day of such calendar year; and
:::(B) were within the K or L cost ascertainment grouping as of June 23, 2011..
</blockquote>


===(2) Definition– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404a Section 404a of title 39, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(d) For purposes of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404a subsection (a)(4)] , the term postal facility means a post office, post office branch, post office classified station, or other facility which is operated by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] , and the primary function of which is to provide retail postal services..
</blockquote>


==(b) Alternative postal access choice– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 Section 404(d)of title 39, United States Code] , is amended by striking all that precedes [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 paragraph (2)] and inserting the following:

<blockquote>
:(d) 
::(1) The [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] , prior to making a determination under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 subsection (a)(3)] as to the necessity for the closing or consolidation or any post office, shall—
:::(A) provide adequate notice of its intention to close or consolidate such post office at least 60 days prior to the proposed date of such closing or consolidation to postal customers served by such post office;
:::(B) conduct a nonbinding survey on the proposed closing or consolidation to allow postal customers served by such post office an opportunity to indicate their preference between or among—
::::(i) the closing or consolidation; and
::::(ii) 1 or more alternative options; and
:::(C) ensure that—
::::(i) should the closure or consolidation of a post office be deemed necessary, it shall be the policy of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] to provide alternative access to postal services to those served by the post office by—
:::::(I) the alternative option under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 subparagraph (B)(ii)] ; or
:::::(II) if more than one, the option under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 subparagraph (B)(ii)] preferred by the greatest number of survey respondents; and
::::(ii) if the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] is unable to provide alternative access through the option described in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 clause (i)] or if such option would be cost prohibitive, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] —
:::::(I) may provide alternative access through a different means; and
:::::(II) shall provide written notice to postal customers served by the post office identifying and explaining why the option identified by [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 clause (i)] was not possible or cost prohibitive, as the case may be..
</blockquote>
