{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act and the Federal Water Pollution Control Act to modernize and enhance the Federal Government’s response to oil spills, to improve oversight and regulation of offshore drilling, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HEB1B81B0B6AC4FAAB6E5ACEE8E556D32}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Secure All Facilities to Effectively Guard the United States Against and Respond to Dangerous Spills Act of 2013”or the“SAFEGUARDS Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|HBB19941E75E142E997BFDBC08D5A6E06}}Sec. 2. Oil spill response plans for drilling on the outer Continental Shelf=

The Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1331 43 U.S.C. 1331 et seq.] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

={{anchor|H389EDFFF70834AF19B30311A9A59BAAB}}Sec. 32. Oil spill response plan requirements=

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may not issue any permit or other authorization for exploration for or production of oil or gas under a lease under this Act unless an oil spill response plan for the operations of the facility on which the activity is conducted has been approved by the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] of the Department in which the Coast Guard is operating as meeting the requirements for such a plan under section 311(j) of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/33/1321 33 U.S.C. 1321(j)] )..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H593D3A9D48404834BD6B7331F6A34B3C}}Sec. 3. Approval of oil spill response plans=

 Section 311(j)(5)(A) of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/33/1321 33 U.S.C. 1321(j)(5)(A)] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(iii) Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of the SAFEGUARDS Act of 2013, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall issue regulations to ensure that an owner or operator of an offshore facility is not in compliance with requirements under this paragraph unless the owner or operator submits the plan required under clause (i)to the [[w:Department of Homeland Security|Secretary]] of the Department in which the Coast Guard is operating and the Secretary approves the plan..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H8EA45303901F44C19118C1F88D73BEDC}}Sec. 4. Containment device or blowout preventer failure=

 Section 311(j)(5)(A)(i) of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/33/1321 33 U.S.C. 1321(j)(5)(A)(i)] ] )is amended by inserting before the period at the end the following:“, including as the result of an uncontrolled or uncontained discharge from a well”.

={{anchor|H5D0043C99BF04DBCB626CF500D02146C}}Sec. 5. Water quality monitoring=

 Section 311(d)(2) of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/33/1321 33 U.S.C. 1321(d)(2)] ] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(N) Water quality monitoring by the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] of waters affected by discharges of oil or other hazardous substances that begins not later than 48 hours after such a discharge is reported..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|H0A3B7C50B238425888B1018A0E16D33C}}Sec. 6. Extension of deadline for review of exploration plan=

 Section 11(c)(1) of the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1340 43 U.S.C. 1340(c)(1)] ] )is amended—
:(1) by striking“thirty days”and inserting“90 days”;
:(2) by inserting before the last sentence the following:“The Secretary may extend such 90-day period by 60 days if the Secretary certifies that such extension is necessary to allow adequate consideration of the plan.”; and
:(3) by striking“under the preceding sentence”and inserting“under this paragraph”.

={{anchor|HEB6AA7AC26684C969399F6F6E8D5B380}}Sec. 7. National Incident Commander=

 Section 311(d)(2)(I) of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/33/1321 33 U.S.C. 1321(d)(2)(I)] ] )is amended by inserting before the period at the end the following:“, which shall include the designation of the Commandant of the Coast Guard as the National Incident Commander for all activities in response to such a discharge or threat if such a discharge or threat affects waters with respect to which the Coast Guard is responsible for response efforts under the Plan”.

={{anchor|H7233D1D1EE4049B88E3BAA23B3FCD281}}Sec. 8. Categorical exclusions prohibited=

 Section 11(c) of the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1340 43 U.S.C. 1340(c)] ] )is amended by redesignating paragraph (4)as paragraph (5), and by inserting after paragraph (3)the following:

<blockquote>
:(4) An exploration plan submitted under this subsection shall not be eligible for a categorical exclusion under the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] )..
</blockquote>


={{anchor|HCA670D9472984EBCB1E79B47CEE79F34}}Sec. 9. Update of National Contingency Plan=

Not later than 6 months after the date of enactment of this Act, and at least every 5 years thereafter, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] shall update the National Contingency Plan required under section 311(d) of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/33/1321 33 U.S.C. 1321(d)] )and shall ensure that each update includes separate response plans for—
:(1) discharges of oil or other hazardous materials into or upon land; and
:(2) discharges of oil or other hazardous materials into or upon water.
