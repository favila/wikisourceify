{{Center|A BILL}}

 To establish in the Department of Veterans Affairs a continuing medical education program for licensed medical professionals to increase knowledge and recognition of medical conditions common to veterans and family members of veterans, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Department of Veterans Affairs continuing medical professional ed­u­ca­tion program=



==(a) Establishment of continuing medical education program– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall establish a continuing medical education program for medical professionals with the goal of—
:(A) increasing knowledge and recognition of medical conditions common to veterans and family members of veterans; and
:(B) improving outreach to veterans and family members of veterans.

===(2) Education provided– ===

The program shall include education on the following:
:(A) Dealing with patients who are veterans or family members of veterans.
:(B) Identifying and treating common mental and physical conditions of veterans and family members of veterans.
:(C) Programs and benefits available to veterans and family members of veterans through the following organizations:
::(i) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].
::(ii) The [[w:United States Department of Labor|Department of Labor]].
::(iii) Such other Federal agencies and non-Federal organizations as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]considers appropriate.
:(D) The health care system of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].
:(E) Such other matters as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]considers appropriate.

==(b) Duration of program– ==

The program shall be carried out during the five-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act.

==(c) Administration of program– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall carry out the program on an Internet website of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

===(2) Curriculum and credit provided– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall determine the curriculum of the program and the number of hours of credit to provide to participating medical professionals for continuing medical education.

===(3) Accreditation– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall ensure that the program is accredited in as many States as practicable.

===(4) Consistency with existing rules– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall ensure that the program is consistent with the rules and regulations of the following:
:(A) The medical licensing agency of each State in which the program is accredited.
:(B) Such medical credentialing organizations as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]considers appropriate.

===(5) User cost– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall carry out the program at no cost to participating medical professionals.

==(d) Medical professional defined– ==

In this section, the term medical professional means any of the following individuals who are licensed by an appropriate medical authority in the United States and in good standing:
:(1) A doctor.
:(2) A nurse.
:(3) A physician assistant.
:(4) A psychologist.
:(5) A psychiatrist.
:(6) Such other individuals as the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]considers appropriate.
