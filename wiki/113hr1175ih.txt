{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Federal Water Pollution Control Act and direct the Secretary of the Interior to conduct a study with respect to stormwater runoff from oil and gas operations, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Focused Reduction of Effluence and Stormwater runoff through Hydrofracking Environmental Regulation Act of 2013 ”or the“ FRESHER Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Stormwater runoff from oil, gas, and mining operations=



==(a) Limitation on permit requirement– ==

   Section 402 of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/33/1342 33 U.S.C. 1342])is amended by striking subsection (l) and inserting the following:

<blockquote> 

===(l) Limitation on permit requirement– ===

The [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] shall not require a permit under this section for discharges composed entirely of return flows from irrigated agriculture, nor shall the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]] directly or indirectly, require any State to require such a permit. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Definitions– ==

   Section 502 of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/33/1362 33 U.S.C. 1362])is amended—
:(1) by striking paragraph (24) ; and
:(2) by redesignating paragraph (25) as paragraph (24) .

==(c) Study– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] shall conduct a study of stormwater impacts with respect to any area that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] determines may be contaminated by stormwater runoff associated with oil or gas operations, which shall include—
:(A) an analysis of measurable contamination in such area;
:(B) an analysis of ground water resources in such area; and
:(C) an analysis of the susceptibility of aquifers in such area to contamination from stormwater runoff associated with such operations.

===(2) Report– ===

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this section, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the results of studies conducted under paragraph (1) .
