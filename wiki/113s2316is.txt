{{Center|A BILL}}

 To require the Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs to submit a report on wait times for veterans seeking medical appointments and treatment from the Department of Veterans Affairs, to prohibit closure of medical facilities of the Department, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Report on wait times for veterans seeking medical appointments and treatment from the Department of Veterans Affairs and prohibition on closure of medical facilities=



==(a) Report– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General of the Department of Veterans Affairs]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on wait times for veterans seeking medical appointments and treatment from the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]].

===(2) Elements– ===

The report required byparagraph (1)shall include the following:
:(A) Information on wait times for veterans throughout the United States in seeking medical appointments and treatment from the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].
:(B) A determination by the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General]]as to whether the wait times for veterans seeking medical appointments and treatment from the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]have been prolonged by employees of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]without a valid medical or administrative reason.

==(b) Prohibition on closure of medical facilities of the Department– ==

After the date of the enactment of this Act, and before the submittal of the report undersubsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall not—
:(1) close any medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]; or
:(2) use any funds of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to prepare any environment impact statement that is related to the closure of a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].

==(c) Prolonged wait times without valid reason– ==



===(1) Prohibition on closure of medical facilities of the Department– ===

Except as provided inparagraphs (2)and(3), if, in the report submitted undersubsection (a), the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General]]determines that wait times for veterans seeking medical appointments and treatment from the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]have been prolonged by employees of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]without a valid medical or administrative reason, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall not close any medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]or use any funds of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to prepare any environment impact statement that is related to the closure of a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]].

===(2) Exception– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]may close a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]or use funds of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]to prepare an environmental impact statement related to the closure of a medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]if, not later than 180 days before such closure or use of funds, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines and certifies to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that the closure of such medical facility will not do any of the following:
:(A) Increase the wait times for veterans in the catchment area of such medical facility in seeking medical appointments and treatment from such medical facility.
:(B) Decrease the quality of care provided to such veterans.
:(C) Impede the access of such veterans to specialized health care services.
:(D) Impede the access of such veterans to residential rehabilitation treatment programs of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]and other inpatient care.
:(E) Increase the distance required to be traveled by such veterans to receive inpatient and outpatient care.
:(F) Impede the access of such veterans that are members of Indian tribes (as that term is defined in  section 4 of the Indian Self-Determination and Education Assistance Act ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/450b 25 U.S.C. 450b])) to medical care.

===(3) Termination– ===

The prohibition underparagraph (1)shall terminate on the date on which the [[w:Inspector General|Inspector General]]determines and certifies to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that wait times for veterans seeking medical appointments and treatment from the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]are not being prolonged by employees of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]without a valid medical or administrative reason.

==(d) Environmental impact statement defined– ==

In this section, the term environmental impact statement means the detailed statement of environmental impacts of a project required to be prepared pursuant to the  National Environmental Policy Act of 1969 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.]).
