{{Center|A BILL}}

To allow consumers to unlock mobile wireless devices for interoperability purposes.

={{anchor|S1}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Wireless Device Independence Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|idE8634A1A348E41AB9575A82035E216FC}}Sec. 2. Allowing consumers to unlock cell phones=



=={{anchor|idB068FDC011A045768BF4159531FA4F40}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/1201 Section 1201(a)(1)(B) of title 17, United States Code] , is amended by striking“not apply to persons”and inserting the following: “not apply to—

<blockquote>
:(i) a user of a computer program, in the form of firmware or software, that enables a wireless telephone handset, or other wireless device that can connect to the Internet, originally acquired from the operator of a wireless telecommunications network or retailer to connect to a different wireless telecommunications network if—
::(I) the user legally owns a copy of the computer program;
::(II) the use of the computer program by the user is solely for the purpose of connecting to such wireless telecommunications network; and
::(III) the access to such wireless telecommunications network is authorized by the operator of the network; or
:(ii) persons.
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|id5AFF92347AA2442F9EE707786EFF4A8F}}(b) Conforming amendments– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/1201 Section 1201(a)(1) of title 17, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/1201 subparagraph (C)] , by striking“subparagraph (B)”and inserting“[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/1201 subparagraph (B)(ii)] ”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/1201 subparagraph (E)] , by striking“Neither the exception”and inserting“Neither of the exceptions”.

={{anchor|idB1958051D6D54472B31E6C04AA75B61D}}Sec. 3. Effective date=

The amendments made by this Act shall take effect as if enacted on January 26, 2013.
