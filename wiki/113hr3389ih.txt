{{Center|A BILL}}

 To repeal the Consumer Financial Civil Penalty Fund and to deposit existing amounts in such Fund into the Treasury, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ CFPB Slush Fund Elimination Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Repeal of Fund=



==(a) In general– ==

   Section 1017 of the Consumer Financial Protection Act of 2010 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/5497 12 U.S.C. 5497] )is amended—
:(1) in subsection (c) , by striking“and in the Civil Penalty Fund established under subsection (d)”; and
:(2) by amending subsection (d) to read as follows:

<blockquote> 

====(d) Penalty funds deposited into the general fund– ====

If the [[w:|Bureau]]obtains a civil penalty against any person in any judicial or administrative action under Federal consumer financial laws, the [[w:|Bureau]]shall deposit the amount of the penalty collected into the general fund of the Treasury. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Funds deposited in Treasury– ==

The [[w:Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System]]shall deposit all amounts in the Consumer Financial Civil Penalty Fund into the general fund of the Treasury.
