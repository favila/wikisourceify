{{Center|JOINT RESOLUTION}}

 Approving the location of a memorial to commemorate the more than 5,000 slaves and free Black persons who fought for independence in the American Revolution.    Whereas[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8908  section 8908(b)(1)of title 40, United States Code], provides that the location of a commemorative work in Area I, as depicted on the map entitled“Commemorative Areas Washington, DC and Environs”, numbered 869/86501 B, and dated June 24, 2003, shall be deemed to be authorized only if a recommendation for that location is approved by law not later than 150 calendar days after [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]is notified of the recommendation;   Whereas [[Public Law 112-239|section 2860 ofPublic Law 112–239 ]] ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8903  40 U.S.C. 8903note])authorized the National Mall Liberty Fund D.C. to establish a memorial on Federal land in Area I or Area II, as depicted on such map, to honor the more than 5,000 slaves and free Black persons who fought for American independence in the Revolutionary War; and   Whereas the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]]has notified [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]of the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator’s]]determination that such memorial should be located in Area I: Now, therefore, be it    

= =

That the location of a commemorative work to honor the more than 5,000 slaves and free Black persons who fought in the American Revolution, authorized by [[Public Law 112-239|section 2860 of division B ofPublic Law 112–239 ]] ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/40/8903  40 U.S.C. 8903note]), within Area I as described on the map entitled“Commemorative Areas Washington, DC and Environs”, numbered 869/86501 B and dated June 24, 2003, is approved.
