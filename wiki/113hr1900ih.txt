{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for the timely consideration of all licenses, permits, and approvals required under Federal law with respect to the siting, construction, expansion, or operation of any natural gas pipeline projects.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Natural Gas Pipeline Permitting Reform Act” .

=Sec. 2. Regulatory approval of natural gas pipeline projects=

 Section 7 of the Natural Gas Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/717f 15 U.S.C. 717f] )is amended by adding at the end the following new subsections:

<blockquote>
:(i) The [[w:Federal Energy Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall approve or deny a certificate of public convenience and necessity that is sought under this Act not later than 12 months after providing public notice of the application.
:(j) 
::(1) The agency responsible for issuing any license, permit, or approval required under Federal law in connection with the siting, construction, expansion, or operation of any natural gas pipeline project for which a certificate of public convenience and necessity is sought under this Act shall approve or deny the issuance of the license, permit, or approval not later than 90 days after the [[w:Federal Energy Regulatory Commission|Commission]] issues its final environmental document relating to the project.
::(2) An agency may request that the [[w:Federal Energy Regulatory Commission|Commission]] extend the time period under paragraph (1)by 30 days. The [[w:Federal Energy Regulatory Commission|Commission]] shall grant such extension if the agency demonstrates that the extension is necessary because of unforeseen circumstances beyond the control of the agency.
::(3) If an agency described in paragraph (1)does not approve or deny the issuance of the license, permit, or approval within the time period specified under para-graph (1)or (2), as applicable, such license, permit, or approval shall go into effect..
</blockquote>
