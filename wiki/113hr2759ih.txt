{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 to establish a Volunteer Teacher Advisory Committee.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Teachers at the Table Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Volunteer Teacher Advisory Committee=

The  Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/6301 20 U.S.C. 6301 et seq.] )is amended by adding after  section 1004 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/6304 20 U.S.C. 6304] )the following:

<blockquote> 

=Sec. 1005. Volunteer Teacher Advisory Committee=



==(a) Establishment– ==

The [[w:|Secretary]]shall establish an advisory committee, to be known as the [[w:|Volunteer Teacher Advisory Committee]](referred to in this section as the“Committee”).

==(b) Duties and focus– ==

The duty of the [[w:|Committee]]shall be to monitor the effects of this Act, on the ground and in classrooms, and the focus of the [[w:|Committee]]shall solely be on children and families.

==(c) Membership– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The membership of the [[w:|Committee]]shall consist of 20 public elementary school or secondary school classroom teachers who—
:(A) are past or present Teachers of the Year;
:(B) have received training, or demonstrated experience, in methods of data collection, analysis, and reporting; and
:(C) have submitted an application to the [[w:|Secretary]]to serve on the [[w:|Committee]].

===(2) List; appointment– ===

The [[w:|Secretary]]shall compile a list of teachers submitting applications under paragraph (1)(B) . The members of the [[w:|Committee]]shall be appointed as follows:
:(A) The [[w:|Secretary]]shall appoint 4 teachers from the list.
:(B) The [[w:United States Senate|Majority Leader of the Senate]]shall appoint 4 teachers from the list.
:(C) The [[w:United States Senate|Minority Leader of the Senate]]shall appoint 4 teachers from the list.
:(D) The [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives]]shall appoint 4 teachers from the list.
:(E) The [[w:United States House of Representatives|Minority Leader of the House of Representatives]]shall appoint 4 teachers from the list.

===(3) Representation requirement– ===

The members of the [[w:|Committee]]shall represent diverse and multiple geographic, grade level, and specialty areas.

===(4) Term– ===

Each member of the [[w:|Committee]]shall serve on the [[w:|Committee]]for a 2-year term.

==(d) Annual report– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:|Committee]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]and the [[w:|Secretary]]—
:(A) on an annual basis, a report on the monitoring carried out under subsection (b) ; and
:(B) on a quarterly basis, updates on such monitoring.

===(2) Contents of report– ===

The report submitted under paragraph (1) shall include, at a minimum, the following:
:(A) Both quantitative and qualitative data that evaluate the effect of this section on student achievement.
:(B) The effect of this section on closing the achievement gap between high- and low-performing students, including the students identified under section 1111(b)(2)(C)(v)(II) .

==(e) Authorization of funds– ==

Of the amounts appropriated to, and available at the discretion of, the [[w:|Secretary]]for programmatic and administrative expenditures for fiscal years 2014 through 2018, a total of $500,000 shall be used to establish and carry out the functions of the [[w:|Committee]]established under this section. . 
</blockquote>
