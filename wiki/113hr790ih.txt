{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 to require the disclosure of the total number of a company’s domestic and foreign employees.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Outsourcing Accountability Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Required disclosure of number of domestic and foreign employees=

 Section 13 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/78m 15 U.S.C. 78m] )is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

==(s) Disclosure of number of domestic and foreign employees– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Beginning the first full fiscal year that begins after the date of enactment of this subsection, each issuer required to file reports with the [[w:U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|Commission]] pursuant to subsection (a)shall disclose annually to the [[w:U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|Commission]] and to shareholders—
:(A) the total number of employees of the issuer and each consolidated subsidiary of the issuer who are domiciled in the United States and listed by number in each State;
:(B) the total number of such employees physically working in and domiciled in any country other than the United States, listed by number in each country; and
:(C) the percentage increase or decrease in the numbers required under subparagraphs (A)and (B)from the previous reporting year.

===(2) Exemptions– ===



====(A) Newer public companies– ====

An issuer shall not be subject to the requirement under paragraph (1)for the first 5 years after the issuer is first required to file reports with the [[w:U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|Commission]] pursuant to subsection (a).

====(B) Smaller companies– ====

An issuer that had total annual gross revenues of less than $1,000,000,000 during its most recently completed fiscal year shall not be subject to the requirement under paragraph (1).

===(3) Regulations– ===

The [[w:U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission|Commission]] may promulgate such regulations as it considers necessary to implement the requirement set forth in paragraph (1)..
</blockquote>
