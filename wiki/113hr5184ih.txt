{{Center|A BILL}}

 To establish a National Regulatory Budget, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   National Regulatory Budget Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Establishment of the Office of Regulatory Analysis=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/ Part I of title 5, United States Code], is amended by inserting afterchapter 6the following:

<blockquote> 

===6A National Regulatory Budget and Office of Regulatory Analysis– ===

 Sec. 651. Definitions. 652. Office of Regulatory Analysis; establishment; powers. 653. Functions of Office of Regulatory Analysis; Executive branch agency compliance. 654. Public disclosure of estimate methodology and data; privacy. 655. National Regulatory Budget; timeline. 656. Executive branch agency cooperation mandatory; information sharing. 657. Enforcement. 658. Regulatory Analysis Advisory Board. 

===Sec. 651. Definitions===

In this chapter:

====(1) Aggregate costs– ====

The term aggregate costs , with respect to a covered Federal rule, means the sum of—
:(A) the direct costs of the covered Federal rule; and
:(B) the regulatory costs of the covered Federal rule.

====(2) Covered Federal rule– ====

The term covered Federal rule means—
:(A) a rule (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/551 section 551]);
:(B) an information collection requirement given a control number by the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]]; or
:(C) guidance or a directive that—
::(i) is not described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/651 subparagraph (A)]or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/651 (B)];
::(ii) 
:::(I) is mandatory in its application to regulated entities; or
:::(II) represents a statement of agency position that regulated entities would reasonably construe as reflecting the enforcement or litigation position of the agency; and
::(iii) imposes not less than $25,000,000 in annual costs on regulated entities.

====(3) Direct costs– ====

The term direct costs means—
:(A) expenditures made by an Executive branch agency that relate to the promulgation, administration, or enforcement of a covered Federal rule; or
:(B) costs incurred by an Executive branch agency, a Government corporation, the [[w:United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]], or any other instrumentality of the Federal Government because of a covered Federal rule.

====(4) Director– ====

The term Director means the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director of the Office of Regulatory Analysis]]established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/652 section 652(b)].

====(5) Executive branch agency– ====

The term Executive branch agency means—
:(A) an Executive department (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/101 section 101]); and
:(B) an independent establishment (as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/104 section 104]).

====(6) Regulated entity– ====

The term regulated entity means—
:(A) a for-profit private sector entity (including an individual who is in business as a sole proprietor);
:(B) a not-for-profit private sector entity; or
:(C) a State or local government.

====(7) Regulatory costs– ====

The term regulatory costs means all costs incurred by a regulated entity because of covered Federal rules.

===Sec. 652. Office of Regulatory Analysis; establishment; powers===



====(a) Establishment– ====

There is established in the executive branch an independent establishment to be known as the“  [[w:Executive Office of the President|Office of Regulatory Analysis]] ”.

====(b) Director– ====



=====(1) Establishment of position– =====

There shall be at the head of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Office of Regulatory Analysis]]a [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]], who shall be appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]], by and with the advice and consent of the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]].

=====(2) Term– =====



======(A) In general– ======

The term of office of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall—
:(i) be 4 years; and
:(ii) expire on the last day of February following each Presidential election.

======(B) Appointments prior to expiration of term– ======

Subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/652 subparagraph (C)], an individual appointed as [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]to fill a vacancy prior to the expiration of a term shall serve only for the unexpired portion of the term.

======(C) Service until appointment of successor– ======

An individual serving as [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]at the expiration of a term may continue to serve until a successor is appointed.

=====(3) Powers– =====



======(A) Appointment of Deputy Directors, officers, and employees– ======



=======(i) In general– =======

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]may appoint Deputy Directors, officers, and employees, including attorneys, in accordance withchapter 51andsubchapter III of chapter 53.

=======(ii) Term of Deputy Directors– =======

A Deputy Director shall serve until the expiration of the term of office of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]who appointed the Deputy Director (and until a successor to that [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]is appointed), unless sooner removed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]].

======(B) Contracting– ======



=======(i) In general– =======

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]may contract for financial and administrative services (including those related to budget and accounting, financial reporting, personnel, and procurement) with the [[w:General Services Administration|General Services Administration]], or such other Federal agency as the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]determines appropriate, for which payment shall be made in advance, or by reimbursement, from funds of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Office of Regulatory Analysis]]in such amounts as may be agreed upon by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]and the head of the Federal agency providing the services.

=======(ii) Subject to appropriations– =======

Contract authority under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/652 clause (i)]shall be effective for any fiscal year only to the extent that appropriations are available for that purpose.

===Sec. 653. Functions of Office of Regulatory Analysis; Executive branch agency compliance===



====(a) Annual report required– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

Not later than January 30 of each year, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall submit to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs|Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate]], the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Small Business and Entrepreneurship|Committee on Small Business and Entrepreneurship of the Senate]], the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives]], and the [[w:United States House Committee on Small Business|Committee on Small Business of the House of Representatives]]a report on national regulatory costs (referred to in this section as the Report ) that includes the information specified under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 paragraph (2)].

=====(2) Contents– =====

Each Report shall include the following:
:(A) An estimate, for the fiscal year during which the Report is submitted and for the preceding fiscal year, of each of the following:
::(i) The regulatory costs imposed by each Executive branch agency on regulated entities.
::(ii) The aggregate costs imposed by each Executive branch agency.
::(iii) The aggregate costs imposed by all Executive branch agencies combined.
::(iv) The direct costs incurred by the Federal Government because of covered Federal rules issued by each Executive branch agency.
::(v) The sum of the costs described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 clauses (iii)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 (iv)].
::(vi) The regulatory costs imposed by each Executive branch agency on small businesses, small organizations, and small governmental jurisdictions (as those terms are defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/601 section 601]).
::(vii) The sum of the costs described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 clause (vi)].
:(B) An analysis of any major changes in estimation methodology used by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Office of Regulatory Analysis]]since the previous annual report.
:(C) An analysis of any major estimate changes caused by improved or inadequate data since the previous annual report.
:(D) Recommendations, both general and specific, regarding the following:
::(i) How regulations may be streamlined, simplified, and modernized.
::(ii) Regulations that should be repealed.
::(iii) How the Federal Government may reduce the costs of regulations without diminishing the effectiveness of regulations.
:(E) Any other information that the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]determines may be of assistance to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]in determining the National Regulatory Budget required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/655 section 655].

====(b) Regulatory analysis of new rules– ====



=====(1) Requirement– =====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall publish in the Federal Register and on the website of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Office of Regulatory Analysis]]a regulatory analysis of each proposed covered Federal rule issued by an Executive branch agency, and each proposed withdrawal or modification of a covered Federal rule by an Executive branch agency, that—
:(A) imposes costs on a regulated entity; or
:(B) reduces costs imposed on a regulated entity.

=====(2) Contents– =====

Each regulatory analysis published under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 paragraph (1)]shall include—
:(A) an estimate of the change in regulatory cost of each proposed covered Federal rule (or proposed withdrawal or modification of a covered Federal rule); and
:(B) any other information or recommendation that the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]may choose to provide.

=====(3) Timing of regulatory analysis– =====



======(A) Initial regulatory analysis– ======

Not later than 60 days after the date on which the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]receives a copy of a proposed covered Federal rule from the head of an Executive branch agency under paragraph (4) , the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall publish an initial regulatory analysis.

======(B) Revised regulatory analysis– ======

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]may publish a revised regulatory analysis at any time.

=====(4) Notice to Director of proposed covered Federal rule– =====

The head of an Executive branch agency shall provide a copy of each proposed covered Federal rule to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]in a manner prescribed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]].

====(c) Effective dates– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

Except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 paragraph (2)], a covered Federal rule may not take effect earlier than 75 days after the date on which the head of the Executive branch agency proposing the covered Federal rule submits a copy of the proposed covered Federal rule to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]in the manner prescribed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 subsection (b)(4)].

=====(2) Exception– =====

If the head of the Executive branch agency proposing a covered Federal rule determines that the public health or safety or national security requires that the covered Federal rule be promulgated earlier than the date specified under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 paragraph (1)], the head of the Executive branch agency may promulgate the covered Federal rule without regard to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 paragraph (1)].

===Sec. 654. Public disclosure of estimate methodology and data; privacy===



====(a) Privacy– ====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall comply with all relevant privacy laws, including—
:(1) the  Confidential Information Protection and Statistical Efficiency Act of 2002 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/3501 44 U.S.C. 3501 note]);
:(2)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/13/9  section 9of title 13]; and
:(3)    [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/6103 section 6103]of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 .

====(b) Disclosure– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

To the maximum extent permitted by law, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall disclose, by publication in the Federal Register and on the website of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Office of Regulatory Analysis]], the methodology and data used to generate the estimates in the report on national regulatory costs required pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 section 653].

=====(2) Goal of disclosure– =====

In disclosing the methodology and data under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 paragraph (1)], the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall seek to provide sufficient information so that outside researchers may replicate the results contained in the report on national regulatory costs.

===Sec. 655. National Regulatory Budget; timeline===



====(a) Definition– ====

In this section:

=====(1) Annual agency regulatory cost cap– =====

The term annual agency regulatory cost cap means the maximum amount of regulatory costs that an Executive branch agency may impose in a fiscal year.

=====(2) Annual overall regulatory cost cap– =====

The term annual overall regulatory cost cap means the maximum amount of regulatory costs that all Executive branch agencies combined may impose in a fiscal year.

=====(3) National regulatory budget– =====

The term National Regulatory Budget means an Act of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]that establishes, for a fiscal year—
:(A) the annual overall regulatory cost cap; and
:(B) an annual agency regulatory cost cap for each Executive branch agency.

====(b) Committee deadlines– ====



=====(1) Referral– =====

Not later than March 31 of each year—
:(A) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Small Business and Entrepreneurship|Committee on Small Business and Entrepreneurship of the Senate]]shall refer to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs|Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate]]a bill that sets forth a National Regulatory Budget for the fiscal year beginning on October 1 of that year; and
:(B) the [[w:United States House Committee on Small Business|Committee on Small Business of the House of Representatives]]shall refer to the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives]]a bill that sets forth a National Regulatory Budget for the fiscal year beginning on October 1 of that year.

=====(2) Reporting– =====

Not later than May 31 of each year—
:(A) the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs|Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate]]shall report a bill establishing a National Regulatory Budget for the fiscal year beginning on October 1 of that year; and
:(B) the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives]]shall report a bill establishing a National Regulatory Budget for the fiscal year beginning on October 1 of that year.

====(c) Passage– ====

Not later than July 31 of each year, the [[w:United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate|Senate]]shall each pass a bill establishing a National Regulatory Budget for the fiscal year beginning on October 1 of that year.

====(d) Presentment– ====

Not later than September 15 of each year, [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]shall pass and present to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]a National Regulatory Budget for the fiscal year beginning on October 1 of that year.

====(e) Default budget– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

If a National Regulatory Budget is not enacted with respect to a fiscal year, the most recently enacted National Regulatory Budget shall apply to that fiscal year.

=====(2) Default initial budget– =====



======(A) Calculation– ======

If a National Regulatory Budget is not enacted with respect to a fiscal year, and no National Regulatory Budget has previously been enacted—
:(i) the annual agency regulatory cost cap for an Executive branch agency for the fiscal year shall be equal to the amount of regulatory costs imposed by that Executive branch agency on regulated entities during the preceding fiscal year, as estimated by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]in the annual report submitted to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 section 653(a)]; and
:(ii) the annual overall regulatory cost cap for the fiscal year shall be equal to the sum of the amounts described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/655 clause (i)].

======(B) Effect– ======

For purposes of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/657 section 657], an annual agency regulatory cost cap described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/655 subparagraph (A)]that applies to a fiscal year shall have the same effect as if the annual agency regulatory cost cap were part of a National Regulatory Budget applicable to that fiscal year.

====(f) Initial budget– ====

The first National Regulatory Budget shall be with respect to fiscal year 2016.

===Sec. 656. Executive branch agency cooperation mandatory; information sharing===



====(a) Executive branch agency cooperation mandatory– ====

Not later than 45 days after the date on which the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]requests any information from an Executive branch agency, the Executive branch agency shall provide the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]with the information.

====(b) Memoranda of understanding regarding confidentiality– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

An Executive branch agency may require the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]to enter into a memorandum of understanding regarding the confidentiality of information provided by the Executive branch agency to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 subsection (a)]as a condition precedent to providing any requested information.

=====(2) Degree of confidentiality or data protection– =====

An Executive branch agency may not require a greater degree of confidentiality or data protection from the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]in a memorandum of understanding entered into under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 paragraph (1)]than the Executive branch agency itself must adhere to.

=====(3) Scope– =====

A memorandum of understanding entered into by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]and an Executive branch agency under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 paragraph (1)]shall—
:(A) be general in scope; and
:(B) govern all pending and future requests made to the Executive branch agency by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]].

====(c) Sanctions for non-Cooperation– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

Any discretionary appropriations made available to an Executive branch agency for a fiscal year shall be reduced pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 paragraph (2)]if, during that fiscal year, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]finds that—
:(A) the Executive branch agency has failed to timely provide information that the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]requested under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 subsection (a)];
:(B) the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]has provided notice of the failure described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 subparagraph (A)]to the Executive branch agency;
:(C) the Executive branch agency has failed to cure the failure described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 subparagraph (A)]within 30 days of being notified under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 subparagraph (B)]; and
:(D) the information that the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]requested under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 subsection (a)]—
::(i) is in the possession of the Executive branch agency; or
::(ii) may reasonably be developed by the Executive branch agency.

=====(2) Rescission– =====

Subject to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 paragraph (3)], on the date that the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]], in consultation with the [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Federal Financial Management]]and [[w:Financial Management Service|Financial Management Service]], makes a finding under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 paragraph (1)]that an Executive branch agency is non-cooperative, there is rescinded one-half of 1 percent of the unobligated discretionary appropriations available to such agency.

=====(3) Appeals– =====



======(A) In general– ======

The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]]may reduce the amount of the rescission under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 paragraph (2)], or except as provided in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 subparagraph (B)], waive, a sanction imposed under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 paragraph (1)]if the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]finds that—
:(i) the sanction is unwarranted;
:(ii) the sanction is disproportionate to the gravity of the failure;
:(iii) the failure has been cured; or
:(iv) providing the requested information would adversely affect national security.

======(B) No waiver for historically non-compliant agencies– ======

The [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Director of the Office of Management and Budget]]may not waive a sanction imposed on an Executive branch agency under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 paragraphs (1)]and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 (2)]if the Executive branch agency has a history of non-compliance with requests for information by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director of the Office of Regulatory Analysis]]under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 subsection (a)].

====(d) National security– ====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]may not require an Executive branch agency to provide information under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/656 subsection (a)]that would adversely affect national security.

===Sec. 657. Enforcement===



====(a) Exceeding annual agency regulatory cost cap– ====

An Executive branch agency that exceeds the annual agency regulatory cost cap imposed by the National Regulatory Budget for a fiscal year may not promulgate a new covered Federal rule that increases regulatory costs until the Executive branch agency no longer exceeds the annual agency regulatory cost cap imposed by the applicable National Regulatory Budget.

====(b) Determination of Director– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

An Executive branch agency may not promulgate a covered Federal rule unless the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]determines, in conducting the regulatory analysis of the covered Federal rule under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 section 653(b)(3)(A)]that, after the Executive branch agency promulgates the covered Federal rule, the Executive branch agency will not exceed the annual agency regulatory cost cap for that Executive branch agency.

=====(2) Timing– =====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall make a determination under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/657 paragraph (1)]with respect to a proposed covered Federal rule not later than 60 days after the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]receives a copy of the proposed covered Federal rule under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 section 653(b)(4)].

====(c) Effect of violation of this section– ====



=====(1) No force or effect– =====

A covered Federal rule that is promulgated in violation of this section shall have no force or effect.

=====(2) Judicial Enforcement– =====

Any party may bring an action in a district court of the United States to declare that a covered Federal rule has no force or effect because the covered Federal rule was promulgated in violation of this section.

===Sec. 658. Regulatory Analysis Advisory Board===



====(a) Establishment of Board– ====

In accordance with the  Federal Advisory Committee Act (5 U.S.C. App.), the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall—
:(1) establish a [[w:Executive Office of the President|Regulatory Analysis Advisory Board]]; and
:(2) appoint not fewer than 9 and not more than 15 individuals as members of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Regulatory Analysis Advisory Board]].

====(b) Qualifications– ====

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall appoint individuals with technical and practical expertise in economics, law, accounting, science, management, and other areas that will aid the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]in preparing the annual report on national regulatory costs required under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/653 section 653]. . 
</blockquote>

=Sec. 3. Report on duplicative personnel; report on regulatory analysis=



==(a) Report on duplicative personnel– ==

Not later than December 31, 2014, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report determining positions in the Federal Government that are—
:(1) duplicative of the work performed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Office of Regulatory Analysis]]established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/652 section 652 of title 5, United States Code]; or
:(2) otherwise rendered cost ineffective by the work of the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Office of Regulatory Analysis]].

==(b) Report on regulatory analysis– ==



===(1) Report required– ===

Not later than June 30, 2015, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|Director]]shall provide to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report analyzing the practice with respect to, and the effectiveness of—
:(A)    chapter 6of title 5, United States Code(commonly known as the“  Regulatory Flexibility Act  ”); 
:(B) the  Small Business Regulatory Enforcement Fairness Act of 1996 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/601  5 U.S.C. 601note]);
:(C)    chapter 35of title 44, United States Code(commonly known as the“  Paperwork Reduction Act  ”);
:(D) each Executive order that mandates economic analysis of Federal regulations; and
:(E)   [[w:Office of Management and Budget|Office of Management and Budget]]circulars, directives, and memoranda that mandate the economic analysis of Federal regulation.

===(2) Recommendations– ===

The report under paragraph (1) shall include recommendations about how Federal regulatory analysis may be improved.

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553  Section 553(b)of title 5, United States Code], is amended, following the flush text, in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553 subparagraph (A)]by striking“interpretative rules, general statements of policy, or”.
