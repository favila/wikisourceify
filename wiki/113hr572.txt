{{Center|A BILL}}

To suspend the issuance of visas to nationals of Brazil
		  until such time as Brazil amends its laws to remove the prohibition on
		  extradition of nationals of Brazil to other countries.


{{section|HCB2AD2A30B61459C8201A86788057361|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          End Immuity for Brazilian Criminals
			 Act

        
.

{{section|HAA97FC751D054FC89F95529D1BBFCA43|2. Suspension of visas for Brazilians until Brazil amends its laws to remove the prohibition on extradition of nationals of Brazil to other countries}}
(a)
Suspension of
			 visa issuance
Notwithstanding
			 any other provision of law, no immigrant or nonimmigrant visa may be issued to
			 a citizen, subject, national, or resident of Brazil until the [[Executive Office of the President|President of the
			 United States]] determines and certifies to the [[United States Congress|Congress]] that the Government of
			 Brazil has amended its laws to remove the prohibition on extradition of
			 nationals of Brazil to other countries.

(b)
Waiver
The [[Executive Office of the President|President]] may waive the application of
			 subsection (a)
 on a case-by-case basis if the [[Executive Office of the President|President]] determines and
			 certifies to the [[United States Congress|Congress]] that it is in the national interests of the United
			 States to do so.

(c)
Effect of
			 unauthorized issuance
Any visa issued in violation of this
			 subsection shall be null and void.
