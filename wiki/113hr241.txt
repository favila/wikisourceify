        {{Center|A BILL}}
    
		To direct the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to establish
		  standards of access to care for veterans seeking health care from Department of
		  Veterans Affairs medical facilities, and for other purposes.
	
	
		
        =={{section|HB8257270E06E4C25ACCD4EE549BAC3F9|
      1.
      Short title
      This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Veterans Timely Access to Health Care
			 Act
        .
		}}==
    
    
        =={{section|H43767ADE297441CDA33E7285FD1B7260|
      2.
      Department of Veterans
			 Affairs access to medical care
			
        (a)
        Standard for
			 access to care
        
          Section 1706
			 of title 38, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following
			 new subsection:
				
					
            (d)
            
              (1)
              In managing the provision of hospital care
				and medical services under section 1710(a) of this title, the 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]
             shall
				ensure that for each veteran seeking such care or services from a 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]
            
				medical facility, the standard for access to care, determined from the date on
				which the veteran contacts the 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]
             seeking an appointment until the date
				on which a visit with an appropriate health care provider is completed, is 30
				days.
						
            
              (2)
              The 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]
             shall periodically
				review the performance of 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]
             medical facilities compared to the
				standard established under paragraph (1). The 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]
             shall submit to the
				Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate and House of Representatives a
				semi-annual report providing an assessment of the 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department’s]]
             performance in
				meeting that standard.
						
            
              (3)
              
                (A)
                Not later than 60 days
				after the end of a calendar-year quarter, the 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]
             shall submit to the
				Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate and House of Representatives a
				report for that calendar-year quarter on the experience of the 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]
            
				during the quarter covered by the report with respect to waiting times for
				veterans seeking appointments at 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]
             medical facilities.
							
              
                (B)
                Each report under subparagraph (A)
				shall include each of the following:
								
                  (i)
                  The total number of veterans waiting
				by the following categories:
									
                    (I)
                    Those waiting under 30 days for
				scheduled appointments.
									
                  
                    (II)
                    Those waiting over 30 days but
				less than 60 days.
									
                  
                    (III)
                    Those waiting over 60 days but
				less than 4 months.
									
                  
                    (IV)
                    Those waiting over 4 months but
				who cannot be scheduled within 6 months.
									
                  
                    (V)
                    Any remaining veterans who cannot
				be scheduled, with the reasons therefor.
									
                
                
                  (ii)
                  For each category set forth in
				subparagraph (A), distinctions between—
									
                    (I)
                    waiting times for primary care and
				specialty care; and
									
                  
                    (II)
                    waiting times for veterans who are
				newly enrolled versus those who were enrolled before October 1, 2001.
									
                
                
                  (iii)
                  The number of veterans who have
				enrolled in the patient enrollment system under section 1705 of this title but
				have not since such enrollment sought care at a 
                [[United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]
             medical
				facility.
								
              
            
          
          .
        
			
      
        (b)
        Effective
			 date
        
          Subsection (d) of section 1706 of title 38, United States
			 Code, shall apply with respect to hospital care and medical services provided
			 on or after the date of the enactment of this Act.
