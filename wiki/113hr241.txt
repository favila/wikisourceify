{{Center|A BILL}}

To direct the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to establish standards of access to care for veterans seeking health care from Department of Veterans Affairs medical facilities, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HB8257270E06E4C25ACCD4EE549BAC3F9}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Veterans Timely Access to Health Care Act”.

={{anchor|H43767ADE297441CDA33E7285FD1B7260}}Sec. 2. Department of Veterans Affairs access to medical care=



=={{anchor|H6AF05A45DEF344CCA2843C18BB6B1264}}(a) Standard for access to care– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1706 Section 1706 of title 38, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>
:(d) 
::(1) In managing the provision of hospital care and medical services under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1710 section 1710(a) of this title] , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall ensure that for each veteran seeking such care or services from a [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] medical facility, the standard for access to care, determined from the date on which the veteran contacts the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] seeking an appointment until the date on which a visit with an appropriate health care provider is completed, is 30 days.
::(2) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall periodically review the performance of [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] medical facilities compared to the standard established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1710 paragraph (1)] . The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall submit to theCommittees on Veterans’ Affairs of the SenateandHouse of Representativesa semi-annual report providing an assessment of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department’s]] performance in meeting that standard.
::(3) 
:::(A) Not later than 60 days after the end of a calendar-year quarter, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall submit to theCommittees on Veterans’ Affairs of the SenateandHouse of Representativesa report for that calendar-year quarter on the experience of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] during the quarter covered by the report with respect to waiting times for veterans seeking appointments at [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] medical facilities.
:::(B) Each report under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1706 subparagraph (A)] shall include each of the following:
::::(i) The total number of veterans waiting by the following categories:
:::::(I) Those waiting under 30 days for scheduled appointments.
:::::(II) Those waiting over 30 days but less than 60 days.
:::::(III) Those waiting over 60 days but less than 4 months.
:::::(IV) Those waiting over 4 months but who cannot be scheduled within 6 months.
:::::(V) Any remaining veterans who cannot be scheduled, with the reasons therefor.
::::(ii) For each category set forth in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1706 subparagraph (A)] , distinctions between—
:::::(I) waiting times for primary care and specialty care; and
:::::(II) waiting times for veterans who are newly enrolled versus those who were enrolled before October 1, 2001.
::::(iii) The number of veterans who have enrolled in the patient enrollment system under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705 section 1705 of this title] but have not since such enrollment sought care at a [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] medical facility..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|HEB5AC3F12EB843B6A36503308120901E}}(b) Effective date– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1706 Subsection (d) of section 1706 of title 38, United States Code] , shall apply with respect to hospital care and medical services provided on or after the date of the enactment of this Act.
