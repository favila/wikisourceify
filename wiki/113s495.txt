{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to require Federal agencies to hire veterans, to require States to recognize the military experience of veterans when issuing licenses and credentials to veterans, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|S1}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Careers for Veterans Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|idA65762202CE4498D8F8608819B404F1D}}Sec. 2. Employment of veterans with the Federal Government=

<blockquote>

={{anchor|id70D2B57E80204842BD8CD869639F1B76}}Sec. 3. Requirement that States recognize military experience of veterans when issuing licenses and credentials to veterans=



=={{anchor|idE8B540C4D4B349B2A2B4BEDF8BD01DA2}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4102A Section 4102A(c) of title 38, United States Code] , is amended by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4102A paragraph (9)] and inserting the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4102A paragraph (9)] :

<blockquote>
:(9) 
::(A) As a condition of a grant or contract under which funds are made available to a State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4102A subsection (b)(5)] in order to carry out[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4103A section 4103A] or[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4104 4104] of this title, the State shall—
:::(i) establish a program under which the State administers an examination to each veteran seeking a license or credential issued by the State and issues such license or credential to such veteran without requiring such veteran to undergo any training or apprenticeship if the veteran—
::::(I) receives a satisfactory score on completion of such examination, as determined by the State; and
::::(II) has not less than 10 years of experience in a military occupational specialty that, as determined by the State, is similar to a civilian occupation for which such license or credential is required by the State; and
:::(ii) submit each year to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] a report on the exams administered under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4102A clause (i)] during the most recently completed 12-month period that includes, for the period covered by the report the number of veterans who completed an exam administered by the State under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4102A clause (i)] and a description of the results of such exams, disaggregated by occupational field.
::(B) Not less frequently than once each year, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] and the [[w:United States Department of Defense|Secretary of Defense]] a report summarizing the information received by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/4102A subparagraph (A)(ii)] ..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|idFE2F211DBE1B4AD2873BDC5AEF951CF6}}(b) Effective date– ==

={{anchor|id6C220473AF4A4376BE325A323746DE0C}}Sec. 4. Support for job searches of veterans through one-stop centers=



=={{anchor|id1488FC1FCBEC465690DB4240D3F5B657}}(a) Furnishing of list of Internet resources– ==

Not later than 30 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]] shall furnish each one-stop center with a list of all Internet websites and applications that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] has identified as beneficial for veterans in pursuit of employment to their pursuit.

=={{anchor|id8B1A26E08F664FD083DA1C404BEB70E8}}(b) Identification of additional resources– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall coordinate with public and private sector entities to identify Internet websites and applications not already included in a list furnished under subsection (a)that—
:(1) match veterans seeking employment with available jobs based on the skills the veterans acquired as members of the Armed Forces; and
:(2) allow employers to post information about available jobs.

=={{anchor|idC4466783C5424905AFE3A6A663B04219}}(c) Supplements– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]] shall furnish each one-stop center with a list of Internet websites and applications identified under subsection (b).

=={{anchor|id87CF94E9F6A3450794FB09609FF1018A}}(d) Report– ==

Not later than 455 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Labor|Secretary of Labor]] shall submit to the appropriate committees of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] a report on the use of the Internet websites and applications identified under subsection (b)for the benefit of veterans in pursuit of employment.

=={{anchor|id776C2092B8DB4C0796608388B5FCD5C1}}(e) Definitions– ==

In this section:

==={{anchor|idEEAE631DC8384358B071E531237A02E6}}(1) Appropriate committees of Congress– ===

The term appropriate committees of Congress means—
:(A) theCommittee on Veterans' Affairsand theCommittee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions of the Senate; and
:(B) theCommittee on Veterans' Affairsand theCommittee on Education and the Workforce of the House of Representatives.

==={{anchor|idD50DF5A386504757B94655F434711D0C}}(2) One-stop center– ===

The term one-stop center means a center described in section 134(c) of the Workforce Investment Act of 1998([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/29/2864 29 U.S.C. 2864(c)] ).

={{anchor|id99B0DB8D6BD842D89BBF7B24092818AE}}Sec. 6. Modification of treatment under contracting goals and preferences of Department of Veterans Affairs for small businesses owned by veterans of small businesses after death of disabled veteran owners=



=={{anchor|idBB3A71C8F8B9431FA8A2F1CFDECBB197}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 Section 8127(h) of title 38, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 paragraph (3)] , by striking“rated as”and all that follows through“disability.”and inserting a period; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 paragraph (2)] , by amending[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subparagraph (C)] to read as follows:

<blockquote>
::(C) The date that—
:::(i) in the case of a surviving spouse of a veteran with a service-connected disability rated as 100 percent disabling or who dies as a result of a service-connected disability, is 10 years after the date of the veteran's death; or

={{anchor|id183868BF89EA478DB161FBACC5060CCA}}Sec. 7. Treatment of businesses after deaths of servicemember-owners for purposes of Department of Veterans Affairs contracting goals and preferences=



=={{anchor|id6F4F8BFB3F804E4E8221B9DD326F9D5B}}(a) In general– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 Section 8127 of title 38, United States Code] , is amended—
:(1) by redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subsections (i) through (l)] as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subsections (j) through (m)] , respectively; and
:(2) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subsection (h)] the following new[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 subsection (i)] :

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|id1196F465C8744E3495B948D4A7DE87D0}}(i) Treatment of businesses after death of servicemember-Owner– ====


:(1) If a member of the Armed Forces owns at least 51 percent of a small business concern and such member is killed in line of duty in the active military, naval, or air service, the surviving spouse or dependent of such member who acquires such ownership rights in such small business concern shall, for the period described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 paragraph (2)] , be treated as if the surviving spouse or dependent were a veteran with a service-connected disability for purposes of determining the status of the small business concern as a small business concern owned and controlled by veterans for purposes of contracting goals and preferences under this section.
:(2) The period referred to in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 paragraph (1)] is the period beginning on the date on which the member of the Armed Forces dies and ending on the date as follows:
::(A) In the case of a surviving spouse, the earliest of the following dates:
:::(i) The date on which the surviving spouse remarries.
:::(ii) The date on which the surviving spouse relinquishes an ownership interest in the small business concern and no longer owns at least 51 percent of such small business concern.
:::(iii) The date that is ten years after the date of the member's death.
::(B) In the case of a dependent who is not a spouse, the earliest of the following dates:
:::(i) The date on which the surviving dependant relinquishes an ownership interest in the small business concern and no longer owns at least 51 percent of such small business concern.
:::(ii) The date that is ten years after the date of the member's death..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|idFF4B95883D5E48B2906E3932C9AF74E4}}(b) Effective date– ==

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 Subsection (i) of section 8127 of such title] , as added by subsection (a), take effect on the date of the enactment of this Act and shall apply with respect to the deaths of members of the Armed Forces occurring on or after such date.

={{anchor|id2343402114664BD8A8514941E4EF4154}}Sec. 8. Special rule for treatment under contracting goals and preferences of Department of Veterans Affairs of small business concerns licensed in community property States=

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 Section 8127 of title 38, United States Code] , as amended by section 7, is further amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|id98B642B05BD244CD8050C45FA0C76AA7}}(n) Special rule for community property States– ==

Whenever the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] assesses, for purposes of this section, the degree of ownership by an individual of a small business concern licensed in a community property State, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall also assess what that degree of ownership would be if such small business concern had been licensed in a State other than a community property State. If the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] determines that such individual would have had a greater degree of ownership of the small business concern had such small business concern been licensed in a State other than a community property State, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall treat, for purposes of this section, such small business concern as if it had been licensed in a State other than a community property State..
</blockquote>
