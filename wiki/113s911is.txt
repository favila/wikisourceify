To establish an emergency transportation safety fund, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|id601206F1ADA44BAAA8BD37163758D928}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Emergency Transportation Safety Fund Act”.

=={{anchor|id10258883B9C741038270EAF4598B78E3}}Sec. 102. Establishment of Emergency Transportation Safety Fund==



==={{anchor|id966F5072652A4CEB8AA8B2073BCD53A8}}(a) In general– ===

There is established in the Treasury of the United States a trust fund to be known as the“Emergency Transportation Safety Fund”.

==={{anchor|id68B3596AB70F425D8F94AEB0BBA52DEA}}(b) Transfers to Emergency Transportation Safety Fund– ===



===={{anchor|idEE6C8D7BD18143529C48DB54D732FDC8}}(1) In general– ====

There are hereby appropriated to the Emergency Transportation Safety Fund amounts equivalent to 50 percent of the excess of—
:(A) the taxes received in the United States Treasury which are attributable to eligible 965 dividends received by corporations which are United States shareholders, over
:(B) the amount of the foreign tax credit allowed under section 901 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986which is attributable to the non-deductible portion of such eligible 965 dividends.

===={{anchor|id8F493194A1AA4315AB8D3F05116FCF92}}(2) Definitions– ====

For purposes of this subsection—

====={{anchor|id1C462A52C5DA4FFF9D81A631E0EEA04F}}(A) Eligible 965 dividend– =====

The term eligible 965 dividend means any amount received from a controlled foreign corporation for which a deduction is allowed under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 965] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as determined based on estimates made by the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]] , or the Secretary's delegate.

====={{anchor|id4549E9F9E9F549DA848F11F47B3BD68F}}(B) Non-deductible portion– =====

The term non-deductible portion means the excess of the amount of any eligible 965 dividend over the deductible portion (as defined in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 965(d)(3)] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986) of such amount.

==={{anchor|idD0B13905A6DA4FE5997078DDAFE7E0E0}}(c) Emergency relief expenditures– ===

[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/23/125 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 125(c)] of title 23, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===={{anchor|id0690490D59684964BCCF4DF3122E4D20}}(3) Emergency transportation safety fund– ====

Amounts deposited into the Emergency Transportation Safety Fund established under section 102(a) of theEmergency Transportation Safety Fund Actare authorized to be obligated to carry out, in priority order, the projects on the current list compiled by the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] under section 201(b)(1) of such Actthat meet the eligibility requirements set forth in subsection (a)..
</blockquote>


=={{anchor|id17F39D9B33574A678EA91C684FE43836}}Title II— Emergency Transportation Safety Priority List==



=={{anchor|id5293862C00054DFC80FCC2F4609E5424}}Sec. 201. Emergency transportation priorities==



==={{anchor|id66790AFB29384FFCA78D94B393D52E87}}(a) List– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] , in consultation with a representative sample of State and local government transportation officials, shall compile a prioritized list of emergency transportation projects, which will guide the allocation of funding to the States from the Emergency Transportation Safety Fund.

==={{anchor|id866A5253F3C7484495FB907C6D0968AB}}(b) Criteria– ===

In compiling the list under subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] , in addition to other criteria established by the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary]] , shall rank priorities in descending order, beginning with—
:(1) whether the project is part of the interstate highway system;
:(2) whether the project is a road or bridge that is closed for safety reasons;
:(3) the impact of the project on interstate commerce;
:(4) the volume of traffic affected by the project; and
:(5) the overall value of the project or entity.

==={{anchor|idD9C0E691C9F840E0A9E2B236AEBEE48E}}(c) Report– ===

Not later than 120 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] shall submit a report to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that includes—
:(1) a prioritized list of emergency transportation projects to be funded through the Emergency Transportation Safety Fund; and
:(2) a description of the criteria used to establish the list referred to in paragraph (1).

==={{anchor|id19B0DC3EED6B471EA24E658C9091D907}}(d) Quarterly updates– ===

Not less frequently than 4 times per year, the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Secretary of Transportation]] shall—
:(1) update the report submitted pursuant to subsection (c);
:(2) send a copy of the report to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] ; and
:(3) make a copy of the report available to the public through the [[w:United States Department of Transportation|Department of Transportation's]] website.
