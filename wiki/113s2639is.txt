{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 38, United States Code, to increase the number of graduate medical education residency positions at medical facilities of the Department of Veterans Affairs, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Veterans Affairs Health Workforce Enhancement Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Increase of graduate medical education residency positions at medical facilities of the Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302  Section 7302of title 38, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

<blockquote> 
:(e) 
::(1) In carrying out this section, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall establish medical residency programs, or ensure a sufficient number of residency positions in medical residency programs, at any medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]that the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines is experiencing a shortage of physicians.
::(2) In carrying out[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302 paragraph (1)], the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall—
:::(A) determine the specialty of health care professionals with respect to which each medical facility of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]for which the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]has made a determination under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302 paragraph (1)]is experiencing a shortage of physicians, including with respect to primary care and mental health;
:::(B) allocate the residency programs and residency positions under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302 paragraph (1)]based on the determination required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302 subparagraph (A)]; and
:::(C) notify Congress of the information used by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]to make the determination required by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302 subparagraph (A)]. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Five-Year increase– ==



===(1) In general– ===

In carrying out[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/7302  section 7302(e)of title 38, United States Code], as added by subsection (a) , during the five-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall increase the number of graduate medical education residency positions at medical facilities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]by not less than 2,000 positions.

===(2) Priority– ===

In increasing the number of graduate medical education residency positions at medical facilities of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]under paragraph (1) , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]shall give priority to medical facilities that—
:(A) as of the date of the enactment of this Act, do not have a medical residency program; and
:(B) are located in a community in which there is a high need for health care for veterans.
