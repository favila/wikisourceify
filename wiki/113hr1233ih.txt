={{anchor|H662FACB35C014ABCB49A84EF8E5EA9D8}}Sec. 2. Presidential records=



=={{anchor|HDDD4927D2317404CBF76A41E0C352F26}}(a) Procedures for consideration of claims of constitutionally based privilege against disclosure– ==



==={{anchor|H0820724B03884C8897BEA057F40EB2F4}}(1) Amendment– ===

Chapter 22of title 44, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|H6AAABF0AE9D045F1B0DACE4B986C569A}}Sec. 2208. Claims of constitutionally based privilege against disclosure===


:(a) 
::(1) When the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] determines under this chapter to make available to the public any Presidential record that has not previously been made available to the public, the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall—
:::(A) promptly provide notice of such determination to—
::::(i) the former [[w:President of the United States|President]] during whose term of office the record was created; and
::::(ii) the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] ; and
:::(B) make the notice available to the public.
::(2) The notice under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 paragraph (1)] —
:::(A) shall be in writing; and
:::(B) shall include such information as may be prescribed in regulations issued by the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] .
::(3) 
:::(A) Upon the expiration of the 60-day period (excepting Saturdays, Sundays, and legal public holidays) beginning on the date the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] provides notice under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 paragraph (1)(A)] , the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall make available to the public the Presidential record covered by the notice, except any record (or reasonably segregable part of a record) with respect to which the Archivist receives from a former [[w:President of the United States|President]] or the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] notification of a claim of constitutionally based privilege against disclosure under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 subsection (b)] .
:::(B) A former [[w:President of the United States|President]] or the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] may extend the period under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 subparagraph (A)] once for not more than 30 additional days (excepting Saturdays, Sundays, and legal public holidays) by filing with the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] a statement that such an extension is necessary to allow an adequate review of the record.
:::(C) Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 subparagraphs (A)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 (B)] , if the 60-day period under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 subparagraph (A)] , or any extension of that period under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 subparagraph (B)] , would otherwise expire during the 6-month period after the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] first takes office, then that 60-day period or extension, respectively, shall expire at the end of that 6-month period.
:(b) 
::(1) For purposes of this section, the decision to assert any claim of constitutionally based privilege against disclosure of a Presidential record (or reasonably segregable part of a record) must be made personally by a former [[w:President of the United States|President]] or the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] , as applicable.
::(2) A former [[w:President of the United States|President]] or the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] shall notify the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] , the [[w:United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives]] , and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs|Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate]] of a privilege claim under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 paragraph (1)] on the same day that the claim is asserted under such paragraph.
:(c) 
::(1) If a claim of constitutionally based privilege against disclosure of a Presidential record (or reasonably segregable part of a record) is asserted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 subsection (b)] by a former [[w:President of the United States|President]] , the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall consult with the incumbent [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] , as soon as practicable during the period specified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 paragraph (2)(A)] , to determine whether the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] will uphold the claim asserted by the former [[w:President of the United States|President]] .
::(2) 
:::(A) Not later than the end of the 30-day period beginning on the date of which the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] receives notification from a former [[w:President of the United States|President]] of the assertion of a claim of constitutionally based privilege against disclosure, the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall provide notice to the former [[w:President of the United States|President]] and the public of the decision of the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 paragraph (1)] regarding the claim.
:::(B) If the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] upholds the claim of privilege asserted by the former [[w:President of the United States|President]] , the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall not make the Presidential record (or reasonably segregable part of a record) subject to the claim publicly available unless—
::::(i) the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] withdraws the decision upholding the claim of privilege asserted by the former [[w:President of the United States|President]] ; or
::::(ii) the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] is otherwise directed by a final court order that is not subject to appeal.
:::(C) If the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] determines not to uphold the claim of privilege asserted by the former [[w:President of the United States|President]] , or fails to make the determination under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 paragraph (1)] before the end of the period specified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2208 subparagraph (A)] , the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall release the Presidential record subject to the claim at the end of the 90-day period beginning on the date on which the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] received notification of the claim, unless otherwise directed by a court order in an action initiated by the former [[w:President of the United States|President]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/2204 section 2204(e)] of this title or by a court order in another action in any Federal court.
:(d) The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall not make publicly available a Presidential record (or reasonably segregable part of a record) that is subject to a privilege claim asserted by the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] unless—
::(1) the incumbent [[w:President of the United States|President]] withdraws the privilege claim; or
::(2) the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] is otherwise directed by a final court order that is not subject to appeal.
:(e) The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall adjust any otherwise applicable time period under this section as necessary to comply with the return date of any congressional subpoena, judicial subpoena, or judicial process..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|H81BC761388B046FE9BD103ECE5DB50F3}}Sec. 3106. Unlawful removal, destruction of records=



=={{anchor|H50524B4975C343EB81EEB5C4D08AC63A}}(a) Federal agency notification– ==

={{anchor|H35C52F07027A4499BD0EFD4E6136AA72}}Sec. 5. Disposal of records=



=={{anchor|H2FCE2350116040F1B0E9527F611CEC31}}(a) Definition of records– ==

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|H0568CCB689844868B0BD04BD23DBF35C}}Sec. 3301. Definition of records==



==={{anchor|H56F9E18EF1A449CEB9B6CDE4AD89FFE9}}(a) Records defined– ===



===={{anchor|H45B1C93526BD4C54B3086279DC831F12}}(1) In general– ====

As used in this chapter, the term records —
:(A) includes all recorded information, regardless of form or characteristics, made or received by a Federal agency under Federal law or in connection with the transaction of public business and preserved or appropriate for preservation by that agency or its legitimate successor as evidence of the organization, functions, policies, decisions, procedures, operations, or other activities of the United States Government or because of the informational value of data in them; and
:(B) does not include—
::(i) library and museum material made or acquired and preserved solely for reference or exhibition purposes; or
::(ii) duplicate copies of records preserved only for convenience.

===={{anchor|HC6BA4653AEB64CE0983EC8AE9777DDE9}}(2) Recorded information defined– ====

For purposes of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/3301 paragraph (1)] , the term recorded information includes all traditional forms of records, regardless of physical form or characteristics, including information created, manipulated, communicated, or stored in digital or electronic form.

==={{anchor|HFB84F31AA5CC49E6A1279A6CCC1E12A2}}(b) Determination of definition– ===

The [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist’s]] determination whether recorded information, regardless of whether it exists in physical, digital, or electronic form, is a record as defined in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/3301 subsection (a)] shall be binding on all Federal agencies..
</blockquote>

={{anchor|H945D7E5BAD3F4E57A5124C7E5D52D890}}Sec. 6. Procedures to prevent unauthorized removal of classified records from National Archives=



=={{anchor|H23EA9A909ECA4DBCB0C682D5C069484A}}(a) Classified records– ==

Not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act, the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] shall prescribe internal procedures to prevent the unauthorized removal of classified records from the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|National Archives and Records Administration]] or the destruction or damage of such records, including when such records are accessed or searched electronically. Such procedures shall include, at a minimum, the following prohibitions:
:(1) An individual, other than covered personnel, may not view classified records in any room that is not secure, except in the presence of [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|National Archives and Records Administration]] personnel or under video surveillance.
:(2) An individual, other than covered personnel, may not be left alone with classified records, unless that individual is under video surveillance.
:(3) An individual, other than covered personnel, may not review classified records while possessing any cellular phone, electronic personal communication device, or any other devices capable of photographing, recording, or transferring images or content.
:(4) An individual seeking access to review classified records, as a precondition to such access, must consent to a search of their belongings upon conclusion of their records review.
:(5) All notes and other writings prepared by an individual, other than covered personnel, during the course of a review of classified records shall be retained by the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|National Archives and Records Administration]] in a secure facility until such notes and other writings are determined to be unclassified, are declassified, or are securely transferred to another secure facility.

=={{anchor|H78440FE6051B4C7A9022DFAD2A766D6B}}(b) Definitions– ==

In this section:

==={{anchor|H32BD9D35FD3E46B8B234F584133A67BD}}(1) Covered personnel– ===

The term covered personnel means any individual—
:(A) who has an appropriate and necessary reason for accessing classified records, as determined by the [[w:National Archives and Records Administration|Archivist]] ; and
:(B) who is either—
::(i) an officer or employee of the United States Government with appropriate security clearances; or
::(ii) any personnel with appropriate security clearances of a Federal contractor authorized in writing to act for purposes of this section by an officer or employee of the United States Government.

==={{anchor|HD7C219315DE646D995AE41779602489D}}(2) Records– ===

The term records has the meaning given that term under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/44/3301 section 3301 of title 44, United States Code] .
