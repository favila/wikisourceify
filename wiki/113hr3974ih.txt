{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 10, United States Code, to improve the TRICARE Program for adult children of members and former members of the uniformed services, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Enrollment of adult children under TRICARE program plan of sponsor=



==(a) Increase of maximum age– ==



===(1) Children– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 Subparagraph (D) ofsection 1072(2)of title 10, United States Code] , is amended—
:(A) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 clause (i)] , by striking “21;” and inserting “26; or” ;
:(B) by striking [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 clause (ii)] ;
:(C) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 clause (iii)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 clause (ii)] ; and
:(D) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 clause (ii)] , as redesignated by subparagraph (C), by striking “or (ii)” .

===(2) Custodial dependents– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 Clause (ii) of section 1072(2)(I) of such title] is amended—
:(A) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 subclause (I)] , by striking “21;” and inserting “26; or” ;
:(B) by striking [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 subclause (II)] ;
:(C) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 subclause (III)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 subclause (II)] ; and
:(D) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1072 subclause (II)] , as redesignated by subparagraph (C), by striking “or (II)” .

===(3) Certain children who survive sponsor– ===


:(A)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1076a Section 1076a(k) of such title] is amended by striking [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1076a paragraph (3)] .
:(B)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 Section 1079(g) of such title] is amended—
::(i) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 paragraph (1)] , by striking “When a member dies” and inserting “Except as provided by [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 paragraph (2)] , when a member dies” ;
::(ii) in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 paragraph (2)] , by striking “shall be the longer” and all that follows through “attains 23 years of age” and inserting “shall be the period ending on the date on which such dependent attains 26 years of age” ;
::(iii) by striking [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 paragraph (3)] ; and
::(iv) by redesignating [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 paragraphs (4)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 (5)] as [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 paragraphs (3)] and [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1079 (4)] , respectively.

==(b) Conforming repeal– ==



===(1) In general– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1110b Section 1110b of such title] is repealed.

===(2) Clerical amendment– ===

The table of sections ofchapter 55 of such titleis amended by striking the item relating to [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/1110b section 1110b] .

==(c) Offset– ==

It is the sense of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that any increase in expenditures of funds by reason of the enactment of this Act should be fully offset by reducing expenditures of funds for United States foreign assistance programs (other than United States foreign assistance programs for Israel).
