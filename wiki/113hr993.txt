{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for the conveyance of certain parcels of
		  National Forest System land to the city of Fruit Heights,
		  Utah.


{{section|H16ABF0280ADB4641AA69378E449A87A0|1. Short title}}
This Act may be cited as the
			 
          Fruit Heights Land Conveyance
			 Act

        
.

{{section|H3AF0AF8F41AE4C6392ED18A09E5D69BE|2. Definitions}}
In this Act:
(1)
City
The
			 term City
 means the city of Fruit Heights, Utah.

(2)
Map
The
			 term map
 means the map entitled Proposed Fruit Heights City
			 Conveyance
 and dated September 13, 2012.

(3)
National Forest
			 System land
The term National Forest System land

			 means the approximately 100 acres of National Forest System land, as depicted
			 on the map.

(4)
Secretary
The
			 term Secretary
 means the Secretary of Agriculture.


{{section|HBFCE8316AB5146D98435F1EBA0868EDC|3. Conveyance of certain land to the city of Fruit Heights, Utah}}
(a)
In
			 general
The [[United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall
			 convey to the City, without consideration, all right, title, and interest of
			 the United States in and to the [[United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land.

(b)
Survey
(1)
In
			 general
If determined by the [[United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] to be necessary, the exact
			 acreage and legal description of the [[United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land shall be
			 determined by a survey approved by the [[United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]].

(2)
Costs
The
			 City shall pay the reasonable survey and other administrative costs associated
			 with a survey conducted under paragraph (1)
.


(c)
Easement
As
			 a condition of the conveyance under subsection (a)
, the [[United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall reserve
			 an easement to the [[United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land for the Bonneville Shoreline
			 Trail.

(d)
Use of national
			 forest system land
As a
			 condition of the conveyance under subsection (a)
, the City shall use the
			 [[United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land only for public purposes.

(e)
Reversionary
			 Interest
In the quitclaim
			 deed to the City for the [[United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land, the [[United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]] shall
			 provide that the [[United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land shall revert to the [[United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]], at
			 the election of the [[United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary]], if the [[United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land is used for
			 other than a public purpose.
