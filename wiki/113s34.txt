{{Center|A BILL}}

To increase public safety by permitting the Attorney General to deny the transfer of firearms or the issuance of firearms and explosives licenses to known or suspected dangerous terrorists.

={{anchor|S1}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Denying Firearms and Explosives to Dangerous Terrorists Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|idAD88A0613646408894DA2C004F3A8BBE}}Sec. 2. Granting the Attorney General the authority to deny the sale, delivery, or transfer of a firearm or the issuance of a firearms or explosives license or permit to dangerous terrorists=


:(a) Standard for exercising Attorney General discretion regarding transferring firearms or issuing firearms permits to dangerous terrorists– Chapter 44 of title 18, United States Code, is amended—
::(1) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922] the following:

<blockquote>

==={{anchor|idF4FEF22C506E4718A97D3358BF274B8F}}Sec. 922A. Attorney General's discretion to deny transfer of a firearm.===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may deny the transfer of a firearm under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(t)(1)(B)(ii)] of this title if the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] —
:(1) determines that the transferee is known (or appropriately suspected) to be or have been engaged in conduct constituting, in preparation for, in aid of, or related to terrorism, or providing material support or resources for terrorism; and
:(2) has a reasonable belief that the prospective transferee may use a firearm in connection with terrorism.

==={{anchor|id57A9A7F95B4E4ACC847EA637307A7D78}}Sec. 922B. Attorney General's discretion regarding applicants for firearm permits which would qualify for the exemption provided under section 922(t)(3).===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] may determine that—
:(1) an applicant for a firearm permit which would qualify for an exemption under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922 section 922(t)] is known (or appropriately suspected) to be or have been engaged in conduct constituting, in preparation for, in aid of, or related to terrorism, or providing material support or resources for terrorism; and
:(2) the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] has a reasonable belief that the applicant may use a firearm in connection with terrorism.;
</blockquote>

=={{anchor|id2A7628B683764091A15654E945C55C97}}(s) Guidelines– ==



==={{anchor|id2494CF0D974349F58CBB368C72E72E28}}(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] shall issue guidelines describing the circumstances under which the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]] will exercise the authority and make determinations under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/843 subsections (d)(1)(B)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/843 (j) of section 843] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922A sections 922A] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922B 922B of title 18, United States Code] , as amended by this Act.

==={{anchor|id2DD44816BEAC471DA1F1558D86968783}}(2) Contents– ===

The guidelines issued under paragraph (1)shall—
:(A) provide accountability and a basis for monitoring to ensure that the intended goals for, and expected results of, the grant of authority under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/843 subsections (d)(1)(B)] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/843 (j) of section 843] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922A sections 922A] and[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/922B 922B of title 18, United States Code] , as amended by this Act, are being achieved; and
:(B) ensure that terrorist watch list records are used in a manner that safeguards privacy and civil liberties protections, in accordance with requirements outlines in Homeland Security Presidential Directive 11 (dated August 27, 2004).
