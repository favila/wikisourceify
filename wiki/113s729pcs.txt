{{Center|A BILL}}

 To protect law abiding citizens by preventing criminals from obtaining firearms.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Disarm Criminals and Protect Communities Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Prosecution of felons and fugitives who attempt to illegally purchase firearms=



==(a) Taskforce– ==



===(1) Establishment– ===

There is established a task force within the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]], which shall be known as the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Felon and Fugitive Firearm Task Force]](referred to in this section as the“Task Force”), to strengthen the efforts of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]to investigate and prosecute cases of convicted felons and fugitives from justice who illegally attempt to purchase a firearm.

===(2) Membership– ===

The members of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Task Force]]shall be—
:(A) the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Deputy Attorney General]], who shall serve as the Chairperson of the Task Force;
:(B) the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Assistant Attorney General for the Criminal Division]];
:(C) the [[w:Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives|Director of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives]];
:(D) the [[w:Federal Bureau of Investigation|Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation]]; and
:(E) such other officers or employees of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]as the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]may designate.

===(3) Duties– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Task Force]]shall—
:(A) provide direction for the investigation and prosecution of cases of convicted felons and fugitives from justice attempting to illegally purchase a firearm; and
:(B) provide recommendations to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]relating to—
::(i) the allocation and reallocation of resources of the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]]for investigation and prosecution of cases of convicted felons and fugitives from justice attempting to illegally purchase a firearm;
::(ii) enhancing cooperation among agencies and entities of the Federal Government in the investigation and prosecution of cases of convicted felons and fugitives from justice attempting to illegally purchase a firearm;
::(iii) enhancing cooperation among Federal, State, and local authorities responsible for the investigation and prosecution of cases of convicted felons and fugitives from justice attempting to illegally purchase a firearm; and
::(iv) changes in rules, regulations, or policy to improve the effective investigation and prosecution of cases of convicted felons and fugitives from justice attempting to illegally purchase a firearm.

===(4) Meetings– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Task Force]]shall meet not less than once a year.

===(5) Termination– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Justice|Task Force]]shall terminate on the date that is 5 years after the date of enactment of this Act.

==(b) Authorization for use of funds– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524  Section 524(c)(1)of title 28, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 subparagraph (H)], by striking“and”at the end;
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 subparagraph (I)], by striking the period at the end and inserting“; and”; and
:(3) by inserting after[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/524 subparagraph (I)]the following:

<blockquote> 
::(J) the investigation and prosecution of cases of convicted felons and fugitives from justice who illegally attempt to purchase a firearm, in accordance with the  Disarm Criminals and Protect Communities Act  , provided that—
:::(i) not more than $10,000,000 shall be available to the [[w:United States Department of Justice|Attorney General]]for each of fiscal years 2014 through 2018 under this subparagraph; and
:::(ii) not more than 5 percent of the amounts made available under this subparagraph may be used for the administrative costs of the task force established under the  Disarm Criminals and Protect Communities Act  . . 
</blockquote>
