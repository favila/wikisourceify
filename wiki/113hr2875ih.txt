{{Center|A BILL}}

 To authorize programs and activities for the improvement and protection of ports and harbors, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Waterfront of Tomorrow Act of 2013 ”. 

=Sec. 2. Studies=



==(a) Measures To improve flood protection and climate resilience for New York City– ==



===(1) Study– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary of the Army]]shall conduct a study of measures to improve flood protection and climate resilience for New York City, using both traditional engineering and green infrastructure technologies.

===(2) Contents– ===

In conducting the study, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]]shall—
:(A) assess traditional engineering solutions, including tide gates and seawalls;
:(B) assess, in consultation with the [[w:National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration|Administrator of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration]], the use of alternative approaches, including oyster reef restoration, tidal wetland restoration and construction, and other natural designs that reduce storm surge impacts and retain storm water while providing additional environmental benefits;
:(C) identify the cost and time associated with implementing the measures described in subparagraphs (A) and (B) , the potential impact of such measures on the surrounding environment, and any adverse impacts of such measures on local housing, commerce, or recreation; and
:(D) make a recommendation as to which of such measures would provide the greatest protection for New York City coastal communities and critical infrastructure from an event of a magnitude that is equal to or greater than Hurricane Sandy.

===(3) Coordination to prevent duplication of efforts– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]]shall carry out the activities under this section in coordination with the study to be conducted by the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]]under the heading Corps of Engineers—Civil—Investigations in [[Public Law 113-2|title II ofPublic Law 113–2 ]] (127 Stat. 5).

===(4) Report– ===

Not later than 18 months after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]]shall transmit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on the results of the study.

===(5) Corps of Engineers– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]]shall carry out this subsection acting through the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Chief of Engineers]].

==(b) Environmental impact of major disasters– ==



===(1) Study– ===

The [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]], in consultation with the [[w:Occupational Safety and Health Administration|Administrator of the Occupational Safety and Health Administration]], shall conduct a study of the environmental impact of each major disaster that the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]determines will have a significant environmental impact on the waters of the United States.

===(2) Determinations– ===

In making determinations under paragraph (1) , the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]]shall ensure that the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]conducts studies under this subsection with respect to, at a minimum, 10 percent of the major disasters declared in a fiscal year.

===(3) Contents– ===

The [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]shall conduct a study under this subsection with respect to a major disaster with the specific goal of determining—
:(A) whether industrial facilities discharged pollutants or other hazards into local waterways or the water supply during the major disaster; and
:(B) if so, how to avoid or minimize the risk of such pollution incidents in the future.

===(4) Report– ===

Not later than 180 days after the date of the declaration of a major disaster described in paragraph (1) , the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator]]shall transmit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report on the results of the study conducted under this subsection with respect to the major disaster.

===(5) Major disaster defined– ===

In this subsection, the term major disaster has the meaning given that term in  section 102 of the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/5122 42 U.S.C. 5122] ).

=Sec. 4. Next generation ports=



==(a) Improvements to port infrastructure and intermodal rail and highway networks– ==



===(1) Grants– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary of the Army]]is authorized to make grants to States and local governments for projects to improve port infrastructure and intermodal rail and highway networks.

===(2) Consideration of local preferences and environmental concerns– ===

In making grants to States and local governments under this subsection, the [[w:United States Department of the Army|Secretary]]shall ensure that local preferences and environmental concerns are incorporated into any port infrastructure and intermodal transportation improvements, so that disruptions and adverse impacts are minimized.

==(b) Green ports– ==



===(1) Designation– ===

The [[w:United States Maritime Administration|Administrator of the Maritime Administration]], acting jointly with the [[w:United States Environmental Protection Agency|Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency]], shall establish a green port designation for ports that meet certain environmental standards.

===(2) Grants and technical assistance– ===

The [[w:United States Maritime Administration|Administrator of the Maritime Administration]]is authorized to provide grants and technical assistance to a port designated as a green port pursuant to paragraph (1) to implement innovations that minimize the environmental impacts of port operations. 

=Sec. 5. Waterfronts as main streets=



==(a) Establishment of grant program– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]], acting through the [[w:Economic Development Administration/Assistant Secretary for Economic Development|Administrator of the Economic Development Administration]], shall carry out a grant program in accordance with the requirements of this section.

==(b) Award of grants– ==

In carrying out the program, the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]]may make grants to States and local governments for projects to establish environmentally sustainable waterfront areas.

==(c) Applications– ==

A State or local government seeking a grant under the program shall submit to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]]an application at such time and containing such information as the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]]may require.

==(d) Criteria– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]]shall establish criteria for awarding grants for projects under the program.

===(2) Priority– ===

The criteria shall include an assessment of whether the applicant is able to demonstrate the potential of a project—
:(A) to generate economic growth and job creation; or
:(B) to improve the environmental sustainability of waterfront areas.

==(e) Maximum grant amount– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]]may not make grants under this section to a State or local government in an amount that exceeds $10,000,000 in the aggregate.

==(f) Reporting requirement– ==

As a condition for receiving a grant under the program, a State or local government shall agree to submit to the [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary]]an annual report on the achievement of performance measures, including the criteria described in subsection (d)(2) , by the State or local government for a period of 3 years after the grant is awarded.

==(g) Federal share– ==

The Federal share of the cost of a project carried out with funds from a grant under the program may not exceed 75 percent.
