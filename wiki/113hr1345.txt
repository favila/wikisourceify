{{Center|A BILL}}

To address the forest health, public safety, and wildlife habitat threat presented by the risk of wildfire, including catastrophic wildfire, on National Forest System lands and public lands managed by the Bureau of Land Management by requiring the Secretary of Agriculture and the Secretary of the Interior to expedite forest management projects relating to hazardous fuels reduction, forest health, and economic development, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HA1B995369620497192251DB4250B7AFB}}Sec. 2. Purposes=

The purposes of this Act are as follows:
:(1) Provide the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] and the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] the tools to reduce the potential for wildfires.
:(2) Expedite wildfire prevention projects to reduce the chances of wildfire on certain high-risk Federal lands.
:(3) Reduce threats to endangered species from wildfires.

=={{anchor|H8CF55D255C8F486E973EB4D7CE630307}}Sec. 102. Forest Service and Bureau of Land Management good-neighbor cooperation==



==={{anchor|H10E2729A174A4D94847DC8D62FC0A623}}(a) Definitions– ===

In this section:

===={{anchor|HB4AF9BD8E14F435C90540ADCAB7B094C}}(1) Eligible State– ====

The term eligible State means a State that contains National Forest System land or Bureau of Land Management land.

===={{anchor|H98CCCA75B4A14327B99D0FC725EE01A5}}(2) Federal land– ====

The term Federal land means—
:(A) land of the [[w:United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] (as defined in section 11(a) of the Forest and Rangeland Renewable Resources Planning Act of 1974([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1609 16 U.S.C. 1609(a)] ] )); or
:(B) public lands (as defined in section 103 of the Federal Land Policy and Management Act of 1976([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 43 U.S.C. 1702] )).

===={{anchor|H82056108623145BBB27B49AD58C7B5EC}}(3) Secretary concerned– ====

The term Secretary concerned means—
:(A) the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] , in the case of National Forest System land; and
:(B) the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] , in the case of public lands administered by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of Interior]] through the [[w:Bureau of Land Management|Bureau of Land Management]] .

===={{anchor|HD4DED9CDF04F401383434F4764AFFCA9}}(4) State Forester– ====

The term State Forester means the head of a State agency with jurisdiction over State forestry programs in an eligible State.

==={{anchor|H222AD5FC111C44E9B60EB27799D6909B}}(b) Cooperative agreements and contracts– ===



===={{anchor|HFA98ED00971A483B856D9429AE8CB9EF}}(1) In general– ====

The Secretary concerned may enter into a cooperative agreement or contract (including a sole source contract) with a State Forester to authorize the State Forester to provide the forest, rangeland, and watershed restoration and protection services described in paragraph (2)on Federal lands administered by the Secretary concerned, in the eligible State.

===={{anchor|HC564DA0A27FD4E72ACBCD1423F6B33D8}}(2) Authorized services– ====

The forest, rangeland, and watershed restoration and protection services referred to in paragraph (1)include the conduct of—
:(A) activities to treat insect infected trees;
:(B) activities to reduce hazardous fuels; or
:(C) any other activities determined by the Secretary concerned to appropriate to restore or improve forest, rangeland, and watershed health, including fish and wildlife habitat.

===={{anchor|H79444E993EA84743851D21353769E204}}(3) State as agent– ====

Except as provided in paragraph (6), a cooperative agreement or contract entered into under paragraph (1)may authorize the State Forester to serve as the agent for the Secretary concerned in providing the restoration and protection services authorized under that paragraph.

===={{anchor|H90A6C9E52D074FBF8F7DD1AA99FF5D23}}(4) Subcontracts– ====

In accordance with applicable contract procedures for the eligible State, a State Forester may enter into subcontracts to provide the restoration and protection services authorized under a cooperative agreement or contract entered into under paragraph (1).

===={{anchor|H68AC9F6BFB7D4FE2AE7C3CC9B6BE31C0}}(5) Timber sales– ====

 Subsections (d)and (g) of section 14 of the National Forest Management Act of 1976([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 16 U.S.C. 472a] )shall not apply to services performed under a cooperative agreement or contract entered into under paragraph (1).

===={{anchor|HB921A8EA904E4C42B1FFD7D9FE0C94EC}}(6) Retention of NEPA responsibilities– ====

Any decision required to be made under the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] ] )with respect to any restoration and protection services to be provided under this section by a State Forester on Federal land, shall not be delegated to a State Forester or any other officer or employee of the eligible State.

==={{anchor|H903FED066A864B75863887920EF136E1}}(c) Applicable law– ===

The restoration and protection services to be provided under this section shall be carried out on a project-to-project basis under existing applicable authorities of the Forest Service and the Bureau of Land Management.

==={{anchor|HE4BDB72B84C943ED9D1848464097D7E4}}(d) Termination of effectiveness– ===

The authority of the Secretary concerned to enter into cooperative agreements and contracts under this Act terminates on September 30, 2023.

==={{anchor|HF72905528E9D40ED9AFFDA466CFFF1EF}}(e) Duration of contracts– ===

A cooperative agreement or contract entered into under this Act shall not extend beyond 20 years.

=={{anchor|H25AF86CC179B4FCBA9F1198591DD7A2D}}Sec. 103. Clarification of Flame Wildfire Suppression Reserve Fund authority==



==={{anchor|H3777FBB7547C488C92A756E80FCF2A2A}}(a) Clarification– ===

 Section 502(c) of the Federal Land Assistance, Management, and Enhancement Act of 2009([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/43/1748a 43 U.S.C. 1748a(c)] ] )is amended by inserting“and burn area responses, including flood prevention,”after“events”.

=={{anchor|HE33E412DDEF44537B0E31669A3918849}}Title II— Expedited process for the implementation of wildfire prevention projects near at-risk communities.==



=={{anchor|H7A4772769DA54AEAA77CC4F7910C3954}}Sec. 201. Definitions==

In this title:

==={{anchor|H1BCF5A3ACE294412BD4303D8DC8B96EC}}(1) At-risk community– ===

The term at-risk community has the meaning given that term in section 101 of the Healthy Forests Restoration Act of 2003([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 16 U.S.C. 6511] ).

==={{anchor|H258DCE974411455197B83E60BF86809E}}(2) At-risk forest– ===

The term at-risk forest means—
:(A) Federal land in condition class II or III, as those classes were developed by the Forest Service Rocky Mountain Research Station in the general technical report titled Development of Coarse-Scale Spatial Data for Wildland Fire and Fuel Management (RMRS–87) and dated April 2000 or any subsequent revision of the report; or
:(B) Federal land where there exists a high risk of losing an at-risk community, key ecosystem, water supply, wildlife, or wildlife habitat to wildfire, including catastrophic wildfire and post-fire disturbances, as designated by the Secretary concerned.

==={{anchor|HC4C8E11C4CC6489BB63B702C5A15DD9D}}(3) Federal land– ===



===={{anchor|HE40607CB4FED44A29D9D00C7D292B5B1}}(A) Covered land– ====

The term Federal land means—
:(i) land of the [[w:United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] (as defined in section 11(a) of the Forest and Rangeland Renewable Resources Planning Act of 1974([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1609 16 U.S.C. 1609(a)] ] )); or
:(ii) public lands (as defined in section 103 of the Federal Land Policy and Management Act of 1976([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 43 U.S.C. 1702] )).

===={{anchor|HB434D52D43B34257ADD3C5B130BCA637}}(B) Excluded land– ====

The term does not include land in which the removal of vegetation is specifically prohibited by Federal law unless the land is in an inventoried roadless area or Wilderness Study Areas (WSAs).

==={{anchor|H794E0FCAC9BF49C4A64E403ABDB8760F}}(4) Secretary concerned– ===

The term Secretary concerned means—
:(A) the [[w:United States Department of Agriculture|Secretary of Agriculture]] , in the case of [[w:United States Forest Service|National Forest System]] land; and
:(B) the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] , in the case of public lands administered by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of Interior]] through the [[w:Bureau of Land Management|Bureau of Land Management]] .

==={{anchor|H08F23D66C2F34120B7EC88C6EE5A7D41}}(5) Threatened and endangered species habitat– ===

The term threatened and endangered species habitat means Federal land where natural fire regimes are identified as being important for, or unnatural wildfire is identified as a threat to, an endangered species, a threatened species, or habitat of an endangered species or threatened species.

==={{anchor|HA8B084D0DC324D52BF75B272C4F9FD9E}}(6) Eligible wildfire prevention project– ===

The term eligible wildfire prevention project means the measures and methods developed for a project to be carried out on Federal land or on threatened and endangered species habitat by the Secretary concerned for hazardous fuels reduction, forest health, forest restoration, watershed restoration, or threatened and endangered species habitat protection using ecological restoration principles consistent with the forest type where such project will occur.

=={{anchor|H13626A324CAE475BA8DEBF809A94E284}}Sec. 202. Eligible wildfire prevention projects==



==={{anchor|H90D40A729C7A42E68444E4D43441E562}}(a) Implementation– ===

As soon as practicable after the date of the enactment of this Act, the Secretary concerned shall implement eligible wildfire prevention projects in at-risk forests and on threatened and endangered species habitat in a manner that focuses on surface, ladder, and canopy fuels reduction activities using ecological restoration principles consistent with the forest type in the location where such project will occur.

==={{anchor|H314DBCB80659405CAD26D8CB1B8562BB}}(b) Project elements– ===



===={{anchor|H431F44DD704B4BAB871089A7996F6D63}}(1) Threatened and endangered species habitat– ====

Wildfire prevention projects carried out on threatened and endangered species habitat shall be carried out to provide enhanced protection from wildfire, including unnatural wildfire, for the endangered species, threatened species, or habitat of the endangered species or threatened species.

===={{anchor|H4A7DACCC925144BD92FB279F498D59CE}}(2) At-risk forests– ====

In the case of an eligible wildfire prevention project carried out in an at-risk forest, the project shall be carried out to move Federal land in condition class II or III toward condition class I, using ecological restoration principles consistent with the forest type in the location where such project will occur.

==={{anchor|H644969FDF63E4B43BC46B3888CD48F3D}}(c) Authorized practices– ===



===={{anchor|HBA831A18E3104A23883015440DA74A8E}}(1) In general– ====

An eligible wildfire prevention project may include livestock grazing and timber harvest projects carried out for the purposes of hazardous fuels reduction, forest health, forest restoration, watershed restoration, or threatened and endangered species habitat protection or improvement, if the management action is consistent with achieving long-term ecological restoration of the forest type in the location where such project will occur.

===={{anchor|H9ABCD4799265427EAE16DBAF433BD918}}(2) Grazing– ====

Domestic livestock grazing may be used in an eligible wildfire prevention project to reduce surface fuel loads and to recover burned areas. Utilization standards shall not apply when domestic livestock grazing is used in an eligible wildfire prevention project.

===={{anchor|HCCEBFD9C48F44DD1BD302194392A66D5}}(3) Timber harvesting and thinning– ====

Timber harvesting and thinning, where the ecological restoration principles are consistent with the forest type in the location where such project will occur, may be used in an eligible wildfire prevention project to reduce ladder and canopy fuel loads to prevent unnatural fire.

==={{anchor|H6D85516906AD4713A2CE8D989DE4477C}}(d) Relation to land and resource management plans and land use plan– ===

Nothing in this section requires the Secretary concerned, as a condition of conducting an eligible wildfire prevention project, to revise or amend the land and resource management plan applicable to the National Forest System lands or the land use plan applicable to the public lands on which the project will be conducted.

=={{anchor|HE9D5CA705B3A4D5D9D5232777ABFF874}}Sec. 203. Environmental analysis==



==={{anchor|H759B8B08196E4E0ABEE135D25C4697BA}}(a) Analysis of proposed action and no action alternative– ===



===={{anchor|H6D54CDB7141841CE9D7863BF554032CC}}(1) Environmental assessment or environmental impact statement required– ====

For each proposed eligible wildfire prevention project, the Secretary concerned shall—
:(A) study, develop, and describe the proposed action and the no action alternative; and
:(B) prepare an environmental assessment or an environmental impact statement pursuant to section 102(2) of the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4332 42 U.S.C. 4332(2)] ).

===={{anchor|HE52A6672AF4D46B1B08ED696B6454C6A}}(2) No requirement for alternative action study– ====

Except as provided in subsection (b), the Secretary concerned is not required to study, develop, or describe any alternative actions, other than the no action alternative, to the proposed agency action.

===={{anchor|H2C2B939B754F4D6A99FC55AFCCA94894}}(3) Deadlines for completion– ====



====={{anchor|H96DD753E3A48413498A1E9C9435A7E73}}(A) Environmental assessment deadline– =====

An environmental assessment prepared for a proposed eligible wildfire prevention project shall be completed within 60 days of the commencement of preparation of the assessment.

====={{anchor|H50ADB3DC4BD1493A8547B9D8D055EA32}}(B) Environmental impact statement deadline– =====

An environmental impact statement prepared for a proposed eligible wildfire prevention project shall be completed within 90 days of the commencement of preparation of the environmental impact statement.

====={{anchor|H512982A8219C4B91BE7CEF73B0EB47F4}}(C) Effect of failure to meet deadline– =====

The proposed eligible wildfire prevention project shall be deemed compliant with all requirements of the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969if the Secretary concerned fails to meet the specified deadline.

===={{anchor|H1C417012B1154F9B8EF0EB40DA681E8D}}(4) Environmental analysis duration– ====

The environmental assessment of an authorized practice utilized in an eligible wildfire prevention project shall be deemed sufficient for a minimum of—
:(A) 10 years in the case of a livestock grazing project; or
:(B) 20 years in the case of a timber harvest project.

==={{anchor|HE2A0932011204BFC9707EB3D14A90D8D}}(b) Categorical exclusion– ===

If an eligible wildfire prevention project, located in a at-risk area, involves the removal of insect-infected trees or other hazardous fuels within 500 feet of utility or communications infrastructure, campgrounds, roadsides, heritage sites, recreation sites, schools, or other infrastructure, that project is categorically excluded from the requirement to prepare an environmental assessment or an environmental impact statement under the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] ] )if the project will otherwise be conducted consistent with the relevant agency or department’s procedures and the applicable land and resource management plan or land use plan.

==={{anchor|HBEE21531EC7B46518BAB867F0ED94AE8}}(c) Environmental analysis generally– ===

Except as otherwise provided in this Act, the Secretary concerned shall comply with the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] ] )and other applicable laws in planning and conducting an eligible wildfire prevention project.

==={{anchor|HEA6904C42C384FE1968E78B4F5C7D3A1}}(d) Interagency cooperation– ===

The informal consultation requirements of the Endangered Species Act of 1973([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1531 16 U.S.C. 1531 et seq.] ), as codified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 402.05] of title 50, Code of Federal Regulations shall apply to an eligible wildfire prevention project.

==={{anchor|H6F7BDB401FDE404080A357E0A4F89871}}(e) Effect of compliance– ===

Compliance with this section shall be deemed to satisfy the requirements of the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4331 42 U.S.C. 4331 et seq.] ] ), section 14 of the National Forest Management Act of 1976([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 16 U.S.C. 472a] ), the Endangered Species Act of 1973([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1531 16 U.S.C. 1531 et seq.] ] ), and the Multiple-Use Sustained-Yield Act of 1960([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/528 16 U.S.C. 528 et seq.] ] ).

==={{anchor|H986E312F27444C9EB2B3AA48A7507D8E}}(f) Effect of a wildfire emergency– ===



===={{anchor|H79A01E79C27E4861866B7B3FEE267EA8}}(1) Council on environmental quality– ====

The Secretary concerned, pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 1506.11] of title 40, Code of Federal Regulations, shall request the [[w:Council on Environmental Quality|Council on Environmental Quality]] to develop and approve alternative arrangements for the proposed wildfire prevention project if—
:(A) the county in which the proposed eligible wildfire prevention project is to be carried out declares a state of emergency because of wildfire or the threat of wildfire in consultation with the State Forester or equivalent State official of the State containing the county; and
:(B) a categorical exclusion is unavailable for a proposed eligible wildfire prevention project.

===={{anchor|HB4862FC8883044C5870F5EC88208B2CC}}(2) Mandatory information– ====

When requesting alternative arrangements under paragraph (1), the Secretary concerned shall transmit to the [[w:Council on Environmental Quality|Council on Environmental Quality]] the following information:
:(A) A description of the proposed eligible project.
:(B) The condition of forest fuels within or near the proposed eligible wildfire prevention project.
:(C) The threat to public safety, welfare, infrastructure, watersheds, wildlife habitat, or other vital assets due to the accumulation of forest fuels and the associated risk of extreme fire that the proposed eligible project is to relieve.
:(D) The degree to which delaying the implementation of the proposed eligible project will increase the risk of serious harm to public safety, welfare, infrastructure, watersheds, wildlife habitat, or other vital assets due to the accumulation of forest fuels and the associated risk of extreme fire.
:(E) Any other information the Secretary concerned determines relevant.

===={{anchor|HD6959046A29E420691F8D3EC9A4624E0}}(3) Deadline for alternative arrangements– ====



====={{anchor|H33D64C019C1B43ED9B83A0F127B76490}}(A) Deadline– =====

Not later than 15 days after receipt of a request under paragraph (1)for approval of alternative arrangements for a proposed eligible wildfire prevention project, the [[w:Council on Environmental Quality|Council on Environmental Quality]] shall submit to the Secretary concerned the alternative arrangements under which the Secretary may proceed immediately and to completion of the proposed wildfire prevention project.

====={{anchor|H7025F1A6A3B04F05B0CBDE3965EFFB3F}}(B) Failure to comply– =====

If the [[w:Council on Environmental Quality|Council on Environmental Quality]] fails to comply with the deadline in subparagraph (A), the Secretary concerned shall proceed immediately and to completion of the proposed eligible wildfire prevention project notwithstanding any other provision of law, including the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] )and the National Forest Management Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1601 16 U.S.C. 1601 et seq.] ).

===={{anchor|HAD79E7AA8E5C406487947D8A370ECD61}}(4) Administrative and judicial review– ====

Actions under this subsection shall not be subject to—
:(A) the notice, comment, and appeal requirements of[[Public Law 102-381|section 322 of[[Public Law -|Public Law 102–381]] ]] (the Appeals Reform Act;[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/16/1612 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 16 U.S.C. 1612] note] ); and
:(B) judicial review by any court of the United States.

=={{anchor|H8FB0FD1D82D04FD6B399922F58CA98F7}}Sec. 204. Administrative and judicial review==



==={{anchor|H72D60A9377664E648718BD1944EC96F4}}(a) Administrative review– ===

Administrative review of an eligible wildfire prevention project shall occur in accordance with the special administrative review process established under section 105 of the Healthy Forests Restoration Act of 2003([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 16 U.S.C. 6515] ).

==={{anchor|H9B64DB744A934DD5A9962855C67B3944}}(b) Judicial review– ===

Judicial review of an eligible wildfire prevention project shall occur in accordance with section 106 of the Healthy Forests Restoration Act of 2003([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 16 U.S.C. 6516] ).
