{{Center|A BILL}}

 To increase from $10,000,000,000 to $50,000,000,000 the threshold figure at which regulated depository institutions are subject to direct examination and reporting requirements of the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Consumer Financial Protection Bureau Examination and Reporting Threshold Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Increase in the examination threshold=

   Section 1025(a) of the Consumer Financial Protection Act of 2010 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/5515 12 U.S.C. 5515(a)] )is amended by striking“$10,000,000,000”each place that term appears and inserting“$50,000,000,000”. 

=Sec. 3. Increase in the reporting threshold=

   Section 1026(a) of the Consumer Financial Protection Act of 2010 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/12/5516 12 U.S.C. 5516(a)] )is amended by striking“$10,000,000,000”each place that term appears and inserting“$50,000,000,000”. 

=Sec. 4. Effective date=

This Act and the amendments made by this Act shall take effect on the date that is 45 days after the date of enactment of this Act.
