=Sec. 6. Improving the certification process=



==(a) Definitions– ==

As used in this section—
:(1) the term“act of terrorism”has the same meaning as in  section 102(1) of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701 15 U.S.C. 6701 note]);
:(2) the term“certification process”means the process by which the Secretary determines whether to certify an act as an act of terrorism under  section 102(1) of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701  15 U.S.C. 6701note]); and
:(3) the term“Secretary”means the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary of the Treasury]].

==(b) Study– ==

Not later than 9 months after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]shall conduct and complete a study on the certification process.

==(c) Required content– ==

The study required under subsection (a) shall include an examination and analysis of—
:(1) the establishment of a reasonable timeline by which the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]must make an accurate determination on whether to certify an act as an act of terrorism;
:(2) the impact that the length of any timeline proposed to be established under paragraph (1) may have on the insurance industry, policyholders, consumers, and taxpayers as a whole;
:(3) the factors the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]would evaluate and monitor during the certification process, including the ability of the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]to obtain the required information regarding the amount of projected and incurred losses resulting from an act which the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]would need in determining whether to certify the act as an act of terrorism;
:(4) the appropriateness, efficiency, and effectiveness of the consultation process required under  section 102(1)(A) of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701 15 U.S.C. 6701 note])and any recommendations on changes to the consultation process; and
:(5) the ability of the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]to provide guidance and updates to the public regarding any act that may reasonably be certified as an act of terrorism.

==(d) Report– ==

Upon completion of the study required under subsection (a) , the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]shall submit a report on the results of such study to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs|Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senate]]and the [[w:United States House Committee on Financial Services|Committee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives]].

==(e) Rulemaking– ==

   Section 102(1) of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701  15 U.S.C. 6701note])is amended—
:(1) by redesignating subparagraph (D) as subparagraph (E) ; and
:(2) by inserting after subparagraph (C) the following:

<blockquote> 

====(D) Timing of certification– ====

Not later than 9 months after the report required under section 6 of the Terrorism Risk Insurance Program Reauthorization Act of 2014 is submitted to the appropriate committees of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]], the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]shall issue final rules governing the certification process, including any timeline applicable to any certification by the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]]on whether an act is an act of terrorism under this paragraph. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 7. GAO study on upfront premiums=



==(a) Study– ==

Not later than 2 years after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]]shall complete a study on the viability and effects of the Federal Government assessing and collecting upfront premiums on insurers that participate in the Terrorism Insurance Program established under the  Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/6701  15 U.S.C. 6701note])(hereafter in this section referred to as the“Program”).

==(b) Required content– ==

The study required under subsection (a) shall examine, but shall not be limited to, the following issues:
:(1) How the Federal Government could determine the price of such upfront premiums on insurers that participate in the Program.
:(2) How the Federal Government could collect and manage such upfront premiums.
:(3) How the Federal Government could ensure that such upfront premiums are not spent for purposes other than claims through the Program.
:(4) How the assessment and collection of such upfront premiums could affect take-up rates for terrorism risk coverage in different regions and industries and how it could impact small businesses and consumers in both metropolitan and non-metropolitan areas.
:(5) The effect of collecting such upfront premiums on insurers both large and small.
:(6) The effect of collecting such upfront premiums on the private market for terrorism risk reinsurance.
:(7) The size of any Federal Government subsidy insurers may receive through their participation in the Program, taking into account the Program’s current post-event recoupment structure.

==(c) Report– ==

Upon completion of the study required under subsection (a) , the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General]]shall submit a report on the results of such study to the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs|Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senate]]and the [[w:United States House Committee on Financial Services|Committee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives]].

==(d) Public availability– ==

The study and report required under this section shall be made available to the public in electronic form and shall be published on the website of the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Government Accountability Office]].
