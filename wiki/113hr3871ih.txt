{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to allow increased contributions to health savings accounts, to allow Medicare and VA healthcare participants to contribute to health savings accounts, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Promoting Health for Future Generations Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Increase in HSA contribution limitation=



==(a) In general– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 Subsection (b)]of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 section 223]of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (relating to monthly limitation) is amended—
:(1) by striking“$2,250”in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 paragraph (2)(A)]and inserting“the amount in effect under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 subsection (c)(2)(A)(ii)(I)] ”, and
:(2) by striking“$4,500”in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 paragraph (2)(B)]and inserting“the amount in effect under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 subsection (c)(2)(A)(ii)(II)] ”.

==(b) Conforming amendment– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 Paragraph (1) of section 223(g) of such Code]is amended by striking“subsections (b)(2) and”and inserting“subsection”.

==(c) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to contributions for taxable years beginning after December 31, 2014. 

=Sec. 3. Medicare and VA healthcare enrollees eligible to contribute to HSA=



==(a) In general– ==


:(1)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 Subsection (b) ofsection 223of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986]is amended by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 paragraph (7)]and redesignating[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 paragraph (8)]as[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 paragraph (7)].
:(2)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 Subsection (c) of section 223 of such Code](relating to definitions and special rules) is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 

====(6) Special rule for individuals entitled to benefits under Medicare or enrolled for health benefits from VA– ====

In the case of an individual—
:(A) 
::(i) who is entitled to benefits under title XVIII of the Social Security Act , and
::(ii) with respect to whom a health savings account is established in a month before the first month such individual is entitled to such benefits, or
:(B) 
::(i) who is enrolled in the patient enrollment system established by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] pursuant to[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/1705  section 1705of title 38, United States Code], and
::(ii) with respect to whom a health savings account is established in a month before the first month such individual is enrolled in such system,such individual shall be deemed to be an eligible individual. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2014. 

=Sec. 4. Allowing MSA and HSA rollover to adult child of account holder=



==(a) MSAs– ==


:(1)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/220 Subparagraph (A) ofsection 220(f)(8)of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986](relating to treatment after death of account holder) is amended—
::(A) by inserting“or adult child”after“surviving spouse”,
::(B) by inserting“or adult child, as the case may be,”after“the spouse”, and
::(C) by inserting“ or adult child ”after“ spouse ”in the heading thereof.
:(2)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/220 Paragraph (8) of section 220(f) of such Code]is amended by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote> 

====(C) Adult child– ====

For purposes of this paragraph, the term adult child means an individual—
:(i) who is a child of the deceased individual, and
:(ii) with respect to whom a deduction under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/151 section 151]would not be allowable to another taxpayer for a taxable year beginning in the calendar year in which such individual’s taxable year begins. . 
</blockquote>


==(b) HSAs– ==


:(1)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 Subparagraph (A) of section 223(f)(8) of such Code](relating to treatment after death of account beneficiary) is amended—
::(A) by inserting“or adult child”after“surviving spouse”,
::(B) by inserting“or adult child, as the case may be,”after“the spouse”, and
::(C) by inserting“ or adult child ”after“ spouse ”in the heading thereof.
:(2)  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/223 Paragraph (8) of section 223(f) of such Code]is amended by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote> 

====(C) Adult child– ====

For purposes of this paragraph, the term adult child has the meaning given to such term by[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26/220 section 220(f)(8)(C)]. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Effective date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall apply to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2014.
