{{Center|A BILL}}

To prohibit the use of lethal military force against citizens of the United States located within the United States.

={{anchor|HC1CE539D828647C1805DDF414B0CC4A9}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Life, Liberty, and Justice for All Americans Act”.

={{anchor|HEAF5410A894149378AA39C8CAC52DE29}}Sec. 2. Prohibition on use of lethal military force against citizens of the United States within the United States=



=={{anchor|HB82012B9DC9B489791E8F70BB96D004D}}(a) Prohibition– ==

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] may not use lethal military force against a citizen of the United States who is located in the United States.

=={{anchor|H821D3F51F2D44B04B7A7E2D0C44D6E12}}(b) Exception– ==

The prohibition under subsection (a)shall not apply to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] using lethal military force against an individual if the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] determines that—
:(1) the individual poses an imminent threat of death or serious bodily injury to another individual; and
:(2) using such force will prevent or minimize such deaths or serious bodily injuries.

=={{anchor|HFD12164CEBAF42D1BAE63886CE48B26C}}(c) Construction– ==

Nothing in this section shall be construed to suggest that the Constitution would otherwise allow the killing of a citizen of the United States without due process of law.

=={{anchor|HF205A82BB96E4637A51DC49913360F1B}}(d) Definitions– ==

In this section:
:(1) The term lethal military force means a targeted killing or other lethal action by the Federal Government that is typically used against an enemy of the United States, including through the use of an unmanned aircraft or other aircraft, regardless of the department or agency carrying out the targeted killing or other action.
:(2) The term serious bodily injury has the meaning given that term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1365 section 1365(h)(3) of title 18, United States Code] .
:(3) The term United States , as used in a territorial sense, has the meaning given that term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// section 5] of title 18, United States Code.
:(4) The term unmanned aircraft has the meaning given that term in section 331(8) of the FAA Modernization and Reform Act of 2012([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/40101 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 49 U.S.C. 40101] note] ).
