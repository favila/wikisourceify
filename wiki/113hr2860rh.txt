{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend title 5, United States Code, to provide that the Inspector General of the Office of Personnel Management may use amounts in the revolving fund of the Office to fund audits, investigations, and oversight activities, and for other purposes.    

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ OPM IG Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Use of Office of Personnel Management revolving fund for audits, investigations, and oversight activities=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1304 Subsection (e) ofsection 1304of title 5, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1304 paragraph (1)], by adding before the period at the end of the first sentence the following:“, and for the cost of audits, investigations, and oversight activities, conducted by the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Inspector General of the Office]] , of the fund and the activities financed by the fund”; and
:(2) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1304 paragraph (5)]—
::(A) by striking“The Office”and inserting“(A) The [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Office]]  ”; and
::(B) by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote> 
:::(B) Such budget shall include an estimate from the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Inspector General of the Office]] of the amount required to pay the expenses to audit, investigate, and provide other oversight activities with respect to the fund and the activities financed by the fund. 
:::(C) The amount requested by the [[w:Office of Personnel Management|Inspector General]] under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1304 subparagraph (B)]shall not exceed .33 percent of the total budgetary authority requested by the Office under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/1304 subparagraph (A)]. . 
</blockquote>
