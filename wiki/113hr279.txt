{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Act of June 18, 1934, to reaffirm the authority of the Secretary of the Interior to take land into trust for Indian tribes, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|H33317332098F430EABC346E42C66FC8A}}Section 1. Authority reaffirmed=



=={{anchor|HD83113FB422A48F2B1468C9E5C59184A}}(a) Reaffirmation– ==

Section 19 of the Act of June 18, 1934 (commonly known as the “Indian Reorganization Act”; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/479 25 U.S.C. 479]), is amended—
:(1) in the first sentence—
::(A) by striking “The term” and inserting “Effective beginning on June 18, 1934, the term”; and
::(B) by striking “any recognized Indian tribe now under Federal jurisdiction” and inserting “any federally recognized Indian tribe”; and
:(2) by striking the third sentence and inserting the following: “In said sections, the term “Indian tribe” means any Indian or Alaska Native tribe, band, nation, pueblo, village, or community that the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]] acknowledges to exist as an Indian tribe.”.

=={{anchor|H47E98EA7B97244BBB57B18768A7AB87C}}(b) Effective Date– ==

The amendments made by this section shall take effect as if included in the Act of June 18, 1934 (commonly known as the “Indian Reorganization Act”; [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/479 25 U.S.C. 479]), on the date of enactment of that Act.
