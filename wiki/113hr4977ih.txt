{{Center|A BILL}}

 To establish a commission to examine the evidence-based therapy treatment model used by the Secretary of Veterans Affairs for treating mental illnesses of veterans and the potential benefits of incorporating complementary alternative treatments available in non-Department of Veterans Affairs medical facilities within the community.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   Creating Options for Veterans Expedited Recovery Act   ”or the“  COVER Act  ”. 

=Sec. 2. Establishment and duties=



==(a) Establishment– ==

There is established the [[w:|Veterans Expedited Recovery Commission]](in this Act referred to as the“Commission”).

==(b) Duties– ==

The [[w:|Commission]]shall perform the following duties:
:(1) Examine the efficacy of the evidence-based therapy model used by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]for treating mental health illnesses of veterans and identify areas to improve wellness-based outcomes.
:(2) Conduct a patient-centered survey within each of the Veterans Integrated Service Networks to examine—
::(A) the experience of veterans with the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]]when seeking medical assistance for mental health issues through the health care system of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]];
::(B) the experience of veterans with non- [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]medical facilities and health professionals for treating mental health issues;
::(C) the preferences of veterans regarding available treatments for mental health issues and which methods the veterans believe to be most effective;
::(D) the experience, if any, of veterans with respect to the complementary alternative treatment therapies described in subparagraphs (A) through (I) in paragraph (3) ;
::(E) the prevalence of prescribing prescription medication among veterans seeking treatment through the health care system of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]as remedies for addressing mental health issues; and
::(F) the outreach efforts of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]regarding the availability of benefits and treatments for veterans for addressing mental health issues, including by identifying ways to reduce barriers to and gaps in such benefits and treatments.
:(3) Examine available research on complementary alternative treatment therapies for mental health issues and identify what benefits could be made with the inclusion of such treatments for veterans, including with respect to—
::(A) music therapy;
::(B) equine therapy;
::(C) training and caring for service dogs;
::(D) yoga therapy;
::(E) acupuncture therapy;
::(F) meditation therapy;
::(G) outdoor sports therapy;
::(H) hyperbaric oxygen therapy;
::(I) accelerated resolution therapy; and
::(J) other therapies the [[w:|Commission]]determines appropriate.
:(4) Study the potential increase in the approval by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]of claims for compensation relating to mental health issues for veterans who served Operation Enduring Freedom, Operation Iraqi Freedom, and Operation New Dawn. 

=Sec. 3. Membership=



==(a) Number and appointment– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The [[w:|Commission]]shall be composed of 10 members, appointed as follows:
:(A) Two members appointed by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Speaker of the House of Representatives]], at least one of whom shall be a veteran.
:(B) Two members appointed by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|Minority Leader of the House of Representatives]], at least one of whom shall be a veteran.
:(C) Two members appointed by the [[w:United States Senate|Majority Leader of the Senate]], at least one of whom shall be a veteran.
:(D) Two members appointed by the [[w:United States Senate|Minority Leader of the Senate]], at least one of whom shall be a veteran.
:(E) Two members appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]], at least one of whom shall be a veteran.

===(2) Qualifications– ===

Members of the [[w:|Commission]]shall be—
:(A) individuals who are of recognized standing and distinction within the medical community with a background in treating mental health; and
:(B) individuals with experience working with the military and veteran population.

==(b) Chairman– ==

The [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]shall designate a member of the [[w:|Commission]]to be the chairman.

==(c) Period of Appointment– ==

Members of the [[w:|Commission]]shall be appointed for the life of the [[w:|Commission]].

==(d) Vacancy– ==

A vacancy in the [[w:|Commission]]shall be filled in the manner in which the original appointment was made.

==(e) Appointment Deadline– ==

The appointment of members of the [[w:|Commission]]in this section shall be made not later than 90 days after the date of the enactment of this Act. 

=Sec. 4. Powers of Commission=



==(a) Meeting– ==



===(1) Initial meeting– ===

The [[w:|Commission]]shall hold its first meeting not later than 30 days after a majority of members are appointed to the [[w:|Commission]].

===(2) Meeting– ===

The [[w:|Commission]]shall regularly meet at the call of the [[w:|Chairman]]. Such meetings may be carried out through the use of telephonic or other appropriate telecommunication technology if the [[w:|Commission]]determines that such technology will allow the members to communicate simultaneously.

==(b) Hearing– ==

The [[w:|Commission]]may hold such hearings, sit and act at such times and places, take such testimony, and receive evidence as the [[w:|Commission]]considers advisable to carry out the responsibilities of the [[w:|Commission]].

==(c) Information from Federal Agencies– ==

The [[w:|Commission]]may secure directly from any department or agency of the Federal Government such information as the [[w:|Commission]]considers necessary to carry out the duties of the [[w:|Commission]].

==(d) Information from nongovernmental organizations– ==

In carrying out section 2(b) , the [[w:|Commission]]may seek guidance through consultation with foundations, veterans service organizations, nonprofit groups, faith-based organizations, private and public institutions of higher education, and other organizations as the [[w:|Commission]]determines appropriate.

==(e) Commission Records– ==

The [[w:|Commission]]shall keep an accurate and complete record of the actions and meetings of the [[w:|Commission]]. Such record shall be made available for public inspection and the [[w:Government Accountability Office|Comptroller General of the United States]]may audit and examine such record.

==(f) Personnel Matters– ==

Upon request of the chairman of the [[w:|Commission]], the head of any department or agency of the Federal Government may detail, on a reimbursable basis, any personnel of that department or agency to assist the [[w:|Commission]]in carrying out the duties of the [[w:|Commission]].

==(g) Compensation of Members; Travel Expenses– ==

Each member shall serve without pay, except that each member shall receive travel expenses to perform the duties of the [[w:|Commission]]under section 2(b) of this Act , including per diem in lieu of subsistence, at rates authorized under subchapter I of chapter 57of title 5, United States Code.

==(h) Staff– ==

The [[w:|Chairman]], in accordance with rules agreed upon by the [[w:|Commission]], may appoint and fix the compensation of a staff director and such other personnel as may be necessary to enable the [[w:|Commission]]to carry out its functions, without regard to the provisions of[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/ title 5, United States Code], governing appointments in the competitive service, without regard to the provision ofchapter 51andsubchapter III of chapter 53 of such titlerelating to classification and General Schedule pay rates, except that no rate of pay fixed under this subsection may exceed the equivalent of that payable for a position at a level IV of the Executive Schedule under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5316 section 5316 of title 5, United States Code] .

==(i) Personnel as Federal employees– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The executive director and any personnel of the [[w:|Commission]]are employees under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/2105 section 2105 of title 5, United States Code], for purpose ofchapters 63,81,83,84,85,87,89, and90 of such title.

===(2) Members of the Commission– ===

  Paragraph (1) shall not be construed to apply to members of the [[w:|Commission]].

==(j) Contracting– ==

The [[w:|Commission]]may, to such extent and in such amounts as are provided in appropriations Acts, enter into contracts to enable the [[w:|Commission]]to discharge the duties of the [[w:|Commission]]under this Act.

==(k) Expert and Consultant Service– ==

The Commission may procure the services of experts and consultants in accordance with[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3109 section 3109 of title 5], United States Code, at rates not to exceed the daily rate paid to a person occupying a position at level IV of the Executive Schedule under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5315  section 5315of title 5, United States Code].

==(l) Postal Service– ==

The [[w:|Commission]]may use the United States mails in the same manner and under the same conditions as departments and agencies of the United States.

==(m) Physical Facilities and Equipment– ==

Upon the request of the [[w:|Commission]], the [[w:General Services Administration|Administrator of General Services]]shall provide to the [[w:|Commission]], on a reimbursable basis, the administrative support services necessary for the [[w:|Commission]]to carry out its responsibilities under this Act. These administrative services may include human resource management, budget, leasing, accounting, and payroll services. 

=Sec. 5. Report=



==(a) Interim Reports– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Not later than 60 days after the date on which the [[w:|Commission]]first meets, and each 30-day period thereafter ending on the date on which the [[w:|Commission]]submits the final report under subsection (b) , the [[w:|Commission]]shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Senate]]and the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]]a report detailing the level of cooperation the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]](and the heads of other departments or agencies of the Federal Government) has provided to the Commission.

===(2) Other reports– ===

In carrying out the duties pursuant to section 2(b) , at times that the [[w:|Commission]]determines appropriate, the [[w:|Commission]]shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Senate]]and any other appropriate entities an interim report with respect to the findings identified by the [[w:|Commission]].

==(b) Final report– ==

Not later than 18 months after the first meeting of the [[w:|Commission]], the [[w:|Commission]]shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Senate]], the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]], and the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]a final report on the findings of the [[w:|Commission]]. Such report shall include the following:
:(1) Recommendations to implement in a feasible, timely, and cost-effective manner the solutions and remedies identified within the findings of the [[w:|Commission]]pursuant to section 2(b) .
:(2) An analysis of the evidence-based therapy model used by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]for treating veterans with mental health care issues, and an examination of the prevalence and efficacy of prescription drugs as a means for treatment.
:(3) The findings of the patient-centered survey conducted within each of the Veterans Integrated Service Networks pursuant to section 2(b)(2) .
:(4) An examination of complementary alternative treatments described in section 2(b)(3) and the potential benefits of incorporating such treatments in the therapy model used by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for treating veterans with mental health issues.

==(c) Plan– ==

Not later than 90 days after the date on which the [[w:|Commission]]submits the final report under subsection (b) , the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary of Veterans Affairs]]shall submit to the [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the House of Representatives]]and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Senate]]a report on the following:
:(1) An action plan for implementing the recommendations established by the [[w:|Commission]]on such solutions and remedies for improving wellness-based outcomes for veterans with mental health care issues.
:(2) A feasible timeframe on when complementary alternative treatments described in section 2(b)(3) can be implemented [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]]-wide.
:(3) If the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]determines that implementing the recommendations established by the [[w:|Commission]], including with respect to such complementary alternative treatments, is not appropriate or feasible, a justification for such determination and an alternative solution to improve the efficacy of the therapy model used by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]]for treating veterans with mental health issues. 

=Sec. 6. Termination of Commission=

The [[w:|Commission]]shall terminate 30 days after the [[w:|Commission]]submits the final report under section 5(b) .
