{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend titles 10, 32, 37, and 38 of the United States Code, to add a definition of spouse for purposes of military personnel policies and military and veteran benefits that recognizes new State definitions of spouse.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“ Military Spouses Equal Treatment Act ”. 

=Sec. 2. Definition of spouse for purposes of military personnel policies and military and veteran benefits to reflect new State definitions of spouse=



==(a) Title 10– ==



===(1) Definition– ===

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/101 Paragraph (5) of section 101(f) of title 10, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 
:(5) Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 section 7 of title 1], an individual shall be considered a“spouse”if the marriage of the individual is valid in the State in which the marriage was entered into or, in the case of a marriage entered into outside any State, if the marriage is valid in the place in which the marriage was entered into and the marriage could have been entered into in a State. In this paragraph, the term“State”means the several States, the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands, and the territories and possessions. . 
</blockquote>


===(2) Conforming amendments– ===

Such section is further amended—
:(A) in the matter preceding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 paragraph (1)]. by striking“In this title—”and inserting“The following rules of construction apply in this title:”;
:(B) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 paragraph (1)]—
::(i) by inserting“The term”after“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 (1)] ”; and
::(ii) by striking the semicolon at the end and inserting a period;
:(C) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 paragraph (2)]—
::(i) by inserting“The term”after“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 (2)] ”; and
::(ii) by striking the semicolon at the end and inserting a period;
:(D) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 paragraph (3)]—
::(i) by inserting“The phrase”after“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 (3)] ”; and
::(ii) by striking the semicolon at the end and inserting a period; and
:(E) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 paragraph (4)]—
::(i) by inserting“The term”after“ [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 (4)] ”; and
::(ii) by striking“; and”and inserting a period.

==(b) Title 32– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/32/101 Paragraph (18) of section 101 of title 32, United States Code], is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote> 
:(18) Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 section 7 of title 1], an individual shall be considered a“spouse”if the marriage of the individual is valid in the State in which the marriage was entered into or, in the case of a marriage entered into outside any State, if the marriage is valid in the place in which the marriage was entered into and the marriage could have been entered into in a State. In this paragraph, the term“State”means the several States, the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands, and the territories and possessions. . 
</blockquote>


==(c) Title 37– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/37/101 Section 101 of title 37, United States Code], is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
:(27) Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 section 7 of title 1], an individual shall be considered a“spouse”if the marriage of the individual is valid in the State in which the marriage was entered into or, in the case of a marriage entered into outside any State, if the marriage is valid in the place in which the marriage was entered into and the marriage could have been entered into in a State. In this paragraph, the term“State”means the several States, the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands, and the territories and possessions. . 
</blockquote>


==(d) Title 38– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/101 Section 101 of title 38, United States Code], is amended—
:(1) in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/101 paragraph (3)], by striking“of the opposite sex”; and
:(2) by striking[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/101 paragraph (31)]and inserting the following new paragraph:

<blockquote> 
::(31) Notwithstanding[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/1/7 section 7 of title 1], an individual shall be considered a“spouse”if the marriage of the individual is valid in the State in which the marriage was entered into or, in the case of a marriage entered into outside any State, if the marriage is valid in the place in which the marriage was entered into and the marriage could have been entered into in a State. In this paragraph, the term“State”has the meaning given that term in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/101 paragraph (20)], except that the term also includes the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands. . 
</blockquote>
