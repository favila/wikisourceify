{{Center|A BILL}}

 To amend the Act of June 18, 1934, to reaffirm the authority of the Secretary of the Interior to take land into trust for Indian tribes.   

=Section 1. Reaffirmation of authority=



==(a) Modification– ==



===(1) In general– ===

The first sentence of  section 19 of the Act of June 18, 1934 (commonly known as the“  Indian Reorganization Act  ”) ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/479 25 U.S.C. 479] ), is amended—
:(A) by striking“The term”and inserting“Effective beginning on June 18, 1934, the term”; and
:(B) by striking“any recognized Indian tribe now under Federal jurisdiction”and inserting“any federally recognized Indian tribe”.

===(2) Effective date– ===

The amendments made byparagraph (1)shall take effect as if included in the  Act of June 18, 1934 (commonly known as the“  Indian Reorganization Act  ”) ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/479 25 U.S.C. 479] ), on the date of enactment of that Act.

==(b) Ratification and Confirmation of Actions– ==

Any action taken by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]]pursuant to the  Act of June 18, 1934 (commonly known as the“  Indian Reorganization Act  ”) ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/461 25 U.S.C. 461 et seq.] ), for any Indian tribe that was federally recognized on the date of that action is ratified and confirmed, to the extent such action is subjected to challenge based on whether the Indian tribe was federally recognized or under Federal jurisdiction on June 18, 1934, as if the action had, by prior Act of Congress, been specifically authorized and directed.

==(c) Effect on Other Laws– ==



===(1) In general– ===

Nothing in this section or the amendments made by this section shall affect—
:(A) the application or effect of any Federal law other than the  Act of June 18, 1934 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/461 25 U.S.C. 461 et seq.]), as amended bysubsection (a); or
:(B) any limitation on the authority of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]]under any Federal law or regulation other than the  Act of June 18, 1934 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/461 25 U.S.C. 461 et seq.] ), as so amended.

===(2) References in other laws– ===

An express reference to the  Act of June 18, 1934 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/461 25 U.S.C. 461 et seq.] ), contained in any other Federal law shall be considered to be a reference to that Act as amended bysubsection (a).

==(d) Study; publication– ==



===(1) Study– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]]shall conduct, and submit to [[w:United States Congress|Congress]]a report describing the results of, a study that—
:(A) assesses the effects of the decision of the [[w:Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]]in the case styled Carcieri v. Salazar (129 S. Ct. 1058) on Indian tribes and tribal land; and
:(B) includes a list of each Indian tribe and parcel of tribal land affected by that decision.

===(2) Publication– ===

On completion of the report underparagraph (1)and by not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this Act, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary of the Interior]]shall publish the list described inparagraph (1)(B)—
:(A) in the Federal Register; and
:(B) on the public website of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Department of the Interior]].
