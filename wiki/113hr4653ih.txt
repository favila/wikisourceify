{{Center|A BILL}}

 To reauthorize the United States Commission on International Religious Freedom, and for other purposes.   

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“   United States Commission on International Religious Freedom Reauthorization Act of 2014   ”. 

=Sec. 2. Establishment and composition=



==(a) In general– ==

   Subsection (a) of section 201 of the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/6431 22 U.S.C. 6431] )is amended by inserting before the period at the end the following:“, which shall be an independent Federal Government advisory body”.

==(b) Membership– ==

   Subsection (b)(3) of section 201 of the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/6431 22 U.S.C. 6431])is amended by striking“The appointments required by paragraph (1) shall be made not later than 120 days after the date of the enactment of this Act.”and inserting the following:“The President and Members of Congress are encouraged to appoint members of the Commission within 90 days of a vacancy on the Commission.”.

==(c) Vacancies– ==

   Subsection (g) of section 201 of the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/6431 22 U.S.C. 6431] )is amended by striking the second sentence. 

=Sec. 3. Training for Foreign Service officers=

   Subsection (a) of section 708 of the Foreign Service Act of 1980 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/4028 22 U.S.C. 4028] )is amended—
:(1) in the matter preceding paragraph (1) ,
::(A) by striking“and the director”and inserting“the director”; and
::(B) inserting“and members of the [[w:United States Commission on International Religious Freedom|United States Commission on International Religious Freedom]],”after“Training Center,”; and
:(2) in paragraph (2) —
::(A) by striking“and the various”and inserting“the various”; and
::(B) by inserting“, the relationship between religious freedom and security, and the role of religious freedom in United States foreign policy”after“violations of religious freedom”. 

=Sec. 4. Commission personnel matters=



==(a) In general– ==

   Subsection (a) of section 204 of the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/6432b 22 U.S.C. 6432b] )is amended in the second sentence, by inserting“voting”after“nine”.

==(b) Compensation– ==

   Subsection (b) of section 204 of the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/6432b 22 U.S.C. 6432b] )is amended by inserting“voting members of the”after“The”.

==(c) Security clearances– ==

   Subsection (e) of section 204 of the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/6432b 22 U.S.C. 6432b] )is amended by adding at the end the following new sentence:“The [[w:United States Department of State|Department of State]]is encouraged to allow Commissioners and Commission staff with the appropriate security clearance access to classified information, in order to fulfill the duties and responsibilities of their positions.”. 

=Sec. 5. Authorization of appropriations=

   Subsection (a) of section 207 of the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/6435 22 U.S.C. 6435] )is amended by striking“2014”and inserting“   2019  ”. 

=Sec. 6. Standards of conduct and disclosure=

   Paragraph (2) of section 208(d)(2) of the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 ([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/6435a 22 U.S.C. 6435a(d)(2)])is amended by adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

<blockquote> 
:(H) Intern, fellowship, and volunteer programs that are primarily of educational benefit to the intern, fellow, or volunteer. Sponsoring private parties may provide compensation and benefits to interns, fellows, and volunteers, provided that no conflict of interest arises. . 
</blockquote>
 

=Sec. 7. Termination=

   Section 209 of the International Religious Freedom Act of 1998 ( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/22/6436 22 U.S.C. 6436] )is amended by striking“September 30, 2014”and inserting“September 30, 2019”.
