{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Social Security Act to prohibit an individual who is the subject of an outstanding arrest warrant for a felony from receiving various cash benefits under the Social Security Act.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Control Unlawful Fugitive Felons Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Revisions to provisions limiting payment of benefits to fugitive felons under titles II, VIII, and XVI of the Social Security Act=



==(a) Title II amendments– ==



===(1) Fugitive felon warrant requirement– ===

 Section 202(x)(1)(A)(iv) of the Social Security Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/402 42 U.S.C. 402(x)(1)(A)(iv)] )is amended—
:(A) by striking “fleeing to avoid” and inserting “the subject of an arrest warrant for the purpose of” ;
:(B) by striking “the place from which the person flees” the first place it appears and inserting “the jurisdiction issuing the warrant” ; and
:(C) by striking “the place from which the person flees” the second place it appears and inserting “the jurisdiction” .

===(2) Probation and parole violator warrant requirement– ===

 Section 202(x)(1)(A)(v) of the Social Security Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/402 42 U.S.C. 402(x)(1)(A)(v)] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>
:(v) is the subject of an arrest warrant for violating a condition of probation or parole imposed under Federal or State law..
</blockquote>


==(b) Title VIII amendments– ==



===(1) Fugitive felon warrant requirement– ===

 Section 804(a)(2) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1004 42 U.S.C. 1004(a)(2)] )is amended—
:(A) by striking “fleeing to avoid” and inserting “the subject of an arrest warrant for the purpose of” ;
:(B) by striking “the jurisdiction within the United States from which the person has fled” and inserting “any jurisdiction within the United States” ; and
:(C) by striking “place from which the person has fled” and inserting “jurisdiction issuing the warrant” .

===(2) Probation and parole warrant requirement– ===

 Section 804(a)(3) of the Social Security Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1004 42 U.S.C. 1004(a)(3)] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>
:(3) during any part of which the individual is the subject of an arrest warrant for violating a condition of probation or parole imposed under Federal or State law; or.
</blockquote>


===(3) Disclosure– ===

 Section 804 of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1004 42 U.S.C. 1004] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>
:(c) Notwithstanding the provisions of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/552a section 552aof title 5, United States Code] , or any other provision of Federal or State law (other than section 6103 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986and section 1106(c) of this Act), the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner]] shall furnish any Federal, State, or local law enforcement officer, upon written request of the officer, with the current address, Social Security number, and photograph (if applicable) of any individual who is a recipient of (or would be such a recipient but for the application of paragraph (2)or (3) of subsection (a)) benefits under this title, if the officer furnishes the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner]] with the name of the individual and other identifying information as reasonably required by the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner]] to establish the unique identity of the individual, and notifies the [[w:Social Security Administration|Commissioner]] that—
::(1) the individual is described in paragraph (2)or (3) of subsection (a); and
::(2) the location or apprehension of such individual is within the officer’s official duties..
</blockquote>


==(c) Title XVI amendments– ==



===(1) Fugitive felon warrant requirement– ===

 Section 1611(e)(4)(A)(i) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1382 42 U.S.C. 1382(e)(4)(A)(i)] )is amended—
:(A) by striking “fleeing to avoid” and inserting “the subject of an arrest warrant for the purpose of” ;
:(B) by striking “the place from which the person flees” the first place it appears and inserting “the jurisdiction issuing the warrant” ; and
:(C) by striking “the place from which the person flees” the second place it appears and inserting “the jurisdiction” .

===(2) Probation and parole warrant requirement– ===

 Section 1611(e)(4)(A)(ii) of the Social Security Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1382 42 U.S.C. 1382(e)(4)(A)(ii)] )is amended to read as follows:

<blockquote>
:(ii) the subject of an arrest warrant for violating a condition of probation or parole imposed under Federal or State law.
</blockquote>


===(3) Disclosure– ===

 Section 1611(e)(5) of such Act( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1382 42 U.S.C. 1382(e)(5)] )is amended—
:(A) by striking “any recipient of” and inserting “any individual who is a recipient of (or would be such a recipient but for the application of paragraph (4)(A)” ; and
:(B) by striking “the recipient” each place it appears and inserting “the individual” .

=Sec. 3. Effective date=

The amendments made by this Act shall be effective with respect to benefits payable under the Social Security Actfor months that begin after 270 days following the date of enactment of this Act.
