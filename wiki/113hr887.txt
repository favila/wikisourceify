{{Center|A BILL}}

To terminate the Economic Development Administration, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HBB8585B08DD649E595DD6FE112F42F58}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“EDA Elimination Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H6997DA35AE27432CB5F06A86BEF2E31F}}Sec. 2. Termination of Economic Development Administration=



=={{anchor|H2C36014D1B5A49DD895ABDA5E840BF2F}}(a) Termination– ==

The [[w:|Economic Development Administration]] is terminated.

=={{anchor|H4AF0C484CC8F43E9B67BE80FB09C2099}}(b) Repeal– ==

The Public Works and Economic Development Act of 1965([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/3121 42 U.S.C. 3121 et seq.] )is repealed.

=={{anchor|HAF7BEAD7A35941FCA602D5ACD209DCF2}}(c) Conclusion of business– ==

The [[w:United States Department of Commerce|Secretary of Commerce]] shall take such actions as may be necessary and appropriate to conclude the outstanding affairs of the [[w:|Economic Development Administration]] , including dispositions of personnel, assets, and obligations.

=={{anchor|H9E6766BE18A04D5F95AF1F8D8C581C74}}(d) Limitation on statutory construction– ==

This section may not be construed to prevent the expenditure of any funds received before the effective date of this Act. Such funds shall be subject to the laws and regulations that would have applied to the funds if this section had not been enacted.

={{anchor|H56D1CE73C9384506805327086BEC16A8}}Sec. 3. Effective date=

This Act shall take effect on the first day of the first fiscal year beginning after the date of enactment of this Act.
