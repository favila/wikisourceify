{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946 to suspend the salary of Members of Congress and deem Members of Congress as non-essential employees during a government shutdown.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Shutdown Pay for Members of Congress Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Elimination of pay for Members of Congress=

 Section 601(a)(2) of the Legislative Reorganization Act of 1946( [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/2/31 2 U.S.C. 31] )is amended by amending the section to read as follows:

<blockquote>
:(C) Effective with the termination of a fiscal year at the end of which the [[w:United States House of Representatives|U.S. House of Representatives]] and the [[w:United States Senate|U.S. Senate]] fail to reach agreement on government spending, Members of Congress may not receive pay until a government spending agreement by the [[w:United States House of Representatives|U.S. House of Representatives]] and [[w:United States Senate|U.S. Senate]] on future spending is agreed to..
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Definition of essential and non-Essential Federal employees=

Consistent with the Constitution and the Anti-Deficiency Act, [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1341 31 U.S.C. 1341] , [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1342 1342] , the [[w:United States House Committee on House Administration|Committee on House Administration]] is required to consider the following definitions for the legislative branch including:
:(1)  “Employing authority” under House Rules where Members of Congress would be deemed “non-essential” .

=Sec. 4. Effective date=

The amendments made by this Act shall apply with respect to any adjustment which, but for this Act, would otherwise become effective in the event of a lapse in appropriations for government spending where the government would thereby shut down.
