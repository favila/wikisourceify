{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to require that the Secretary of the Treasury follow certain procedures relating to status applications of 501(c)(4) organizations.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Strict Standards and Accountability Act” .

=Sec. 2. Certain procedures relating to status applications of501(c)(4)organizations=



==(a) In general– ==

 Part I of subchapter F ofchapter 1of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 506. Procedures relating to applications of501(c)(4)organizations==



===(a) Initial notice– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall acknowledge in writing the receipt of an application to be treated as an organization described in section 501(c)(4)and exempt from tax under section 501(a)not later than 28 days after receipt of such application.

===(b) 6 months– ===

In the case that the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] has not approved or denied such application after 6 months after the date of the acknowledgment specified in paragraph (1)the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall—
:(1) 
::(A) inform the applicant in writing as to the status of its application,
::(B) enumerate reasons specific to the application for its delay, and
::(C) provide an approximate expectation of when a final decision may be made, and
:(2) consider such application under fast track procedures which the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall provide by regulation or other guidance.

===(c) 1-Year– ===



====(1) In general– ====

In the case that the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] has not approved or denied such application after 365 days after the date of the acknowledgment specified in paragraph (1), the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall—
:(A) inform the applicant in writing as to the status of its application, and
:(B) enumerate reasons specific to the application for its delay.

====(2) Notice to Congress– ====

If the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] cannot make a decision before two months after 365 days from the date of the initial acknowledgment under subsection (a), the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall report during that time to the Chairpersons of the [[w:United States House Committee on Ways and Means|Committee on Ways and Means Committee]] and [[w:United States Senate Committee on Finance|Senate Finance Committee]] on the extenuating circumstances of each such case that prevents timely determination thereof and provide the numerical amount of 501(c)(4) applications pending for over 365 days..
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendment– ==

The table of sections for part I of subchapter F of chapter 1 of such Codeis amended by adding at the end the following new item:

<blockquote>**[[#H80D547E538E843B98AA9F05683745507|Sec. 506. Procedures relating to applications of501(c)(4)organizations.]]
.
</blockquote>


==(c) Effective date– ==

The amendment made by this section shall apply to applications submitted after the date of the enactment of this Act.
