{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 18, United States Code, to provide for penalties for aggravated identity theft facilitated by employment at an agency implementing the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “No Identity Theft in Health Care Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Certain healthcare-related offenses=

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028A Section 1028Aof title 18, United States Code] , is amended by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028A paragraph (2)] :

<blockquote>

==(3) Certain healthcare-related offenses– ==

Whoever, during and in relation to any felony violation enumerated in [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/1028A subsection (c)] , knowingly transfers, possesses, or uses, without lawful authority, a means of identification of another person, if that transfer, possession, or use was facilitated by being an officer or employee of the United States or of any department or agency thereof charged with the implementation of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, shall, in addition to the punishment provided for such felony, be sentenced to a term of imprisonment of 5 years..
</blockquote>
