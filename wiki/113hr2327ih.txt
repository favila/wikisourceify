{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend title 38, United States Code, to establish in the Department of Veterans Affairs a Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Establishment of Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration of Department of Veterans Affairs=



==(a) Economic Opportunity Administration– ==

 [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/ Part V of title 38, United States Code] , is amended by adding at the end the following new chapter:

<blockquote>

===80 Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration– ===

**[[#HED9AE05B18B44CFB8497FFB24B5F45D5|8001. Organization of Administration.]]
**[[#HCD098E6B407F4D5E9EA227856D15311A|8002. Functions of Administration.]]


===Sec. 8001. Organization of Administration===



====(a) Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration– ====

There is in the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department of Veterans Affairs]] a [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration]] . The primary function of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration]] is the administration of the programs of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] which provide assistance related to economic opportunity to veterans and their dependents and survivors.

====(b) Under Secretary for Economic Opportunity– ====

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration]] is under the Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity, who is directly responsible to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] for the operations of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Administration]] .

===Sec. 8002. Functions of Administration===


: The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration]] is responsible for the administration of the following programs of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] :
::(1) Vocational rehabilitation and employment programs.
::(2) Educational assistance programs.
::(3) Veterans’ housing loan and related programs.
::(4) Veterans small business programs, including the program under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/8127 section 8127 of this title] ..
</blockquote>


==(b) Clerical amendments– ==

The tables of chapters at the beginning of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/ title 38] , and of [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/ part V of title 38] , are each amended by inserting after the item relating tochapter 79the following new item:

<blockquote>80.Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration8001.
</blockquote>


=Sec. 3. Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity=



==(a) Under Secretary– ==

Chapter 3of title 38, United States Code, is amended by inserting after [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/306 section 306] the following new section:

<blockquote>

==Sec. 306A. Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity==



===(a) Under Secretary– ===

There is in the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] an [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity]] , who is appointed by the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] , by and with the advice and consent of the [[w:|Senate]] . The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity]] shall be appointed without regard to political affiliation or activity and solely on the basis of demonstrated ability in—
:(1) information technology; and
:(2) the administration of programs within the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration]] or programs of similar content and scope.

===(b) Responsibilities– ===

The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity]] is the head of, and is directly responsible to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] for the operations of, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration]] .

===(c) Vacancies– ===


:(1) Whenever a vacancy in the position of [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity]] occurs or is anticipated, the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall establish a commission to recommend individuals to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] for appointment to the position.
:(2) A commission established under this subsection shall be composed of the following members appointed by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] :
::(A) Three persons representing education and training, vocational rehabilitation, employment, real estate, mortgage finance and related industries, and survivor benefits activities affected by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration]] .
::(B) Two persons representing veterans served by the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans Economic Opportunity Administration]] .
::(C) Two persons who have experience in the management of private sector benefits programs of similar content and scope to the economic opportunity programs of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Department]] .
::(D) The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Deputy Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] .
::(E) The chairman of the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Veterans’ Advisory Committee on Education]] formed under [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/38/3692 section 3692 of this title] .
::(F) One person who has held the position of [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity]] , if the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] determines that it is desirable for such person to be a member of the commission.
:(3) A commission established under this subsection shall recommend at least three individuals for appointment to the position of [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity]] . The commission shall submit all recommendations to the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] . The [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] shall forward the recommendations to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] and the [[w:United States Senate Committee on Veterans' Affairs|Committees on Veterans’ Affairs of the Senate]] and [[w:United States House Committee on Veterans' Affairs|House of Representatives]] with any comments the [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Secretary]] considers appropriate. Thereafter, the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] may request the commission to recommend additional individuals for appointment.
:(4) The Assistant Secretary or [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Deputy Assistant Secretary of Veterans Affairs]] who performs personnel management and labor relations functions shall serve as the executive secretary of a commission established under this subsection.

===(d) Qualifications of recommended individuals– ===

Each individual recommended to the [[w:Executive Office of the President|President]] by the commission for appointment to the position of [[w:United States Department of Veterans Affairs|Under Secretary for Veterans Economic Opportunity]] shall be an individual who has held a senior level position in the private sector with responsibilities relating to at least one of the following:
:(1) Education policy.
:(2) Vocational rehabilitation.
:(3) Employment.
:(4) Home loan finance.
:(5) Small business development..
</blockquote>
