{{Center|A BILL}}

To recalculate and restore retirement annuity obligations of the United States Postal Service, eliminate the requirement that the United States Postal Service pre-fund the Postal Service Retiree Health Benefits Fund, place restrictions on the closure of postal facilities, create incentives for innovation for the United States Postal Service, to maintain levels of postal service, and for other purposes.

=Section 1. Short title; table of contents=



==(a) Short title– ==

This Act may be cited as the“Postal Service Protection Act of 2013”.

==Sec. 105. Effective date; rule of construction==



===(a) In general– ===

This title and the amendments made by this title shall take effect on the date of enactment of this Act.

===(b) Intent of Congress– ===

It is the intent of [[w:United States Congress|Congress]] that this title apply with respect to the allocation of past, present, and future benefit liabilities between the [[w:United States Postal Service|United States Postal Service]] and the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Treasury of the United States]] .

===(c) Rule of construction– ===

Nothing in this Act or the amendments made by this Act shall be construed to prohibit the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] from paying any amount of surplus contributions to the Civil Service Retirement and Disability Fund and the Federal Employees’ Retirement System into—
:(1) the Postal Service Retiree Health Benefits Fund established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/8909a section 8909a of title 5, United States Code] ; and
:(2) the Postal Service Fund established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/2003 section 2003 of title 39, United States Code] .

:::(A) conduct an investigation to assess the need for the proposed closure or consolidation; and
:::(B) ensure that each person served by the postal facility has an opportunity to present views by providing appropriate notice—
::::(i) to each person by mail; and
::::(ii) by publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the area in which each person resides.
::(2) In conducting an investigation under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 paragraph (1)(A)] and determining whether to close or consolidate a postal facility under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 subsection (a)(3)] , the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] —
:::(A) shall consider—
::::(i) the effect the closing or consolidation would have on the community served by the postal facility;
::::(ii) the effect the closing or consolidation would have on employees of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] employed at the postal facility; and
::::(iii) whether the closing or consolidation would be consistent with the policy of the Government under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/101 section 101(b)] , that the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] shall provide a maximum degree of effective and regular postal services to rural areas, communities, and small towns where postal facilities are not self-sustaining; and

:::(B) Not later than 90 days before the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] makes a final determination under this subsection, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] shall—
::::(i) post notice of the decision and findings under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 subparagraph (A)] in a prominent place in each postal facility that would be affected by the closing or consolidation; and
::::(ii) send notice of each posting under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 clause (i)] by mail to each person served by the postal facility in which the notice is posted.

::::(ii) a brief summary of the findings of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] with respect to the factors specified in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 paragraph (2)(A)] ; and
::::(iii) the amount of cost savings the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] estimates the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] will realize from the closing or consolidation, in the first year following the date of the closing or consolidation, together with an explanation of the assumptions and methodologies used in making the estimate.
::(4) The [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] shall make a final determination to close or consolidate a postal facility in writing after taking into consideration any comments received during the 90-day period described under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/404 paragraph (3)] . The [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] shall take no action to close or consolidate a postal facility before the date that is 60 days after the date on which the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] —
:::(A) posts a copy of the final determination in a prominent location in each affected postal facility; and

::::(ii) provide any other information or affirmation that the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] may require, including with respect to the prepayment of State alcohol beverage taxes.
::(3) For purposes of this subsection—
:::(A) a winery shall be considered to be licensed if that winery holds an appropriate basic permit issued—
::::(i) under the Federal Alcohol Administration Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/27/201 27 U.S.C. 201 et seq.] ); and
::::(ii) under the law of the State in which the winery is located; and
:::(B) a brewery shall be considered to be licensed if that brewery—
::::(i) possesses a notice of registration and bond approved by the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau of the Department of the Treasury]] ; and
::::(ii) is licensed to manufacture and sell beer in the State in which the brewery is located..
</blockquote>

:(2) 120 days after the date of enactment of this Act.

==Sec. 303. Innovation and accountability==



===(a) In general– ===

Chapter 2 of title 39, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

===Sec. 209. Innovation and accountability===



====(a) Chief innovation officer– ====

There shall be in the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] a [[w:United States Postal Service|Chief Innovation Officer]] appointed by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]] who shall have proven expertise and a record of success in—
:(1) the postal and shipping industry;
:(2) innovative product research and development;
:(3) marketing brand strategy;
:(4) emerging communications technology; or
:(5) business process management.

====(b) Duties– ====

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Chief Innovation Officer]] shall—
:(1) lead the development of innovative nonpostal products and services that maximize the revenue of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] ;
:(2) develop innovative postal products and services, particularly products and services that use emerging information technologies, to maximize the revenue of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] ;
:(3) supervise the implementation of products and services described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 paragraphs (1) and (2)] ; and
:(4) monitor the performance of products and services described in[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 paragraphs (1) and (2)] and modify the products and services as needed to address changing market trends.

====(c) Considerations– ====

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Chief Innovation Office]] shall take into consideration comments or advisory opinions, if applicable, issued by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Regulatory Committee]] before the initial sale of innovative postal or nonpostal products and services.

====(d) Designation– ====



=====(1) Deadline– =====

As soon as practicable after the date of enactment of this section, and not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of this section, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]] shall appoint a [[w:United States Postal Service|Chief Innovation Officer]] .

=====(2) Condition– =====

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Chief Innovation Officer]] may not, while serving as [[w:United States Postal Service|Chief Innovation Officer]] , hold any other office or position in the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] .

=====(3) Rule of construction– =====

Nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit an individual who holds another office or position in the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] at the time the individual is appointed [[w:United States Postal Service|Chief Innovation Officer]] from serving as the [[w:United States Postal Service|Chief Innovation Officer]] under this section.

====(e) Advisory commission– ====



=====(1) Establishment– =====

There is established the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Innovation Advisory Commission]] (hereinafter the Commission ).

=====(2) Membership– =====



======(A) In general– ======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] shall be composed of—
:(i) the [[w:United States Postal Service|Chief Innovation Officer]] established under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 subsection (a)] , who shall serve as [[w:United States Postal Service|Chairperson of the Commission]] ; and
:(ii) 5 other members as the [[w:|Postal Regulatory Commission]] shall appoint, of whom—
::(I) 1 member shall have expertise in labor;
::(II) 1 member shall have expertise in small business issues;
::(III) 1 member shall have expertise in consumer protection;
::(IV) 1 member shall have expertise in small periodicals and newspapers; and
::(V) 1 member shall have expertise in the postal industry.

======(B) Period of appointment– ======

Members shall be appointed for 5-year terms.

======(C) Vacancies– ======

Any vacancy in the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] shall not affect the powers of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] , but shall be filled in the same manner as the original appointment.

======(D) Meetings– ======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] shall meet at the call of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Chairperson]] .

======(E) Quorum– ======

A majority of the members of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] shall constitute a quorum, but a lesser number of members may hold hearings.

=====(3) Duties of the commission– =====



======(A) Study– ======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] shall study—
:(i) additional postal and nonpostal services that the Postal Service may offer to raise revenue; and
:(ii) services that postal services in foreign countries have offered to respond to the increasing demand for high-tech services, including—
::(I) banking and insurance services;
::(II) communication services;
::(III) physical delivery of e-mail correspondence to individuals who do not have Internet access;
::(IV) scanning physical mail and delivering that mail through e-mail; and
::(V) communication through secure servers.

======(B) Recommendations– ======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] shall generate recommendations for additional services that the Postal Service should provide based on the study conducted under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 subparagraph (A)] .

======(C) Report– ======

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this section, and each year thereafter, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] shall submit to the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]] a report that contains each recommendation described under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 subparagraph (B)] that the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] approves by a majority vote.

=====(4) Powers of the commission– =====



======(A) Hearings– ======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] may hold such hearings, take such testimony, and receive such evidence as is necessary to carry out this subsection.

======(B) Information from federal agencies– ======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] may secure directly from any Federal department or agency such information as the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] considers necessary to carry out this subsection. Upon request of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Chairperson of the Commission]] , the head of such department or agency shall furnish such information to the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] .

======(C) Postal services– ======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] may use the United States mails in the same manner and under the same conditions as other departments and agencies of the Federal Government.

======(D) Gifts– ======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] may accept, use, and dispose of gifts or donations of services or property.

=====(5) Commission personnel matters– =====



======(A) Compensation of members– ======

Each member of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] who is not an officer or employee of the Federal Government (including the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] ) shall be compensated at a rate equal to the daily equivalent of the annual rate of basic pay prescribed for level IV of the Executive Schedule under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5315 section 5315 of title 5] for each day (including travel time) during which such member is engaged in the performance of the duties of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] . All members of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] who are officers or employees of the United States, including the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] , shall serve without compensation in addition to that received for their services as officers or employees of the United States.

======(B) Travel expenses– ======

The members of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] shall be allowed travel expenses, including per diem in lieu of subsistence, at rates authorized for employees of agencies undersubchapter I of chapter 57 of title 5while away from their homes or regular places of business in the performance of services for the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] .

======(C) Staff– ======



=======(i) In general– =======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Chairperson of the Commission]] may, without regard to the civil service laws and regulations, appoint and terminate an executive director and such other additional personnel as may be necessary to enable the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] to perform its duties. The employment of an executive director shall be subject to confirmation by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] .

=======(ii) Compensation– =======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Chairperson of the Commission]] may fix the compensation of the executive director and other personnel without regard tochapter 51 and subchapter III of chapter 53 of title 5relating to classification of positions and General Schedule pay rates, except that the rate of pay for the executive director and other personnel may not exceed the rate payable for level V of the Executive Schedule under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5316 section 5316 of title 5] .

======(D) Detail of government employees– ======

Any Federal Government employee, including an employee of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] , may be detailed to the [[w:|Commission]] without reimbursement, and such detail shall be without interruption or loss of civil service status or privilege.

======(E) Procurement of temporary and intermittent services– ======

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Chairperson of the Commission]] may procure temporary and intermittent services under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/3109 section 3109(b) of title 5] at rates for individuals that do not exceed the daily equivalent of the annual rate of basic pay prescribed for level V of the Executive Schedule under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/5316 section 5316 of title 5] .

=====(6) Permanent advisory commission– =====

 Section 14 of the Federal Advisory Committee Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/App. 5 U.S.C. App.] )shall not apply to the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] .

=====(7) Authorization of appropriations– =====



======(A) In general– ======

There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be necessary to the [[w:United States Postal Service|Commission]] to carry out this Act.

======(B) Availability– ======

Any sums appropriated under the authorization contained in this section shall remain available, without fiscal year limitation, until expended.

====(f) Innovation strategy– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

Not later than 1 year after the date of enactment of this section, the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]] shall submit, at the same time the President submits the annual budget request under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105 of title 31] for that year, and together with the report on performance under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 subsection (g)] , a comprehensive strategy for maximizing the revenue of the Postal Service through innovative postal and nonpostal products and services to—
:(A) theCommittee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate;
:(B) theCommittee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives; and
:(C) the [[w:|Postal Regulatory Commission]] .

=====(2) Matters to be addressed– =====

At a minimum, the strategy required by this subsection shall address—
:(A) the specific innovative postal and nonpostal products and services to be developed and offered by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] , including the nature of the market demand to be satisfied by each product and service and the estimated date by which each product and service will be introduced;
:(B) the cost of developing and offering each product or service;
:(C) the anticipated sales volume for each product and service;
:(D) the anticipated revenues and profits expected to be generated by each product and service;
:(E) the likelihood of success of each product and service and the risks associated with the development and sale of each product and service;
:(F) the trends anticipated in market conditions that may affect the success of each product and service during the 5-year period following the submission of the report; and
:(G) the metrics that will be used to assess the effectiveness of the innovation strategy.

=====(3) Strategy updates– =====

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]] shall—
:(A) update the strategy required under this subsection every 2 years; and
:(B) submit the updated strategy, at the same time the President submits the annual budget request under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105 of title 31] for that year, and together with the report on performance under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 subsection (g)] , to—
::(i) theCommittee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate;
::(ii) theCommittee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives; and
::(iii) the [[w:|Postal Regulatory Commission]] .

====(g) Report on performance– ====



=====(1) In general– =====

The [[w:United States Postal Service|Postmaster General]] shall submit an annual report that describes the progress of the Postal Service in implementing the strategy under this section, at the same time the President submits the annual budget request under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/31/1105 section 1105 of title 31] , to—
:(A) theCommittee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate;
:(B) theCommittee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives; and
:(C) the [[w:|Postal Regulatory Commission]] .

=====(2) Matters to be addressed– =====

At a minimum, a report under this subsection shall include—
:(A) the revenue generated by each product and service developed through the strategy under this section and the costs of developing and offering each product or service for the preceding year;
:(B) the total sales volume and revenue generated by each product and service on a monthly basis for the preceding year;
:(C) trends in each market in which a product or service is intended to satisfy a demand;
:(D) products and services identified in the strategy under this section that are to be discontinued, the date on which the discontinuance will occur, and the reasons for the discontinuance;
:(E) alterations in products and services identified in the strategy under this section that will be made to address changing market conditions, and an explanation of how the alterations will ensure the success of the products and services; and
:(F) the performance of the strategy under this section according to the metrics identified under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 subsection (e)(2)(G)] .

====(h) Study and report by Comptroller General– ====



=====(1) Study– =====



======(A) In general– ======

The [[w:Comptroller General of the United States|Comptroller General of the United States]] shall conduct a study on the implementation of the strategy under this section that assesses the effectiveness of the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] in identifying, developing, and selling innovative postal and nonpostal products and services.

======(B) Contents– ======

The study under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 subparagraph (A)] shall include—
:(i) an audit of the costs of developing each innovative postal and nonpostal product and service developed or offered by the [[w:United States Postal Service|Postal Service]] ;
:(ii) the sales volume of each product and service;
:(iii) the revenues and profits generated by each product and service; and
:(iv) the likelihood of continued success of each product and service.

=====(2) Report– =====

Not later than 3 years after the date of enactment of this section, the [[w:Comptroller General of the United States|Comptroller General]] shall submit a report concerning the study under[http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/39/209 paragraph (1)] to—
:(A) theCommittee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs of the Senate; and
:(B) theCommittee on Oversight and Government Reform of the House of Representatives..
</blockquote>
