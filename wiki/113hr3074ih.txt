{{Center|A BILL}}

To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to require the Secretary of the Treasury to notify the taxpayer each time the taxpayer’s information is accessed by the Internal Revenue Service.

=Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the “IRS Abuse Protection Act of 2013” .

=Sec. 2. Notice relating to accessing account, return, or return information=



==(a) In general– ==

  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 6103] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986is amended by redesignating subsection (q)as subsection (r)and by inserting after subsection (p)the following new subsection:

<blockquote>

===(q) Notice relating to accessing account, return, or return information– ===



====(1) In general– ====

The [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall provide notice, in writing, to a taxpayer any time the taxpayer’s account, return, or return information is accessed by the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] .

====(2) Special rules relating to investigations– ====



=====(A) Investigations by the Secretary– =====

In the case of any civil or criminal investigation, the notice required by paragraph (1)shall be provided not later than 1 year after such investigation is closed.

=====(B) Investigations by States– =====

In the case of any investigation by a State using information provided pursuant to subsection (d), the notice required by paragraph (1)shall be provided after the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] receives notice with respect to such investigation pursuant to subsection (d)(7). Notice provided pursuant to this subparagraph shall include all information provided to the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] pursuant to subsection (d)(7).

====(3) Notice– ====

The notice required by paragraph (1)shall include the following:
:(A) Who accessed such account, return, or return information.
:(B) The purpose for which such account, return, or return information was accessed.
:(C) How such account, return, or return information was accessed.

====(4) Copy of information accessed– ====

In addition to the notice required to be provided by paragraph (1), the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall provide with such notice a copy of all information accessed.

====(5) Subsequent use of accessed information– ====

If a report or other use of an account, return, or return information for which notice is provided under paragraph (1)is made, the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall provide such report or a report of such use to the taxpayer.

====(6) Taxpayer rights– ====

The [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] shall include with each notice provided under this subsection a notice of taxpayer rights pursuant to the Taxpayer Bill of Rights 2..
</blockquote>


==(b) Availability of Inspector General for Tax Administration reports– ==

  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 7803(d)] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

===(4) Availability of Inspector General for Tax Administration reports– ===

If the [[w:Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration|Inspector General for Tax Administration]] investigates any unauthorized use a taxpayer’s account, return, or return information, the [[w:Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration|Inspector General for Tax Administration]] shall notify the taxpayer of such investigation and provide full access to any report by the [[w:Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration|Inspector General for Tax Administration]] with respect to the investigation..
</blockquote>


==(c) State access to taxpayer information– ==

  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 6103(d)] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

===(7) Submission of notification to Secretary– ===

The [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] may not provide any access or disclosure under the preceding paragraphs of this subsection until the entity to be provided access or disclosure agrees to notify the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] within 1 year after an investigation is closed the identity of who accessed such information, what was accessed, why it was accessed and how it was accessed..
</blockquote>


==(d) Reports of Unauthorized access to Congress– ==

  [http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// Section 6103(f)] of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

<blockquote>

===(6) Unauthorized access reports, etc– ===

Notwithstanding any other provision of this section, the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] with respect to [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]] employees, and the [[w:Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration|Inspector General for Tax Administration]] with respect to any audit, shall submit to each member of the committees referred to in paragraph (1)any report of the [[w:United States Department of the Treasury|Secretary]] or the [[w:Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration|Inspector General for Tax Administration]] , as the case may be, regarding unauthorized access, violation of rights, laws, or any rules or regulations of the [[w:Internal Revenue Service|Internal Revenue Service]] ..
</blockquote>


==(e) Effective dates– ==



===(1) Subsection– ===

The amendment made by subsection (a)shall apply with respect to information accessed after the date of the enactment of this Act.

===(2) Subsection– ===

The amendment made by subsection (b)shall apply with respect to investigations closed after the date of the enactment of this Act.

===(3) Subsection– ===

The amendment made by subsection (c)shall apply to access and disclosures after the date of the enactment of this Act.

===(4) Subsection– ===

The amendment made by subsection (d)shall apply with respect to information accessed and reports prepared after the date of the enactment of this Act.
