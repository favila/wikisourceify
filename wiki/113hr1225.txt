{{Center|A BILL}}

To direct the Secretary of the Interior to place certain lands in Skagit and San Juan Counties, Washington, into trust for the Samish Indian Nation, and for other purposes.

={{anchor|HE8113BDAE34041C488742F54F8A15B86}}Section 1. Short title=

This Act may be cited as the“Samish Indian Nation Homelands Act of 2013”.

={{anchor|H926E4475C8C1417EB80FD7EB892B5122}}Sec. 2. Definitions=



=={{anchor|H4E7F3FE5899244DE93ED03D5A4A85430}}(a) Parcels– ==

The term“parcels”means the 16 parcels of approximately 95 acres of land owned by the Tribe, located in Skagit County and San Juan County, Washington, and depicted on the Map.

=={{anchor|HEFDAA0883F374DCE8DC0D75186F6A2CF}}(b) Map– ==

The term“Map”means the map titled“the Samish Indian Nation Homeland Map”dated June 20, 2012.

=={{anchor|H8AE69A9F132D4FC3BB94B1439F15AA1E}}(c) Secretary– ==

The term“Secretary”means the Secretary of the Interior.

=={{anchor|H720AD44A8262498D9E182FC873790E6B}}(d) Tribe– ==

The term“Tribe”means the Samish Indian Nation, a federally recognized Indian Tribe.

={{anchor|H7CFFBDFE6955412AB5E9391F473C10FD}}Sec. 3. Land into trust=



=={{anchor|H85BF9FBA37D7444AA237EEBE014036B8}}(a) Action by the Secretary of the Interior– ==



==={{anchor|HC86B90F1456243F8A77D974733361CB1}}(1) Fulfillment of conditions– ===

On fulfillment of each condition described in paragraph (2), and in accordance with the regulations of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Department of the Interior]] for implementing the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/4321 42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.] )that are applicable to trust land acquisitions for Indian tribes that are mandated by Federal legislation, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall take the land described in the Map into trust for the benefit of the Tribe.

==={{anchor|HDEBBE3D00ADF44679ED42A4EC901BAC2}}(2) Conditions– ===

The conditions referred to in paragraph (1)are that the Tribe shall—
:(A) convey to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] all right, title, and interest in and to the parcels of land described in the Map; and
:(B) submit to the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] a request to take the parcels of land described in the Map into trust for the Tribe.

=={{anchor|HD877C774D58E45CA937C75F785D2B5C7}}(b) Survey– ==



==={{anchor|HE66B8F80971D43E7AEB9CDAAE919A58F}}(1) Responsibility for survey– ===

The Tribe shall conduct a survey of the boundaries of the parcels described in the Map and designated for the Tribe and submit the survey to the [[w:Bureau of Indian Affairs|Bureau of Indian Affairs]] .

==={{anchor|H59180124BAED46C6B7212407A5A57565}}(2) Approval of survey– ===


:(A) Not later than 90 days after the date on which the survey is submitted under this subsection, the [[w:Bureau of Indian Affairs|Director of the Bureau of Indian Affairs]] shall complete a review of the survey, and provide the Tribe with notice of concurrence of the survey.
:(B) Not later than 120 days after the date on which concurrence is provided to the Tribe, the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] shall submit a copy of the survey to the appropriate Committees of Congress, and make the survey available to the public at the appropriate office of the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] .

=={{anchor|H15EA2F17E78C430989B4BFD16AAA2B2E}}(c) Effect of Act– ==

Nothing in this Act limits the existing rights or claims of the Samish Indian Nation.

={{anchor|H7C9ED12A610C430AB5F815D4CFF98777}}Sec. 4. Hunting, fishing, trapping, and gathering=

This Act shall not grant, restore, or diminish any hunting, fishing, trapping or gathering treaty right of any tribe.

={{anchor|HF44A86E5F0DF488085E43F28447FF702}}Sec. 5. Gaming prohibition=

The Tribe may not conduct on any land taken into trust pursuant to this Act any gaming activities—
:(1) as a matter of claimed inherent authority; or
:(2) under any Federal law (including the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text// 25 U.S.C. 2701 et seq.] )(including any regulations promulgated by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] or the [[w:|National Indian Gaming Commission]] pursuant to that Act)).
