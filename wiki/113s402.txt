{{Center|A BILL}}

To provide for the addition of certain real property to the reservation of the Siletz Tribe in the State of Oregon.

={{anchor|H7A826C9078C74C5BA0AEA20A8AD7DCAB}}Section 1. Treatment of certain property of the Siletz tribe of the State of Oregon=

 Section 7 of the Siletz Tribe Indian Restoration Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/711e 25 U.S.C. 711e] )is amended by adding at the end the following:

<blockquote>

=={{anchor|HDC59CB11C81D42E28733DC9DEB9A1617}}(f) Treatment of certain property– ==



==={{anchor|HAED60C4422C74FCF8AC7D20804782FA6}}(1) In general– ===



===={{anchor|id0FE219F3F6AC49ADA87F972EE8662A71}}(A) Title– ====

The [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] may accept title to any additional number of acres of real property located within the boundaries of the original 1855 Siletz Coast Reservation established by Executive Order dated November 9, 1855, comprised of land within the political boundaries of Benton, Douglas, Lane, Lincoln, Tillamook, and Yamhill Counties in the State of Oregon, if that real property is conveyed or otherwise transferred to the United States by or on behalf of the tribe.

===={{anchor|idA774A45BBB7347FC924074A8F687B918}}(B) Trust– ====

Land to which title is accepted by the [[w:United States Department of the Interior|Secretary]] under this paragraph shall be held in trust by the United States for the benefit of the tribe.

==={{anchor|H28724BFDB5C641E1AC18535B97BC6237}}(2) Treatment as part of reservation– ===

All real property that is taken into trust under paragraph (1)shall—
:(A) be considered and evaluated as an on-reservation acquisition under part 151.10 of title 25, Code of Federal Regulations (or successor regulations); and
:(B) become part of the reservation of the tribe.

==={{anchor|H8CECDEE316E1428FA67C0727898A162C}}(3) Prohibition on gaming– ===

Any real property taken into trust under paragraph (1)shall not be eligible, or used, for any gaming activity carried out under the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act([http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/25/2701 25 U.S.C. 2701 et seq.] )..
</blockquote>
